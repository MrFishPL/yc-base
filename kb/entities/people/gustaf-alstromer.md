# Gustaf Alströmer — Compiled Truth

Former head of growth at Airbnb and now a YC Group Partner, Gustaf Alströmer is the corpus's most consistent voice on growth and money discipline. His recurring thesis: growth work only makes sense after product-market fit is proven via cohort retention, not vanity metrics [gustaf-alstromer-how-to-get-users-and-grow @ 13:11](https://youtu.be/T9ikpoF2GH0?t=791), and money should be spent as if another round will never come, with founders themselves doing sales and customer contact until fit is real [the-right-and-wrong-way-to-spend-money-at-your-startup @ 02:22](https://youtu.be/IRROi-Q1V44?t=142), [how-much-should-you-spend-after-fundraising-gustaf-alstr-mer @ 00:16](https://youtu.be/6IPuXAK24RE?t=16). He repeatedly draws on his years scaling Airbnb's and Facebook's growth teams (referral emails, share-sheet A/B tests, SEO, session-cookie tweaks, Northstar metrics) to argue that most companies grow through one dominant channel plus rigorous, hypothesis-driven experimentation rather than scattered tactics [advice-on-organizing-and-running-growth-teams-from-dan-hocke @ 06:09](https://youtu.be/tsg4vcDUyJA?t=369), [gustaf-alstr-mer-growth-for-startups @ 25:16](https://youtu.be/6lY9CYIY4pQ?t=1516). He also pushes founders to talk directly to users, launch earlier than they're comfortable with, and never spend on paid acquisition before revenue and unit economics justify it [yc-sus-gustaf-alstr-mer-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-growth-t @ 03:20](https://youtu.be/oog7n_1fR-o?t=200).
