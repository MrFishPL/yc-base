# Jessica Livingston — Compiled Truth

Y Combinator co-founder and its only non-technical founding partner, whose "social radar" let her judge applicants' earnestness, determination, and cofounder chemistry — qualities her technical cofounders (Paul Graham, Robert Morris, Trevor Blackwell) couldn't evaluate — and who effectively held veto power over funding decisions for YC's first seven years. Her recurring themes across talks: determination is the single most important founder trait, ranked above intelligence or idea quality; the two biggest early-startup killers are co-founder breakups and failing to make something people want; the best ideas grow organically out of founders' own lives rather than being invented deliberately, and almost every huge startup looked like a bad idea at first; YC bets on founders over initial plans, since ideas (Airbnb, PayPal) routinely pivot beyond recognition; batches, weekly dinners, and founder-first events were core to YC's DNA from day one; and having no LPs let YC take more risk and be benevolent toward struggling founders. She also gives candid, women-specific advice on rejection, empathy for users, and balancing kids with a startup.
