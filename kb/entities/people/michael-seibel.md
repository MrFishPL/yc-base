# Michael Seibel — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel is a Y Combinator partner and co-founder of Justin.tv/Twitch and Socialcam, and
the corpus's most prolific individual voice on early-stage execution. His recurring throughline:
startups are won or lost on **speed of learning from real customers**, not on ideas, pedigree,
fundraising, or hiring. He repeatedly attacks "cargo culting" — copying the surface trappings of
successful companies (funding announcements, org charts, PR, brand) instead of doing the hard,
boring work of finding a real problem, shipping a bare MVP fast, and personally listening to the
first desperate users [michael-seibel-building-product @ 05:16](https://youtu.be/C27RVio2rOs?t=316),
[silicon-valley-s-cargo-culting-problem @ 12:37](https://youtu.be/ECLJ95XNxA4?t=757). He draws
constantly on his own Justin.tv/Twitch scars — near-death cash crunches, feature bets that failed,
hiring too early — as cautionary tales, and pairs a blunt, metrics-driven view of startup mechanics
(vesting, equity splits, default-alive math, dev shops vs. technical co-founders) with a
persistent argument that founders should trust conviction and direct observation over investor
or "expert" opinion.
