# David Lieb — Compiled Truth

Co-founder of Bump (a viral but low-frequency, low-value app that never became a durable business)
who pivoted the team through direct user conversations into Photo Roll, acquired by Google and
built into Google Photos, which he led to a billion users before a leukemia diagnosis pushed him to
leave Google and coach founders at YC. His recurring throughline is that cohort retention, not
total usage or vanity growth, is the one honest signal of product-market fit; that founders should
go talk to users when stuck but trust behavioral data over what people say; and that consumer
products win by scaling a durable existing human need rather than chasing a trendy format or a
single optimizable metric.
