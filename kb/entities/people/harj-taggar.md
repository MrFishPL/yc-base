# Harj Taggar — Compiled Truth

Harj Taggar is a Y Combinator group partner and Lightcone Podcast co-host, and a co-founder of Triplebyte and (with Kevin Hale, Patrick Collison and others) auctomatic/Justin.tv-era startups. Across dozens of talks he returns to a consistent set of positions: evaluate startups and startup jobs like an investor evaluating growth and team rather than credentials or market size [harj-taggar-choosing-a-startup-to-work-at @ 00:51](https://youtu.be/evIZRpNQUx4?t=51); resilience, not confidence or pedigree, is the trait that actually predicts founder success [should-you-start-a-startup-startup-school @ 02:07](https://youtu.be/BUE-icVYRFU?t=127); founders should launch fast and repeatedly instead of waiting for a mythical, polished "the launch" moment [when-to-launch-your-startup-and-when-to-wait @ 05:14](https://youtu.be/f0tPjcgcwnQ?t=314); pre-product-market-fit hiring advice is toxic because almost all popular hiring wisdom is written for already-scaled companies [don-t-make-these-hiring-mistakes @ 01:29](https://youtu.be/CUZ6PKJZvXE?t=89); and, as a recurring Lightcone panelist, that AI has genuinely reset old rules on moats, distribution, hiring bars, and how fast companies can scale [ai-revolution-what-nobody-else-is-seeing @ 06:17](https://youtu.be/0LMK5JYkB94?t=377).
