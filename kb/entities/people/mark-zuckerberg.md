# Mark Zuckerberg — Compiled Truth

Facebook co-founder and CEO who, across YC/Startup School appearances, repeatedly frames Facebook's origin as an accident of curiosity rather than a deliberate company-building exercise: he never set out to start a company, chased a problem he personally cared about, and only formalized Facebook as a Delaware entity around six months in when Peter Thiel invested [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2012 @ 16:37](https://youtu.be/5bJi7k-y1Lo?t=997), [mark-zuckerberg-on-taking-risks-and-finding-talented-people @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/VAUt2j6juHU?t=0). A constant throughline is Peter Thiel's advice that in a fast-changing world the biggest risk is taking no risk at all, which Zuckerberg cites as the reason he turned down Yahoo's roughly billion-dollar acquisition offer just a couple of years into the company [mark-zuckerberg-on-taking-risks-and-finding-talented-people @ 01:04](https://youtu.be/VAUt2j6juHU?t=64), [mark-zuckerberg-how-to-build-the-future @ 22:34](https://youtu.be/Lb4IcGF5iTQ?t=1354). He consistently credits a learning-driven, growth-engineering culture — treating company-building like the scientific method and running tens of thousands of live product experiments — as the mechanism behind Facebook's repeated innovation [mark-zuckerberg-how-to-build-the-future @ 09:55](https://youtu.be/Lb4IcGF5iTQ?t=595), [mark-zuckerberg-how-to-build-the-future @ 10:37](https://youtu.be/Lb4IcGF5iTQ?t=637). On hiring, he returns often to the same point: because he started Facebook at 19 with no track record, the company institutionally favors raw talent over prior experience, and nearly all of its ~12 product group leads grew into the role internally [mark-zuckerberg-how-to-build-the-future @ 16:44](https://youtu.be/Lb4IcGF5iTQ?t=1004), [mark-zuckerberg-on-taking-risks-and-finding-talented-people @ 02:23](https://youtu.be/VAUt2j6juHU?t=143), [mark-zuckerberg-on-taking-risks-and-finding-talented-people @ 03:49](https://youtu.be/VAUt2j6juHU?t=229).
