# Garry Tan — Compiled Truth

President and CEO of **Y Combinator**, co-founder of Posterous and Initialized Capital, and one of the
Lightcone podcast's recurring hosts. Across design critiques, Lightcone episodes, and startup-school
talks he repeatedly pushes: (1) design is about clarity and function, not decoration, and homepages must
instantly answer "what is this / is it for me"
[critiquing-ai-startup-websites-with-yc-president-garry-tan @ 01:38](https://youtu.be/uIYujpFmvo8?t=98);
(2) the best AI startup ideas look "boring" and solve a specific business-logic problem rather than being
generic co-pilots or "GPT wrappers"
[the-truth-about-building-ai-startups-today @ 08:27](https://youtu.be/TwDJhUJL-5o?t=507);
(3) classic moats (Hamilton Helmer's Seven Powers) still apply in AI, just with new concrete shapes —
process power, cornered resources, switching costs
[the-7-most-powerful-moats-for-ai-startups @ 10:05](https://youtu.be/bxBzsSsqQAM?t=605);
(4) technical depth and youth/urgency are undervalued — the "safe" corporate path is now the risky one, and
starting young maximizes compounding
[why-this-is-the-perfect-time-to-start-a-startup @ 03:30](https://youtu.be/0TNTlMZFTWw?t=210);
(5) AI is shifting company-building economics, letting small teams (or even solo founders) ship what used
to take large teams
[the-age-of-the-40-year-old-solo-founder-is-here @ 40:07](https://youtu.be/8OOuCnZB-4o?t=2407).
