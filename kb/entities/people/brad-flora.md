# Brad Flora — Compiled Truth

YC Group Partner who appears across office-hours-style panels (often alongside Michael Seibel and
Harj Taggar) and solo in Startup School. His recurring throughline is discipline before
product-market fit — spend almost nothing, hire almost no one, launch fast and ugly, and charge
money early — paired with a debunking streak aimed at founder myths about competitors, fundraising,
and enterprise vs. startup customers.

**Recurring positions (cited):**
- Pre-product-market-fit, money can't buy product-market fit, only time to find it, so founders
  should spend almost nothing [the-right-and-wrong-way-to-spend-money-at-your-startup @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/IRROi-Q1V44?t=0).
- Almost all popular hiring advice is written for post-PMF companies, so applying it pre-PMF can
  accelerate a startup's death [don-t-make-these-hiring-mistakes @ 01:29](https://youtu.be/CUZ6PKJZvXE?t=89).
- There is no single "the launch" — founders launch over and over, and it's fine to show an
  imperfect product to the world more than once before people get it [when-to-launch-your-startup-and-when-to-wait @ 05:14](https://youtu.be/f0tPjcgcwnQ?t=314).
- The best feedback about a product comes in the three seconds after telling a customer the price,
  from their facial expression or tone of voice [turning-your-users-into-paying-customers @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/9pQJXR0Tcos?t=0).
- The core advice on competitors is depressing but simple: it's a hard problem, not a complicated
  one — building a genuinely better product beats land-grab fear [how-to-compete-with-amazon-and-google @ 15:09](https://youtu.be/tzsmJtKZ2No?t=909).
- YC's core fundraising advice is "believe the no, don't believe the why" — investor rejection
  reasons are rarely reliable expert diagnoses [investors-said-no-now-what @ 02:18](https://youtu.be/euZH0tVotPQ?t=138).
- Real fundraising looks like quiet one-on-one coffee-shop conversations, not Shark-Tank-style
  pitch drama, and startups don't need to impress investors, just convince them [how-startup-fundraising-works-startup-school @ 04:56](https://youtu.be/zBUhQPPS9AY?t=296).
- Founders should identify which proven "game" (Stripe, Gusto, or AWS-style) fits their product
  rather than cargo-culting a "sell to startups first" strategy [the-better-customer-startups-or-big-enterprise @ 15:24](https://youtu.be/Hwfu1zNwSWI?t=924).
- Lacking capital and connections forces first-time founders into behaviors — talking to real
  users, building something genuinely good — that actually improve their odds [advantages-of-a-first-time-founder @ 07:07](https://youtu.be/bp_kMA-eTsE?t=427).
