# Reid Hoffman — Compiled Truth

LinkedIn co-founder and early PayPal executive turned venture investor, Hoffman is a recurring
YC speaker whose talks return to a small set of frameworks: founders need an explicit, trackable
"investment thesis" about their idea rather than blind faith or pure data-following; distribution
and financing are as fundamental to strategy as the product itself; great founders have a couple
of real edges rather than being universally strong, which is why two or three complementary
co-founders usually beat a solo founder; and scaling a company ("blitzscaling") requires
deliberately tolerating chaos and inefficiency, hiring for values and aptitude over static skills,
and reorganizing constantly because "what got you here won't get you there."
