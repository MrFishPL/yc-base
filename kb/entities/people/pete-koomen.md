# Pete Koomen — Compiled Truth

Co-founder of **Optimizely** and a YC group partner who teaches sales, conversion, and AI-native
product design. Across design reviews, startup-school talks, and Lightcone episodes he repeatedly
pushes: (1) conversion is an engineering problem — map the funnel to the product's "aha moment,"
cut every unnecessary step, collapse competing calls-to-action into one, and remove friction like
forced signup forms
[design-tips-to-convert-more-customers-design-review @ 10:30](https://youtu.be/tJTxDqjO4vg?t=630);
(2) before product-market fit, sales is inescapably a founder's job because only the founder has the
conviction and problem expertise to sell, and money can't buy PMF — only time to find it
[enterprise-sales-startup-school @ 01:51](https://youtu.be/0fKYVl12VTA?t=111),
[the-right-and-wrong-way-to-spend-money-at-your-startup @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/IRROi-Q1V44?t=0);
(3) top-down and bottoms-up go-to-market are both legitimate paths whose fit depends on whether the
product resonates with individual users or executives, not on ideology
[which-sales-strategy-is-best-for-your-startup @ 05:18](https://youtu.be/o1HFZ8P4rA8?t=318);
(4) most AI features are "horseless carriages" that bolt a model onto old software UX instead of
exposing the system prompt so users can personalize how their software behaves
[how-to-design-better-ai-apps @ 10:37](https://youtu.be/WJoZK9sMwvw?t=637); and (5) inside YC he
championed rebuilding the org as AI-native by giving agents unrestricted read access to one shared
database and defaulting every agent conversation to be open and broadcast
[inside-yc-s-ai-playbook @ 06:13](https://youtu.be/B246K_G7mHU?t=373).
