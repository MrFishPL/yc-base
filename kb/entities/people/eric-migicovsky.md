# Eric Migicovsky — Compiled Truth

Founder of **Pebble** (the smartwatch company, whose $10M Kickstarter he tells the origin story of) and a YC
partner who draws on his five-year, hardware-heavy founder journey to coach YC companies on hiring, users,
pricing, growth, and fundraising. Across his talks he repeatedly pushes:
(1) direct founder-to-user contact is non-negotiable at every stage and can never be outsourced to
salespeople or product heads
[eric-migicovsky-how-to-talk-to-users @ 00:22](https://youtu.be/MT4Ig2uqjTc?t=22);
(2) "do things that don't scale" — personal calls, in-person onboarding, emailing users from your own
Gmail — remains a durable founder advantage even after moving to software
[yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-hosts-founder-office-hours @ 19:45](https://youtu.be/4ynbXUaKHHs?t=1185);
(3) getting one real paying customer, not signups or hypothetical enthusiasm, is the actual bar for
validating an idea or a pivot
[yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-dalton-caldwell-discuss-pivoting-pitc @ 05:29](https://youtu.be/msWFarwD-fU?t=329);
(4) early hires should be chosen for trust, flexibility, and multidisciplinary skill over deep domain
expertise, since the roadmap and product will inevitably change
[hiring-tips-from-pebble-watch-founder-eric-migicovsky @ 00:41](https://youtu.be/IGj7CK-K6q4?t=41);
(5) frugality and picking the right "business-model-company fit" matter as much as product-market fit —
Pebble nearly died from misjudging its hits-driven market and overspending on an early manufacturing run
[a-conversation-on-hard-tech-with-eric-migicovsky @ 20:06](https://youtu.be/7atClUTkR3A?t=1206);
(6) crowdfunding only makes sense either to fund a napkin-idea prototype or as a marketing channel once a
product is already validated
[a-conversation-on-hard-tech-with-eric-migicovsky @ 12:04](https://youtu.be/7atClUTkR3A?t=724);
(7) sustainable growth requires knowing unit economics (ROI per acquired user, baseline event analytics)
before scaling any channel
[yc-sus-gustaf-alstr-mer-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-growth-t @ 03:20](https://youtu.be/oog7n_1fR-o?t=200);
(8) landing pages convert on trust signals and a single clear call to action, not clever copy
[yc-sus-aaron-epstein-and-eric-migicovsky-give-website-feedba @ 10:35](https://youtu.be/MP32_y4JZDQ?t=635);
(9) advisors are most valuable when they are only a few years ahead of the founder in the same domain, since
their experience is still fresh and specific
[startup-advisor-equity-pebble-watch-founder-eric-migicovsky @ 00:19](https://youtu.be/XvlKwwKfS5c?t=19);
(10) Silicon Valley's network and competitive density are real forcing functions, but founders should stay
put if their customers, an unfair advantage, or a cheaper hiring pool are already where they are
[should-you-move-your-company-to-silicon-valley-eric-migicovs @ 04:46](https://youtu.be/Zj1WyGpSbrM?t=286).
