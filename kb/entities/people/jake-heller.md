# Jake Heller — Compiled Truth

Co-founder and CEO of **Casetext**, a legal-AI company he ran through a decade-long "trough of sorrow"
before early GPT-4 access finally produced true product-market fit and a **$650M acquisition by Thomson
Reuters**. Across his talks he repeatedly pushes: (1) AI addressable markets should be sized against the
salaries/spend on the humans currently doing the job, not legacy SaaS seat pricing — often 10x-1000x larger
than the old TAM [from-idea-to-650m-exit-lessons-in-building-ai-startups @ 06:03](https://youtu.be/l0h3nAW13ao?t=363);
(2) reliable AI products come from decomposing the best human expert's process into deterministic code plus
judgment prompts, then grinding on rigorous, objectively-gradable evals
[from-idea-to-650m-exit-lessons-in-building-ai-startups @ 15:30](https://youtu.be/l0h3nAW13ao?t=930);
(3) a "golden demo" that compresses days of expert work into minutes is what makes skeptical enterprise
buyers sign immediately
[ai-and-the-future-of-law-the-10-year-overnight-success-story @ 08:54](https://youtu.be/qFBUd0b8TjY?t=534);
(4) real product-market fit is unmistakable — enterprise sales cycles collapsing from 9-18 months to one
month, servers falling over, and inbound press
[why-vertical-llm-agents-are-the-new-1-billion-saas-opportuni @ 11:39](https://youtu.be/eBVi_sLaYsc?t=699);
(5) durable moats in AI products come from proprietary data, deep systems integrations, and accumulated
prompt/business logic — not the underlying LLM call
[why-vertical-llm-agents-are-the-new-1-billion-saas-opportuni @ 25:42](https://youtu.be/eBVi_sLaYsc?t=1542).
