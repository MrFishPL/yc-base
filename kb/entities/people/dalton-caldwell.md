# Dalton Caldwell — Compiled Truth

A Y Combinator Group Partner and long-time startup founder (imeem, App.net) who co-hosts YC's
"Dalton & Michael" series with Michael Seibel and gives many solo Startup School talks. His
recurring throughline: founders win by talking directly to customers, moving fast enough to
generate their own luck, avoiding "cargo culting" of superficial startup signifiers, and staying
brutally honest with themselves about whether something is actually working — rather than
outsourcing conviction to investors, hype, or borrowed idols.

Signature positions he repeats across talks:
- **Pivot fast, on real signal.** A pivot is just changing your idea, and founders should take many
  high-quality shots on goal rather than clinging to a little bit of traction (an "uncanny valley"
  trap) out of loss aversion [dalton-caldwell-all-about-pivoting @ 05:06](https://youtu.be/8pNxKX1SUGE?t=306), [dalton-caldwell-all-about-pivoting @ 06:46](https://youtu.be/8pNxKX1SUGE?t=406).
- **Luck is created by speed.** Moving faster than everyone else — talking to customers, changing
  ideas, writing code — increases a founder's surface area for a lucky break, as with Brex pivoting
  from a VR headset to a corporate card in six weeks [how-to-create-luck-dalton-caldwell-y-combinator-partner @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/NmEyx9TEkRw?t=0), [how-to-create-luck-dalton-caldwell-y-combinator-partner @ 01:01](https://youtu.be/NmEyx9TEkRw?t=61).
- **Cargo culting kills.** Founders copy the superficial trappings of successful companies (open
  offices, pricing models, a16z-style raises) instead of understanding the underlying reasons those
  choices worked in context [silicon-valley-s-cargo-culting-problem @ 00:44](https://youtu.be/ECLJ95XNxA4?t=44), [silicon-valley-s-cargo-culting-problem @ 10:25](https://youtu.be/ECLJ95XNxA4?t=625).
- **Founders shouldn't think like investors.** VC-style market-sizing and comps frameworks are
  built for evaluating companies near an exit, not for picking a first idea; what matters early is
  whether the founder understands the problem and can get customers [why-founders-shouldn-t-think-like-investors @ 00:48](https://youtu.be/SMi3JO-hsyY?t=48), [why-founders-shouldn-t-think-like-investors @ 03:21](https://youtu.be/SMi3JO-hsyY?t=201).
- **Investors can't fix your company.** Different investor archetypes (finance-background,
  big-company-exec, junior, influencer) push generic, autobiographical advice; only the founder can
  take full accountability for making the company work [why-investors-can-t-fix-your-company-dalton-caldwell-and-mic @ 01:49](https://youtu.be/UqKzpLqXuI0?t=109), [why-investors-can-t-fix-your-company-dalton-caldwell-and-mic @ 20:05](https://youtu.be/UqKzpLqXuI0?t=1205).
- **Most startups undercharge.** Successful products (Instacart, DoorDash, Airbnb, Zapier) launched
  as premium options, not cheaper substitutes, and "we win on price" is often a misleading signal
  [most-startups-are-undercharging-dalton-caldwell @ 01:27](https://youtu.be/RxjZbmr1d1w?t=87), [most-startups-are-undercharging-dalton-caldwell @ 02:10](https://youtu.be/RxjZbmr1d1w?t=130).
- **Default alive over default dead.** Founders should do the survival math early and cut costs,
  ad spend, or raise prices rather than assume the next round will be as easy as the last
  [save-your-startup-during-an-economic-downturn @ 01:28](https://youtu.be/0OVSTWozvfY?t=88), [save-your-startup-during-an-economic-downturn @ 19:39](https://youtu.be/0OVSTWozvfY?t=1179).
- **ZIRP distorted everything.** Zero interest rates forced public-market investors into venture,
  inflating unicorn counts and valuation multiples disconnected from revenue, and YC warned founders
  the music would stop [what-is-zirp-and-how-did-it-poison-startups @ 00:46](https://youtu.be/iMo5-4h_XwE?t=46), [what-is-zirp-and-how-did-it-poison-startups @ 09:23](https://youtu.be/iMo5-4h_XwE?t=563).
- **Applying to YC has near-zero downside.** "Too early" and "too far along" are self-imposed
  excuses; roughly 40% of a batch enters with just an idea, and reapplying after rejection with
  demonstrated progress is viewed favorably [how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-by-dalton-caldwell @ 04:34](https://youtu.be/8yiOcCPvyNE?t=274), [the-truth-about-y-combinator @ 09:20](https://youtu.be/DOez-RwJ7mg?t=560).
- **Don't copy your tech idols.** Sam Altman, Elon Musk, and Peter Thiel each took a conventional
  path before their famous later moves, and advice-givers are usually just telling their past selves
  what they wish they'd known [why-you-shouldn-t-copy-your-tech-idols @ 03:45](https://youtu.be/09mXPGVkfVA?t=225), [why-you-shouldn-t-copy-your-tech-idols @ 10:53](https://youtu.be/09mXPGVkfVA?t=653).
- **AI won't kill startups.** Fast technology shifts historically advantage startups over
  incumbents, and OpenAI/Anthropic are focused on AGI, not on building the specific products that
  make people's lives better — leaving that opportunity to founders [will-openai-kill-all-startups @ 02:38](https://youtu.be/smHw9kEwcgM?t=158), [will-openai-kill-all-startups @ 12:05](https://youtu.be/smHw9kEwcgM?t=725).
- **Most businesses shouldn't raise venture capital.** VC is a product built for 100x-1000x
  outcomes; forcing that model onto a business that won't grow that big makes everyone lose, while a
  bootstrapped founder building a small, non-VC business can still have "won" [should-your-startup-bootstrap-or-raise-venture-capital @ 02:50](https://youtu.be/D81y-kh11oI?t=170), [should-your-startup-bootstrap-or-raise-venture-capital @ 06:31](https://youtu.be/D81y-kh11oI?t=391).
- **Caring about customers is the moat.** Big companies are structurally built without liking their
  customers, so genuinely caring — letting them talk directly to the founder — is a competitive
  advantage incumbents can't replicate [how-to-earn-customers-for-life @ 06:00](https://youtu.be/LJay5YW4fLA?t=360), [how-to-earn-customers-for-life @ 08:54](https://youtu.be/LJay5YW4fLA?t=534).
- **Things that don't scale are a badge of honor, not a hack.** Early Facebook, Twitch, and Google
  survived by duct-taping unscalable, embarrassing infrastructure together; founders must earn the
  privilege to build "real" scalable systems by first proving people want the product [things-that-don-t-scale-the-software-edition-dalton-caldwell @ 00:56](https://youtu.be/TCPjk8Tpb5c?t=56), [things-that-don-t-scale-the-software-edition-dalton-caldwell @ 24:23](https://youtu.be/TCPjk8Tpb5c?t=1463).
