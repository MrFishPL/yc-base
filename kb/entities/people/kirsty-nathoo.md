# Kirsty Nathoo — Compiled Truth

YC's CFO and finance partner, who teaches founders to treat cash mechanics and cap-table math as
things they must personally own rather than outsource. Across her talks she pushes three recurring
positions: (1) financial survival is a discipline of a few simple, frequently-checked numbers —
bank balance, burn, and runway — not a job for lawyers, bookkeepers, or a full-time CFO until well
after scale [kirsty-nathoo-managing-startup-finances @ 01:29](https://youtu.be/LBC16jhiwak?t=89),
[kirsty-nathoo-managing-startup-finances @ 24:39](https://youtu.be/LBC16jhiwak?t=1479); (2)
founders must understand SAFE and cap-table mechanics themselves — dilution math, conversion
order, and option-pool timing — because "my lawyers deal with my cap table" is a dangerous mindset
[understanding-safes-and-priced-equity-rounds-by-kirsty-natho @ 01:57](https://youtu.be/Dk6JNTDec9I?t=117); and (3)
co-founder equity should default to roughly equal splits because the bulk of the value-creating
work still lies ahead, not in who had the idea first
[y-combinator-partners-q-a @ 20:19](https://youtu.be/ZTv4iO7e8bQ?t=1219),
[legal-and-accounting-basics-for-startups-with-kirsty-nathoo- @ 08:27](https://youtu.be/sd9yLmJ1Jfk?t=507).
