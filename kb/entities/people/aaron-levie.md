# Aaron Levie — Compiled Truth

Co-founder and CEO of **Box**. Across enterprise-software and AI-era talks he repeatedly pushes: (1) as
model intelligence commoditizes toward the cost of the underlying GPU, durable value shifts entirely to the
software layer wrapped around the model — the workflow, proprietary data, and business logic that delivers
an outcome
[how-ai-is-changing-enterprise @ 17:17](https://youtu.be/aIKfA3gIXwo?t=1037);
(2) enterprises should split AI initiatives into "context" (non-differentiating, best bought from vendors)
versus "core" (the proprietary value worth building in-house)
[how-ai-is-changing-enterprise @ 28:27](https://youtu.be/aIKfA3gIXwo?t=1707);
(3) AI has reopened a rare "new nouns and verbs" window for startups — unlike cloud, enterprises are already
convinced AI matters and just need someone to build the agent-based solution
[aaron-levie-why-startups-win-in-the-ai-era @ 17:53](https://youtu.be/uqc_vt95GJg?t=1073);
(4) seat-based SaaS pricing breaks down with agents, since agents can perform unlimited work regardless of
headcount, pushing pricing toward consumption tied to volume of work done
[aaron-levie-why-startups-win-in-the-ai-era @ 20:43](https://youtu.be/uqc_vt95GJg?t=1243);
(5) startups win in enterprise by starting narrow on a wedge incumbents can't copy and finding asymmetries
(technical, platform, or economic) that incumbents can't or won't replicate
[building-for-the-enterprise-with-aaron-levie-how-to-start-a- @ 38:36](https://youtu.be/Kd3CLs-vLtw?t=2316).
