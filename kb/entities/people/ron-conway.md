# Ron Conway — Compiled Truth

Founder of **SV Angel** and one of Silicon Valley's most prolific angels, with early bets on Twitter,
Facebook, Pinterest, Airbnb, and 750+ other companies. Across his Startup School and Startup Investor
School talks he repeatedly pushes: (1) SV Angel invests in the founder first and the idea second, often
deciding within minutes whether they like a founder
[ron-conway-startup-investor-school-day-4 @ 05:38](https://youtu.be/bgwqM6J3WeM?t=338);
(2) the single biggest predictor of success is inborn determination and "rifle focus" on the product,
seen in founders like Zuckerberg, Dorsey, and Silbermann
[ron-conway-startup-investor-school-day-4 @ 06:28](https://youtu.be/bgwqM6J3WeM?t=388),
[ron-conway-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 20:23](https://youtu.be/qvHhhIfu7Lo?t=1223);
(3) founders should pick fundraising partners for the value they add rather than chasing the highest
valuation, citing Airbnb's Brian Chesky choosing Jeff Jordan over higher offers
[ron-conway-at-startup-school-2013 @ 15:53](https://youtu.be/7bm-Xj2jMCk?t=953);
(4) because 40-60% of any portfolio fails and predicting winners individually is nearly impossible, SV
Angel invented a portfolio approach — spreading small checks across many founders/companies in a space
[ron-conway-startup-investor-school-day-4 @ 08:36](https://youtu.be/bgwqM6J3WeM?t=516),
[ron-conway-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 23:01](https://youtu.be/qvHhhIfu7Lo?t=1381);
(5) the biggest founder failure mode is denial about lack of traction — success is binary, and admitting
it isn't working actually boosts team morale
[ron-conway-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 29:56](https://youtu.be/qvHhhIfu7Lo?t=1796).
