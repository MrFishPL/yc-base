# Katie Dill — Compiled Truth

Stripe's Head of Design (previously at Lyft and Airbnb), who argues across her talks that design
is not decoration but the mechanism that builds user trust and confidence in novel or high-stakes
products. Her recurring positions: quality has a strict priority order (functional, then usable,
then crafted); teams left alone will drift toward mediocrity and must actively fight for
exceptional work ("progress over perfection," aiming for an "MVQP" rather than a bare MVP or a
perfect launch); founders who only value design when it "moves the numbers" will systematically
underinvest in it; small inconsistencies (typos, mismatched button capitalization, decorative
elements that communicate nothing) erode trust disproportionately; and ongoing, direct exposure to
real users — via practices like Stripe's "walking the store" — is what keeps design honest as a
company and its product surface grow.
