# Adora Cheung — Compiled Truth

Adora Cheung co-founded and led Homejoy, a home-cleaning marketplace, after three years and roughly a dozen failed ideas, then became a Y Combinator partner and Startup School host teaching founders to find ideas from lived problems, ruthlessly prioritize by KPI impact, and scale only once product-market fit is real. Across her talks she repeats a few core positions: pick one primary metric and bet the company on it [adora-cheung-how-to-set-kpis-and-goals @ 01:37](https://youtu.be/lL6GdUHIBsM?t=97); the highest-leverage work is almost always talking to users and building/iterating product [adora-cheung-how-to-prioritize-your-time @ 03:00](https://youtu.be/XcCmMOWuAF4?t=180); don't scale anything until you've confirmed product-market fit, a large opportunity, and good timing [adora-cheung-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 01:51](https://youtu.be/Z9C1kBmEdtY?t=111); and do-things-that-don't-scale tactics (unscalable acquisition, manual onboarding, founders becoming the product's own supply) are how you learn what to eventually automate [adora-cheung @ 11:32](https://youtu.be/lVDmyRxeGCo?t=692). As interviewer and mentor, she pushes founders past indecision toward fast, forcing-function deadlines, and champions Startup School as a low-cost way to give founders anywhere in the world the mentorship and community YC provides.
