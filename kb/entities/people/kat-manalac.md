# Kat Mañalac — Compiled Truth

Kat Mañalac is a Y Combinator partner whose recurring themes are distribution and go-to-market discipline (launch is a repeatable process, not a single moment; content and PR only work once you have something people want; do unscalable manual things to win first users), founding-team fundamentals (talk to real users, co-founder fit, equal equity splits), and the YC application/selection lens (concise pitches, domain experts on huge problems, balanced teams). She also hosts founder interviews on scaling and on shutting down, and has appeared on multi-partner Q&A panels.

- Founders should stop treating launch as one high-stakes moment and instead launch continuously across many channels, since waiting months to perfect a product before shipping risks the startup dying before it gets a second chance [kat-ma-alac-how-to-launch-again-and-again @ 00:37](https://youtu.be/3xU050kMbHM?t=37)
- Press is not a scalable acquisition strategy and content marketing should only start after you've made something people want — both only work once product-market fit is real [pr-content-for-growth-by-kat-man-alac-and-craig-cannon @ 02:43](https://youtu.be/JL9yoVFHx-Q?t=163)
- While solo founding is possible, it's rare among top YC companies (only 3 of the top 50 by valuation were solo-founded), and YC favors balanced co-founding teams with roughly equal equity splits [how-to-find-a-cofounder-kat-manalac @ 00:37](https://youtu.be/CRLzkbtKPGM?t=37)
- If she could give founders only one piece of advice, it would be to talk to your users, since founders often over-polish product before getting it in front of real people [kat-manalac-s-whale-ama @ 02:46](https://youtu.be/0eWRcwmjNBQ?t=166)
- Do manual, unscalable groundwork to win first users — e.g. Lugg's founders physically approaching people outside IKEA — and don't chase "whale" enterprise customers early since smaller customers give faster feedback [yc-sus-kat-ma-alac-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-week-2-sus-le @ 47:16](https://youtu.be/OK6sdErKWW0?t=2836)
