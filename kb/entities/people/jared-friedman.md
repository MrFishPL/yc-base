# Jared Friedman — Compiled Truth

Y Combinator Group Partner and co-founder of Scribd (which he applied to YC with while still just a founding team and an idea, going through YC's Summer 2006 batch). A regular host on The Lightcone podcast alongside Garry Tan, Diana Hu, and Harj Taggar, and a frequent Startup School speaker on hard tech, biotech, and idea-evaluation. His recurring positions: hard tech and biotech are underrated because they carry technical risk but little market risk, and can be de-risked cheaply with small, fast milestones [inside-the-hard-tech-startups-turning-sci-fi-into-reality @ 02:49](https://youtu.be/erDE2e69dlc?t=169); the "GPT wrapper" and "foundation models capture all the value" fears of 2023 were both proven wrong by durable, fast-growing application-layer startups [2024-the-year-the-gpt-wrapper-myth-proved-wrong @ 01:11](https://youtu.be/z0wt2pe_LZM?t=71); vertical AI agents can absorb payroll spend, not just software spend, making them potentially 10x bigger than the SaaS wave they resemble [vertical-ai-agents-could-be-10x-bigger-than-saas @ 20:39](https://youtu.be/ASABxNenD_U?t=1239); durable AI moats still map to classic categories (evals/data, switching costs, counter-positioning, brand) rather than to the underlying model [the-7-most-powerful-moats-for-ai-startups @ 06:04](https://youtu.be/bxBzsSsqQAM?t=364); and the best startup ideas come from founders mining their own unique experience or going "undercover" into unfamiliar industries rather than brainstorming or chasing trends [how-to-get-ai-startup-ideas @ 03:14](https://youtu.be/TANaRNMbYgk?t=194).
