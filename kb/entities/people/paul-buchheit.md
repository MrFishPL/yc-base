# Paul Buchheit — Compiled Truth

Creator of Gmail, coiner of Google's "Don't be evil," an early YC partner, and a prolific angel
investor (Facebook, Instagram, Reddit, AngelList, Buffer, and others). His recurring throughline
across talks is that great founders matter more than ideas, markets, or price, and that startups
win by being radically focused and frugal — doing more with less — because that is the only real
advantage an underpowered startup has against big incumbents. He argues good opportunities look bad
or unappealing at first, since an obviously good idea would already be taken by a better-resourced
player, and that reaching massive scale ultimately requires riding a large exponential shift in the
world rather than just working harder. He is also outspoken on AI policy, arguing that AI's power
should be distributed to individuals via open source rather than centralized in a few institutions
or governments.
