# Sam Altman — Compiled Truth

Former president of Y Combinator (Loopt founder before that) and CEO of OpenAI, who taught much
of YC's "How to Start a Startup" curriculum. Across a decade of talks he pushes a consistent
worldview: judge markets by their future growth rate rather than today's size, favor extreme
founder conviction over consensus, insist a small number of users must love (not just like) the
product before you scale, treat startups (and startup jobs) as investments to be evaluated like an
investor would, prize evangelism/speed/bias-toward-action over credentials and polish, and manage
founder psychology deliberately — highs and lows both widen with company size, and giving up is
what actually kills most startups.

**Talks:** 19 in the corpus (as sole, featured, or co-speaker; one as interviewer of Greg Brockman
and Szymon Sidor).
