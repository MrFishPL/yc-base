# Building at the Edge of Technology — Compiled Truth

**Thesis:** In the AI wave, the primary source of advantage is understanding the technology
*10x better than everyone else* and working at its frontier — more than mastering go-to-market or
business model. Because everyone worldwide grasps AI's importance at once, distribution has become
meritocratic: a superior, outcome-driving product can win even without warm intros, US presence,
or a prior network.

**State:** maturity: emerging (1 talk) · open threads: how this trades off against classic
"talk to users / GTM" advice for non-AI-infra startups.

**Why it holds (cited):**
- "This wave is more about are you living at the edge of the technology... do you understand this
  technology 10x better than everyone else"
  [india-can-create-the-largest-ai-companies @ 05:07](https://youtu.be/Ju8LVdvuxGM?t=307).
- Distribution is now meritocratic — a third-year IIT student cold-emailed and sold to US
  insurers; "people want meritocratic solutions... this is about whose solution is better"
  [india-can-create-the-largest-ai-companies @ 06:26](https://youtu.be/Ju8LVdvuxGM?t=386).
- The unlock requires *using* frontier tools at full strength — "letting the tokens rip"; paying
  for frontier-level usage is what puts you near the edge
  [india-can-create-the-largest-ai-companies @ 18:17](https://youtu.be/Ju8LVdvuxGM?t=1097).

**Open threads:** This is framed for the current AI wave and technically deep products. It is *not*
a blanket rejection of go-to-market work — reconcile with YC's classic distribution/sales advice
as more talks are ingested.
