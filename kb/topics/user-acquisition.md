# User Acquisition — Compiled Truth

YC's collective advice is that acquisition only really works once product-market fit is real — retention has to flatten into a curve rather than decay to zero, or every acquisition tactic just accelerates churn. Once fit exists, the strongest consumer companies get the large majority of growth organically (word of mouth, referrals, network effects, SEO), and at meaningful scale roughly 70-80% of new customers tend to come from a single dominant channel matched to how the product is naturally discovered, rather than a scattershot of tactics. Paid acquisition is treated as a disciplined, later-stage accelerant — only once real revenue exists and CAC is tracked to a retained, monetized user and stays safely under LTV — never a primary strategy or a substitute for a great product. Early on, founders should do unscalable, manual, personal things — cold emails written and sent by the founder, cold calls, door-to-door pitches, friends-and-family launches — to land first customers and learn what resonates, and continuously re-launch through communities rather than treating launch as a single event, before automating what works. Landing-page and funnel conversion (single clear CTA, low friction, trust signals, language pulled from real users) is treated as inseparable from acquisition, since a leaky funnel wastes every unit of acquisition effort spent filling it.
