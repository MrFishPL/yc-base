# Company Culture — Compiled Truth

YC's collective advice treats culture not as perks or a values poster but as the implicit, founder-modeled behavior that the first ~20 hires absorb and then propagate as the company scales — so founders should define a short list of values early, hire and fire against them without compromise, build small self-organizing teams once past ~25 people, and default to radical transparency and direct conflict resolution rather than letting friction or politics accumulate silently.

**State:** #talks: 74 · maturity: strong
**Open threads:** (1) Whether traditional management is necessary — Camille Fournier argues good management is essential and the "flat org, no managers" ZIRP-era assumption was wrong, while Replit's Amjad Masad found that stripping out management layers and roadmaps (after hiring executives made the company miserable) made the team far more productive; the AI-org talks go further, arguing middle management itself may be structurally unnecessary. (2) Perks and remote work — one talk frames cutting perks and mandating return-to-office as a healthy sign of a company "getting real," while Zapier's remote-first, autonomy-driven culture is credited as a genuine hiring and retention advantage.
