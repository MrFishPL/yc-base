# Pricing — Compiled Truth

YC's collective advice on pricing is remarkably consistent: almost every startup underprices out of fear, and pricing is the highest-leverage, least-used growth lever available. Founders should set price from perceived customer value (not cost-plus), treat every price as a hypothesis to be tested and raised rather than a permanent decision, and let the price point dictate the go-to-market motion (self-serve at the low end, enterprise sales engineers at the high end). For B2B/enterprise deals, quantify the value delivered with the customer's champion and charge a meaningful fraction of it; for consumer/SMB self-serve products, showing a real price (or any price) filters for serious users and signals a legitimate product. The one live fault line is how AI is reshaping this: legacy seat/subscription pricing is giving way to usage- and outcome-based models for agents and AI-native services, even though committed recurring revenue is still prized for its downturn resilience.
