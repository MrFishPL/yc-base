# B2B — Selling Software to Businesses — Compiled Truth

YC's collective advice on B2B is that founders themselves must sell before product-market fit, price on quantified customer value rather than cost or gut feel, and pick a go-to-market motion (self-serve, inside sales, top-down enterprise, or embedded forward-deployed engineering) that matches the price point and the buyer's decision-making style. Durable advantage comes less from the product alone than from owning the workflow, the data, and a go-to-market experience competitors can't copy — a narrow wedge that expands upmarket, a champion mapped through the whole buying org, and founder-led implementation, not just the sale. In the AI era this playbook is being stress-tested: models are commoditizing fast, vertical AI agents can now absorb payroll cost as well as software spend, enterprises are proving structurally bad at building AI in-house, and founders are experimenting with outcome- and usage-based pricing and deep, Palantir-style embedding at the customer instead of classic SaaS subscriptions.
