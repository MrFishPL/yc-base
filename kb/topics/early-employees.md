# Early Employees — Compiled Truth

YC's collective advice is that a founder's first ~10-20 hires are the highest-leverage decisions in the company's life: they define culture, velocity, and who gets hired next, so founders should recruit them personally and proactively (like an outbound sales funnel) rather than waiting for inbound applicants — but only once the company has product-market fit, since pre-PMF hiring just adds cost and burn without fixing the underlying problem. Startups win talent against big tech not with money but with speed, personalized attention, and access to outsized responsibility and learning; in exchange, early employees take on outsized risk and should be compensated with outsized, transparently-explained equity, vesting on a standard schedule, with deliberate, values-driven culture-building starting on day one.

**State:** #talks: 29 · maturity: strong
**Open threads:** (1) How much equity is "fair" for an early technical hire — market-standard ranges (~1-2% for a first engineer) vs. the view that employees doing co-founder-equivalent work are routinely under-compensated at ~1% and deserve co-founder-level treatment. (2) Where to source early hires — lean on trusted personal networks/friends (fastest path to trust) vs. the warning that hiring only from founders' own networks breeds a homogeneous team that's hard to fix later.
