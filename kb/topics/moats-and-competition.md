# Moats and Competition — Compiled Truth

YC's collective advice is that fear of competitors is mostly wasted energy — startups almost never die from a rival, they die from never building something people want — so founders should obsess over customers, not competitor headlines or funding announcements. At the same time, the strongest founders eventually need a real, deliberate answer for what makes their business hard to copy, and the durable answers keep coming back to the same classic sources — proprietary data and workflows, switching costs, regulatory moats, network effects, and brand — rather than to whatever technology is currently trendy. In the AI wave specifically, raw model capability and thin prompt-wrapper features are explicitly not a moat, since they commoditize fast; the moat is what's built on top (evals, integrations, accumulated business logic) plus sheer execution speed, while incumbents' own legacy pricing and business-model conflicts can leave them structurally unable to respond even once they see the threat clearly.
