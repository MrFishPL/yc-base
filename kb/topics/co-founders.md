# Co-Founders — Compiled Truth

YC's near-unanimous advice is that most founders should not go it alone: a co-founder multiplies the amount of high-quality work that gets done, provides the emotional ballast to survive a decade-long "rollercoaster," and matches the pattern of nearly every iconic startup (Apple, Microsoft, Facebook). The single trait that matters more than any specific skill is how a person handles stress, ideally verified by having already worked with them under real pressure — because co-founder breakups, not competitors or markets, are repeatedly named as the number one killer of startups. The healthiest teams share the same high-level goals, split equity close to equal (with vesting, not stinginess, as the real hedge against a bad fit), pressure-test their relationship with a low-commitment trial project before formalizing, and treat conflict as inevitable — scheduling regular one-on-ones, addressing small frictions before they compound into "emotional debt," and separating cleanly rather than repairing a relationship once real conversation has stopped for months. Complementary, non-overlapping responsibilities reduce friction, but trust and enjoying the work together matter more than an exact skills match, since skills can be learned on the job.

(2) Do you need a "business" co-founder? (5) How should you handle a conflict-style mismatch?
