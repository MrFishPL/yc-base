# Valuation and Dilution — Compiled Truth

YC's collective advice treats valuation as a mechanism, not a scoreboard: the goal of any round is to get money in the bank on terms that let the company survive and keep founders in control, not to maximize the headline number. Founders must own their own cap-table math (post-money valuation = pre-money + amount raised; ownership = amount raised ÷ post-money or cap), understand that a SAFE's valuation cap rewards early risk rather than pricing the company today, and expect cumulative dilution of roughly 10-30% per round from seed through Series A — tracked carefully, since stacked SAFEs and option-pool increases can silently compound into much larger giveaways than founders expect. Because a too-high valuation can kill a raise (there's no credible way to lower the price later) while a too-low one over-dilutes, and because deal terms (board seats, participating preferred, pro rata) can hurt a founder as much as a bad price, the practical advice converges on: build leverage through growth and low desperation, don't over-optimize the exact cap number, vet the partner as carefully as the price, and treat every raise as diluted ownership you must justify.
