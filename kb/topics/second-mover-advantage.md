# Second-Mover Advantage — Compiled Truth

**Thesis:** With AI coding agents, a small team with strong product clarity can enter an existing
market and win by simply building a **better product, faster** — beating incumbents with far more
headcount. The classic caveat still applies: this works *unless* the first mover has strong
network effects (which few products truly do).

**State:** maturity: emerging (1 talk) · open threads: how to judge whether an incumbent's moat is
real network effects vs. mere head start.

**Why it holds (cited):**
- "A kind of reasonable way to compete right now is find something that's kind of working and then
  do it better... unless that first thing had incredible network effects, which few things do, you
  can just beat them from a better product"
  [india-can-create-the-largest-ai-companies @ 17:17](https://youtu.be/Ju8LVdvuxGM?t=1037).
- Case study: Giga (Varun) beat DoorDash for a contract with **8 people vs. several hundred** — a
  strong technical team building a superior product
  [india-can-create-the-largest-ai-companies @ 16:11](https://youtu.be/Ju8LVdvuxGM?t=971).
- Emergent cited as another company pulling this off and scaling fast
  [india-can-create-the-largest-ai-companies @ 17:17](https://youtu.be/Ju8LVdvuxGM?t=1037).

**Open threads:** The counter-force is network effects / distribution moats — capture examples of
where second-mover attempts *failed* as more talks are ingested.
