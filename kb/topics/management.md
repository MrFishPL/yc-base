# Management — Compiled Truth

YC's collective advice treats management as a genuine, learnable discipline rather than overhead: new managers should run real one-on-ones, build team identity early, resolve conflict instead of avoiding it, delegate via task-relevant maturity, and provide clarity in ambiguity. As companies scale, flat structures reliably break somewhere around 20-25 employees, at which point every founder's job shifts from building product to building a company — giving everyone one clear manager, formalizing raises and firing decisions, and deliberately engineering "mechanisms" (not just good intentions) to keep culture, alignment, and decision quality intact. A live, unresolved fault line runs through all of it: whether more management (hierarchy, executives, formal process) makes a company better, or whether the best-performing teams get there by aggressively minimizing it — a tension AI is now sharpening by threatening to replace human management "middleware" outright.
