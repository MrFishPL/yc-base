# The YC Application — Compiled Truth

YC's collective advice is that almost every self-imposed reason not to apply (too early, too niche, solo, already raised money, no US visa, has competitors) is not a real disqualifier, and that the right time to apply is the moment you have a founding team and an idea you're excited about — roughly a quarter to half of any batch is funded on team-and-idea alone, with zero MRR being a perfectly fine stage to apply at. What actually gets weighed is founder signal: technical ability to build and ship, a bias for action over polished narrative, honest and plainly-stated answers, and evidence of real conviction — not traction, pedigree, networking, or a slick pitch. The application itself is a low-cost, high-upside forcing function that clarifies a founder's own thinking, the video and interview reward blunt clarity over sales technique, misrepresenting traction is an automatic disqualifier, and rejected founders who visibly act on feedback and reapply are viewed favorably rather than penalized.
