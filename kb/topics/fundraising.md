# Fundraising — Compiled Truth

YC's collective advice treats fundraising as a means to an end, not a milestone: money buys time to run experiments toward the next real milestone, and it should be raised from a position of leverage (growth, revenue, demand) rather than desperation. The mechanics are converging on a standard playbook — a post-money SAFE for speed, ~10-20% dilution at seed and ~20-25% at Series A, roughly 12-24 months of runway per raise, and spending every dollar as if it's the last you'll ever raise, since most seed companies never raise a Series A at all. Investors are not experts on your market; they pattern-match founders against past outcomes and stack-rank live deals, so a "no" should be believed but its stated reason usually shouldn't — the fix for rejection is almost always more traction, not a better pitch or a reshaped deck. Pitch investors differently than customers (plain language, six things: what you do, stage, market size, pricing, unique insight, team), keep cold outreach short, vet investors as hard as they vet you (they are "employees you can't fire"), and weigh deal terms as heavily as valuation. Underneath all of it, the most repeated meta-point across speakers is that fundraising is an outcome of building a good business, not a substitute for one.
