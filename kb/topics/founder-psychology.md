# Founder Psychology — Compiled Truth

YC's collective advice treats founder psychology as an operating system, not a soft skill: determination beats intelligence as the single best predictor of success, but that determination has to be paired with the self-awareness to tolerate cognitive dissonance (holding bad news and continued drive at once), resist ego and denial when a company is actually dying, and treat rejection as noise rather than verdict. Motivation works best when it's intrinsic — obsession, focus, frugality, and love for the problem rather than chasing hype or investor approval — and conviction has to be built internally before it can be sold to anyone else. Because the work is genuinely brutal (loneliness, anxiety, suppressed conflict that shows up as insomnia), the same speakers who praise all-in obsession also normalize therapy, journaling, protecting sleep and peer groups, and auditing lifestyle as infrastructure for resilience, not indulgence.
