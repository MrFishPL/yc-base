# Distribution — Compiled Truth

YC's collective advice treats distribution as a discipline separate from (and often harder than) building the product: a great product with no way to reach people fails, and a mediocre product riding the right channel can win. The consistent pattern across founders and investors is that companies scale through one or two dominant channels chosen to match how their specific customer naturally discovers and shares things — not a scattershot of many — and that the earliest distribution is almost always manual, unscalable, and personal (cold-emailing bloggers, launching to friends-and-family, driving product around by car) before it gets automated into SEO, paid, referrals, or community loops. Launch early and often rather than waiting to be ready, favor low-risk community channels (Hacker News, Product Hunt, Bookface) for first traction, and remember that AI and even a superior product do not automatically solve distribution — incumbents still hold a real distribution advantage, and every new consumer or dev-tools company still has to earn its channel from scratch.
