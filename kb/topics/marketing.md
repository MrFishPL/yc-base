# Marketing — Compiled Truth

YC's collective advice treats marketing as downstream of product, not a substitute for it: nail what you're building and who it's for before spending a dollar on ads, PR, or brand. The most repeated tactical lesson — landing-page design, pitching, content, launches — reduces to the same few moves: say one clear thing, in the customer's own exact words, back it with real (not decorative) proof, and make the next action trivially easy to take. The recurring Design Review series converges on a single-CTA, no-jargon, "mom test" standard for homepage copy, real screenshots or demos over vague illustrations, and a low-friction path to the product's "aha moment" before any signup wall. Content and PR are earned, not bought — write things genuinely useful to actual customers (not vanity metrics), publish consistently for years, and pitch press yourself with a real news hook rather than hiring an agency. Paid acquisition, brand spend, and PR firms are treated as late-stage tools gated by real revenue and CAC-under-LTV discipline, while SEO, documentation quality, and organic word of mouth remain the most durable, underused channels — increasingly including documentation written for AI agents, not just humans. Pitching splits cleanly by audience: investors want concise, jargon-free clarity on market and mechanism, while customers respond to specific, concrete language lifted from how they themselves describe the problem.

**State:** #talks: 49 · maturity: strong
**Open threads:** Should founders build a public content/personal-brand presence as a growth engine (PostHog's deliberate "get talked about" strategy, Marques Brownlee's decade of consistency) or avoid chasing visibility altogether (Dalton Caldwell notes most successful YC founders have no public presence in their first 5-6 years and calls most "thought leadership" disguised content marketing that distracts from building)? Separately, is website conversion-rate optimization a universal lever (Kevin Hale's whole CRO framework, the entire Design Review series) or the wrong metric for enterprise products, which are won through sales and relationships rather than landing-page clicks (Linear's Karri Saarinen)?
