# Sales — Compiled Truth

YC's collective advice is unambiguous: founders, not hired reps, must do the earliest sales themselves, because only the founder has the conviction, product knowledge, and improvisational judgment needed before a repeatable playbook exists. Early sales is a numbers game of warm intros and targeted, personal outreach (not automation), qualifying hard before demoing, and treating pricing as a live experiment rather than a fixed truth — charge something real, raise it until deals start slipping, and let the value equation (not cost) set the number. Once a founder has personally closed deals for months, the motion (self-serve, inside sales, or enterprise) should be chosen to match the price point, an internal champion should be recruited and mapped through the full buying org, and the founder's job continues past signature into implementation, since a signed-but-unused contract isn't really a sale.
