# Equity — Compiled Truth

YC's collective advice treats equity as a motivation and alignment tool, not a prize to be negotiated or hoarded: split it close to equal among co-founders (the default that pattern-matches to nearly every successful company, with execution over the years ahead mattering far more than who had the idea first), protect that split with standard four-year/one-year-cliff vesting rather than a stingy split, and be generous with early employees and advisors because no successful founder has ever regretted giving away too much. Founders must also own the mechanics themselves — cap tables, SAFEs, option-pool sequencing, 83(b) elections, Delaware incorporation — since "my lawyers handle that" is a dangerous blind spot, and on the other side of the table, equity is only worth what the underlying company is actually worth, so engineers evaluating an offer, unicorn employees, and angel investors alike should sanity-check dilution, strike price, and real revenue rather than trust a headline valuation or vesting schedule designed to trap them.
