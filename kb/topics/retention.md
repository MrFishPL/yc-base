# Retention — Compiled Truth

YC's collective advice treats retention as the single most reliable signal of product-market fit: measure it by cohort against an action tied to real value, expect the curve to flatten (not decay to zero) at a level appropriate to your business model, and don't spend on growth, paid acquisition, or PR until it does. Founders should distrust verbal praise and vanity engagement numbers, actively cut users and features that don't serve real retention, chase net-dollar-retention above 100% once monetized, and remember both that reducing churn is usually cheaper than improving conversion and that small retention differences compound enormously over time.

**State:** #talks: 48 · maturity: strong
**Open threads:** Freemium's effect on retention/conversion is contested — HubSpot's Halligan says freemium works when the addressable market is very large, while Front's Mathilde Collin found her freemium tier drove only ~15% of paid conversions, was mostly a distraction, and was cut in favor of paid-only tiers. Also unresolved: whether raw engagement (time-on-app, algorithmic feed dwell time) is a healthy retention proxy or actively corrosive to it — Twitter/X's shift to an algorithmic feed grew engagement while eroding the retention of its best users as they quietly churned, whereas Suhail Doshi's data at Mixpanel found daily-active-usage correlated with reduced churn.
