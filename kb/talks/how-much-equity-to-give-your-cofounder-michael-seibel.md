# How Much Equity to Give Your Cofounder - Michael Seibel — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel argues that a founder's equity split should be designed to maximize long-term co-founder motivation, not to reflect a negotiated deal, and that vesting with a cliff is the real safety mechanism, not equity stinginess.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Most founders miss that equity splits exist to motivate co-founders to stick around for the years it takes to build a large company [how-much-equity-to-give-your-cofounder-michael-seibel @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/9NhEBVPlJs4?t=0)
- It's a fallacy to think the equity split should be based on what you "negotiated" rather than on what maximizes teammate motivation [how-much-equity-to-give-your-cofounder-michael-seibel @ 01:11](https://youtu.be/9NhEBVPlJs4?t=71)
- Standard four-year vesting is the CEO's real hedge against a bad co-founder choice, not a stingy equity split [how-much-equity-to-give-your-cofounder-michael-seibel @ 01:30](https://youtu.be/9NhEBVPlJs4?t=90)
- A one-year cliff means someone who leaves or is fired in year one gets nothing, giving the CEO a "get out of jail free" correction window [how-much-equity-to-give-your-cofounder-michael-seibel @ 01:52](https://youtu.be/9NhEBVPlJs4?t=112)
- Because vesting already provides that hedge, it usually benefits the CEO to be more generous with equity, not less [how-much-equity-to-give-your-cofounder-michael-seibel @ 02:12](https://youtu.be/9NhEBVPlJs4?t=132)
- The goal is for equity to be the thing that gets co-founders to work late, work weekends, and recruit their friends without needing daily motivation from the CEO [how-much-equity-to-give-your-cofounder-michael-seibel @ 02:51](https://youtu.be/9NhEBVPlJs4?t=171)
- Seibel's rule of thumb is that equal equity splits are a nice, easy default, though it can't be applied in every situation [how-much-equity-to-give-your-cofounder-michael-seibel @ 03:06](https://youtu.be/9NhEBVPlJs4?t=186)
- If you're not considering a co-founder's long-term motivation or don't think you'll need them long term, you should reconsider whether to make them a co-founder at all [how-much-equity-to-give-your-cofounder-michael-seibel @ 03:29](https://youtu.be/9NhEBVPlJs4?t=209)
