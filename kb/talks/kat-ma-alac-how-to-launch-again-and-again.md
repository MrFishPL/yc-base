# Kat Mañalac - How to Launch (Again and Again) — Compiled Truth

Kat Mañalac argues founders should stop treating launch as a single high-stakes moment and instead treat it as a continuous, repeatable process across many channels.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Waiting months to perfect a product before launching is dangerous — most launches get little attention, so a startup can die before it gets a second chance [kat-ma-alac-how-to-launch-again-and-again @ 00:37](https://youtu.be/3xU050kMbHM?t=37)
- A "silent launch" only needs a domain, company name, short description, contact, and a call to action, and can be shipped in a weekend [kat-ma-alac-how-to-launch-again-and-again @ 02:35](https://youtu.be/3xU050kMbHM?t=155)
- Do a friends-and-family launch early to test the pitch, but move past it quickly since friends and family aren't always the ideal user [kat-ma-alac-how-to-launch-again-and-again @ 03:38](https://youtu.be/3xU050kMbHM?t=218)
- Lugg's founders manually approached strangers struggling outside Ikea to validate a "hair on fire" problem before the app's back end even worked [kat-ma-alac-how-to-launch-again-and-again @ 05:05](https://youtu.be/3xU050kMbHM?t=305)
- Launching inside online communities like YC's Bookface or the Startup School forum (~40,000 founders) gives low-risk, high-quality early feedback [kat-ma-alac-how-to-launch-again-and-again @ 06:29](https://youtu.be/3xU050kMbHM?t=389)
- When posting to communities like Hacker News, write like a human, share genuinely interesting insights from users, and avoid marketing jargon [kat-ma-alac-how-to-launch-again-and-again @ 10:18](https://youtu.be/3xU050kMbHM?t=618)
- A request-for-access launch with viral skip-the-line mechanics (e.g., Magic, Superhuman) can turn a waitlist into a referral engine [kat-ma-alac-how-to-launch-again-and-again @ 11:36](https://youtu.be/3xU050kMbHM?t=696)
- Joy grew early by cold-emailing ~50 relevant bloggers for "best of" list placements and got meaningful traction from just four responses, without paying for placement [kat-ma-alac-how-to-launch-again-and-again @ 12:57](https://youtu.be/3xU050kMbHM?t=777)
- Stripe and Glossier treat every new product launch as a full press-community-social cycle repeated on a regular cadence (Glossier roughly every six to eight weeks) [kat-ma-alac-how-to-launch-again-and-again @ 15:34](https://youtu.be/3xU050kMbHM?t=934)
- Building a personal email list of everyone you've ever discussed your startup with — even loosely — pays off when you need to spread news like a press hit [kat-ma-alac-how-to-launch-again-and-again @ 16:55](https://youtu.be/3xU050kMbHM?t=1015)
