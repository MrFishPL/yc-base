# India Can Create The Largest AI Companies — Compiled Truth

A closing YC panel (moderated by YC partners, with guests [[puneet]] and [[arnav]]) on why the AI
wave is the moment for Indian founders to build **global** companies, and what mindset it takes.
Core thesis: this wave rewards **living at the edge of the technology** far more than mastering
go-to-market or business model — and AI has made distribution meritocratic, so outsiders can win.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**

- **The AI wave is about technical edge, not go-to-market.** "This wave is more about are you

  everyone else" [india-can-create-the-largest-ai-companies @ 05:07](https://youtu.be/Ju8LVdvuxGM?t=307). See [[building-at-the-edge-of-technology]].

  [india-can-create-the-largest-ai-companies @ 04:49](https://youtu.be/Ju8LVdvuxGM?t=289).
- **You no longer need warm intros to sell globally.** An IIT student cold-emailed and sold to US

  you know [india-can-create-the-largest-ai-companies @ 06:26](https://youtu.be/Ju8LVdvuxGM?t=386).
- **Don't take advice from people who aren't AI-native.** The traditional "safe, prestigious job"
  path may now be the risky one; surround yourself with high-agency people at the cutting edge
