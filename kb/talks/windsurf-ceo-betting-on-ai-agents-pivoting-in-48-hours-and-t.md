# Windsurf CEO: Betting On AI Agents, Pivoting In 48 Hours, And The Future of Coding — Compiled Truth

Windsurf co-founder Varun Mohan recounts pivoting from a GPU-virtualization company to Codeium to Windsurf, and argues that any tech insight is a "depreciating insight" that founders must keep re-earning by executing and being willing to be wrong.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Windsurf began as GPU-virtualization company Exafunction; seeing transformer models like GPT-3 commoditize custom deep-learning pipelines, the eight-person, revenue-generating team decided over a single weekend to pivot the whole company [windsurf-ceo-betting-on-ai-agents-pivoting-in-48-hours-and-t @ 02:15](https://youtu.be/LKgAx7FWva4?t=135)
- Startups need two opposing traits at once: irrational optimism to act despite uncertainty, and uncompromising realism to change course the moment facts on the ground contradict the thesis [windsurf-ceo-betting-on-ai-agents-pivoting-in-48-hours-and-t @ 07:09](https://youtu.be/LKgAx7FWva4?t=429)

- Windsurf was built as a VS Code fork and shipped across all operating systems in under three months, driven by the belief that agentic editing (not chat plus autocomplete) was where the technology needed to go [windsurf-ceo-betting-on-ai-agents-pivoting-in-48-hours-and-t @ 15:35](https://youtu.be/LKgAx7FWva4?t=935)
