# Does Your Startup Website Pass The First Impression Test? | Design Review — Compiled Truth

Former Dribbble CEO Zach Onisko and the hosts live-review YC founders' homepages against a strict few-second first-impression test, finding that clarity and focus beat busy design almost every time.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Visitors form a lasting impression of a website in just a few seconds, so if you can't capture attention in that window nothing else on the site matters [does-your-startup-website-pass-the-first-impression-test-des @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/pqwOL6YfRIo?t=0)
- Giving a page two equal-weight headlines or too many calls to action creates a paradox of choice where users end up clicking nothing [does-your-startup-website-pass-the-first-impression-test-des @ 02:36](https://youtu.be/pqwOL6YfRIo?t=156)
- Motion that draws the eye to one specific thing (like a rotating word showing the product) helps visitors instantly grasp what a site is about [does-your-startup-website-pass-the-first-impression-test-des @ 05:21](https://youtu.be/pqwOL6YfRIo?t=321)
- Too much simultaneous animation and jargon-heavy copy can distract from the core message so badly that reviewers couldn't tell what the product did [does-your-startup-website-pass-the-first-impression-test-des @ 06:41](https://youtu.be/pqwOL6YfRIo?t=401)
- Jargon that confuses outsiders may still be the right language if it instantly resonates with the actual target customer for that product [does-your-startup-website-pass-the-first-impression-test-des @ 10:06](https://youtu.be/pqwOL6YfRIo?t=606)
- A short headline plus a short sub-headline (as few as five and six words) lets visitors read and digest the message within five seconds [does-your-startup-website-pass-the-first-impression-test-des @ 12:59](https://youtu.be/pqwOL6YfRIo?t=779)
- The best animations guide the user to one specific takeaway or illustrate a concept that's hard to convey in static text or images [does-your-startup-website-pass-the-first-impression-test-des @ 12:01](https://youtu.be/pqwOL6YfRIo?t=721)
- Heavy, image-loaded pages load slowly, which Google penalizes in search rankings and which loses mobile visitors before they even see the content within their first few seconds [does-your-startup-website-pass-the-first-impression-test-des @ 19:44](https://youtu.be/pqwOL6YfRIo?t=1184)
