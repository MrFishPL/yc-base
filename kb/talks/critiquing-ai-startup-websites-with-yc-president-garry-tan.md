# Critiquing AI Startup Websites with YC President Garry Tan — Compiled Truth

In this Design Review episode, YC president Garry Tan critiques real AI startup homepages, showing that clarity about "what is this" and "is it for me" matters more than clever taglines or flashy animation.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Founders often try to be clever with taglines, but clever that equals confusing fails the first thing visitors need: understanding what the product is and whether it's for them [critiquing-ai-startup-websites-with-yc-president-garry-tan @ 01:38](https://youtu.be/uIYujpFmvo8?t=98)
- A homepage should have one clear, opinionated call to action rather than multiple equal-weight buttons that confuse users about where to click [critiquing-ai-startup-websites-with-yc-president-garry-tan @ 03:45](https://youtu.be/uIYujpFmvo8?t=225)
- Founders should identify their main KPI and the single outcome they want visitors to take, then move secondary products or features to other pages so everyone stays focused on that one action [critiquing-ai-startup-websites-with-yc-president-garry-tan @ 04:20](https://youtu.be/uIYujpFmvo8?t=260)
- For AI products, a video or demo showing a "feat of strength" — something users have never been able to do before — is what makes people obsessed with the technology [critiquing-ai-startup-websites-with-yc-president-garry-tan @ 04:59](https://youtu.be/uIYujpFmvo8?t=299)
- Generic workflow tools struggle because nobody wakes up wanting "workflow software"; focusing copy and demos on the most common specific use case pulls people into the product [critiquing-ai-startup-websites-with-yc-president-garry-tan @ 06:38](https://youtu.be/uIYujpFmvo8?t=398)
- Forcing account creation before letting someone try the product breaks trust; letting users complete one free action before prompting signup works better since they're already invested [critiquing-ai-startup-websites-with-yc-president-garry-tan @ 09:50](https://youtu.be/uIYujpFmvo8?t=590)
- Poor spacing and inconsistent line heights subtly undermine trust, especially for enterprise-facing products where visual polish signals quality [critiquing-ai-startup-websites-with-yc-president-garry-tan @ 11:01](https://youtu.be/uIYujpFmvo8?t=661)
- A homepage should be written for a specific ideal customer profile, not a nebulous "anyone who needs this"; the best tactic is to ask existing users how they'd describe the product and use their exact words [critiquing-ai-startup-websites-with-yc-president-garry-tan @ 14:05](https://youtu.be/uIYujpFmvo8?t=845)
- Concrete, specific claims (e.g., "30% cheaper and 25% faster") build more credibility than vague promises, especially when addressing objections users would raise about not building it themselves [critiquing-ai-startup-websites-with-yc-president-garry-tan @ 08:06](https://youtu.be/uIYujpFmvo8?t=486)
- Excessive bright colors and constant motion create sensory overload; animation works best when used sparingly to draw attention to one specific next step [critiquing-ai-startup-websites-with-yc-president-garry-tan @ 17:01](https://youtu.be/uIYujpFmvo8?t=1021)
