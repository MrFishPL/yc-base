# There's an Art to Getting Brilliant People to Surprise Themselves - Kevin Slavin of The Shed — Compiled Truth

Kevin Slavin (area/code, MIT Media Lab, The Shed) reflects on a career of collaborating with brilliant specialists, bootstrapping a company without fundraising, and building risk-taking cultures inside large institutions.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Slavin describes "an art to getting brilliant people to surprise themselves" as the core skill he's relied on across every venture, from games to biology to art [there-s-an-art-to-getting-brilliant-people-to-surprise-thems @ 07:30](https://youtu.be/xamF4CBk69Y?t=450)
- What he contributes to collaborations is usually the beginning of an idea, which then snowballs as each new specialist he recruits adds their own expertise on top [there-s-an-art-to-getting-brilliant-people-to-surprise-thems @ 09:47](https://youtu.be/xamF4CBk69Y?t=587)
- He says he's especially good at demonstrating the value of a project to whoever holds the money to pay for it — a skill many highly capable specialists lack because it requires detachment from their own work [there-s-an-art-to-getting-brilliant-people-to-surprise-thems @ 10:58](https://youtu.be/xamF4CBk69Y?t=658)
- His company area/code was bootstrapped by two co-founders using personal savings, with no outside fundraising [there-s-an-art-to-getting-brilliant-people-to-surprise-thems @ 12:07](https://youtu.be/xamF4CBk69Y?t=727)
- He argues fundraising forces founders to tell "a story of scale," since the premise of raising money is that it will produce growth [there-s-an-art-to-getting-brilliant-people-to-surprise-thems @ 13:09](https://youtu.be/xamF4CBk69Y?t=789)
- Treating growth as a priority from day one would have prevented area/code's riskiest early experiments, including a scrapped Pokemon pitch to Nintendo [there-s-an-art-to-getting-brilliant-people-to-surprise-thems @ 13:49](https://youtu.be/xamF4CBk69Y?t=829)
- The MIT Media Lab's primary hiring criterion for new faculty was that they be a "misfit" — someone representing a discipline that doesn't exist yet [there-s-an-art-to-getting-brilliant-people-to-surprise-thems @ 27:10](https://youtu.be/xamF4CBk69Y?t=1630)
- He believes it's rare for any institution today to take an authentic risk, and cites a deliberately "obviously bad idea" show that turned out extraordinary as a model The Shed wants to emulate [there-s-an-art-to-getting-brilliant-people-to-surprise-thems @ 43:00](https://youtu.be/xamF4CBk69Y?t=2580)
