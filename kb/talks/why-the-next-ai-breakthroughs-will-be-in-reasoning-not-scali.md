# Why The Next AI Breakthroughs Will Be In Reasoning, Not Scaling — Compiled Truth

The Lightcone hosts argue that o1-style reasoning models are an orthogonal, underappreciated unlock alongside raw model scaling, and that the resulting competitive edge for startups comes from proprietary evals and data rather than the base model itself.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A chip-design AI would eliminate one of AI's own bottlenecks to greater intelligence, and o1 already shows a path toward that kind of self-reinforcing capability [why-the-next-ai-breakthroughs-will-be-in-reasoning-not-scali @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/JiwiqYGw4iU?t=0)
- At a YC hackathon, the startup Diode used o1 to go beyond earlier GPT-4-based tooling and actually do system design and component selection for circuit boards, something GPT-4o could not do at all [why-the-next-ai-breakthroughs-will-be-in-reasoning-not-scali @ 09:13](https://youtu.be/JiwiqYGw4iU?t=553)
- A common emerging pattern is chaining different models for different steps of a workflow, e.g. using GPT-4o-mini to extract structured data from PDFs and o1 for the actual reasoning [why-the-next-ai-breakthroughs-will-be-in-reasoning-not-scali @ 08:30](https://youtu.be/JiwiqYGw4iU?t=510)
- o1's reasoning ability traces back to OpenAI's early reinforcement-learning work on Dota and ideas from AlphaGo/AlphaZero, later applied to reward-function-driven chain-of-thought training [why-the-next-ai-breakthroughs-will-be-in-reasoning-not-scali @ 14:36](https://youtu.be/JiwiqYGw4iU?t=876)
- Because most public/consumer data is already in the base model, a startup's real moat becomes proprietary data from unglamorous verticals (accounts receivable, legal, forensic accounting) used to build large eval sets [why-the-next-ai-breakthroughs-will-be-in-reasoning-not-scali @ 20:43](https://youtu.be/JiwiqYGw4iU?t=1243)
- Strongest technical teams should "go all the way" after customers who will pay for the final 10% of accuracy, rather than settling for an 80%-good demo [why-the-next-ai-breakthroughs-will-be-in-reasoning-not-scali @ 22:06](https://youtu.be/JiwiqYGw4iU?t=1326)
- Customer-support startup Gigl went from a roughly 70% error rate with GPT-4-plus-rules to about 5% error using o1 preview plus a rigorous eval-driven approach, taking one customer (Zepto) from 0% to 85% automation accuracy [why-the-next-ai-breakthroughs-will-be-in-reasoning-not-scali @ 29:13](https://youtu.be/JiwiqYGw4iU?t=1753)
- Classic moats still apply in the o1 era: prompts and evals alone aren't a product, so UI, integrations, and switching costs around a proprietary workflow are what create defensibility [why-the-next-ai-breakthroughs-will-be-in-reasoning-not-scali @ 23:02](https://youtu.be/JiwiqYGw4iU?t=1382)
- AI coding-agent startups that already built heavy chain-of-thought infrastructure may see less relative benefit from o1, since it doesn't represent as large a leap forward for that specific use case [why-the-next-ai-breakthroughs-will-be-in-reasoning-not-scali @ 32:07](https://youtu.be/JiwiqYGw4iU?t=1927)
- o1's math/physics strength is expected to unlock startups in mechanical, electrical, chemical, and bioengineering that make the physical world better, not just incremental productivity gains [why-the-next-ai-breakthroughs-will-be-in-reasoning-not-scali @ 33:48](https://youtu.be/JiwiqYGw4iU?t=2028)
