# Gustaf Alströmer - Growth for Startups — Compiled Truth

Drawing on his years leading Airbnb's growth team, Gustaf Alströmer argues that growth work is meaningless before you have product-market fit and real retention, and that once you have it, growth splits into conversion-rate optimization and growth-channel work backed by data-driven decision making.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Working on growth before achieving product-market fit and good retention is not a good idea — most startups never actually have PMF even though founders convince themselves otherwise [gustaf-alstr-mer-growth-for-startups @ 00:15](https://youtu.be/6lY9CYIY4pQ?t=15)
- Airbnb's founders did things that don't scale by flying to New York undercover as photographers to fix listing photos and sit with hosts to learn what was broken in the product [gustaf-alstr-mer-growth-for-startups @ 05:25](https://youtu.be/6lY9CYIY4pQ?t=325)
- The best way to measure product-market fit is unbiased retention data: pick the metric that represents your product's core value and the natural frequency users should repeat it at [gustaf-alstr-mer-growth-for-startups @ 09:01](https://youtu.be/6lY9CYIY4pQ?t=541)
- Plotting percent of users retained against weeks since first use shows PMF as a curve that flattens out rather than decaying to zero, as seen in examples like DoorDash and GitHub [gustaf-alstr-mer-growth-for-startups @ 13:30](https://youtu.be/6lY9CYIY4pQ?t=810)
- Net promoter score, surveys, registered users, visitors, and conversion rate are all weak proxies for product-market fit compared to actual repeat usage [gustaf-alstr-mer-growth-for-startups @ 14:31](https://youtu.be/6lY9CYIY4pQ?t=871)
- Growth splits into conversion-rate optimization (funnel work like onboarding, authentication, internationalization) and growth channels (external platforms like Google, Facebook, and referrals) [gustaf-alstr-mer-growth-for-startups @ 17:06](https://youtu.be/6lY9CYIY4pQ?t=1026)
- For paid growth, the number one rule is not to spend on ads unless you have revenue, and customer acquisition cost must be paid back on a short, defined timeline rather than an optimistic distant one [gustaf-alstr-mer-growth-for-startups @ 28:49](https://youtu.be/6lY9CYIY4pQ?t=1729)
- Most big companies scaled through just one or two dominant channels rather than spreading across many, such as TripAdvisor and Pinterest relying almost entirely on SEO [gustaf-alstr-mer-growth-for-startups @ 25:16](https://youtu.be/6lY9CYIY4pQ?t=1516)
- If you can enumerate your customers (even 100,000-500,000 of them), you should just build a list and sell to them directly instead of overcomplicating outbound growth [gustaf-alstr-mer-growth-for-startups @ 23:25](https://youtu.be/6lY9CYIY4pQ?t=1405)
- Airbnb's live-audience AB test demos on share-sheet design and signup walls showed founders' product instincts are often wrong, which is why experimentation and data should decide close calls rather than the loudest voice in the room [gustaf-alstr-mer-growth-for-startups @ 37:39](https://youtu.be/6lY9CYIY4pQ?t=2259)
