# How to Apply and Succeed at Y Combinator by Dalton Caldwell — Compiled Truth

Dalton Caldwell, YC's head of admissions, debunks common self-imposed reasons founders don't apply and explains what makes applications, videos, and interviews actually succeed.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Completing the application itself is valuable because it forces founders to answer questions they should already be discussing, like their equity split [how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-by-dalton-caldwell @ 00:42](https://youtu.be/8yiOcCPvyNE?t=42)
- There is no such thing as "too early" to apply — YC has funded people who pivoted mid-batch, and the right MRR to apply with is zero [how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-by-dalton-caldwell @ 04:34](https://youtu.be/8yiOcCPvyNE?t=274)
- Most self-imposed reasons not to apply (too experienced, too niche, must live in the Bay Area, solo founder, already raised money, has competitors) are barriers founders put on themselves, not real disqualifiers [how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-by-dalton-caldwell @ 06:39](https://youtu.be/8yiOcCPvyNE?t=399)
- A legitimate reason not to apply is having a business that isn't fast-growing or venture-scale by ambition — that's fine, just don't raise VC for it [how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-by-dalton-caldwell @ 09:17](https://youtu.be/8yiOcCPvyNE?t=557)
- Good applications are crisp and require zero effort to understand — clarity on what you do, who's building it, and what stage you're at beats jargon or hedging every time [how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-by-dalton-caldwell @ 14:48](https://youtu.be/8yiOcCPvyNE?t=888)
- The founder video should be about one minute long with the entire founding team present, following the stated directions exactly [how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-by-dalton-caldwell @ 12:07](https://youtu.be/8yiOcCPvyNE?t=727)
- Having at least one person on the team who can actually build the product (or clear evidence of ability to bring it to market) meaningfully raises odds of an interview [how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-by-dalton-caldwell @ 22:22](https://youtu.be/8yiOcCPvyNE?t=1342)
- Reapplying after rejection is common and viewed positively — about a third of a given batch had applied before, and showing concrete progress since the last application is a strong signal [how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-by-dalton-caldwell @ 25:49](https://youtu.be/8yiOcCPvyNE?t=1549)
- It's essentially impossible to network your way into YC; the vast majority of accepted founders are complete strangers to YC before applying [how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-by-dalton-caldwell @ 27:03](https://youtu.be/8yiOcCPvyNE?t=1623)
- YC interviews are 10-minute, non-adversarial conversations aimed at understanding the business plainly; overconfidence and memorized pitches read as a lack of self-awareness [how-to-apply-and-succeed-at-y-combinator-by-dalton-caldwell @ 36:33](https://youtu.be/8yiOcCPvyNE?t=2193)
