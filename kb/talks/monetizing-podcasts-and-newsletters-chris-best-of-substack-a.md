# Monetizing Podcasts and Newsletters - Chris Best of Substack and Jonathan Gill of Backtracks — Compiled Truth

Substack's Chris Best and Backtracks' Jonathan Gill discuss why paid subscriptions, not just advertising, are the future of monetizing creator content like newsletters and podcasts.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Substack's core hypothesis is that society massively underpays for culture, and more efficient payment mechanisms would make everyone richer [monetizing-podcasts-and-newsletters-chris-best-of-substack-a @ 06:13](https://youtu.be/b7N53KsEIlQ?t=373)
- Subscriptions are "magical" because a small monthly fee feels cheaper than a lump sum, yet people keep paying it and end up spending more over time [monetizing-podcasts-and-newsletters-chris-best-of-substack-a @ 08:22](https://youtu.be/b7N53KsEIlQ?t=502)
- For the first time, internet technology lets people effectively subscribe to an individual creator/brand rather than only to institutional publications [monetizing-podcasts-and-newsletters-chris-best-of-substack-a @ 12:06](https://youtu.be/b7N53KsEIlQ?t=726)
- Someone with 10,000 deeply devoted fans can never make a living from YouTube ad revenue, but the same audience can easily sustain them through subscriptions [monetizing-podcasts-and-newsletters-chris-best-of-substack-a @ 13:41](https://youtu.be/b7N53KsEIlQ?t=821)
- Podcast advertising suffers from weak measurement (download counts as a proxy, like early banner-ad impressions), unlike direct engagement metrics available for text and video [monetizing-podcasts-and-newsletters-chris-best-of-substack-a @ 03:08](https://youtu.be/b7N53KsEIlQ?t=188)
- The advice for new writers is to make the most accessible, focused content free so it spreads via sharing, and reserve rawer, more personal content for paying subscribers [monetizing-podcasts-and-newsletters-chris-best-of-substack-a @ 21:21](https://youtu.be/b7N53KsEIlQ?t=1281)
- Newsletter growth compounds through low-friction sharing (forwarding emails, web share buttons) plus referral programs that let super-fans gift subscriptions to friends [monetizing-podcasts-and-newsletters-chris-best-of-substack-a @ 20:11](https://youtu.be/b7N53KsEIlQ?t=1211)
- Substack itself grew its writer base early by doing in-person, emotionally resonant pitches to writers, and eventually turned that story into a podcast to scale the pitch [monetizing-podcasts-and-newsletters-chris-best-of-substack-a @ 41:01](https://youtu.be/b7N53KsEIlQ?t=2461)
- Educational content commands a premium because people will pay for things that make them "a better version of themselves," a dynamic borne out strongly in the Chinese paid-content market [monetizing-podcasts-and-newsletters-chris-best-of-substack-a @ 26:14](https://youtu.be/b7N53KsEIlQ?t=1574)
- The single best predictor of newsletter success is consistency: just start writing and publishing regularly, since a regularly-read newsletter can almost always be monetized later [monetizing-podcasts-and-newsletters-chris-best-of-substack-a @ 46:36](https://youtu.be/b7N53KsEIlQ?t=2796)
