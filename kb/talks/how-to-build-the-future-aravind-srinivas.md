# How To Build The Future: Aravind Srinivas — Compiled Truth

Perplexity co-founder Aravind Srinivas describes how the company pivoted from an enterprise database-search demo to a consumer search engine bet on the idea that ever-improving models would let a simple, general "dumb approach" beat hand-built retrieval systems, and how relentless product obsession became the company's edge against Google.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Srinivas and his co-founder targeted search specifically because it's one of only two domains (with self-driving) where product usage data creates a flywheel that keeps improving the underlying AI as the model itself improves [how-to-build-the-future-aravind-srinivas @ 02:36](https://youtu.be/SP7Ua8FKZN4?t=156)
- Early attempts built structured, templated SQL-generation search over specific datasets (Crunchbase, Twitter); the team later realized a simpler, more general unstructured approach that let the LLM do the work at inference time would scale better as models improved [how-to-build-the-future-aravind-srinivas @ 10:51](https://youtu.be/SP7Ua8FKZN4?t=651)
- Perplexity's "dumb approach" — feed top-k search snippets directly into the prompt with no scrolling or agentic browsing — only started working once instruction-following in models like 3.5-turbo got good enough; a similar approach from OpenAI's WebGPT team a year earlier failed because the models weren't ready [how-to-build-the-future-aravind-srinivas @ 12:45](https://youtu.be/SP7Ua8FKZN4?t=765)
- The signal that they were onto something was a compounding growth metric: adding follow-up questions doubled engagement time and queries per day started growing exponentially, at which point they decided not to pivot to enterprise [how-to-build-the-future-aravind-srinivas @ 15:48](https://youtu.be/SP7Ua8FKZN4?t=948)
- Srinivas didn't originally think of Perplexity as competing with Google because Google couldn't ship the same product on its own cluttered homepage without cannibalizing its ad business [how-to-build-the-future-aravind-srinivas @ 16:23](https://youtu.be/SP7Ua8FKZN4?t=983)
- The company's primary metric is queries per day, reviewed at every all-hands, with weekly and monthly growth rates tracked closely and any decline triggering investigation [how-to-build-the-future-aravind-srinivas @ 22:15](https://youtu.be/SP7Ua8FKZN4?t=1335)
- Srinivas applies Larry Page's "the user is never wrong" principle, designing so the product clarifies ambiguity rather than blaming the user for being a bad prompt engineer, which he contrasts with typical enterprise software design [how-to-build-the-future-aravind-srinivas @ 19:16](https://youtu.be/SP7Ua8FKZN4?t=1156)
- He hires for people who obsess over doing good work and product taste rather than screening explicitly for it, believing culture flows down from founder obsession with detail [how-to-build-the-future-aravind-srinivas @ 24:03](https://youtu.be/SP7Ua8FKZN4?t=1443)
- As the company scales, shipping speed is slowing due to production breakage and engineers lacking full codebase context, not primarily due to headcount growth itself [how-to-build-the-future-aravind-srinivas @ 25:30](https://youtu.be/SP7Ua8FKZN4?t=1530)
- Srinivas argues the long-term winner in AI search will be whoever builds the orchestration layer that routes between small models, knowledge graphs, widgets, and multi-step reasoning while also solving monetization at scale — comparing it to becoming "the next Google" [how-to-build-the-future-aravind-srinivas @ 29:46](https://youtu.be/SP7Ua8FKZN4?t=1786)
- He believes Perplexity is better positioned than OpenAI or Anthropic to build the full commerce/travel/shopping experience because the team has product and user obsession in its DNA rather than being purely focused on model research [how-to-build-the-future-aravind-srinivas @ 33:42](https://youtu.be/SP7Ua8FKZN4?t=2022)
