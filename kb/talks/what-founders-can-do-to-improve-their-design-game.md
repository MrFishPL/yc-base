# What Founders Can Do To Improve Their Design Game — Compiled Truth

Raphael Schaad, the designer-founder who built Cron (acquired by Notion), argues that design is not decoration but a core founder skill spanning desirability, viability, and feasibility, and shares concrete practices for founders to build design taste and process.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Building a successful product requires three overlapping things — desirable, viable, and feasible — which map to design, business, and technology [what-founders-can-do-to-improve-their-design-game @ 01:25](https://youtu.be/ksGNfd-wQY4?t=85)
- Software now lets designers control the means of production directly through code, unlike pre-digital eras when designers didn't build the final object themselves [what-founders-can-do-to-improve-their-design-game @ 02:44](https://youtu.be/ksGNfd-wQY4?t=164)
- In the AI age, design shifts from static nouns (buttons, fields) to verbs (autocomplete, summarize, send an agent), which requires prototyping with real, time-based interaction rather than static mockups [what-founders-can-do-to-improve-their-design-game @ 04:56](https://youtu.be/ksGNfd-wQY4?t=296)
- To build design taste: learn by doing even if early work is bad, surround yourself with well-designed objects and software, and read classic design books like Grid Systems, The Elements of Typographic Style, and The Design of Everyday Things [what-founders-can-do-to-improve-their-design-game @ 06:10](https://youtu.be/ksGNfd-wQY4?t=370)
- Taste can't be directly taught but can be acquired by refusing to tolerate bad design and constantly evaluating whether everyday interactions work or don't [what-founders-can-do-to-improve-their-design-game @ 09:03](https://youtu.be/ksGNfd-wQY4?t=543)
- Good design in early-stage startups is a "lost art" because founders prioritize shipping speed, but companies that invest in design now — especially as AI reshapes products — gain an outsized edge [what-founders-can-do-to-improve-their-design-game @ 12:16](https://youtu.be/ksGNfd-wQY4?t=736)
- Design isn't just how something looks or works — it's also how it's built, including technical choices like loading states and latency, which are felt by end users [what-founders-can-do-to-improve-their-design-game @ 13:18](https://youtu.be/ksGNfd-wQY4?t=798)
- One of the highest-leverage tactical moves for founders is hiring talented designers early, including by tapping networks like Y Combinator and Designer Fund [what-founders-can-do-to-improve-their-design-game @ 13:52](https://youtu.be/ksGNfd-wQY4?t=832)
- His design process starts with talking to users and sketching on paper, then moves quickly into high-fidelity prototypes — sometimes jumping straight into a code editor to feel whether the UI actually works [what-founders-can-do-to-improve-their-design-game @ 14:32](https://youtu.be/ksGNfd-wQY4?t=872)
- Paper sketching remains valuable even in the AI era because it's unconstrained and approachable for non-designers, and a sketch can now be photographed and fed directly into an AI design tool [what-founders-can-do-to-improve-their-design-game @ 18:51](https://youtu.be/ksGNfd-wQY4?t=1131)
