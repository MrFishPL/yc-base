# How To Get AI Startup Ideas — Compiled Truth

YC partners argue the best AI startup ideas come from going "all the way to the edge" of a domain, either by mining your own unique work history or by aggressively immersing yourself in outside industries, rather than chasing hackathon-style or trend-following ideas.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Most of the best startup ideas are somewhat hard to actually ship a first version of, unlike easy "hackathon" ideas that founders naturally gravitate toward [how-to-get-ai-startup-ideas @ 02:49](https://youtu.be/TANaRNMbYgk?t=169).
- Founders should either aggressively introspect on unique prior experience or aggressively "get out of the house" into outside industries to find ideas [how-to-get-ai-startup-ideas @ 03:14](https://youtu.be/TANaRNMbYgk?t=194).
- Salient's founder built an AI voice agent for auto-loan collections after noticing the manual payment-recovery process while working in Tesla's finance ops team [how-to-get-ai-startup-ideas @ 04:45](https://youtu.be/TANaRNMbYgk?t=285).
- Diode Computer's founders, who were both electrical and software engineers, saw a gap where hardware engineering lacked git-like tooling and automated verification that software engineers take for granted [how-to-get-ai-startup-ideas @ 05:35](https://youtu.be/TANaRNMbYgk?t=335).
- A huge percentage of YC's billion-dollar companies trace back to a founder's internship, so students should pursue internships at companies on the bleeding edge [how-to-get-ai-startup-ideas @ 12:08](https://youtu.be/TANaRNMbYgk?t=728).
- When a founder pivots and has lost their idea, the first question is whether they are an expert in anything, since expertise often points to an underexploited startup idea [how-to-get-ai-startup-ideas @ 10:43](https://youtu.be/TANaRNMbYgk?t=643).
- Founders without connections or expertise can go "undercover" by literally taking a remote job (e.g., medical biller) to learn an industry from the inside before automating it [how-to-get-ai-startup-ideas @ 23:43](https://youtu.be/TANaRNMbYgk?t=1423).
- Any category of job currently outsourced to a low-wage country is a strong signal that there's a startup to build with AI right now [how-to-get-ai-startup-ideas @ 29:46](https://youtu.be/TANaRNMbYgk?t=1786).
- Founders often shy away from good ideas because a space seems too crowded, but technical strength can let a team deliver what look-alike competitors only pitch [how-to-get-ai-startup-ideas @ 40:49](https://youtu.be/TANaRNMbYgk?t=2449).
- It's normal for founders to take about a year to find the right idea, which contradicts old seed-fund thinking that startups must be great immediately [how-to-get-ai-startup-ideas @ 41:23](https://youtu.be/TANaRNMbYgk?t=2483).
