# Advice for Students and Recent Graduates on Finding Jobs – Liz Wessel of WayUp — Compiled Truth

WayUp co-founder Liz Wessel traces her path from a college side-hustle to deliberately spending two years at Google before leaving to build WayUp with cofounder JJ, and shares tactical advice on cold-emailing, interviewing, hiring, and managing a growing team.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Wessel's first venture — a paid student loyalty-discount card at Penn — is what got her "addicted" to "creating something from nothing and solving a problem," before she'd built any real software [advice-for-students-and-recent-graduates-on-finding-jobs-liz @ 01:31](https://youtu.be/c-beGCkpcMs?t=91)
- The early WayUp side-project (a site to help students find campus-ambassador jobs) grew to tens of thousands of users with barely any marketing, which she took as the "make something people want" signal to quit and found the company for real [advice-for-students-and-recent-graduates-on-finding-jobs-liz @ 03:20](https://youtu.be/c-beGCkpcMs?t=200)
- She deliberately chose a two-year stint at Google over a VC job specifically to learn management, legal, marketing, and sales before starting a company, telling HR upfront that she planned to leave in two years [advice-for-students-and-recent-graduates-on-finding-jobs-liz @ 04:36](https://youtu.be/c-beGCkpcMs?t=276)
- To decide between job offers, she cold-emailed Sequoia's Roelof Botha, framing the email around a specific reason for asking him (not a generic "pick your brain") [advice-for-students-and-recent-graduates-on-finding-jobs-liz @ 06:45](https://youtu.be/c-beGCkpcMs?t=405)
- Her interview framework, "REAF," is Research the company, show Enthusiasm, Ask thoughtful questions, and Follow up with a specific thank-you note within 24 hours [advice-for-students-and-recent-graduates-on-finding-jobs-liz @ 17:22](https://youtu.be/c-beGCkpcMs?t=1042)
- She found cofounder JJ by combing through a hackathon's signup database for the most technically impressive candidate and cold-emailing him directly [advice-for-students-and-recent-graduates-on-finding-jobs-liz @ 25:51](https://youtu.be/c-beGCkpcMs?t=1551)
- The single biggest thing YC didn't prepare her for was how to hire and manage a growing team — ironic, she notes, given WayUp is a recruiting business [advice-for-students-and-recent-graduates-on-finding-jobs-liz @ 29:42](https://youtu.be/c-beGCkpcMs?t=1782)
- When recruiting a senior hire, she found the biggest selling point is convincing them she'll be a good manager to work for, since "people quit their manager before they quit their company" [advice-for-students-and-recent-graduates-on-finding-jobs-liz @ 32:11](https://youtu.be/c-beGCkpcMs?t=1931)
- Rather than forcing one management style on everyone, she keeps directly-reporting employees who fit her type-A style and moves others who don't gel with her to report to someone else [advice-for-students-and-recent-graduates-on-finding-jobs-liz @ 33:34](https://youtu.be/c-beGCkpcMs?t=2014)
- People join startups for the team and mission rather than compensation, so early hiring pitches should emphasize the company's mission and the quality of the team [advice-for-students-and-recent-graduates-on-finding-jobs-liz @ 42:57](https://youtu.be/c-beGCkpcMs?t=2577)
