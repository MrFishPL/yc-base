# Should You Start A Startup? | Startup School — Compiled Truth

A YC group partner argues that resilience, not credentials or confidence, is the trait that predicts startup success, and lays out practical steps for deciding whether and when to take the leap.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- School and work success are weaker predictors of startup success than expected; resilience to push through early rejection matters most [should-you-start-a-startup-startup-school @ 02:07](https://youtu.be/BUE-icVYRFU?t=127)
- Confidence during a funding interview is not a reliable proxy for resilience — quiet, soft-spoken founders (e.g. Benchling's Saji) turned out to be some of the most resilient [should-you-start-a-startup-startup-school @ 02:45](https://youtu.be/BUE-icVYRFU?t=165)
- Benchling struggled for two years post-YC to get any enterprise sales revenue before becoming a company on track to go public [should-you-start-a-startup-startup-school @ 04:24](https://youtu.be/BUE-icVYRFU?t=264)
- Initial motivations for starting a company (money, curiosity) don't matter much — what matters is that motivation can deepen into genuine interest in the problem and the people over time [should-you-start-a-startup-startup-school @ 05:40](https://youtu.be/BUE-icVYRFU?t=340)
- Before starting, ask "what do I have to lose" and honestly assess whether you can survive a year with no or little salary if the startup fails [should-you-start-a-startup-startup-school @ 07:11](https://youtu.be/BUE-icVYRFU?t=431)
- Even a failed startup builds valuable, transferable skills, and many top employers and YC companies explicitly seek out former founders to hire [should-you-start-a-startup-startup-school @ 08:25](https://youtu.be/BUE-icVYRFU?t=505)
- Rippling, a $10B YC company, deliberately hires former founders and puts roughly 50 of them in charge of product divisions as general managers [should-you-start-a-startup-startup-school @ 09:40](https://youtu.be/BUE-icVYRFU?t=580)
- Nick Grandy's first YC startup failed, but he then became Airbnb's first employee and later founded Outschool, now valued over $3 billion, showing career paths aren't linear [should-you-start-a-startup-startup-school @ 10:08](https://youtu.be/BUE-icVYRFU?t=608)
- To prepare for founding later, seek out smart people you enjoy debating ideas with, and working at a startup is a good environment to meet future co-founders [should-you-start-a-startup-startup-school @ 12:48](https://youtu.be/BUE-icVYRFU?t=768)
