# Ruchi Sanghvi Speaks at Female Founders Conference 2015 — Compiled Truth

Drawing on her time at Facebook, her own startup Cove, and Dropbox, Ruchi Sanghvi argues that approaching product, hiring, and culture from first principles — rather than pattern-matching to what already succeeded elsewhere — is what most improves a startup's odds.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Thinking from first principles rather than pattern-matching to existing successful products, hiring practices, or cultures materially raises the odds of success, even though it's uncomfortable [ruchi-sanghvi-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 01:27](https://youtu.be/01FjJyBAOUE?t=87).
- Facebook's Newsfeed launched despite internal fears it would hurt page-level ad monetization, and was met the next morning with organized user outrage and hate groups [ruchi-sanghvi-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 02:58](https://youtu.be/01FjJyBAOUE?t=178).
- Even though users said they hated Newsfeed, engagement and page views doubled, proving the team was right to stick with a product decision grounded in actual usage rather than complaints [ruchi-sanghvi-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 06:11](https://youtu.be/01FjJyBAOUE?t=371).
- At her startup Cove, the team held itself to unrealistic growth-rate expectations borrowed from social and gaming companies, failing to recognize their product would grow slower over a longer horizon [ruchi-sanghvi-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 11:30](https://youtu.be/01FjJyBAOUE?t=690).
- Cove suffered from "second company syndrome," overcorrecting for Facebook's fragile early infrastructure by over-investing time in scalable infrastructure before proving the product worked [ruchi-sanghvi-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 12:27](https://youtu.be/01FjJyBAOUE?t=747).
- Startups have a binary win-or-lose outcome with low odds of success, so founders should look for every possible advantage rather than defaulting to conventional startup wisdom [ruchi-sanghvi-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 13:23](https://youtu.be/01FjJyBAOUE?t=803).
- "Move fast and break things" worked at Facebook because the only goal was connecting more people, but the same value would have been disastrous at Dropbox, where reliability mattered because users' irreplaceable files (wedding photos, thesis work) were at stake [ruchi-sanghvi-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 16:32](https://youtu.be/01FjJyBAOUE?t=992).
- Joining Dropbox to help it scale, she chose to focus first on recruiting, setting and hitting an audacious goal of growing the company from 90 to 270 people in under seven months [ruchi-sanghvi-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 17:49](https://youtu.be/01FjJyBAOUE?t=1069).
