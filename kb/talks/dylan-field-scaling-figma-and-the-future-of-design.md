# Dylan Field: Scaling Figma and the Future of Design — Compiled Truth

Figma co-founder and CEO Dylan Field recounts Figma's winding path from a WebGL exploration to a design tool, and argues that in the AI era design and craft become the key differentiator, with designers needing to step into founder and leadership roles.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Figma took years of exploration (including a meme generator) before Dylan Field and Evan Wallace committed to building what became Figma, starting in earnest August 2012 but not going all-in until mid-2013 [dylan-field-scaling-figma-and-the-future-of-design @ 02:44](https://youtu.be/-7Qz7tSTfUU?t=164)
- Early users came almost entirely from cold-emailing designers Field admired and asking for feedback, which he says worked far more often than expected [dylan-field-scaling-figma-and-the-future-of-design @ 06:08](https://youtu.be/-7Qz7tSTfUU?t=368)
- Field says in hindsight founders should launch and charge money much faster than Figma did — it took about 5 years and a nudge from Microsoft before Figma started charging [dylan-field-scaling-figma-and-the-future-of-design @ 08:34](https://youtu.be/-7Qz7tSTfUU?t=514)
- "Product market pull" — customers obsessively engaging and demanding more — is a stronger and earlier signal to double down on than waiting to build every requested feature [dylan-field-scaling-figma-and-the-future-of-design @ 11:32](https://youtu.be/-7Qz7tSTfUU?t=692)
- Figma's product strategy is to notice organic behaviors happening inside Figma Design (like slides or brainstorming) and spin them into dedicated standalone products (FigJam, Slides, Draw, Buzz) [dylan-field-scaling-figma-and-the-future-of-design @ 16:48](https://youtu.be/-7Qz7tSTfUU?t=1008)
- In the AI era, since building software gets easier, design, craft, and point of view become the key differentiator for companies like Airbnb [dylan-field-scaling-figma-and-the-future-of-design @ 14:21](https://youtu.be/-7Qz7tSTfUU?t=861)
- Current AI models are better suited to early-stage/prototyping ("zero to one") work than to late-stage, established codebases ("one to a hundred") [dylan-field-scaling-figma-and-the-future-of-design @ 20:09](https://youtu.be/-7Qz7tSTfUU?t=1209)
- Field expects designer-founders and designers leading large orgs to multiply, with designers becoming the in-house experts on problem-solving and craft much like a company's best writer today [dylan-field-scaling-figma-and-the-future-of-design @ 26:01](https://youtu.be/-7Qz7tSTfUU?t=1561)
- Embedding designers directly into AI research teams, including having them contribute to evals, has been critical because researchers often lack designers' intuition for end users [dylan-field-scaling-figma-and-the-future-of-design @ 23:43](https://youtu.be/-7Qz7tSTfUU?t=1423)
- Figma started narrowly focused on product design for digital products rather than a broad design audience, deliberately slimming ambition to make the initial pitch clearer [dylan-field-scaling-figma-and-the-future-of-design @ 36:54](https://youtu.be/-7Qz7tSTfUU?t=2214)
