# Drew Houston : How to Build the Future — Compiled Truth

Dropbox co-founder Drew Houston tells Sam Altman how Dropbox's idea was born from personal frustration, how he found a co-founder days before a YC deadline, and how he learned to scale from writing code to running a large company.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The idea for Dropbox came from personal frustration: Drew kept forgetting his thumb drive, crystallized on a Chinatown bus ride from Boston to New York where he couldn't get anything done [drew-houston-how-to-build-the-future @ 00:16](https://youtu.be/U_g-fBfPOF8?t=16).
- He applied to YC as a solo founder and got an email from Paul Graham saying the idea was interesting but he needed to find a co-founder — with the application deadline only three weeks away [drew-houston-how-to-build-the-future @ 02:16](https://youtu.be/U_g-fBfPOF8?t=136).
- Arash was introduced through Kyle Vogt and they met at the MIT student center, hit it off, and started working together and discussing equity almost immediately, having known each other only two or three hours [drew-houston-how-to-build-the-future @ 03:43](https://youtu.be/U_g-fBfPOF8?t=223).
- To validate demand and strengthen the YC application, Drew made a three-minute demo video that hit the top of Hacker News for two days before the product even existed [drew-houston-how-to-build-the-future @ 04:45](https://youtu.be/U_g-fBfPOF8?t=285).
- Investors pushed back hard that Google could easily build the same thing and it was "commodity online storage," and Drew agreed those risks were real but didn't think they made it a bad idea — he'd ask investors if they personally used any of the 50 existing alternatives and they'd say no [drew-houston-how-to-build-the-future @ 08:08](https://youtu.be/U_g-fBfPOF8?t=488).
- The early hard problem after building a working prototype was simply keeping up with growth — every day required doing everything from before plus something new, forcing hiring, fundraising, and legal/accounting help in a widening circle [drew-houston-how-to-build-the-future @ 10:06](https://youtu.be/U_g-fBfPOF8?t=606).
- A CEO's job changes every six months to a year, and skills that made you good at an earlier stage (like being the main coder) can become a hindrance later, so Drew had to stop coding as the company scaled [drew-houston-how-to-build-the-future @ 12:18](https://youtu.be/U_g-fBfPOF8?t=738).
- Dropbox expanded into photos (Carousel) and email (Mailbox) but later cut both in 2014 after Drew re-read Andy Grove's "Only the Paranoid Survive" and recognized the company was at a strategic inflection point requiring focus on one basket [drew-houston-how-to-build-the-future @ 23:24](https://youtu.be/U_g-fBfPOF8?t=1404).
- Growth from seven users to seven million with under 50 people relied on a referral program giving both sides free space, which drove about 30% of signups, with shared folders' inherent virality driving another 20% [drew-houston-how-to-build-the-future @ 28:09](https://youtu.be/U_g-fBfPOF8?t=1689).
