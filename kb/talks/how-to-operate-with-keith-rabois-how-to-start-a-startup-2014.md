# How to Operate with Keith Rabois (How to Start a Startup 2014: Lecture 14) — Compiled Truth

Rabois frames the founder's job as being an "editor" of the company: simplify, ask clarifying questions, allocate resources, delegate deliberately, and obsess over details, all in service of building a high-performance machine that eventually runs without heroics.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The founder's real job, per Andy Grove, is to maximize the output of the organization, not manage inputs or motion [how-to-operate-with-keith-rabois-how-to-start-a-startup-2014 @ 01:35](https://youtu.be/w19IBxDu2Y4?t=95)
- Think of yourself as an editor: your core job is to simplify, simplify, simplify so people can repeat a plan without thinking about it [how-to-operate-with-keith-rabois-how-to-start-a-startup-2014 @ 03:17](https://youtu.be/w19IBxDu2Y4?t=197)
- Every eliminated step in a process can improve performance by roughly 30 to 50%, per Andy Grove's estimate [how-to-operate-with-keith-rabois-how-to-start-a-startup-2014 @ 06:14](https://youtu.be/w19IBxDu2Y4?t=374)
- Delegating without abdicating is guided by "task-relevant maturity": give experienced people more rope and new tasks more oversight [how-to-operate-with-keith-rabois-how-to-start-a-startup-2014 @ 09:44](https://youtu.be/w19IBxDu2Y4?t=584)
- Use a conviction-vs-consequence 2x2 to decide when to delegate fully (low conviction, low consequence) versus override (high conviction, high consequence) [how-to-operate-with-keith-rabois-how-to-start-a-startup-2014 @ 11:11](https://youtu.be/w19IBxDu2Y4?t=671)
- Companies need "barrels" (people who can take an idea from conception to shipping) far more than "ammunition," and adding barrels is what actually increases velocity [how-to-operate-with-keith-rabois-how-to-start-a-startup-2014 @ 14:19](https://youtu.be/w19IBxDu2Y4?t=859)
- Identify barrels by giving people small responsibilities and continually expanding scope until it breaks, and by watching who colleagues informally go to for help [how-to-operate-with-keith-rabois-how-to-start-a-startup-2014 @ 15:59](https://youtu.be/w19IBxDu2Y4?t=959)
- Borrowing from Peter Thiel at PayPal, assigning each person exactly one focus area forces them toward high-impact A+ problems instead of easier, procrastination-friendly B+ problems [how-to-operate-with-keith-rabois-how-to-start-a-startup-2014 @ 19:20](https://youtu.be/w19IBxDu2Y4?t=1160)
- A founder-drafted dashboard, reviewed by nearly the whole company daily, is the key tool for scaling decision-making without the founder personally making every call [how-to-operate-with-keith-rabois-how-to-start-a-startup-2014 @ 21:16](https://youtu.be/w19IBxDu2Y4?t=1276)
- Watch for anomalous data rather than expected behavior — PayPal's power-seller "please pay with PayPal" listings revealed its actual best market [how-to-operate-with-keith-rabois-how-to-start-a-startup-2014 @ 26:11](https://youtu.be/w19IBxDu2Y4?t=1571)
- Getting small details right (like a receptionist answering the phone properly) compounds into overall organizational excellence, per Bill Walsh's "the score takes care of itself" [how-to-operate-with-keith-rabois-how-to-start-a-startup-2014 @ 28:35](https://youtu.be/w19IBxDu2Y4?t=1715)
