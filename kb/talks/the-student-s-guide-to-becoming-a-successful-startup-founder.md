# The Student's Guide To Becoming A Successful Startup Founder — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell give high schoolers and young people advice for building the skills and character that make a successful startup founder, arguing the startup game rewards patient, long-term investment over rushing in.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Young people should "grind" and upskill now (deferring some satisfaction) rather than rush to start a company immediately, since leveling up early pays off later [the-student-s-guide-to-becoming-a-successful-startup-founder @ 01:35](https://youtu.be/O5KCB2p6SB8?t=95)
- Learning to code is one of the most valuable skills even for aspiring non-technical founders, since coders tend to be the most powerful, highest-compensated, and hardest-to-replace people inside software companies [the-student-s-guide-to-becoming-a-successful-startup-founder @ 03:21](https://youtu.be/O5KCB2p6SB8?t=201)
- Shipping low-stakes side projects (like teenage Mark Zuckerberg's Winamp music-recommendation plugin) builds the reps of going through the full founder motions of ideating, building, and releasing something to the world [the-student-s-guide-to-becoming-a-successful-startup-founder @ 05:30](https://youtu.be/O5KCB2p6SB8?t=330)
- Social skills like talking to people and making friends are entirely learnable, and high school is a low-stakes sandbox to practice being uncomfortable without long-term reputational cost [the-student-s-guide-to-becoming-a-successful-startup-founder @ 06:48](https://youtu.be/O5KCB2p6SB8?t=408)
- Practicing genuine care for others (as in tutoring) early builds the empathy a founder needs to actually care about customers and users [the-student-s-guide-to-becoming-a-successful-startup-founder @ 09:09](https://youtu.be/O5KCB2p6SB8?t=549)
- On credentials, the best approach is neither blindly embracing the system nor rejecting it outright, but understanding it as a tool you can use toward your goals [the-student-s-guide-to-becoming-a-successful-startup-founder @ 13:10](https://youtu.be/O5KCB2p6SB8?t=790)
- Distinguishing real, value-creating trends from passing fads is a skill worth training, since the fad often obscures what's actually going on underneath [the-student-s-guide-to-becoming-a-successful-startup-founder @ 14:25](https://youtu.be/O5KCB2p6SB8?t=865)
- Young people should stay optimistic about technology's ability to improve lives even as news coverage swings toward exclusively negative framing [the-student-s-guide-to-becoming-a-successful-startup-founder @ 17:04](https://youtu.be/O5KCB2p6SB8?t=1024)
- Startups run on a high-trust environment, so honesty with yourself and with stakeholders (customers, investors, co-founders, employees) is foundational to making things work [the-student-s-guide-to-becoming-a-successful-startup-founder @ 18:39](https://youtu.be/O5KCB2p6SB8?t=1119)
- Startups are long games — the average one takes seven to ten years to exit or IPO — so true ambition requires patience over many years rather than expecting overnight success [the-student-s-guide-to-becoming-a-successful-startup-founder @ 20:46](https://youtu.be/O5KCB2p6SB8?t=1246)
