# David Rusenko - How To Find Product Market Fit — Compiled Truth

Weebly's founder walks through the practical, non-theoretical process of finding product-market fit, using Weebly's own 18-months-to-traction journey and concrete metrics, prioritization, and scaling rules.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- It took Weebly 18 months of declining or flat signup graphs after multiple press spikes (TechCrunch, Newsweek, Time) before traction finally turned around [david-rusenko-how-to-find-product-market-fit @ 08:24](https://youtu.be/0LNQxT9LvM0?t=504)
- There are only two truly hard things at a startup: finding product-market fit and hiring/building a world-class team; making money is a distant third [david-rusenko-how-to-find-product-market-fit @ 11:00](https://youtu.be/0LNQxT9LvM0?t=660)
- The best companies create a new market rather than compete in an existing one, so traditional market research won't help since it only studies existing markets [david-rusenko-how-to-find-product-market-fit @ 12:23](https://youtu.be/0LNQxT9LvM0?t=743)
- Watch where customers "hack" your product to do something it wasn't built for — that's a signal of a hidden need worth doubling down on [david-rusenko-how-to-find-product-market-fit @ 14:42](https://youtu.be/0LNQxT9LvM0?t=882)
- Building a fully functional product just to test a hypothesis is expensive; get to a functional prototype fast and expect roughly 10x more iterations than you think you'll need [david-rusenko-how-to-find-product-market-fit @ 19:15](https://youtu.be/0LNQxT9LvM0?t=1155)
- Run UX testing sessions (3-5 is enough): let a user attempt a task in front of you and say nothing, even when it's painful to watch them struggle [david-rusenko-how-to-find-product-market-fit @ 23:12](https://youtu.be/0LNQxT9LvM0?t=1392)
- Track three key metrics for product-market fit: returning usage (day/3-day/7-day/30-day), NPS (above 50 suggests fit), and paying-customer renewal rates rather than churn [david-rusenko-how-to-find-product-market-fit @ 29:16](https://youtu.be/0LNQxT9LvM0?t=1756)
- Don't scale the team past about 20-25 people until you have product-market fit, since that's the point where flat organizations break and management layers become necessary [david-rusenko-how-to-find-product-market-fit @ 35:28](https://youtu.be/0LNQxT9LvM0?t=2128)
- Once you have product-market fit, scale aggressively but never more than double headcount in a year, since faster hypergrowth builds a foundation that eventually collapses [david-rusenko-how-to-find-product-market-fit @ 38:00](https://youtu.be/0LNQxT9LvM0?t=2280)
- Focus on customers over competitors: cloning a competitor's feature means you're always a step behind, since whatever they just launched they started three months ago [david-rusenko-how-to-find-product-market-fit @ 55:48](https://youtu.be/0LNQxT9LvM0?t=3348)
