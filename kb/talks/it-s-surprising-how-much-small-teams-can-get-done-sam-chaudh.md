# It's Surprising How Much Small Teams Can Get Done - Sam Chaudhary of ClassDojo — Compiled Truth

ClassDojo co-founder Sam Chaudhary describes how deep early user research, an emergent community strategy, and deliberately small empowered teams let ClassDojo scale to hundreds of thousands of schools without bloating headcount.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Before writing any product, ClassDojo's founders spent six to seven weeks doing hundreds of in-person and video calls with teachers, asking simply "what's the worst problem you face every day?" [it-s-surprising-how-much-small-teams-can-get-done-sam-chaudh @ 13:08](https://youtu.be/wvMdXPt-Pq0?t=788).
- Early growth was entirely organic and product-driven: one teacher would adopt ClassDojo and it would spread teacher-to-teacher within a school with zero paid acquisition or viral mechanics engineered in [it-s-surprising-how-much-small-teams-can-get-done-sam-chaudh @ 23:28](https://youtu.be/wvMdXPt-Pq0?t=1408).
- A former teacher who joined as an intern (Jenna) discovered that growth was driven by "mentor" teachers who evangelized ClassDojo within their school, leading the company to intentionally build a supportive community of these mentors rather than push top-down adoption [it-s-surprising-how-much-small-teams-can-get-done-sam-chaudh @ 27:04](https://youtu.be/wvMdXPt-Pq0?t=1624).
- ClassDojo deliberately stayed at around 30 people despite $30M+ raised and reach into 90% of US K-8 schools, structuring the org into small 3-10 person empowered teams with full access to company information (financials, board decks) so they can make globally-optimal decisions [it-s-surprising-how-much-small-teams-can-get-done-sam-chaudh @ 30:21](https://youtu.be/wvMdXPt-Pq0?t=1821).
- A CEO's role changes as the company grows — Chaudhary cites the idea that past roughly 25 people a founder's job shifts from building the product to building the culture and stays there [it-s-surprising-how-much-small-teams-can-get-done-sam-chaudh @ 33:44](https://youtu.be/wvMdXPt-Pq0?t=2024).
- ClassDojo runs an internally-public, anonymous-allowed running log of company issues, reviewed weekly with a guarantee that every submitted issue gets acted on or a public explanation given within a week, modeled on Toyota/GM's NUMMI suggestion-box practice [it-s-surprising-how-much-small-teams-can-get-done-sam-chaudh @ 41:58](https://youtu.be/wvMdXPt-Pq0?t=2518).
- After facing public scrutiny over student data and privacy several years in, ClassDojo learned that clear communication (rewriting policies in plain sixth-grade language) and aligning incentives (never selling data to advertisers) were essential once a startup reaches meaningful scale [it-s-surprising-how-much-small-teams-can-get-done-sam-chaudh @ 49:09](https://youtu.be/wvMdXPt-Pq0?t=2949).
- Chaudhary distinguishes technical systems (like a MacBook or Tesla, best designed centrally then sold as-is) from human systems (like classrooms), arguing education products must be co-designed with users rather than imposed on them [it-s-surprising-how-much-small-teams-can-get-done-sam-chaudh @ 57:06](https://youtu.be/wvMdXPt-Pq0?t=3426).
- ClassDojo's monetization strategy centers on selling directly and affordably to parents (roughly $10/month) rather than slow school/district sales cycles, reasoning that parents are underserved and will pay for value that improves their kids' education [it-s-surprising-how-much-small-teams-can-get-done-sam-chaudh @ 55:47](https://youtu.be/wvMdXPt-Pq0?t=3347).
