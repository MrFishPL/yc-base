# The FDE Playbook for AI Startups with Bob McGrew — Compiled Truth

Former Palantir executive and OpenAI chief research officer Bob McGrew explains the forward-deployed-engineer (FDE) model Palantir invented, why over 100 YC AI-agent startups are now hiring for FDE roles, and how to run the model without sliding into pure consulting.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A forward deployed engineer is a technical person embedded at the customer site who fills the gap between what the product does and what the customer actually needs [the-fde-playbook-for-ai-startups-with-bob-mcgrew @ 02:15](https://youtu.be/Zyw-YA0k3xo?t=135)
- Palantir's FDE strategy emerged because, unlike businesses that reach product-market fit and then scale by treating all customers the same, Palantir's customer needs were slightly different at every site, so it built a customizable platform instead of one fixed product [the-fde-playbook-for-ai-startups-with-bob-mcgrew @ 04:50](https://youtu.be/Zyw-YA0k3xo?t=290)
- FDEs act as product discovery from inside the customer, building a "gravel road" solution that the core product team then generalizes into a "paved highway" for the next 5-10 customers [the-fde-playbook-for-ai-startups-with-bob-mcgrew @ 06:34](https://youtu.be/Zyw-YA0k3xo?t=394)
- The team split into an "echo" team of embedded domain-expert analysts/account managers who identify the key problem to solve, and a "delta" team of engineers who prototype and ship a working solution fast, embodying founder-like skills [the-fde-playbook-for-ai-startups-with-bob-mcgrew @ 09:51](https://youtu.be/Zyw-YA0k3xo?t=591)
- The FDE model only avoids becoming disguised consulting if cost-per-value keeps dropping as the product improves and margins turn from negative to positive over time at a given customer [the-fde-playbook-for-ai-startups-with-bob-mcgrew @ 15:01](https://youtu.be/Zyw-YA0k3xo?t=901)
- AI agent startups are adopting the FDE model now because there is no incumbent product to replace, so there is enormous product discovery work that can only happen from inside the enterprise [the-fde-playbook-for-ai-startups-with-bob-mcgrew @ 27:37](https://youtu.be/Zyw-YA0k3xo?t=1657)
- The FDE model flips the classic "do things that don't scale" advice: instead of driving cost per customer down while holding contract size fixed, you drive contract size up while holding customization work per customer roughly constant — "doing things that don't scale at scale" [the-fde-playbook-for-ai-startups-with-bob-mcgrew @ 36:24](https://youtu.be/Zyw-YA0k3xo?t=2184)
- Pricing under the FDE model is fundamentally different from SaaS subscriptions or seats: you are selling a delivered outcome, so contracts start smaller and flexible and grow as trust and value increase, as seen with YC companies Castle and Happy Robot ramping bank and logistics contracts based on delivered outcomes [the-fde-playbook-for-ai-startups-with-bob-mcgrew @ 30:52](https://youtu.be/Zyw-YA0k3xo?t=1852)
- Winning executive buy-in matters because you must be solving one of the customer CEO's top-five priorities to get the authority needed to bypass IT and organizational rules that would otherwise block a startup's implementation [the-fde-playbook-for-ai-startups-with-bob-mcgrew @ 33:04](https://youtu.be/Zyw-YA0k3xo?t=1984)
- Demo-driven development works well for FDE-style products because building a demo forces the team to think from the customer's perspective and surface integration pain points between features long before a real deployment would reveal them [the-fde-playbook-for-ai-startups-with-bob-mcgrew @ 42:08](https://youtu.be/Zyw-YA0k3xo?t=2528)
