# Startup Experts Reveal Their Top Productivity Advice — Compiled Truth

YC group partners argue that startup "productivity" is really about ruthless prioritization and saying no, not tools or hacks, and share concrete tactics like stack-ranking priorities and separating maker vs. manager schedules.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Founders often chase "shiny objects" like a fun new AI chatbot instead of doing the hard, tiring work of talking to customers, which is a trap that pulls focus from what actually moves the business [startup-experts-reveal-their-top-productivity-advice @ 01:22](https://youtu.be/nF_YWdz6S0Y?t=82)
- A useful discipline is writing down your explicit priorities and main bottleneck KPI (e.g. "is revenue growing, why or why not"), then auditing your calendar to confirm your time actually matches those priorities [startup-experts-reveal-their-top-productivity-advice @ 02:32](https://youtu.be/nF_YWdz6S0Y?t=152)
- Building elaborate Notion documentation systems before product-market fit is compared to "cleaning your room because you don't want to do your homework" — a warning sign of avoidance [startup-experts-reveal-their-top-productivity-advice @ 03:28](https://youtu.be/nF_YWdz6S0Y?t=208)
- "Productivity porn" — reading extreme lifestyle-optimization content — feels productive but most successful founders aren't into it; they just love working on their startup so much that heavy work doesn't feel like work [startup-experts-reveal-their-top-productivity-advice @ 04:31](https://youtu.be/nF_YWdz6S0Y?t=271)
- "Fake work" is described as more dangerous than obviously wasting time (like watching Netflix) because it feels like progress while not actually advancing the specific goal [startup-experts-reveal-their-top-productivity-advice @ 06:10](https://youtu.be/nF_YWdz6S0Y?t=370)
- Paul Graham's maker-schedule vs. manager-schedule concept is highlighted as genuinely valuable: makers need long uninterrupted blocks, managers need back-to-back meetings, and mixing the two ruins deep work [startup-experts-reveal-their-top-productivity-advice @ 06:46](https://youtu.be/nF_YWdz6S0Y?t=406)
- One technical founder kept writing code for years by scheduling meetings only in the morning and reserving several uninterrupted afternoon/evening hours purely for a maker schedule [startup-experts-reveal-their-top-productivity-advice @ 08:01](https://youtu.be/nF_YWdz6S0Y?t=481)
- Chasing social media validation is called a distraction and even a danger, since founders can start believing their public "media personality" instead of focusing on how the product is actually doing [startup-experts-reveal-their-top-productivity-advice @ 08:51](https://youtu.be/nF_YWdz6S0Y?t=531)
- Stack-ranking every project and committing to only the top few (e.g. top 3 of 10) is described as a forcing function that makes founders actually prioritize instead of working a little on everything [startup-experts-reveal-their-top-productivity-advice @ 11:09](https://youtu.be/nF_YWdz6S0Y?t=669)
- The most important productivity model isn't what you decide to do but what you deliberately decide not to do, since there's an infinite supply of things competing for founder time [startup-experts-reveal-their-top-productivity-advice @ 13:01](https://youtu.be/nF_YWdz6S0Y?t=781)
