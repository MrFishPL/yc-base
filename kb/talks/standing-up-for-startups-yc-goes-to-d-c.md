# Standing Up For Startups - YC Goes To D.C. — Compiled Truth

YC's Gary Tan and head of public policy Luther Lowe explain why YC opened a Washington D.C. presence to advocate for "little tech" founders against the outsized policy influence of big tech incumbents.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- YC believes "little tech" should exist in large numbers (not just one or two winners) because that kind of entrepreneurial prosperity is what actually solves American problems [standing-up-for-startups-yc-goes-to-d-c @ 00:02](https://youtu.be/6WtdFqgsEY0?t=2)
- YC opened a D.C. office because for 15+ years tech policy conversations have been dominated by a handful of the largest players, leaving no seat at the table for emerging "little tech" founders [standing-up-for-startups-yc-goes-to-d-c @ 01:50](https://youtu.be/6WtdFqgsEY0?t=110)
- A top policy priority is access to talent, specifically making it easier for high-skilled individuals to immigrate and build companies in the U.S. [standing-up-for-startups-yc-goes-to-d-c @ 03:05](https://youtu.be/6WtdFqgsEY0?t=185)
- YC supports a national non-compete ban, pointing to California's de facto ban as having unlocked significant innovation [standing-up-for-startups-yc-goes-to-d-c @ 03:25](https://youtu.be/6WtdFqgsEY0?t=205)
- YC backs legislation like the American Innovation and Choice Online Act and the Open App Markets Act to prevent dominant platforms from self-preferencing and choking out downstream competition [standing-up-for-startups-yc-goes-to-d-c @ 03:43](https://youtu.be/6WtdFqgsEY0?t=223)
- On AI policy, YC wants to ensure that open-source tools and open-source foundation models aren't inadvertently criminalized, which would squelch entrepreneurship [standing-up-for-startups-yc-goes-to-d-c @ 04:30](https://youtu.be/6WtdFqgsEY0?t=270)
- Because YC has 11,000 founders spanning nearly every congressional district, it can offer policymakers authentic constituent stories as an antidote to the "astroturf" campaigns that dominate D.C. lobbying [standing-up-for-startups-yc-goes-to-d-c @ 07:52](https://youtu.be/6WtdFqgsEY0?t=472)
- Tech went from a fringe California phenomenon with roughly $50-100B max market caps two decades ago to now representing half of the S&P 500's value, which is why "big tech" now draws significant political scrutiny [standing-up-for-startups-yc-goes-to-d-c @ 08:45](https://youtu.be/6WtdFqgsEY0?t=525)
- Younger congressional staff are often more sophisticated on tech issues than outsiders assume, but without direct founder engagement, policymakers only hear from the largest, most powerful interests [standing-up-for-startups-yc-goes-to-d-c @ 10:00](https://youtu.be/6WtdFqgsEY0?t=600)
