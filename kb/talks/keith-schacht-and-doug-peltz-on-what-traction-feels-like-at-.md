# Keith Schacht and Doug Peltz on What Traction Feels Like - at YC Edtech Night — Compiled Truth

Mystery Science founders Keith Schacht and Doug Peltz recount pivoting through roughly seven product ideas before finding traction with an elementary science curriculum, and the near-death funding crisis before they figured out sales.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Positive-sounding teacher feedback ("this is so interesting") was misleading; real usage data, not verbal enthusiasm, was the true signal of product-market fit [keith-schacht-and-doug-peltz-on-what-traction-feels-like-at- @ 08:42](https://youtu.be/2H15qBGa3Hs?t=522)
- Keith told Doug directly "this isn't what traction feels like... you will know when we have traction," pushing them to pivot again despite strong verbal interest [keith-schacht-and-doug-peltz-on-what-traction-feels-like-at- @ 09:18](https://youtu.be/2H15qBGa3Hs?t=558)
- The real insight came from users misusing the product in an unexpected way: elementary teachers (not the middle-school science specialists they targeted) loved the picture-based lessons, revealing the actual market [keith-schacht-and-doug-peltz-on-what-traction-feels-like-at- @ 10:10](https://youtu.be/2H15qBGa3Hs?t=610)
- Once they built out a full 8-lesson pilot for 20 elementary teachers and homeschoolers, the enthusiastic response and unprompted anecdotes made it obvious they finally had a product people wanted [keith-schacht-and-doug-peltz-on-what-traction-feels-like-at- @ 13:45](https://youtu.be/2H15qBGa3Hs?t=825)
- They initially had no idea of the difference between "supplemental resource" and "curricular resource" markets, and modeled themselves on BrainPOP once they researched competitors [keith-schacht-and-doug-peltz-on-what-traction-feels-like-at- @ 15:38](https://youtu.be/2H15qBGa3Hs?t=938)
- Hiring a VP of Sales failed fast — they hired one and fired them three months later — right before the company nearly ran out of money at 60 days of runway [keith-schacht-and-doug-peltz-on-what-traction-feels-like-at- @ 20:16](https://youtu.be/2H15qBGa3Hs?t=1216)
- Their core "secret sauce" for selling into schools was making something teachers and kids genuinely loved, which turned teachers into unpaid, willing salespeople who'd push purchase orders to their own principals [keith-schacht-and-doug-peltz-on-what-traction-feels-like-at- @ 19:22](https://youtu.be/2H15qBGa3Hs?t=1162)
- Despite already being profitable, they joined YC's Summer 2017 batch mainly to help scale the team and support fundraising, having previously been rejected as a very different idea [keith-schacht-and-doug-peltz-on-what-traction-feels-like-at- @ 22:35](https://youtu.be/2H15qBGa3Hs?t=1355)
