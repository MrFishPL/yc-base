# Gustaf Alstromer - How to Get Users and Grow — Compiled Truth

Former Airbnb growth lead Gustaf Alstromer argues growth work is dangerous before product-market fit, lays out how to measure fit via retention curves, and walks through concrete growth-channel and experimentation tactics from his time scaling Airbnb.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Applying growth tactics before finding product-market fit produces a "grow like crazy then crash" pattern, since the underlying retention isn't there to sustain acquired users [gustaf-alstromer-how-to-get-users-and-grow @ 06:55](https://youtu.be/T9ikpoF2GH0?t=415)
- Facebook's data science team forecast the platform would plateau near 400M users, but successive intentional growth bets (localization/translation, mobile shift, internet.org connectivity) repeatedly broke through the ceiling to reach 2+ billion [gustaf-alstromer-how-to-get-users-and-grow @ 08:39](https://youtu.be/T9ikpoF2GH0?t=519)
- To measure product-market fit, pick the one metric that represents your product's core value (e.g., bookings for Airbnb, payroll runs for Gusto) and measure repeat usage of that metric over time as a cohort retention curve [gustaf-alstromer-how-to-get-users-and-grow @ 13:11](https://youtu.be/T9ikpoF2GH0?t=791)
- A retention curve that keeps declining toward zero (like the anonymized "Stripe-like" example shown) signals no product-market fit; one that flattens out (Shopify, Netflix at 70% after 7 years) signals real fit worth investing growth effort into [gustaf-alstromer-how-to-get-users-and-grow @ 17:27](https://youtu.be/T9ikpoF2GH0?t=1047)
- Growth channels should match your product's natural discovery behavior: rare/annual-decision products should invest in SEO, viral products in referrals, and high-LTV paid products in Facebook/Google/Instagram/YouTube ads [gustaf-alstromer-how-to-get-users-and-grow @ 25:38](https://youtu.be/T9ikpoF2GH0?t=1538)
- Airbnb's referral emails were the product of dozens of experiments — using the referrer's name, a clear dollar-value headline, urgency deadlines, exclusivity language ("accept"), and social proof photos — each element chosen for a measured reason [gustaf-alstromer-how-to-get-users-and-grow @ 32:49](https://youtu.be/T9ikpoF2GH0?t=1969)
- Paid acquisition should only be used once you have real revenue, and success depends on customer acquisition cost staying below lifetime value/payback time [gustaf-alstromer-how-to-get-users-and-grow @ 34:37](https://youtu.be/T9ikpoF2GH0?t=2077)
- SEO still matters because Google can't render images/JavaScript, so on-page optimization (clear indexable text, keyword research) and off-page optimization (earned links, e.g. from press coverage) both directly drove Airbnb's organic traffic [gustaf-alstromer-how-to-get-users-and-grow @ 37:27](https://youtu.be/T9ikpoF2GH0?t=2247)
- Doing things that don't scale (manually recruiting drivers, handing out coupons) is fine early but should transition to automated, scalable channels once ROI on the manual approach declines [gustaf-alstromer-how-to-get-users-and-grow @ 57:24](https://youtu.be/T9ikpoF2GH0?t=3444)
- Without A/B testing, teams "guess or use the gun" and end up lucky or wrong — Airbnb ran experiment reviews every 2-3 weeks and found gut intuition wrong roughly half the time, including a 40% share-rate lift from a redesigned share sheet [gustaf-alstromer-how-to-get-users-and-grow @ 44:01](https://youtu.be/T9ikpoF2GH0?t=2641)
