# Laks Srini on Making Homeownership in Reach with ZeroDown — Compiled Truth

ZeroDown's CTO explains how the company spent over a year researching the mortgage industry before building a model that buys homes outright for customers and lets them earn purchase credits toward ownership over time.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- ZeroDown started from a personal problem: even well-paid tech employees in the Bay Area couldn't save the $300-400k needed to buy a home [laks-srini-on-making-homeownership-in-reach-with-zerodown @ 00:21](https://youtu.be/qNQk29B_oEo?t=21)
- Before building anything, the founders spent close to 18 months researching and ran a "help Abhijit buy a house" side project to work out the mechanics [laks-srini-on-making-homeownership-in-reach-with-zerodown @ 01:19](https://youtu.be/qNQk29B_oEo?t=79)
- They deliberately studied mortgage startups that had failed rather than just successful ones, to learn from survivorship bias [laks-srini-on-making-homeownership-in-reach-with-zerodown @ 02:00](https://youtu.be/qNQk29B_oEo?t=120)
- A key lesson from failed competitors: customers cared more about a 50-basis-point rate difference than a better UX, since mortgages are a commoditized, once-in-a-lifetime transaction [laks-srini-on-making-homeownership-in-reach-with-zerodown @ 04:09](https://youtu.be/qNQk29B_oEo?t=249)
- The product buys the customer's chosen house outright and hands over keys in seven days, with customers earning purchase credits (like vesting) toward ownership each month [laks-srini-on-making-homeownership-in-reach-with-zerodown @ 06:23](https://youtu.be/qNQk29B_oEo?t=383)
- The core value proposition is the zero down payment itself, since $200,000 today is worth more than $200,000 five years from now [laks-srini-on-making-homeownership-in-reach-with-zerodown @ 10:04](https://youtu.be/qNQk29B_oEo?t=604)
- The business model borrows the "hub-and-spoke" idea from Zenefits: keep the core financing at cost and monetize ancillary services instead [laks-srini-on-making-homeownership-in-reach-with-zerodown @ 21:49](https://youtu.be/qNQk29B_oEo?t=1309)
- Revenue comes from a $10,000 upfront fee and splitting commission with the buy-side broker, plus a cut of concierge/furniture rewards [laks-srini-on-making-homeownership-in-reach-with-zerodown @ 22:46](https://youtu.be/qNQk29B_oEo?t=1366)
- The team hired a distributed engineering group in Vancouver and a data science team in India, influenced by Paul Graham's essay that 95% of talent is outside the US [laks-srini-on-making-homeownership-in-reach-with-zerodown @ 18:43](https://youtu.be/qNQk29B_oEo?t=1123)
- The company records customer interviews continuously and shares them across the whole team so everyone keeps learning what customers actually want [laks-srini-on-making-homeownership-in-reach-with-zerodown @ 26:19](https://youtu.be/qNQk29B_oEo?t=1579)
- His advice to founders still searching for an idea: don't start a company unless you truly believe it should exist, because there will be deep, dark days [laks-srini-on-making-homeownership-in-reach-with-zerodown @ 32:19](https://youtu.be/qNQk29B_oEo?t=1939)
