# How To Talk To Users | Startup School — Compiled Truth

A former Airbnb employee and YC group partner explains how to find early users, run unbiased problem-discovery interviews, and translate what you learn into an MVP.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Great founders talk to future customers before they even have a product, as Brian Chesky did by living in 50 different Airbnbs to learn directly from hosts [how-to-talk-to-users-startup-school @ 01:19](https://youtu.be/z1iF1c8w5Lg?t=79)
- Airbnb's founders put their personal cell numbers on the site and still get calls from hosts today, rejecting anonymous "do-not-reply" support emails in favor of direct customer contact [how-to-talk-to-users-startup-school @ 02:38](https://youtu.be/z1iF1c8w5Lg?t=158)
- Good early-user sources beyond your own network include former co-workers, LinkedIn, relevant Reddit/Slack/Discord communities, and in-person events [how-to-talk-to-users-startup-school @ 03:55](https://youtu.be/z1iF1c8w5Lg?t=235)
- Interviews should happen over video/phone/in-person (a 5-minute video call teaches more than 5,000 survey responses), and the founder's job is to listen, not pitch [how-to-talk-to-users-startup-school @ 06:02](https://youtu.be/z1iF1c8w5Lg?t=362)
- Never introduce your idea until the very end of the interview (or not at all) to avoid biasing the interviewee's answers [how-to-talk-to-users-startup-school @ 06:24](https://youtu.be/z1iF1c8w5Lg?t=384)
- Ask concrete questions like "tell me how you do X today," "why is it hard," and "why is it important for your company" instead of leading yes/no or feature-suggestion questions [how-to-talk-to-users-startup-school @ 08:29](https://youtu.be/z1iF1c8w5Lg?t=509)
- Avoid asking "will you use our product," "what features would make it better," or double-barreled questions — users have real problems but generally bad solution ideas [how-to-talk-to-users-startup-school @ 09:45](https://youtu.be/z1iF1c8w5Lg?t=585)
- Gmail users asking to see inbox and email on the same screen really meant "Gmail is too slow," and Airbnb guests asking for host phone numbers really meant they didn't yet trust the platform [how-to-talk-to-users-startup-school @ 11:40](https://youtu.be/z1iF1c8w5Lg?t=700)
- To judge if a problem is valuable, check whether people already pay for existing (even crude) solutions like Excel, and whether the audience is easy to sell to versus notoriously hard groups like plumbers or contractors [how-to-talk-to-users-startup-school @ 13:22](https://youtu.be/z1iF1c8w5Lg?t=802)
- Test MVPs by handing users an unbuilt prototype (e.g. an InVision click-through) with a goal but no instructions, and have them think aloud while you watch rather than guide them [how-to-talk-to-users-startup-school @ 14:32](https://youtu.be/z1iF1c8w5Lg?t=872)
