# Shana Fisher at Startup School NY 2014 — Compiled Truth

High Line Venture Partners founder Shana Fisher deliberately gives contrarian advice on runway, co-founders, launch timing, design, and people management, arguing conventional startup wisdom is often wrong.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Founders should raise money to get the company done "by any means necessary" rather than framing a raise as a fixed number of months of runway [shana-fisher-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 03:05](https://youtu.be/Vp48ko2VJgE?t=185)
- Some founders are simply better suited to being single founders than to having co-founders, and if you do have co-founders they should not have overlapping skills [shana-fisher-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 05:06](https://youtu.be/Vp48ko2VJgE?t=306)
- Contrary to "launch fast and iterate," Fisher argues that in a crowded market founders should take as much time as needed, even a year, to make the product perfect rather than rushing out an MVP [shana-fisher-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 06:50](https://youtu.be/Vp48ko2VJgE?t=410)
- Investors are highly subjective and pattern-matching is unreliable, since markets constantly change, so a founder should not assume rejection means the idea is bad [shana-fisher-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 10:16](https://youtu.be/Vp48ko2VJgE?t=616)
- Product development should prioritize "building for today" rather than over-investing in infrastructure disciplines carried over from bigger companies [shana-fisher-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 13:15](https://youtu.be/Vp48ko2VJgE?t=795)
- Great design has become homogenized across startups (same templates, flat design, sans-serif fonts), so Fisher looks past surface-level design for genuine product breakthroughs underneath [shana-fisher-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 14:50](https://youtu.be/Vp48ko2VJgE?t=890)
- Founders should hire and perfect management one person at a time rather than hiring several people at once, since most first-time founders don't know how to manage people [shana-fisher-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 17:05](https://youtu.be/Vp48ko2VJgE?t=1025)
- Prioritizing diversity (gender, racial, mental) in early hiring gives founders more views of their users and is something Fisher has seen consistently in her successful portfolio companies [shana-fisher-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 18:03](https://youtu.be/Vp48ko2VJgE?t=1083)
- Fisher describes "the equinox" as the time horizon when it stops being acceptable to not make money, and founders often fail to recognize when that point has passed [shana-fisher-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 22:27](https://youtu.be/Vp48ko2VJgE?t=1347)
