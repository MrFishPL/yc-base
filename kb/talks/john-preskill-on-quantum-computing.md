# John Preskill on Quantum Computing — Compiled Truth

Caltech physicist John Preskill traces quantum computing from Feynman's early-1980s "universal quantum simulator" idea through entanglement, error correction, and near-term "50-qubit" milestones, then closes with advice on where quantum startups cluster and how to build cross-disciplinary teams.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Feynman's early-1980s insight that "nature is quantum mechanical... a simulation of nature should be quantum mechanical" is the origin of what we now call a quantum computer [john-preskill-on-quantum-computing @ 02:00](https://youtu.be/o3hHO3S8Unk?t=120)
- Quantum error correction, worked out theoretically in the mid-1990s, protects information by encoding it in entanglement so the environment can't "read" and disturb it [john-preskill-on-quantum-computing @ 03:16](https://youtu.be/o3hHO3S8Unk?t=196)
- Peter Shor's factoring algorithm caused excitement about quantum computing roughly a decade after Feynman's proposal, largely because of its implications for breaking cryptography [john-preskill-on-quantum-computing @ 02:25](https://youtu.be/o3hHO3S8Unk?t=145)
- Grover's algorithm speeds up exhaustive search via quantum interference, but the practical speedup is modest — roughly doubling the problem size solvable in the same time, not exponential [john-preskill-on-quantum-computing @ 08:39](https://youtu.be/o3hHO3S8Unk?t=519)
- Preskill expected devices with roughly 50-100 qubits within a couple of years — already too many states for even the most powerful classical supercomputers to simulate, though gate error rates (~1 in 1,000) remain the limiting factor [john-preskill-on-quantum-computing @ 19:36](https://youtu.be/o3hHO3S8Unk?t=1176)
- He sees the most likely near-term practical impact in designing new materials, catalysts, and pharmaceuticals, since predicting quantum chemical behavior is intractable for classical computers [john-preskill-on-quantum-computing @ 14:33](https://youtu.be/o3hHO3S8Unk?t=873)
- Quantum computers threaten widely-used public-key cryptosystems like RSA and elliptic-curve cryptography, motivating research into post-quantum cryptography and quantum key distribution [john-preskill-on-quantum-computing @ 37:14](https://youtu.be/o3hHO3S8Unk?t=2234)
- Quantum-computing startups have clustered in the Bay Area mainly because that's where tech financing and investors interested in innovative technical startups are concentrated [john-preskill-on-quantum-computing @ 1:20:17](https://youtu.be/o3hHO3S8Unk?t=4817)
- His advice to would-be quantum-computing entrepreneurs building a team: favor people who can communicate across disciplinary boundaries (hardware, control engineering, software), since no one can be an expert in all of them [john-preskill-on-quantum-computing @ 1:22:37](https://youtu.be/o3hHO3S8Unk?t=4957)
