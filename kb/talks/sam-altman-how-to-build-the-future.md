# Sam Altman : How to Build the Future — Compiled Truth

In an interview with his brother, Sam Altman lays out how he chooses what to work on, why most people give up too early, and why taking risk early in life and holding strong long-term convictions compound into outsized impact.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Look for the intersection of what you're good at, what you enjoy, and where you can create value for the world — impact is hard without hitting that intersection [sam-altman-how-to-build-the-future @ 00:14](https://youtu.be/sYMqVwsewSg?t=14)
- The way things get done in the world is a combination of focus, personal connection, and self-belief — a deep conviction that something others call stupid will actually work [sam-altman-how-to-build-the-future @ 02:59](https://youtu.be/sYMqVwsewSg?t=179)
- Working hard early in a career compounds, since improvements made early benefit you for the rest of the time you work, making the beginning of a career the most valuable time to invest effort [sam-altman-how-to-build-the-future @ 03:53](https://youtu.be/sYMqVwsewSg?t=233)
- Most people give up on ideas far too early, often within weeks, when the best entrepreneurs Altman knows spent a long time working through periods when critics said their idea sucked or, worse, said nothing at all [sam-altman-how-to-build-the-future @ 05:34](https://youtu.be/sYMqVwsewSg?t=334)
- The decision to quit should be internal, not driven by external criticism or lack of usage — the right time to stop is when you've run out of ideas and something still isn't working [sam-altman-how-to-build-the-future @ 06:47](https://youtu.be/sYMqVwsewSg?t=407)
- Burnout comes not from working too hard but from failing and lacking momentum; momentum is energizing and founders should recognize that a failing project, not lack of passion, is often the real cause of low energy [sam-altman-how-to-build-the-future @ 09:06](https://youtu.be/sYMqVwsewSg?t=546)
- Most people take too little risk, especially early in their career when being young, unknown, and poor is a gift, and the real risk is doing nothing and later regretting not trying [sam-altman-how-to-build-the-future @ 10:35](https://youtu.be/sYMqVwsewSg?t=635)
- Founders should ask for what they want even though they'll be told no often, and each subsequent company crisis feels less scary than the one before it [sam-altman-how-to-build-the-future @ 12:52](https://youtu.be/sYMqVwsewSg?t=772)
- Time is one of the last remaining arbitrage opportunities in markets — Altman believes in making a long-term commitment (holding stock in the best companies "forever") rather than chasing quarterly cycles [sam-altman-how-to-build-the-future @ 15:45](https://youtu.be/sYMqVwsewSg?t=945)
- The best approach is a strong opinion strongly held about the future combined with flexibility in implementation details, distinguishing healthy product refinement from unfocused pivoting [sam-altman-how-to-build-the-future @ 17:41](https://youtu.be/sYMqVwsewSg?t=1061)
