# Kevin Hale - How to Improve Conversion Rates — Compiled Truth

Kevin Hale presents a "knowledge spectrum" framework and a seven-question checklist for diagnosing and fixing weak conversion rates on landing pages and other interfaces, illustrated with live critiques of two Startup School companies.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Growth is the balance between conversion and churn, and churn is actually easier to work on than conversion, which is the harder problem this talk addresses [kevin-hale-how-to-improve-conversion-rates @ 00:37](https://youtu.be/PGqX9fpweyc?t=37)
- Only work on conversion rate if you have a "leaky bucket"; freemium SaaS benchmarks run 1.5-5% (about 3% average), and around 3% usually means you should focus elsewhere instead [kevin-hale-how-to-improve-conversion-rates @ 01:17](https://youtu.be/PGqX9fpweyc?t=77)
- Every interface problem is about closing the "knowledge gap" between the user's current knowledge and the target knowledge needed to use the product, per Jared Spool's knowledge spectrum [kevin-hale-how-to-improve-conversion-rates @ 03:46](https://youtu.be/PGqX9fpweyc?t=226)
- Imagine reducing your landing page to a single button and ask what minimum information is needed to get someone to push it, and what information is blocking them [kevin-hale-how-to-improve-conversion-rates @ 05:06](https://youtu.be/PGqX9fpweyc?t=306)
- The call to action should sit as close as possible to the "magic moment," the instant a user suddenly gets excited about the product [kevin-hale-how-to-improve-conversion-rates @ 06:12](https://youtu.be/PGqX9fpweyc?t=372)
- Use the "mom test" for clarity: you should be able to copy a sentence off the page and have your mom understand exactly what the product is [kevin-hale-how-to-improve-conversion-rates @ 07:19](https://youtu.be/PGqX9fpweyc?t=439)
- The seven diagnostic questions are: what's the call to action, what is this, is it for me, is it legit, who else uses it, how much does it cost, and where can I get help [kevin-hale-how-to-improve-conversion-rates @ 05:45](https://youtu.be/PGqX9fpweyc?t=345)
- B2B and enterprise companies often avoid stating price, but hiding cost or the "catch" hurts conversion because no one commits to using something without knowing what it costs [kevin-hale-how-to-improve-conversion-rates @ 09:22](https://youtu.be/PGqX9fpweyc?t=562)
- In a live critique of meetingroom.io, Kevin found competing calls to action and a seven-step carousel that delayed the magic moment of actually booking and meeting in a room [kevin-hale-how-to-improve-conversion-rates @ 11:19](https://youtu.be/PGqX9fpweyc?t=679)
- In a critique of DivJoy, he noted the real magic moment (a full generated SaaS codebase) was buried, while showing a usage counter of downloads was a good substitute for customer logos as social proof [kevin-hale-how-to-improve-conversion-rates @ 16:21](https://youtu.be/PGqX9fpweyc?t=981)
