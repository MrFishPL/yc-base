# What Basic Game Theory Teaches Us About Startups — Compiled Truth

Startups and life choices can be framed as zero-sum games (where one person's gain is another's loss) versus positive-sum games (where lasting value is created), and founders should consciously choose to build positive-sum businesses despite FOMO from people who appear to be winning zero-sum games.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A zero-sum game is one where your gain comes directly out of someone else's pocket with no new value created, like a bet or gambling, while a positive-sum game creates lasting value, like building a house [what-basic-game-theory-teaches-us-about-startups @ 00:46](https://youtu.be/gUuQsMERjUc?t=46)
- Many activities that resemble zero-sum gambling aren't labeled as such, including confidence games and, historically, banks knowingly selling bad debt to unsuspecting customers before financial regulation existed [what-basic-game-theory-teaches-us-about-startups @ 04:03](https://youtu.be/gUuQsMERjUc?t=243)
- Society's progress is built by people playing positive-sum games, while zero-sum players slowly harvest and degrade that created value, becoming especially visible during financial crises [what-basic-game-theory-teaches-us-about-startups @ 07:56](https://youtu.be/gUuQsMERjUc?t=476)
- When markets are rising it's easy to feel like a genius; when they fall, the truly skilled zero-sum players reveal themselves by taking money from everyone else, fueling a loss of faith in the system [what-basic-game-theory-teaches-us-about-startups @ 09:06](https://youtu.be/gUuQsMERjUc?t=546)
- People playing positive-sum games and failing still walk away having learned something, whereas zero-sum players who don't cash out get no such lesson until they're suddenly wiped out [what-basic-game-theory-teaches-us-about-startups @ 09:49](https://youtu.be/gUuQsMERjUc?t=589)
- To resist FOMO toward people who look like they're winning at zero-sum games, ask whether they are actually cashing out and building something sustainable rather than just riding a temporary streak [what-basic-game-theory-teaches-us-about-startups @ 11:54](https://youtu.be/gUuQsMERjUc?t=714)
- Founders solving a real problem keep growing even when external market conditions worsen, while those dependent on market hype collapse the moment "the music stops" [what-basic-game-theory-teaches-us-about-startups @ 13:16](https://youtu.be/gUuQsMERjUc?t=796)
- Wealthy but unhappy people are often unhappy because they equate making money with making impact, when in fact legacy—not money—is what makes people feel good about themselves [what-basic-game-theory-teaches-us-about-startups @ 14:17](https://youtu.be/gUuQsMERjUc?t=857)
- A useful self-check is to imagine the decision as advice you'd give your own child or employee, which tends to point people toward the positive-sum choice [what-basic-game-theory-teaches-us-about-startups @ 15:56](https://youtu.be/gUuQsMERjUc?t=956)
- This zero-sum versus positive-sum framework applies broadly to startups, jobs, and relationships, and choosing positive-sum interactions compounds in ways that are hard to fully anticipate [what-basic-game-theory-teaches-us-about-startups @ 17:33](https://youtu.be/gUuQsMERjUc?t=1053)
