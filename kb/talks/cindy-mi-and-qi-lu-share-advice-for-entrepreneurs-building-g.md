# Cindy Mi and Qi Lu Share Advice for Entrepreneurs Building Global Companies — Compiled Truth

VIPKid founder Cindy Mi describes how a childhood English-tutoring hustle became a global, two-sided marketplace connecting North American teachers to Chinese students, and what it took to find product-market fit, build company culture, and scale globally from day one.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- VIPKid targeted a market where Chinese parents spend roughly 50% of household income on education versus 2% in the US, while facing a severe shortage of just 27,000 North American English teachers nationwide [cindy-mi-and-qi-lu-share-advice-for-entrepreneurs-building-g @ 08:14](https://youtu.be/EoffBerre24?t=494)
- It took VIPKid a year and a half to find product-market fit, tracked through efficacy (test scores), effectiveness (time on platform and progress), and engagement (ratings, retention, NPS) metrics on both the student and teacher sides of the marketplace [cindy-mi-and-qi-lu-share-advice-for-entrepreneurs-building-g @ 14:58](https://youtu.be/EoffBerre24?t=898)
- The company launched its MVP with just four students found through personal networks, growing roughly ten students per month to reach 200 students and 20 teachers by March 2015 [cindy-mi-and-qi-lu-share-advice-for-entrepreneurs-building-g @ 17:20](https://youtu.be/EoffBerre24?t=1040)
- A key early mistake was over-building the technology platform for three months on a "perfect" blueprint before realizing they needed to focus on what was most essential and relaunch quickly [cindy-mi-and-qi-lu-share-advice-for-entrepreneurs-building-g @ 26:33](https://youtu.be/EoffBerre24?t=1593)
- After a viral WeChat post generated 2,000 parent signups in a day, the team wasn't ready for growth and every employee, including engineers, had to become customer service reps to respond over three months [cindy-mi-and-qi-lu-share-advice-for-entrepreneurs-building-g @ 27:41](https://youtu.be/EoffBerre24?t=1661)
- The business model lowered one-on-one tutoring cost for parents by 50-60% while paying teachers around $20/hour (versus a US market rate near $16/hour with a commute), creating value on both sides of the marketplace [cindy-mi-and-qi-lu-share-advice-for-entrepreneurs-building-g @ 21:47](https://youtu.be/EoffBerre24?t=1307)
- Building a global company meant establishing a real US team (San Francisco and Dallas) dedicated to teacher success, plus culture practices like scheduling meetings mindful of a 16-hour time difference [cindy-mi-and-qi-lu-share-advice-for-entrepreneurs-building-g @ 38:16](https://youtu.be/EoffBerre24?t=2296)
- Cindy Mi's core advice to founders is to obsess over customer success daily, citing her practice of personally listening to customer service calls each morning and flying to meet teachers in person [cindy-mi-and-qi-lu-share-advice-for-entrepreneurs-building-g @ 54:16](https://youtu.be/EoffBerre24?t=3256)
- She frames AI's opportunity in education as empowering teachers with intelligent assistant tools so one teacher can effectively support far more students rather than replacing the human relationship [cindy-mi-and-qi-lu-share-advice-for-entrepreneurs-building-g @ 45:06](https://youtu.be/EoffBerre24?t=2706)
