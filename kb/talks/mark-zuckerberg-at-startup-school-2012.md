# Mark Zuckerberg at Startup School 2012 — Compiled Truth

Mark Zuckerberg recounts Facebook's earliest days — school email addresses as an identity hack, an $85-a-month server as the constraint on growth, and a philosophy of building something fundamental rather than deciding upfront to "start a company."

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Facebook bootstrapped trust and identity by requiring school-issued email addresses, since students typically had only one and couldn't fake a new school affiliation [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2012 @ 01:00](https://youtu.be/5bJi7k-y1Lo?t=60)
- Zuckerberg and Dustin Moskovitz manually parsed each school's course catalog to keep data clean, choosing quality and a defined culture over the speed of launching more schools faster [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2012 @ 04:07](https://youtu.be/5bJi7k-y1Lo?t=247)
- The first server cost $85/month, and the company deliberately never spent money it didn't have, expanding only as ad revenue funded additional $85 servers [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2012 @ 06:18](https://youtu.be/5bJi7k-y1Lo?t=378)
- Slow, capacity-constrained growth (a year to reach one million users) gave the team time to "bake" the product and avoid the scaling collapse that hurt Friendster [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2012 @ 07:36](https://youtu.be/5bJi7k-y1Lo?t=456)
- Facebook deliberately launched next at Yale, Stanford, and Columbia — schools with existing campus social networks — because beating a real competitor validated that the product was worth continued investment [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2012 @ 10:32](https://youtu.be/5bJi7k-y1Lo?t=632)
- Watching users repeatedly re-upload a single profile photo revealed strong latent demand for photo sharing, which later became a core Facebook feature [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2012 @ 13:39](https://youtu.be/5bJi7k-y1Lo?t=819)
- Zuckerberg says he never set out to start a company — Facebook grew out of something he personally wanted to exist, and he warns that committing to a company too early sacrifices the flexibility to change direction [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2012 @ 16:37](https://youtu.be/5bJi7k-y1Lo?t=997)
- He argues the most durable products address something fundamental to human behavior (e.g., people's innate interest in other people) rather than a narrow, tangible problem [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2012 @ 22:08](https://youtu.be/5bJi7k-y1Lo?t=1328)
- On Myspace: copying a competitor once you feel threatened by them is not a winning strategy — you win by doing something genuinely valuable rather than reacting to a rival [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2012 @ 23:46](https://youtu.be/5bJi7k-y1Lo?t=1426)
- What people call "pivots" were, in Facebook's case, really expansions in scope (college to non-college, website to platform) rather than the company's original thing failing [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2012 @ 19:02](https://youtu.be/5bJi7k-y1Lo?t=1142)
