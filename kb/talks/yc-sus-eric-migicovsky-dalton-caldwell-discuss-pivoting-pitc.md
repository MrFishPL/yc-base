# YC SUS: Eric Migicovsky & Dalton Caldwell discuss pivoting & pitching — Compiled Truth

Dalton Caldwell answers founder questions on when and how to pivot, arguing that founders should make metrics-driven decisions rather than getting lost in conceptual arguments about their market.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Don't start a new company to pivot — keep the same cap table, investors, and Delaware C-corp since changing corporate entities is unproductive work with zero impact on product-market fit [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-dalton-caldwell-discuss-pivoting-pitc @ 00:54](https://youtu.be/msWFarwD-fU?t=54)
- Founders often build elaborate hypothetical theories about why something isn't working instead of just getting one real paying customer, which is the actual bar for validation [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-dalton-caldwell-discuss-pivoting-pitc @ 05:29](https://youtu.be/msWFarwD-fU?t=329)
- Set up one-week experiments that change either the product or the target user segment (never both at once, to preserve a control) to test whether a pivot direction is working [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-dalton-caldwell-discuss-pivoting-pitc @ 08:14](https://youtu.be/msWFarwD-fU?t=494)
- People's excitement about an idea ("would you like to learn more/better/faster?") is a leading question and not real validation — you need a concrete product, a price, and to actually ask for money [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-dalton-caldwell-discuss-pivoting-pitc @ 10:05](https://youtu.be/msWFarwD-fU?t=605)
- Hard tech companies face financing risk on top of product risk, so they must design a business model and pricing that fits customers' actual budgets from the start, as a tomato-picking robot startup did by studying greenhouse P&L before setting price [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-dalton-caldwell-discuss-pivoting-pitc @ 13:15](https://youtu.be/msWFarwD-fU?t=795)
- Total addressable market slides mostly don't matter at the early stage; when investors focus on TAM it's usually a polite way of saying they're not excited about the company [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-dalton-caldwell-discuss-pivoting-pitc @ 17:55](https://youtu.be/msWFarwD-fU?t=1075)
- A key pivot trigger is literally zero traction after a long effort, or a product where users always say two or three more features would make them regular users, cycle after cycle [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-dalton-caldwell-discuss-pivoting-pitc @ 20:45](https://youtu.be/msWFarwD-fU?t=1245)
- PostHog (funded for a different idea) pivoted mid-batch after their original analytics idea's excitement evaporated the moment they started asking customers for money, showing that willingness to pay is the real signal [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-dalton-caldwell-discuss-pivoting-pitc @ 26:12](https://youtu.be/msWFarwD-fU?t=1572)
- It's fine to mention a pivot or other ideas in the YC application's "other ideas" section, but avoid disjointed applications covering unrelated ideas — pick the one you're most excited about as primary [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-dalton-caldwell-discuss-pivoting-pitc @ 23:11](https://youtu.be/msWFarwD-fU?t=1391)
- Roughly 40% of the current YC batch targets markets outside the US exclusively, and YC's value is in Silicon Valley connections and fundraising help rather than domain or geography expertise [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-dalton-caldwell-discuss-pivoting-pitc @ 34:13](https://youtu.be/msWFarwD-fU?t=2053)
