# YC Panel at Female Founders Conference 2015 — Compiled Truth

A panel of YC partners answers audience questions on applications, co-founder equity, solo founding, and life as a founder, aimed at a female-founders audience.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A strong YC application articulates the idea in a concise way, since "what do you do" is one of the most important questions [yc-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 01:31](https://youtu.be/bJ2rUa_pHxo?t=91)
- Applicants who fill answers with marketing speak like "leveraging this platform" make reviewers' eyes glaze over [yc-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 01:54](https://youtu.be/bJ2rUa_pHxo?t=114)
- YC gets excited about founders who are domain experts tackling a hugely broken problem with a huge market opportunity [yc-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 02:16](https://youtu.be/bJ2rUa_pHxo?t=136)
- YC evaluates nonprofit applicants on the same criteria as for-profits: a good founding team with clarity on the idea and demonstrated ability to execute [yc-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 03:25](https://youtu.be/bJ2rUa_pHxo?t=205)
- Equity should generally be split more or less equally between co-founders, since heavily mismatched splits (e.g. 90/10) tend to cause resentment later given both founders work extremely long hours [yc-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 08:02](https://youtu.be/bJ2rUa_pHxo?t=482)
- Founders should decide equity by looking forward, not backward — the default should be roughly equal because what matters is the work still ahead, not who had the idea or who has an MBA [yc-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 09:28](https://youtu.be/bJ2rUa_pHxo?t=568)
- Solo founders are accepted less often proportionally because there is simply too much to do in a startup for one person, and the emotional ups and downs are harder to handle alone [yc-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 11:50](https://youtu.be/bJ2rUa_pHxo?t=710)
- Having a co-founder helps because when one founder is having a bad day, the other can offer reassurance, and the roles can reverse the next day [yc-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 12:28](https://youtu.be/bJ2rUa_pHxo?t=748)
- The average age of founders in the panel's current YC batch was about 30 (youngest 20, oldest in his late 60s), while the average age across YC's top portfolio companies by valuation was found to be 24 [yc-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 13:27](https://youtu.be/bJ2rUa_pHxo?t=807)
- Female VCs report that female founders often under-sell their own achievements when pitching, and founders need to practice taking credit for what they built [yc-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 16:09](https://youtu.be/bJ2rUa_pHxo?t=969)
