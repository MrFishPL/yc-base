# How Top 1% Founders Navigate Co-founder Conflict — Compiled Truth

Lightcone hosts Garry Tan, Harj Taggar, and Diana Hu share personal stories of co-founder conflict, self-abandonment, and burnout, and lay out frameworks (non-violent communication, "authoritative vs authoritarian," "throwing things over the net") for resolving it productively.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Harj Taggar describes joining forces with Patrick Collison after a PG-brokered "co-founder match," but the company sold via acqui-hire within a year largely because neither founder was truly excited about the idea [how-top-1-founders-navigate-co-founder-conflict @ 05:24](https://youtu.be/bvjyaz4ZiVI?t=324)
- Garry Tan explains that at Posterous he practiced self-abandonment — going along with his co-founder's decisions instead of voicing disagreement — which he later realized is not the same as having a good co-founder relationship [how-top-1-founders-navigate-co-founder-conflict @ 08:18](https://youtu.be/bvjyaz4ZiVI?t=498)
- Suppressed conflict manifested physically for Tan (insomnia, inability to eat or go to the office) before his co-founder sold the company to Twitter for $20 million, an outcome he believes could have been 10-100x bigger with earlier self-awareness [how-top-1-founders-navigate-co-founder-conflict @ 10:01](https://youtu.be/bvjyaz4ZiVI?t=601)
- Tan defines "authoritative" leadership as sitting with people you trust to have a real debate and reach agreement, versus "authoritarian" (skipping straight to a decision to avoid discomfort) or self-abandonment (never asserting an opinion at all) [how-top-1-founders-navigate-co-founder-conflict @ 13:31](https://youtu.be/bvjyaz4ZiVI?t=811)
- Non-violent communication is offered as a tool: it's fair to describe your own feelings and observations, but not fair to speculate about a co-founder's intentions or motivations ("going over the net") [how-top-1-founders-navigate-co-founder-conflict @ 21:33](https://youtu.be/bvjyaz4ZiVI?t=1293)
- Diana Hu recounts spending "tens of hours" and calling in Paul Graham to mediate a dispute over what to name her company (Scripted), a trivial fight in hindsight that nonetheless blocked launch [how-top-1-founders-navigate-co-founder-conflict @ 20:08](https://youtu.be/bvjyaz4ZiVI?t=1208)
- Harj Taggar's experience with Triple Byte co-founders who came from a culture of "extreme aggressive debate" (from Justin.tv) led him to adapt his own style to match theirs, which he now says he should not have done — it should be forced to fit him as CEO instead [how-top-1-founders-navigate-co-founder-conflict @ 32:10](https://youtu.be/bvjyaz4ZiVI?t=1930)
- The panel argues a bad co-founder is worse than being solo, but the truly exceptional breakout companies have a healthy co-founder relationship, and finding a great co-founder is easier once you yourself are recognizably excellent at something [how-top-1-founders-navigate-co-founder-conflict @ 36:31](https://youtu.be/bvjyaz4ZiVI?t=2191)
- Founders are advised to seek outside support (therapy or an executive coach) to surface conflict patterns rooted in personal upbringing before they build up into an "everything conflict" [how-top-1-founders-navigate-co-founder-conflict @ 34:51](https://youtu.be/bvjyaz4ZiVI?t=2091)
