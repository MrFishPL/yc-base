# Lessons From Doing YC Twice - Harj Taggar — Compiled Truth

Harj Taggar, who went through YC in 2007 with Auctomatic and again in 2015 with Triple Byte after years as a YC partner, explains why he redid the program and what changes across a decade taught him about hiring, fundraising, and founder psychology.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Doing YC a second time gave Triple Byte a forcing function and deadline when the team had only an idea and no prototype a month before the batch began [lessons-from-doing-yc-twice-harj-taggar @ 08:48](https://youtu.be/vaRrq0qWAkQ?t=528).
- For a B2B company, being part of a YC batch can hugely help with getting a first set of customers and feedback in a low-risk way, thanks to the network of ~100 other batch companies [lessons-from-doing-yc-twice-harj-taggar @ 09:26](https://youtu.be/vaRrq0qWAkQ?t=566).
- Enterprise startups shouldn't optimize for week-on-week revenue growth early on; the right early metric is discovery activity like how many potential customers you talked to and how much outbound you did [lessons-from-doing-yc-twice-harj-taggar @ 12:33](https://youtu.be/vaRrq0qWAkQ?t=753).
- Founders who talk loudest during a batch about revenue and investor interest don't correlate with actually doing well — some of the best companies (like Instacart) were founders heads-down and quiet [lessons-from-doing-yc-twice-harj-taggar @ 14:36](https://youtu.be/vaRrq0qWAkQ?t=876).
- YC's real focus is action and focus, not growth for its own sake — if you have action and focus on the right metric, growth follows as the output [lessons-from-doing-yc-twice-harj-taggar @ 16:38](https://youtu.be/vaRrq0qWAkQ?t=998).
- Fundraising ultimately only rewards traction, not a founder's past success or pedigree — a prior track record can even be a liability if it lets you raise too much too early and feel falsely validated [lessons-from-doing-yc-twice-harj-taggar @ 22:03](https://youtu.be/vaRrq0qWAkQ?t=1323).
- A startup isn't real until it has real users, real traction, and real growth — everything else (money raised, VC on the board, headcount) is fake by comparison [lessons-from-doing-yc-twice-harj-taggar @ 23:31](https://youtu.be/vaRrq0qWAkQ?t=1411).
- The best way to find a co-founder is not to explicitly search for one, but to build friendships with people who have complementary skills while working on problems together over time [lessons-from-doing-yc-twice-harj-taggar @ 28:49](https://youtu.be/vaRrq0qWAkQ?t=1729).
- First-time founders often succeed because they're unaware how many other people are working on the same idea, whereas experienced insiders know an idea like "digital loyalty cards" already has 20 competing YC applicants [lessons-from-doing-yc-twice-harj-taggar @ 32:04](https://youtu.be/vaRrq0qWAkQ?t=1924).
