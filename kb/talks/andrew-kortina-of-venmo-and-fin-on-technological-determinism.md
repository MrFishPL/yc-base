# Andrew Kortina of Venmo and Fin on Technological Determinism and Work's Relationship to Dignity — Compiled Truth

Venmo and Fin co-founder Andrew Kortina reflects on why he returned to building companies after selling Venmo, arguing that culturally-inherited notions of dignity-through-work, honest founder-to-employee framing, and disciplined focus on hard problems matter more than any grand mission narrative.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Kortina argues it's hard to shed an inherited sense that dignity comes from work, even after selling a company and having no financial need to keep working [andrew-kortina-of-venmo-and-fin-on-technological-determinism @ 00:43](https://youtu.be/aengRJUUNLw?t=43).
- He believes tying dignity to productive work made sense historically as an incentive to build surplus against scarcity, but becomes a "dangerous" connection as abundance grows and not everyone has the opportunity to be productive [andrew-kortina-of-venmo-and-fin-on-technological-determinism @ 06:44](https://youtu.be/aengRJUUNLw?t=404).
- He criticizes Silicon Valley companies for overselling "meaningful work" and personal impact to recruits, contrasting it with Wall Street's more honest "come here, work hard, make money" pitch [andrew-kortina-of-venmo-and-fin-on-technological-determinism @ 28:21](https://youtu.be/aengRJUUNLw?t=1701).
- He describes Venmo as chronically hard to fund because it wasn't monetizing and had to navigate SEC-related constraints, unlike Fin where prior social capital and an existing business model made fundraising easier [andrew-kortina-of-venmo-and-fin-on-technological-determinism @ 49:07](https://youtu.be/aengRJUUNLw?t=2947).
- Fin was built as "AAI" (artificial artificial intelligence): a human-staffed personal-assistant network enabled by technology (like Uber), rather than pure-software AI, because pure NLP assistants couldn't yet act as full-time assistants [andrew-kortina-of-venmo-and-fin-on-technological-determinism @ 42:53](https://youtu.be/aengRJUUNLw?t=2573).
- The hardest part of Fin, unlike a typical focused YC startup, is that it must do literally anything a human assistant would do, making measurement of quality, staffing, and demand forecasting extremely complex [andrew-kortina-of-venmo-and-fin-on-technological-determinism @ 50:33](https://youtu.be/aengRJUUNLw?t=3033).
- Braintree acquired Venmo because CEO Bill Ready saw that Venmo's wired-up consumer base could eventually convert Braintree's thin credit-card-processing margins into PayPal-like margins by getting merchants to accept Venmo [andrew-kortina-of-venmo-and-fin-on-technological-determinism @ 57:56](https://youtu.be/aengRJUUNLw?t=3476).
- He notes managing people was a first-time challenge at Venmo, and that experience carried over made people-management comparatively easier the second time at Fin [andrew-kortina-of-venmo-and-fin-on-technological-determinism @ 49:50](https://youtu.be/aengRJUUNLw?t=2990).
- Kortina says he's wary of "financial independence" being marketed to young people as a multi-year game that can consume their entire 20s without them building or doing anything [andrew-kortina-of-venmo-and-fin-on-technological-determinism @ 30:08](https://youtu.be/aengRJUUNLw?t=1808).
