# How YC Was Created With Jessica Livingston — Compiled Truth

YC co-founder Jessica Livingston recounts the origin story of Y Combinator on the Lightcone podcast, tracing its founding, the role of events and community, and what has and hasn't changed since the first 2005 batch.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- YC was started because there was no early-stage funding option between VCs wanting a tested business plan and scarce angels, so YC would write a small check to let a founder quit their job and test an idea [how-yc-was-created-with-jessica-livingston @ 02:18](https://youtu.be/6u4JVz7iQTY?t=138)
- The batch model wasn't the original plan — YC first intended to fund companies asynchronously like a normal investor from a small Cambridge office under the name "Cambridge Seed" before becoming Y Combinator [how-yc-was-created-with-jessica-livingston @ 04:19](https://youtu.be/6u4JVz7iQTY?t=259)
- Events (weekly dinners, interviews, demo days) were core to YC's DNA from day one, unlike traditional VCs who treated portfolio events as an afterthought handled by assistants [how-yc-was-created-with-jessica-livingston @ 05:41](https://youtu.be/6u4JVz7iQTY?t=341)
- YC standardized legal paperwork and investment terms into simple fill-in-the-blank documents, removing the daunting incorporation/legal overhead that had been the hardest part of starting a company [how-yc-was-created-with-jessica-livingston @ 14:57](https://youtu.be/6u4JVz7iQTY?t=897)
- The first 2005 batch included Sam Altman, Steve Huffman and Alexis Ohanian (Reddit), and Justin Kan and Emmett Shear (later Twitch), and felt magical because everyone genuinely wanted to be there and start a company [how-yc-was-created-with-jessica-livingston @ 18:26](https://youtu.be/6u4JVz7iQTY?t=1106)
- YC's growth from underdog to prestigious came slowly and organically, driven by funded companies becoming well known (Dropbox, Reddit, Airbnb) rather than any single deliberate change [how-yc-was-created-with-jessica-livingston @ 31:13](https://youtu.be/6u4JVz7iQTY?t=1873)
- Russian investor Yuri Milner offered to fund every company in a batch on the spot with $150k uncapped notes, a first-of-its-kind deal that gave founders confidence and runway heading into demo day [how-yc-was-created-with-jessica-livingston @ 38:34](https://youtu.be/6u4JVz7iQTY?t=2314)
- Founders Livingston has known since they were teenagers didn't fundamentally change as they became successful — they gained confidence and access, but stayed independent-minded and true to themselves [how-yc-was-created-with-jessica-livingston @ 51:53](https://youtu.be/6u4JVz7iQTY?t=3113)
- Many big companies pivoted from very different original ideas (e.g. PayPal started as encryption for Palm Pilots before users demanded payments), reinforcing that YC bets on founders, not the initial idea [how-yc-was-created-with-jessica-livingston @ 57:27](https://youtu.be/6u4JVz7iQTY?t=3447)
- Startup School was created in the same scrappy, sponsor-free, free-to-attend spirit as YC's early events, because Paul Graham and Livingston had an "allergic reaction" to sponsors diluting the founder-first focus [how-yc-was-created-with-jessica-livingston @ 44:10](https://youtu.be/6u4JVz7iQTY?t=2650)
