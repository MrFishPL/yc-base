# David Lee at Startup School NY 2014 — Compiled Truth

SV Angel partner David Lee explains what his firm looks for in founders, how founders should evaluate investors, and the biggest lesson he learned from Ron Conway: your reputation is your biggest asset.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- SV Angel's founding philosophy, written by Ron Conway 20 years ago, is "founders first, idea second" because ideas morph but people don't [david-lee-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 08:44](https://youtu.be/Dt-8Io0i_F4?t=524)
- The single most important founder skill is the ability to express a vision clearly to employees, investors, and customers amid heavy market noise [david-lee-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 10:08](https://youtu.be/Dt-8Io0i_F4?t=608)
- Rick Morrison of Comprehend Clinical hooked David Lee in a 45-minute coffee pitch by articulating why the problem was broken, why the timing was right, and why he was the person to solve it [david-lee-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 11:28](https://youtu.be/Dt-8Io0i_F4?t=688)
- The best founders are great listeners who synthesize many inputs (investors, advisers, customers) into a decision and then stick to it, unlike founders who are strong-willed but inflexible [david-lee-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 14:33](https://youtu.be/Dt-8Io0i_F4?t=873)
- When investors and customers said no, Rick Morrison kept asking "why" and pivoted his product from a hosted solution to a SaaS solution based on that feedback, eventually winning over Sequoia Capital [david-lee-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 17:19](https://youtu.be/Dt-8Io0i_F4?t=1039)
- Founders should evaluate investors on value-add rather than fixating on valuation and dilution; the valuation discussion should flow from what value-add is agreed on [david-lee-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 19:37](https://youtu.be/Dt-8Io0i_F4?t=1177)
- Jason Tan of Sift Science accepted less favorable terms from Max Levchin in exchange for hands-on mentorship, illustrating that the best people add more value than optimizing the cap table [david-lee-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 21:09](https://youtu.be/Dt-8Io0i_F4?t=1269)
- This is one of the best times ever to raise money because of the proliferation of financing sources (Angel List, crowdfunding, Kickstarter, YC), giving founders more choice and reason to be discerning [david-lee-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 23:16](https://youtu.be/Dt-8Io0i_F4?t=1396)
- The biggest lesson David Lee learned from Ron Conway is "never forget your reputation is your biggest asset," illustrated by founders like Joseph Walla (HelloSign) who treated a casual cocktail party as work by bringing a list of people to meet [david-lee-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 24:23](https://youtu.be/Dt-8Io0i_F4?t=1463)
