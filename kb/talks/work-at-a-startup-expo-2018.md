# Work at a Startup Expo 2018 — Compiled Truth

Justin Kan and Sam Altman open YC's "Work at a Startup" expo with candid advice on when joining a startup is (and isn't) worth it, and how to evaluate which one to join like an investment.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Startups often have bad management and offer little mentorship, so people wanting career stability should not join one. [work-at-a-startup-expo-2018 @ 01:57](https://youtu.be/cN62reafxLg?t=117)
- Joining a startup is statistically unlikely to make you rich, so that shouldn't be the reason to join. [work-at-a-startup-expo-2018 @ 02:17](https://youtu.be/cN62reafxLg?t=137)
- The biggest reason to join a startup is that it gives you access to jobs you're completely unqualified for, illustrated by an engineer who went from junior hire to architecting Twitch's entire live-video backend. [work-at-a-startup-expo-2018 @ 03:48](https://youtu.be/cN62reafxLg?t=228)
- Working at a startup is a good gateway into eventually starting your own, since you end up around people who want to do what you want to do. [work-at-a-startup-expo-2018 @ 05:48](https://youtu.be/cN62reafxLg?t=348)
- The most important reason to join a startup is to maximize your rate of learning, echoing Paul Buchheit's line that "it's not your y-intercept, it's your slope that's important." [work-at-a-startup-expo-2018 @ 14:57](https://youtu.be/cN62reafxLg?t=897)
- Sam Altman frames picking a startup to work at as making an investment: you trade lower cash comp for equity, so evaluate it the way an investor would. [work-at-a-startup-expo-2018 @ 17:31](https://youtu.be/cN62reafxLg?t=1051)
- It's more important that a small number of users love the product than that a lot of users merely like it — a strong signal of product-market fit. [work-at-a-startup-expo-2018 @ 18:05](https://youtu.be/cN62reafxLg?t=1085)
- Trust exponential growth and momentum: if a startup is growing exponentially without faking it, that trajectory tends to keep going. [work-at-a-startup-expo-2018 @ 19:17](https://youtu.be/cN62reafxLg?t=1157)
- The size of a market today matters far less than its growth rate and how big it will be in ten years, as shown by the iPhone App Store and early Uber. [work-at-a-startup-expo-2018 @ 20:13](https://youtu.be/cN62reafxLg?t=1213)
- The biggest startups tend to emerge from technological platform shifts, such as the wave of companies that became possible after the iPhone App Store launched. [work-at-a-startup-expo-2018 @ 21:48](https://youtu.be/cN62reafxLg?t=1308)
- A key market inefficiency YC has exploited is ideas that sound bad but are actually good, since big companies' internal approval chains rationally kill them while a startup only needs one yes. [work-at-a-startup-expo-2018 @ 26:22](https://youtu.be/cN62reafxLg?t=1582)
- Most startups are not generous enough with employee equity relative to the value early employees create, and founders should demand to be treated fairly. [work-at-a-startup-expo-2018 @ 29:26](https://youtu.be/cN62reafxLg?t=1766)
