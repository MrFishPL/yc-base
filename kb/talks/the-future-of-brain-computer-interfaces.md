# The Future Of Brain-Computer Interfaces — Compiled Truth

Max Hodak (Neuralink co-founder, now founder of Science) argues BCI will become a broad category like pharma rather than one product, and reframes medicine around directly engineering the nervous system instead of drug discovery.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Max argues BCI won't be a single product; it will be a category, like pharma, with different modalities and probe types suited to different applications [the-future-of-brain-computer-interfaces @ 00:13](https://youtu.be/5gspRJVp9dI?t=13)
- Science's retinal implant (Prima) is a tiny 2mm chip placed under the retina that acts like a solar panel, restoring sight by bypassing dead rods and cones [the-future-of-brain-computer-interfaces @ 01:02](https://youtu.be/5gspRJVp9dI?t=62)
- Neuroplasticity has genuine critical periods early in life (adults blind from birth due to cataracts couldn't adapt to restored vision), yet the adult brain remains highly plastic under feedback [the-future-of-brain-computer-interfaces @ 06:08](https://youtu.be/5gspRJVp9dI?t=368)
- Max frames Science's mission as "neural engineering" versus drug discovery: humanity is bad at finding drugs but comparatively much better at directly engineering the nervous system [the-future-of-brain-computer-interfaces @ 13:28](https://youtu.be/5gspRJVp9dI?t=808)
- Science's clinical trial produced the first true "form vision" (a coherent image in the mind's eye) from a retinal implant, unlike earlier phosphene-only stimulators such as Second Sight [the-future-of-brain-computer-interfaces @ 16:11](https://youtu.be/5gspRJVp9dI?t=971)
- The key technical insight was stimulating the retina's bipolar cells (before compression) rather than the optic nerve's ganglion cells, which Second Sight targeted and which only produced meaningless flashes [the-future-of-brain-computer-interfaces @ 20:13](https://youtu.be/5gspRJVp9dI?t=1213)
- Science's biohybrid neural interface seeds implants with hypoimmunogenic stem-cell-derived neurons that grow and wire into the brain, avoiding one-way genetic modification of the patient's own neurons [the-future-of-brain-computer-interfaces @ 30:37](https://youtu.be/5gspRJVp9dI?t=1837)
- Neuralink's breakthrough was less a new scientific idea than "the ultimate startup PhD" in executing a complex multidisciplinary company, aided by the "smartphone dividend" of cheap, low-power, miniaturized electronics [the-future-of-brain-computer-interfaces @ 38:18](https://youtu.be/5gspRJVp9dI?t=2298)
- Max's advice to his younger self: know exactly what you want and pursue it with high agency, but also go work for someone like Elon early to learn "how the game is played" rather than always going it alone [the-future-of-brain-computer-interfaces @ 49:30](https://youtu.be/5gspRJVp9dI?t=2970)
- Max believes BCI and AI are twin, parallel revolutions, and that it's plausible the first people to live to 1,000 years old are alive today [the-future-of-brain-computer-interfaces @ 51:33](https://youtu.be/5gspRJVp9dI?t=3093)
