# Dalton Caldwell - All About Pivoting — Compiled Truth

Dalton Caldwell argues pivoting is just changing your idea, should feel lightweight in the early stages, and that most founders err by pivoting too slowly rather than too quickly.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A pivot simply means changing your idea, and early on you should be doing this constantly to find the right version of it [dalton-caldwell-all-about-pivoting @ 00:38](https://youtu.be/8pNxKX1SUGE?t=38)
- The core reason to pivot is opportunity cost: continuing to work on something not working means forgoing something that could work [dalton-caldwell-all-about-pivoting @ 01:51](https://youtu.be/8pNxKX1SUGE?t=111)
- Founders take too long to pivot because of loss aversion, politeness from users being mistaken for traction, and fear of admitting defeat [dalton-caldwell-all-about-pivoting @ 05:06](https://youtu.be/8pNxKX1SUGE?t=306)
- A little bit of traction is an "uncanny valley" trap that can capture talented founders for years longer than a total, obvious failure would [dalton-caldwell-all-about-pivoting @ 06:46](https://youtu.be/8pNxKX1SUGE?t=406)
- Taking several high-quality shots on goal at finding product-market fit makes it much easier to get lucky than taking only one [dalton-caldwell-all-about-pivoting @ 09:16](https://youtu.be/8pNxKX1SUGE?t=556)
- When choosing a new idea, pick something that excites you more (not less) and is easy to quickly build and validate rather than requiring years of R&D [dalton-caldwell-all-about-pivoting @ 10:10](https://youtu.be/8pNxKX1SUGE?t=610)
- A venture-fundable idea should plausibly generate hundreds of millions in annual revenue within 5-10 years, often via proprietary technology and high margins [dalton-caldwell-all-about-pivoting @ 13:09](https://youtu.be/8pNxKX1SUGE?t=789)
- Pivoting repeatedly causes founder whiplash, which is more deadly to a company than persisting on a mediocre but enjoyable idea [dalton-caldwell-all-about-pivoting @ 15:11](https://youtu.be/8pNxKX1SUGE?t=911)
- Adding employees while still uncertain about the idea only slows a team down, so scale up only after the idea is validated [dalton-caldwell-all-about-pivoting @ 16:18](https://youtu.be/8pNxKX1SUGE?t=978)
- A four-factor quality score (idea size, founder-market fit, ease of getting started, early market feedback) can be used to compare pre- and post-pivot ideas, illustrated with real YC pivots like Brex, Retool, Magic, and Segment [dalton-caldwell-all-about-pivoting @ 16:31](https://youtu.be/8pNxKX1SUGE?t=991)
