# David Zeevi on Personalized Nutrition Based on Your Gut Microbiome — Compiled Truth

David Zeevi explains how his research team proved that blood glucose response to identical foods varies wildly between people, then built a machine-learning predictor using microbiome, blood, and lifestyle data that outperforms carb-counting and now underlies the DayTwo personalized-nutrition product.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- One out of 10 Americans is diabetic, diabetes and its complications cost about $250 billion annually (2012), and nutrition is widely accepted as a major driver of the obesity and diabetes epidemic. [david-zeevi-on-personalized-nutrition-based-on-your-gut-micr @ 01:21](https://youtu.be/PE0W4kEpNHo?t=81)
- The team chose postprandial blood glucose response as their measurement metric because, unlike weight, it can be measured every 5 minutes per meal via a continuous glucose monitor rather than only tracked noisily over weeks. [david-zeevi-on-personalized-nutrition-based-on-your-gut-micr @ 05:35](https://youtu.be/PE0W4kEpNHo?t=335)
- Two people eating the identical standardized meal (e.g., white bread) can have completely different glucose spikes, while each individual's own response is highly reproducible day to day — proving food is not categorically good or bad. [david-zeevi-on-personalized-nutrition-based-on-your-gut-micr @ 09:20](https://youtu.be/PE0W4kEpNHo?t=560)
- The gut microbiome carries about 150 times more genes than the human genome (roughly 3 million genes), giving it huge metabolic potential that can be harnessed to affect health — and unlike genetics, it can be changed. [david-zeevi-on-personalized-nutrition-based-on-your-gut-micr @ 16:15](https://youtu.be/PE0W4kEpNHo?t=975)
- In a landmark study, transplanting an obese twin's gut microbiome into germ-free mice made the mice obese, while the lean twin's microbiome kept mice lean on identical diets, showing the microbiome actively drives health outcomes rather than just reflecting them. [david-zeevi-on-personalized-nutrition-based-on-your-gut-micr @ 20:21](https://youtu.be/PE0W4kEpNHo?t=1221)
- The team built a boosted-decision-tree model on ~800 people and more than 45,000 logged meals, combining microbiome composition, meal nutrients, blood parameters, and lifestyle data, lifting glucose-response prediction accuracy from R=0.38 (simple carb counting) to R=0.68. [david-zeevi-on-personalized-nutrition-based-on-your-gut-micr @ 31:44](https://youtu.be/PE0W4kEpNHo?t=1904)
- Tested on a new, held-out cohort of 100 people, the model generalized with an R of 0.7 — essentially matching the theoretical ceiling of a single person eating the same meal on two different days. [david-zeevi-on-personalized-nutrition-based-on-your-gut-micr @ 33:47](https://youtu.be/PE0W4kEpNHo?t=2027)
- In a follow-up intervention, mostly pre-diabetic participants who followed algorithm-personalized "good" and "bad" isocaloric weeks (in random, blinded order) saw glucose spikes normalize during the good week and worsen during the bad week, matching results from an expert-labeled gold standard. [david-zeevi-on-personalized-nutrition-based-on-your-gut-micr @ 53:19](https://youtu.be/PE0W4kEpNHo?t=3199)
- Because responses are so individualized, the team deliberately chose not to issue any universal dietary recommendations, since any one-size-fits-all advice would be suboptimal. [david-zeevi-on-personalized-nutrition-based-on-your-gut-micr @ 34:48](https://youtu.be/PE0W4kEpNHo?t=2088)
- The research has been commercialized by a company called DayTwo, which collects a microbiome sample and questionnaire and predicts whether a given food is good or bad for an individual. [david-zeevi-on-personalized-nutrition-based-on-your-gut-micr @ 28:00](https://youtu.be/PE0W4kEpNHo?t=1680)
