# Harj Taggar - How Startups can Compete with FAANG Companies when Hiring Employees — Compiled Truth

Harj Taggar explains that startups can beat big tech offers not with money but with speed, personalized attention, and a pitch around meaningful work that doesn't get cancelled.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Big tech companies like Facebook, Apple, or Google can take over a month from initial call to formal offer letter, while startups can move far faster [harj-taggar-how-startups-can-compete-with-faang-companies-wh @ 00:28](https://youtu.be/4dJ6IaP_0dM?t=28)
- At Triplebyte, Harj's team would call a standout candidate the same day, promise offer details by the next morning, and stay available to answer questions immediately [harj-taggar-how-startups-can-compete-with-faang-companies-wh @ 00:46](https://youtu.be/4dJ6IaP_0dM?t=46)
- Fast-moving offers confirm a candidate's fear that big companies are slow and bureaucratic, making them feel like "a cog in the wheel" [harj-taggar-how-startups-can-compete-with-faang-companies-wh @ 01:05](https://youtu.be/4dJ6IaP_0dM?t=65)
- Speed to offer is described as "low hanging fruit" — the first, easiest way to compete with FAANG companies [harj-taggar-how-startups-can-compete-with-faang-companies-wh @ 01:45](https://youtu.be/4dJ6IaP_0dM?t=105)
- The second tactic is individualized attention: everyone who interacted with the candidate should send a personalized follow-up email naming something they liked about that person [harj-taggar-how-startups-can-compete-with-faang-companies-wh @ 02:08](https://youtu.be/4dJ6IaP_0dM?t=128)
- This kind of personalized, multi-person follow-up doesn't happen when interviewing at Apple or Google, and signals a special culture where the candidate feels valued [harj-taggar-how-startups-can-compete-with-faang-companies-wh @ 02:28](https://youtu.be/4dJ6IaP_0dM?t=148)
- A third pitch point is that big companies often cancel projects people spent a year working on, leaving employees feeling they wasted their time and energy [harj-taggar-how-startups-can-compete-with-faang-companies-wh @ 02:46](https://youtu.be/4dJ6IaP_0dM?t=166)
- At a startup, everything worked on from day one directly impacts real users and customers, which is a more satisfying feeling than shelved big-company projects [harj-taggar-how-startups-can-compete-with-faang-companies-wh @ 03:05](https://youtu.be/4dJ6IaP_0dM?t=185)
- Putting the candidate on the phone with someone in your network who had a project cancelled at a big company can viscerally communicate how painful that experience is [harj-taggar-how-startups-can-compete-with-faang-companies-wh @ 03:27](https://youtu.be/4dJ6IaP_0dM?t=207)
