# Tom Preston Werner at Startup School 2012 — Compiled Truth

Tom Preston-Werner argues that a company is only its people's decisions, that decisions produce products, and that decisions should flow from an explicit philosophy — so people, product, and philosophy are inseparable and none alone is "the only thing that matters."

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A company is nothing but the decisions its people make, so the people you assemble are what actually matters [tom-preston-werner-at-startup-school-2012 @ 01:58](https://youtu.be/mGTpU5XUAA8?t=118)
- GitHub's earliest signal of real traction was users asking to pay before there was even a billing system [tom-preston-werner-at-startup-school-2012 @ 03:33](https://youtu.be/mGTpU5XUAA8?t=213)
- Hiring career executives too early can hurt a startup because deep experience can blind people to unconventional solutions; beginner's mind matters [tom-preston-werner-at-startup-school-2012 @ 05:57](https://youtu.be/mGTpU5XUAA8?t=357)
- Having worked at bad companies before is valuable because seeing wrong examples teaches founders what problems are worth solving [tom-preston-werner-at-startup-school-2012 @ 06:39](https://youtu.be/mGTpU5XUAA8?t=399)
- In the early days, titles like CEO/CTO/CFO don't matter and should be thrown away since there's too much to do to specialize [tom-preston-werner-at-startup-school-2012 @ 09:54](https://youtu.be/mGTpU5XUAA8?t=594)
- GitHub's four founders each represented a distinct personality vector (logical/pragmatic, product visionary, business operations, culture) that checked and balanced each other in decisions [tom-preston-werner-at-startup-school-2012 @ 10:29](https://youtu.be/mGTpU5XUAA8?t=629)
- Product is what customers actually experience, and design should exclude as much as it includes — "focus over features," since every added feature dilutes the whole product [tom-preston-werner-at-startup-school-2012 @ 13:13](https://youtu.be/mGTpU5XUAA8?t=793)
- A company's mission should be statable in under 10 words, and can broaden over time as the company matures — GitHub went from "hosting no longer a pain in the ass" to "make it easier to work together than to work alone" [tom-preston-werner-at-startup-school-2012 @ 14:54](https://youtu.be/mGTpU5XUAA8?t=894)
- GitHub never set out to create a culture — it emerged from the people hired, and was later codified into five values: optimizing for happiness, best argument wins, first principles, creating super fans, and being awesome [tom-preston-werner-at-startup-school-2012 @ 21:15](https://youtu.be/mGTpU5XUAA8?t=1275)
- A strong, well-communicated culture becomes an effective hiring tool that attracts people who naturally fit the company's philosophy [tom-preston-werner-at-startup-school-2012 @ 24:25](https://youtu.be/mGTpU5XUAA8?t=1465)
