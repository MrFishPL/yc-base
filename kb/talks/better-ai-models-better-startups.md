# Better AI Models, Better Startups — Compiled Truth

YC Group Partners discuss how the GPT-4o and Gemini 1.5 releases change the calculus for AI startups, arguing that more capable, more numerous models are good for startups as long as no single provider has a runaway lead.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- If only one company has the most powerful model by a wide margin that's bad for startups, but multiple equivalently powerful models make startups much safer since they aren't locked into one vendor's API [better-ai-models-better-startups @ 04:25](https://youtu.be/4aMQPG9gPoM?t=265)
- GPT-4o is architecturally still primarily the GPT-4 text transformer with bolted-on modules for speech (from Whisper) and image generation (from DALL-E), rather than a reasoning leap [better-ai-models-better-startups @ 05:06](https://youtu.be/4aMQPG9gPoM?t=306)
- Gemini 1.5 is a true mixture-of-experts model trained from the ground up across data types, giving it a 1-10 million token context window, which is technically more novel than GPT-4o even though its demo captured less attention [better-ai-models-better-startups @ 06:41](https://youtu.be/4aMQPG9gPoM?t=401)
- Despite massive context windows, RAG infrastructure will remain necessary for data privacy, permissioning, fine-tuning on proprietary data, and precise retrieval, especially in enterprise use cases [better-ai-models-better-startups @ 11:48](https://youtu.be/4aMQPG9gPoM?t=708)
- A useful historical lesson from the Google era: startups that competed head-on with Google's core product got crushed, but vertical search engines like Redfin, Zillow, and Kayak survived by monetizing differently and integrating proprietary data sources Google couldn't easily replicate [better-ai-models-better-startups @ 19:02](https://youtu.be/4aMQPG9gPoM?t=1142)
- A large, underexploited opportunity is that big consumer-focused AI labs like Google and OpenAI have historically never built B2B products, leaving that whole category open to startups [better-ai-models-better-startups @ 27:48](https://youtu.be/4aMQPG9gPoM?t=1668)
- The safest bet for founders is to ask whether what they're building is likely to appear in the next big model release; if it's not (e.g., filing construction permits), it's a durable niche rather than roadkill [better-ai-models-better-startups @ 24:21](https://youtu.be/4aMQPG9gPoM?t=1461)
- As underlying models get better, B2B software companies can pass improvements along as upsell or premium features, driving rapid revenue growth without needing new customers [better-ai-models-better-startups @ 30:36](https://youtu.be/4aMQPG9gPoM?t=1836)
- Products carrying legal or PR risk (deepfakes, AI companions) are hard for large incumbents to touch, which is exactly the kind of edgy space that creates startup opportunity, as OpenAI itself benefited from when Google avoided releasing risky image-generation tech [better-ai-models-better-startups @ 34:01](https://youtu.be/4aMQPG9gPoM?t=2041)
- Founders should worry less about being killed by an OpenAI or Google release and more about competing startups building the best product on top of the same models [better-ai-models-better-startups @ 32:43](https://youtu.be/4aMQPG9gPoM?t=1963)
