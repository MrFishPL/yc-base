# YC SUS: Gustaf Alströmer and Eric Migicovsky discuss growth tactics — Compiled Truth

Gustaf Alströmer (YC partner, ex-Airbnb growth) and Eric Migicovsky answer founder questions on monetization, marketplaces, referrals, and scaling growth channels, arguing that sustainable growth requires knowing your unit economics before you scale.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Before scaling paid growth you need an ROI calculation showing how much money you make back per acquired user, or the spend is just an unsustainable, time-boxed experiment [yc-sus-gustaf-alstr-mer-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-growth-t @ 03:20](https://youtu.be/oog7n_1fR-o?t=200)
- Monetization models should align with how users get repeat value from the product rather than gating features, since a monetization model that hurts growth is a bad model [yc-sus-gustaf-alstr-mer-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-growth-t @ 04:33](https://youtu.be/oog7n_1fR-o?t=273)
- Every company needs baseline event analytics (Amplitude, Mixpanel, Segment, or Heap) from day one to understand onboarding, conversion, and retention flows; Google Analytics alone is not enough [yc-sus-gustaf-alstr-mer-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-growth-t @ 08:33](https://youtu.be/oog7n_1fR-o?t=513)
- In the early days founders should do things that don't scale — send emails from their own personal account and personally handle customer support instead of automating outreach [yc-sus-gustaf-alstr-mer-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-growth-t @ 10:01](https://youtu.be/oog7n_1fR-o?t=601)
- To kickstart a two-sided marketplace, artificially build a small curated supply first (e.g., 10 friends committing to drive) before going out to generate demand [yc-sus-gustaf-alstr-mer-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-growth-t @ 16:22](https://youtu.be/oog7n_1fR-o?t=982)
- Referral programs work best for products that already have word-of-mouth, that benefit from a friend's trust, and that have a regularly active user base; the incentive typically needs to be at least around $15 to matter to users [yc-sus-gustaf-alstr-mer-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-growth-t @ 21:41](https://youtu.be/oog7n_1fR-o?t=1301)
- There are only a handful of truly scalable growth channels — paid marketing, SEO, word of mouth, sales, and viral growth — and founders should identify which ones actually fit their product [yc-sus-gustaf-alstr-mer-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-growth-t @ 27:20](https://youtu.be/oog7n_1fR-o?t=1640)
- SEO works best for newer, less competitive keywords/questions rather than established high-competition queries that big incumbent teams already dominate [yc-sus-gustaf-alstr-mer-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-growth-t @ 39:18](https://youtu.be/oog7n_1fR-o?t=2358)
- If there is real demand for a product, even at the MVP stage, ask for a price immediately rather than assuming interest means people will pay [yc-sus-gustaf-alstr-mer-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-growth-t @ 45:02](https://youtu.be/oog7n_1fR-o?t=2702)
- The way to win as a B2B company is to apply consumer-grade growth tactics — like building virality into shareable documents — that traditional B2B competitors relying on case studies and events won't do [yc-sus-gustaf-alstr-mer-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-growth-t @ 54:20](https://youtu.be/oog7n_1fR-o?t=3260)
