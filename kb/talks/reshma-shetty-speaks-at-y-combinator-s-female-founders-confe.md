# Reshma Shetty Speaks at Y Combinator's Female Founders Conference 2016 — Compiled Truth

Ginkgo Bioworks co-founder Reshma Shetty tells the origin story of a biotech built on scrappy resourcefulness and a mission defined before there was any product, and closes with reflections on why diversity should be tackled early rather than deferred.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Ginkgo's founders gave themselves just one year to try to get the company off the ground, choosing the startup path because a graduate student "knows how to live cheap" [reshma-shetty-speaks-at-y-combinator-s-female-founders-confe @ 09:11](https://youtu.be/JoqKkXv6-gI?t=551)
- She argues the first step in starting a company is neither inventing tech, building a prototype, nor raising money — it's defining your mission [reshma-shetty-speaks-at-y-combinator-s-female-founders-confe @ 10:00](https://youtu.be/JoqKkXv6-gI?t=600)
- Ginkgo's mission — "to make biology easier to engineer" — was set before the company had any product, technology, revenue, or customers [reshma-shetty-speaks-at-y-combinator-s-female-founders-confe @ 11:15](https://youtu.be/JoqKkXv6-gI?t=675)
- They built their first biology lab for about $150,000 by scavenging secondhand equipment from biotech companies going out of business during the 2008 financial crisis, versus an industry norm of millions [reshma-shetty-speaks-at-y-combinator-s-female-founders-confe @ 13:36](https://youtu.be/JoqKkXv6-gI?t=816)
- For roughly five years they used the scientific method on business models — hypothesize, talk to customers, test — while government grants and contracts paid the bills [reshma-shetty-speaks-at-y-combinator-s-female-founders-confe @ 14:36](https://youtu.be/JoqKkXv6-gI?t=876)
- Ginkgo didn't feel it had product-market fit until signing its first customer in 2012, with multiple customers following in 2013 around "cultured ingredients" made from yeast [reshma-shetty-speaks-at-y-combinator-s-female-founders-confe @ 15:37](https://youtu.be/JoqKkXv6-gI?t=937)
- The company deliberately avoided raising venture money for its first five years until it had a clear picture of its market, customers, and traction, to avoid founder-investor strife over direction [reshma-shetty-speaks-at-y-combinator-s-female-founders-confe @ 17:06](https://youtu.be/JoqKkXv6-gI?t=1026)
- Ginkgo joined YC in 2014, then raised a $9 million Series A and later a $45 million Series B [reshma-shetty-speaks-at-y-combinator-s-female-founders-confe @ 17:59](https://youtu.be/JoqKkXv6-gI?t=1079)
- Female representation at Ginkgo sat around 27% pre-2015 and actually declined in job offers once hiring ramped up after the Series B, which Shetty found troubling [reshma-shetty-speaks-at-y-combinator-s-female-founders-confe @ 19:06](https://youtu.be/JoqKkXv6-gI?t=1146)
- Inspired by Moneyball, she reasons that underrepresented groups in tech are likely systematically undervalued, so building a company where they want to work could become a competitive edge [reshma-shetty-speaks-at-y-combinator-s-female-founders-confe @ 21:28](https://youtu.be/JoqKkXv6-gI?t=1288)
