# How Zepto Became India's Fastest Growing Startup — Compiled Truth

Zepto's founder Aadit Palicha describes how the company went from a WhatsApp grocery-delivery side project during the pandemic to a $5B unicorn by obsessing over customer retention, owning the full logistics stack, and surviving multiple near-death moments through radical candor and forced capital efficiency.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Incumbents built grocery delivery "supply chain backward" (2-4 hour windows optimized for logistics economics), while Zepto built "customer backward," starting from a 10-minute promise [how-zepto-became-india-s-fastest-growing-startup @ 01:17](https://youtu.be/aYK0H85E_oU?t=77)
- The founders personally commandeered a store and ran deliveries themselves to test whether owning the full customer experience, including delivery time, actually created delight and repeat usage [how-zepto-became-india-s-fastest-growing-startup @ 08:56](https://youtu.be/aYK0H85E_oU?t=536)
- Early on they had "no retention" in their pickup-and-drop model and seriously considered the business dead before pivoting to owning first-party logistics via dark stores [how-zepto-became-india-s-fastest-growing-startup @ 16:19](https://youtu.be/aYK0H85E_oU?t=979)
- YC partner Jared Friedman pushed them past anecdotal praise to test whether cohorts were actually repeating, which became their rigorous test for product-market fit [how-zepto-became-india-s-fastest-growing-startup @ 10:17](https://youtu.be/aYK0H85E_oU?t=617)
- Losing access to capital markets in March 2022, while competitors held 7x their cash, forced an "execute or die" efficiency discipline that later let them scale profitably [how-zepto-became-india-s-fastest-growing-startup @ 12:29](https://youtu.be/aYK0H85E_oU?t=749)
- The real product-market-fit signal came from a single Bandra dark-store experiment that outgrew the rest of the city and produced visibly delighted repeat customers [how-zepto-became-india-s-fastest-growing-startup @ 21:29](https://youtu.be/aYK0H85E_oU?t=1289)
- Palicha treats PMF as dynamic, not static — the team keeps adding new categories (Zepto Cafe, electronics, an in-house ad stack) based on what users search for but can't yet get [how-zepto-became-india-s-fastest-growing-startup @ 29:28](https://youtu.be/aYK0H85E_oU?t=1768)
- The biggest roadblocks were people and hiring mistakes, not strategy — wrong hires and wrong people-management calls in finance, marketing, and ops were the costliest errors, especially in the existential 2022-2023 period [how-zepto-became-india-s-fastest-growing-startup @ 14:32](https://youtu.be/aYK0H85E_oU?t=872)
- Palicha's advice to his younger self: build for the love of building, not for money or fame, because that intrinsic motivation is what carries founders through repeated near-death moments [how-zepto-became-india-s-fastest-growing-startup @ 36:53](https://youtu.be/aYK0H85E_oU?t=2213)
