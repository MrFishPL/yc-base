# How To Find A Co-Founder | Startup School — Compiled Truth

Harj Taggar explains why almost every founder needs a co-founder, what to look for in one, where to find them, and how to avoid the co-founder breakups that are the number one cause of startup failure.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A co-founder is needed for three reasons: more high-quality work gets done, emotional support through the startup rollercoaster, and pattern-matching since nearly all famous successful startups had co-founding teams [how-to-find-a-co-founder-startup-school @ 00:26](https://youtu.be/Fk9BCr5pLTU?t=26)
- Going solo is only advisable in the roughly 10% of cases where you have a specific idea you're an expert on and you're technical enough to build an MVP alone, as Drew Houston did with Dropbox before adding Arash Ferdowsi [how-to-find-a-co-founder-startup-school @ 03:13](https://youtu.be/Fk9BCr5pLTU?t=193)
- The single most important trait in a co-founder is how well they handle stress, best judged by having already worked with them under real stress, which is why failed co-founder relationships are YC's number one cause of startup failure [how-to-find-a-co-founder-startup-school @ 04:32](https://youtu.be/Fk9BCr5pLTU?t=272)
- Co-founders must share the same high-level goals for the company (e.g. hypergrowth vs. lifestyle business), since mismatched motivations eventually fracture even skill-complementary teams [how-to-find-a-co-founder-startup-school @ 06:08](https://youtu.be/Fk9BCr5pLTU?t=368)
- Don't overly fixate on a co-founder's current specific technical skills; what matters more is whether they are smart and can learn and adapt as the startup grows [how-to-find-a-co-founder-startup-school @ 07:33](https://youtu.be/Fk9BCr5pLTU?t=453)
- Never assume people you know aren't available as co-founders; always make the direct ask, and if they decline, ask them for an introduction to who they would pick as their own co-founder [how-to-find-a-co-founder-startup-school @ 09:47](https://youtu.be/Fk9BCr5pLTU?t=587)
- On the YC co-founder matching platform, the best pairings are people who likely would have met anyway (similar age, background, interests), not pairs with nothing in common [how-to-find-a-co-founder-startup-school @ 12:04](https://youtu.be/Fk9BCr5pLTU?t=724)
- Default advice is to split equity equally between co-founders, since differences in early contribution become insignificant over a company's 10-year life [how-to-find-a-co-founder-startup-school @ 14:14](https://youtu.be/Fk9BCr5pLTU?t=854)
- The most common causes of co-founder breakups are eroding mutual respect over perceived performance, competing desires to be CEO, and mismatched work-ethic expectations [how-to-find-a-co-founder-startup-school @ 15:15](https://youtu.be/Fk9BCr5pLTU?t=915)
- To avoid slow-building resentment, don't delay hard conversations; schedule regular co-founder one-on-ones to release relationship pressure over time [how-to-find-a-co-founder-startup-school @ 18:02](https://youtu.be/Fk9BCr5pLTU?t=1082)
