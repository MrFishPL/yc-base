# Jessica Livingston Shares 9 Things She Learned From Founding YC — Compiled Truth

Jessica Livingston tells her personal story as YC's only non-technical cofounder and distills nine lessons for founders about playing to unconventional strengths rather than fitting a mold.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- YC began the same way as most startups, with a hypothesis about what people wanted, and has since funded more than 1,800 startups worth over $100 billion [jessica-livingston-shares-9-things-she-learned-from-founding @ 00:56](https://youtu.be/8d-cApFHjeY?t=56)
- YC's founding bet was to give small amounts of money to large numbers of early-stage startups and help them heavily, rather than large checks to a few established companies, initially targeting programmers who could handle the technical side but were clueless about everything else [jessica-livingston-shares-9-things-she-learned-from-founding @ 08:55](https://youtu.be/8d-cApFHjeY?t=535)
- YC recognized the power of funding in batches almost immediately — it gave founders peers during an otherwise lonely process and let YC help many startups efficiently at once [jessica-livingston-shares-9-things-she-learned-from-founding @ 10:11](https://youtu.be/8d-cApFHjeY?t=611)
- YC deliberately moved its next batch to Silicon Valley itself, knowing others would copy the model and not wanting a copycat to become "the YC of Silicon Valley" [jessica-livingston-shares-9-things-she-learned-from-founding @ 11:44](https://youtu.be/8d-cApFHjeY?t=704)
- As the only non-technical cofounder, Livingston's "social radar" let her judge applicants' earnestness, determination, flexibility, and cofounder relationships — qualities her technical cofounders couldn't evaluate [jessica-livingston-shares-9-things-she-learned-from-founding @ 14:26](https://youtu.be/8d-cApFHjeY?t=866)
- Because YC had no LPs and wasn't driven by money, it could take more risk on who to fund and be benevolent toward failing startups, even when that put YC in conflict with other investors [jessica-livingston-shares-9-things-she-learned-from-founding @ 19:36](https://youtu.be/8d-cApFHjeY?t=1176)
- The most successful startups, including YC itself, tend to grow organically out of the founders' own lives rather than fitting a conventional mold [jessica-livingston-shares-9-things-she-learned-from-founding @ 20:47](https://youtu.be/8d-cApFHjeY?t=1247)
- Her nine lessons include: there's no single successful founder mold, focus on making something people want, don't let rejection hold you back, start small to stay nimble, and it's fine not to have gone to an elite college [jessica-livingston-shares-9-things-she-learned-from-founding @ 23:45](https://youtu.be/8d-cApFHjeY?t=1425)
- Find a cofounder with complementary skills but the same moral compass — she and Paul Graham agreed on the big questions and deferred to each other's expertise on the small ones [jessica-livingston-shares-9-things-she-learned-from-founding @ 24:39](https://youtu.be/8d-cApFHjeY?t=1479)
