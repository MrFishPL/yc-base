# Does Your Startup Need To Be In San Francisco? — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell debate lifestyle preferences about where to live but agree that founders aiming for the very top of their field should be in the Bay Area to capture its network effects.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Many of the biggest tech companies (Google, Facebook, Apple) actually have headquarters outside San Francisco proper, so claiming every company must be in the city is factually wrong [does-your-startup-need-to-be-in-san-francisco @ 02:02](https://youtu.be/ZqGOCF4O6ks?t=122).
- Michael argues a young startup founder benefits from city life, including the motivating "chip on the shoulder" of seeing success he couldn't yet afford [does-your-startup-need-to-be-in-san-francisco @ 03:29](https://youtu.be/ZqGOCF4O6ks?t=209).
- Both agree they would not recommend starting a startup outside the Bay Area, even though they personally choose to live differently within it [does-your-startup-need-to-be-in-san-francisco @ 04:51](https://youtu.be/ZqGOCF4O6ks?t=291).
- Certain cities are simply better for certain industries — finance clusters in New York, entertainment in LA — because being top 0.01% requires being around other people aiming for the same [does-your-startup-need-to-be-in-san-francisco @ 05:51](https://youtu.be/ZqGOCF4O6ks?t=351).
- Politicians courting startups to their cities are really after tech jobs and don't realize a "startup friendly" culture alone won't replace the network effect of an established hub [does-your-startup-need-to-be-in-san-francisco @ 07:36](https://youtu.be/ZqGOCF4O6ks?t=456).
- Network effects are hard to notice from the outside — people who leave the Bay Area often don't realize what they're missing until they experience the gap [does-your-startup-need-to-be-in-san-francisco @ 08:34](https://youtu.be/ZqGOCF4O6ks?t=514).
- Founders who left during COVID largely returned, admitting they had been unknowingly "consuming" a community of startup-focused people that wasn't replicated in cities like Seattle or Austin [does-your-startup-need-to-be-in-san-francisco @ 10:02](https://youtu.be/ZqGOCF4O6ks?t=602).
- Founders should aim for greatness rather than just "being in the game," similar to how an athlete must be the best on every team along the way to reach the NBA [does-your-startup-need-to-be-in-san-francisco @ 11:18](https://youtu.be/ZqGOCF4O6ks?t=678).
- Anecdotes of successful startups built outside the Bay Area (e.g., MongoDB in New York) are true but the advice is about improving statistical odds of success, not guaranteeing individual outcomes [does-your-startup-need-to-be-in-san-francisco @ 12:35](https://youtu.be/ZqGOCF4O6ks?t=755).
- Choosing to move to the Bay Area is described as an easy, low-cost decision that meaningfully multiplies a founder's odds of success compared to the many harder choices ahead [does-your-startup-need-to-be-in-san-francisco @ 14:06](https://youtu.be/ZqGOCF4O6ks?t=846).
