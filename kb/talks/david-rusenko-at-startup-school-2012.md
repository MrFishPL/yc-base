# David Rusenko at Startup School 2012 — Compiled Truth

Weebly's founder walks through six-plus years of signup and bank-account graphs to show that the company looked like a failure for nearly two years before real traction appeared, arguing that persistence through long flat periods is what separates eventual big companies from those that quit early.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Weebly started in 2006 as a Penn State class project after the founders kept seeing people struggle to build a website [david-rusenko-at-startup-school-2012 @ 01:47](https://youtu.be/ZOeyYAfKXLI?t=107).
- Six months after writing the first line of code, Weebly's best signup day brought only 12 new users, mostly friends and family [david-rusenko-at-startup-school-2012 @ 02:06](https://youtu.be/ZOeyYAfKXLI?t=126).
- The founders applied to YC with less than an hour left before the deadline, on a whim, without even asking their co-founders first [david-rusenko-at-startup-school-2012 @ 03:13](https://youtu.be/ZOeyYAfKXLI?t=193).
- Paul Graham's acceptance call opened with "I'm calling to end your college career," prompting all three founders to drop out and move to San Francisco [david-rusenko-at-startup-school-2012 @ 05:16](https://youtu.be/ZOeyYAfKXLI?t=316).
- By April 2007, 14 months in, the team had less than $100 in the bank and was considering moving to San Jose to cut rent before closing a $650K angel round just in time [david-rusenko-at-startup-school-2012 @ 08:26](https://youtu.be/ZOeyYAfKXLI?t=506).
- Despite being featured in TechCrunch, Newsweek, and Time, signups kept declining for a year and a half, showing that press coverage produced only temporary spikes, not sustained growth [david-rusenko-at-startup-school-2012 @ 10:31](https://youtu.be/ZOeyYAfKXLI?t=631).
- Real organic, word-of-mouth traction only began 20 months after starting, finally showing linear day-over-day growth in new signups [david-rusenko-at-startup-school-2012 @ 11:09](https://youtu.be/ZOeyYAfKXLI?t=669).
- In fall 2008, facing a cash crunch, the team mapped out every bill by due date and prioritized paying whichever ones would get their servers shut off over ones that merely incurred late fees [david-rusenko-at-startup-school-2012 @ 13:44](https://youtu.be/ZOeyYAfKXLI?t=824).
- Weebly finally hit break-even and a working business model in January 2009, 35 months (about three years) after the team started [david-rusenko-at-startup-school-2012 @ 14:26](https://youtu.be/ZOeyYAfKXLI?t=866).
- Weebly's net promoter score is over 80%, compared to roughly 67% for Apple, which the founder credits as explaining the whole business [david-rusenko-at-startup-school-2012 @ 15:39](https://youtu.be/ZOeyYAfKXLI?t=939).
- The core takeaway: you can't succeed if you quit, and building something truly big and meaningful takes 7 to 10 years of persistence through periods that don't look like they're working [david-rusenko-at-startup-school-2012 @ 18:13](https://youtu.be/ZOeyYAfKXLI?t=1093).
