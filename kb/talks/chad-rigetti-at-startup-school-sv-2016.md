# Chad Rigetti at Startup School SV 2016 — Compiled Truth

Chad Rigetti, founder of Rigetti Computing, explains why hard tech startups face a unique risk (fundamental possibility, not just execution/market risk) and what it takes to build and lead an organization racing to build a quantum computer.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Rigetti joined YC in summer 2014 with no working qubits, and less than two years later had built a 5-qubit quantum computer with a team of ~35 people [chad-rigetti-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 02:39](https://youtu.be/GzMvG8UO6Eg?t=159)
- Quantum computers unlock two broad classes of high-impact applications: quantum chemistry (better drugs, materials, catalysts for capturing carbon) and machine learning/AI (fundamentally more powerful forms of AI) [chad-rigetti-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 05:09](https://youtu.be/GzMvG8UO6Eg?t=309)
- Classical supercomputing is hitting limits from the end of Moore's Law and the diminishing returns described by Amdahl's Law, since massively parallel machines only speed up problems that parallelize [chad-rigetti-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 07:35](https://youtu.be/GzMvG8UO6Eg?t=455)
- A single chip with 60-70 qubits would be more powerful than a half-a-football-field-sized classical supercomputer like Tianhe-2 [chad-rigetti-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 09:20](https://youtu.be/GzMvG8UO6Eg?t=560)
- A hard tech company is distinguished from a regular tech company by having to answer fundamental questions of possibility on top of all the normal tech/execution/market risk [chad-rigetti-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 13:59](https://youtu.be/GzMvG8UO6Eg?t=839)
- Succeeding at a hard tech company creates monumental leverage and defensibility, comparable to the Manhattan Project or the Apollo missions [chad-rigetti-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 14:47](https://youtu.be/GzMvG8UO6Eg?t=887)
- The defensibility and passion hard tech provides pulls existing tech companies (Uber into self-driving, Google, Microsoft) to push into hard tech themselves [chad-rigetti-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 16:42](https://youtu.be/GzMvG8UO6Eg?t=1002)
- Founders should craft a clear, tangible mission ("build the world's most powerful computer to solve humanity's most important problems") because how you talk about the mission is a long-term organizational weapon [chad-rigetti-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 18:14](https://youtu.be/GzMvG8UO6Eg?t=1094)
- Founders should hire people who "pump entropy out" of the vision and the organization — people who create order, build systems, and reinforce them, rather than people who generate entropy [chad-rigetti-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 22:16](https://youtu.be/GzMvG8UO6Eg?t=1336)
