# Avni Patel Thompson at the Seattle Female Founders Conference — Compiled Truth

Poppy founder Avni Patel Thompson breaks founding into two phases — "get going" (validate the idea cheaply and fast) and "get growing" (build a durable, scalable company) — and shares the hard lessons from each.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Her prior company, a subscription e-commerce startup, failed because customer acquisition cost never lined up with lifetime value, a mismatch she learned the hard way [avni-patel-thompson-at-the-seattle-female-founders-conferenc @ 07:52](https://youtu.be/Octm_7llbGA?t=472)
- For Poppy she gave herself a bounded four-week, $200 test using only off-the-shelf tools (SMS, manual vetting) to see if people wanted the solution before building any tech [avni-patel-thompson-at-the-seattle-female-founders-conferenc @ 12:07](https://youtu.be/Octm_7llbGA?t=727)
- She held herself to a strict discipline of 10% week-over-week growth off a baseline of four bookings, which compounded quickly and built confidence after her prior failure [avni-patel-thompson-at-the-seattle-female-founders-conferenc @ 13:10](https://youtu.be/Octm_7llbGA?t=790)
- She refused to outsource engineering to a dev shop or contractor, insisting a true tech company needs a technical co-founder at the table pushing on ideas, even though it took three to four months to find one [avni-patel-thompson-at-the-seattle-female-founders-conferenc @ 16:05](https://youtu.be/Octm_7llbGA?t=965)
- Demonstrated growth (not just talk) is what convinced her technical co-founder to join and later convinced YC and investors to back the company [avni-patel-thompson-at-the-seattle-female-founders-conferenc @ 18:40](https://youtu.be/Octm_7llbGA?t=1120)
- Her best hire, a head of ops, came from a customer who cold-emailed her out of the blue — she learned to hire for mission passion and resourcefulness over pedigree or prior experience [avni-patel-thompson-at-the-seattle-female-founders-conferenc @ 20:41](https://youtu.be/Octm_7llbGA?t=1241)
- Switching from a membership pricing model to taking a cut of the rental transaction was necessary for sustainability but blindsided early users who felt ownership of the product, teaching her to bring users along with transparency [avni-patel-thompson-at-the-seattle-female-founders-conferenc @ 22:13](https://youtu.be/Octm_7llbGA?t=1333)
- In the "get growing" phase she started leaning on trusted mentors to hold her accountable and began sending investor updates, something she wished she'd started before YC [avni-patel-thompson-at-the-seattle-female-founders-conferenc @ 23:46](https://youtu.be/Octm_7llbGA?t=1426)
- She treats self-care (yoga) as essential founder infrastructure, since burning out as a founder is as damaging as burning out as a parent [avni-patel-thompson-at-the-seattle-female-founders-conferenc @ 26:30](https://youtu.be/Octm_7llbGA?t=1590)
- Frames founding as two distinct stages — validate-and-get-going, then get-growing toward product-market fit and scale — each requiring a different founder mindset [avni-patel-thompson-at-the-seattle-female-founders-conferenc @ 04:07](https://youtu.be/Octm_7llbGA?t=247)
