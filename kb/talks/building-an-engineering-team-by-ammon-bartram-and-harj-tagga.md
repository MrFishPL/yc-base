# Building an Engineering Team by Ammon Bartram and Harj Taggar — Compiled Truth

Triplebyte co-founders Harj Taggar and Ammon Bartram lay out a practical funnel for sourcing, screening, and closing engineering hires, backed by Triplebyte's data on interview noise and success rates.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Personal networks are the best source for early hires: make an exhaustive list of every good engineer you know, ask each one directly if they'd join, and if they say no ask who else they'd recommend [building-an-engineering-team-by-ammon-bartram-and-harj-tagga @ 06:28](https://youtu.be/AZidfpz9KfY?t=388)
- Wait until you've made your first engineering hire yourself before bringing on a recruiter, and don't hire a recruiter until you have a cadence of roughly one engineer a month or are spending over 50% of your time on hiring [building-an-engineering-team-by-ammon-bartram-and-harj-tagga @ 17:20](https://youtu.be/AZidfpz9KfY?t=1040)
- Interviews are surprisingly noisy: Triplebyte measured inter-rater reliability among interviewers at about 0.1, similar to how much Netflix viewers agree on movie ratings [building-an-engineering-team-by-ammon-bartram-and-harj-tagga @ 27:09](https://youtu.be/AZidfpz9KfY?t=1629)
- Decide explicitly which skills matter for your company (fast vs. careful, product vs. hard-tech-motivated, specific tech stack vs. general smarts) before interviewing, or your interviewers will substitute their own biased criteria [building-an-engineering-team-by-ammon-bartram-and-harj-tagga @ 29:45](https://youtu.be/AZidfpz9KfY?t=1785)
- Use structured interviews with the same questions and defined evaluation criteria for every candidate, and route decisions through one centralized decision-maker rather than letting each interviewer make a global yes/no call [building-an-engineering-team-by-ammon-bartram-and-harj-tagga @ 32:12](https://youtu.be/AZidfpz9KfY?t=1932)
- Good interview questions have a gradual multi-step ramp (not a single "leap of insight" fact), avoid specialized/niche knowledge, and should take about a third of the interview's time when the interviewer solves them [building-an-engineering-team-by-ammon-bartram-and-harj-tagga @ 35:23](https://youtu.be/AZidfpz9KfY?t=2123)
- Hide credentials (school, past employer) from interviewers during technical evaluation since they bias graders, and factor credentials back in only at the final decision stage [building-an-engineering-team-by-ammon-bartram-and-harj-tagga @ 39:45](https://youtu.be/AZidfpz9KfY?t=2385)
- For the earliest hires, prioritize productivity and ownership over code quality — get someone who can figure out what to build and ship it, even messily [building-an-engineering-team-by-ammon-bartram-and-harj-tagga @ 48:57](https://youtu.be/AZidfpz9KfY?t=2937)
- Startups have a speed advantage over big companies in closing candidates — respond fast at every step, since big companies can take weeks to deliver final offer details [building-an-engineering-team-by-ammon-bartram-and-harj-tagga @ 53:36](https://youtu.be/AZidfpz9KfY?t=3216)
- Never leave a candidate to negotiate against a hypothetical — present full, transparent offer details (salary, equity, exercise price) rather than asking "how excited are you" before showing numbers [building-an-engineering-team-by-ammon-bartram-and-harj-tagga @ 57:39](https://youtu.be/AZidfpz9KfY?t=3459)
- To compete with big tech offers, emphasize real decision-making responsibility, faster career progression, and that the specific startup opportunity is unique and won't come again, unlike generic big-company jobs [building-an-engineering-team-by-ammon-bartram-and-harj-tagga @ 59:34](https://youtu.be/AZidfpz9KfY?t=3574)
