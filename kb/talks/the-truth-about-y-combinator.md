# The Truth About Y Combinator — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell debrief on a just-finished batch's feedback, correcting misconceptions about what YC actually is, who should apply, and how fundraising really works once you're a YC company.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Many founders didn't realize before applying that YC includes follow-on investing and help raising a Series A or scaling beyond the three-month program [the-truth-about-y-combinator @ 00:47](https://youtu.be/DOez-RwJ7mg?t=47)
- Founders often didn't know YC assigns a group partner and puts companies into small groups and sections until they were already in the batch [the-truth-about-y-combinator @ 01:06](https://youtu.be/DOez-RwJ7mg?t=66)
- Getting into YC grants access to a network and resources in a way the hosts compare, half-jokingly, to the privileges of being "made" in the mafia [the-truth-about-y-combinator @ 04:53](https://youtu.be/DOez-RwJ7mg?t=293)
- A common misconception is that investors on your cap table are what create product-market fit; in reality founders do all the work and YC's value is the peer environment and resources, not doing the work for them [the-truth-about-y-combinator @ 07:17](https://youtu.be/DOez-RwJ7mg?t=437)
- It's a myth that you need significant traction to apply: roughly 40% of the last batch came in with just an idea, and most applicants were still working day jobs [the-truth-about-y-combinator @ 09:20](https://youtu.be/DOez-RwJ7mg?t=560)
- YC would rather fund a brand-new founder with a clean cap table than one who already raised a pre-seed, gave away 30%, went through a bad co-founder split, and burned half a million dollars [the-truth-about-y-combinator @ 10:02](https://youtu.be/DOez-RwJ7mg?t=602)
- Despite fears of a summer 2022 VC slowdown, batch valuations held steady at roughly $15-25M, with some companies raising at $30M, and no change in deal speed or terms [the-truth-about-y-combinator @ 13:03](https://youtu.be/DOez-RwJ7mg?t=783)
- The YC fundraising ecosystem functions like an auction where founders can name their price to investors, unlike typical early-stage fundraising where founders are "begging for money" [the-truth-about-y-combinator @ 15:18](https://youtu.be/DOez-RwJ7mg?t=918)
- A typical batch company received between 10 and 60-70 inbound cold emails from investors wanting to invest, which stunned founders who previously had to chase investors [the-truth-about-y-combinator @ 16:03](https://youtu.be/DOez-RwJ7mg?t=963)
- A pre-YC founder took a large early check that bought over a third of the company, signaling to later investors that the founders would be diluted too much, and the company entered YC with almost 50% dilution [the-truth-about-y-combinator @ 21:23](https://youtu.be/DOez-RwJ7mg?t=1283)
- Pre-YC horror stories included investors demanding the founder pay $50k of the investor's legal fees for a priced round, or committing money only conditionally as "last money in" [the-truth-about-y-combinator @ 22:46](https://youtu.be/DOez-RwJ7mg?t=1366)
