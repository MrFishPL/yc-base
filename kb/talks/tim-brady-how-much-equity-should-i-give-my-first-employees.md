# Tim Brady - How Much Equity Should I Give My First Employees? — Compiled Truth

Tim Brady lays out rules of thumb for allocating equity to early employees, since there is no exact formula, only judgment calls based on risk, effort, and cash position.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- There's no chart to look up the right equity number; it's more art than science, so founders need rules of thumb instead [tim-brady-how-much-equity-should-i-give-my-first-employees @ 00:09](https://youtu.be/B2QdN0-fAbw?t=9)
- Early employees should get more equity than later ones because they take on bigger risk and put in more grueling effort in an uncertain, hectic environment [tim-brady-how-much-equity-should-i-give-my-first-employees @ 00:26](https://youtu.be/B2QdN0-fAbw?t=26)
- Startups traditionally reserve 10-20% of equity for an employee incentive pool, which VCs will insist on creating before investing [tim-brady-how-much-equity-should-i-give-my-first-employees @ 01:11](https://youtu.be/B2QdN0-fAbw?t=71)
- An outside CEO typically gets roughly 5% of the company, while an outside CTO or COO would get roughly 3% [tim-brady-how-much-equity-should-i-give-my-first-employees @ 01:54](https://youtu.be/B2QdN0-fAbw?t=114)
- Before distributing any equity, founders should plan out how many people they'll need to hire and how much equity that will require [tim-brady-how-much-equity-should-i-give-my-first-employees @ 01:54](https://youtu.be/B2QdN0-fAbw?t=114)
- The first employee, typically an engineer at a traditional Silicon Valley startup, traditionally gets between 1-2%, with online ranges spanning 0.5% to 3% [tim-brady-how-much-equity-should-i-give-my-first-employees @ 02:17](https://youtu.be/B2QdN0-fAbw?t=137)
- Founders with limited cash on hand can offer less salary in exchange for more equity, though not every candidate can make that trade [tim-brady-how-much-equity-should-i-give-my-first-employees @ 02:41](https://youtu.be/B2QdN0-fAbw?t=161)
- How much a candidate personally values equity versus salary certainty should shape the mix offered to keep them motivated [tim-brady-how-much-equity-should-i-give-my-first-employees @ 03:01](https://youtu.be/B2QdN0-fAbw?t=181)
- Since most startups fail and only a few become big successes, equity should be seen as a tool to increase the odds of success rather than a fixed pie to divide [tim-brady-how-much-equity-should-i-give-my-first-employees @ 03:25](https://youtu.be/B2QdN0-fAbw?t=205)
