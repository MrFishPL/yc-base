# How To Earn Customers For Life — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell argue that genuinely liking and caring about your customers, rather than treating them as a means to raise VC dollars, is an underrated competitive advantage for early-stage startups.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A flailing startup often tries to bully or cram a product down customers' throats instead of asking whether the founder actually likes their users [how-to-earn-customers-for-life @ 00:59](https://youtu.be/LJay5YW4fLA?t=59)
- A good gut check for outreach: would you personally respond to the email you're about to mass-send? [how-to-earn-customers-for-life @ 01:56](https://youtu.be/LJay5YW4fLA?t=116)
- Founders often treat serving customers as merely a means to get VC dollars rather than as the end goal itself [how-to-earn-customers-for-life @ 02:43](https://youtu.be/LJay5YW4fLA?t=163)
- Big companies like Comcast and Facebook are structurally built without liking their customers, and founders shouldn't emulate that end state [how-to-earn-customers-for-life @ 04:18](https://youtu.be/LJay5YW4fLA?t=258)
- Caring about customers is a startup's secret sauce and competitive advantage precisely because big companies can't replicate it [how-to-earn-customers-for-life @ 06:00](https://youtu.be/LJay5YW4fLA?t=360)
