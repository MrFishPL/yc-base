# Fundraising Fundamentals By Geoff Ralston — Compiled Truth

Geoff Ralston gives a high-level overview of startup fundraising: why VC exists, how much to raise, how to tell your story, and how to avoid the most common founder mistakes.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The whole fundraising loop in one minute: craft your story, find the right investors, pitch repeatedly and iterate, agree on price, get the money in the bank, then get back to work [fundraising-fundamentals-by-geoff-ralston @ 02:43](https://youtu.be/gcevHkNGrWQ?t=163)
- The best time to raise money is when you don't need it — investors can smell desperation, and having money itself becomes a competitive advantage [fundraising-fundamentals-by-geoff-ralston @ 06:19](https://youtu.be/gcevHkNGrWQ?t=379)
- Treat every raise as if it's the last one you'll get: raise enough (rule of thumb ~18 months of runway at seed) to hit milestones or reach profitability [fundraising-fundamentals-by-geoff-ralston @ 06:44](https://youtu.be/gcevHkNGrWQ?t=404)
- Choosing too high a valuation can outright kill a fundraise, because you can't credibly go back to a passed-on investor and lower your price later; choosing too low over-dilutes you, so the real priority is getting money in the bank, not optimizing valuation [fundraising-fundamentals-by-geoff-ralston @ 11:12](https://youtu.be/gcevHkNGrWQ?t=672)
- YC's standard instrument is now a post-money safe (e.g., $150,000 for 7%) instead of a pre-money safe, because it gives founders and investors a clear, unambiguous read on ownership percentage at the moment of investment [fundraising-fundamentals-by-geoff-ralston @ 16:45](https://youtu.be/gcevHkNGrWQ?t=1005)
- Typical dilution benchmarks: roughly 10-20% (up to ~30% is a caution flag) at seed, and 20-25% (sometimes 30%) at Series A, with later rounds too variable to generalize [fundraising-fundamentals-by-geoff-ralston @ 46:33](https://youtu.be/gcevHkNGrWQ?t=2793)
- Good traction just means usage of any kind (free or paid); what actually matters to investors is growth rate over time, not a static revenue number [fundraising-fundamentals-by-geoff-ralston @ 38:48](https://youtu.be/gcevHkNGrWQ?t=2328)
- Customer testimonials alone carry little weight; what matters is whether customers actually pay, since reference checks matter far more at later stages than at seed [fundraising-fundamentals-by-geoff-ralston @ 44:57](https://youtu.be/gcevHkNGrWQ?t=2697)
- Long-term (e.g., five-year) financial projections at seed are a red flag from either party — Ralston calls investors who demand them "noobs," since pre-product forecasts carry 50-100% error bars [fundraising-fundamentals-by-geoff-ralston @ 43:50](https://youtu.be/gcevHkNGrWQ?t=2630)
- Founders will hear "no" far more often than "yes" and must stay resilient and keep believing in the story, since surviving the process requires toughness regardless of how many times you're told the idea won't work [fundraising-fundamentals-by-geoff-ralston @ 01:33](https://youtu.be/gcevHkNGrWQ?t=93)
