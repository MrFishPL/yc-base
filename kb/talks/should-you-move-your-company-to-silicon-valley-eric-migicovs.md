# Should You Move Your Company to Silicon Valley? - Eric Migicovsky, Pebble Founder — Compiled Truth

Eric Migicovsky, who moved Pebble from Waterloo, Ontario to Silicon Valley after joining YC in 2011, weighs the concrete benefits (network, competitive pressure) against the costs (high expense) of basing a startup in the Bay Area.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The simplest reason to move to Silicon Valley is if your customers are there, since proximity lets you iterate faster on feedback [should-you-move-your-company-to-silicon-valley-eric-migicovs @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/Zj1WyGpSbrM?t=0)
- Silicon Valley's high-density tech network enables serendipitous connections, like meeting a fellow open-source creator at a coffee shop or getting a same-week lunch with an original Macintosh team member [should-you-move-your-company-to-silicon-valley-eric-migicovs @ 01:01](https://youtu.be/Zj1WyGpSbrM?t=61)
- That network proved critical at an inflection point: the night Pebble's Kickstarter took off, Migicovsky met a YC friend for burgers at 2am to chart their course forward [should-you-move-your-company-to-silicon-valley-eric-migicovs @ 01:43](https://youtu.be/Zj1WyGpSbrM?t=103)
- Being surrounded by other fast-moving, successful companies creates a valuable forcing function; Migicovsky felt he compressed three years of progress into six months after moving [should-you-move-your-company-to-silicon-valley-eric-migicovs @ 03:08](https://youtu.be/Zj1WyGpSbrM?t=188)
- Silicon Valley is expensive: rent jumped from $450/month in Waterloo to $3,000/month, and salaries at big tech companies make recruiting harder [should-you-move-your-company-to-silicon-valley-eric-migicovs @ 03:48](https://youtu.be/Zj1WyGpSbrM?t=228)
- One mitigation is to take a job at a big tech company in the Bay Area before starting a company, to save money and build a network to draw on later [should-you-move-your-company-to-silicon-valley-eric-migicovs @ 04:10](https://youtu.be/Zj1WyGpSbrM?t=250)
- You should probably stay where you are if your customers are already based in your region, especially at the early product-market-fit stage [should-you-move-your-company-to-silicon-valley-eric-migicovs @ 04:46](https://youtu.be/Zj1WyGpSbrM?t=286)
- Staying put also makes sense if you have a built-in unfair advantage, such as a direct connection to a research lab commercializing the technology or to a strong, lower-cost hiring pool [should-you-move-your-company-to-silicon-valley-eric-migicovs @ 05:08](https://youtu.be/Zj1WyGpSbrM?t=308)
- YC's three-month program is designed so international founders can build a Bay Area network and raise a seed round without being required to relocate permanently [should-you-move-your-company-to-silicon-valley-eric-migicovs @ 06:05](https://youtu.be/Zj1WyGpSbrM?t=365)
