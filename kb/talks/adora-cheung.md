# Adora Cheung — Compiled Truth

Homejoy co-founder and CEO Adora Cheung recounts three years of failed ideas before landing on a home-cleaning marketplace, and the scrappy, do-things-that-don't-scale grind that got it off the ground and funded.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Finding a great startup idea is like searching for a needle in a haystack — most founders must patiently sift through many bad ideas one by one rather than instantly spotting the right one [adora-cheung @ 04:27](https://youtu.be/lVDmyRxeGCo?t=267)
- Cheung and her co-founder spent over three years and roughly a dozen failed ideas (including a therapist-matching platform and an SEO content-arbitrage blog network) before discovering the home-cleaning idea [adora-cheung @ 05:46](https://youtu.be/lVDmyRxeGCo?t=346)
- The homejoy idea came directly from a personal, lived problem: her co-founder couldn't find an affordable, trustworthy cleaner and had to choose between expensive vetted agencies or an unvetted Craigslist listing [adora-cheung @ 09:24](https://youtu.be/lVDmyRxeGCo?t=564)
- To get the first customers with zero traffic, Cheung stood outside a fair handing out free water bottles to convince people to book cleanings, discovering that most followed through — a signal of real demand [adora-cheung @ 11:32](https://youtu.be/lVDmyRxeGCo?t=692)
- To solve the marketplace's supply side, the founders became the cleaners themselves first, which surfaced painful lessons (a 9,000 sq ft post-construction house took 5 days to clean) [adora-cheung @ 12:27](https://youtu.be/lVDmyRxeGCo?t=747)
- Cheung took an actual job at a cleaning company for weeks to learn the trade and to understand operational inefficiencies (manual scheduling, no easy online booking) that technology could fix [adora-cheung @ 16:46](https://youtu.be/lVDmyRxeGCo?t=1006)
- After three years with no successful track record, the founders set a hard deadline: two investor meetings in one day, and if neither led to funding they would shut the company down [adora-cheung @ 19:33](https://youtu.be/lVDmyRxeGCo?t=1173)
- They closed $25k from former boss Max Levchin and another $25k from Paul Graham in office hours the same day, turning that $50k into the seed that let homejoy start growing [adora-cheung @ 22:53](https://youtu.be/lVDmyRxeGCo?t=1373)
- Cheung's advice for enduring hard times: don't add stress to an already stressful situation — acknowledge the setback, then immediately ask "how am I going to deal with this" [adora-cheung @ 24:49](https://youtu.be/lVDmyRxeGCo?t=1489)
- She rejects the idea that success is mostly luck, arguing that aside from circumstances of birth, sustained success comes from working extremely hard and never giving up [adora-cheung @ 25:33](https://youtu.be/lVDmyRxeGCo?t=1533)
