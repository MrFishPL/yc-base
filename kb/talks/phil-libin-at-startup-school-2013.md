# Phil Libin at Startup School 2013 — Compiled Truth

Phil Libin traces Evernote's roots through two earlier startups, arguing the biggest lessons were to lock in great co-founders early, avoid cleverness in legal/cap structure, and finally build only something you personally love enough to never want to exit.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Cultivate a small group of brilliant, high-energy co-founders as young as possible; the same crew followed him through three companies. [phil-libin-at-startup-school-2013 @ 01:13](https://youtu.be/KEy_ocXnrdY?t=73)
- Don't bother making friends with people you can't imagine starting a company with. [phil-libin-at-startup-school-2013 @ 02:05](https://youtu.be/KEy_ocXnrdY?t=125)
- Pure consulting work feels like being your own boss but builds no long-term value — you get paid but nothing accumulates. [phil-libin-at-startup-school-2013 @ 04:17](https://youtu.be/KEy_ocXnrdY?t=257)
- With Evernote, they deliberately chose to build only things they themselves loved and to treat it as life's work with no exit strategy, just a liquidity strategy. [phil-libin-at-startup-school-2013 @ 09:13](https://youtu.be/KEy_ocXnrdY?t=553)
- Being clever with an unconventional legal/cap-table structure cost Evernote about 18 months of being unfundable; only be innovative about your core idea, do everything else by the book. [phil-libin-at-startup-school-2013 @ 14:34](https://youtu.be/KEy_ocXnrdY?t=874)
- A $10M term sheet fell through on the exact day Lehman Brothers collapsed, leaving the company with three weeks of cash. [phil-libin-at-startup-school-2013 @ 15:41](https://youtu.be/KEy_ocXnrdY?t=941)
- Hours before planning to shut down the company, an unsolicited email from a fan in Sweden led to a $500K bridge investment that kept Evernote alive. [phil-libin-at-startup-school-2013 @ 19:04](https://youtu.be/KEy_ocXnrdY?t=1144)
- That bridge bought six months during which the company built real cohort/unit-economics data, which finally made VC pitches work. [phil-libin-at-startup-school-2013 @ 20:28](https://youtu.be/KEy_ocXnrdY?t=1228)
- Running a company gets harder, not easier, over time even as it becomes more rewarding — don't found a startup if you're optimizing for ease. [phil-libin-at-startup-school-2013 @ 25:02](https://youtu.be/KEy_ocXnrdY?t=1502)
- Building something for yourself lets you be an honest judge of its quality, and the tech world's meritocracy means millions of others likely share your taste. [phil-libin-at-startup-school-2013 @ 27:26](https://youtu.be/KEy_ocXnrdY?t=1646)
