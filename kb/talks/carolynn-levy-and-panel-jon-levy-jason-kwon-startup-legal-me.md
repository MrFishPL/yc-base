# Carolynn Levy And Panel (Jon Levy, Jason Kwon) - Startup Legal Mechanics — Compiled Truth

YC's legal team walks founders through the basic legal mechanics of starting a company — incorporation, founder stock, vesting, and employment — and then answers founder-submitted questions on the trickiest recurring mistakes.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Form a Delaware C-corp sooner rather than later: it protects founders from personal liability, makes the corporation the correct owner of the IP being created, and is required before you can raise real money or run payroll [carolynn-levy-and-panel-jon-levy-jason-kwon-startup-legal-me @ 13:59](https://youtu.be/BTShgZxiNV8?t=839)
- Starting as an LLC to save on taxes is usually a mistake because most angels and VCs won't invest in an LLC, and converting later is a costly headache [carolynn-levy-and-panel-jon-levy-jason-kwon-startup-legal-me @ 15:15](https://youtu.be/BTShgZxiNV8?t=915)
- Founder stock should always carry a standard ~4-year vesting schedule, even for solo founders or teams who've worked together for years, since no vesting scares off investors and sets up future breakup disputes [carolynn-levy-and-panel-jon-levy-jason-kwon-startup-legal-me @ 18:32](https://youtu.be/BTShgZxiNV8?t=1112)
- Every founder must file an 83(b) election within a hard, non-negotiable 30-calendar-day window after buying stock, or risk a large future tax liability as the stock appreciates [carolynn-levy-and-panel-jon-levy-jason-kwon-startup-legal-me @ 25:51](https://youtu.be/BTShgZxiNV8?t=1551)
- Every founder and employee must sign a CIIA (confidential/proprietary information and invention assignment agreement) to ensure the company — not individuals — owns all IP created; formal employment agreements are usually unnecessary since employment is at-will by default [carolynn-levy-and-panel-jon-levy-jason-kwon-startup-legal-me @ 09:34](https://youtu.be/BTShgZxiNV8?t=574)
- A reasonable early-stage equity pool for employees is roughly 10-20%, though the exact number depends heavily on company size and hiring plans [carolynn-levy-and-panel-jon-levy-jason-kwon-startup-legal-me @ 30:20](https://youtu.be/BTShgZxiNV8?t=1820)
- Paying a friend only in stock for work is generally a mistake unless they're properly classified as a consultant/contractor; unpaid interns are usually illegal too [carolynn-levy-and-panel-jon-levy-jason-kwon-startup-legal-me @ 20:54](https://youtu.be/BTShgZxiNV8?t=1254)
- Founders should read every word of their founder stock purchase agreement (and fundraising documents), even though the legalese is tedious, because you don't want anything happening to your company's stock that you don't fully understand [carolynn-levy-and-panel-jon-levy-jason-kwon-startup-legal-me @ 19:55](https://youtu.be/BTShgZxiNV8?t=1195)
- If a co-founder relationship (especially with a friend) isn't working, resolve it quickly rather than letting it fester — delay hurts both the company and the friendship [carolynn-levy-and-panel-jon-levy-jason-kwon-startup-legal-me @ 41:15](https://youtu.be/BTShgZxiNV8?t=2475)
- Trying to build a company part-time while keeping a full-time job essentially never produces a giant outcome — great companies require full-time, all-consuming commitment [carolynn-levy-and-panel-jon-levy-jason-kwon-startup-legal-me @ 37:24](https://youtu.be/BTShgZxiNV8?t=2244)
