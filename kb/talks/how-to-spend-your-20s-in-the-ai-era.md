# How to Spend Your 20s in the AI Era — Compiled Truth

In this live Lightcone panel, YC partners argue that the "safe" credentialed career path is no longer safe, that real leverage now comes from agency and domain expertise rather than fake credentials like fundraising milestones, and that niche, substance-driven building beats chasing social-media hype.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A New York Fed stat from February 2025 found computer science majors had 6.1% unemployment versus 3.0% for art history majors, undercutting the idea that a CS degree is the "safe" path [how-to-spend-your-20s-in-the-ai-era @ 03:17](https://youtu.be/ShYKkPPhOoc?t=197)
- Many CS curricula are outdated because they still forbid students from using tools like Cursor and AI coding assistants that students will actually need in their careers [how-to-spend-your-20s-in-the-ai-era @ 05:37](https://youtu.be/ShYKkPPhOoc?t=337)
- Raising a Series A used to be the milestone founders bragged about a year or two out of college, but now founders like Cursor's reach a $10B company in a similar timeframe, showing the pace of company-building has grown by orders of magnitude [how-to-spend-your-20s-in-the-ai-era @ 09:03](https://youtu.be/ShYKkPPhOoc?t=543)
- Raising money from investors or getting press attention is a "fake credential" disconnected from real outcomes, unlike revenue that "just goes in the bank," and treating fundraising as the goal misses the point [how-to-spend-your-20s-in-the-ai-era @ 10:09](https://youtu.be/ShYKkPPhOoc?t=609)
- Pre-AI, domain expertise (understanding customers and markets) mattered more than technical skill because web software was commoditized, but AI has flipped this back toward technical expertise being the missing piece since it's hard to make AI work reliably [how-to-spend-your-20s-in-the-ai-era @ 12:53](https://youtu.be/ShYKkPPhOoc?t=773)
- The advice for gaining domain expertise without industry experience is to become a "forward deployed engineer" — go undercover into a business (like the founder of Flexport who became one of the top importers of medical hot tubs) to learn what people actually need [how-to-spend-your-20s-in-the-ai-era @ 15:01](https://youtu.be/ShYKkPPhOoc?t=901)
- Entrepreneurship programs that treat startups as a checklist of tests to pass are dangerous because they teach founders to fake it and lie to investors, unlike the current moment which rewards real substance and honesty [how-to-spend-your-20s-in-the-ai-era @ 20:41](https://youtu.be/ShYKkPPhOoc?t=1241)
- Rather than chasing social-media attention or "aura farming," founders are advised to work backwards from a real, substantive update (e.g., a simple Loom video of a shipped feature) every two-week sprint [how-to-spend-your-20s-in-the-ai-era @ 25:47](https://youtu.be/ShYKkPPhOoc?t=1547)
- If doing a first startup, founders are advised to find a co-founder rather than go solo, since the learning curve is too wide to tackle alone, and the biggest practical blocker is aligning timing so both co-founders can quit their jobs together [how-to-spend-your-20s-in-the-ai-era @ 33:25](https://youtu.be/ShYKkPPhOoc?t=2005)
- Starting niche has always been the winning recipe — Airbnb began as air beds during conferences, Stripe's early wedge was instant payment activation, and Coinbase targeted a "non-existent" market of regular people wanting a clean Bitcoin interface — and niches matter even more now because AI lets a small, unlikely-to-be-copied niche become a durable moat [how-to-spend-your-20s-in-the-ai-era @ 35:21](https://youtu.be/ShYKkPPhOoc?t=2121)
