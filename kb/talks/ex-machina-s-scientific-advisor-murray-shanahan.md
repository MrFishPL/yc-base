# Ex Machina's Scientific Advisor - Murray Shanahan — Compiled Truth

AI researcher and Ex Machina scientific advisor Murray Shanahan discusses the "frame problem" running through his career, why embodiment matters for intelligence, and how far deep reinforcement learning still is from general intelligence.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- When Shanahan did his AI PhD in the 1980s, the field was niche enough that researchers could work quietly in a corner without outside attention, unlike today [ex-machina-s-scientific-advisor-murray-shanahan @ 00:38](https://youtu.be/sNk68_E2T84?t=38)
- The "frame problem" — how a thinking system figures out what's relevant versus irrelevant to its current situation — recurred across his work in classical AI, brain modeling, and now deep reinforcement learning [ex-machina-s-scientific-advisor-murray-shanahan @ 04:00](https://youtu.be/sNk68_E2T84?t=240)
- Physical embodiment is central to human intelligence because brains evolved primarily to help navigate and manipulate a complex 3D world [ex-machina-s-scientific-advisor-murray-shanahan @ 12:38](https://youtu.be/sNk68_E2T84?t=758)
- DeepMind's DQN system, which learned to play Atari games from raw pixels alone, was one of the first true general-purpose learners, but it is strikingly slow compared to how quickly humans learn general principles [ex-machina-s-scientific-advisor-murray-shanahan @ 45:05](https://youtu.be/sNk68_E2T84?t=2705)
- Shanahan's current research interest is rehabilitating ideas from classical symbolic AI and applying them to modern deep reinforcement learning systems to address their shortcomings [ex-machina-s-scientific-advisor-murray-shanahan @ 46:29](https://youtu.be/sNk68_E2T84?t=2789)
- Even if computing power reaches human-brain scale by around 2020-2022, that alone won't tell us how to actually build human-level intelligence — an unknown number of conceptual breakthroughs still separate current AI from AGI [ex-machina-s-scientific-advisor-murray-shanahan @ 36:48](https://youtu.be/sNk68_E2T84?t=2208)
- Asimov's Three Laws of Robotics are a science-fiction device, not applicable to real robotics today, because current AI systems don't have the capacity to comprehend such laws in the first place [ex-machina-s-scientific-advisor-murray-shanahan @ 39:43](https://youtu.be/sNk68_E2T84?t=2383)
