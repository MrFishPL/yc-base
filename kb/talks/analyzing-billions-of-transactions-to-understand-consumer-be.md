# Analyzing Billions of Transactions to Understand Consumer Behavior - Michael Babineau and Kevin Hale — Compiled Truth

Second Measure's Michael Babineau describes how a chance question about loading two terabytes of credit-card data into Excel led to building a self-serve analytics platform that turned messy transaction data into an investor and enterprise research tool.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A friend at a hedge fund asking "I've got two terabytes of data on a hard drive, how do I load this into Excel" revealed that most hedge funds lack in-house engineering talent, exposing the opportunity [analyzing-billions-of-transactions-to-understand-consumer-be @ 01:16](https://youtu.be/hsMePYTcIik?t=76)
- Their video-game background taught them that building self-serve tools for non-coders (game designers) to answer their own questions beats being a human go-between fetching reports [analyzing-billions-of-transactions-to-understand-consumer-be @ 07:11](https://youtu.be/hsMePYTcIik?t=431)
- Before their product existed, questions like "did Chipotle's food poisoning incident hurt revenue" could only be answered slowly and expensively through market-research surveys with tiny sample sizes [analyzing-billions-of-transactions-to-understand-consumer-be @ 10:17](https://youtu.be/hsMePYTcIik?t=617)
- Because VCs are eager to meet YC companies before demo day, early sales meetings doubled as product demos, turning many VCs into first paying customers [analyzing-billions-of-transactions-to-understand-consumer-be @ 12:07](https://youtu.be/hsMePYTcIik?t=727)
- The core technical challenge is entity resolution: normalizing roughly 1 billion unique transaction description strings down to the few thousand merchants they actually track [analyzing-billions-of-transactions-to-understand-consumer-be @ 37:50](https://youtu.be/hsMePYTcIik?t=2270)
- Hiring for data science prioritizes "scientists with a capital S" who reason from first principles and question assumptions in messy data, over people who just know the tools [analyzing-billions-of-transactions-to-understand-consumer-be @ 33:06](https://youtu.be/hsMePYTcIik?t=1986)
- Reading Clayton Christensen's "jobs to be done" framework reframed their narrow investor focus and opened them up to serving corporate customers too [analyzing-billions-of-transactions-to-understand-consumer-be @ 44:55](https://youtu.be/hsMePYTcIik?t=2695)
- Expansion into enterprise customers happened organically: portfolio-company CEOs saw VCs using the product in board meetings and asked how to get it themselves [analyzing-billions-of-transactions-to-understand-consumer-be @ 46:44](https://youtu.be/hsMePYTcIik?t=2804)
- All 150 clients came through inbound referrals with zero outbound sales effort, driven by the "wow" factor of seeing previously invisible data [analyzing-billions-of-transactions-to-understand-consumer-be @ 47:09](https://youtu.be/hsMePYTcIik?t=2829)
- Rather than selling proprietary trading signals (whose value dilutes as more customers buy them), they chose to sell an empowerment tool so customers find their own edge by asking better questions [analyzing-billions-of-transactions-to-understand-consumer-be @ 53:53](https://youtu.be/hsMePYTcIik?t=3233)
