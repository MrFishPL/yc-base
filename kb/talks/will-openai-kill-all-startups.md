# Will OpenAI Kill All Startups? — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell argue that, as with prior major tech shifts (electricity, internet, mobile, cloud, open source), major AI advances create more startup opportunity than they destroy, and that OpenAI is not trying to kill startups but to build AGI.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Companies like OpenAI and Anthropic are fundamentally trying to build AGI, not AI-powered CRMs or better search products [will-openai-kill-all-startups @ 00:43](https://youtu.be/smHw9kEwcgM?t=43)
- Historically, major technology shifts have advantaged startups over incumbents, and the faster the shift, the greater the advantage to startups [will-openai-kill-all-startups @ 02:38](https://youtu.be/smHw9kEwcgM?t=158)
- There's a real difference between "cargo culting" AI (bolting it on for hype or fundraising) and genuinely using AI to build features that dramatically increase retention and let you charge more [will-openai-kill-all-startups @ 03:41](https://youtu.be/smHw9kEwcgM?t=221)
- Two groups of founders are drawn to AI right now: well-paid domain experts leaving great jobs because they know this is their moment, and inexperienced people chasing quick money, and the latter group is not really showing up for AI the way it did for crypto [will-openai-kill-all-startups @ 09:06](https://youtu.be/smHw9kEwcgM?t=546)
- It's not obvious everyone will build OpenAI competitors, and open-ended low-hanging-fruit ideas may just get absorbed by OpenAI itself, so founders should look for second-order insights, the way Uber was a second-order consequence of the iPhone rather than an obvious first use case [will-openai-kill-all-startups @ 10:15](https://youtu.be/smHw9kEwcgM?t=615)
- It's fine if a product starts as "a thin wrapper on OpenAI" as long as that's a starting point rather than the destination, much like early Dropbox was dismissed as a thin wrapper on S3 [will-openai-kill-all-startups @ 11:10](https://youtu.be/smHw9kEwcgM?t=670)
- Until AGI is solved, it remains OpenAI's first, second, and third priority, meaning using existing AI tools to make people's lives and businesses better is not their focus, leaving a huge opportunity for startups [will-openai-kill-all-startups @ 12:05](https://youtu.be/smHw9kEwcgM?t=725)
- OpenAI was originally created as a non-profit specifically so that startups could bloom and be enabled to create value using the resulting technology [will-openai-kill-all-startups @ 06:30](https://youtu.be/smHw9kEwcgM?t=390)
