# Which Sales Strategy Is Best For Your Startup? — Compiled Truth

A YC group partner contrasts top-down (selling to executives) and bottoms-up (selling to individual users first) go-to-market motions and explains when each works best.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Top-down sales starts by identifying a decision-maker, validating their problem, convincing them, and pushing the deal through procurement to a signed contract [which-sales-strategy-is-best-for-your-startup @ 00:23](https://youtu.be/o1HFZ8P4rA8?t=23)
- The biggest advantage of top-down selling is that there's a proven playbook that works if you understand your customers' problems, and executives who buy tend to become great, high-retention customers [which-sales-strategy-is-best-for-your-startup @ 01:04](https://youtu.be/o1HFZ8P4rA8?t=64)
- The main risks of top-down selling are getting pulled into building one-off features that turn into consulting relationships, and the fact that scaling it requires an expensive enterprise sales team, creating a price floor of roughly 10K for mid-market and 100K for enterprise [which-sales-strategy-is-best-for-your-startup @ 01:42](https://youtu.be/o1HFZ8P4rA8?t=102)
- Building a top-down motion means defining your target customer and buyer persona, then using channels like LinkedIn and personalized cold email to get their attention [which-sales-strategy-is-best-for-your-startup @ 02:01](https://youtu.be/o1HFZ8P4rA8?t=121)
- Bottoms-up sales starts with building a self-serve product, finding a cheap scalable distribution channel, acquiring lots of individual users, and then converting companies where usage has taken hold into paid contracts [which-sales-strategy-is-best-for-your-startup @ 02:42](https://youtu.be/o1HFZ8P4rA8?t=162)
- Slack is the classic bottoms-up example: small teams adopted it without talking to a salesperson, and usage spread virally inside organizations before the sales team stepped in [which-sales-strategy-is-best-for-your-startup @ 03:19](https://youtu.be/o1HFZ8P4rA8?t=199)
- In reality, bottoms-up rarely grows purely virally — most companies still need salespeople and cold calling, and the model's real efficiency comes from selling into prospects who are already using the product [which-sales-strategy-is-best-for-your-startup @ 03:57](https://youtu.be/o1HFZ8P4rA8?t=237)
- Bottoms-up founders should obsess over removing product friction: test landing pages for clarity, watch onboarding for confusion points, instrument funnels, and A/B test drop-offs [which-sales-strategy-is-best-for-your-startup @ 04:40](https://youtu.be/o1HFZ8P4rA8?t=280)
- A common freemium tactic is to make individual-user features free and charge only for the collaboration features that teams need [which-sales-strategy-is-best-for-your-startup @ 04:59](https://youtu.be/o1HFZ8P4rA8?t=299)
- Among top YC B2B SaaS companies the split between top-down and bottoms-up is roughly even, so the right choice depends on whether your product resonates most with individual contributors or with executives [which-sales-strategy-is-best-for-your-startup @ 05:18](https://youtu.be/o1HFZ8P4rA8?t=318)
