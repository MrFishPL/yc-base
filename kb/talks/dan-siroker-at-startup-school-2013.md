# Dan Siroker at Startup School 2013 — Compiled Truth

Dan Siroker traces his path from a solo failed startup through Carrot Sticks, Spradley, and Optimizely to argue that the only durable startup algorithm is: do something, get feedback, use the feedback to get better, and repeat.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- His first solo startup (Sentiment Solutions) failed because he never had paying customers giving feedback on what to build [dan-siroker-at-startup-school-2013 @ 00:56](https://youtu.be/k4_3uluQsq4?t=56)
- The biggest lesson from being a Google Chrome PM was the value of a tight feedback loop with users [dan-siroker-at-startup-school-2013 @ 02:19](https://youtu.be/k4_3uluQsq4?t=139)
- Carrot Sticks struggled because it sold to parents/teachers rather than users the founders were themselves, making prioritization hard and distribution slow and unscalable [dan-siroker-at-startup-school-2013 @ 06:53](https://youtu.be/k4_3uluQsq4?t=413)
- Optimizely was born from building the product they themselves had wished for back on the Obama campaign, and got a paying customer before writing a line of code [dan-siroker-at-startup-school-2013 @ 11:00](https://youtu.be/k4_3uluQsq4?t=660)
- Listening closely to what prospective customers asked for (targeting, analytics, integrations) and shipping it created a tight feedback loop that made growth much easier than prior startups [dan-siroker-at-startup-school-2013 @ 13:27](https://youtu.be/k4_3uluQsq4?t=807)
- As the company scaled, hiring decisions used a simple rule: only hire candidates better than the current team average [dan-siroker-at-startup-school-2013 @ 14:19](https://youtu.be/k4_3uluQsq4?t=859)
- For their Series A, Optimizely ran mock board meetings, spending three hours with each finalist investor to see how they'd actually behave as a board member [dan-siroker-at-startup-school-2013 @ 16:56](https://youtu.be/k4_3uluQsq4?t=1016)
- Word-of-mouth referrals from happy customers were a bigger driver of sales than Optimizely's own sales team [dan-siroker-at-startup-school-2013 @ 18:56](https://youtu.be/k4_3uluQsq4?t=1136)
- The "universal startup algorithm" is: do everything, get feedback, use it to improve, and repeat — this applies to hiring, fundraising, and product [dan-siroker-at-startup-school-2013 @ 19:32](https://youtu.be/k4_3uluQsq4?t=1172)
- A founder's real job is to write the algorithm for their company, not just execute someone else's — falling into pure execution is "the activity trap" [dan-siroker-at-startup-school-2013 @ 20:10](https://youtu.be/k4_3uluQsq4?t=1210)
