# Figma's Dylan Field: Exploring the idea maze, vibe coding, and the power of "locking in" — Compiled Truth

Figma CEO Dylan Field walks through Figma's idea-maze origin story and argues that in the AI era, design and human judgment become more valuable, not less, even as AI tools accelerate how fast software can be built.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- AI is currently "very much in the tool category," lowering the floor so more people can participate in design while also raising the ceiling on what's possible [figma-s-dylan-field-exploring-the-idea-maze-vibe-coding-and- @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/4UE4e6b2qtA?t=0)
- Current models are stuck in an "uncanny valley" where prompt-to-app tools can produce something that works but not something well-designed, because no one has figured out how to combine diffusion (art) and LLM (problem-solving) techniques for design [figma-s-dylan-field-exploring-the-idea-maze-vibe-coding-and- @ 06:57](https://youtu.be/4UE4e6b2qtA?t=417)
- Models are described as lacking the "empathy" and "agency" piece of design — understanding user context, culture, and emotional state — which is why Field remains confident designers will stay a critical role in building software [figma-s-dylan-field-exploring-the-idea-maze-vibe-coding-and- @ 08:06](https://youtu.be/4UE4e6b2qtA?t=486)
- Figma's founding thesis, formed after pivoting through drones, WebGL gaming, meme generation, and photo editing, was "as software gets easier to build, design becomes more important" — a bet made despite designers looking like a tiny addressable market at the time [figma-s-dylan-field-exploring-the-idea-maze-vibe-coding-and- @ 17:40](https://youtu.be/4UE4e6b2qtA?t=1060)
