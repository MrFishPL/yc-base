# Office Hours at Startup School 2013 with Paul Graham and Sam Altman — Compiled Truth

Paul Graham and Sam Altman run live office hours with four founders (a coding game, a progress-video site, and a customs-brokerage startup), probing their traction, pricing, and go-to-market decisions in real time.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A coding game startup launched to Reddit unprepared and got the "hug of death," maxing out their server at 15,000 people within 24 hours of shipping [office-hours-at-startup-school-2013-with-paul-graham-and-sam @ 01:19](https://youtu.be/syoqjYLDs48?t=79)
- The team learned their first levels assumed too much prior knowledge, since beta users with no programming background didn't even understand basic notation [office-hours-at-startup-school-2013-with-paul-graham-and-sam @ 03:42](https://youtu.be/syoqjYLDs48?t=222)
- Outside game-design advisors told them to reskin the game as robots instead of wizard/spell fantasy because "people understand robots" and code-as-magic was harder to grasp [office-hours-at-startup-school-2013-with-paul-graham-and-sam @ 06:50](https://youtu.be/syoqjYLDs48?t=410)
- PG noted that founders who worked a long stint at a large company are often a bad signal, because the best startup founders "could not stand" that kind of job [office-hours-at-startup-school-2013-with-paul-graham-and-sam @ 07:59](https://youtu.be/syoqjYLDs48?t=479)
- Sam Altman told a founder testing a video-progress site to be empirical and not over-optimize toward one use case (kids vs. hobbies) too early, letting the product "grow into whatever it's going to grow into" [office-hours-at-startup-school-2013-with-paul-graham-and-sam @ 18:48](https://youtu.be/syoqjYLDs48?t=1128)
- The customs-brokerage founder (Flexport) explained the business was defensible because it requires a hard-to-get license and, until recently, clearance could only happen at your local port [office-hours-at-startup-school-2013-with-paul-graham-and-sam @ 23:45](https://youtu.be/syoqjYLDs48?t=1425)
- PG advised against quoting very large total market sizes to investors, saying "really big markets are bad for startups, not good" because big numbers aren't believed [office-hours-at-startup-school-2013-with-paul-graham-and-sam @ 27:58](https://youtu.be/syoqjYLDs48?t=1678)
- The customs startup planned a flat $100 fee per clearance regardless of shipment size, with an estimated 75% gross margin per clearance [office-hours-at-startup-school-2013-with-paul-graham-and-sam @ 26:26](https://youtu.be/syoqjYLDs48?t=1586)
