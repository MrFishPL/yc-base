# Legal and Accounting Basics for Startups with Kirsty Nathoo and Carolynn Levy — Compiled Truth

YC's general counsel and finance lead give a founder's crash course in incorporation, equity, vesting, fundraising paperwork, payroll, and firing, arguing that keeping everything simple and standardized (Delaware, Clerky, vesting, payroll services) prevents expensive mistakes later.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Incorporate in Delaware and keep it standard: a YC company that stayed a Connecticut LLC by mistake instead of converting to a Delaware corporation racked up a $500,000 legal bill to fix it before a raise [legal-and-accounting-basics-for-startups-with-kirsty-nathoo- @ 03:50](https://youtu.be/sd9yLmJ1Jfk?t=230)
- Execution has more value than the idea, so founders should resist giving disproportionate equity to whoever came up with the idea [legal-and-accounting-basics-for-startups-with-kirsty-nathoo- @ 08:27](https://youtu.be/sd9yLmJ1Jfk?t=507)
- Among top YC companies by valuation, there are zero instances of significantly disproportionate founder equity splits [legal-and-accounting-basics-for-startups-with-kirsty-nathoo- @ 10:35](https://youtu.be/sd9yLmJ1Jfk?t=635)
- Standard Silicon Valley vesting is four years with a one-year cliff, and even solo founders should vest their own stock to signal skin in the game and set the tone for employees [legal-and-accounting-basics-for-startups-with-kirsty-nathoo- @ 14:25](https://youtu.be/sd9yLmJ1Jfk?t=865)
- Raising on an uncapped safe/note is faster than a priced round, and a valuation cap sets an upper bound used later to convert the investment into shares, rewarding early risk [legal-and-accounting-basics-for-startups-with-kirsty-nathoo- @ 19:57](https://youtu.be/sd9yLmJ1Jfk?t=1197)
- Founders must track future dilution across stacked safes and a priced round — e.g. $2M on safes at a $6M cap plus new investors at 20% can already give away 45% of the company [legal-and-accounting-basics-for-startups-with-kirsty-nathoo- @ 21:53](https://youtu.be/sd9yLmJ1Jfk?t=1313)
- Founders are legally employees and must be paid; one YC company that ignored payroll taxes for three years faced a "huge expensive disaster" [legal-and-accounting-basics-for-startups-with-kirsty-nathoo- @ 32:38](https://youtu.be/sd9yLmJ1Jfk?t=1958)
- Founder breakups turn ugly specifically when founders haven't been paying themselves, because unpaid wages become leverage for a fired founder to demand vesting acceleration [legal-and-accounting-basics-for-startups-with-kirsty-nathoo- @ 34:01](https://youtu.be/sd9yLmJ1Jfk?t=2041)
- When firing an employee: do it quickly, communicate directly without apology, pay all wages and accrued vacation immediately, and cut off digital access right away [legal-and-accounting-basics-for-startups-with-kirsty-nathoo- @ 40:22](https://youtu.be/sd9yLmJ1Jfk?t=2422)
- Investors will ask for board seats, informal advisory roles, pro-rata rights, and information rights — founders bear the burden of understanding all of these terms, not just the valuation [legal-and-accounting-basics-for-startups-with-kirsty-nathoo- @ 24:25](https://youtu.be/sd9yLmJ1Jfk?t=1465)
