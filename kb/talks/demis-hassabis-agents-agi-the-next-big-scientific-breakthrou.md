# Demis Hassabis: Agents, AGI & The Next Big Scientific Breakthrough — Compiled Truth

Demis Hassabis argues that current AI (pre-training, RLHF, chain-of-thought) is likely part of AGI's final architecture but that continual learning, long-term reasoning, and memory remain unsolved, and he gives founders a framework for finding defensible deep-tech AI startups.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Continual learning, long-term reasoning, and aspects of memory are still unsolved, and Hassabis puts roughly 50/50 odds that one or two more big ideas (beyond scaling existing techniques) are needed for AGI [demis-hassabis-agents-agi-the-next-big-scientific-breakthrou @ 02:50](https://youtu.be/JNyuX1zoOgU?t=170)
- Not having continual learning is currently one of the things holding agents back from completing full tasks end-to-end since they don't adapt to the context they're placed in [demis-hassabis-agents-agi-the-next-big-scientific-breakthrou @ 12:32](https://youtu.be/JNyuX1zoOgU?t=752)
- DeepMind's core strength is distilling frontier capability into small, fast, cheap models (like Flash and Gemma) because it must serve more than a dozen billion-user Google products at low latency [demis-hassabis-agents-agi-the-next-big-scientific-breakthrou @ 08:22](https://youtu.be/JNyuX1zoOgU?t=502)
- We're still in an agent experimentation phase — nobody has yet vibe-coded a chart-topping AAA game, which shows the tools/process aren't quite there yet even though people are running dozens of agents for many hours [demis-hassabis-agents-agi-the-next-big-scientific-breakthrou @ 16:17](https://youtu.be/JNyuX1zoOgU?t=977)
- For AI-for-science startups, the most defensible sweet spot is combining frontier AI with a hard, interdisciplinary deep-tech domain (materials, medicine) involving the physical world, since that isn't easily swamped by the next foundation-model update [demis-hassabis-agents-agi-the-next-big-scientific-breakthrou @ 30:45](https://youtu.be/JNyuX1zoOgU?t=1845)
- The recipe behind AlphaGo and AlphaFold: look for a massive combinatorial search space, a clearly specifiable objective function to hill-climb on, and enough data or a simulator to generate in-distribution synthetic data [demis-hassabis-agents-agi-the-next-big-scientific-breakthrou @ 33:24](https://youtu.be/JNyuX1zoOgU?t=2004)
- Founders starting a 10-year deep-tech journey today must plan for AGI potentially arriving mid-journey, and think about how their venture could leverage general systems (like Gemini/Claude) using specialized tools such as AlphaFold rather than one giant brain [demis-hassabis-agents-agi-the-next-big-scientific-breakthrou @ 39:04](https://youtu.be/JNyuX1zoOgU?t=2344)
- Current systems still can't perform genuine scientific discovery (inventing a new hypothesis or a new Millennium Prize-worthy problem), which Hassabis frames as his personal "Einstein test": train on knowledge up to 1901 and see if it can independently derive special relativity by 1905 [demis-hassabis-agents-agi-the-next-big-scientific-breakthrou @ 37:14](https://youtu.be/JNyuX1zoOgU?t=2234)
- His advice to founders working on hard, deep problems: given how short life is, put your energy into something that will genuinely make a difference if you hadn't pushed it, rather than something shallower just because it's easier [demis-hassabis-agents-agi-the-next-big-scientific-breakthrou @ 38:28](https://youtu.be/JNyuX1zoOgU?t=2308)
