# Tim Urban of Wait But Why — Compiled Truth

Tim Urban explains why he writes Wait But Why for the sheer joy of curiosity and craft, then argues humanity is at a uniquely pivotal "page 500" moment in history where AI, crypto, VR/AR, and biotech will define whether the future is a golden age or an ending, and describes his method for going from layman to a "level six" explainer on any hard topic.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- He does Wait But Why because it lets him stay curious, keep learning new topics, and feel like he's playing rather than working, not out of a sense of obligation. [tim-urban-of-wait-but-why @ 01:13](https://youtu.be/7a9lsGtVziM?t=73)
- He measures impact by how many smart people he can redirect toward important problems, citing MIT students who went into SpaceX and AI because of his posts. [tim-urban-of-wait-but-why @ 01:49](https://youtu.be/7a9lsGtVziM?t=109)
- His "500-page book of human history" analogy frames the present as the final page before an unprecedented and irreversible leap, whether toward a golden era or the end. [tim-urban-of-wait-but-why @ 03:08](https://youtu.be/7a9lsGtVziM?t=188)
- He argues the way to change human behavior at scale isn't to fight human nature but to build systems that make culture reward the right things, pointing to AI safety as an area that needs to become as socially prestigious as getting rich. [tim-urban-of-wait-but-why @ 09:11](https://youtu.be/7a9lsGtVziM?t=551)
- His research process for a deep-dive post: broad Wikipedia/Google research across dozens of tabs to reach a foundational understanding, then targeted "rabbit hole" research on specific gaps, aiming to reach a self-assessed "level six" of understanding rather than expert-level mastery. [tim-urban-of-wait-but-why @ 17:56](https://youtu.be/7a9lsGtVziM?t=1076)
- He deliberately seeks out skeptics and opposing viewpoints for every topic (e.g., calling a skeptical doctor friend on cryonics) because the clash of one-sided arguments, like a courtroom, is what produces a trustworthy conclusion. [tim-urban-of-wait-but-why @ 37:11](https://youtu.be/7a9lsGtVziM?t=2231)
- He reads both left- and right-leaning media on any contested topic to counteract confirmation bias and avoid the "conspiracy land" spectrum that stems from only hearing one side. [tim-urban-of-wait-but-why @ 38:26](https://youtu.be/7a9lsGtVziM?t=2306)
- On crypto, he compares the current landscape to buying domain names in 1988 before the internet's winners existed, cautioning that most individual currencies could become worthless even if the underlying paradigm shift is real. [tim-urban-of-wait-but-why @ 42:12](https://youtu.be/7a9lsGtVziM?t=2532)
- He predicts VR/AR will follow the hype-crash-then-real-adoption curve of major technologies and that a billion people could be using VR within a decade, meaning entrepreneurs who start building now could become "the Larry Page of VR." [tim-urban-of-wait-but-why @ 49:43](https://youtu.be/7a9lsGtVziM?t=2983)
- He tells aspiring founders not to be intimidated into thinking they need to be great at math or coding to contribute to big future-shaping fields — being good at communicating ideas, ethics, or big-picture thinking is also a valuable role.
