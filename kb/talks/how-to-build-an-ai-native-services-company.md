# How to Build an AI-Native Services Company — Compiled Truth

A YC playbook for founders building "AI-native service companies" — trillion-dollar-scale services businesses like insurance, tax, audit, and law rebuilt from scratch with AI doing most of the work and delivering outcomes rather than a co-pilot tool.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The best markets for AI services share four traits: low trust (work is already outsourced), low judgment at the task level, a high intelligence threshold for the overall work, and regulation that raises the moat rather than just adding friction [how-to-build-an-ai-native-services-company @ 01:32](https://youtu.be/gSNFJbgoaHI?t=92)
- Apply the "Sam Altman test" to any market: as models get better, does your service get stronger, or does the model itself commoditize you — you want to be in the former camp [how-to-build-an-ai-native-services-company @ 03:19](https://youtu.be/gSNFJbgoaHI?t=199)
- The best founding teams for AI services combine domain fluency (direct or learned), model fluency (know what frontier models can do and design to ride the curve), and operational rigor (variance, throughput, cycle times, SOPs) [how-to-build-an-ai-native-services-company @ 04:18](https://youtu.be/gSNFJbgoaHI?t=258)
- In these businesses the human is the interface to the customer, not the product, so the product must help the human scale their work non-linearly — throughput and cycle time become product metrics tracked like DAUs [how-to-build-an-ai-native-services-company @ 05:36](https://youtu.be/gSNFJbgoaHI?t=336)
- Variance in output quality is the existential risk: customers will fire an AI services vendor for inconsistency faster than for being slower or pricier than incumbents [how-to-build-an-ai-native-services-company @ 05:54](https://youtu.be/gSNFJbgoaHI?t=354)
- It's fine to do things that don't scale at the very beginning, but the "early demand trap" of signing too many pilot customers too fast can overwhelm capacity before the product is ready — cap initial pilots to a small handful [how-to-build-an-ai-native-services-company @ 06:38](https://youtu.be/gSNFJbgoaHI?t=398)
- Price on value: per-unit pricing (per return, per claim, per loan) is cleanest, outcome-based pricing aligns incentives but is harder to forecast, and cost-plus or straight-line undercutting should both be avoided [how-to-build-an-ai-native-services-company @ 07:13](https://youtu.be/gSNFJbgoaHI?t=433)
- Obsess over COGS (model costs, hosting, humans in the loop) from day one, and be deeply suspicious of zero- or negative-margin pilots even though they're useful for learning [how-to-build-an-ai-native-services-company @ 08:35](https://youtu.be/gSNFJbgoaHI?t=515)
- The bet on these businesses is that "AI operating leverage" pushes traditional services margins (capped around 30%) toward software-like margins (50%+) on markets 2-3x bigger than pure software TAMs [how-to-build-an-ai-native-services-company @ 09:37](https://youtu.be/gSNFJbgoaHI?t=577)
- Buying an existing legacy services business and bolting AI on top rarely works because you can't acquire product-market fit — legacy hiring, metrics, and performance expectations don't change just because AI is added, except when you need a regulatory moat fast [how-to-build-an-ai-native-services-company @ 10:00](https://youtu.be/gSNFJbgoaHI?t=600)
