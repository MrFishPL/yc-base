# Vidit Aatrey on Building Meesho, India's Top Reselling Platform, with Adora Cheung — Compiled Truth

Meesho co-founder Vidit Aatrey describes how the company pivoted twice before finding product-market fit enabling anyone in India, especially women in tier-2/3 cities, to run a social-commerce reselling business on WhatsApp and Facebook with no capital.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Meesho lets anyone in India start and grow a social commerce store on WhatsApp, Facebook, or Instagram without needing capital to open an offline shop [vidit-aatrey-on-building-meesho-india-s-top-reselling-platfo @ 00:55](https://youtu.be/L325PVEvm7Q?t=55)
- Meesho's growth was fueled by macro timing: Reliance Jio's cheap data brought hundreds of millions of new Indian users online just as Meesho launched, and those tier-2/3 users preferred unbranded products [vidit-aatrey-on-building-meesho-india-s-top-reselling-platfo @ 04:55](https://youtu.be/L325PVEvm7Q?t=295)
- In India's low-trust commerce market, buying from someone in your own community makes the trust deficit around unbranded goods disappear, and Meesho's reseller model borrows exactly that trust [vidit-aatrey-on-building-meesho-india-s-top-reselling-platfo @ 07:39](https://youtu.be/L325PVEvm7Q?t=459)
- Meesho deliberately avoided scaling up customer support headcount, treating a rising volume of support calls as a signal the product itself was broken rather than a cost to staff around [vidit-aatrey-on-building-meesho-india-s-top-reselling-platfo @ 15:59](https://youtu.be/L325PVEvm7Q?t=959)
- After running two parallel products for six months, Meesho shut down its original WhatsApp-commerce tool built for offline shop owners and renamed "Meesho Supply" (the tool resellers were actually using) to Meesho [vidit-aatrey-on-building-meesho-india-s-top-reselling-platfo @ 26:29](https://youtu.be/L325PVEvm7Q?t=1589)
- The decision to pivot was reinforced by necessity: Meesho was struggling to raise money on the old model while the new reselling product was already generating revenue [vidit-aatrey-on-building-meesho-india-s-top-reselling-platfo @ 28:05](https://youtu.be/L325PVEvm7Q?t=1685)
- To stay close to users despite not belonging to their target demographic, the CEO personally added early users on WhatsApp to do customer support himself [vidit-aatrey-on-building-meesho-india-s-top-reselling-platfo @ 30:32](https://youtu.be/L325PVEvm7Q?t=1832)
- Meesho created a monthly "We Hear You" video update for its reseller users, treating them like part of the company with town-hall-style communication [vidit-aatrey-on-building-meesho-india-s-top-reselling-platfo @ 32:23](https://youtu.be/L325PVEvm7Q?t=1943)
- A formative early mistake was chasing VC-set fundraising milestones for months before realizing founders can't outsource conviction in their idea to investors [vidit-aatrey-on-building-meesho-india-s-top-reselling-platfo @ 38:07](https://youtu.be/L325PVEvm7Q?t=2287)
- Advice to Indian founders: stay extremely close to users and challenge default startup assumptions, like not needing a call center, rather than copying Western UX patterns [vidit-aatrey-on-building-meesho-india-s-top-reselling-platfo @ 44:28](https://youtu.be/L325PVEvm7Q?t=2668)
