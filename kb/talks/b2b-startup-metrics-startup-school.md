# B2B Startup Metrics | Startup School — Compiled Truth

Tom Blomfield (YC group partner) explains which metrics B2B startups should track, why revenue beats vanity metrics, and how retention and gross margin determine whether a business compounds or bleeds out.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Pick four or five key metrics to track accurately before launch rather than building metrics reactively after launching blind [b2b-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 03:03](https://youtu.be/_mKeVGSqQac?t=183)
- Teams must agree on and write down centralized definitions of each metric, since inconsistent definitions between teams (e.g. sales vs. marketing on "qualified leads") destroy meeting productivity [b2b-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 03:44](https://youtu.be/_mKeVGSqQac?t=224)
- Never change a metric's definition to make a bad number look better; keeping definitions consistent over time is what lets you tell if you're actually improving [b2b-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 04:24](https://youtu.be/_mKeVGSqQac?t=264)
- Revenue, not vanity metrics like gross merchandise value or gross transaction value, should be the key metric for most B2B companies [b2b-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 06:03](https://youtu.be/_mKeVGSqQac?t=363)
- Revenue, net burn rate, and runway are the three numbers that must appear at the top of every investor update [b2b-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 07:43](https://youtu.be/_mKeVGSqQac?t=463)
- Stacking monthly cohorts into a "layer cake" reveals whether high retention compounds revenue over years even without adding new customers [b2b-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 10:48](https://youtu.be/_mKeVGSqQac?t=648)
- Net dollar retention above 100% (125-150% is strong for early-stage B2B SaaS) means existing cohorts are growing from upsells and price increases, driving exponential growth [b2b-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 14:30](https://youtu.be/_mKeVGSqQac?t=870)
- Net dollar retention below 100%, especially in enterprise B2B SaaS, signals customers don't love the product and warrants fixing retention before investing more in sales and marketing [b2b-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 15:57](https://youtu.be/_mKeVGSqQac?t=957)
- Gross margin (revenue minus cost of goods sold) has become critical again for AI companies because model API costs are a real, non-hidden cost of goods sold even when covered by free credits [b2b-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 17:56](https://youtu.be/_mKeVGSqQac?t=1076)
- Startups should fix negative unit economics before scaling the customer base rather than blitz-scaling a negative-margin business the way Uber did during the zero-interest-rate era [b2b-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 21:55](https://youtu.be/_mKeVGSqQac?t=1315)
