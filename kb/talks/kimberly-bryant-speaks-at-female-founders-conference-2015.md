# Kimberly Bryant Speaks at Female Founders Conference 2015 — Compiled Truth

Kimberly Bryant, founder of Black Girls Code, shares founder lessons (drawn from "Good to Great" and Oprah) from her path building an organization teaching girls of color to code.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Black Girls Code, founded in April 2011, teaches girls ages 7-17 to code in a culturally sensitive setting paired with mentors who look like them, aiming to move them from consumers to creators of technology [kimberly-bryant-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 01:07](https://youtu.be/qeeRsWofAv0?t=67)
- The organization's big audacious goal is to train 1 million girls of color to code by 2040, deliberately chosen because that's when US demographics shift so today's minorities become the majority [kimberly-bryant-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 11:15](https://youtu.be/qeeRsWofAv0?t=675)
- Women earned about 36% of computer science degrees around 1989 (when Bryant got hers), a share that has since fallen to roughly 12-14%, with African-American women earning only about 3% and Latinas/Native Americans under 1% [kimberly-bryant-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 09:26](https://youtu.be/qeeRsWofAv0?t=566)
- A BHAG (big hairy audacious goal) should be scary enough that you're not sure you'll reach it — if it doesn't scare you a little, it's probably not big enough [kimberly-bryant-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 10:57](https://youtu.be/qeeRsWofAv0?t=657)
- In 2012, with just two people and a "reckless disregard for the impossible," Black Girls Code set a goal to take its program to six cities and reach 200 girls over one summer, and ended up reaching 1,000 [kimberly-bryant-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 13:37](https://youtu.be/qeeRsWofAv0?t=817)
- That result pushed the organization to shift from a hyper-local model to a chapter-based model leveraging a large volunteer pool [kimberly-bryant-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 15:20](https://youtu.be/qeeRsWofAv0?t=920)
- Building a great team is one of the hardest parts of founding, because everyone wants to "ride the bus" but few want to do the required work, and founders must hire people who are better than them across the board [kimberly-bryant-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 16:50](https://youtu.be/qeeRsWofAv0?t=1010)
- Bryant calls herself an "accidental social entrepreneur" — she originally intended to start a for-profit company but pivoted after realizing how underrepresented women and girls of color were in tech [kimberly-bryant-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 08:18](https://youtu.be/qeeRsWofAv0?t=498)
- Her closing advice, once a cliché to her, is to follow your passion — find the work you'd do even unpaid, since that clarity resolves the other questions about your path [kimberly-bryant-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 20:29](https://youtu.be/qeeRsWofAv0?t=1229)
