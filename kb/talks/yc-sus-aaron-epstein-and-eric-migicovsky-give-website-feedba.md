# YC SUS: Aaron Epstein and Eric Migicovsky give website feedback — Compiled Truth

Aaron Epstein and Eric Migicovsky do live, cold-eyes teardown reviews of Startup School founders' landing pages, repeatedly flagging vague copy, missing "who is this for," and weak trust signals as the core problems.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Social proof like signup counts, urgency badges, and "X companies did this action" stats significantly increase conversion, echoing tactics used by Airbnb and booking.com [yc-sus-aaron-epstein-and-eric-migicovsky-give-website-feedba @ 02:44](https://youtu.be/MP32_y4JZDQ?t=164)
- Sell benefits, not features — frame what you do as the "superpower" you give people rather than the literal technical capability [yc-sus-aaron-epstein-and-eric-migicovsky-give-website-feedba @ 06:34](https://youtu.be/MP32_y4JZDQ?t=394)
- A landing page needs to hit a baseline of trust and credibility (logos, product images, founder story) before asking visitors to share sensitive info, or the page itself can hurt conversion [yc-sus-aaron-epstein-and-eric-migicovsky-give-website-feedba @ 10:35](https://youtu.be/MP32_y4JZDQ?t=635)
- Every page should drive toward a single clear call to action placed prominently (e.g., in the header), not buried further down the page [yc-sus-aaron-epstein-and-eric-migicovsky-give-website-feedba @ 11:15](https://youtu.be/MP32_y4JZDQ?t=675)
- Founders should resist describing their product for "everybody" — focusing the copy on who benefits most makes the value proposition instantly clear to that audience [yc-sus-aaron-epstein-and-eric-migicovsky-give-website-feedba @ 21:55](https://youtu.be/MP32_y4JZDQ?t=1315)
- Concrete customer walkthroughs ("Company X came to us with this problem, here's what happened") are far more persuasive than abstract step-by-step explanations of how a product works [yc-sus-aaron-epstein-and-eric-migicovsky-give-website-feedba @ 23:13](https://youtu.be/MP32_y4JZDQ?t=1393)
- Use familiar e-commerce/UI patterns (like Shopify) instead of custom flows, since users already have mental models for how checkout and similar processes should work [yc-sus-aaron-epstein-and-eric-migicovsky-give-website-feedba @ 27:22](https://youtu.be/MP32_y4JZDQ?t=1642)
- Visitors scan headlines and images rather than reading blocks of text, so the core explanation of the product must be conveyed visually and in short callouts, not paragraphs [yc-sus-aaron-epstein-and-eric-migicovsky-give-website-feedba @ 24:19](https://youtu.be/MP32_y4JZDQ?t=1459)
- When copy is confusing, take the actual words customers use to describe the product (from talking to them) and put those exact phrases on the homepage instead of internal jargon [yc-sus-aaron-epstein-and-eric-migicovsky-give-website-feedba @ 36:32](https://youtu.be/MP32_y4JZDQ?t=2192)
- It's better to show than tell — communicate ease of use and value through a concrete example or visual rather than stating adjectives like "easy to use" [yc-sus-aaron-epstein-and-eric-migicovsky-give-website-feedba @ 1:16:37](https://youtu.be/MP32_y4JZDQ?t=4597)
