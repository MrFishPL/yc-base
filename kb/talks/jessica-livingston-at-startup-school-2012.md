# Jessica Livingston at Startup School 2012 — Compiled Truth

Jessica Livingston frames early startup failure as a gauntlet of recurring "monsters" — external doubt, co-founder breakups, investor herd mentality, distracting corp-dev meetings, and above all the difficulty of making something people want — and argues resilience plus drive is the core defense.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Even hugely successful startups like Airbnb looked like an "ugly duckling" at launch (a site to rent airbeds at a design conference) and got little external validation compared to a conventional job [jessica-livingston-at-startup-school-2012 @ 02:36](https://youtu.be/KQJ6zsNCA-4?t=156)
- By the time Airbnb entered YC the founders had maxed out credit cards and were eating leftover cereal, yet during YC they talked to users, set goals, and measured everything, and the metrics turned around [jessica-livingston-at-startup-school-2012 @ 03:45](https://youtu.be/KQJ6zsNCA-4?t=225)
- Pebble's Eric Migicovsky was rejected by more than 30 investors who wouldn't fund hardware, so he skipped investors and raised $10.2 million on Kickstarter instead of his $100k goal [jessica-livingston-at-startup-school-2012 @ 04:37](https://youtu.be/KQJ6zsNCA-4?t=277)
- Founders should focus almost exclusively on writing code, talking to users, and exercising during early building — networking and hiring interns are the wrong use of scarce time [jessica-livingston-at-startup-school-2012 @ 16:50](https://youtu.be/KQJ6zsNCA-4?t=1010)
- Corp-dev conversations are a uniquely dangerous distraction: founders get flattered into thinking an acquisition is imminent, lose momentum on the actual startup, and often end up settling for an offer no better than a job [jessica-livingston-at-startup-school-2012 @ 17:54](https://youtu.be/KQJ6zsNCA-4?t=1074)
- Not making something people want is the single biggest cause of early startup failure, and founders typically have to change their idea substantially — Airbnb went from conference airbeds to renting a couch to whole-home rentals [jessica-livingston-at-startup-school-2012 @ 19:19](https://youtu.be/KQJ6zsNCA-4?t=1159)
- Investors have a herd mentality — nobody wants to be first, so founders must work to land the first few believers before the crowd follows [jessica-livingston-at-startup-school-2012 @ 13:21](https://youtu.be/KQJ6zsNCA-4?t=801)
- Fundraising is a necessary distraction that can stall a whole company while founders wait on investors who face no cost from delaying [jessica-livingston-at-startup-school-2012 @ 14:22](https://youtu.be/KQJ6zsNCA-4?t=862)
- Co-founder breakups are the second-biggest cause of startup failure YC sees, and founders should treat early doubts about a co-founder's trustworthiness or competence as a red flag, not something to ignore [jessica-livingston-at-startup-school-2012 @ 12:33](https://youtu.be/KQJ6zsNCA-4?t=753)
- A deal is "not a deal till the money is in the bank" — she recounts a startup that sold homes and relocated the whole team only to have a VC back out days before wiring funds [jessica-livingston-at-startup-school-2012 @ 22:21](https://youtu.be/KQJ6zsNCA-4?t=1341)
