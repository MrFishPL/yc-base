# Startup Hiring Advice from Lever CEO Sarah Nahm with Holly Liu — Compiled Truth

Lever CEO Sarah Nahm argues that hiring is a founder's number one job, and that startups win talent not through resources but through proactive outreach, honest "impact"-based job descriptions, and deliberately building an inclusive culture from day one.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Startups can't just post a job and wait for great people to apply — proactive, outbound recruiting is the strategy, the same way you can't just wait for customers to find you [startup-hiring-advice-from-lever-ceo-sarah-nahm-with-holly-l @ 19:05](https://youtu.be/UKVmfinc8qE?t=1145)
- At Lever, 81% of the engineering team built by co-founder Nate Smith was proactively sourced rather than inbound [startup-hiring-advice-from-lever-ceo-sarah-nahm-with-holly-l @ 20:24](https://youtu.be/UKVmfinc8qE?t=1224)
- Nurturing candidate relationships for 2-3 years before they're ready to move pays off: those people get hired 35% faster than first-time contacts, per Lever's own data [startup-hiring-advice-from-lever-ceo-sarah-nahm-with-holly-l @ 21:10](https://youtu.be/UKVmfinc8qE?t=1270)
- The cliché that founders should spend 50% of their time on hiring is true, and it takes many forms including personally meeting people with no strings attached [startup-hiring-advice-from-lever-ceo-sarah-nahm-with-holly-l @ 21:30](https://youtu.be/UKVmfinc8qE?t=1290)
- Lever's first hiring stage isn't a screening call, it's explicitly called "motivation fit," probing whether a candidate's career goals align with the company and role [startup-hiring-advice-from-lever-ceo-sarah-nahm-with-holly-l @ 24:21](https://youtu.be/UKVmfinc8qE?t=1461)
- Behavioral interviewing (a "career trajectory" walk through someone's whole career) de-risks hiring for roles founders have never personally done [startup-hiring-advice-from-lever-ceo-sarah-nahm-with-holly-l @ 26:53](https://youtu.be/UKVmfinc8qE?t=1613)
- Job postings should be written as "impact descriptions" — what someone needs to achieve in 1/3/6/12 months — rather than lists of required skills and tasks [startup-hiring-advice-from-lever-ceo-sarah-nahm-with-holly-l @ 33:08](https://youtu.be/UKVmfinc8qE?t=1988)
- Impact descriptions instead of skills-requirement lists attract more top talent and drive more diverse applicants by not broadcasting assumptions about who should apply [startup-hiring-advice-from-lever-ceo-sarah-nahm-with-holly-l @ 33:08](https://youtu.be/UKVmfinc8qE?t=1988)
- Small, seemingly mundane decisions — like who does the office dishes or which holidays make the company calendar — are "cultural surface area" that founders must consciously and continuously manage [startup-hiring-advice-from-lever-ceo-sarah-nahm-with-holly-l @ 41:50](https://youtu.be/UKVmfinc8qE?t=2510)
- Lever deliberately rejected the "technical vs. non-technical" stereotype, training staff so non-technical people adopt an engineering mindset and technical people deeply understand customers and go-to-market [startup-hiring-advice-from-lever-ceo-sarah-nahm-with-holly-l @ 46:20](https://youtu.be/UKVmfinc8qE?t=2780)
- A founder's number one job is recruiting — embracing that fully is the shortest path to building both a strong culture and strong results [startup-hiring-advice-from-lever-ceo-sarah-nahm-with-holly-l @ 57:19](https://youtu.be/UKVmfinc8qE?t=3439)
