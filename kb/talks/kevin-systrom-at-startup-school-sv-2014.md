# Kevin Systrom at Startup School SV 2014 — Compiled Truth

Instagram's founder traces the winding path from a Stanford classifieds site through Odeo, Google, and the HTML5 check-in app Burbn to Instagram, arguing that skills and relationships built along the way matter more than any single idea being "right."

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- His first startup, a Stanford classifieds site called TheTreeList, taught him Ruby on Rails and marketing rather than producing a lasting product — the skills learned mattered more than the idea itself [kevin-systrom-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 03:08](https://youtu.be/_h_6xM36Z4g?t=188)
- He credits a Marines concept, "bias towards action," for the idea that founders should act with the information and risk they have rather than searching for the perfect next move [kevin-systrom-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 08:02](https://youtu.be/_h_6xM36Z4g?t=482)
- He describes generating startup ideas via "combinatorial entrepreneurship" — combining hot trends like HTML5, check-ins, badges, and points until something interesting emerges [kevin-systrom-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 14:09](https://youtu.be/_h_6xM36Z4g?t=849)
- Burbn, the HTML5 location check-in app, wasn't gaining traction because it was slow and couldn't get a home-screen icon, pushing the team to go native [kevin-systrom-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 15:12](https://youtu.be/_h_6xM36Z4g?t=912)
- The team noticed photo-sharing was the part of Burbn people loved most, which is what led them to strip everything else away and pivot to Instagram [kevin-systrom-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 16:43](https://youtu.be/_h_6xM36Z4g?t=1003)
- Systrom built Instagram's first filter, X-Pro II, in one afternoon on a $40-a-night hostel's dial-up connection after his girlfriend pushed back on posting unfiltered photos [kevin-systrom-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 17:49](https://youtu.be/_h_6xM36Z4g?t=1069)
- It took about eight weeks to build the first native version of Instagram, which had 25,000 signups on day one and kept growing without stopping [kevin-systrom-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 18:20](https://youtu.be/_h_6xM36Z4g?t=1100)
- Great hiring and launch support start well before you need them — relationships built over years at prior jobs (including meeting Jack Dorsey on his first day at Odeo) fueled Instagram's launch buzz [kevin-systrom-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 19:15](https://youtu.be/_h_6xM36Z4g?t=1155)
- Founders have to be their own advocate because everyone will tell you your idea won't work, and since most ideas genuinely don't, that feedback is usually "right" but not useful [kevin-systrom-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 20:28](https://youtu.be/_h_6xM36Z4g?t=1228)
- Instagram's first company value was "Community First," and its very first hire was a community manager rather than an engineer, because users were treated as the company's greatest asset [kevin-systrom-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 25:16](https://youtu.be/_h_6xM36Z4g?t=1516)
