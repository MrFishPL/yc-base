# Scaling Product | Fireside with Joe Gebbia and Reid Hoffman — Compiled Truth

Airbnb co-founder Joe Gebbia recounts how abandoning the "solve it with code" myth and doing unscalable, hands-on work (like personally photographing hosts' apartments) surfaced the product-market gap and drove Airbnb's early growth, plus how the company preserved its design and trust culture while scaling.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Paul Graham told the Airbnb founders it was okay to do things that don't scale, which unlocked the idea of personally flying to New York to take professional photos of host listings instead of coding a fix [scaling-product-fireside-with-joe-gebbia-and-reid-hoffman @ 02:35](https://youtu.be/TCA_7RVo7Uo?t=155)
- Sitting in hosts' living rooms doing photography ("ethnographic research") revealed UX train wrecks — tasks meant to take two steps were taking twelve — that the founders had no idea existed while coding remotely from San Francisco [scaling-product-fireside-with-joe-gebbia-and-reid-hoffman @ 04:51](https://youtu.be/TCA_7RVo7Uo?t=291)
- Implementing host feedback the same night it was given, combined with better photos, doubled weekly revenue from $200 to $400 and drove pure word-of-mouth growth from 30 to 65 New York listings with no press or paid marketing [scaling-product-fireside-with-joe-gebbia-and-reid-hoffman @ 09:09](https://youtu.be/TCA_7RVo7Uo?t=549)
- The unscalable photography process was only industrialized into a scalable, free, on-demand photographer network after the founders fully understood the operation from the inside out [scaling-product-fireside-with-joe-gebbia-and-reid-hoffman @ 10:23](https://youtu.be/TCA_7RVo7Uo?t=623)
- Airbnb had to explicitly design for trust between strangers, discovering through iteration that message length had a sweet spot — too short (e.g. "yo") or too long and oversharing both dropped host acceptance rates [scaling-product-fireside-with-joe-gebbia-and-reid-hoffman @ 13:53](https://youtu.be/TCA_7RVo7Uo?t=833)
- Tony Shay's advice — get culture right early because it's easier to mold concrete when wet than to chip away at it dry, and that people make the culture rather than the other way around — shaped how Airbnb hired [scaling-product-fireside-with-joe-gebbia-and-reid-hoffman @ 15:45](https://youtu.be/TCA_7RVo7Uo?t=945)
- To scale founder involvement in hiring, Airbnb created "core values interviewers," training employees to screen for belief in the company's mission the same way the founders would in every interview [scaling-product-fireside-with-joe-gebbia-and-reid-hoffman @ 16:54](https://youtu.be/TCA_7RVo7Uo?t=1014)
- After growing too fast with property-management companies as supply (which optimized for profit over guest experience and caused a spike in complaints), Airbnb refocused on primary-home hosts as its core supply [scaling-product-fireside-with-joe-gebbia-and-reid-hoffman @ 26:44](https://youtu.be/TCA_7RVo7Uo?t=1604)
- Reid Hoffman frames a useful design exercise as first imagining the perfect, non-scalable experience as a "North Star," then working backward to what's economically and operationally feasible [scaling-product-fireside-with-joe-gebbia-and-reid-hoffman @ 21:40](https://youtu.be/TCA_7RVo7Uo?t=1300)
- Looking back, Gebbia says Airbnb was too averse to hiring senior leadership early and wishes they had hired ahead of anticipated scale rather than waiting until it was urgently needed [scaling-product-fireside-with-joe-gebbia-and-reid-hoffman @ 25:07](https://youtu.be/TCA_7RVo7Uo?t=1507)
