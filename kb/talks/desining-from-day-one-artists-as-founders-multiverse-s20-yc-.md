# Desining from Day One: Artists as Founders: Multiverse (S20) - YC Gaming Tech Talks 2020 — Compiled Truth

Multiverse's founders argue that professional illustrators make excellent startup co-founders because entertainment products require a compelling point of view that can't be tested the way typical tech products are, and illustrators already run entrepreneurial, audience-driven businesses of their own.

- Multiverse's founding team paired two MIT engineers with a professional illustrator, Sarah, as creative director and co-founder [desining-from-day-one-artists-as-founders-multiverse-s20-yc- @ 00:41](https://youtu.be/elVbENZLbak?t=41)
- Entertainment products can't be tested right away like typical tech products — you need a compelling point of view and trust that people want to hear it before you know how they'll react [desining-from-day-one-artists-as-founders-multiverse-s20-yc- @ 02:08](https://youtu.be/elVbENZLbak?t=128)

- Illustrators, especially those active on social media, already have founder-like skills — they run businesses, generate recurring revenue via Patreon/Twitch subs, and pull in tens of thousands of dollars at conventions [desining-from-day-one-artists-as-founders-multiverse-s20-yc- @ 04:24](https://youtu.be/elVbENZLbak?t=264)
