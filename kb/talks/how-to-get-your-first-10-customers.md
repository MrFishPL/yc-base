# How to Get Your First 10 Customers — Compiled Truth

A YC partner compiles founder-sourced tactics showing that the first 10 customers come not from tools but from founders personally tapping their network and doing unscalable, in-person work.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Before picking a channel, figure out where your target customer actually spends their time, since cold email/LinkedIn only works for buyers who live on a laptop [how-to-get-your-first-10-customers @ 01:03](https://youtu.be/_FBivfgOvuE?t=63)
- A founder who struggled for months with cold email closed more deals in 3 days at an industry trade show than in 3 months of outreach [how-to-get-your-first-10-customers @ 01:50](https://youtu.be/_FBivfgOvuE?t=110)
- The first two or three customers almost always come from a founder's warm network — former colleagues, classmates, and friends — because early buyers are betting on the founder's trustworthiness [how-to-get-your-first-10-customers @ 02:39](https://youtu.be/_FBivfgOvuE?t=159)
- Prospecting tools like Apollo and Clay only start to matter once a founder already has 10-20 quality customers from their own network [how-to-get-your-first-10-customers @ 04:06](https://youtu.be/_FBivfgOvuE?t=246)
- Showing up in person — flying to buyers repeatedly, attending small industry conferences, or hosting micro founder-dinners for 6-10 prospects — converts far better than cold outreach [how-to-get-your-first-10-customers @ 04:23](https://youtu.be/_FBivfgOvuE?t=263)
- Finding where customers already complain online (Reddit threads, Facebook groups, industry forums) and DMing them directly sourced entire first-customer cohorts for some founders [how-to-get-your-first-10-customers @ 06:09](https://youtu.be/_FBivfgOvuE?t=369)
- Framing early outreach as asking for advice, mentorship, or feedback (while genuinely meaning it) opened doors that a direct sales pitch would not [how-to-get-your-first-10-customers @ 08:22](https://youtu.be/_FBivfgOvuE?t=502)
- Effective cold outreach copy should stay under 75 words and have one unmistakably clear call to action [how-to-get-your-first-10-customers @ 10:07](https://youtu.be/_FBivfgOvuE?t=607)
- Giving the prospect something valuable before asking for anything, like a free audit or vulnerability scan, was the highest-converting outreach approach for several founders [how-to-get-your-first-10-customers @ 10:57](https://youtu.be/_FBivfgOvuE?t=657)
- Customers 1-3 come from personal network, 4-10 come from unscalable manual work, and only 10-50 is where automated, higher-volume tools like Apollo and Clay start to make sense [how-to-get-your-first-10-customers @ 11:49](https://youtu.be/_FBivfgOvuE?t=709)
