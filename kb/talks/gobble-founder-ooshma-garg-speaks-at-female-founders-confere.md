# Gobble founder Ooshma Garg speaks at Female Founders Conference 2016 — Compiled Truth

Ooshma Garg recounts Gobble's five-year "snakes and ladders" journey through a failed launch, two rejected Series A raises, and multiple product pivots before finding product-market fit with a personalized dinner-kit subscription.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Gobble started as Ooshma solving her own problem of eating unhealthy takeout alone, prototyping the idea with just a Craigslist ad, email, PayPal, and her own car as a delivery vehicle [gobble-founder-ooshma-garg-speaks-at-female-founders-confere @ 04:31](https://youtu.be/A21qyXsAfME?t=271)
- She decided to start the company without a technical co-founder after failing to find one who was equally passionate, and investors backed her anyway because of her passion, market proof, and prior startup experience [gobble-founder-ooshma-garg-speaks-at-female-founders-confere @ 06:55](https://youtu.be/A21qyXsAfME?t=415)
- Their first engineer quit weeks before launch, forcing her to beg a contractor to learn the codebase and delaying the launch — one of the earliest "snakes" in the company's history [gobble-founder-ooshma-garg-speaks-at-female-founders-confere @ 08:04](https://youtu.be/A21qyXsAfME?t=484)
- A Series A round she believed was a "done deal" collapsed after she shared her fundraising process with a single investor instead of running a broad process with 40 firms, teaching her that a deal isn't done until the money is in the bank [gobble-founder-ooshma-garg-speaks-at-female-founders-confere @ 10:38](https://youtu.be/A21qyXsAfME?t=638)
- An on-demand delivery pivot ("Gobble Instant") was a total flop because food wilted during 30-minute drives to suburban customers and every order lost money [gobble-founder-ooshma-garg-speaks-at-female-founders-confere @ 12:21](https://youtu.be/A21qyXsAfME?t=741)
- Realizing dinner is a daily, low-variance habit, she pivoted to a personalized dinner subscription modeled on how Netflix and Pandora learn taste, which generated five times the revenue of any prior version [gobble-founder-ooshma-garg-speaks-at-female-founders-confere @ 13:51](https://youtu.be/A21qyXsAfME?t=831)
- Facing a near-empty bank account and two failed Series A attempts, she joined YC as a last resort, calling it a choice between "join YC or die," and it became a lifeline that helped the team refocus on the basics [gobble-founder-ooshma-garg-speaks-at-female-founders-confere @ 16:33](https://youtu.be/A21qyXsAfME?t=993)
- Hiring a 10x executive chef and lead engineer let the team apply the restaurant concept of "mise en place" to create dinner kits that customers could finish cooking themselves, which triggered viral word-of-mouth growth and product-market fit with zero marketing hires [gobble-founder-ooshma-garg-speaks-at-female-founders-confere @ 20:58](https://youtu.be/A21qyXsAfME?t=1258)
- The same two firms that had rejected her Series A twice before eventually led the round once product-market fit was clear, teaching her to accept rejection with grace since startups are a long game [gobble-founder-ooshma-garg-speaks-at-female-founders-confere @ 22:22](https://youtu.be/A21qyXsAfME?t=1342)
