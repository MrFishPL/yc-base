# How To Compete With Amazon and Google — Compiled Truth

Founders routinely psych themselves out over competitors when they should instead focus on building a better product and tracking their own metrics; big companies rarely nail the products they copy, so structural advantage alone rarely wins.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Founders often replace excitement about their company with fear the moment they hear a competitor raised money, even though that's a weak signal [how-to-compete-with-amazon-and-google @ 01:04](https://youtu.be/tzsmJtKZ2No?t=64)
- A dangerous founder mindset is assuming a competitor's product roadmap must be great and copying it without first-principles thinking about your own customers [how-to-compete-with-amazon-and-google @ 02:07](https://youtu.be/tzsmJtKZ2No?t=127)
- Most startups fail outright, and many markets support multiple winners, so founders shouldn't assume a competitor's mere existence dooms them [how-to-compete-with-amazon-and-google @ 02:28](https://youtu.be/tzsmJtKZ2No?t=148)
- Every YC company, even ones that eventually become huge, has some fatal-looking flaw when a partner examines it closely, which founders can't see from inside their own company [how-to-compete-with-amazon-and-google @ 05:02](https://youtu.be/tzsmJtKZ2No?t=302)
- The right questions to defuse competitor fear are whether customers are actually talking about the competitor and whether deals are being lost, not press or hiring announcements [how-to-compete-with-amazon-and-google @ 06:01](https://youtu.be/tzsmJtKZ2No?t=361)
- At Justin.tv/Twitch, copying a competitor's copyright-takedown feature that had no user value was a sign the whole industry was watching each other instead of talking to users, and the company only improved once it focused on user needs [how-to-compete-with-amazon-and-google @ 07:20](https://youtu.be/tzsmJtKZ2No?t=440)
- Claiming "we have no competitors" in a YC interview is a red flag showing a lack of understanding of how customers currently solve the problem [how-to-compete-with-amazon-and-google @ 09:03](https://youtu.be/tzsmJtKZ2No?t=543)
- Real structural advantage plus a genuinely good product is dangerous (e.g. Facebook launching Custom Audiences and cutting off Perfect Audience's ad exchange access, prompting a sale), but structural advantage paired with a mediocre product still loses [how-to-compete-with-amazon-and-google @ 10:32](https://youtu.be/tzsmJtKZ2No?t=632)
- Big incumbents often fail to "nail it" even when they copy a startup's product, as shown by Amazon failing to out-execute Instacart on something as simple as picking good avocados [how-to-compete-with-amazon-and-google @ 13:40](https://youtu.be/tzsmJtKZ2No?t=820)
- The core advice is depressing but simple: it's a hard problem, not a complicated one — building a genuinely better product for users is the club that beats land-grab fears and competitor anxiety [how-to-compete-with-amazon-and-google @ 15:09](https://youtu.be/tzsmJtKZ2No?t=909)
