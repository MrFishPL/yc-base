# Alexandr Wang: Building Scale AI, Transforming Work With Agents & Competing With China — Compiled Truth

Scale AI's Alexandr Wang traces the company's path from a "chatbot for doctors" idea through Mechanical-Turk-style data labeling to a self-driving-focused pivot and, later, an infinite-market agentic-applications business, while arguing that specialized data and models — not shared evals — are the real enterprise moat.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Scale's first idea (chatbots for doctors) failed because young founders are highly mimetic and don't yet know where their unique "alpha" is [alexandr-wang-building-scale-ai-transforming-work-with-agent @ 04:19](https://youtu.be/5noIKN8t69U?t=259)
- The pivot to "an API for human labor" beat incumbent Mechanical Turk because a widely-used-but-hated product is usually a sign of opportunity [alexandr-wang-building-scale-ai-transforming-work-with-agent @ 07:32](https://youtu.be/5noIKN8t69U?t=452)
- Narrowly focusing on the seemingly small self-driving-car data market, against investor pushback, gave Scale the momentum to scale quickly even though it wasn't ultimately a big enough market alone [alexandr-wang-building-scale-ai-transforming-work-with-agent @ 08:58](https://youtu.be/5noIKN8t69U?t=538)
- The real long-term moat isn't shared evals but a firm's proprietary data, environments, and fine-tuned models built on its own unique business problems [alexandr-wang-building-scale-ai-transforming-work-with-agent @ 17:12](https://youtu.be/5noIKN8t69U?t=1032)
