# Mariya Nurislamova, Founder of Scentbird at the Female Founders Conference — Compiled Truth

Scentbird's founder recounts a "year of misery" spent building fragrance-discovery products in isolation from customers, and how a mentor's reframing plus finally talking to users led to the subscription model that made the company work.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The founding team spent a full year reading books about building an MVP while never talking to a single customer, building a fragrance recommendation engine that turned out to be unmonetizable [mariya-nurislamova-founder-of-scentbird-at-the-female-founde @ 00:38](https://youtu.be/gmA-BOIVKQU?t=38).
- Early paid-sample and "try before you buy" full-bottle models each failed commercially — the sample kit converted almost no one to buy a full bottle, and the try-before-you-buy model suffered a 30% fraud rate [mariya-nurislamova-founder-of-scentbird-at-the-female-founde @ 02:10](https://youtu.be/gmA-BOIVKQU?t=130) and [mariya-nurislamova-founder-of-scentbird-at-the-female-founde @ 03:40](https://youtu.be/gmA-BOIVKQU?t=220).
- A phone call with mentor Michael Seibel reframed the whole business: instead of asking people to find one signature fragrance, what if they wanted to rotate fragrances like clothing — leading to the subscription rental model within two hours [mariya-nurislamova-founder-of-scentbird-at-the-female-founde @ 04:56](https://youtu.be/gmA-BOIVKQU?t=296).
- The pivot to a $15/month fragrance subscription worked immediately, generating 105 orders in the first week after prior models had produced almost nothing [mariya-nurislamova-founder-of-scentbird-at-the-female-founde @ 06:33](https://youtu.be/gmA-BOIVKQU?t=393).
- Investors offered predatory terms — one pair offered $25,000 instead of the requested $250,000 at a 3x lower valuation plus 5% advisory shares, and a real estate investor wanted 10% equity for a year of office space [mariya-nurislamova-founder-of-scentbird-at-the-female-founde @ 09:12](https://youtu.be/gmA-BOIVKQU?t=552).
- She learned a "scarcity" fundraising tactic: pitch a round as almost fully subscribed (e.g., say only $50K is left in a $150K round) to create urgency, which got 5 yeses out of 6 pitches [mariya-nurislamova-founder-of-scentbird-at-the-female-founde @ 11:20](https://youtu.be/gmA-BOIVKQU?t=680).
- Scentbird was rejected by YC twice before getting in on the third application, after which YC's emphasis on true user interviews and week-over-week growth changed the company's DNA [mariya-nurislamova-founder-of-scentbird-at-the-female-founde @ 12:47](https://youtu.be/gmA-BOIVKQU?t=767) and [mariya-nurislamova-founder-of-scentbird-at-the-female-founde @ 13:01](https://youtu.be/gmA-BOIVKQU?t=781).
- A user interview where a woman feared Scentbird was "going out of business" and offered to buy 10 subscriptions to keep it alive was the moment she realized they were building something people genuinely wanted [mariya-nurislamova-founder-of-scentbird-at-the-female-founde @ 13:17](https://youtu.be/gmA-BOIVKQU?t=797).
- Key lessons for founders: hire 10x people instead of the cheapest candidates, have and openly communicate an audacious goal, and don't take advice from people who don't have what you want [mariya-nurislamova-founder-of-scentbird-at-the-female-founde @ 14:09](https://youtu.be/gmA-BOIVKQU?t=849) and [mariya-nurislamova-founder-of-scentbird-at-the-female-founde @ 15:41](https://youtu.be/gmA-BOIVKQU?t=941).
