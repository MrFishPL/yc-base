# Mark Zuckerberg On Yahoo's Billion Dollar Offer — Compiled Truth

Mark Zuckerberg recounts turning down Yahoo's billion-dollar acquisition offer for Facebook a couple of years in, and the painful fallout of losing most of the early management team who disagreed with the decision.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Before the Yahoo offer, the team simply came in each day and did whatever seemed like the right next thing to do, without a grander plan [mark-zuckerberg-on-yahoo-s-billion-dollar-offer @ 00:12](https://youtu.be/mH11ImPJeDc?t=12)
- Yahoo offered about a billion dollars only a couple of years into the company, when Facebook had just 10 million users, so success was far from obvious [mark-zuckerberg-on-yahoo-s-billion-dollar-offer @ 00:57](https://youtu.be/mH11ImPJeDc?t=57)
- Zuckerberg and Dustin decided they could connect far more than the 10 million people in schools and chose to go for it instead of selling [mark-zuckerberg-on-yahoo-s-billion-dollar-offer @ 01:32](https://youtu.be/mH11ImPJeDc?t=92)
- He admits he hadn't communicated well that the company was pursuing a bigger mission, so many early joiners weren't aligned with that goal [mark-zuckerberg-on-yahoo-s-billion-dollar-offer @ 01:45](https://youtu.be/mH11ImPJeDc?t=105)
- The most painful part wasn't rejecting the offer itself but that a huge amount of the company, including essentially the whole management team, quit within about a year because they didn't believe in the direction [mark-zuckerberg-on-yahoo-s-billion-dollar-offer @ 02:17](https://youtu.be/mH11ImPJeDc?t=137)
- The decision was validated quickly: the month after turning down the offer, Facebook launched Newsfeed, which became one of the most-used products in the world [mark-zuckerberg-on-yahoo-s-billion-dollar-offer @ 03:09](https://youtu.be/mH11ImPJeDc?t=189)
- Opening signups to anyone right after immediately accelerated growth, and within a few months it was clear the decision to stay independent was right [mark-zuckerberg-on-yahoo-s-billion-dollar-offer @ 03:09](https://youtu.be/mH11ImPJeDc?t=189)
- Later decisions became harder still, since some bets on company direction or billions of dollars of spend don't get validated for five to ten years [mark-zuckerberg-on-yahoo-s-billion-dollar-offer @ 03:27](https://youtu.be/mH11ImPJeDc?t=207)
- After going through that experience once, Zuckerberg resolved to hire people who want to be at the company for the long term rather than for a quick exit [mark-zuckerberg-on-yahoo-s-billion-dollar-offer @ 03:41](https://youtu.be/mH11ImPJeDc?t=221)
