# Why Startup Founders Should Launch Companies Sooner Than They Think — Compiled Truth

A panel of YC partners argues that founders delay launching out of fear, but launching early and often is the fastest way to learn whether an idea works.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Founders often believe their launch is a one-shot, unforgettable event like an Apple product launch, but almost no one remembers the launches of even the biggest companies (Uber, DoorDash, Google) [why-startup-founders-should-launch-companies-sooner-than-the @ 03:12](https://youtu.be/Nsx5RDVKZSk?t=192)
- Working at a big company before founding a startup can instill a false "one shot, get it right" mental model of launching that doesn't apply to startups [why-startup-founders-should-launch-companies-sooner-than-the @ 03:45](https://youtu.be/Nsx5RDVKZSk?t=225)
- Delaying launch delays the real learning that comes from having actual people react to your product [why-startup-founders-should-launch-companies-sooner-than-the @ 02:03](https://youtu.be/Nsx5RDVKZSk?t=123)
- Airbnb launched three times before anyone paid attention, improving the product each time [why-startup-founders-should-launch-companies-sooner-than-the @ 02:23](https://youtu.be/Nsx5RDVKZSk?t=143)
- Early-stage B2B sales isn't about convincing everyone to use the product, it's about filtering a large sign-up pool down to the handful of customers with a hair-on-fire problem [why-startup-founders-should-launch-companies-sooner-than-the @ 07:40](https://youtu.be/Nsx5RDVKZSk?t=460)
- YC tells founders to focus on building something 100 people love rather than something a million people merely like, since those 100 become a viral salesforce [why-startup-founders-should-launch-companies-sooner-than-the @ 10:13](https://youtu.be/Nsx5RDVKZSk?t=613)
- If nobody uses the product after launch, treat it as an analytical problem: tweak one variable at a time (messaging, targeting) and observe the effect [why-startup-founders-should-launch-companies-sooner-than-the @ 11:20](https://youtu.be/Nsx5RDVKZSk?t=680)
- If a launch is fully stalled, ask which of your assumptions was wrong rather than assuming the idea itself is bad [why-startup-founders-should-launch-companies-sooner-than-the @ 12:22](https://youtu.be/Nsx5RDVKZSk?t=742)
- Framing a risky launch as a learning goal rather than a revenue goal removes the possibility of failure, since any reaction (even hate) is progress [why-startup-founders-should-launch-companies-sooner-than-the @ 13:27](https://youtu.be/Nsx5RDVKZSk?t=807)
- Cutting scope to the bare core (e.g., Brex's early account setup was just emailing a password) is a way to test true demand before building fully polished features [why-startup-founders-should-launch-companies-sooner-than-the @ 14:18](https://youtu.be/Nsx5RDVKZSk?t=858)
- The only real version of launching "too early" is when the product provides no value at all, such as a broken button that does nothing [why-startup-founders-should-launch-companies-sooner-than-the @ 16:32](https://youtu.be/Nsx5RDVKZSk?t=992)
