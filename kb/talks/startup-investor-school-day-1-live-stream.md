# Startup Investor School Day 1 Live Stream — Compiled Truth

Day one of YC's Startup Investor School opens with Sam Altman's framework for angel investing (power law thinking, founder traits, real vs. fake trends) followed by Carolyn Levy and Kirsty walking through the mechanics of the SAFE.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Angel investing is a power-law business: your single best investment returns more than all others combined, so the right question is "how big could this be if it works," not "what could go wrong" [startup-investor-school-day-1-live-stream @ 15:31](https://youtu.be/4b7EoQTRU88?t=931)
- The best investments are "good ideas that look like bad ideas," which is why the biggest winners are the easiest to talk yourself out of if you start by looking for flaws [startup-investor-school-day-1-live-stream @ 15:50](https://youtu.be/4b7EoQTRU88?t=950)
- A real trend is one where a small number of early users use the product constantly (hourly, not just daily) and spontaneously evangelize it to friends, as the iPhone did versus VR headsets sitting unused on shelves [startup-investor-school-day-1-live-stream @ 35:18](https://youtu.be/4b7EoQTRU88?t=2118)
- Founders who create giant companies tend to share obsession, focus, ability to generate new ideas, strong communication skills, and unusually fast execution and improvement speed [startup-investor-school-day-1-live-stream @ 27:33](https://youtu.be/4b7EoQTRU88?t=1653)
- Prefer a small market growing quickly over a large market today, since huge current markets already have big incumbents and don't let a startup ride a new technology wave [startup-investor-school-day-1-live-stream @ 33:36](https://youtu.be/4b7EoQTRU88?t=2016)
- Investor reputation for treating founders well now matters more than negotiating leverage, since founders reference-check investors just as investors reference-check founders [startup-investor-school-day-1-live-stream @ 19:26](https://youtu.be/4b7EoQTRU88?t=1166)
- The SAFE (simple agreement for future equity) is not debt, has no interest or maturity date, and only two negotiated terms: investment amount and valuation cap [startup-investor-school-day-1-live-stream @ 1:13:30](https://youtu.be/4b7EoQTRU88?t=4410)
- When a priced round closes, the option pool is created/increased first, then SAFEs convert into shares using the cap, and finally new money buys shares at the round's price, which determines a SAFE holder's real ownership percentage [startup-investor-school-day-1-live-stream @ 1:20:35](https://youtu.be/4b7EoQTRU88?t=4835)
