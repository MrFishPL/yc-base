# How to Get and Test Startup Ideas - Michael Seibel — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel argues founders should start from problems rather than ideas, use personal connection to a problem as a filter, and validate quickly with a scrappy MVP tested on hand-picked initial users.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Your initial idea does not need to be great to start a company — Justin.tv began as an online reality TV show and became Twitch, sold to Amazon for almost a billion dollars [how-to-get-and-test-startup-ideas-michael-seibel @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/vDXkpJw16os?t=0)
- Start with a problem rather than an idea, because ideas get graded immediately while problems are easier to reason about [how-to-get-and-test-startup-ideas-michael-seibel @ 00:56](https://youtu.be/vDXkpJw16os?t=56)
- Judge a problem by whether you have a personal relationship with it — yourself, friends, family, work, or community — since that connection helps validate the solution and sustains you through discouragement [how-to-get-and-test-startup-ideas-michael-seibel @ 01:18](https://youtu.be/vDXkpJw16os?t=78)
- Keep a "problem book" instead of an idea book, noting problems you and others encounter daily [how-to-get-and-test-startup-ideas-michael-seibel @ 01:57](https://youtu.be/vDXkpJw16os?t=117)
- Brainstorming about problems with friends is also a way to recruit co-founders, by seeing whether you trade and build ideas well together [how-to-get-and-test-startup-ideas-michael-seibel @ 02:13](https://youtu.be/vDXkpJw16os?t=133)
- Ask why you are uniquely qualified to work on the problem now — looking for a distinct angle, perspective, or personal experience rather than a resume credential [how-to-get-and-test-startup-ideas-michael-seibel @ 02:33](https://youtu.be/vDXkpJw16os?t=153)
- Study prior attempts and failed products addressing the same problem to gut-check whether your insight is genuinely different [how-to-get-and-test-startup-ideas-michael-seibel @ 03:12](https://youtu.be/vDXkpJw16os?t=192)
- Don't fall in love with your MVP — it likely won't solve the problem well, and it's only the first step in learning whether the problem is solvable at all [how-to-get-and-test-startup-ideas-michael-seibel @ 03:49](https://youtu.be/vDXkpJw16os?t=229)
- Release the MVP quickly, in days or weeks, even if imperfect, to start learning [how-to-get-and-test-startup-ideas-michael-seibel @ 04:25](https://youtu.be/vDXkpJw16os?t=265)
- Hand-pick and heavily filter initial users so they are willing to try a startup and genuinely have the problem — the goal is proving the solution works for some group, not maximizing usage [how-to-get-and-test-startup-ideas-michael-seibel @ 04:47](https://youtu.be/vDXkpJw16os?t=287)
