# How To Build A Tech Startup With No Technical Skills — Compiled Truth

Dalton and Michael argue that non-technical founders' single most distinguishing skill is recruiting a great technical co-founder, and that skipping this step tanks a software startup's odds of success.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The best business founders distinguish themselves not by resume or ideas but by their ability to recruit amazing technical co-founders [how-to-build-a-tech-startup-with-no-technical-skills @ 01:09](https://youtu.be/ZpKu2wvquWg?t=69)
- Trying to build a software startup without a technical co-founder is compared to trying to build a rocket to the moon without knowing physics — you're "not even in the stadium" [how-to-build-a-tech-startup-with-no-technical-skills @ 03:49](https://youtu.be/ZpKu2wvquWg?t=229)
- Michael's own experience at Justin.tv/Twitch as the single non-technical founder among three technical co-founders showed him that "without my three other co-founders none of that happens" [how-to-build-a-tech-startup-with-no-technical-skills @ 04:36](https://youtu.be/ZpKu2wvquWg?t=276)
- Companies like DoorDash and Airbnb lived and died by their speed of shipping software, and using white-label software or an outsourced dev shop instead would have given them a 0% chance of success [how-to-build-a-tech-startup-with-no-technical-skills @ 05:37](https://youtu.be/ZpKu2wvquWg?t=337)
- Starting with a team of business people assessing engineering candidates tends to compound badly, since non-technical founders naturally hire worse engineers first [how-to-build-a-tech-startup-with-no-technical-skills @ 08:37](https://youtu.be/ZpKu2wvquWg?t=517)
- Founders often needlessly disqualify potential co-founders by demanding unrealistic experience (e.g., wanting a CTO with 10 years managing 50-100 people) [how-to-build-a-tech-startup-with-no-technical-skills @ 09:37](https://youtu.be/ZpKu2wvquWg?t=577)
- If you don't know great engineers, the advice is to go get a job at a startup to change who you know rather than start the company right now [how-to-build-a-tech-startup-with-no-technical-skills @ 09:56](https://youtu.be/ZpKu2wvquWg?t=596)
- The recommended exercise: visualize the best engineer you've ever worked with and actually ask them to co-found, rather than assuming they'd say no [how-to-build-a-tech-startup-with-no-technical-skills @ 10:16](https://youtu.be/ZpKu2wvquWg?t=616)
- Pitching a co-founder role as "come build a company together as co-owners" rather than "be my worker to execute my idea" is the key framing that makes top talent say yes [how-to-build-a-tech-startup-with-no-technical-skills @ 11:15](https://youtu.be/ZpKu2wvquWg?t=675)
- Justin Kahn's success recruiting his Justin.tv co-founders is framed as selling the "adventure" of building something together, not just pitching an idea [how-to-build-a-tech-startup-with-no-technical-skills @ 13:12](https://youtu.be/ZpKu2wvquWg?t=792)
