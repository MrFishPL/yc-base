# Cursor Head of Design Roasts Startup Websites — Compiled Truth

Cursor's head of design, Rio Lu, live-reviews YC founders' Cursor-built websites and distills recurring "vibe coded" failure patterns and fixes for landing-page clarity and craft.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Visitors ask four questions on landing: what is this, is it for me, does it work, is it credible — and most reviewed sites made him work too hard to answer them. [cursor-head-of-design-roasts-startup-websites @ 08:06](https://youtu.be/RynySryqM_0?t=486)
- Talk to your actual target user in plain, specific language instead of vague, high-level marketing copy that could describe any product. [cursor-head-of-design-roasts-startup-websites @ 03:33](https://youtu.be/RynySryqM_0?t=213)
- To avoid generic "AI slop" (purple gradients, heavy shadows, default fonts), go safe with system fonts and a small, robust set of design tokens/components that AI can compose well. [cursor-head-of-design-roasts-startup-websites @ 04:32](https://youtu.be/RynySryqM_0?t=272)
- Every scroll section should have exactly one clear primary CTA and message; kill competing buttons, logos, and distractions (Discord, GitHub, docs links) that dilute focus. [cursor-head-of-design-roasts-startup-websites @ 09:18](https://youtu.be/RynySryqM_0?t=558)
- Don't invent your own jargon (e.g. "progressive discovery") — use the words users themselves would say when describing their problem. [cursor-head-of-design-roasts-startup-websites @ 12:05](https://youtu.be/RynySryqM_0?t=725)
- Marketing pages, in-product UI, and sub-pages should share one consistent visual system; switching colors/styles between pages (as several sites did) breaks continuity and trust. [cursor-head-of-design-roasts-startup-websites @ 15:59](https://youtu.be/RynySryqM_0?t=959)
- Describing your product purely in terms of other well-known products ("Cursor for email," "best of Superhuman") signals you haven't defined what you actually do. [cursor-head-of-design-roasts-startup-websites @ 22:03](https://youtu.be/RynySryqM_0?t=1323)
- Distracting scroll-hijacking and moving elements during a voice/product demo undercut otherwise clear messaging. [cursor-head-of-design-roasts-startup-websites @ 23:54](https://youtu.be/RynySryqM_0?t=1434)
- When an AI product has to "think" or generate, show the actual states/steps happening rather than a generic spinner, so users know it's working. [cursor-head-of-design-roasts-startup-websites @ 31:09](https://youtu.be/RynySryqM_0?t=1869)
- Let users try a product for free (a few actions) before hitting a signup wall, rather than blocking the very first interaction. [cursor-head-of-design-roasts-startup-websites @ 34:57](https://youtu.be/RynySryqM_0?t=2097)
