# Updates for Startup School 2019 and Office Hours with Kevin Hale — Compiled Truth

Kevin Hale previews changes to Startup School 2019 (shorter lectures, co-founder matching, city meetups) and gives office-hours advice on customer support, idea evaluation, and fundraising discipline, drawing heavily on his own experience building Wufoo.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Startup School 2019 shifts content toward evaluating ideas like an investor and adds a co-founder matching directory, since 63% of prior participants were single founders [updates-for-startup-school-2019-and-office-hours-with-kevin- @ 02:27](https://youtu.be/kyYHUXxyjvI?t=147)
- Great early customer experience mainly requires three things: respond to every question, respond fast (Wufoo averaged 7-12 minutes), and have the person answering be someone who actually built the software [updates-for-startup-school-2019-and-office-hours-with-kevin- @ 08:47](https://youtu.be/kyYHUXxyjvI?t=527)
- Wufoo deliberately kept its interface simple and resisted adding features/complexity because every added control created more customer support burden [updates-for-startup-school-2019-and-office-hours-with-kevin- @ 10:08](https://youtu.be/kyYHUXxyjvI?t=608)
- Before writing code, Wufoo's founders spent two years building a blog audience of 100,000 subscribers, which gave them trust and a warm audience of ideal customers when they finally launched [updates-for-startup-school-2019-and-office-hours-with-kevin- @ 17:40](https://youtu.be/kyYHUXxyjvI?t=1060)
- Wufoo validated demand pre-product with an interactive "fake" interface demo that collected over 100,000 signups before the real product existed [updates-for-startup-school-2019-and-office-hours-with-kevin- @ 16:34](https://youtu.be/kyYHUXxyjvI?t=994)
- Viral growth came from a freemium "Powered by Wufoo" watermark on free-tier forms and embeddable forms on other websites, which spread the product on users' behalf [updates-for-startup-school-2019-and-office-hours-with-kevin- @ 20:02](https://youtu.be/kyYHUXxyjvI?t=1202)
- YC pivots aren't rare or disqualifying — Wufoo itself pivoted mid-interview from a "reversible forms" content management idea into what became a form builder [updates-for-startup-school-2019-and-office-hours-with-kevin- @ 26:16](https://youtu.be/kyYHUXxyjvI?t=1576)
- YC's seed-stage bar is whether the team and a well-told, sound idea make it plausible to imagine the company succeeding — not proof it will become a $10B company [updates-for-startup-school-2019-and-office-hours-with-kevin- @ 27:35](https://youtu.be/kyYHUXxyjvI?t=1655)
- Product-market fit has no universal definition; some rare companies (like Wufoo) achieve a controlled, disciplined growth path rather than the classic "can't keep up with demand" breakaway feeling [updates-for-startup-school-2019-and-office-hours-with-kevin- @ 36:45](https://youtu.be/kyYHUXxyjvI?t=2205)
- To gauge market size, work bottom-up from pricing to required customer count to reach $100M revenue, and check whether that customer count is plausible relative to total market size [updates-for-startup-school-2019-and-office-hours-with-kevin- @ 44:15](https://youtu.be/kyYHUXxyjvI?t=2655)
- Founders should treat every fundraise as if it's their last and have strong confidence that giving up equity and increasing burn will actually produce growth before doing either [updates-for-startup-school-2019-and-office-hours-with-kevin- @ 40:07](https://youtu.be/kyYHUXxyjvI?t=2407)
