# The Biggest Mistakes First-Time Founders Make - Michael Seibel — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel walks through the recurring, often-avoidable mistakes he sees first-time founders make, from picking a problem they don't truly care about to prioritizing press over product.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Startups often fail because founders lose motivation on a problem they never truly cared about, rather than picking it based on genuine years-long commitment [the-biggest-mistakes-first-time-founders-make-michael-seibel @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/D56QeyyQMLI?t=0)
- At Justin.tv/Twitch, the team only regained love for their product once Emmett refocused it around gamers, a user group he was genuinely passionate about [the-biggest-mistakes-first-time-founders-make-michael-seibel @ 01:06](https://youtu.be/D56QeyyQMLI?t=66)
- Choosing co-founders you don't already know well is risky since startups are hard enough that a pre-existing relationship helps you gauge whether you can get through tough times together [the-biggest-mistakes-first-time-founders-make-michael-seibel @ 01:29](https://youtu.be/D56QeyyQMLI?t=89)
- Co-founding teams need transparent, non-confrontational conversations about performance, goals, and role division, or unresolved resentment eventually erupts into a damaging fight [the-biggest-mistakes-first-time-founders-make-michael-seibel @ 02:06](https://youtu.be/D56QeyyQMLI?t=126)
- Most people don't remember the actual day Snapchat, Instagram, WhatsApp, Uber, or Lyft launched, so founders should stop overrating launch day and ship an MVP within a month whenever possible [the-biggest-mistakes-first-time-founders-make-michael-seibel @ 03:36](https://youtu.be/D56QeyyQMLI?t=216)
- Not using analytics to measure what users actually do on the product is a common and costly early mistake [the-biggest-mistakes-first-time-founders-make-michael-seibel @ 04:34](https://youtu.be/D56QeyyQMLI?t=274)
- Founders who ask "where do I find my first users" are often solving a problem they and no one they know actually has; the first few users should come from people you already know [the-biggest-mistakes-first-time-founders-make-michael-seibel @ 04:54](https://youtu.be/D56QeyyQMLI?t=294)
- Prioritizing "sizzle" like press, hiring, conferences, and investors over the real work of shipping product and talking to users is cargo-culting a startup rather than running one [the-biggest-mistakes-first-time-founders-make-michael-seibel @ 05:33](https://youtu.be/D56QeyyQMLI?t=333)
