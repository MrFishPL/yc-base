# How To Cold Email Investors - Michael Seibel — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel gives tactical dos and don'ts for cold-emailing investors, arguing the sole goal of the first email is to get a reply, not a meeting or a check.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Keep the email readable in 60 seconds or less; a wall of text just gets saved for later and may never get a reply [how-to-cold-email-investors-michael-seibel @ 00:24](https://youtu.be/A3MmYbH1hbs?t=24)
- The goal of a cold email is not to sell an investment, it's to get the investor to read enough to want to reply [how-to-cold-email-investors-michael-seibel @ 00:42](https://youtu.be/A3MmYbH1hbs?t=42)
- Include the problem, the solution, whether you've launched or have growth, market size, whether you have co-founders and can code, and any controversial insight you have [how-to-cold-email-investors-michael-seibel @ 01:04](https://youtu.be/A3MmYbH1hbs?t=64)
- Avoid jargon and origin-story narrative; use plain language a friend outside your industry could understand, since investor pitches differ from customer pitches [how-to-cold-email-investors-michael-seibel @ 01:27](https://youtu.be/A3MmYbH1hbs?t=87)
- Send from a company email address with your name in it, since weirdly formatted personal or "info@" addresses look off and hide identity-lookup tools investors use [how-to-cold-email-investors-michael-seibel @ 02:12](https://youtu.be/A3MmYbH1hbs?t=132)
- A deck is optional, but if attached it should follow the standard Silicon Valley format (e.g., modeled on Airbnb's fundraising deck) rather than formats common in other industries [how-to-cold-email-investors-michael-seibel @ 02:54](https://youtu.be/A3MmYbH1hbs?t=174)
- Track email opens to confirm investors are actually seeing the message [how-to-cold-email-investors-michael-seibel @ 04:02](https://youtu.be/A3MmYbH1hbs?t=242)
- Don't immediately request an in-person meeting; pushing for a meeting too soon can make investors feel pressured and reluctant [how-to-cold-email-investors-michael-seibel @ 05:28](https://youtu.be/A3MmYbH1hbs?t=328)
- Don't send multiple quick follow-ups; if you're tracking opens you already know whether they've read it and made their choice [how-to-cold-email-investors-michael-seibel @ 05:52](https://youtu.be/A3MmYbH1hbs?t=352)
- The most important rule is to actually describe what your company does in the email itself, rather than asking to meet in person just to explain it, since that gives you far less leverage [how-to-cold-email-investors-michael-seibel @ 06:33](https://youtu.be/A3MmYbH1hbs?t=393)
