# Travis Kalanick at Startup School 2012 — Compiled Truth

Uber's co-founder walks through the company's growth metrics, operational scaling, and the regulatory fights it had to win city by city.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Uber launched in June 2010, owns no cars and employs no drivers, and ran 17 cities with only 120 employees at the time of the talk [travis-kalanick-at-startup-school-2012 @ 02:15](https://youtu.be/rQ6GoY2_Ujw?t=135)
- Uber sustained 26% month-over-month growth averaged over 16-17 months, which compounds to being 16x bigger than 12 months prior [travis-kalanick-at-startup-school-2012 @ 03:23](https://youtu.be/rQ6GoY2_Ujw?t=203)
- 50% of everyone who has ever ridden with Uber rode in the last 30 days, a retention number Kalanick frames like a commerce site's repeat-purchase rate [travis-kalanick-at-startup-school-2012 @ 03:03](https://youtu.be/rQ6GoY2_Ujw?t=183)
- Raising the early angel round was hard because investors thought "limos in San Francisco" sounded crazy, until the growth proved otherwise [travis-kalanick-at-startup-school-2012 @ 08:32](https://youtu.be/rQ6GoY2_Ujw?t=512)
- Launching in New York, Seattle, and Chicago proved the model wasn't a one-city fluke, and each new city rollout became progressively more operationally efficient [travis-kalanick-at-startup-school-2012 @ 09:37](https://youtu.be/rQ6GoY2_Ujw?t=577)
- Introducing the lower-cost Uber X option deepened engagement because riders could choose cheap most of the time and still upgrade for a special night [travis-kalanick-at-startup-school-2012 @ 14:10](https://youtu.be/rQ6GoY2_Ujw?t=850)
- Scaling a physical-world business required "process managers" alongside product managers, since changes to what happens in a car must dovetail with changes to the tech [travis-kalanick-at-startup-school-2012 @ 12:25](https://youtu.be/rQ6GoY2_Ujw?t=745)
