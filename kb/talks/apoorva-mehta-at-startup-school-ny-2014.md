# Apoorva Mehta at Startup School NY 2014 — Compiled Truth

Instacart founder Apoorva Mehta recounts quitting Amazon, building ~20 failed products (including a social network for lawyers), and finally succeeding with Instacart by solving a problem he personally cared about and doing early operations unscalably.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The reason to start a company should never be to start a company — it should be to solve a problem you truly care about, a lesson learned only after a failed social network for lawyers [apoorva-mehta-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 07:10](https://youtu.be/wkmR7TYUt_c?t=430)
- After building and testing ~20 failed products over roughly a year, Mehta built Instacart around the personal, long-felt pain of grocery shopping and found that friends adopted it without being pushed to [apoorva-mehta-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 08:16](https://youtu.be/wkmR7TYUt_c?t=496)
- As a founder you have to be extremely resilient, moving from failure to failure without losing steam, because any given iteration could be the one that works [apoorva-mehta-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 12:19](https://youtu.be/wkmR7TYUt_c?t=739)
- YC encourages startups to do things unscalably at the beginning — such as Mehta personally fulfilling orders via Uber black cars — because larger companies structurally cannot do this, making it an early competitive advantage [apoorva-mehta-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 15:50](https://youtu.be/wkmR7TYUt_c?t=950)
- To launch Trader Joe's on Instacart with no available item API, the team bought one of every item in the store and photographed each one by hand to build the catalog [apoorva-mehta-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 18:36](https://youtu.be/wkmR7TYUt_c?t=1116)
- Raising a seed round is one of the hardest things a founder does because you have the least data and traction, and investors were especially wary given past grocery-delivery failures like Webvan [apoorva-mehta-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 17:12](https://youtu.be/wkmR7TYUt_c?t=1032)
- Expanding beyond the Bay Area revealed unexpectedly fast growth in new markets — Chicago outpaced 33 weeks of Bay Area deliveries in just 3 weeks, and Boston and DC grew even faster still [apoorva-mehta-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 20:00](https://youtu.be/wkmR7TYUt_c?t=1200)
- As the company scaled to 10 cities with revenue growing 10% week-over-week for 20 weeks, the operational challenge shifted to making variable orders, shoppers, and store inventories work at scale without warehouses or trucks [apoorva-mehta-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 20:44](https://youtu.be/wkmR7TYUt_c?t=1244)
- The founder journey is filled with hundreds of failures, and persisting long enough is what eventually produces a success [apoorva-mehta-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 22:10](https://youtu.be/wkmR7TYUt_c?t=1330)
