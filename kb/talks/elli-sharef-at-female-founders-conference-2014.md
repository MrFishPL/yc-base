# Elli Sharef at Female Founders Conference 2014 — Compiled Truth

Hired (formerly Hired.com) co-founder Elli Sharef shares three lessons from building her company: just start, build unshakeable confidence, and learn to get up from near-failure.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- There is never a "perfect moment" to start a company — you just have to put your foot down and begin [elli-sharef-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 02:02](https://youtu.be/97nJPjZwK2Y?t=122)
- Hired's idea stayed roughly the same but the company went through many small pivots as they listened to what customers were telling them [elli-sharef-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 03:24](https://youtu.be/97nJPjZwK2Y?t=204)
- Founders need deep internal confidence — truly believing your own pitch — before you can externalize that belief to investors, hires, and friends [elli-sharef-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 04:24](https://youtu.be/97nJPjZwK2Y?t=264)
- She observed that male founders often project bravado and genuinely believe they will dominate their industry, while women tend to be more humble and hesitant to make the same claims [elli-sharef-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 05:14](https://youtu.be/97nJPjZwK2Y?t=314)
- Startups constantly feel like they're on the edge of failure without ever fully failing, like sprinting to catch a flight you're likely to miss [elli-sharef-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 06:26](https://youtu.be/97nJPjZwK2Y?t=386)
- After YC demo day, when other batchmates had closed funding and Hired had raised nothing (including a passed-on Sequoia investment), Sharef broke down but her co-founders pushed her to keep pitching until they found investors [elli-sharef-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 09:50](https://youtu.be/97nJPjZwK2Y?t=590)
- Meg Whitman's story of losing the California governor's race and then immediately becoming CEO of Hewlett-Packard illustrates getting back up after a very public failure [elli-sharef-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 08:32](https://youtu.be/97nJPjZwK2Y?t=512)
- Her final advice is to enjoy the ride — the ups and downs of founding a company are a rare, "otherworldly" experience worth savoring [elli-sharef-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 11:27](https://youtu.be/97nJPjZwK2Y?t=687)
