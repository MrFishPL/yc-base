# The REAL potential of generative AI — Compiled Truth

Raza Habib (Human Loop) explains that large language models are statistical text predictors whose recent leap in usefulness comes from fine-tuning and human feedback, and that building a differentiated AI product requires solving prototyping, evaluation, and customization rather than just accessing a base model.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Large language models are fundamentally statistical models predicting the next word, but at scale they must develop something like world knowledge and reasoning to do that prediction well [the-real-potential-of-generative-ai @ 01:37](https://youtu.be/hQC5O3WTmuo?t=97)
- Hallucinations happen because models are trained purely to predict text, not to be honest, but pulling factual context into the prompt is a proven technique for reducing them [the-real-potential-of-generative-ai @ 03:50](https://youtu.be/hQC5O3WTmuo?t=230)
- What made ChatGPT so good that a million users signed up in five days over a near-identical base model was the fine-tuning exercise, not the underlying model itself [the-real-potential-of-generative-ai @ 04:55](https://youtu.be/hQC5O3WTmuo?t=295)
- Reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) makes a huge performance difference — a 1-2B parameter instruction-tuned model with RLHF was preferred over the full, 100x-larger GPT-3 [the-real-potential-of-generative-ai @ 05:14](https://youtu.be/hQC5O3WTmuo?t=314)
- Developers building LLM products typically need help with three problems: prototyping, evaluation, and customization [the-real-potential-of-generative-ai @ 07:45](https://youtu.be/hQC5O3WTmuo?t=465)
- On a longer horizon, developers may shift toward product-manager-like roles, writing specs and documentation while models handle more of the grunt work and boilerplate [the-real-potential-of-generative-ai @ 10:39](https://youtu.be/hQC5O3WTmuo?t=639)
- Expanding the context window is a well-known near-term improvement that will unlock a lot more capability for LLM applications [the-real-potential-of-generative-ai @ 11:37](https://youtu.be/hQC5O3WTmuo?t=697)
- A major coming shift is augmenting LLMs with the ability to take actions, treating them like agents rather than pure text generators, as startups like Adept AI are exploring [the-real-potential-of-generative-ai @ 11:52](https://youtu.be/hQC5O3WTmuo?t=712)
- There isn't a strong network-effect moat around a single dominant model — the barriers to training one are mostly capital and talent, and labs like OpenAI and DeepMind have published much of their approach [the-real-potential-of-generative-ai @ 14:01](https://youtu.be/hQC5O3WTmuo?t=841)
- Feedback-data flywheels give real differentiation for narrow, end-user applications, but less advantage for a lab trying to keep one general model good at everything [the-real-potential-of-generative-ai @ 14:52](https://youtu.be/hQC5O3WTmuo?t=892)
