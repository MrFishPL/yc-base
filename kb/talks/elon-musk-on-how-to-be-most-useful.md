# Elon Musk On How To Be Most Useful — Compiled Truth

Elon Musk lays out a mental model for choosing what to work on — multiplying the "utility delta" of an idea by the number of people it affects — and explains why he started SpaceX despite it being a poor risk-adjusted bet.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Musk says most people trying to be useful should mostly not get PhDs [elon-musk-on-how-to-be-most-useful @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/Om5XuTbXP1U?t=0).
- His framework for evaluating usefulness: the utility delta versus the current state of the art, multiplied by how many people it would affect [elon-musk-on-how-to-be-most-useful @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/Om5XuTbXP1U?t=0).
- He argues that something making a big difference for a small-to-moderate number of people can be just as valuable as something making a small difference for a vast number of people, since the "area under the curve" is roughly similar [elon-musk-on-how-to-be-most-useful @ 00:22](https://youtu.be/Om5XuTbXP1U?t=22).
- He says the goal, when estimating probability of success, is really to ask whether the thing will be useful and matter [elon-musk-on-how-to-be-most-useful @ 00:39](https://youtu.be/Om5XuTbXP1U?t=39).
- Musk agrees the decision to actually start SpaceX was "quite crazy" at the time [elon-musk-on-how-to-be-most-useful @ 00:59](https://youtu.be/Om5XuTbXP1U?t=59).
- He states that if the objective were to achieve the best risk-adjusted return, starting a company like SpaceX would be insane — but that was not his objective [elon-musk-on-how-to-be-most-useful @ 01:17](https://youtu.be/Om5XuTbXP1U?t=77).
