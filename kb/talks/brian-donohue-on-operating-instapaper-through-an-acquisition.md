# Brian Donohue on Operating Instapaper Through an Acquisition — Compiled Truth

Brian Donohue recounts running Instapaper through beta works, its acquisition by Pinterest, and later spinning it back out as an independent, cashflow-positive company.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Instapaper's original appeal solved three pain points of the early iPhone era: slow mobile networks, an unreadable mobile web, and a lack of good reading apps [brian-donohue-on-operating-instapaper-through-an-acquisition @ 01:54](https://youtu.be/u3Gu0EX98Cg?t=114)
- As those pain points got solved industry-wide, the market shrank from broad appeal to a narrower niche of voracious media consumers who need a place to stage content [brian-donohue-on-operating-instapaper-through-an-acquisition @ 03:15](https://youtu.be/u3Gu0EX98Cg?t=195)
- Instapaper's paid downloads started tanking after free competitor Pocket relaunched with iOS and Android apps in April 2012 [brian-donohue-on-operating-instapaper-through-an-acquisition @ 08:08](https://youtu.be/u3Gu0EX98Cg?t=488)
- To test a freemium hypothesis, they ran Apple's Free App of the Week with a two-month free subscription trial, generating more downloads in one week than in the prior two years combined and a 10-15% subscription increase [brian-donohue-on-operating-instapaper-through-an-acquisition @ 14:24](https://youtu.be/u3Gu0EX98Cg?t=864)
- Based on that data, Instapaper raised subscription price, added paywalled features, and made the app itself fully free in September 2014 [brian-donohue-on-operating-instapaper-through-an-acquisition @ 17:18](https://youtu.be/u3Gu0EX98Cg?t=1038)
- A signed letter of intent from a large media company for an eight-figure acquisition collapsed six weeks into due diligence, teaching him "don't count your eggs before they hatch" [brian-donohue-on-operating-instapaper-through-an-acquisition @ 20:40](https://youtu.be/u3Gu0EX98Cg?t=1240)
- Pinterest acquired Instapaper in August 2016 after a roughly 13-month process from first interest to signed deal [brian-donohue-on-operating-instapaper-through-an-acquisition @ 23:31](https://youtu.be/u3Gu0EX98Cg?t=1411)
- Post-acquisition, leadership chose maintenance mode over a full business unit because every dollar invested in Instapaper returned far less than a dollar invested in core Pinterest [brian-donohue-on-operating-instapaper-through-an-acquisition @ 30:30](https://youtu.be/u3Gu0EX98Cg?t=1830)
- In 2018 Instapaper was spun back out as an independent company, raising $80,000 from friends and family via preferred shares that convert to common at a 125% return [brian-donohue-on-operating-instapaper-through-an-acquisition @ 37:54](https://youtu.be/u3Gu0EX98Cg?t=2274)
- The product roadmap has always been built by scoring requested features against user demand, competitive advantage, and business-model fit [brian-donohue-on-operating-instapaper-through-an-acquisition @ 50:12](https://youtu.be/u3Gu0EX98Cg?t=3012)
- The single biggest "growth hack" was removing the paid app download, which had been a major cap on growth [brian-donohue-on-operating-instapaper-through-an-acquisition @ 59:05](https://youtu.be/u3Gu0EX98Cg?t=3545)
