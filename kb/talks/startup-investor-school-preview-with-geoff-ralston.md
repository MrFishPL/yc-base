# Startup Investor School Preview with Geoff Ralston — Compiled Truth

Geoff Ralston previews YC's new four-day Investor School, explaining why it exists, what it will teach accredited angels, and sharing his own hard-won lessons from over a decade of angel investing.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Investor School is a four-day course (two sessions a day) for accredited investors, taught live and streamed as a MOOC, built to teach the basics of effective angel investing [startup-investor-school-preview-with-geoff-ralston @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/AJDR-jMaFcg?t=0)
- The school exists because YC partners have themselves made many angel-investing mistakes and want to help others avoid the common pitfalls [startup-investor-school-preview-with-geoff-ralston @ 01:04](https://youtu.be/AJDR-jMaFcg?t=64)
- The curriculum starts with fundamentals: the history of angel investing, how convertible notes and cap tables work, and the "handshake protocol" for verbally sealing a deal before paperwork [startup-investor-school-preview-with-geoff-ralston @ 06:37](https://youtu.be/AJDR-jMaFcg?t=397)
- When evaluating founders, the first thing Ralston reads for is genuine belief and near-obsessive passion, because without that resilience nothing else — market size, competition, funding, intelligence — matters [startup-investor-school-preview-with-geoff-ralston @ 08:50](https://youtu.be/AJDR-jMaFcg?t=530)
- He tests founders by pushing hard on every way an idea could fail and watching how they react to that pressure [startup-investor-school-preview-with-geoff-ralston @ 10:22](https://youtu.be/AJDR-jMaFcg?t=622)
- New angels should calibrate by meeting many companies (roughly ten to thirty) before making a first investment, rather than assuming the very first pitch is the next Google [startup-investor-school-preview-with-geoff-ralston @ 15:30](https://youtu.be/AJDR-jMaFcg?t=930)
- A default mental model: treat every investment as worth its full value until there is concrete evidence otherwise, but still be mentally prepared to lose it all [startup-investor-school-preview-with-geoff-ralston @ 17:53](https://youtu.be/AJDR-jMaFcg?t=1073)
- Being a good angel means investing out of genuine passion without letting emotion drive the decision, and learning to say no explicitly, kindly, and with real reasons rather than going silent [startup-investor-school-preview-with-geoff-ralston @ 25:10](https://youtu.be/AJDR-jMaFcg?t=1510)
- Treating founders poorly to squeeze out extra return can cost far more long-term, since deal flow comes from founders recommending investors to each other as good people to have on the cap table [startup-investor-school-preview-with-geoff-ralston @ 28:57](https://youtu.be/AJDR-jMaFcg?t=1737)
- Trying to be the last money into a round signals a lack of conviction; the better posture is being willing to be the first check into a founder you believe in [startup-investor-school-preview-with-geoff-ralston @ 30:31](https://youtu.be/AJDR-jMaFcg?t=1831)
