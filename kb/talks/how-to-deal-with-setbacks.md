# How To Deal With Setbacks — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell argue that setbacks are a permanent, recurring feature of running a startup rather than isolated events, and that a founder's only real control is over their reaction to them.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Setbacks are the sport itself, not an obstacle to it — like a boxer who signed up to take punches [how-to-deal-with-setbacks @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/AWa4qZvtMuY?t=0)
- Fundraising is the classic source of setbacks; founders often go in expecting a deal will happen and come out feeling personally wronged when it doesn't [how-to-deal-with-setbacks @ 02:05](https://youtu.be/AWa4qZvtMuY?t=125)
- Most Series A raises fail but no one publicizes failures, only successes, which warps founders' sense of how hard fundraising really is for everyone [how-to-deal-with-setbacks @ 05:27](https://youtu.be/AWa4qZvtMuY?t=327)
- Co-founder disputes can hit harder than investor issues because the relationship is "home," and having a real friendship gives a relationship a fighting chance to survive inevitable friction [how-to-deal-with-setbacks @ 06:53](https://youtu.be/AWa4qZvtMuY?t=413)
- The "magical deal" mindset — believing one deal will save the company — is dangerous, illustrated by a six-figure advertising deal that led to a costly, failed TV show side project [how-to-deal-with-setbacks @ 07:54](https://youtu.be/AWa4qZvtMuY?t=474)
- Founders often expect a "roll credits" moment where the company is suddenly won, but real startups have setbacks for eight to ten years even after big success [how-to-deal-with-setbacks @ 10:29](https://youtu.be/AWa4qZvtMuY?t=629)
- Launches almost never go like a movie premiere; most of the time no one cares, and the fix is to keep launching repeatedly to get past the emotional expectation [how-to-deal-with-setbacks @ 12:04](https://youtu.be/AWa4qZvtMuY?t=724)
- Legal threats and lawsuits are a normal cost of doing business at scale, not a special catastrophe unique to one founder [how-to-deal-with-setbacks @ 16:14](https://youtu.be/AWa4qZvtMuY?t=974)
- Founders can't control whether setbacks happen but can fully control and practice their reaction to them, and doing an "inventory" of the actual worst-case facts often makes the situation feel less scary [how-to-deal-with-setbacks @ 20:03](https://youtu.be/AWa4qZvtMuY?t=1203)
- How a founder reacts to a setback sets the example that co-founders and employees will copy when bad things later happen to them [how-to-deal-with-setbacks @ 18:43](https://youtu.be/AWa4qZvtMuY?t=1123)
