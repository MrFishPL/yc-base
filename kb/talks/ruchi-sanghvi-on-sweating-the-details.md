# Ruchi Sanghvi on Sweating the Details — Compiled Truth

Ruchi Sanghvi recounts joining Dropbox after Cove's acquisition and realizing that Facebook's "move fast and break things" culture had to be replaced with a quality-obsessed, detail-sweating culture suited to Dropbox's mission.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- After Cove was acquired by Dropbox, Sanghvi's first instinct was that Dropbox needed to move and launch faster, like the daily release cycle at Facebook [ruchi-sanghvi-on-sweating-the-details @ 00:22](https://youtu.be/JdC-wFSZhSU?t=22)
- She initially couldn't understand why Dropbox spent large amounts of time fixing esoteric bugs affecting less than 0.1% of users, until she realized it could mean someone losing their wedding pictures or life's work [ruchi-sanghvi-on-sweating-the-details @ 00:46](https://youtu.be/JdC-wFSZhSU?t=46)
- "Move fast and break things" worked at Facebook because the only thing that mattered there was adding as many people as possible, but applying that value at Dropbox would have been a disaster [ruchi-sanghvi-on-sweating-the-details @ 01:22](https://youtu.be/JdC-wFSZhSU?t=82)
- Dropbox's culture had to foster sweating the details, since quality was paramount and that last 10% of polish took about 50% of engineering time, making Dropbox move slower than Facebook but not slowly [ruchi-sanghvi-on-sweating-the-details @ 02:16](https://youtu.be/JdC-wFSZhSU?t=136)
- Sanghvi realized the fastest way to accelerate progress was to grow the team, since Dropbox had only 30 engineers spread too thin across web, desktop, and mobile [ruchi-sanghvi-on-sweating-the-details @ 02:39](https://youtu.be/JdC-wFSZhSU?t=159)
- When CEO Drew asked what she wanted to work on first, she chose recruiting despite never having done it before, setting an audacious goal of growing the company from 90 to 270 people in under 7 months [ruchi-sanghvi-on-sweating-the-details @ 03:06](https://youtu.be/JdC-wFSZhSU?t=186)
- The hiring goal wasn't just headcount; they were determined to hire people who would raise the bar on quality and could be culturally integrated into the company [ruchi-sanghvi-on-sweating-the-details @ 03:35](https://youtu.be/JdC-wFSZhSU?t=215)
- Sanghvi worked across communications, marketing, and product beyond recruiting, arguing that job title doesn't matter as much as doing what it takes to win and what you actually build [ruchi-sanghvi-on-sweating-the-details @ 03:55](https://youtu.be/JdC-wFSZhSU?t=235)
