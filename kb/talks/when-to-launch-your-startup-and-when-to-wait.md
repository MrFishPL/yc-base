# When to Launch Your Startup and When to Wait — Compiled Truth

Founders imagine launch as a singular, high-stakes "Oscar ceremony" moment and delay it trying to make it perfect, but the real advice is to launch fast, launch repeatedly, and treat early versions as hacked-together learning tools rather than polished products.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Founders picture the launch as a glamorous singular event like the Oscars, which makes them want to "get in shape" before showing up, rather than shipping quickly [when-to-launch-your-startup-and-when-to-wait @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/f0tPjcgcwnQ?t=0)
- Founders don't know what "fast" actually is until they see hundreds of companies; startup speed is a different ballgame than big-company speed and requires being uncomfortable [when-to-launch-your-startup-and-when-to-wait @ 01:22](https://youtu.be/f0tPjcgcwnQ?t=82)
- Instacart's early YC demo was hacked together with no real backend — a friend just drove to the store to buy beer when an order came in — yet it looked good enough to work [when-to-launch-your-startup-and-when-to-wait @ 02:21](https://youtu.be/f0tPjcgcwnQ?t=141)
- Founders fantasize about a "parallel universe" reaction to their perfect launch, and letting that imagined scenario win over reality slows them down by making them optimize for something not real [when-to-launch-your-startup-and-when-to-wait @ 04:00](https://youtu.be/f0tPjcgcwnQ?t=240)
- There is no single "the launch" — founders launch over and over again, and it's fine to show an imperfect product to the world more than once before people get it [when-to-launch-your-startup-and-when-to-wait @ 05:14](https://youtu.be/f0tPjcgcwnQ?t=314)
- Founders build elaborate "waitlist" mental gymnastics to feel like they've launched without actually putting a product in front of users, but a waitlist is just a form and doesn't generate real product feedback [when-to-launch-your-startup-and-when-to-wait @ 06:37](https://youtu.be/f0tPjcgcwnQ?t=397)
- The company Magic launched two days after ideation with just a website and a phone number saying "text us and we'll do whatever you like," embodying the right spirit of learning live [when-to-launch-your-startup-and-when-to-wait @ 07:25](https://youtu.be/f0tPjcgcwnQ?t=445)
- Rippling's founder took ~2 years to officially launch, but that was justified only because he'd already built the same product once before (at Zenefits) and had unusual deep domain expertise — a rare exception [when-to-launch-your-startup-and-when-to-wait @ 08:40](https://youtu.be/f0tPjcgcwnQ?t=520)
- The first time that same founder built the Zenefits product, he did not spend a year and a half — he showed up quickly with what looked like a full HR product but was actually just forms with no real backend [when-to-launch-your-startup-and-when-to-wait @ 10:34](https://youtu.be/f0tPjcgcwnQ?t=634)
- Founders citing exceptions like Dropbox, Rippling, or Stripe usually only know 5% of the real story behind those "slow launches" and should dig into what actually happened before using them to justify delay [when-to-launch-your-startup-and-when-to-wait @ 11:30](https://youtu.be/f0tPjcgcwnQ?t=690)
