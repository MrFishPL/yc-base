# Top Ways Startups Waste Money — Compiled Truth

YC Group Partners walk through the recurring ways early-stage founders waste money — overpaying for "Fang" hires, contractors, paid ads, PR agencies, customized legal work, and equity-hungry advisors — and argue most of this spend should wait until after product-market fit.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Hiring an expensive "Fang" engineer or salesperson to join pre-PMF is usually a mistake because their productivity depended on the big company's systems, not just their talent [top-ways-startups-waste-money @ 03:00](https://youtu.be/BtzUo6vL3Iw?t=180)
- Replacing one full-time hire with an army of contractors rarely works well because contractors lack skin in the game and don't get caught up in the system [top-ways-startups-waste-money @ 04:49](https://youtu.be/BtzUo6vL3Iw?t=289)
- Pouring money into Facebook/Google ads early can scale fast but teaches founders little, and platforms will happily take unlimited spend [top-ways-startups-waste-money @ 06:58](https://youtu.be/BtzUo6vL3Iw?t=418)
- A founder's own experience shows ad spend that starts as small messaging tests can silently balloon to nearly a million dollars a month, becoming unprofitable and substituting for real product building [top-ways-startups-waste-money @ 07:42](https://youtu.be/BtzUo6vL3Iw?t=462)
- Spending on brand events and sponsorships before having product-market fit rarely creates a brand, versus scrappy stunts that get outsized attention for near-zero cost [top-ways-startups-waste-money @ 10:21](https://youtu.be/BtzUo6vL3Iw?t=621)
- Hiring a PR agency is described as almost inevitable but nearly always wasted money, since journalists want a direct relationship with the founder rather than an intermediary [top-ways-startups-waste-money @ 13:10](https://youtu.be/BtzUo6vL3Iw?t=790)
- Customizing legal work (like employment agreements) instead of using standard templates burns cash on lawyers learning on the founder's dime, and founders should get cost estimates upfront [top-ways-startups-waste-money @ 15:46](https://youtu.be/BtzUo6vL3Iw?t=946)
- Paid advisors rarely make a measurable difference; founders should push back on equity asks (e.g., a professor demanding 30% dropped to 1% after pushback) or invite advisors to invest instead of being paid [top-ways-startups-waste-money @ 23:26](https://youtu.be/BtzUo6vL3Iw?t=1406)
- The general rule: earn the right to spend money by first testing cheaply and doing it yourself, and save hiring/marketing/ads/PR spend for after product-market fit [top-ways-startups-waste-money @ 25:03](https://youtu.be/BtzUo6vL3Iw?t=1503)
