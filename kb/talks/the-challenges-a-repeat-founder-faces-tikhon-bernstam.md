# The Challenges a Repeat Founder Faces - Tikhon Bernstam — Compiled Truth

Scribd and Parse co-founder Tikhon Bernstam, now an angel investor, walks through how he validated ideas faster the second time around, what he looks for when hiring and investing, and the specific psychological traps repeat founders fall into.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- After five and a half years building Scribd, he left specifically to pursue something more B2B/enterprise, which became Parse [the-challenges-a-repeat-founder-faces-tikhon-bernstam @ 08:15](https://youtu.be/P-EMcAvE5YI?t=495)
- Rather than spending a year on one idea like at Scribd, for Parse they built 12 different landing pages in weeks and watched signups to test demand before committing to a direction [the-challenges-a-repeat-founder-faces-tikhon-bernstam @ 09:18](https://youtu.be/P-EMcAvE5YI?t=558)
- His test for product-market fit is whether there's a niche fanatic about the product; most of his startup failures came from pushing growth before reaching real PMF [the-challenges-a-repeat-founder-faces-tikhon-bernstam @ 18:43](https://youtu.be/P-EMcAvE5YI?t=1123)
- Parse's most effective hiring strategy was asking every great hire "who's the best person you've worked with," then recruiting down that chain of trusted referrals [the-challenges-a-repeat-founder-faces-tikhon-bernstam @ 24:08](https://youtu.be/P-EMcAvE5YI?t=1448)
- As an angel, his first filter is market size, then whether the founding team is relentless — he gauges relentlessness partly by whether founders get pulled into irrelevant distractions [the-challenges-a-repeat-founder-faces-tikhon-bernstam @ 32:04](https://youtu.be/P-EMcAvE5YI?t=1924)
- He specifically likes backing solo founders, citing Dropbox's Drew Houston, because persevering alone for a year or more is a strong signal of grit [the-challenges-a-repeat-founder-faces-tikhon-bernstam @ 33:07](https://youtu.be/P-EMcAvE5YI?t=1987)
- He prefers large, already-crowded, fragmented markets where incumbents execute badly, since a 10x better product can still win big even when famous investors call the space "too crowded" — he cites investors passing on Dropbox at a $2M valuation [the-challenges-a-repeat-founder-faces-tikhon-bernstam @ 38:14](https://youtu.be/P-EMcAvE5YI?t=2294)
- He warns that second- and third-time founders tend to raise money too fast, which locks them into a market or product before they've found true product-market fit and makes it awkward to later pivot [the-challenges-a-repeat-founder-faces-tikhon-bernstam @ 45:28](https://youtu.be/P-EMcAvE5YI?t=2728)
- His core advice to first-time founders: everything that isn't building product and finding product-market fit is a waste of time, including worrying about fundraising [the-challenges-a-repeat-founder-faces-tikhon-bernstam @ 47:09](https://youtu.be/P-EMcAvE5YI?t=2829)
