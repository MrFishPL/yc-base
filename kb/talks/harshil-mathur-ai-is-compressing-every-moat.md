# Harshil Mathur: AI Is Compressing Every Moat — Compiled Truth

Razorpay's Harshil Mathur recounts pivoting from education payments to startups, surviving early near-death moments through radical customer trust, and argues AI is compressing capital and build-speed moats, leaving execution speed and human trust as the only durable advantages.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Razorpay's original YC plan to sell digital fee payments to education institutes failed because the actual payer (students/parents), not the institute, cared about digital collection, so the team pivoted to selling to startups instead [harshil-mathur-ai-is-compressing-every-moat @ 05:11](https://youtu.be/X5bABLCuIHA?t=311)
- Regulated industries can be a deep moat rather than just a hurdle, since every competitor has to clear the same lengthy licensing and approval process (it took Razorpay about a year after YC to process its first live transaction) [harshil-mathur-ai-is-compressing-every-moat @ 07:51](https://youtu.be/X5bABLCuIHA?t=471)
- When a partner bank abruptly pulled the plug on all 50 live merchants two weeks after demo day, the founders' survival principle was to personally call and stay on the phone with every angry customer rather than go silent, which preserved trust and kept many merchants long-term [harshil-mathur-ai-is-compressing-every-moat @ 12:30](https://youtu.be/X5bABLCuIHA?t=750)
- Razorpay deliberately refuses to let AI replace customer support, because in a trust-based B2B/fintech business, support is less about efficiently solving problems and more a channel to prove a human is on top of the issue [harshil-mathur-ai-is-compressing-every-moat @ 14:22](https://youtu.be/X5bABLCuIHA?t=862)
- Being small and having "nothing to lose" let Razorpay be the first payment gateway to go live on India's UPI months before the two biggest banks, creating a wedge that took competitors six months to close once UPI took off after demonetization [harshil-mathur-ai-is-compressing-every-moat @ 21:15](https://youtu.be/X5bABLCuIHA?t=1275)
- In B2B, capital efficiency is a virtue, not a weakness: Razorpay kept burn under $200k/month on an $11M Series A, earned more from bank FD interest than it burned, and reasoned that in B2B a customer only stays if you keep adding more value than you charge — unlike growth-at-all-costs B2C models [harshil-mathur-ai-is-compressing-every-moat @ 19:07](https://youtu.be/X5bABLCuIHA?t=1147)
- AI is compressing the "build" moat the way capital and infrastructure moats already compressed, so the only differentiation left is deciding what to build and how fast you can move — companies that plan to "respond to the market" once it changes are already too late [harshil-mathur-ai-is-compressing-every-moat @ 26:17](https://youtu.be/X5bABLCuIHA?t=1577)
- Founders who shift fully into "manager mode" (delegating everything to hired leaders) make a critical mistake, because no leader will ever care about the company or its core product direction as much as the founder does [harshil-mathur-ai-is-compressing-every-moat @ 29:10](https://youtu.be/X5bABLCuIHA?t=1750)
- His closing advice: AI makes building software easier but doesn't make entrepreneurship easier — founders should only pursue problems they can commit to spending the next 10 years solving, not ones that are just cool or easy to build with AI [harshil-mathur-ai-is-compressing-every-moat @ 30:59](https://youtu.be/X5bABLCuIHA?t=1859)
