# Kathryn Minshew at Female Founders Conference 2014 — Compiled Truth

Kathryn Minshew, founder of the Muse, walks through the company's ugly-MVP-to-scale journey and distills lessons on shipping early, testing what actually matters, and scrappy user acquisition.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The Muse started as an ugly WordPress site targeting a market investors called too small, but validated demand fast, drawing 20,000 visitors in month one and 75,000 by month three [kathryn-minshew-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 03:43](https://youtu.be/zoF10tFmNmY?t=223)
- Paul Graham cut off an early, overly ambitious product demo and told them to just ship, which pushed the team to rebuild and relaunch a basic version on TechCrunch just 13 days later [kathryn-minshew-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 06:30](https://youtu.be/zoF10tFmNmY?t=390)
- You've got to start somewhere: it's tempting to polish a product in private, but withholding an imperfect launch means missing out on the real-world data needed to find the right direction [kathryn-minshew-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 07:30](https://youtu.be/zoF10tFmNmY?t=450)
- Your college roommate's approval does not mean market demand — feedback from people who love you is unreliable, so ideas must be tested against people in the actual target demographic who have no stake in your feelings [kathryn-minshew-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 08:02](https://youtu.be/zoF10tFmNmY?t=482)
- Track everything but know what's actually important: after obsessing over video-watch metrics, a YC partner asked whether that even mapped to a core success metric, prompting the team to refocus on visits, applications, and shares [kathryn-minshew-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 10:04](https://youtu.be/zoF10tFmNmY?t=604)
- With no budget for marketing, the team manually emailed like-minded niche groups (e.g. Stanford Women in Law) asking for feedback and shares, which drove access to networks they could never have reached otherwise [kathryn-minshew-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 11:41](https://youtu.be/zoF10tFmNmY?t=701)
- Making word-of-mouth effortless — literally drafting the tweet or Facebook post for someone to copy-paste — dramatically increased the odds people would actually share [kathryn-minshew-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 12:46](https://youtu.be/zoF10tFmNmY?t=766)
- Becoming your own PR machine by writing guest content for outlets (even ones that ignore you for over a year) slowly built brand recognition and SEO when no press wanted to cover an unknown startup [kathryn-minshew-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 13:33](https://youtu.be/zoF10tFmNmY?t=813)
- Founders should lead by example on compensation and hire people who buy into the mission rather than the salary, since a scrappy startup can't out-pay big companies [kathryn-minshew-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 16:02](https://youtu.be/zoF10tFmNmY?t=962)
