# Ben Silbermann at Startup School SV 2016 — Compiled Truth

Pinterest co-founder Ben Silbermann recounts pivoting from a failed iPhone catalog app (Tote) into Pinterest, and the scrappy, user-obsessed tactics that got it off the ground.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Pinterest emerged as a side "toy" project while Silbermann and Evan were struggling to launch their first company, Tote, an iPhone catalog app that ultimately went nowhere [ben-silbermann-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 09:47](https://youtu.be/LzhXIBPYyxw?t=587)
- The founders built Pinterest because they personally wanted it, not from market research — "we just thought it would be cool for ourselves at first" [ben-silbermann-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 11:05](https://youtu.be/LzhXIBPYyxw?t=665)
- Early adopters weren't the stereotypical tech crowd but ordinary people from Silbermann's hometown network, planning homes and meals, reached via a targeted marketing event with lifestyle bloggers [ben-silbermann-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 12:42](https://youtu.be/LzhXIBPYyxw?t=762)
- Silbermann personally staffed customer support and would stand in the Apple Store setting demo computers to Pinterest to fake momentum, illustrating extreme early scrappiness [ben-silbermann-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 13:14](https://youtu.be/LzhXIBPYyxw?t=794)
- He sat in coffee shops watching users try specific actions and asking "what did you expect to see" versus what they actually saw, to find UX gaps — since what people say they want differs from what they mean [ben-silbermann-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 15:11](https://youtu.be/LzhXIBPYyxw?t=911)
- Fundraising and money are a tool to reach the next step, not the goal itself — confusing the two is a common founder mistake [ben-silbermann-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 18:36](https://youtu.be/LzhXIBPYyxw?t=1116)
- Early hiring leaned on personal networks and cheap, scrappy tactics (a Craigslist ad, weekly backyard barbecues) and prized "builders" who could show things they'd made, not just talk [ben-silbermann-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 19:28](https://youtu.be/LzhXIBPYyxw?t=1168)
- As Pinterest scaled, Silbermann intentionally sought founders and hires from diverse, non-traditional backgrounds to counteract the natural tendency to hire people like yourself [ben-silbermann-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 20:20](https://youtu.be/LzhXIBPYyxw?t=1220)
- A founder's core, unchanging job is driving clarity on what's being built and why and setting the cultural standard; what changes with scale is building an organization of people who execute better than you [ben-silbermann-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 27:02](https://youtu.be/LzhXIBPYyxw?t=1622)
- The biggest startup risk isn't failure itself but founders burning out from tying their self-worth to the company's survival-or-die outcome [ben-silbermann-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 25:37](https://youtu.be/LzhXIBPYyxw?t=1537)
