# Julia Hartz at Female Founders Conference 2014 — Compiled Truth

Julia Hartz, co-founder of Eventbrite, reflects on hindsight lessons from choosing a co-founder, picking a location, monetizing, scaling leadership, and building culture.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Putting real care and attention into how you work with your co-founder — especially when it's also your spouse — is critical to making the partnership succeed [julia-hartz-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 05:19](https://youtu.be/fxIN9J3Vcv4?t=319)
- Eventbrite deliberately defied conventional wisdom by basing itself in San Francisco instead of following everyone south to Silicon Valley, which let it grow up alongside a cluster of other founding teams sharing the same office space [julia-hartz-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 06:55](https://youtu.be/fxIN9J3Vcv4?t=415)
- Launching with a free basic tier next to an undifferentiated premium tier was a mistake; once they removed the free option, attrition stayed flat while conversion skyrocketed [julia-hartz-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 09:46](https://youtu.be/fxIN9J3Vcv4?t=586)
- An accidental pricing bug that zeroed out fees on free events became a strength: free events now make up 70% of activity and attendees of those events convert into the next wave of paid organizers, driving a virtuous growth cycle [julia-hartz-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 11:35](https://youtu.be/fxIN9J3Vcv4?t=695)
- As headcount grew from 15 to 100+, the company reorganized into a hierarchy almost overnight, saddling many people with first-time leadership roles they weren't ready for [julia-hartz-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 13:27](https://youtu.be/fxIN9J3Vcv4?t=807)
- Founders should deliberately think about who, why, and when someone becomes a leader, rather than pushing strong individual contributors into management too early [julia-hartz-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 14:52](https://youtu.be/fxIN9J3Vcv4?t=892)
- Setting an explicit company vision and mission statement, even years in, gave the team an anchor to move in the same direction, and philosophies are allowed to evolve over time [julia-hartz-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 16:29](https://youtu.be/fxIN9J3Vcv4?t=989)
- A brand exercise asking users to describe Eventbrite produced words that also described the employees, so Julia repurposed that brand language directly into the company's culture and hiring tenets [julia-hartz-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 18:39](https://youtu.be/fxIN9J3Vcv4?t=1119)
- Eventbrite implemented an employee Net Promoter Score, publishing results transparently every month, to build a high-velocity feedback loop and trust across the growing team [julia-hartz-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 19:23](https://youtu.be/fxIN9J3Vcv4?t=1163)
