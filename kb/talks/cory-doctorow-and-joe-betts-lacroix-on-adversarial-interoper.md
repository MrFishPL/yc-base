# Cory Doctorow and Joe Betts-Lacroix on Adversarial Interoperability — Compiled Truth

Cory Doctorow argues that "adversarial interoperability" — plugging into a dominant platform without its permission, as Samba, Apple's iWork, and Mint once did — has historically been how new entrants broke incumbents' grip, and that the legal thicket built up since then (CFAA, DMCA, Terms of Service litigation) now protects incumbents like Facebook and Google in ways that create real startup opportunity for founders willing to challenge it.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Adversarial interoperability is when you plug something into a product without the maker's permission, turning their customers into your customers, and it was pivotal in cases like Samba reverse-engineering Microsoft's SMB protocol and Apple's iWork suite reverse-engineering Microsoft Office file formats [cory-doctorow-and-joe-betts-lacroix-on-adversarial-interoper @ 01:01](https://youtu.be/1RsI-Vh-KWI?t=61)
- Today's legal thicket of software patents, DMCA anti-circumvention rules, and Terms-of-Service-as-hacking theories (via the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act) makes this kind of disruption much harder than it used to be [cory-doctorow-and-joe-betts-lacroix-on-adversarial-interoper @ 04:36](https://youtu.be/1RsI-Vh-KWI?t=276)
- Mint built a large startup by scraping bank websites (adversarial interoperability via screen-scraping) when no APIs existed, showing how a company can bootstrap distribution off an incumbent's users [cory-doctorow-and-joe-betts-lacroix-on-adversarial-interoper @ 06:29](https://youtu.be/1RsI-Vh-KWI?t=389)
- If a business does something legally fraught but that people love, it creates both a political constituency for normative/legal change and surplus capital to fund the legal fight to make it more legal [cory-doctorow-and-joe-betts-lacroix-on-adversarial-interoper @ 11:15](https://youtu.be/1RsI-Vh-KWI?t=675)
- Facebook's targeting model isn't mind control — it's just finding people who are statistically likelier to want a product (e.g., refrigerator buyers), which massively improves an otherwise near-zero conversion rate [cory-doctorow-and-joe-betts-lacroix-on-adversarial-interoper @ 36:11](https://youtu.be/1RsI-Vh-KWI?t=2171)
- Rather than trying to beat Facebook outright, a founder could peel off a narrow, underserved community (e.g., a medical support group) by building a tool that syncs messages in and out of Facebook while shielding sensitive data, capturing a real market without needing all users to leave at once [cory-doctorow-and-joe-betts-lacroix-on-adversarial-interoper @ 33:40](https://youtu.be/1RsI-Vh-KWI?t=2020)
