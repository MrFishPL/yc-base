# Startup Technology - Technical Founder Advice — Compiled Truth

Four technical co-founders (PlanGrid, Segment, 6D.ai/Escher, Second Measure) compare notes on v1 builds, tech-stack trade-offs, engineering process, and how to work with non-technical co-founders as they scale.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- PlanGrid's v1 was a duct-taped iPad blueprint viewer built to prove something "technically impossible" was actually possible, hiding manual backend work from early customers [startup-technology-technical-founder-advice @ 08:45](https://youtu.be/tSW-GePDwn4?t=525)
- Segment pivoted twice — from a classroom lecture tool, to a Mixpanel-style analytics competitor — before finding product-market fit with an open-source analytics API that got 1,000 GitHub stars in a day [startup-technology-technical-founder-advice @ 10:51](https://youtu.be/tSW-GePDwn4?t=651)
- After burning half a million dollars over 15 months with no traction, PG told the Segment founders "you're still at square one" but still had runway to try again [startup-technology-technical-founder-advice @ 12:51](https://youtu.be/tSW-GePDwn4?t=771)
- Diana's AR startup built its slam-based prototype at 5 frames per second before optimizing to 30fps, and pivoted timing collided with Apple announcing ARKit on the first day of YC, pushing them to "double down" [startup-technology-technical-founder-advice @ 16:44](https://youtu.be/tSW-GePDwn4?t=1004)
- Early-stage advice across the panel: speed of iteration matters more than test coverage or scalability until product-market fit is found, since "nobody's gonna pay you for having excellent test coverage" [startup-technology-technical-founder-advice @ 23:19](https://youtu.be/tSW-GePDwn4?t=1399)
- Segment only started investing heavily in testing, CI/CD, and best practices once they had enterprise customers paying six figures per year [startup-technology-technical-founder-advice @ 30:05](https://youtu.be/tSW-GePDwn4?t=1805)
- All four companies describe their process as "agile-ish" — self-organizing teams picking their own tools rather than adhering strictly to Scrum, Sprints, or OKRs [startup-technology-technical-founder-advice @ 34:41](https://youtu.be/tSW-GePDwn4?t=2081)
- With non-technical co-founders, panelists recommend lightweight project management and communicating two sets of deadlines — the stated deadline and the realistic one [startup-technology-technical-founder-advice @ 42:52](https://youtu.be/tSW-GePDwn4?t=2572)
- All the panelists initially hired full-time employees over contractors, arguing that outsourcing core technology early means losing critical product-learning and IP [startup-technology-technical-founder-advice @ 48:01](https://youtu.be/tSW-GePDwn4?t=2881)
- Asked what they'd do differently, panelists advised building two or three throwaway prototypes early, choosing a single cross-platform stack instead of native-for-every-platform, and hiring engineers sooner instead of personally coding longer [startup-technology-technical-founder-advice @ 57:47](https://youtu.be/tSW-GePDwn4?t=3467)
