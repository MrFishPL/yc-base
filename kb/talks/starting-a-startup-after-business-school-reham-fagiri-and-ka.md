# Starting a Startup After Business School - Reham Fagiri and Kalam Dennis of AptDeco — Compiled Truth

AptDeco's founders describe validating a furniture marketplace idea with scrappy market tests before YC, then learning the hard way how to price, staff, and manage a scaling operations business.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Before building anything, they tested demand by copying existing Craigslist furniture listings and simply adding "delivery" to see how buyers reacted [starting-a-startup-after-business-school-reham-fagiri-and-ka @ 01:23](https://youtu.be/uPrIsSWhW7o?t=83)
- Market research showed furniture was the fifth most popular category on Craigslist, signaling a real opportunity before they built the product [starting-a-startup-after-business-school-reham-fagiri-and-ka @ 01:43](https://youtu.be/uPrIsSWhW7o?t=103)
- Their first "Frankenstein" MVP launched in 2014 and produced a transaction on day one, despite the founders not fully knowing what they were doing [starting-a-startup-after-business-school-reham-fagiri-and-ka @ 02:05](https://youtu.be/uPrIsSWhW7o?t=125)
- Getting accepted into YC was the moment that made the idea feel real and worth pursuing full-time [starting-a-startup-after-business-school-reham-fagiri-and-ka @ 02:26](https://youtu.be/uPrIsSWhW7o?t=146)
- They deliberately chose never to warehouse inventory, likening their model to making every seller's home into a "hotel room" rather than capping scale with warehouse space and cost [starting-a-startup-after-business-school-reham-fagiri-and-ka @ 42:16](https://youtu.be/uPrIsSWhW7o?t=2536)
- Early on they didn't understand true transaction costs and ran negative margins; once they learned their real costs, transaction fees rose from 10-15% to 19-29% [starting-a-startup-after-business-school-reham-fagiri-and-ka @ 27:16](https://youtu.be/uPrIsSWhW7o?t=1636)
- They ran A/B tests on pricing to find the right tradeoff between item price and delivery fee before settling on a formula [starting-a-startup-after-business-school-reham-fagiri-and-ka @ 29:40](https://youtu.be/uPrIsSWhW7o?t=1780)
- They built management structure early — weekly direct-report meetings, public project-list sheets, daily standups, and a weekly KPI review — borrowed from their corporate backgrounds [starting-a-startup-after-business-school-reham-fagiri-and-ka @ 21:17](https://youtu.be/uPrIsSWhW7o?t=1277)
- On firing, they stress giving people the chance to course-correct first; if someone is surprised when let go, that means the manager failed to communicate expectations [starting-a-startup-after-business-school-reham-fagiri-and-ka @ 26:36](https://youtu.be/uPrIsSWhW7o?t=1596)
- Several intuitive feature ideas failed outright — furniture photography and cleaning add-on services both flopped because customers wouldn't pay for them [starting-a-startup-after-business-school-reham-fagiri-and-ka @ 43:40](https://youtu.be/uPrIsSWhW7o?t=2620)
- A Facebook ad campaign using customer testimonials, revisited a year after being abandoned as a failed channel, became their most profitable acquisition channel once they understood how to read the data [starting-a-startup-after-business-school-reham-fagiri-and-ka @ 44:58](https://youtu.be/uPrIsSWhW7o?t=2698)
