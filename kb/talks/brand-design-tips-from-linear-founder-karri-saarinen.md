# Brand Design Tips From Linear Founder Karri Saarinen — Compiled Truth

Linear co-founder Karri Saarinen reviews YC startup websites, arguing that early-stage brand and copy should be simple, authentic to the company's actual stage, and written in the specific language of the narrow early-adopter the founder is trying to filter for.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Startups should avoid copying the polished sites of mature companies like Stripe or Linear, because it sets false expectations that the product is more finished than it is [brand-design-tips-from-linear-founder-karri-saarinen @ 01:02](https://youtu.be/uEeFsW9343g?t=62)
- Linear's original 2019 site, built in about a day, deliberately used the specific term "issue tracking" rather than vague language like "work platform" in order to filter for and attract the technical early-adopter it wanted [brand-design-tips-from-linear-founder-karri-saarinen @ 02:49](https://youtu.be/uEeFsW9343g?t=169)
- Founders can be ambitious with investors while keeping the public-facing website narrow and specific to avoid attracting the wrong users [brand-design-tips-from-linear-founder-karri-saarinen @ 03:40](https://youtu.be/uEeFsW9343g?t=220)
- A homepage should function as a simple, curiosity-building "front page" that gives an unfamiliar visitor just enough information to decide interest, deferring deeper detail to other pages as the product and company mature [brand-design-tips-from-linear-founder-karri-saarinen @ 08:59](https://youtu.be/uEeFsW9343g?t=539)
- Distracting looping animations can pull attention away from a page's core message, whereas animation should be used deliberately to draw attention to the one thing that matters [brand-design-tips-from-linear-founder-karri-saarinen @ 11:38](https://youtu.be/uEeFsW9343g?t=698)
- For enterprise-focused products, website conversion rate is often the wrong metric to optimize because enterprise buyers are won through sales, events, and relationships rather than by clicking buttons on a landing page [brand-design-tips-from-linear-founder-karri-saarinen @ 15:37](https://youtu.be/uEeFsW9343g?t=937)
- Website copy and demos should mirror the exact language and use cases the target buyer already uses internally (e.g. what an enterprise buyer's CEO actually asked for) rather than generic descriptions [brand-design-tips-from-linear-founder-karri-saarinen @ 19:21](https://youtu.be/uEeFsW9343g?t=1161)
- Early on, Linear identified that speed was the single attribute startups (its target early-adopter) cared about most, and built the brand and homepage around that one differentiator rather than trying to appeal to everyone [brand-design-tips-from-linear-founder-karri-saarinen @ 34:04](https://youtu.be/uEeFsW9343g?t=2044)
- A distinctive, unconventional visual identity (unique illustrations, a scrolling narrative) can make a brand memorable even without a clear product yet, as seen with the "Unreal Milk" site [brand-design-tips-from-linear-founder-karri-saarinen @ 24:03](https://youtu.be/uEeFsW9343g?t=1443)
- Non-technical buyer audiences respond more to trust signals, social proof, and simple messaging than to feature-dense or highly stylized pages meant for technical audiences [brand-design-tips-from-linear-founder-karri-saarinen @ 40:31](https://youtu.be/uEeFsW9343g?t=2431)
