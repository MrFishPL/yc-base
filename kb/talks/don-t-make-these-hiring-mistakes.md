# Don't Make These Hiring Mistakes — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel, Harj Taggar, and Brad Flora argue that YC's common advice to "not hire" is aimed specifically at pre-product-market-fit founders, since most hiring advice online is written for already-scaled, post-PMF companies.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Almost all popular hiring advice is written for post-product-market-fit companies scaling a loved product, so applying it while pre-PMF can accelerate a startup's death [don-t-make-these-hiring-mistakes @ 01:29](https://youtu.be/CUZ6PKJZvXE?t=89)
- Founders convince themselves hiring will solve problems (more features, faster profitability) when in reality new hires add cost without fixing the underlying pre-PMF problem [don-t-make-these-hiring-mistakes @ 02:11](https://youtu.be/CUZ6PKJZvXE?t=131)
- Headcount becomes a vanity KPI because founders feel investors and friends will be more impressed by a bigger team, even though it isn't a sign of real progress [don-t-make-these-hiring-mistakes @ 02:49](https://youtu.be/CUZ6PKJZvXE?t=169)
- Being a startup founder doesn't automatically make someone good at hiring or managing people, and hiring an experienced specialist (e.g. a VP of Sales) doesn't mean they can do the zero-to-one work only the founder can do [don-t-make-these-hiring-mistakes @ 05:09](https://youtu.be/CUZ6PKJZvXE?t=309)
- Founders should not hire preemptively for problems they merely expect to have in the future, since a startup has many "smoldering fires" and it's more efficient to fight them only once they become real (Justin.tv/Twitch only hired a bandwidth specialist after spending too much became an actual problem) [don-t-make-these-hiring-mistakes @ 08:13](https://youtu.be/CUZ6PKJZvXE?t=493)
- Even top companies like Airbnb and Stripe hired very slowly at first (Airbnb took about 18 months to hire its first employee, Stripe stayed around three people for roughly a year) before ramping up once product-market fit was found [don-t-make-these-hiring-mistakes @ 11:06](https://youtu.be/CUZ6PKJZvXE?t=666)
- Every hire carries a real risk of a bad culture fit, and even a modest 5-10% failure rate compounds badly as team size grows, making restraint the safer default [don-t-make-these-hiring-mistakes @ 18:07](https://youtu.be/CUZ6PKJZvXE?t=1087)
- Whenever a post-YC company runs low on money, the cause is almost always over-hiring and salary burn rather than any other expense [don-t-make-these-hiring-mistakes @ 16:24](https://youtu.be/CUZ6PKJZvXE?t=984)
- Over-hiring can also burn out founders themselves, since managing a large team can drain energy that founders assumed they'd enjoy spending on people [don-t-make-these-hiring-mistakes @ 17:11](https://youtu.be/CUZ6PKJZvXE?t=1031)
- The bottom-line rule: if you're post-PMF, hire aggressively; if you're pre-PMF, don't hire; and if you're unsure which you are, figure that out before making any hiring decision [don-t-make-these-hiring-mistakes @ 18:51](https://youtu.be/CUZ6PKJZvXE?t=1131)
