# Ilya Volodarsky - Analytics for Startups — Compiled Truth

Segment co-founder Ilya Volodarsky gives a tactical guide to instrumenting the acquisition-retention-monetization funnel with analytics tools so founders can measure and test product-market fit.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Every product has a funnel of acquisition, engagement/retention, and monetization, with primary and secondary metrics tracked at each stage [ilya-volodarsky-analytics-for-startups @ 02:00](https://youtu.be/LLerCc7MOQo?t=120)
- Retention should be measured by cohort: track a weekly signup cohort's usage over subsequent weeks to see what percentage stick around [ilya-volodarsky-analytics-for-startups @ 02:40](https://youtu.be/LLerCc7MOQo?t=160)
- Products without product-market fit show retention curves that trend to zero, while products with PMF show a natural plateau, generally in the 20-30% range [ilya-volodarsky-analytics-for-startups @ 07:35](https://youtu.be/LLerCc7MOQo?t=455)
- In private beta (10-30 customers) with direct channels like Slack, products with PMF get customers pulling for more features, while products without it get silence [ilya-volodarsky-analytics-for-startups @ 10:44](https://youtu.be/LLerCc7MOQo?t=644)
- Segment sent a "43-minute founder email" after signup inviting direct contact, generating hundreds of thousands of responses over the years [ilya-volodarsky-analytics-for-startups @ 14:40](https://youtu.be/LLerCc7MOQo?t=880)
- Watching session recordings (e.g. Full Story) revealed a confusing button causing users to abandon a new feature, and fixing it improved the metrics immediately [ilya-volodarsky-analytics-for-startups @ 13:51](https://youtu.be/LLerCc7MOQo?t=831)
- Founders shouldn't over-optimize their analytics tool choice (e.g. Amplitude vs. Mixpanel) since best-in-class tools change roughly every two years anyway [ilya-volodarsky-analytics-for-startups @ 15:49](https://youtu.be/LLerCc7MOQo?t=949)
- Putting metrics dashboards on a TV in the office and sending regular investor/advisor update emails builds a data-driven culture and social accountability [ilya-volodarsky-analytics-for-startups @ 08:52](https://youtu.be/LLerCc7MOQo?t=532)
- The right growth rate and retention period needed depends entirely on the business: an enterprise B2B company selling $10-20k deals needs far fewer customers than a high-volume consumer product [ilya-volodarsky-analytics-for-startups @ 18:43](https://youtu.be/LLerCc7MOQo?t=1123)
