# YC SUS: Eric Migicovsky hosts founder office hours — Compiled Truth

Eric Migicovsky runs live, rapid-fire office hours with several Startup School founders, repeatedly pushing them to get a paying customer first, measure everything, and personally do unscalable manual work (calls, in-person onboarding) before trying to automate.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The right sequencing for an early solo founder is to get a paying customer first, then figure out how to fulfill and delight them, rather than over-building process or product before anyone is paying [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-hosts-founder-office-hours @ 04:58](https://youtu.be/4ynbXUaKHHs?t=298)
- Be as transparent as possible with early pilot customers about what success looks like, ideally writing the success criteria directly into the pilot contract so converting to a paid recurring deal later is an easy conversation [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-hosts-founder-office-hours @ 06:50](https://youtu.be/4ynbXUaKHHs?t=410)
- Signups are not a good sign by themselves because nobody makes money from signups — the founder should focus on active users and paying subscribers instead [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-hosts-founder-office-hours @ 13:30](https://youtu.be/4ynbXUaKHHs?t=810)
- Before trying to convert a large free user base to paid, put proper analytics instrumentation (Mixpanel or Amplitude) in place to understand usage patterns like note-taking frequency [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-hosts-founder-office-hours @ 16:36](https://youtu.be/4ynbXUaKHHs?t=996)
- Doing things that don't scale, like personally phoning customers to close a $13/month sale, is a secret advantage startup founders have over big companies who can't justify that effort at scale [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-hosts-founder-office-hours @ 19:45](https://youtu.be/4ynbXUaKHHs?t=1185)
- Phone calls give real-time feedback on which words and pitches resonate, letting a founder improve their pitch call by call and then use those learnings to improve mass channels like email [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-hosts-founder-office-hours @ 22:17](https://youtu.be/4ynbXUaKHHs?t=1337)
- To test product-market fit, look at how fast revenue is growing month over month and run two-week segmentation experiments to find which customer segment converts best [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-hosts-founder-office-hours @ 27:18](https://youtu.be/4ynbXUaKHHs?t=1638)
- When running an unscalable, high-touch service (e.g., weekly coaching calls), check the underlying unit economics — if calls cost more in labor than the customer pays per month, the model breaks before it can scale [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-hosts-founder-office-hours @ 54:05](https://youtu.be/4ynbXUaKHHs?t=3245)
- Track and segment which sources/lists produce actual revenue (not just usage) by walking backwards through affiliate or conversion data to find the highest-value customer segments [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-hosts-founder-office-hours @ 45:21](https://youtu.be/4ynbXUaKHHs?t=2721)
- Moving to software doesn't remove the need to do things that don't scale — founders should manually onboard their first users in person (literally sitting next to them) rather than assuming people will self-onboard [yc-sus-eric-migicovsky-hosts-founder-office-hours @ 1:01:34](https://youtu.be/4ynbXUaKHHs?t=3694)
