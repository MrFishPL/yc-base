# How to Sell by Tyler Bosmeny — Compiled Truth

Clever CEO Tyler Bosmeny argues that founders themselves must own early sales, walking through a prospecting-conversations-closing funnel with concrete tactics learned from selling to schools and newspapers.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Founders should be the first salespeople because they have unmatched passion and industry expertise, even without prior sales experience [how-to-sell-by-tyler-bosmeny @ 08:33](https://youtu.be/xZi4kTJG-LE?t=513)
- Per the technology adoption curve, only about 2.5% of potential customers are "innovators" willing to buy from an unproven startup, so expect to contact ~100 companies to find just a couple of viable early buyers [how-to-sell-by-tyler-bosmeny @ 12:29](https://youtu.be/xZi4kTJG-LE?t=749)
- Industry conferences (not massive trade shows, but smaller gatherings of buyers) are an underrated prospecting channel: identify the relevant ones, get the attendee list in advance, and pre-book meetings in 30-minute increments [how-to-sell-by-tyler-bosmeny @ 15:03](https://youtu.be/xZi4kTJG-LE?t=903)
- Cold emails should be short, personalized, and actionable — just asking for a call, not a long pitch about why your product is great [how-to-sell-by-tyler-bosmeny @ 19:50](https://youtu.be/xZi4kTJG-LE?t=1190)
- The single most important sales skill is listening: the best salespeople talk 30% of the time and listen 70%, while inexperienced founders often do the opposite [how-to-sell-by-tyler-bosmeny @ 22:41](https://youtu.be/xZi4kTJG-LE?t=1361)
- Closing enterprise deals takes many steps and persistent follow-up over months (one example deal took ~2 months and many unanswered emails before closing $100k/year), but a clear "no" is valuable because it frees you to move on to other prospects [how-to-sell-by-tyler-bosmeny @ 25:47](https://youtu.be/xZi4kTJG-LE?t=1547)
- Avoid closing traps: don't let contract redlining drag on over small clauses, be wary of "I'll buy if you add this one feature" requests, and avoid free trials in favor of annual agreements with a 30-day opt-out [how-to-sell-by-tyler-bosmeny @ 28:33](https://youtu.be/xZi4kTJG-LE?t=1713)
- YC provides an open-source sales contract template that founders can use as a starting point for enterprise agreements [how-to-sell-by-tyler-bosmeny @ 29:21](https://youtu.be/xZi4kTJG-LE?t=1761)
- Pricing is often a guess — set a number that feels reasonable, and if you can close deals at it, try doubling the price with the next prospect [how-to-sell-by-tyler-bosmeny @ 43:44](https://youtu.be/xZi4kTJG-LE?t=2624)
- Your pricing point determines your sales motion: high-price products ($10k-100k+) support high-touch enterprise sales, while low-price products ($10-1000) need scalable, low-touch channels like self-service signup and demand generation, per Christoph Janz's "five ways to build a $100M business" framework [how-to-sell-by-tyler-bosmeny @ 35:39](https://youtu.be/xZi4kTJG-LE?t=2139)
- Don't hire sales reps until you've sold the product yourself for months, since only then will you know what skills to hire for; look for "Renaissance" reps who figure things out with minimal playbook rather than reps who need scripts [how-to-sell-by-tyler-bosmeny @ 49:51](https://youtu.be/xZi4kTJG-LE?t=2991)
