# Working at Big Tech Companies Can Be a Trap - Michael Seibel — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel argues the common belief that you need big-tech experience before founding a startup is largely a misconception, and that big companies use compensation structures to trap employees into staying longer than intended.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A common startup misconception is that you need experience at a big company first, though there's a nugget of truth: it exposes you to problems you might not otherwise consider [working-at-big-tech-companies-can-be-a-trap-michael-seibel @ 00:21](https://youtu.be/6amgO4dIotk?t=21)
- In a big company you're an extremely small part of a large machine, so you learn less and more slowly than you would running your own startup, where you are the entire team [working-at-big-tech-companies-can-be-a-trap-michael-seibel @ 00:41](https://youtu.be/6amgO4dIotk?t=41)
- Big companies are very good at retaining workers, using signing bonuses and recurring stock grants to structure comp so that leaving always feels uncomfortable [working-at-big-tech-companies-can-be-a-trap-michael-seibel @ 01:04](https://youtu.be/6amgO4dIotk?t=64)
- High salaries often lead employees to inflate their lifestyle (nicer apartment, car, vacations), which then makes leaving money on the table an even stronger reason to stay [working-at-big-tech-companies-can-be-a-trap-michael-seibel @ 01:28](https://youtu.be/6amgO4dIotk?t=88)
- Working at a big company can become a trap especially if you don't enter with clear goals for what you want out of it [working-at-big-tech-companies-can-be-a-trap-michael-seibel @ 02:07](https://youtu.be/6amgO4dIotk?t=127)
- If your goal is to save money, learn a specific technology, or meet a co-founder, go do that specifically rather than getting caught by retention tactics [working-at-big-tech-companies-can-be-a-trap-michael-seibel @ 02:23](https://youtu.be/6amgO4dIotk?t=143)
