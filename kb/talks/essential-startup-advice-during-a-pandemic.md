# Essential Startup Advice During a Pandemic — Compiled Truth

In a TechCrunch interview during COVID-19, YC's Jeff Ralston argues the fundamentals of startup advice haven't changed in the pandemic, just intensified: get to default alive, hire slower, and stay focused on product-market fit rather than the macro climate.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The venture world transitioned surprisingly seamlessly to investing over Zoom after an initial slowdown in March-April, and application volume and founder quality have stayed strong [essential-startup-advice-during-a-pandemic @ 01:15](https://youtu.be/_oYJR6FNcdA?t=75)
- The core advice hasn't changed: be default alive, find a way to survive without raising more money, and think harder about reaching and confirming real product-market fit [essential-startup-advice-during-a-pandemic @ 03:03](https://youtu.be/_oYJR6FNcdA?t=183)
- The guidance to "hire slowly and fire quickly" should be pushed even further during uncertainty — hire even more slowly and be more careful about growth decisions [essential-startup-advice-during-a-pandemic @ 03:22](https://youtu.be/_oYJR6FNcdA?t=202)
- Default alive means getting enough revenue that in the worst case the company can survive on it, though whether to prioritize revenue early depends heavily on the sector and the startup [essential-startup-advice-during-a-pandemic @ 04:42](https://youtu.be/_oYJR6FNcdA?t=282)
- The amount of creativity and innovation in the YC application set hasn't changed, though there is a visible trend of applying proven models (like Shopify) to new geographies such as India [essential-startup-advice-during-a-pandemic @ 07:23](https://youtu.be/_oYJR6FNcdA?t=443)
- Early-stage startups should largely ignore vaccine-news optimism and instead focus on getting to product-market fit and keeping runway as long as possible [essential-startup-advice-during-a-pandemic @ 13:36](https://youtu.be/_oYJR6FNcdA?t=816)
- Beyond cash conservation, founders should focus on finding the right people for the startup and can afford to be even slower in hiring than usual [essential-startup-advice-during-a-pandemic @ 15:01](https://youtu.be/_oYJR6FNcdA?t=901)
- Remote YC batches made it far easier to interview and fund companies in places like Brazil, India, and Southeast Asia while they build their local businesses on the ground [essential-startup-advice-during-a-pandemic @ 17:52](https://youtu.be/_oYJR6FNcdA?t=1072)
