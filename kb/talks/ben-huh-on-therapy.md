# Ben Huh on Therapy — Compiled Truth

Ben Huh, former CEO of Cheezburger and co-founder of Circa, argues therapy and CEO coaching should be used proactively to stay in an optimal state, not just as crisis recovery, and explains how honesty and treating a therapist like a service provider maximizes its value.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Many people wait for a crisis before seeking therapy, which is like waiting until an injury is severe before seeing a doctor — much harder to fix by then [ben-huh-on-therapy @ 00:57](https://youtu.be/e2644ylntQQ?t=57)
- Stigma around therapy causes people to delay until problems get much worse, which creates bigger downstream problems [ben-huh-on-therapy @ 01:10](https://youtu.be/e2644ylntQQ?t=70)
- Therapy isn't only about recovering from something negative — entrepreneurial therapy and CEO coaching should be about maintaining an optimal state [ben-huh-on-therapy @ 01:10](https://youtu.be/e2644ylntQQ?t=70)
- CEO coaching focuses on what's preventing a founder from making difficult decisions and understanding relationships with employees and executives, distinct from personal therapy [ben-huh-on-therapy @ 01:47](https://youtu.be/e2644ylntQQ?t=107)
- His coach's mantra "so what" strips emotion out of a situation and forces focus on what options are actually available and what you'll do about it [ben-huh-on-therapy @ 02:30](https://youtu.be/e2644ylntQQ?t=150)
- Honesty is essential: if you hide your real state from a therapist (e.g., denial about depression), they can't actually help you, similar to giving an engineering team a vague spec instead of what you actually need [ben-huh-on-therapy @ 03:26](https://youtu.be/e2644ylntQQ?t=206)
- Being honest about personal motivations (even something like just wanting to make money) is useful and something to actually discuss rather than avoid [ben-huh-on-therapy @ 04:36](https://youtu.be/e2644ylntQQ?t=276)
- Founders often hesitate to spend money on therapy or coaching as a personal expense, but he argues the ROI from improved mental and emotional performance far outweighs the cost [ben-huh-on-therapy @ 05:14](https://youtu.be/e2644ylntQQ?t=314)
