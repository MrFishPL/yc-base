# Preston Silverman on Building out a Marketplace in Education - at YC Edtech Night — Compiled Truth

Raise.me founder Preston Silverman recounts building a two-sided scholarship marketplace, describing the slow path to product-market fit, a long first fundraise, and how the CEO job changed as the company scaled from solo founder to 50 employees.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Raise.me lets students accrue scholarship and grant money throughout high school based on their progress, instead of waiting until after they've applied and been accepted to college [preston-silverman-on-building-out-a-marketplace-in-education @ 01:35](https://youtu.be/82MhV6S8a5s?t=95)
- Facing a marketplace chicken-and-egg problem, he bet on securing the funding side first, believing students would follow once aid was available [preston-silverman-on-building-out-a-marketplace-in-education @ 06:23](https://youtu.be/82MhV6S8a5s?t=383)
- Building density in specific geographies and regions, rather than spreading thin, helped balance supply (funds) and demand (students) in the marketplace [preston-silverman-on-building-out-a-marketplace-in-education @ 06:40](https://youtu.be/82MhV6S8a5s?t=400)
- Product-market fit felt like a gradual awakening rather than a single moment, confirmed when the platform opened up and thousands of students started signing up [preston-silverman-on-building-out-a-marketplace-in-education @ 07:50](https://youtu.be/82MhV6S8a5s?t=470)
- The first funding round took over a year to close, during which he bootstrapped on savings from his previous job [preston-silverman-on-building-out-a-marketplace-in-education @ 08:17](https://youtu.be/82MhV6S8a5s?t=497)
- A $100,000 grant from the Gates Foundation was pivotal, funding the company's first employee hire [preston-silverman-on-building-out-a-marketplace-in-education @ 09:02](https://youtu.be/82MhV6S8a5s?t=542)
- The early-stage CEO job was highly tactical — customer interviews, prototyping, and hands-on selling to colleges and teachers consumed about 80% of his time [preston-silverman-on-building-out-a-marketplace-in-education @ 12:03](https://youtu.be/82MhV6S8a5s?t=723)
- As the company matured, his role shifted away from hands-on work toward setting vision and strategy, and recruiting the right team, since "everything comes down to your team" [preston-silverman-on-building-out-a-marketplace-in-education @ 13:01](https://youtu.be/82MhV6S8a5s?t=781)
- He warns against the "fail-fast" mentality overriding patience: early-stage survival requires resilience to iterate rather than throwing in the towel when things don't feel like a rocket ship on day one [preston-silverman-on-building-out-a-marketplace-in-education @ 13:40](https://youtu.be/82MhV6S8a5s?t=820)
