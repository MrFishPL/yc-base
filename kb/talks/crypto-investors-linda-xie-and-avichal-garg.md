# Crypto Investors - Linda Xie and Avichal Garg — Compiled Truth

Linda Xie (Scalar Capital, ex-Coinbase) and Avichal Garg discuss blockchain fundamentals, realistic timelines for crypto use cases, and how founders should think about ICOs, fundraising, vesting, and where crypto opportunities will actually emerge.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Avichal frames the space as four core building blocks — the blockchain/ledger, consensus mechanisms, token economics, and the smart-contracting layer that different projects mix together [crypto-investors-linda-xie-and-avichal-garg @ 02:06](https://youtu.be/fE_kCkJ8fEA?t=126)
- Decentralized exchanges and collectibles (e.g., CryptoKitties) are the use cases most likely to go mainstream within a few years, while fully decentralized apps are probably 3-10 years out due to scaling issues [crypto-investors-linda-xie-and-avichal-garg @ 04:43](https://youtu.be/fE_kCkJ8fEA?t=283)
- Crypto's early winners are likely to look like "ports" of existing ideas before native applications emerge, the way early web pages just replicated newspapers before social media and Airbnb-style native ideas appeared [crypto-investors-linda-xie-and-avichal-garg @ 11:06](https://youtu.be/fE_kCkJ8fEA?t=666)
- Adoption may leapfrog first in underserved markets — countries without bank accounts or with inflating currencies — even though company-building expertise will likely still concentrate around Silicon Valley's tribal knowledge [crypto-investors-linda-xie-and-avichal-garg @ 12:58](https://youtu.be/fE_kCkJ8fEA?t=778)
- Garg argues most projects should avoid a token sale and raise traditional equity instead, since attaching a token you don't yet need creates friction and regulatory risk [crypto-investors-linda-xie-and-avichal-garg @ 31:46](https://youtu.be/fE_kCkJ8fEA?t=1906)
- For teams that do raise huge sums, Garg recommends staging fund releases against milestones rather than taking it all upfront, since sitting on hundreds of millions of dollars distorts team incentives [crypto-investors-linda-xie-and-avichal-garg @ 32:35](https://youtu.be/fE_kCkJ8fEA?t=1955)
- Vesting schedules for both employees and investors are "tried-and-true" best practices from traditional startups that crypto teams should still adopt to keep incentives aligned [crypto-investors-linda-xie-and-avichal-garg @ 33:09](https://youtu.be/fE_kCkJ8fEA?t=1989)
- Founders should prioritize raising from value-add investors who can help scale the company over 3-5 years rather than crowdsourcing capital from thousands of speculative token buyers [crypto-investors-linda-xie-and-avichal-garg @ 35:39](https://youtu.be/fE_kCkJ8fEA?t=2139)
- Biggest unmet opportunities for founders include decentralized identity/reputation systems, decentralized governance research, scaling infrastructure, and dev tooling [crypto-investors-linda-xie-and-avichal-garg @ 24:13](https://youtu.be/fE_kCkJ8fEA?t=1453)
- Xie predicts mass adoption will happen either directly (people knowingly buying crypto via something like Coinbase) or invisibly, where users interact with blockchain-backed products without realizing it [crypto-investors-linda-xie-and-avichal-garg @ 47:44](https://youtu.be/fE_kCkJ8fEA?t=2864)
