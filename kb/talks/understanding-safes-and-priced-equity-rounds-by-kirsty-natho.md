# Understanding SAFEs and Priced Equity Rounds by Kirsty Nathoo — Compiled Truth

YC CFO Kirsty Nathoo walks founders through how SAFEs work, how post-money SAFEs affect dilution, and how a cap table changes as a company incorporates, raises SAFEs, grants options, and closes a priced Series A round.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Founders are responsible for understanding their own cap table and dilution — "my lawyers deal with my cap table" is a dangerous mindset [understanding-safes-and-priced-equity-rounds-by-kirsty-natho @ 01:57](https://youtu.be/Dk6JNTDec9I?t=117)
- A SAFE is not debt: it has no interest rate or maturity date, and the only two real negotiation points are the amount invested and the valuation cap [understanding-safes-and-priced-equity-rounds-by-kirsty-natho @ 04:20](https://youtu.be/Dk6JNTDec9I?t=260)
- Post-money valuation equals pre-money valuation plus the amount raised, and an investor's ownership from a SAFE or priced round equals amount raised divided by post-money valuation (or cap) [understanding-safes-and-priced-equity-rounds-by-kirsty-natho @ 11:22](https://youtu.be/Dk6JNTDec9I?t=682)
- With post-money SAFEs, later SAFE investors dilute the founders (existing shareholders) but do not dilute earlier SAFE investors [understanding-safes-and-priced-equity-rounds-by-kirsty-natho @ 18:10](https://youtu.be/Dk6JNTDec9I?t=1090)
- Founders often forget to net out SAFE dilution against their nominal cap-table ownership, wrongly believing they still own the pre-dilution percentage after issuing an option pool [understanding-safes-and-priced-equity-rounds-by-kirsty-natho @ 21:08](https://youtu.be/Dk6JNTDec9I?t=1268)
- In a priced round, three things happen in order: SAFEs convert to shares, the option pool is increased (commonly ~10%, rarely above ~15%), and then new investors put in money [understanding-safes-and-priced-equity-rounds-by-kirsty-natho @ 26:11](https://youtu.be/Dk6JNTDec9I?t=1571)
- In the worked example, founders who started at 100% ownership ended up at 51.5% after SAFEs, an option pool increase, and a $5M Series A on a $15M pre-money valuation [understanding-safes-and-priced-equity-rounds-by-kirsty-natho @ 39:29](https://youtu.be/Dk6JNTDec9I?t=2369)
- If a priced round is valued lower than a SAFE's cap, the SAFE converts at the (lower) round price instead of the cap, which can increase dilution beyond what founders expected [understanding-safes-and-priced-equity-rounds-by-kirsty-natho @ 34:11](https://youtu.be/Dk6JNTDec9I?t=2051)
- Nathoo advises against mixing convertible notes and SAFEs (it complicates calculations) and recommends post-money SAFEs going forward even if a company already raised on pre-money SAFEs [understanding-safes-and-priced-equity-rounds-by-kirsty-natho @ 40:54](https://youtu.be/Dk6JNTDec9I?t=2454)
- Founders should not over-optimize for a higher valuation cap when negotiating SAFEs — in her example, raising the cap from $8M to $10M only changed final founder ownership from 51.5% to 52.7% [understanding-safes-and-priced-equity-rounds-by-kirsty-natho @ 43:04](https://youtu.be/Dk6JNTDec9I?t=2584)
