# Diane Greene at Startup School 2013 — Compiled Truth

VMware co-founder Diane Greene tells the founding story of VMware: bootstrapping, careful angel/VC choices, launching a trust-based pricing model, and breaking into the enterprise through hardware-vendor partnerships rather than a direct sales force.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- VMware self-funded first with immediate family only, then deliberately chose angel investors (Andy Bechtolsheim, John Hennessy, David Cheriton) who were technically sophisticated enough to understand the product on a single phone call [diane-greene-at-startup-school-2013 @ 05:34](https://youtu.be/zSEeFxq2X_c?t=334)
- When VCs later offered a higher valuation during the dot-com peak, the founders held their valuation, kept a Dell-led round alive in parallel, and closed with Dell the day after the bubble burst in April 2000 [diane-greene-at-startup-school-2013 @ 07:29](https://youtu.be/zSEeFxq2X_c?t=449)
- With a giant long-term vision, the key early move was to find a smaller, doable first milestone that creates value for somebody rather than trying to build the whole vision at once [diane-greene-at-startup-school-2013 @ 09:59](https://youtu.be/zSEeFxq2X_c?t=599)
- Unsure what to charge, VMware launched a free 30-day trial with a trust-based system: $299 if used commercially, $99 for hobbyists who didn't want to renew every 30 days [diane-greene-at-startup-school-2013 @ 12:29](https://youtu.be/zSEeFxq2X_c?t=749)
- Rather than hiring a marketing firm, the founders self-launched at the Demo conference, auditioned themselves, and got a standing ovation after recovering from a live crash on stage [diane-greene-at-startup-school-2013 @ 13:04](https://youtu.be/zSEeFxq2X_c?t=784)
- VMware filed patents from day one to protect itself sitting between Microsoft and Intel, though Greene admits the patents "never really helped us that much" against a monopoly's legal resources [diane-greene-at-startup-school-2013 @ 16:30](https://youtu.be/zSEeFxq2X_c?t=990)
- Greene recruited a powerful board member (Larry Sonsini) with no prior relationship by giving the law firm a real reason to help beyond money, arguing you can generally convince people if you have a compelling reason [diane-greene-at-startup-school-2013 @ 18:03](https://youtu.be/zSEeFxq2X_c?t=1083)
- Unable to build an enterprise sales force, VMware partnered with hardware vendors (IBM, then resellers after a GPL legal snag killed the direct IBM reseller deal) to break into the enterprise market [diane-greene-at-startup-school-2013 @ 21:16](https://youtu.be/zSEeFxq2X_c?t=1276)
- Setting an artificial deadline for a "preferred hardware vendor" program got competing hardware vendors to sign up even though "I'm not sure they knew why they were signing up" [diane-greene-at-startup-school-2013 @ 23:47](https://youtu.be/zSEeFxq2X_c?t=1427)
