# Understanding Investor Terms & Incentives — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell explain that founders must weigh deal terms as heavily as valuation, and that understanding investor incentives — including how "come back with a lead" really means no — protects founders from getting outnegotiated.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Founders should care as much about deal terms (board control, participating preferred, pro rata rights) as about valuation, since bad terms can hurt a founder regardless of how high the valuation is [understanding-investor-terms-incentives-rookie-mistakes-with @ 00:18](https://youtu.be/wnyI7ZM_Mrk?t=18)
- Some founders have given up board control to raise a relatively small amount of money and were later fired as a result [understanding-investor-terms-incentives-rookie-mistakes-with @ 01:23](https://youtu.be/wnyI7ZM_Mrk?t=83)
- Using standard paperwork and an experienced startup lawyer helps offset the fact that a first-time founder is negotiating against a professional investor who does this repeatedly [understanding-investor-terms-incentives-rookie-mistakes-with @ 01:44](https://youtu.be/wnyI7ZM_Mrk?t=104)
- Investors who have never seen a company go from small to huge tend to structure terms around a modest "base hit" outcome (e.g. a $10-20M acquisition), which can misalign with a founder aiming bigger [understanding-investor-terms-incentives-rookie-mistakes-with @ 02:06](https://youtu.be/wnyI7ZM_Mrk?t=126)
- A term sheet for a smaller amount than the stated round size ("go find the rest yourself") is a soft way of an investor not fully committing [understanding-investor-terms-incentives-rookie-mistakes-with @ 03:08](https://youtu.be/wnyI7ZM_Mrk?t=188)
- When an investor says "come back when you find a lead," it usually functions as a polite no while giving the investor a free option to invest later if someone else validates the company [understanding-investor-terms-incentives-rookie-mistakes-with @ 03:26](https://youtu.be/wnyI7ZM_Mrk?t=206)
- Because investors run this negotiation professionally across many deals, founders should expect to be outmatched on tactics and treat vague future promises skeptically [understanding-investor-terms-incentives-rookie-mistakes-with @ 04:48](https://youtu.be/wnyI7ZM_Mrk?t=288)
- For companies investors already want to fund, investor incentives typically push toward "grow faster, spend more," which can create misalignment if a founder is profitable but not growing fast enough for the investor's target outcome [understanding-investor-terms-incentives-rookie-mistakes-with @ 06:43](https://youtu.be/wnyI7ZM_Mrk?t=403)
- As seed investing has professionalized, funds increasingly have their own ownership targets and LP obligations, which can incentivize convincing founders to raise more money than the company actually needs [understanding-investor-terms-incentives-rookie-mistakes-with @ 08:07](https://youtu.be/wnyI7ZM_Mrk?t=487)
