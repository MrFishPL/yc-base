# Advice on Organizing and Running Growth Teams from Dan Hockenmaier and Gustaf Alströmer — Compiled Truth

Two experienced growth practitioners argue growth is mostly about pairing a genuinely good product with rigorous, hypothesis-driven experimentation rather than growth-hacking tactics, and that most gains cluster in a single acquisition channel plus retention.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Growth advisors most often talk founders out of chasing too many acquisition tactics, since most gains come from one channel and a handful of tactics with a power-law distribution [advice-on-organizing-and-running-growth-teams-from-dan-hocke @ 00:38](https://youtu.be/tsg4vcDUyJA?t=38)
- Heavy paid acquisition before a company has cohorts with clearly leveling-off retention is a common and risky mistake because it can burn cash fast without a base that sticks [advice-on-organizing-and-running-growth-teams-from-dan-hocke @ 01:09](https://youtu.be/tsg4vcDUyJA?t=69)
- To get to $100M in revenue, roughly 70-80% of new customer acquisition typically comes from a single channel, so the best products are built to optimize specifically for that channel [advice-on-organizing-and-running-growth-teams-from-dan-hocke @ 06:09](https://youtu.be/tsg4vcDUyJA?t=369)
- A good product grows along a natural adoption curve that eventually slows; the growth team's job is to keep layering new channels, onboarding, and conversion improvements so it doesn't [advice-on-organizing-and-running-growth-teams-from-dan-hocke @ 07:50](https://youtu.be/tsg4vcDUyJA?t=470)
- Extending Airbnb's login session cookie from 2 days to unlimited lifted company-wide revenue by over 1%, showing how unglamorous product fixes can move core metrics [advice-on-organizing-and-running-growth-teams-from-dan-hocke @ 09:38](https://youtu.be/tsg4vcDUyJA?t=578)
- Testing should prioritize a few clear hypotheses with high effect sizes over running dozens of minor experiments, since a backlog of 80 tests often reduces to only 4-6 real hypotheses [advice-on-organizing-and-running-growth-teams-from-dan-hocke @ 12:01](https://youtu.be/tsg4vcDUyJA?t=721)
- Retention should be measured against the product's core value delivered over time, and companies should also find early proxy metrics for retention so they can experiment against them sooner [advice-on-organizing-and-running-growth-teams-from-dan-hocke @ 16:32](https://youtu.be/tsg4vcDUyJA?t=992)
- Paid marketing works best as an accelerant (seeding new markets or balancing a marketplace) rather than a primary growth strategy, since it degrades in efficiency and is expensive to sustain [advice-on-organizing-and-running-growth-teams-from-dan-hocke @ 37:11](https://youtu.be/tsg4vcDUyJA?t=2231)
- Enterprise sales requires identifying a target list and closing via traditional relationship-driven sales, whereas SMB/SaaS growth can be much more bottom-up and self-serve [advice-on-organizing-and-running-growth-teams-from-dan-hocke @ 30:26](https://youtu.be/tsg4vcDUyJA?t=1826)
- Airbnb's early growth came primarily from word of mouth (over 50% on guest side, over 70% on host side) driven by a uniquely shareable travel experience, supplemented by targeted paid search and SEO/referrals [advice-on-organizing-and-running-growth-teams-from-dan-hocke @ 47:04](https://youtu.be/tsg4vcDUyJA?t=2824)
