# Emily Weiss on the Insights That Grew Glossier — Compiled Truth

Glossier founder Emily Weiss describes how listening obsessively to customers via Into the Gloss and social media, plus disciplined hiring and focus, built glossier into a fast-growing, largely peer-referred beauty brand.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Glossier's product development relies on talking to people directly — posting a product idea to Into the Gloss or Instagram can generate a thousand responses within 20 minutes [emily-weiss-on-the-insights-that-grew-glossier-with-amy-buec @ 04:52](https://youtu.be/ex-fXyRJTU8?t=292)
- Customer feedback is treated as an art, not a science — the team blends editorial judgment with partial crowdsourced signal (e.g., only 30% of respondents saying a shade was off still shifted a decision) [emily-weiss-on-the-insights-that-grew-glossier-with-amy-buec @ 06:04](https://youtu.be/ex-fXyRJTU8?t=364)
- When customer-requested packaging (a pump) turned out to be infeasible with the chosen formula, Glossier explained the tradeoff transparently to customers rather than hiding it, and the product still became a bestseller [emily-weiss-on-the-insights-that-grew-glossier-with-amy-buec @ 07:02](https://youtu.be/ex-fXyRJTU8?t=422)
- Being direct-to-consumer lets Glossier own customer emails and data, enabling replenishment, targeted questions, and a relationship competitors selling through Sephora or drugstores can't replicate [emily-weiss-on-the-insights-that-grew-glossier-with-amy-buec @ 13:17](https://youtu.be/ex-fXyRJTU8?t=797)
- 79% of Glossier's growth in the prior year came from peer-to-peer recommendations rather than paid acquisition, reinforced by extreme focus and discipline in saying no to opportunities like retail distribution [emily-weiss-on-the-insights-that-grew-glossier-with-amy-buec @ 18:16](https://youtu.be/ex-fXyRJTU8?t=1096)
- The company achieved accidental profitability for a couple of months last year despite 600% year-over-year growth, driven by conservative spending rather than aggressive marketing burn [emily-weiss-on-the-insights-that-grew-glossier-with-amy-buec @ 17:19](https://youtu.be/ex-fXyRJTU8?t=1039)
- Weiss spends over 50% of her time on HR, people, and hiring — interviewing every new employee from intern to CEO level and taking multiple coffees a day across the team [emily-weiss-on-the-insights-that-grew-glossier-with-amy-buec @ 24:24](https://youtu.be/ex-fXyRJTU8?t=1464)
- Glossier deliberately takes big bets on people without direct experience, such as promoting a two-years-out-of-HBS hire with no marketing background to SVP of marketing, because experience is a weak predictor of success in a fast-changing field [emily-weiss-on-the-insights-that-grew-glossier-with-amy-buec @ 25:46](https://youtu.be/ex-fXyRJTU8?t=1546)
