# Paul Graham, Founder of Y Combinator, Live from Stockholm — Compiled Truth

Paul Graham argues that founders should go to Silicon Valley (at least temporarily) because it concentrates the best peers, faster-deciding investors, and a pay-it-forward culture, and that Stockholm can become a real startup hub if enough founders go, absorb that, and come back.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The talk poses two linked questions: should an individual founder go to Silicon Valley, and what should Stockholm do to thrive as a startup hub [paul-graham-founder-of-y-combinator-live-from-stockholm @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/QHJkUw31YX8?t=0)
- Moving to a big center expands your talent pool in two dimensions at once: the peers are both better and more numerous [paul-graham-founder-of-y-combinator-live-from-stockholm @ 01:30](https://youtu.be/QHJkUw31YX8?t=90)
- Unplanned, serendipitous meetings seem to matter far more to people doing ambitious work than planned ones [paul-graham-founder-of-y-combinator-live-from-stockholm @ 02:28](https://youtu.be/QHJkUw31YX8?t=148)
