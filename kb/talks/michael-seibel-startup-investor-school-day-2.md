# Michael Seibel - Startup Investor School Day 2 — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel shares blunt, personal lessons from 4.5 years of angel investing, mostly in YC companies, on check sizing, speed, conviction, and how to evaluate founders and cap tables.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Write a big enough check: model out what a billion-dollar exit would actually return you after dilution and taxes, or the investment isn't worth doing [michael-seibel-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 04:13](https://youtu.be/ugmjf0fJY84?t=253)
- He moved from $25k checks to $50k and then $200k checks once he realized small checks don't move the needle on a big outcome [michael-seibel-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 11:15](https://youtu.be/ugmjf0fJY84?t=675)
- Build a fast operational flow (banker, docs, wiring set up in advance) because you often have only hours or a day or two to decide [michael-seibel-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 05:23](https://youtu.be/ugmjf0fJY84?t=323)
- An "A investor" gives money quickly, signs paperwork quickly, and shuts up — most investors are not A investors [michael-seibel-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 05:46](https://youtu.be/ugmjf0fJY84?t=346)
- Only increase check size once you have real conviction in the founders; without conviction, keep learning and asking questions instead [michael-seibel-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 12:23](https://youtu.be/ugmjf0fJY84?t=743)
- The most hyped investment on demo day is often not the best one, because the room is good at getting worked up rather than being smart [michael-seibel-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 09:24](https://youtu.be/ugmjf0fJY84?t=564)
- Investors can do homework before demo day by reading company launches on Product Hunt, TechCrunch, and Hacker News rather than starting cold [michael-seibel-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 07:53](https://youtu.be/ugmjf0fJY84?t=473)
- Many good companies raise a small round six to twelve months after demo day, so staying helpful and maintaining founder relationships post-demo-day creates later opportunities [michael-seibel-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 08:35](https://youtu.be/ugmjf0fJY84?t=515)
- What worries him in a cap table isn't a crowded party round but massively unequal equity splits or founders who already sold off a large chunk of the company [michael-seibel-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 17:47](https://youtu.be/ugmjf0fJY84?t=1067)
- If founders are down to only 15-20% equity after modeling out typical 20-35% dilution at both Series A and Series B, that's a red flag on remaining motivation to produce a great exit [michael-seibel-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 18:33](https://youtu.be/ugmjf0fJY84?t=1113)
