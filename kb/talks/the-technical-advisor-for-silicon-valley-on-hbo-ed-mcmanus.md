# The Technical Advisor for Silicon Valley on HBO: Ed McManus — Compiled Truth

Ed McManus, a YC founder who sold his startup Yard Sale/Phobo in 2015, describes how his real startup experience shaped the technical realism of HBO's Silicon Valley, and how the writers room translated founder experiences like faked growth numbers into story arcs.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- He was the technical co-founder of YC startup Yard Sale (later Phobo), an iPhone marketplace, and sold the company in 2015 before becoming a technical advisor for Silicon Valley season 3 [the-technical-advisor-for-silicon-valley-on-hbo-ed-mcmanus @ 00:21](https://youtu.be/6kk03M3XfVY?t=21)
- The "middle-out" compression algorithm has real technical grounding in machine-learning-applied-to-compression research, though achieving its exact combination of properties (lossless, great for streaming) in the real world would itself be a scientific breakthrough [the-technical-advisor-for-silicon-valley-on-hbo-ed-mcmanus @ 11:39](https://youtu.be/6kk03M3XfVY?t=699)
- The dominant-incumbent threat the show draws on (a la "Hooli") is largely a holdover from 1990s Microsoft-era thinking, and while today's giants like Google feel less threatening, that may not last as companies like Facebook get more aggressive toward small startups [the-technical-advisor-for-silicon-valley-on-hbo-ed-mcmanus @ 13:02](https://youtu.be/6kk03M3XfVY?t=782)
- The plot of Pied Piper faking its user numbers was pulled directly from his own experience launching a beta with Yard Sale — strong early signal followed months later by doubts about whether the traction was real [the-technical-advisor-for-silicon-valley-on-hbo-ed-mcmanus @ 16:04](https://youtu.be/6kk03M3XfVY?t=964)
- That storyline was originally conceived around growth hacking and buying users, a tactic that felt like a legitimate path to product-market fit a few years earlier before drifting into "gray hat" territory and eventually outright fraud in the show [the-technical-advisor-for-silicon-valley-on-hbo-ed-mcmanus @ 16:39](https://youtu.be/6kk03M3XfVY?t=999)
- He pushed the writers to move Pied Piper onto AWS in season 3 because a real startup building and running its own servers felt inauthentic to anyone who had actually built a company [the-technical-advisor-for-silicon-valley-on-hbo-ed-mcmanus @ 18:38](https://youtu.be/6kk03M3XfVY?t=1118)
- To make a character's GitHub profile look authentic for on-screen playback, he built out a fake commit history and projects for it, then forgot to delete the account — it went viral on Reddit once the episode aired and fans found it [the-technical-advisor-for-silicon-valley-on-hbo-ed-mcmanus @ 22:39](https://youtu.be/6kk03M3XfVY?t=1359)
