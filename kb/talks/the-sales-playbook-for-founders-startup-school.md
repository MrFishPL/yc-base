# The Sales Playbook For Founders | Startup School — Compiled Truth

This talk lays out the typical progression of B2B sales stages early-stage founders move through, from unpaid design partnerships to recurring revenue contracts, and gives tactical advice for advancing through each stage faster.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The goal for early-stage companies is to progress rapidly through the sales-stage sequence so they can close new ARR every week, since 90% of founders get stuck too long in early stages [the-sales-playbook-for-founders-startup-school @ 00:51](https://youtu.be/DH7REvnQ1y4?t=51)
- Unpaid design partnerships often drag on for 3-6 months with poorly defined scope and low customer engagement, so founders should instead identify a narrow burning problem and build a wedge product in as little as 48 hours [the-sales-playbook-for-founders-startup-school @ 02:14](https://youtu.be/DH7REvnQ1y4?t=134)
- Once a wedge product is validated with one happy customer, founders should sell that same narrow product to 10 similar customers rather than overbuilding a broad platform [the-sales-playbook-for-founders-startup-school @ 04:06](https://youtu.be/DH7REvnQ1y4?t=246)
- A well-designed pilot or proof of concept should define agreed success metrics and a clear value equation, such as showing a customer service AI can solve 20% of inbound queries to justify a $200,000 price against a $1M savings [the-sales-playbook-for-founders-startup-school @ 07:26](https://youtu.be/DH7REvnQ1y4?t=446)
- Founders should get a financial commitment upfront for pilots since payment makes customers take the engagement seriously, and it's fine to take less money if it shortcuts a long approval process [the-sales-playbook-for-founders-startup-school @ 10:12](https://youtu.be/DH7REvnQ1y4?t=612)
- Time to first value should be tracked like a north-star metric, reducing it from weeks to hours by avoiding full API integrations during pilots and instead using Excel imports/exports [the-sales-playbook-for-founders-startup-school @ 12:24](https://youtu.be/DH7REvnQ1y4?t=744)
- The most sophisticated move is a recurring revenue contract with a 30-60 day opt-out period, so a single sales process converts directly into recurring revenue with no further negotiation needed [the-sales-playbook-for-founders-startup-school @ 13:37](https://youtu.be/DH7REvnQ1y4?t=817)
- Founders should be careful what they report to investors as MRR or ARR when customers are still inside an opt-out period [the-sales-playbook-for-founders-startup-school @ 14:58](https://youtu.be/DH7REvnQ1y4?t=898)
- After signing contracts, customer success and onboarding deserve as much effort as the sale itself, since one company signed $4M in contracts but only implemented $2M of them [the-sales-playbook-for-founders-startup-school @ 15:35](https://youtu.be/DH7REvnQ1y4?t=935)
- Founders should identify an internal champion and treat them like a co-founder, mapping the buying org (economic buyer, technical approver, security gatekeeper, legal, users) and driving the process forward themselves [the-sales-playbook-for-founders-startup-school @ 16:19](https://youtu.be/DH7REvnQ1y4?t=979)
