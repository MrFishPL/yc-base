# Sam Altman - How to Succeed with a Startup — Compiled Truth

Sam Altman lays out YC's core lessons for startup success: build something people spontaneously recommend, bet on markets about to explode rather than today's size, and assemble a team with the evangelism, conviction, and bias toward action that lets you outrun big companies.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The number one predictor of startup success is building a product so good that people spontaneously tell their friends about it — that's 80% of the work. [sam-altman-how-to-succeed-with-a-startup @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/0lJKucu6HJc?t=0)
- Look for a market that is about to undergo exponential growth rather than judging opportunity by today's total addressable market, since the iPhone app market was worth $0 eleven years before the talk. [sam-altman-how-to-succeed-with-a-startup @ 01:15](https://youtu.be/0lJKucu6HJc?t=75)
- Tell real trends from fake trends by checking whether early adopters use the product obsessively and evangelize it, versus buying it but barely using it. [sam-altman-how-to-succeed-with-a-startup @ 01:52](https://youtu.be/0lJKucu6HJc?t=112)
- As of August 2018, VR was a fake trend by this test — most people who own a VR headset use it rarely or never despite the hype. [sam-altman-how-to-succeed-with-a-startup @ 03:06](https://youtu.be/0lJKucu6HJc?t=186)
- Every startup needs at least one evangelical founder, usually the CEO, who can recruit, sell, talk to the press, and raise money with infectious enthusiasm. [sam-altman-how-to-succeed-with-a-startup @ 03:46](https://youtu.be/0lJKucu6HJc?t=226)
- The best founders show a confident and definite view of the future — they may be wrong, but conviction and clear leadership correlate strongly with success. [sam-altman-how-to-succeed-with-a-startup @ 05:47](https://youtu.be/0lJKucu6HJc?t=347)
- Vinod Khosla's line "the team you build is the company you build" holds true — recruiting is one of the most important things a founder does, alongside picking the market and building the product. [sam-altman-how-to-succeed-with-a-startup @ 06:48](https://youtu.be/0lJKucu6HJc?t=408)
- The best early team members say "I've got it" and take a bias toward action rather than saying "that's not my department." [sam-altman-how-to-succeed-with-a-startup @ 09:02](https://youtu.be/0lJKucu6HJc?t=542)
- Great businesses have a clear answer for their long-term competitive advantage or monopoly effect, yet most startups applying to YC have never even considered the question. [sam-altman-how-to-succeed-with-a-startup @ 11:01](https://youtu.be/0lJKucu6HJc?t=661)
- Startups usually beat big companies on ideas that "sound bad but are good," because a startup only needs one investor to say yes while a big company needs everyone up the chain to say yes. [sam-altman-how-to-succeed-with-a-startup @ 12:57](https://youtu.be/0lJKucu6HJc?t=777)
- Startups also win in fast-changing markets (more decisions and tweaks to compound an advantage) and after big platform shifts like the iPhone, since large companies turn too slowly to make a big enough strategic pivot. [sam-altman-how-to-succeed-with-a-startup @ 14:38](https://youtu.be/0lJKucu6HJc?t=878)
