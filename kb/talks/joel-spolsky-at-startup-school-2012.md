# Joel Spolsky at Startup School 2012 — Compiled Truth

Contrasting Fog Creek Software (bootstrapped, organic growth) with Stack Exchange (VC-backed, get-big-fast), Joel Spolsky argues founders must consciously choose one growth model based on whether their market has land-grab network effects, because straddling both is what actually kills companies.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Fog Creek was bootstrapped on consulting revenue (~$250k banked) before spending anything, so the company survived the dot-com crash that wiped out consulting rivals [joel-spolsky-at-startup-school-2012 @ 04:48](https://youtu.be/pPJf8KrvJXU?t=288)
- There are two fundamentally different company types: "get big fast" land-grab businesses (Amazon, Facebook, Uber) versus "organic growth" businesses (Ben & Jerry's, Fog Creek), and the choice is dictated by whether the market has network effects to grab [joel-spolsky-at-startup-school-2012 @ 13:02](https://youtu.be/pPJf8KrvJXU?t=782)
- Network effects create lock-in (e.g., nobody leaves Facebook for something 100% better because their friends are still there), which is why land-grab businesses must move fast and raise money to fund the grab [joel-spolsky-at-startup-school-2012 @ 15:24](https://youtu.be/pPJf8KrvJXU?t=924)
- An organic growth business must be able to break even quickly and be valuable even to a single customer, since mistakes can kill it and there's no big outside capital cushion [joel-spolsky-at-startup-school-2012 @ 16:34](https://youtu.be/pPJf8KrvJXU?t=994)
- You should delay raising money as long as possible, because as the company gets more valuable you dilute less to raise the same amount of cash [joel-spolsky-at-startup-school-2012 @ 21:22](https://youtu.be/pPJf8KrvJXU?t=1282)
- Stack Overflow was technically bootstrapped for two years with just three unpaid full-timers before the founders recognized it had strong network effects and pivoted to raising money to fund a land grab [joel-spolsky-at-startup-school-2012 @ 22:07](https://youtu.be/pPJf8KrvJXU?t=1327)
- Because Stack Exchange delayed fundraising and had 10 years of Fog Creek reputation plus 2 years of momentum behind it, raising money "was like falling off a log" and took only two weeks [joel-spolsky-at-startup-school-2012 @ 25:00](https://youtu.be/pPJf8KrvJXU?t=1500)
- Trello was launched free specifically to reduce friction and capture network effects as a land-grab product, self-funded by Fog Creek's Fog Bugs profits rather than outside VC [joel-spolsky-at-startup-school-2012 @ 27:45](https://youtu.be/pPJf8KrvJXU?t=1665)
- Failing to commit to one growth model or the other is what actually kills companies, illustrated by Juno Online's fear of matching AOL's land-grab spending on network effects and lock-in [joel-spolsky-at-startup-school-2012 @ 30:11](https://youtu.be/pPJf8KrvJXU?t=1811)
