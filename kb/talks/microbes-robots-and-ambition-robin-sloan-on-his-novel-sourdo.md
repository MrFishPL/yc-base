# Microbes, Robots, and Ambition - Robin Sloan on His Novel Sourdough — Compiled Truth

Novelist Robin Sloan discusses using a machine-learning audio model to generate fictional music for his audiobook, his fascination with the creative (not just economic) applications of ML, and the fragility of digital creative work compared to print.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- For the Sourdough audiobook, Sloan trained a machine-learning model (SampleRNN, built on Torch/Lua) on Croatian klapa folk recordings for days to generate a wholly invented "alien" language and music for the book's fictional sound [microbes-robots-and-ambition-robin-sloan-on-his-novel-sourdo @ 03:19](https://youtu.be/muWW7QWUnGk?t=199)
- He believes this may be the first time creative output from a machine-learning system has been included in a published audiobook [microbes-robots-and-ambition-robin-sloan-on-his-novel-sourdo @ 04:20](https://youtu.be/muWW7QWUnGk?t=260)
- Most ML effort goes toward practical, economically valuable applications, leaving what he calls an "arbitrage opportunity" for artists willing to get even a basic technical grip on the tools [microbes-robots-and-ambition-robin-sloan-on-his-novel-sourdo @ 38:49](https://youtu.be/muWW7QWUnGk?t=2329)
- He built an Atom text-editor plugin that autocompletes prose using a text-generation model trained on a chosen corpus, letting a human curate/arrow through multiple generated completions rather than just accepting the raw output [microbes-robots-and-ambition-robin-sloan-on-his-novel-sourdo @ 44:06](https://youtu.be/muWW7QWUnGk?t=2646)
- He argues the real frontier for ML creative tools is building instruments people can get progressively better at using, not just one-shot "run it and see" generators [microbes-robots-and-ambition-robin-sloan-on-his-novel-sourdo @ 42:16](https://youtu.be/muWW7QWUnGk?t=2536)
- Digital creative work is fragile compared to print — his own iOS app "Fish" broke after an OS update and he lost the source code entirely, illustrating how platforms shift under creators' feet [microbes-robots-and-ambition-robin-sloan-on-his-novel-sourdo @ 23:45](https://youtu.be/muWW7QWUnGk?t=1425)
- He deliberately writes and runs a script to delete his old tweets, treating Twitter as an ephemeral "cafe conversation" rather than a permanent record [microbes-robots-and-ambition-robin-sloan-on-his-novel-sourdo @ 31:00](https://youtu.be/muWW7QWUnGk?t=1860)
- He generally starts from an admired format ("book-shaped impulse" or "app-shaped impulse") rather than picking a story first and then choosing its ideal vessel [microbes-robots-and-ambition-robin-sloan-on-his-novel-sourdo @ 22:17](https://youtu.be/muWW7QWUnGk?t=1337)
