# Content Marketing Tips from Experts at First Round Capital and Andreessen Horowitz — Compiled Truth

Camille Ricketts (First Round) and Sonal Chokshi (a16z) share how experienced editorial teams measure content, find distribution, and protect creative focus — arguing that a clear "why" and relentless focus on the reader beat raw output volume.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Neither pageviews nor an arbitrary word count matter as much as tying measurement to your actual strategic goal; both editors weight time-on-site and engagement over pageviews. [content-marketing-tips-from-experts-at-first-round-capital-a @ 00:43](https://youtu.be/xBq4FN7dTZY?t=43)
- First Round set up Slack alerts so the team is notified whenever anyone with over 10,000 followers tweets about a piece, treating social sharing as validation that content was valuable enough to spread. [content-marketing-tips-from-experts-at-first-round-capital-a @ 08:45](https://youtu.be/xBq4FN7dTZY?t=525)
- A borrowed "1,000 true fans" trick: keep curated private Twitter lists of the real influencers/geeks in a given vertical (e.g., mapmakers, ML people) as a starting point for building relationships. [content-marketing-tips-from-experts-at-first-round-capital-a @ 13:14](https://youtu.be/xBq4FN7dTZY?t=794)
- a16z picked long-form text as its core medium only after defining a guiding "North Star" (their phrase: "The New Yorker meets the Harvard Business Review") that clarified what their audience needed and didn't already get elsewhere. [content-marketing-tips-from-experts-at-first-round-capital-a @ 17:22](https://youtu.be/xBq4FN7dTZY?t=1042)
- Good content is roughly 80% about killing ideas that don't fit — having a clear editorial lens lets you decide what not to publish, not just what to publish. [content-marketing-tips-from-experts-at-first-round-capital-a @ 20:04](https://youtu.be/xBq4FN7dTZY?t=1204)
- The best way to find content ideas is to talk to everyone possible — sales, customers, people on the ground — and even ask to sit in on calls or briefings you can't personally attend. [content-marketing-tips-from-experts-at-first-round-capital-a @ 24:51](https://youtu.be/xBq4FN7dTZY?t=1491)
- An editorial calendar isn't just planning — it's what forces the discipline to balance reactive, urgent "flow" content against proactive, high-effort "stock" pieces you're intentionally building toward. [content-marketing-tips-from-experts-at-first-round-capital-a @ 32:59](https://youtu.be/xBq4FN7dTZY?t=1979)
- Data narratives are a hugely underused differentiator for startup content marketers, since proprietary data is something no competitor can copy. [content-marketing-tips-from-experts-at-first-round-capital-a @ 53:14](https://youtu.be/xBq4FN7dTZY?t=3194)
- The single biggest pitfall companies fall into is turning content into a disguised sales pitch — thin interviews built around a hard sell embarrass the subject and give readers no real insight. [content-marketing-tips-from-experts-at-first-round-capital-a @ 55:54](https://youtu.be/xBq4FN7dTZY?t=3354)
- First Round's flagship piece, "Radical Candor" with Kim Scott, worked because it distilled a fuzzy management problem (how to give feedback) into a clear, reusable framework people could print and put on their wall. [content-marketing-tips-from-experts-at-first-round-capital-a @ 1:04:32](https://youtu.be/xBq4FN7dTZY?t=3872)
