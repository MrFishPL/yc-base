# Experiments in Art and Technology with Artforum Editor Michelle Kuo — Compiled Truth

Artforum editor Michelle Kuo recounts the history of Experiments in Art and Technology (E.A.T.), a 1960s group pairing artists with Bell Labs engineers, using its scaling, funding, and public failures as a lens on the recurring tension between technologists, artists, and automation anxiety.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- E.A.T. was founded in 1966 when artist Robert Rauschenberg partnered with Bell Labs engineer Billy Klüver, after Klüver solved a one-off request (a battery-powered neon light) for Jasper Johns. [experiments-in-art-and-technology-with-artforum-editor-miche @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/tQ21eMrEP6c?t=0)
- The founding insight was to scale one lucky artist-engineer pairing into a system: give any curious artist access to an engineer, rather than leaving collaboration to chance social circles. [experiments-in-art-and-technology-with-artforum-editor-miche @ 01:35](https://youtu.be/tQ21eMrEP6c?t=95)
- E.A.T.'s first major undertaking, a 40-person performance series at the Armory, involved months of ambitious promises but almost nothing was tested beforehand, causing embarrassing opening-night delays in front of prominent guests. [experiments-in-art-and-technology-with-artforum-editor-miche @ 04:52](https://youtu.be/tQ21eMrEP6c?t=292)
- E.A.T. grew from an ad hoc friend network into nearly 5,000 members by 1970 through deliberate outreach — booths at engineering trade fairs (I Triple E), campus talks, and open membership forms. [experiments-in-art-and-technology-with-artforum-editor-miche @ 08:15](https://youtu.be/tQ21eMrEP6c?t=495)
- E.A.T. partnered with a labor lawyer to build "Automation House," directly addressing 1960s-era public fear that automation would destroy jobs — the same debate now recurring around AI. [experiments-in-art-and-technology-with-artforum-editor-miche @ 17:47](https://youtu.be/tQ21eMrEP6c?t=1067)
- Their most ambitious project, the Pepsi Pavilion at Osaka's 1970 World's Fair, was a genuinely global collaboration that repurposed satellite-balloon mylar technology to build an inflated mirror dome. [experiments-in-art-and-technology-with-artforum-editor-miche @ 24:07](https://youtu.be/tQ21eMrEP6c?t=1447)
- Despite the technical achievement, Pepsi found the result "too weird," budget overruns and a fog-sculpture scare triggered a legal dispute, and E.A.T. was ultimately expelled and its experimental soundtrack replaced with "It's a Small World." [experiments-in-art-and-technology-with-artforum-editor-miche @ 25:10](https://youtu.be/tQ21eMrEP6c?t=1510)
- On whether machine-made work is still art: a human still has to trigger the process even when an algorithm determines the outcome, and many artists deliberately use machines specifically to challenge traditional notions of individual authorship. [experiments-in-art-and-technology-with-artforum-editor-miche @ 44:25](https://youtu.be/tQ21eMrEP6c?t=2665)
- Artist Trevor Paglen's work tries to make visible the automated, "post-vision" technologies (like drone surveillance) that operate entirely outside human optical perception, forcing viewers to confront systems that need no human in the loop. [experiments-in-art-and-technology-with-artforum-editor-miche @ 39:19](https://youtu.be/tQ21eMrEP6c?t=2359)
- Kuo hopes technology can eventually get a genuine public institution the way art has museums — countering the trend where the internet, instead of democratizing knowledge, produced ever more specialized and walled-off communities. [experiments-in-art-and-technology-with-artforum-editor-miche @ 54:16](https://youtu.be/tQ21eMrEP6c?t=3256)
