# Building The World's Best Image Diffusion Model — Compiled Truth

Playground founder Suhail Doshi explains how his team ripped up their image-generation model architecture to obsess over prompt adherence and text accuracy, choosing a visual, template-first product over raw prompting, and describes the founder journey of pivoting through Mixpanel, Mighty, and finally Playground.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The team completely ripped up their planned architecture about a month and a half before release when text accuracy was stuck at 45%, feeling "lost in the jungle" before it worked [building-the-world-s-best-image-diffusion-model @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/VyIOoqjm8HA?t=0)
- Playground made the product visual-first and template-based rather than text/prompt-first, since most users can't write good prompts and shouldn't have to [building-the-world-s-best-image-diffusion-model @ 11:10](https://youtu.be/VyIOoqjm8HA?t=670)
- Watching how users actually behaved (constant "re-rolling" to fix bad outputs) revealed the real product failure, not just anecdotal feedback that the tool was "fun" [building-the-world-s-best-image-diffusion-model @ 13:01](https://youtu.be/VyIOoqjm8HA?t=781)
- The company deliberately chose to abandon a large existing "near-porn" user base and NSFW use cases in favor of commercial graphic-design use cases like logos, t-shirts, and posters [building-the-world-s-best-image-diffusion-model @ 34:03](https://youtu.be/VyIOoqjm8HA?t=2043)
- Doshi ditched CLIP and the standard stable-diffusion VAE/U-Net stack entirely, betting months of compute on a riskier custom architecture to get better text and prompt adherence [building-the-world-s-best-image-diffusion-model @ 23:53](https://youtu.be/VyIOoqjm8HA?t=1433)
- Being too prompt-adherent created an "entanglement problem" where the model gets penalized on aesthetic A/B evals for correctly following instructions that hurt visual appeal [building-the-world-s-best-image-diffusion-model @ 30:11](https://youtu.be/VyIOoqjm8HA?t=1811)
- At his earlier startup Mixpanel, Doshi learned to deliberately drop a large but low-value gaming customer segment to chase the far bigger mobile/internet market, a decision that paid off completely [building-the-world-s-best-image-diffusion-model @ 36:52](https://youtu.be/VyIOoqjm8HA?t=2212)
- At his prior startup Mighty (cloud browser), Doshi shut it down after concluding it lacked a technology tailwind (fighting Apple's M1 and browser architecture), teaching him the value of building with tailwinds instead of headwinds [building-the-world-s-best-image-diffusion-model @ 44:04](https://youtu.be/VyIOoqjm8HA?t=2644)
- Reaching state-of-the-art required a "maniacal" attention to small details like kerning, skin texture, and film grain that most teams overlook [building-the-world-s-best-image-diffusion-model @ 53:00](https://youtu.be/VyIOoqjm8HA?t=3180)
