# Office Hours at Startup School NY 2014 — Compiled Truth

Live YC-style office hours with three early-stage startups (Salary Fairy, Pare Up, and a couples-expense-splitting app), where partners push each founder toward narrow focus, real retention signals, and hard questions about built-in growth.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- It's far more important to build something a small number of users really love than something a lot of users find mildly interesting, since a startup can't yet win on breadth like Google can [office-hours-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 04:13](https://youtu.be/wKaJPZMOWVg?t=253)
- Track the specific metric that separates idle curiosity (used once, never returns) from users who become genuinely dependent on the product [office-hours-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 03:57](https://youtu.be/wKaJPZMOWVg?t=237)
- Early traction channels like Hacker News, Reddit, and press don't scale forever, so founders need to think about building growth directly into the product rather than treating it as an afterthought [office-hours-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 07:51](https://youtu.be/wKaJPZMOWVg?t=471)
- When testing a marketplace hypothesis on the web but the real intended use case is mobile, results often produce bad data, so getting to the true mobile experience quickly matters [office-hours-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 14:07](https://youtu.be/wKaJPZMOWVg?t=847)
- Hyper-focusing on a very specific area and vertical (not even a whole city) is the right early move once you know what's working [office-hours-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 14:07](https://youtu.be/wKaJPZMOWVg?t=847)
- Splitting expenses with friends/roommates is one of the most common YC application ideas, so a founder must be disciplined about what's actually new and why it will succeed where many others failed [office-hours-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 22:42](https://youtu.be/wKaJPZMOWVg?t=1362)
- A product with no inherent viral loop (like a couples-only expense app) needs some other engineered growth path — PR, an invite flow, etc. — since it can't rely on network spread alone [office-hours-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 21:35](https://youtu.be/wKaJPZMOWVg?t=1295)
- Until a startup has a few hundred or a thousand users who truly love the product, founders are largely guessing, so getting to real usage data is the priority over polishing distribution [office-hours-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 24:15](https://youtu.be/wKaJPZMOWVg?t=1455)
- A high activity/return rate among even a small beta cohort (e.g., ~25 of 40 users engaging every few days) is a strong early signal worth acting on before over-investing in distribution like app store presence [office-hours-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 25:44](https://youtu.be/wKaJPZMOWVg?t=1544)
