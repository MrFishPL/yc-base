# Chase Adam at Startup School NY 2014 — Compiled Truth

Chase Adam, founder of Watsi (Y Combinator's first funded non-profit), tells how a chance encounter on a bus in Central America led to a crowdfunded-healthcare platform, and shares the fundraising, hiring, and focus lessons learned building a mission-driven organization as a volunteer team before and after YC.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The idea for Watsi came from watching a woman on a bus in Central America raise money for her son's medical care by passing around his medical record to earn strangers' trust. [chase-adam-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 04:34](https://youtu.be/Z8_8jNLsZms?t=274)
- Watsi committed early to three founding principles: 100% of every donation funds patient care directly, full financial and operational transparency, and optimizing the ratio of overhead to patient value rather than top-line growth. [chase-adam-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 07:27](https://youtu.be/Z8_8jNLsZms?t=447)
- The team ran Watsi for a year as unpaid volunteers spread across 4 continents and 6 time zones, which removed pressure from users or funders and let them take bigger risks. [chase-adam-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 06:48](https://youtu.be/Z8_8jNLsZms?t=408)
- A single Hacker News post drove 16,000 uniques and funded every patient in the pipeline within hours, immediately exposing how manual and fragile the back-end operations still were. [chase-adam-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 08:39](https://youtu.be/Z8_8jNLsZms?t=519)
- Non-profit fundraising is harder than startup fundraising: there's no round deadline creating urgency, you need many small donors instead of one big check, and people are "too nice" to say no outright. [chase-adam-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 12:55](https://youtu.be/Z8_8jNLsZms?t=775)
- During YC, focusing on one core metric — average weekly donations — rather than chasing every task was the most valuable discipline learned. [chase-adam-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 16:08](https://youtu.be/Z8_8jNLsZms?t=968)
- After YC, setting an arbitrary but fixed close date for a "round" of donations created urgency and helped convert 14 backers, including PG, Ron Conway, and Vinod Khosla. [chase-adam-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 17:47](https://youtu.be/Z8_8jNLsZms?t=1067)
- The team grew deliberately slowly, prioritizing hiring people smarter than himself, because he saw the founding team's culture as something that would compound over the organization's intended decades-long life. [chase-adam-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 20:02](https://youtu.be/Z8_8jNLsZms?t=1202)
- Embracing the awkwardness of being understaffed rather than hiring ahead of growth forced more creativity and kept focus on what mattered most. [chase-adam-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 19:25](https://youtu.be/Z8_8jNLsZms?t=1165)
