# From A Pivot To Building A $9.6 Billion Payroll Company — Compiled Truth

Gusto co-founder and CEO Josh Reeves recounts pivoting from an "ask an expert" idea to payroll within weeks of applying to YC, and the deliberate, focus-driven choices that built Gusto into a large payroll and back-office company.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The original YC application idea was a call-in expert marketplace, but within a week or two most calls were about astrology and relationship advice, which the founders weren't equipped or excited to answer [from-a-pivot-to-building-a-9-6-billion-payroll-company @ 03:22](https://youtu.be/VaQA55ZZWBU?t=202)
- Chasing that expert-marketplace idea's payments logic led step by step to payroll: contractor payouts, then marketplace payouts, then "how does anyone get paid" — landing on payroll before the batch even started [from-a-pivot-to-building-a-9-6-billion-payroll-company @ 04:32](https://youtu.be/VaQA55ZZWBU?t=272)
- The real signal to abandon the expert idea wasn't logic but obsession: the founders weren't losing sleep over it, while talking to small businesses about payroll pain (40% of US companies were making payroll mistakes and getting penalized) resonated deeply [from-a-pivot-to-building-a-9-6-billion-payroll-company @ 06:27](https://youtu.be/VaQA55ZZWBU?t=387)
- Early on Gusto deliberately scoped to one product and the bare-bones functionality needed (tax filing, tax payment, tax calculation, moving money employer-to-employee), even limiting to California to avoid historical tax import complexity [from-a-pivot-to-building-a-9-6-billion-payroll-company @ 08:37](https://youtu.be/VaQA55ZZWBU?t=517)
- The founders added self-imposed pressure by refusing to pay themselves until they could run payroll through their own system [from-a-pivot-to-building-a-9-6-billion-payroll-company @ 09:29](https://youtu.be/VaQA55ZZWBU?t=569)
- After demo day, Gusto deliberately delayed public launch until December 2012, only onboarding known YC companies first rather than opening to strangers immediately [from-a-pivot-to-building-a-9-6-billion-payroll-company @ 10:55](https://youtu.be/VaQA55ZZWBU?t=655)
- Gusto raised its seed round entirely from over 20 angel investors (CEOs and founders of PayPal, Stripe, Mint, Instagram, etc.) rather than VC firms, despite 11+ VC offers [from-a-pivot-to-building-a-9-6-billion-payroll-company @ 13:43](https://youtu.be/VaQA55ZZWBU?t=823)
- Reeves argues competition in a large, fragmented market (payroll incumbents like ADP and Paychecks held only 20-30% share) is a good sign that others also saw the opportunity, and founders should focus on their own execution rather than competitors [from-a-pivot-to-building-a-9-6-billion-payroll-company @ 20:00](https://youtu.be/VaQA55ZZWBU?t=1200)
- Expanding to a second product (benefits) required pulling people off payroll and creating separate "swim lanes" with autonomy, aligned closely to existing customer feedback rather than needing new customer acquisition [from-a-pivot-to-building-a-9-6-billion-payroll-company @ 18:12](https://youtu.be/VaQA55ZZWBU?t=1092)
- Reeves frames AI as enabling Gusto to become an opinionated "back office agent" proactively personalized to a small business's industry and needs, rather than just a set of tools [from-a-pivot-to-building-a-9-6-billion-payroll-company @ 22:41](https://youtu.be/VaQA55ZZWBU?t=1361)
