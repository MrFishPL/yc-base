# Female Founders Conference - New York — Compiled Truth

A multi-speaker YC Female Founders Conference session: Jessica Livingston tells the founding story of Y Combinator, followed by fireside chats with founders of The Muse, Zola, AptDeco, Bulletin, and Human Utility on hiring, fundraising, and early customer discovery.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Y Combinator's founding thesis was to give small amounts of money to large numbers of early-stage startups and then help them a lot, instead of giving large amounts to small numbers of established companies like traditional VCs did [female-founders-conference-new-york @ 25:48](https://youtu.be/kk4yyh81fuo?t=1548)
- YC's first batch in 2005 funded eight startups at $6,000 per founder, run in Cambridge with weekly dinners and guest speakers [female-founders-conference-new-york @ 27:00](https://youtu.be/kk4yyh81fuo?t=1620)
- Jessica Livingston's role at YC was to judge founders' character and the relationships between co-founders — qualities like earnestness, determination, and flexibility — since she couldn't judge technical ability [female-founders-conference-new-york @ 31:11](https://youtu.be/kk4yyh81fuo?t=1871)
- Startup founders don't need a conventional success profile; ask what's distinctive about your own unique combination of abilities and interests rather than editing yourself to fit a mold [female-founders-conference-new-york @ 39:08](https://youtu.be/kk4yyh81fuo?t=2348)
- The Muse's early hiring mistake: an engineer hired for his stated passion instead demanded co-founder status and more equity seven weeks in, revealing he'd wanted the "sexy" YC brand rather than the mission [female-founders-conference-new-york @ 49:30](https://youtu.be/kk4yyh81fuo?t=2970)
- "Culture fit" interviewing is a major vector for bias unless it's tied to specific named values, since untrained interviewers default to "would I be comfortable hanging out with this person" [female-founders-conference-new-york @ 53:07](https://youtu.be/kk4yyh81fuo?t=3187)
- Zola solved two classic e-commerce killers — returns and inventory — by not shipping gifts until the couple requests them (enabling virtual returns) and by drop-shipping from partner warehouses instead of holding stock [female-founders-conference-new-york @ 1:11:46](https://youtu.be/kk4yyh81fuo?t=4306)
- Female founders reported getting consistent investor feedback that they pitch more modestly and factually than male founders, who "bang their fists on the table" with certainty it'll be a billion-dollar business [female-founders-conference-new-york @ 1:19:08](https://youtu.be/kk4yyh81fuo?t=4748)
- Bulletin's original Etsy-competitor idea sold nothing, so the founders talked to those users, learned they actually wanted access to physical retail space, and pivoted to building that instead [female-founders-conference-new-york @ 1:26:03](https://youtu.be/kk4yyh81fuo?t=5163)
- AptDeco solved the marketplace chicken-and-egg problem by manually copying other people's Craigslist furniture listings onto its own platform before sellers even knew they'd been listed [female-founders-conference-new-york @ 1:28:45](https://youtu.be/kk4yyh81fuo?t=5325)
