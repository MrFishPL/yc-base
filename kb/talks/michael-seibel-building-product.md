# Michael Seibel - Building Product — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel lays out a practical framework for defining the problem you're solving, finding desperate first customers, instrumenting metrics correctly, and running a disciplined product development cycle, drawing on failures and lessons from justin.tv/Twitch and Socialcam.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- If you can't state the problem you're solving in one or two sentences, you don't actually know the problem [michael-seibel-building-product @ 05:16](https://youtu.be/C27RVio2rOs?t=316)
- Define the problem narrowly to find who you can help first, rather than trying to solve the "mega problem" for everyone on day one [michael-seibel-building-product @ 06:14](https://youtu.be/C27RVio2rOs?t=374)
- Look for customers with high-frequency, high-intensity problems (the Uber example: needing to travel is intense and happens daily) rather than infrequent, low-intensity ones [michael-seibel-building-product @ 13:37](https://youtu.be/C27RVio2rOs?t=817)
- Starting with a higher price (or charging at all) filters for users who actually have the problem, while free users who don't have the problem will lead you astray [michael-seibel-building-product @ 15:17](https://youtu.be/C27RVio2rOs?t=917)
- Seek out the most desperate customers first for your MVP rather than the hardest-to-win, impressive customers, since an MVP is by definition bad and only desperate people will tolerate it [michael-seibel-building-product @ 21:30](https://youtu.be/C27RVio2rOs?t=1290)
- Ignore feedback from friends and investors — they rarely have the actual problem you're solving and will lead founders astray with good intentions [michael-seibel-building-product @ 23:11](https://youtu.be/C27RVio2rOs?t=1391)
- Use event-based analytics tools (Mixpanel, Amplitude, Heap) instead of Google Analytics to know what users actually do in the product, and bake measurement into every product spec upfront [michael-seibel-building-product @ 27:56](https://youtu.be/C27RVio2rOs?t=1676)
- Run a disciplined product cycle: track one top-line KPI (usually revenue or a usage metric like DAU), brainstorm openly, sort ideas by easy/medium/hard, then write a spec before building [michael-seibel-building-product @ 35:59](https://youtu.be/C27RVio2rOs?t=2159)
- Define pivot as changing the customer or problem (rare) versus iterate as changing the solution (common); two months is not enough time to conclude something isn't working [michael-seibel-building-product @ 44:28](https://youtu.be/C27RVio2rOs?t=2668)
- Real Steve Jobs iterated constantly and talked to customers; the first iPhone had no 3G, one carrier, and no App Store — "fake Steve Jobs" is the myth of shipping perfection from imagination alone [michael-seibel-building-product @ 46:00](https://youtu.be/C27RVio2rOs?t=2760)
