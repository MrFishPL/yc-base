# z1aKRhRnVNk.en-en-US — Compiled Truth

Nicolas Dessaigne (Algolia co-founder, YC group partner) lays out how to start a dev tools company: pick a runtime rather than build-time idea, ship a scrappy prototype fast, talk to developer users directly, and go to market via outreach, Hacker News launches, open source, and founder-led sales and marketing rather than a traditional sales/marketing org.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Runtime dev tool ideas (things that break your customer's product if down, like an API) are better bets than build-time ideas (docs, QA, testing) because they're must-haves with usage-aligned incentives. [z1aKRhRnVNk.en-en-US @ 00:09](https://youtu.be/z1aKRhRnVNk.en-en-US?t=9)
- Don't wait for the perfect differentiated idea (e.g. LLM observability is crowded but still worth starting in); 50% of YC companies eventually pivot anyway. [z1aKRhRnVNk.en-en-US @ 00:09](https://youtu.be/z1aKRhRnVNk.en-en-US?t=9)
- You don't need a business co-founder to sell dev tools: 74% of YC dev tool companies had only technical co-founders, versus 45% for other companies. [z1aKRhRnVNk.en-en-US @ 00:09](https://youtu.be/z1aKRhRnVNk.en-en-US?t=9)
- Build a quick and dirty prototype expecting to throw away 90% of the code, and show it to users even before an MVP exists — Algolia closed its first $2,000/month contract with just a command line and a barebones search page. [z1aKRhRnVNk.en-en-US @ 00:09](https://youtu.be/z1aKRhRnVNk.en-en-US?t=9)
- Founders should do outreach themselves early (network, friends of friends, personalized messages) since nobody knows a new dev tool yet, and Hacker News's Show HN is the best launch venue for feedback and awareness. [z1aKRhRnVNk.en-en-US @ 00:09](https://youtu.be/z1aKRhRnVNk.en-en-US?t=9)
- Doing things that don't scale — like Stripe's team coding side-by-side with early customers — is a valid way to gather fast feedback and satisfy early users. [z1aKRhRnVNk.en-en-US @ 00:09](https://youtu.be/z1aKRhRnVNk.en-en-US?t=9)
- Open source can be a strong go-to-market and trust signal (especially for libraries, sensitive-data tools, or enterprise sales like Medplum's open source EHR), but it must eventually be monetized via hosting, open-core enterprise features, or (less advisably) paid support. [z1aKRhRnVNk.en-en-US @ 00:09](https://youtu.be/z1aKRhRnVNk.en-en-US?t=9)
- Founders should sell the product themselves and wait until roughly $1M ARR before hiring a first salesperson, favoring technical "product specialist" hires over traditional sales titles since developers dislike being sold to. [z1aKRhRnVNk.en-en-US @ 00:09](https://youtu.be/z1aKRhRnVNk.en-en-US?t=9)
- Dev tool sales should show, not tell (demos over sales decks — Algolia had no sales deck until $10M ARR), and marketing should be led by developers doing documentation, support, and community engagement rather than traditional marketers.
