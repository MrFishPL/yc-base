# Ron Conway at Startup School SV 2014 — Compiled Truth

Legendary angel investor Ron Conway explains what he looks for in founders after 20 years and 750+ investments, emphasizing inborn drive, obsessive product focus, and honesty about failure over any specific idea or business plan.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Some founder traits (work ethic, ambition, aggressiveness, toughness) must be inborn, while others like hiring and management can be learned once a founder wants success badly enough [ron-conway-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 02:11](https://youtu.be/qvHhhIfu7Lo?t=131)
- Great entrepreneurs work 24/7 and treat the company like a vocation — "moonlighting" isn't in their vocabulary, and they warn spouses or partners that the company comes first [ron-conway-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 04:39](https://youtu.be/qvHhhIfu7Lo?t=279)
- SV Angel evaluates roughly 30 companies for every one it invests in, and has invested in over 750 companies across 20 years, yet still can't reliably predict success — so they invest in the traits of the individual [ron-conway-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 19:32](https://youtu.be/qvHhhIfu7Lo?t=1172)
- The biggest companies (YouTube, Facebook, Napster) came from founders solving their own personal need first, and only later realizing other people wanted it too [ron-conway-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 12:47](https://youtu.be/qvHhhIfu7Lo?t=767)
- When watching co-founder teams, a warning sign is one person interrupting and answering for the other — it signals they probably won't get along or are already fighting over who's CEO [ron-conway-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 14:40](https://youtu.be/qvHhhIfu7Lo?t=880)
- Single-founder companies are rare and disproportionately unlikely to get accepted into YC, even though roughly half of YC applications are single founders [ron-conway-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 16:58](https://youtu.be/qvHhhIfu7Lo?t=1018)
- The single biggest predictive trait among great founders (Zuckerberg, Larry Page, Jack Dorsey, Ben Silverman, Steve Jobs) is rifle focus on the product, sometimes to the point of seeming rude or arrogant [ron-conway-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 20:23](https://youtu.be/qvHhhIfu7Lo?t=1223)
- The biggest mistake young founders make is staying in denial when the product isn't getting traction instead of admitting it and changing course, since success is binary — it's working or it isn't [ron-conway-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 29:56](https://youtu.be/qvHhhIfu7Lo?t=1796)
- When a founder finally admits the current approach isn't working, team morale tends to go up rather than down, because the team already knew and is relieved to tackle it together [ron-conway-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 30:49](https://youtu.be/qvHhhIfu7Lo?t=1849)
- SV Angel invented the portfolio approach to angel investing: since Conway believed he could never reliably pick winners individually, he invests in the 20 best companies in a given space and hopes one wins [ron-conway-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 23:01](https://youtu.be/qvHhhIfu7Lo?t=1381)
