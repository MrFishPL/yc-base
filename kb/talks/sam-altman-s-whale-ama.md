# Sam Altman's Whale AMA — Compiled Truth

In a rapid-fire AMA, Sam Altman answers questions on starting startups, YC's rejection patterns, enterprise sales, hiring engineers, and his own reading and work habits.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Learning how to start a startup is one of the biggest superpowers in the world — before meeting Paul Graham it seemed impossible, now it seems really hard but still possible [sam-altman-s-whale-ama @ 00:06](https://youtu.be/MYHb6yfFMmw?t=6).
- There's no reason a high school student can't start a startup; treat school as your day job and spend the rest of your time working on projects you're excited about [sam-altman-s-whale-ama @ 00:42](https://youtu.be/MYHb6yfFMmw?t=42).
- The most impactful, important companies tend to have a genuinely new idea, or if it's an old idea, they execute it in a really new way [sam-altman-s-whale-ama @ 02:46](https://youtu.be/MYHb6yfFMmw?t=166).
- The two best things learned about enterprise sales (or sales of any kind) are that personal relationships are super important and that persistence matters, while keeping the sales cycle as short as possible [sam-altman-s-whale-ama @ 04:11](https://youtu.be/MYHb6yfFMmw?t=251).
- The most times a founder has been rejected by YC before getting in is six, and many of YC's most successful startups — including Dropbox with a prior idea — were rejected at least once [sam-altman-s-whale-ama @ 05:15](https://youtu.be/MYHb6yfFMmw?t=315).
- There are far worse things that will happen to you as a startup founder than getting rejected by YC, so if that's a problem for you, you probably shouldn't be doing a startup anyway [sam-altman-s-whale-ama @ 05:35](https://youtu.be/MYHb6yfFMmw?t=335).
- His favorite Paul Graham essay is "How to Start a Startup," which changed his life and, he believes, the lives of thousands of others [sam-altman-s-whale-ama @ 05:57](https://youtu.be/MYHb6yfFMmw?t=357).
- He believes in the 10,000-hours model for becoming a great engineer: the way to get there is to start building a lot of stuff and work with really smart people, without expecting it to happen overnight [sam-altman-s-whale-ama @ 06:15](https://youtu.be/MYHb6yfFMmw?t=375).
