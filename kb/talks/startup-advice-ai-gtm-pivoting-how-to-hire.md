# Startup Advice: AI GTM, Pivoting & How To Hire — Compiled Truth

An Office Hours panel covering how AI startups should go to market in legacy industries, when to hire, and how to recognize the signs that it's time to pivot.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- When bringing AI to a legacy industry (e.g. accounting), founders can build software sold to incumbents, start their own full-stack firm, or buy an existing firm — most successful YC companies choose the first path [startup-advice-ai-gtm-pivoting-how-to-hire @ 00:49](https://youtu.be/nGLmpKi-jRU?t=49)
- If you build a full-stack AI-powered firm, track percent of work automated over time rather than raw revenue, since revenue alone doesn't prove you can automate the work [startup-advice-ai-gtm-pivoting-how-to-hire @ 02:09](https://youtu.be/nGLmpKi-jRU?t=129)
- Early customers in a legacy industry should be pre-qualified for being unusually bought-in and empowered decision-makers, since normal early adopters aren't enough [startup-advice-ai-gtm-pivoting-how-to-hire @ 06:14](https://youtu.be/nGLmpKi-jRU?t=374)
- Early on, the priority is pace of learning, not deal size — going straight for large enterprise deals without a special "in" slows learning versus starting with a smaller, wrappable segment [startup-advice-ai-gtm-pivoting-how-to-hire @ 07:40](https://youtu.be/nGLmpKi-jRU?t=460)
- AI SDRs and AI sales agents work well only when plugged into a sales process the founder has already proven works manually; using them as a substitute for founders who can't sell tends to fail [startup-advice-ai-gtm-pivoting-how-to-hire @ 11:16](https://youtu.be/nGLmpKi-jRU?t=676)
- Firecrawl's pivot from "Mendable" came after hundreds of thousands of dollars in ARR, once the founders realized the crawler component they'd built for their own use was more valuable than the original product, and they validated it before fully committing [startup-advice-ai-gtm-pivoting-how-to-hire @ 16:29](https://youtu.be/nGLmpKi-jRU?t=989)
- A leading indicator that it's time to pivot is simply that you stop believing what you're working on is going to work [startup-advice-ai-gtm-pivoting-how-to-hire @ 22:45](https://youtu.be/nGLmpKi-jRU?t=1365)
- A technically hard idea can be the best idea precisely because the difficulty deters competitors, and founders can reduce scope early (e.g. building a front end on top of someone else's API) to get moving before tackling the hardest technical pieces [startup-advice-ai-gtm-pivoting-how-to-hire @ 26:18](https://youtu.be/nGLmpKi-jRU?t=1578)
- The right time to hire is when things are breaking — you can't even find a calendar slot for interviews — since hiring takes months and things will break further before a new hire starts [startup-advice-ai-gtm-pivoting-how-to-hire @ 30:51](https://youtu.be/nGLmpKi-jRU?t=1851)
- Open-sourcing an enterprise SaaS product (e.g. Medplum's open-source EHR) can shorten sales cycles by building trust with enterprise buyers, even when the product isn't targeted at developers [startup-advice-ai-gtm-pivoting-how-to-hire @ 35:29](https://youtu.be/nGLmpKi-jRU?t=2129)
