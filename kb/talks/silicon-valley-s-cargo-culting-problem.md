# Silicon Valley's Cargo Culting Problem — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell argue that founders who superficially copy famous companies' tactics without understanding why those tactics worked in context are doomed, and that today's founders increasingly cargo-cult unproven, unsuccessful startups too.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Cargo culting means superficially copying something without understanding why it worked, like building a fake landing strip and expecting planes to land [silicon-valley-s-cargo-culting-problem @ 00:44](https://youtu.be/ECLJ95XNxA4?t=44)
- Founders in the 2000s copied Google's open-office, snack-filled, flat-org culture and even lens-flare logos, treating superficial trappings as the cause of Google's success rather than its hard technical work [silicon-valley-s-cargo-culting-problem @ 02:37](https://youtu.be/ECLJ95XNxA4?t=157)
- Google's real strategy of hiring the smartest engineers made sense only in context: search was believed to be a commodity, Google proved it was a hard technical problem, and talent was newly available after the dot-com crash [silicon-valley-s-cargo-culting-problem @ 06:50](https://youtu.be/ECLJ95XNxA4?t=410)
- Facebook's decision not to charge users and instead build an ads business made sense because its product had massive daily engagement, but that strategy doesn't transfer to products used five minutes a month [silicon-valley-s-cargo-culting-problem @ 08:08](https://youtu.be/ECLJ95XNxA4?t=488)
- Most common beliefs about Uber were wrong: investors funded rapid expansion only in cities where unit economics already worked well, not blind city-by-city burning of cash [silicon-valley-s-cargo-culting-problem @ 09:04](https://youtu.be/ECLJ95XNxA4?t=544)
- Superficially copying Uber's playbook or pretending to be Travis Kalanick doesn't work, just as wearing a black turtleneck doesn't make you Steve Jobs or Apple [silicon-valley-s-cargo-culting-problem @ 09:48](https://youtu.be/ECLJ95XNxA4?t=588)
- A newer, scarier trend is founders cargo-culting companies that haven't even succeeded yet, basing strategy on a fundraising announcement or valuation without knowing the company's actual revenue [silicon-valley-s-cargo-culting-problem @ 10:25](https://youtu.be/ECLJ95XNxA4?t=625)
- Modern cargo culting also shows up as founders chasing superficial startup signifiers — raising money, hiring advisors, getting a patent, having a slick pitch deck — instead of focusing on users [silicon-valley-s-cargo-culting-problem @ 12:37](https://youtu.be/ECLJ95XNxA4?t=757)
- The healthy alternative is starting from what users want and who they already pay for a similar service, then working backward to figure out which practices are actually essential [silicon-valley-s-cargo-culting-problem @ 13:43](https://youtu.be/ECLJ95XNxA4?t=823)
- The right way to use other companies' ideas is like being influenced by music you've heard and synthesizing it into original work, rather than plagiarizing a paper by copying and pasting without original thinking [silicon-valley-s-cargo-culting-problem @ 14:43](https://youtu.be/ECLJ95XNxA4?t=883)
