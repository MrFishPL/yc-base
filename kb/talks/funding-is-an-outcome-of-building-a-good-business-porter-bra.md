# Funding Is an Outcome of Building a Good Business - Porter Braswell of Jopwell — Compiled Truth

Jopwell co-founder and CEO Porter Braswell recounts building a career-advancement platform for underrepresented job seekers with no technical co-founder or product at first, and the lesson from YC that fundraising is a byproduct of building a real business.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Jopwell started as just a pitch deck shown to companies, who signed one-page letters of intent to become customers before any platform existed [funding-is-an-outcome-of-building-a-good-business-porter-bra @ 09:27](https://youtu.be/EZgTD3pggpc?t=567)
- Braswell raised $500,000 from friends-and-family within about a month and a half once he had those soft customer commitments in hand [funding-is-an-outcome-of-building-a-good-business-porter-bra @ 10:06](https://youtu.be/EZgTD3pggpc?t=606)
- Jopwell's first four hires were friends from Braswell's network, which meant the founding team wasn't diverse itself and required intentional effort to grow beyond that [funding-is-an-outcome-of-building-a-good-business-porter-bra @ 12:22](https://youtu.be/EZgTD3pggpc?t=742)
- Jopwell was rejected by NY Techstars and by YC once before finally getting into YC six months and four million dollars in traction later [funding-is-an-outcome-of-building-a-good-business-porter-bra @ 14:17](https://youtu.be/EZgTD3pggpc?t=857)
- The number one lesson from YC was that "funding is an outcome of building a good business," which eliminates distraction and lets founders focus on customers and traction instead of chasing VCs [funding-is-an-outcome-of-building-a-good-business-porter-bra @ 41:48](https://youtu.be/EZgTD3pggpc?t=2508)
- Braswell advises founders to first try to build a small business making even $100,000-$500,000 before worrying about the big vision or big-name VC funding [funding-is-an-outcome-of-building-a-good-business-porter-bra @ 44:10](https://youtu.be/EZgTD3pggpc?t=2650)
- Once Jopwell hit around 15-20 employees, Braswell had to shift from being "friends first" to being CEO first, learning to give critical feedback to people he'd hired as friends [funding-is-an-outcome-of-building-a-good-business-porter-bra @ 46:57](https://youtu.be/EZgTD3pggpc?t=2817)
- As CEO of a 35-person company, Braswell now protects his calendar with dedicated agenda-only meeting blocks and daily one-on-one coffee chats with employees for direct feedback [funding-is-an-outcome-of-building-a-good-business-porter-bra @ 47:42](https://youtu.be/EZgTD3pggpc?t=2862)
