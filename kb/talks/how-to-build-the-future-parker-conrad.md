# How To Build The Future: Parker Conrad — Compiled Truth

Parker Conrad recounts the seven-year slog before Zenefits, the rise and forced exit from Zenefits, and how he rebuilt the same insight into Rippling as a "compound startup," while sharing his views on fundraising, product-market fit, and AI's effect on business software.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- After years of pitching ~70-75 investors and getting rejected on a consumer idea, Conrad concluded founders should stop optimizing for fundraising and instead make the business so good that investors chase them with blank term sheets [how-to-build-the-future-parker-conrad @ 09:39](https://youtu.be/FwD0wqwUjAI?t=579)
- He says the "never give up, keep pivoting" advice is often wrong — if something isn't working, founders should give up on it quickly rather than assume they'll eventually pivot into success like Airbnb did [how-to-build-the-future-parker-conrad @ 17:53](https://youtu.be/FwD0wqwUjAI?t=1073)
- Real product-market fit felt like a "sucking sound" of the market pulling the product into existence, where even untested side-bets worked unbelievably well [how-to-build-the-future-parker-conrad @ 18:14](https://youtu.be/FwD0wqwUjAI?t=1094)
- Zenefits' insight was that HR-adjacent systems (insurance, payroll, benefits) needed to talk to each other, and unifying them eliminated huge amounts of manual admin work — the same insight became Rippling's "compound startup" thesis [how-to-build-the-future-parker-conrad @ 14:34](https://youtu.be/FwD0wqwUjAI?t=874)
- Rebuilding Rippling, Conrad deliberately spent about two years building only with engineers/product (no ops or customer support hires) to avoid repeating Zenefits' flaw of scaling on manual operations instead of real software [how-to-build-the-future-parker-conrad @ 27:56](https://youtu.be/FwD0wqwUjAI?t=1676)
- He argues point-solution SaaS companies can't invest as deeply in cross-cutting R&D (analytics, permissions, workflow automation) as compound software businesses can, making compound products a harder-to-displace form of product-market fit [how-to-build-the-future-parker-conrad @ 32:07](https://youtu.be/FwD0wqwUjAI?t=1927)
- Public companies are spending 50% more on sales and marketing but adding only 10% more new ARR, which Conrad says shows the traditional point-solution SaaS sales model is increasingly broken [how-to-build-the-future-parker-conrad @ 34:17](https://youtu.be/FwD0wqwUjAI?t=2057)
- On AI, Conrad predicts it will let large companies run leaner (2,000-person companies operating like 200-person ones) because AI can read and ingest far more organizational context than any single executive can [how-to-build-the-future-parker-conrad @ 35:38](https://youtu.be/FwD0wqwUjAI?t=2138)
- Rippling's AI performance-management feature analyzes a new employee's first 90 days (PRs, sales calls, support tickets) to flag whether they're on a path to success or need intervention [how-to-build-the-future-parker-conrad @ 36:47](https://youtu.be/FwD0wqwUjAI?t=2207)
- He predicts AI-driven verticalization: software will become far more precisely configured to specific industries and companies, creating a "Cambrian explosion" of tailored software [how-to-build-the-future-parker-conrad @ 38:43](https://youtu.be/FwD0wqwUjAI?t=2323)
- On "founder mode," Conrad agrees founders must go all the way to the ground floor when something is broken, but warns it can become an excuse for bad behavior if used when things are actually working fine [how-to-build-the-future-parker-conrad @ 41:45](https://youtu.be/FwD0wqwUjAI?t=2505)
