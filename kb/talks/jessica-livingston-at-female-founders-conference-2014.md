# Jessica Livingston at Female Founders Conference 2014 — Compiled Truth

Jessica Livingston tells YC's origin story and distills her general advice for founders, then gives candid, women-specific advice on kids, being quiet/introverted, and finding a technical co-founder.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- YC discovered its most important insight almost by accident: funding startups in batches together is powerful because peers help each other through the same experience at the same time [jessica-livingston-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 06:24](https://youtu.be/sNrDEIYAMjE?t=384)
- The most important quality a founder can have is determination, since so many smart, talented founders fail simply by not sticking with it when things get tough [jessica-livingston-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 10:21](https://youtu.be/sNrDEIYAMjE?t=621)
- Founders need to be able to withstand rejection, because people, investors, and reporters will all tell you your idea is crazy or lame [jessica-livingston-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 10:46](https://youtu.be/sNrDEIYAMjE?t=646)
- Empathy for and real conversation with your users matters most, which is easiest if you yourself are a target user of your own product [jessica-livingston-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 11:11](https://youtu.be/sNrDEIYAMjE?t=671)
- You need to make something people want enough to sign up, keep coming back, and tell their friends — wanting it "a little bit" isn't enough [jessica-livingston-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 11:29](https://youtu.be/sNrDEIYAMjE?t=689)
- Living cheaply early on buys the runway needed for the "necessary evolution" of figuring out what a startup should actually be doing before the money runs out [jessica-livingston-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 11:51](https://youtu.be/sNrDEIYAMjE?t=711)
- Based on her own experience, Jessica says it's easier to start a startup before having kids, since kids and startups pull founders in competing directions [jessica-livingston-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 13:54](https://youtu.be/sNrDEIYAMjE?t=834)
- Quietly determined founders don't need to become loud public figures to succeed, though they should expect to sometimes be overlooked by people who only hear loud voices [jessica-livingston-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 16:22](https://youtu.be/sNrDEIYAMjE?t=982)
