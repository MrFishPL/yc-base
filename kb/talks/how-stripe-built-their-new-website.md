# How Stripe Built Their New Website — Compiled Truth

Stripe's head of design Katie Dill walks through why and how Stripe rebuilt its homepage after six years, explaining how the site had to reflect an expanded product suite, how AI sped up exploration without replacing craft, and how design principles like "walking the store" keep the company's expanding product surface coherent.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The old site was replaced not because it looked dated but because Stripe's product suite had outgrown the story it told — payments had become just one part of a much larger multi-product business [how-stripe-built-their-new-website @ 01:10](https://youtu.be/ypzNhwpmOD4?t=70)
- Dill treats a company website as an implicit "manifesto" — the colors, typography, and care taken communicate who you are even when you don't say so explicitly [how-stripe-built-their-new-website @ 05:20](https://youtu.be/ypzNhwpmOD4?t=320)
- They built a live GDP-percentage counter as a unique piece of social proof that only Stripe, given its scale, could credibly display [how-stripe-built-their-new-website @ 07:01](https://youtu.be/ypzNhwpmOD4?t=421)
- The "Bento" product-grid section uses minimal text and imagery with expandable modals for progressive disclosure, avoiding overwhelming users while still surfacing dozens of products [how-stripe-built-their-new-website @ 08:45](https://youtu.be/ypzNhwpmOD4?t=525)
- User research showed an accordion-style Bento variant failed because most people simply don't click tabs, which pushed the team toward a more visual, "lean-back" browsing layout instead [how-stripe-built-their-new-website @ 25:48](https://youtu.be/ypzNhwpmOD4?t=1548)
- AI let the design team explore roughly 20 variations of imagery and layout in the time it used to take to look at two, but it doesn't replace craft, taste, or attention to nuance-level detail [how-stripe-built-their-new-website @ 28:48](https://youtu.be/ypzNhwpmOD4?t=1728)
- Dill's core cultural warning is that "the gravitational pull is to mediocrity" — teams must actively fight the urge to accept good-enough work rather than push for something exceptional [how-stripe-built-their-new-website @ 35:27](https://youtu.be/ypzNhwpmOD4?t=2127)
- She favors "progress over perfection," describing the goal as an "MVQP" (minimum viable quality product) rather than either a bare MVP or an unattainable perfect launch [how-stripe-built-their-new-website @ 38:59](https://youtu.be/ypzNhwpmOD4?t=2339)
- Everyone at Stripe, including the founders every Friday in front of the whole company, practices "walking the store" — using the product as different types of users to find dead ends and see how disparate product areas connect [how-stripe-built-their-new-website @ 39:53](https://youtu.be/ypzNhwpmOD4?t=2393)
- With AI tools, design systems are starting to be actively managed by AI — designers sketch an idea, AI assembles it from existing components, and can even help extend the design system itself when no matching component exists [how-stripe-built-their-new-website @ 33:06](https://youtu.be/ypzNhwpmOD4?t=1986)
