# Ideas, Products, Teams, and Execution with Dustin Moskovitz (How to Start a Startup 2014: Lecture 1) — Compiled Truth

Sam Altman opens the "How to Start a Startup" class arguing that a great idea and a great product must come before anything else, then Dustin Moskovitz explains why founders should only start a startup when they can't not do it.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Success is roughly idea times product times execution times team times luck, and luck is a random number between 0 and 10,000 — but doing well on the four controllable factors gives a real shot [ideas-products-teams-and-execution-with-dustin-moskovitz-how @ 02:25](https://youtu.be/egJeFaIXZLo?t=145)
- The best startup ideas often sound terrible at first (the 13th search engine, a 10th social network for college kids, sleeping on strangers' couches) because if they sounded good, too many people would already be working on them [ideas-products-teams-and-execution-with-dustin-moskovitz-how @ 08:10](https://youtu.be/egJeFaIXZLo?t=490)
- Care more about a market's future growth rate than its current size, and be suspicious of an idea if you can't answer "why now" — why couldn't it have been done two years ago and why will two years from now be too late [ideas-products-teams-and-execution-with-dustin-moskovitz-how @ 12:09](https://youtu.be/egJeFaIXZLo?t=729)
- It is better to build something a small number of users love than something a large number of users merely like, because it's far easier to expand from love than from lukewarm liking [ideas-products-teams-and-execution-with-dustin-moskovitz-how @ 16:49](https://youtu.be/egJeFaIXZLo?t=1009)
- Recruit your first users manually and do things that don't scale, the way Ben Silverman personally recruited early Pinterest users by chatting up strangers in Palo Alto coffee shops [ideas-products-teams-and-execution-with-dustin-moskovitz-how @ 22:17](https://youtu.be/egJeFaIXZLo?t=1337)
- Founders should measure growth in active users, activity levels, cohort retention, and revenue rather than vanity metrics like total registrations, since the company builds toward whatever the CEO decides to measure [ideas-products-teams-and-execution-with-dustin-moskovitz-how @ 24:30](https://youtu.be/egJeFaIXZLo?t=1470)
- Starting a startup is genuinely stressful — founders carry fear of failure on behalf of everyone who joined them, are always on call, and face real founder depression, so managing your own psychology is a core CEO responsibility [ideas-products-teams-and-execution-with-dustin-moskovitz-how @ 32:14](https://youtu.be/egJeFaIXZLo?t=1934)
- Benchmark the financial upside of joining a fast-growing late-stage company (employee #100 at Dropbox or Facebook could net $10M–$200M) against what you'd actually make as a founder before assuming a startup is the better path [ideas-products-teams-and-execution-with-dustin-moskovitz-how @ 36:29](https://youtu.be/egJeFaIXZLo?t=2189)
- The best reason to start a company is that you can't not do it — you're so passionate you'll work on it anyway, and/or the world genuinely needs you specifically to solve this problem [ideas-products-teams-and-execution-with-dustin-moskovitz-how @ 40:43](https://youtu.be/egJeFaIXZLo?t=2443)
