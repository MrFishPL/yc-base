# Starting A Company? The Key Terms You Should Know | Startup School — Compiled Truth

Dalton Caldwell walks through core startup vocabulary founders need to know, from MVP and product-market fit to fundraising instruments (safes, convertible notes, seed/series rounds) and key financial metrics like burn rate, ARR, TAM, and valuation.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- An MVP must be "viable," meaning genuinely useful enough to serve a real purpose for the customer, not just a stripped-down product that does nothing [starting-a-company-the-key-terms-you-should-know-startup-sch @ 00:09](https://youtu.be/wH3TKpALlw4?t=9)
- Venture capital invests small amounts of money into risky startups expecting most to fail, but the rare huge winners return the whole fund, similar to the whaling-voyage origin of the VC model [starting-a-company-the-key-terms-you-should-know-startup-sch @ 01:04](https://youtu.be/wH3TKpALlw4?t=64)
- Product-market fit means customers are using and liking what you built enough that your biggest problem shifts from "figure out what people want" to scaling and growth; almost all startups start without it [starting-a-company-the-key-terms-you-should-know-startup-sch @ 07:25](https://youtu.be/wH3TKpALlw4?t=445)
- Burn rate is how much cash a company loses per month (e.g., $1M down to $900K is a $100K burn rate), and founders must track it closely to avoid running out of money even while making revenue [starting-a-company-the-key-terms-you-should-know-startup-sch @ 04:36](https://youtu.be/wH3TKpALlw4?t=276)
- A safe (simple agreement for future equity), created by YC's Carolyn Levy, is an alternative to a convertible note with fewer terms and fewer investor rights, commonly used to close a seed round quickly ahead of a priced series A [starting-a-company-the-key-terms-you-should-know-startup-sch @ 10:46](https://youtu.be/wH3TKpALlw4?t=646)
- Bootstrapping (funding a company from personal funds or revenue instead of venture capital) is a strong option for businesses expected to reach roughly $5-10M/year in revenue without needing venture-scale growth [starting-a-company-the-key-terms-you-should-know-startup-sch @ 09:51](https://youtu.be/wH3TKpALlw4?t=591)
- TAM (total addressable market) is a thought experiment, not a fixed number — companies like Tesla and Uber dramatically expanded what looked like a small initial TAM by creating a better product experience [starting-a-company-the-key-terms-you-should-know-startup-sch @ 12:29](https://youtu.be/wH3TKpALlw4?t=749)
- Valuation reflects what the last investor paid for equity, not a liquid market price like public stocks, so a high valuation does not guarantee a startup will succeed [starting-a-company-the-key-terms-you-should-know-startup-sch @ 14:11](https://youtu.be/wH3TKpALlw4?t=851)
- ARR (annual recurring revenue) requires revenue that actually recurs via subscriptions or renewing contracts on an annual cadence; monthly-billed recurring revenue should instead be reported as MRR [starting-a-company-the-key-terms-you-should-know-startup-sch @ 15:59](https://youtu.be/wH3TKpALlw4?t=959)
