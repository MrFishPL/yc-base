# Eric Migicovsky at Startup School SV 2014 — Compiled Truth

Pebble founder Eric Migicovsky tells the origin story of the smartwatch, from a 2008 university side project (Impulse) through hand-built prototypes, leaked-photo press hacking, a failed VC raise, and a record-breaking $10M Kickstarter campaign.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Migicovsky built the first prototype himself by tearing apart a Nokia 3310 and combining it with an Arduino, proving the concept was something he'd personally use daily before it looked good [eric-migicovsky-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 01:43](https://youtu.be/0-JL7bbQ0tw?t=103).
- The team funded early prototypes by winning cash from elevator-pitch competitions and borrowing money from parents, rather than raising formal capital [eric-migicovsky-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 02:44](https://youtu.be/0-JL7bbQ0tw?t=164).
- To generate buzz before a public launch, Migicovsky deliberately "leaked" a render of the watch to a Blackberry blog (Crackberry), which every other outlet then picked up, and a week later they revealed it was actually their own startup — and every blog ran it again because "everybody loves page views" [eric-migicovsky-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 07:40](https://youtu.be/0-JL7bbQ0tw?t=460).
- Instead of booking media briefings, they got press by simply walking into the Engadget CES studio and showing off the product [eric-migicovsky-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 09:42](https://youtu.be/0-JL7bbQ0tw?t=582).
- They shipped small batches (10, 20, 50, 100 units) rather than building 5,000 at once, which was fortunate since the first 20 units broke during shipping [eric-migicovsky-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 11:07](https://youtu.be/0-JL7bbQ0tw?t=667).
- 80% of Impulse buyers used the device every single day, and founders maintained personal email threads with nearly every customer — a close product-feedback loop that drove rapid iteration [eric-migicovsky-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 11:27](https://youtu.be/0-JL7bbQ0tw?t=687).
- Launching a lightweight SDK let a community build over 100 apps for only 1,000 watches within two to three months, showing organic demand beyond what the founders could build themselves [eric-migicovsky-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 13:08](https://youtu.be/0-JL7bbQ0tw?t=788).
- After 20-30 VC and angel meetings yielded zero investment, Paul Graham suggested trying Kickstarter — a platform Migicovsky hadn't previously considered — which became Pebble's path forward [eric-migicovsky-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 15:16](https://youtu.be/0-JL7bbQ0tw?t=916).
- Pebble's Kickstarter blew past its $100K goal in 2 hours, hit $1M in 28 hours, and raised $10M from 70,000 backers over 30 days [eric-migicovsky-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 16:37](https://youtu.be/0-JL7bbQ0tw?t=997).
- Drawing on Paul Buchheit's advice that a product must do a small number of things really well and be able to explain them clearly, Pebble's pitch centered on exactly three things: notifications, sports/fitness tracking, and watch-face customization [eric-migicovsky-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 17:14](https://youtu.be/0-JL7bbQ0tw?t=1034).
