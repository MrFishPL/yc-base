# Cannabis Startup Founders David Hua and Vincent Ning on Legalization, Banking, and Industry Trends — Compiled Truth

Meadow (dispensary software) founder David Hua and Nabis (cannabis distribution) founder Vincent Ning discuss the fragmented, heavily regulated California cannabis supply chain, fundraising as an ancillary business, and how standard YC startup lessons still applied to their industry.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Nabis' fundraising was unusually difficult because it holds a state cannabis distribution license, and many institutional LPs contractually bar their funds from investing in vice industries like cannabis [cannabis-startup-founders-david-hua-and-vincent-ning-on-lega @ 18:14](https://youtu.be/yhnP0LUXHBI?t=1094)
- Meadow was the first cannabis company accepted into YC, going through an intense interview covering the legal risk of running an "ancillary business" in the space [cannabis-startup-founders-david-hua-and-vincent-ning-on-lega @ 18:56](https://youtu.be/yhnP0LUXHBI?t=1136)
- At their YC demo day pitch, Meadow deliberately tried to bifurcate the audience with a "stand on the right side of history" framing to filter for investors receptive to cannabis [cannabis-startup-founders-david-hua-and-vincent-ning-on-lega @ 21:17](https://youtu.be/yhnP0LUXHBI?t=1277)
- California's cannabis market is fragmented because 33% of the state has local cannabis laws while 67% has none, creating a disconnect where state permits require local permits that often don't exist yet, which has so far prevented any single dominant brand from emerging [cannabis-startup-founders-david-hua-and-vincent-ning-on-lega @ 27:28](https://youtu.be/yhnP0LUXHBI?t=1648)
- Because cannabis isn't federally legal, over 90% of transactions in the industry are cash-based, forcing distributors to rely on safes and a patchwork of small credit unions for banking [cannabis-startup-founders-david-hua-and-vincent-ning-on-lega @ 31:08](https://youtu.be/yhnP0LUXHBI?t=1868)
- California law requires a licensed distributor to move product between any two licensed entities in the supply chain, making distributors a mandatory compliance and tax-collection checkpoint before product reaches retail [cannabis-startup-founders-david-hua-and-vincent-ning-on-lega @ 34:04](https://youtu.be/yhnP0LUXHBI?t=2044)
- Cannabis-focused publicly traded Canadian companies are consolidating the fragmented US market by buying up licensed businesses for their licenses, creating a "brutal M&A climate" the founders compare to a dot-com-style bubble [cannabis-startup-founders-david-hua-and-vincent-ning-on-lega @ 44:13](https://youtu.be/yhnP0LUXHBI?t=2653)
- Vincent describes literally doing things that don't scale — personally driving hundreds of thousands of dollars of pre-roll product across California by car before regulation formalized distribution, which taught him the industry and its people [cannabis-startup-founders-david-hua-and-vincent-ning-on-lega @ 1:00:49](https://youtu.be/yhnP0LUXHBI?t=3649)
- David says standard YC mantras like "make something people want," "do things that don't scale," and resilience/"cockroach" survival mentality all applied directly to building a cannabis startup, along with not worrying about competition and instead focusing on customers and community [cannabis-startup-founders-david-hua-and-vincent-ning-on-lega @ 1:01:52](https://youtu.be/yhnP0LUXHBI?t=3712)
