# Urska Srsen Speaks at Y Combinator Female Founder Conference 2016 — Compiled Truth

Bellabeat co-founder Urska Srsen recounts moving from studying sculpture to building consumer health-hardware for women, and the lessons about commitment, team turnover, and vision that came from scaling a Croatia-born hardware startup through YC.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Srsen co-founded Bellabeat, devoted to designing beautiful, easy-to-use technology for tracking women's health, after studying sculpture rather than business or engineering [urska-srsen-speaks-at-y-combinator-female-founder-conference @ 01:46](https://youtu.be/rHOm01lWd1o?t=106)
- Bellabeat's first product, a device letting expecting mothers listen to their baby's heartbeat, taught the founders how overlooked women are as users of technology [urska-srsen-speaks-at-y-combinator-female-founder-conference @ 07:25](https://youtu.be/rHOm01lWd1o?t=445)
- Starting in Croatia rather than a Silicon Valley hub meant they had a beloved product with users but no prospect of raising money [urska-srsen-speaks-at-y-combinator-female-founder-conference @ 09:25](https://youtu.be/rHOm01lWd1o?t=565)
- She argues Europe lacks not just capital but the accumulated knowledge of investors who've seen founders through scaling, and lacks accomplished talent because it migrates to Silicon Valley [urska-srsen-speaks-at-y-combinator-female-founder-conference @ 10:52](https://youtu.be/rHOm01lWd1o?t=652)
- Feeling like outsiders at YC — unsure of basic norms like email etiquette — bred an "underdog mentality" of watching and listening rather than assuming expertise [urska-srsen-speaks-at-y-combinator-female-founder-conference @ 13:53](https://youtu.be/rHOm01lWd1o?t=833)
- She defines commitment as putting your team's and company's interests ahead of your own comfort, including moving to wherever the work requires [urska-srsen-speaks-at-y-combinator-female-founder-conference @ 14:46](https://youtu.be/rHOm01lWd1o?t=886)
- Scaling fast is like a rocket launch: some early team members will fall away as debris, and that attrition is normal even when people leave who've been there since the start [urska-srsen-speaks-at-y-combinator-female-founder-conference @ 16:23](https://youtu.be/rHOm01lWd1o?t=983)
- For hardware, building the product is hard but shipping it is harder, and the only place to actually ship from is the factory floor, requiring the team to relocate there [urska-srsen-speaks-at-y-combinator-female-founder-conference @ 17:49](https://youtu.be/rHOm01lWd1o?t=1069)
- Vision acts as the glue that fills the cracks in commitment, team cohesion, and product decisions when things get hard [urska-srsen-speaks-at-y-combinator-female-founder-conference @ 19:05](https://youtu.be/rHOm01lWd1o?t=1145)
