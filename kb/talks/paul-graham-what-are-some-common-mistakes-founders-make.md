# Paul Graham: What are some common mistakes founders make? — Compiled Truth

Paul Graham warns that founders' biggest mistake is retreating into their own "vision" instead of talking to users, and shipping too slowly out of fear of embarrassing feedback.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Founders will not pay enough attention to users, instead building an elaborate "vision" alone rather than engaging with the real world. [paul-graham-what-are-some-common-mistakes-founders-make @ 00:05](https://youtu.be/845O4RE1XDM?t=5)
- Talking to users is avoided because it feels like sales, which is uncomfortable and risks rejection. [paul-graham-what-are-some-common-mistakes-founders-make @ 00:28](https://youtu.be/845O4RE1XDM?t=28)
- Founders are better off finding anyone with a problem who will pay to have it fixed, then finding more people like that; best case is if the founder has the problem themselves. [paul-graham-what-are-some-common-mistakes-founders-make @ 00:28](https://youtu.be/845O4RE1XDM?t=28)
- Founders will not ship fast enough because they are embarrassed to release something unfinished. [paul-graham-what-are-some-common-mistakes-founders-make @ 00:47](https://youtu.be/845O4RE1XDM?t=47)
- It is humbling and humiliating to hear how bad what you built actually is, but that feedback is the only way to improve it. [paul-graham-what-are-some-common-mistakes-founders-make @ 00:47](https://youtu.be/845O4RE1XDM?t=47)
- The core mistake is that founders shrink from contact with the real world and with their users. [paul-graham-what-are-some-common-mistakes-founders-make @ 01:12](https://youtu.be/845O4RE1XDM?t=72)
