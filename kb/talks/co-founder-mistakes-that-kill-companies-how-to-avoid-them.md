# Co-Founder Mistakes That Kill Companies & How To Avoid Them — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell walk through anonymous YC founder stories to show that unmanaged co-founder conflict, not skill mismatch, is what actually kills startups.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Founders should write down agreements on equity split and vesting early, since people misremember disagreements after the fact [co-founder-mistakes-that-kill-companies-how-to-avoid-them @ 00:21](https://youtu.be/dlfjs_eEEzs?t=21)
- Choosing a co-founder you like and can learn with matters more than an exact skill match, since skills can be learned on the job [co-founder-mistakes-that-kill-companies-how-to-avoid-them @ 00:41](https://youtu.be/dlfjs_eEEzs?t=41)
- A co-founder lacking the "right" skills is an error but not fatal, while a co-founder relationship that turns toxic actually kills companies [co-founder-mistakes-that-kill-companies-how-to-avoid-them @ 01:39](https://youtu.be/dlfjs_eEEzs?t=99)
- Relationships that have never been "pressure tested" with disagreements are more likely to shatter completely at the first real conflict [co-founder-mistakes-that-kill-companies-how-to-avoid-them @ 01:55](https://youtu.be/dlfjs_eEEzs?t=115)
- Founders don't need to force resolution in every fight — it's fine to pause an unproductive argument and understand how your co-founder handles stress (attacking vs. retreating) [co-founder-mistakes-that-kill-companies-how-to-avoid-them @ 02:34](https://youtu.be/dlfjs_eEEzs?t=154)
- When co-founders stop having real conversations for weeks or months, breakup often becomes inevitable and the CEO's job shifts to separating cleanly rather than repairing the relationship [co-founder-mistakes-that-kill-companies-how-to-avoid-them @ 03:37](https://youtu.be/dlfjs_eEEzs?t=217)
- The longer a broken co-founder relationship persists before splitting, the worse the breakup gets, including higher odds of litigation and cap-table complications from vested stock [co-founder-mistakes-that-kill-companies-how-to-avoid-them @ 04:44](https://youtu.be/dlfjs_eEEzs?t=284)
- Equity should be as close to equal as possible to avoid ownership-driven drama, but a straight 50/50 split risks deadlock, so giving the CEO one extra tiebreaker share is a useful pro tip [co-founder-mistakes-that-kill-companies-how-to-avoid-them @ 05:08](https://youtu.be/dlfjs_eEEzs?t=308)
- Finding a co-founder before settling on the idea (rather than adding one later) creates shared ownership of the idea and better long-term retention through hard times [co-founder-mistakes-that-kill-companies-how-to-avoid-them @ 05:50](https://youtu.be/dlfjs_eEEzs?t=350)
- Having a great co-founder can be a superpower especially in the pre-product-market-fit stage, so founders should invest seriously in the relationship rather than treat it casually [co-founder-mistakes-that-kill-companies-how-to-avoid-them @ 06:44](https://youtu.be/dlfjs_eEEzs?t=404)
