# Zach Sims at Startup School NY 2014 — Compiled Truth

Codecademy co-founder Zach Sims tells the story of pivoting through a string of dismissed ideas inside YC — from a Facebook-login job board to a programmer-hiring platform to random idea generation — before landing on Codecademy weeks before Demo Day.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Sims's first YC application idea, a Facebook-login job-matching product, was told by friends and mentors it was "the dumbest thing I've ever heard," yet that rejection was itself encouraging enough to apply to YC anyway [zach-sims-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 13:13](https://youtu.be/ypLoGFaKdbU?t=793)
- On the flight to their YC interview the founders realized their pitched idea was a horrible idea and pivoted overnight to a programmer-hiring niche instead [zach-sims-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 14:13](https://youtu.be/ypLoGFaKdbU?t=853)
- After the programmer-hiring product also failed to gain interest from programmers, the team spiraled into generating a series of unfocused random startup ideas (like a CRM for club promoters) while burning through their YC check [zach-sims-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 18:23](https://youtu.be/ypLoGFaKdbU?t=1103)
- Only about three to four weeks before Demo Day did the team return to teaching programming, building Codecademy first for Sims himself as "patient zero" since he was learning to code as they built it [zach-sims-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 20:03](https://youtu.be/ypLoGFaKdbU?t=1203)
- Early feedback from investors and YC partners on the first Codecademy version was that "you should be embarrassed when you launch for the first time, but not this embarrassed" [zach-sims-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 21:14](https://youtu.be/ypLoGFaKdbU?t=1274)
- It's much easier to build a product when you're building it for yourself, a lesson reinforced both at GroupMe (built to solve the founders' own festival-communication problem) and at Codecademy [zach-sims-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 21:54](https://youtu.be/ypLoGFaKdbU?t=1314)
- On launch day, expecting fewer than 50 concurrent users, the team instead saw 1,000 people on the site at once and broke their analytics dashboard, going from the least-hyped YC company to the fastest-growing that batch [zach-sims-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 22:32](https://youtu.be/ypLoGFaKdbU?t=1352)
- After raising their Series A, the team found that hiring was extremely difficult, interviewing 100 people before making their first hire [zach-sims-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 25:20](https://youtu.be/ypLoGFaKdbU?t=1520)
- Sims's core takeaway is that startups are a roller coaster where persistence pays off, and being genuinely passionate about the problem makes all the difference through repeated failed pivots [zach-sims-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 25:40](https://youtu.be/ypLoGFaKdbU?t=1540)
