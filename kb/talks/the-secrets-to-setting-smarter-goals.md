# The Secrets To Setting Smarter Goals — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell dissect the common ways founders set "stupid" goals — too aggressive, fake metrics, superficial comparisons, sandbagging, and cheating — and the equally stupid prizes (loss of control, layoffs, burned cash, legal trouble) that come from chasing them.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Founders often set goals that are too aggressive to be motivating, like targeting $30k MRR by demo day just because an alum said that's what's needed to raise [the-secrets-to-setting-smarter-goals @ 02:06](https://youtu.be/rE2XaBGHN2I?t=126)
- Fake metrics — counting registered users instead of paying/active users, counting website hits, or counting downloads without registrations — let founders avoid measuring whether something is actually working [the-secrets-to-setting-smarter-goals @ 03:25](https://youtu.be/rE2XaBGHN2I?t=205)
- Chasing "stupid comparatives" (e.g., assuming you'll raise like another company did after hitting the same revenue number) ignores that investment decisions depend on deeper factors like engagement, not surface resemblance [the-secrets-to-setting-smarter-goals @ 05:43](https://youtu.be/rE2XaBGHN2I?t=343)
- Sandbagging — setting deliberately easy goals to look like you blew them out of the water — works in a corporate job but is self-defeating when you are your own boss [the-secrets-to-setting-smarter-goals @ 08:11](https://youtu.be/rE2XaBGHN2I?t=491)
- If you cheat to hit a goal (e.g., getting friends and family to pad numbers), you haven't actually accomplished the underlying mastery the goal was meant to represent [the-secrets-to-setting-smarter-goals @ 10:16](https://youtu.be/rE2XaBGHN2I?t=616)
- YC isn't grading founders on whether they hit goals — the real job is making progress on the hardest problems in the company, and founders should expect to get better at goal-setting over time rather than making excuses when they miss [the-secrets-to-setting-smarter-goals @ 12:04](https://youtu.be/rE2XaBGHN2I?t=724)
- Playing the "raise as much money as possible" game can mean losing control of your company, over-hiring people who expect a big outcome, and being pushed off a cliff when a pivot becomes necessary [the-secrets-to-setting-smarter-goals @ 15:02](https://youtu.be/rE2XaBGHN2I?t=902)
- Hiring an executive team too early (pre-PMF) is late-stage advice applied too soon, and often backfires because recruits expect a more successful company than actually exists [the-secrets-to-setting-smarter-goals @ 17:51](https://youtu.be/rE2XaBGHN2I?t=1071)
- Scaling negative margins to hit growth numbers for the next fundraise eventually runs out of luck once investors do basic arithmetic on whether the business is real [the-secrets-to-setting-smarter-goals @ 19:27](https://youtu.be/rE2XaBGHN2I?t=1167)
- Trying to become a status-seeking angel investor before your own company works is cargo-culting behavior seen in successful founders, and it ties up money and time you need for your startup [the-secrets-to-setting-smarter-goals @ 24:19](https://youtu.be/rE2XaBGHN2I?t=1459)
