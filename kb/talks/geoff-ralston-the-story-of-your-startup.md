# Geoff Ralston: The Story of Your Startup — Compiled Truth

Geoff Ralston argues that crafting a compelling story about your startup is fundamental to survival, because that story is what keeps a founder going through the extreme difficulty of building a company.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Startups are almost certainly the hardest, most difficult thing a founder will do professionally, and without the right story to tell yourself you will quit and the startup will die [geoff-ralston-the-story-of-your-startup @ 00:34](https://youtu.be/yjOyMqYEX_8?t=34)
- YC partner Aaron Harris thinks and talks a lot about the importance of stories, an influence Ralston credits for these ideas [geoff-ralston-the-story-of-your-startup @ 01:04](https://youtu.be/yjOyMqYEX_8?t=64)
- E.M. Forster's example ("the king died and then the queen died" vs. "the king died and then the queen died of grief") illustrates that a story needs human, causal connection to grab people [geoff-ralston-the-story-of-your-startup @ 01:26](https://youtu.be/yjOyMqYEX_8?t=86)
- Founders constantly tell stories: to parents and friends, to co-founders, to potential customers, to partners, and to investors [geoff-ralston-the-story-of-your-startup @ 02:17](https://youtu.be/yjOyMqYEX_8?t=137)
- Most of all, founders need to tell a story that resonates with themselves, since it is that story which nourishes them and keeps them going through hard times and setbacks [geoff-ralston-the-story-of-your-startup @ 02:17](https://youtu.be/yjOyMqYEX_8?t=137)
- The story is also what persuades a founder to actually take on the crazy task of starting a company, and what persuades others to join the founder on such an uncertain path [geoff-ralston-the-story-of-your-startup @ 02:53](https://youtu.be/yjOyMqYEX_8?t=173)
- YC spends significant time helping founders craft, and sometimes reimagine, their startup's story [geoff-ralston-the-story-of-your-startup @ 02:53](https://youtu.be/yjOyMqYEX_8?t=173)
