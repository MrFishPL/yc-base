# Artist Lauren McCarthy Will Be Your Home's Brain — Compiled Truth

Artist and p5.js lead Lauren McCarthy discusses building performance-art "products" that people mistake for startups, the deliberate design choices behind an open-source project's inclusivity, and her new project "Lauren" — a human who acts as your smart-home assistant instead of Alexa.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- People frequently mistake her art projects, like a conversational centerpiece device, for startup ideas and push her to commercialize or Kickstart them, but she avoids that path because manufacturing and production isn't where she wants to spend her time [artist-lauren-mccarthy-will-be-your-home-s-brain @ 18:22](https://youtu.be/jRzsaVg_Cr4?t=1102)
- In building p5.js, she made deliberate design choices — heavy documentation and approachable, less "clever" code — specifically so non-expert developers could still become open-source contributors [artist-lauren-mccarthy-will-be-your-home-s-brain @ 08:19](https://youtu.be/jRzsaVg_Cr4?t=499)
- She treats who feels welcome using a tool as a first-class product decision, running outreach, translated examples, and curated galleries so p5.js actively grows a broader and more diverse user base [artist-lauren-mccarthy-will-be-your-home-s-brain @ 09:47](https://youtu.be/jRzsaVg_Cr4?t=587)
- She observes that nobody has found an "ideal model" yet for buying, selling, or transferring ownership of digital art or software, unlike the consensus-based market that exists for physical art [artist-lauren-mccarthy-will-be-your-home-s-brain @ 20:08](https://youtu.be/jRzsaVg_Cr4?t=1208)
- She's currently fascinated by home-automation devices like Alexa, noting people accepted the smart speaker without backlash because companies rolled it out as "just a speaker" instead of pitching its full future capability up front [artist-lauren-mccarthy-will-be-your-home-s-brain @ 24:30](https://youtu.be/jRzsaVg_Cr4?t=1470)
- Her new project "Lauren" positions an actual human as a literal alternative to Alexa or Google Home — she personally observes and controls a household's connected devices for a few days, betting human anticipation can outperform an AI assistant [artist-lauren-mccarthy-will-be-your-home-s-brain @ 26:27](https://youtu.be/jRzsaVg_Cr4?t=1587)
- She argues the common assumption that only "high-level" skills survive automation is backwards — emotional/human labor, like greeting someone at a door, is harder to automate than a technically complex task like driving a car [artist-lauren-mccarthy-will-be-your-home-s-brain @ 29:02](https://youtu.be/jRzsaVg_Cr4?t=1742)
- Her advice for getting started in creative technology is to use accessible tools like Processing, p5.js, openFrameworks, and Arduino, lean on online creator communities, and not assume art school is required [artist-lauren-mccarthy-will-be-your-home-s-brain @ 42:13](https://youtu.be/jRzsaVg_Cr4?t=2533)
