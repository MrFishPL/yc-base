# After PMF: People, Customers, Sales by Mathilde Collin — Compiled Truth

Front CEO Mathilde Collin argues that discipline — not a grand vision — is the biggest driver of startup success, and walks through how radical transparency, disciplined hiring, constant customer contact, and careful pricing shaped Front after finding product-market fit.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Discipline matters more than having a grand vision; in the early days Front tracked daily revenue obsessively and aimed everything at moving that one number [after-pmf-people-customers-sales-by-mathilde-collin @ 04:41](https://youtu.be/S2YCcdb1rG4?t=281)
- Front's culture rests on radical transparency — sharing revenue, churn, NPS, cash in the bank, and even board decks with the whole company — because it drives employee engagement and focuses everyone on fixing what's broken [after-pmf-people-customers-sales-by-mathilde-collin @ 18:02](https://youtu.be/S2YCcdb1rG4?t=1082)
- A key hiring bar from Stripe's Patrick Collison: before hiring someone, ask whether you'd want ten of that person, since each hire tends to recruit people like themselves [after-pmf-people-customers-sales-by-mathilde-collin @ 13:37](https://youtu.be/S2YCcdb1rG4?t=817)
- She never compromised on values-fit even under pressure, leaving a head-of-finance role unfilled for six months rather than hire someone who didn't match the culture [after-pmf-people-customers-sales-by-mathilde-collin @ 15:50](https://youtu.be/S2YCcdb1rG4?t=950)
- Feeling ashamed of the product isn't necessarily a sign you lack product-market fit; Front kept growing even while she felt embarrassed by its early state, because customers still had zero churn [after-pmf-people-customers-sales-by-mathilde-collin @ 22:18](https://youtu.be/S2YCcdb1rG4?t=1338)
- A single customer conversation (a doula asking if the product could work over SMS) led Front to add SMS as a channel in a week, driving significant new revenue — a reminder to stay obsessively close to users [after-pmf-people-customers-sales-by-mathilde-collin @ 23:36](https://youtu.be/S2YCcdb1rG4?t=1416)
- Front's freemium tier only ever produced about 15% of paid conversions and mostly acted as a distraction, so they cut it in favor of paid-only tiers starting at $9-19/user and raised prices over time [after-pmf-people-customers-sales-by-mathilde-collin @ 28:39](https://youtu.be/S2YCcdb1rG4?t=1719)
- In early sales calls, the goal isn't to demo features but to deeply understand the customer's workflow and pain point first, then decide honestly whether the product actually solves it [after-pmf-people-customers-sales-by-mathilde-collin @ 32:16](https://youtu.be/S2YCcdb1rG4?t=1936)
- Every fundraise closed within a week largely because of disciplined, consistently strong metrics shared publicly in investor decks, not because of an unusually large vision [after-pmf-people-customers-sales-by-mathilde-collin @ 38:19](https://youtu.be/S2YCcdb1rG4?t=2299)
- Weekly investor and team update emails create forced discipline and honesty; founders who stop sending them or keep changing the format are a red flag she's learned to spot after a few months [after-pmf-people-customers-sales-by-mathilde-collin @ 40:00](https://youtu.be/S2YCcdb1rG4?t=2400)
- Her co-founder's cancer diagnosis reshaped her priorities, leading her to start meditating daily and to now tell every new hire that being happy and healthy comes before the company [after-pmf-people-customers-sales-by-mathilde-collin @ 41:19](https://youtu.be/S2YCcdb1rG4?t=2479)
