# You Don't Have To Be A Billionaire To Launch Satellites — Compiled Truth

The story of Astranis (YC W16) shows that a scrappy, YC-backed team without billionaire founders could build and launch satellites by starting with a radically minimal prototype and ambitious short-term goals.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- turns out the "minimum viable product" for a geostationary telecommunications spacecraft is not very minimal [you-don-t-have-to-be-a-billionaire-to-launch-satellites @ 00:36](https://youtu.be/Q0Xs0lGgwVA?t=36)
- there's a misconception that you need to already be a billionaire (like Musk) or raise huge sums to start a hardtech company [you-don-t-have-to-be-a-billionaire-to-launch-satellites @ 00:54](https://youtu.be/Q0Xs0lGgwVA?t=54)
- in 2016 YC funded three aerospace companies — Astranis, Boom, and Relativity Space — that are now all billion-dollar companies [you-don-t-have-to-be-a-billionaire-to-launch-satellites @ 04:09](https://youtu.be/Q0Xs0lGgwVA?t=249)
- founders set a crazy-ambitious goal to build a first prototype satellite in just three months before demo day, since anything after wouldn't matter to investors [you-don-t-have-to-be-a-billionaire-to-launch-satellites @ 05:43](https://youtu.be/Q0Xs0lGgwVA?t=343)
- the team built the demo satellite on Ikea desks fitted with ESD mats and built a makeshift cleanroom out of PVC piping and shower curtains sprayed with staticide [you-don-t-have-to-be-a-billionaire-to-launch-satellites @ 09:02](https://youtu.be/Q0Xs0lGgwVA?t=542)

- eight years later Astranis has more than 400 employees and secured its own dedicated SpaceX Falcon 9 launch to send up four satellites at once [you-don-t-have-to-be-a-billionaire-to-launch-satellites @ 12:24](https://youtu.be/Q0Xs0lGgwVA?t=744)
