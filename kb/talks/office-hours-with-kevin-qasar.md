# Office Hours with Kevin & Qasar — Compiled Truth

YC partners Kevin Hale and Qasar Younis run live mock office hours with three startups, modeling how founders should show up with specific problems and hard numbers rather than pitches.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Good office hours require founders to know their numbers and bring a specific problem to solve rather than "shooting the shit" or repeating the same unresolved issues each time [office-hours-with-kevin-qasar @ 03:28](https://youtu.be/Ud4Q1Lzh5c8?t=208)
- When evaluating a two-sided marketplace, figure out which side you've actually solved with a deep insight so you can focus all your effort on the harder side [office-hours-with-kevin-qasar @ 06:43](https://youtu.be/Ud4Q1Lzh5c8?t=403)
- An idea can sound "intellectually seductive" and still be unvalidated — the real test is whether someone actually wants the product, not whether the pitch sounds compelling [office-hours-with-kevin-qasar @ 13:45](https://youtu.be/Ud4Q1Lzh5c8?t=825)
- For a company-culture job marketplace, students only trust culture claims once they experience the company firsthand, so lame companies onboarded early can poison the marketplace [office-hours-with-kevin-qasar @ 12:47](https://youtu.be/Ud4Q1Lzh5c8?t=767)
- For a B2B retail-analytics startup with long sales cycles, the founders should have a clear ROI roadmap that tells prospects what to expect at each stage, rather than leaving them to interpret raw data on their own [office-hours-with-kevin-qasar @ 28:19](https://youtu.be/Ud4Q1Lzh5c8?t=1699)
- A product company must avoid turning into a consultancy — it should build features that get customers to a repeatable "touchdown moment" instead of one-off handholding [office-hours-with-kevin-qasar @ 29:01](https://youtu.be/Ud4Q1Lzh5c8?t=1741)
- Long enterprise pilot cycles (three to six months to a pilot, then a year-plus to close) are risky because a startup can run out of money before proving value, especially without any network effects to accelerate growth [office-hours-with-kevin-qasar @ 26:46](https://youtu.be/Ud4Q1Lzh5c8?t=1606)
- Founders should lead with a clear, concise description of what they actually do before explaining the "why" or passion behind it, since investors and users forget vague pitches within seconds [office-hours-with-kevin-qasar @ 32:49](https://youtu.be/Ud4Q1Lzh5c8?t=1969)
- Watching someone unfamiliar with your product use it end-to-end every day reveals basic usability failures (like an unclear signup flow or invisible app launch state) that founders are too close to notice [office-hours-with-kevin-qasar @ 40:38](https://youtu.be/Ud4Q1Lzh5c8?t=2438)
