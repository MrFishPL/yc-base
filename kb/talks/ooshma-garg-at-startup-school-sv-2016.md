# Ooshma Garg at Startup School SV 2016 — Compiled Truth

Gobble founder Ooshma Garg tells the multi-year story of nearly dying twice (a failed platform pivot into enterprise catering, then a failed food-personalization software bet) before finding product-market fit with 10-minute dinner kits, arguing that a shared mission — not just founder ambition — is what carries a team through the "trough of sorrow."

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Most great startups follow Paul Graham's startup curve: an exciting launch, then a multi-year "trough of sorrow," then wiggles of (false) hope before, maybe, the promised land [ooshma-garg-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 03:05](https://youtu.be/KmfuIt96vU4?t=185)
- Unable to find a technical co-founder willing to commit, Garg decided to start the company alone anyway rather than wait [ooshma-garg-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 07:29](https://youtu.be/KmfuIt96vU4?t=449)
- Early growth came from things that don't scale: personally writing and hand-delivering letters door-to-door in Palo Alto to acquire users before figuring out how to scale distribution [ooshma-garg-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 11:51](https://youtu.be/KmfuIt96vU4?t=711)
- Chasing enterprise catering revenue to look "up and to the right" for a Series A backfired because investors saw 80% of revenue was enterprise catering while 100% of the founder's vision was consumer/family-focused, and the round failed [ooshma-garg-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 13:08](https://youtu.be/KmfuIt96vU4?t=788)
- After a second failed Series A and running out of money, Garg treated applying to Y Combinator as a last resort ("your choices are to join Y Combinator or die") and YC became the lifeline that saved the company [ooshma-garg-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 17:31](https://youtu.be/KmfuIt96vU4?t=1051)
- Going back to basics and talking to customers every week revealed not just what the product should do but why it mattered — customers' emotional reactions uncovered the company's true mission of "creating moments of love through food" [ooshma-garg-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 20:03](https://youtu.be/KmfuIt96vU4?t=1203)
- Ambition is singular and will cause hires to leave in tough times, but a shared mission is what gives a team the strength to get through the trough of sorrow to product-market fit [ooshma-garg-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 21:32](https://youtu.be/KmfuIt96vU4?t=1292)
- Product-market fit is not a single aha moment but the summation of many broad experiments and micro-learnings accumulated over years [ooshma-garg-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 28:38](https://youtu.be/KmfuIt96vU4?t=1718)
