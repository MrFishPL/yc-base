# Should Your Startup Bootstrap or Raise Venture Capital? — Compiled Truth

Dalton and Michael argue the bootstrap-vs-VC debate is manufactured outrage: the vast majority of businesses shouldn't and can't raise venture capital, but that doesn't mean bootstrapped success is any less of a win.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The vast majority of businesses should not raise venture capital, and VCs aren't interested in funding most businesses either [should-your-startup-bootstrap-or-raise-venture-capital @ 01:05](https://youtu.be/D81y-kh11oI?t=65)
- Venture capital is a product built specifically for investments that could return 100x to 1000x, so forcing that model onto a business that won't grow that big just makes everyone lose [should-your-startup-bootstrap-or-raise-venture-capital @ 02:50](https://youtu.be/D81y-kh11oI?t=170)
- Starting a VC-backed company is like trying to make the NBA — extremely hard and not a reliable path to getting rich [should-your-startup-bootstrap-or-raise-venture-capital @ 03:32](https://youtu.be/D81y-kh11oI?t=212)
- No trillion-dollar software company has ever been built entirely without VC dollars, so if you want to build at that scale you likely need to raise [should-your-startup-bootstrap-or-raise-venture-capital @ 05:10](https://youtu.be/D81y-kh11oI?t=310)
- A bootstrapped founder can build a small, non-VC-backed software business generating tens of thousands of dollars a month with minimal maintenance and still have "won" [should-your-startup-bootstrap-or-raise-venture-capital @ 06:31](https://youtu.be/D81y-kh11oI?t=391)
