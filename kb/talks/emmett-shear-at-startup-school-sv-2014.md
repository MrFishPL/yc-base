# Emmett Shear at Startup School SV 2014 — Compiled Truth

Twitch's founder walks through eight years of repeated failure and reinvention — from Kiko Calendar through Justin.tv to Twitch — arguing that the accumulated skills from failing matter more than any single idea, and that simply not giving up is the deciding factor in startup success.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The founders started Kiko Calendar in the YC Summer Founders Program knowing almost nothing about programming, product decisions, fundraising, or hiring [emmett-shear-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 01:20](https://youtu.be/cQ3tZ05KCtw?t=80)
- Kiko repeatedly failed to gain traction partly because of "founder ADD" — the team would work on it for five weeks, get demoralized, and jump to other ideas like SoundZap [emmett-shear-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 01:37](https://youtu.be/cQ3tZ05KCtw?t=97)
- Justin.tv only started growing once the founders gave up producing their own reality-TV content and opened the platform for anyone to broadcast live video [emmett-shear-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 06:37](https://youtu.be/cQ3tZ05KCtw?t=397)
- The team never understood what was actually driving Justin.tv's growth, so when growth mysteriously stalled they had no idea how to restart it [emmett-shear-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 07:48](https://youtu.be/cQ3tZ05KCtw?t=468)
- After growth stalled in 2008, the team deliberately set concrete goals for two internal bets — mobile video and gaming, the latter code-named "Zarth" — rather than launching random untested features [emmett-shear-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 09:17](https://youtu.be/cQ3tZ05KCtw?t=557)
- Only about five to six years in did Shear realize he had never actually learned how to talk to users properly, despite believing he had [emmett-shear-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 11:55](https://youtu.be/cQ3tZ05KCtw?t=715)
- Twitch grew through a repeated cycle of talking to broadcasters and viewers, building for them, gaining leverage, and using that to promote and bring on more users [emmett-shear-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 14:46](https://youtu.be/cQ3tZ05KCtw?t=886)
- The team identified broadcasters as their single most important user segment and focused building specifically around making the platform amazing for them [emmett-shear-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 14:31](https://youtu.be/cQ3tZ05KCtw?t=871)
- Shear warns against doing business development deals early, since every BD attempt during Justin.tv's pre-traction period was a distraction and waste of time [emmett-shear-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 14:46](https://youtu.be/cQ3tZ05KCtw?t=886)
- The full journey from Kiko to Twitch took about eight and a half years, and Shear's closing message is that not giving up is "the only way to succeed in startups" [emmett-shear-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 17:21](https://youtu.be/cQ3tZ05KCtw?t=1041)
