# The Muse's Kathryn Minshew Speaks at the Female Founders Conference 2016 — Compiled Truth

Kathryn Minshew of The Muse shares four lessons from four and a half years of building the company: that it can get somewhat easier past the early death-defying stage, that focus must shift with company stage, that culture is a real strategic investment, and that staying connected to your original motivation sustains you long-term.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Contrary to the common claim that "it doesn't get easier," she argues the acute risk of startup death does decrease over time even as the stakes and stress of each decision grow larger [the-muse-s-kathryn-minshew-speaks-at-the-female-founders-con @ 01:58](https://youtu.be/Cmd5yOjnOqo?t=118)
- In late 2013, The Muse was so focused on user love and growth that revenue growth stalled and they came uncomfortably close to needing to fundraise again, teaching her that the most important focus area changes by stage [the-muse-s-kathryn-minshew-speaks-at-the-female-founders-con @ 04:06](https://youtu.be/Cmd5yOjnOqo?t=246)
- A YC partner cut off their pitch for an elaborate first product with badges and multiple data sources and told them to "just launch already," reinforcing that early focus should be on shipping and getting feedback, not perfecting the vision [the-muse-s-kathryn-minshew-speaks-at-the-female-founders-con @ 05:51](https://youtu.be/Cmd5yOjnOqo?t=351)
- Culture is easy to deprioritize early because it's rarely the most imminent risk of death, but it becomes critical to building a company that lasts, and it breaks down into how you hire and how you behave [the-muse-s-kathryn-minshew-speaks-at-the-female-founders-con @ 07:02](https://youtu.be/Cmd5yOjnOqo?t=422)
- At around 25 people, The Muse wrote down six core values and tests every candidate against them rather than relying on a subjective "would I get a beer with them" filter, and will pass on a highly skilled candidate if they're a jerk [the-muse-s-kathryn-minshew-speaks-at-the-female-founders-con @ 07:40](https://youtu.be/Cmd5yOjnOqo?t=460)
- Culture is defined by how founders and executives behave when no one is watching, not by slogans on a wall, illustrated by a company that lied to employees about serving cheap hot dogs during a downturn instead of being honest about cutting costs [the-muse-s-kathryn-minshew-speaks-at-the-female-founders-con @ 08:49](https://youtu.be/Cmd5yOjnOqo?t=529)
- After raising $10 million for their Series A, The Muse shared a full 20-slide budget breakdown with the entire company to preserve a scrappy culture despite having more capital, which led employees to self-edit their own budget requests [the-muse-s-kathryn-minshew-speaks-at-the-female-founders-con @ 11:43](https://youtu.be/Cmd5yOjnOqo?t=703)
- At YC demo day she got only one investor from the pitch itself, with the rest of the round coming from later conversations, showing that demo day is not the make-or-break moment it feels like [the-muse-s-kathryn-minshew-speaks-at-the-female-founders-con @ 13:41](https://youtu.be/Cmd5yOjnOqo?t=821)
- Her advice for staying motivated through a multi-year slog is to remember why you started — for her, the frustration of an awful job-search experience that millions of people are forced to use [the-muse-s-kathryn-minshew-speaks-at-the-female-founders-con @ 14:16](https://youtu.be/Cmd5yOjnOqo?t=856)
