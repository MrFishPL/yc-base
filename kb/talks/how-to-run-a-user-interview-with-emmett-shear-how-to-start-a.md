# How to Run a User Interview with Emmett Shear (How to Start a Startup 2014: Lecture 16) — Compiled Truth

Drawing on Twitch's pivot from Justin.tv, Shear argues that who you interview matters as much as what you ask, and that user interviews should surface underlying goals and problems rather than specific feature requests.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Building only for yourself as the sole user is a limiting shortcut — it works only if you happen to be representative of a large group, as happened partly with Justin.tv [how-to-run-a-user-interview-with-emmett-shear-how-to-start-a @ 01:13](https://youtu.be/lHs9hQBSoJU?t=73)
- Who you talk to is as important as what you ask; interviewing broadcasters versus viewers would have produced completely different, strategically decisive feedback for Twitch [how-to-run-a-user-interview-with-emmett-shear-how-to-start-a @ 04:56](https://youtu.be/lHs9hQBSoJU?t=296)
- Avoid asking users to react to a specific feature idea ("is this feature good?") — real users will almost always say yes, which is unreliable signal [how-to-run-a-user-interview-with-emmett-shear-how-to-start-a @ 23:09](https://youtu.be/lHs9hQBSoJU?t=1389)
- The most validating test for an idea is the money test — getting someone to actually commit to pay for it reveals real interest far better than opinions [how-to-run-a-user-interview-with-emmett-shear-how-to-start-a @ 24:36](https://youtu.be/lHs9hQBSoJU?t=1476)
- Twitch compared feedback from three groups — existing broadcasters, competitors' broadcasters, and non-users — and found each group cared about very different things [how-to-run-a-user-interview-with-emmett-shear-how-to-start-a @ 27:34](https://youtu.be/lHs9hQBSoJU?t=1654)
- Non-users are often the most important group to interview, since most people you're competing with for a new product category are non-users, and their objections show you how to expand the market [how-to-run-a-user-interview-with-emmett-shear-how-to-start-a @ 29:51](https://youtu.be/lHs9hQBSoJU?t=1791)
- Quantitative data (analytics, funnels) tells you where problems occur but not what to build next — that requires direct user interviews [how-to-run-a-user-interview-with-emmett-shear-how-to-start-a @ 34:03](https://youtu.be/lHs9hQBSoJU?t=2043)
- A top mistake startups make in user interviews is showing users the product or pitching a specific feature direction instead of learning what's already in their heads [how-to-run-a-user-interview-with-emmett-shear-how-to-start-a @ 34:48](https://youtu.be/lHs9hQBSoJU?t=2088)
- Recording interviews and playing them back for the team is far more persuasive for getting internal buy-in than simply reporting conclusions [how-to-run-a-user-interview-with-emmett-shear-how-to-start-a @ 36:48](https://youtu.be/lHs9hQBSoJU?t=2208)
- The pool of users you most need to interview shifts over time — the people critical to getting a product started are not the same ones using it years later, so ongoing interviews matter [how-to-run-a-user-interview-with-emmett-shear-how-to-start-a @ 44:38](https://youtu.be/lHs9hQBSoJU?t=2678)
