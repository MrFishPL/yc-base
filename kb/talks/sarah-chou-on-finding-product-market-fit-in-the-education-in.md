# Sarah Chou on Finding Product-Market Fit in the Education Industry - at YC Edtech Night — Compiled Truth

Sarah Chou, co-founder of Informed K12 (formerly ChalkSchools), describes pivoting away from a teacher Q&A tool after a brutal user test and grinding for years to find product-market fit selling paperwork automation to school districts.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A recorded, unsupervised user test where teachers said "I would never use this" was the clear signal to abandon the original Q&A-for-teachers idea [sarah-chou-on-finding-product-market-fit-in-the-education-in @ 05:44](https://youtu.be/n-3Pt6HoWmA?t=344)
- After killing the idea, the team spent one to two weeks talking to as many people as possible and testing ideas before landing on the paperwork problem [sarah-chou-on-finding-product-market-fit-in-the-education-in @ 07:44](https://youtu.be/n-3Pt6HoWmA?t=464)
- They sold their first pilot districts with only a PowerPoint prototype, with nothing actually built yet [sarah-chou-on-finding-product-market-fit-in-the-education-in @ 10:36](https://youtu.be/n-3Pt6HoWmA?t=636)
- In a dispersed market like school systems, figuring out who actually has budget authority to buy (vs. who merely uses the product) took a long time and a lot of trial and error across many staff roles [sarah-chou-on-finding-product-market-fit-in-the-education-in @ 11:23](https://youtu.be/n-3Pt6HoWmA?t=683)
- Even after 25 sales there was no single "aha" moment; product-market fit showed up as a repeatable, consistent sales process rather than a single event [sarah-chou-on-finding-product-market-fit-in-the-education-in @ 13:15](https://youtu.be/n-3Pt6HoWmA?t=795)
- A key "doesn't scale" practice that persisted even after growth was personally calling every customer when something broke, and continuing to check NPS and call office managers directly [sarah-chou-on-finding-product-market-fit-in-the-education-in @ 15:52](https://youtu.be/n-3Pt6HoWmA?t=952)
- Building trust with school districts required understanding each district's unique local context, since customers treat their situations as distinct even when problems are common [sarah-chou-on-finding-product-market-fit-in-the-education-in @ 17:58](https://youtu.be/n-3Pt6HoWmA?t=1078)
- Their fundraising process stalled for months chasing investor meetings and over-polishing a pitch deck (from 10 to 40+ slides) until they refocused on product and pilots, and eventually landed an investor over a year later out of the blue [sarah-chou-on-finding-product-market-fit-in-the-education-in @ 21:12](https://youtu.be/n-3Pt6HoWmA?t=1272)
