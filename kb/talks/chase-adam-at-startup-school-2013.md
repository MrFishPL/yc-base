# Chase Adam at Startup School 2013 — Compiled Truth

Watsi founder Chase Adam tells the honest, unresolved story of building a nonprofit medical crowdfunding platform, from a chance encounter on a bus in Central America through a failed first fundraise to a Paul Graham cold email and a $1.2M raise built on selling a vision rather than emotion or impact metrics.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The idea for Watsi came from watching a woman on a bus in Central America earn strangers' trust and donations by carrying her son's actual medical records in a red folder [chase-adam-at-startup-school-2013 @ 06:20](https://youtu.be/WlT3UhC7NwQ?t=380)
- Nonprofits are harder to run than for-profits because success can't be measured the same way: you must convince one group to give money and a completely different group to receive the product, with little feedback from the second group [chase-adam-at-startup-school-2013 @ 08:46](https://youtu.be/WlT3UhC7NwQ?t=526)
- Watsi's team made five founding decisions early: radical transparency, a 100%-to-patients model with no cut taken, minimal fundraising, no bosses/founder titles, and treating patients and donors as they'd want to be treated [chase-adam-at-startup-school-2013 @ 12:22](https://youtu.be/WlT3UhC7NwQ?t=742)
- The public launch was nearly silent (only friends' and family's mothers donated), but a Hacker News post the founder was too scared to tell his team about hit #1 and drove 16,000 unique visitors and funded the entire patient pipeline [chase-adam-at-startup-school-2013 @ 15:15](https://youtu.be/WlT3UhC7NwQ?t=915)
- The first fundraising attempt failed for three months because "no one will ever tell you no" as a nonprofit — people praise you but never write a check [chase-adam-at-startup-school-2013 @ 17:54](https://youtu.be/WlT3UhC7NwQ?t=1074)
- Paul Graham found the founder's personal email through his Hacker News account, asked to meet, and made Watsi's first-ever donation within about an hour, after three months of nobody giving money [chase-adam-at-startup-school-2013 @ 18:45](https://youtu.be/WlT3UhC7NwQ?t=1125)
- YC gave Watsi three things: focus (letting the scattered team relocate and work only on one metric), a powerful network, and a "stamp of approval" that mattered especially for a risk-averse nonprofit funding world [chase-adam-at-startup-school-2013 @ 19:24](https://youtu.be/WlT3UhC7NwQ?t=1164)
- After failed pitches selling emotion and selling fabricated impact projections, the founder realized Watsi's actual pitch was a simple vision, and raised $1.2M across 138 meetings using it [chase-adam-at-startup-school-2013 @ 22:15](https://youtu.be/WlT3UhC7NwQ?t=1335)
