# YC Founders Made These Fundraising Mistakes — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell use anonymous YC founder stories to argue that leverage, not desperation, determines fundraising outcomes, and that leverage comes from growth metrics, low cash need, and customer obsession rather than investor pleasing.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A founder who couldn't raise from 140 investors with a flat startup raised a seed round in one week once they had a growing metric, showing growth is the easiest lever for fundraising success [yc-founders-made-these-fundraising-mistakes @ 00:17](https://youtu.be/6606a2ka-jQ?t=17)
- Raising before you have real metrics is often fear-based decision-making — founders who secretly doubt their product rush to fundraise before the market can prove them right or wrong [yc-founders-made-these-fundraising-mistakes @ 01:17](https://youtu.be/6606a2ka-jQ?t=77)
- A common failure mode is treating the investor as the "teacher" to please with a pitch instead of staying obsessively focused on the customer [yc-founders-made-these-fundraising-mistakes @ 01:56](https://youtu.be/6606a2ka-jQ?t=116)
- A useful self-audit: if less than 20% of your waking week is spent talking to customers and building product, you're probably doing something wrong [yc-founders-made-these-fundraising-mistakes @ 02:58](https://youtu.be/6606a2ka-jQ?t=178)
- Founders should raise only what they need since they'll find a way to spend whatever is in the bank, and money is better thought of as food (needed, but harmful in excess) than as oxygen [yc-founders-made-these-fundraising-mistakes @ 03:37](https://youtu.be/6606a2ka-jQ?t=217)
- The real "oxygen" of a successful company is customer revenue and demand pulling growth forward, not capital raised [yc-founders-made-these-fundraising-mistakes @ 04:01](https://youtu.be/6606a2ka-jQ?t=241)
- Facebook was reportedly profitable even as a small college network, and Google had huge traction before raising its first dollar, which is why both sets of founders retained unusual control and leverage in every subsequent round [yc-founders-made-these-fundraising-mistakes @ 05:08](https://youtu.be/6606a2ka-jQ?t=308)
- The founders who end up owning the most of their company are typically the ones who were never desperate to raise and negotiated from leverage [yc-founders-made-these-fundraising-mistakes @ 04:48](https://youtu.be/6606a2ka-jQ?t=288)
- Founders should benchmark themselves against companies with at least $100M (ideally $1B+) in revenue rather than local peers or startups that just raised at a high valuation [yc-founders-made-these-fundraising-mistakes @ 06:28](https://youtu.be/6606a2ka-jQ?t=388)
