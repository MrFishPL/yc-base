# Tarpit Ideas: The Sequel — Compiled Truth

Dalton and Michael revisit the "tarpit idea" concept, clarifying it isn't just "an idea I dislike" but a specific, historically over-attempted idea space, and explore how new technology like LLMs can un-tarpit old ideas.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A tarpit idea is not simply a bad idea; it's a small, distinct set of ideas that many founders repeatedly apply with and that consistently fail [tarpit-ideas-the-sequel @ 01:34](https://youtu.be/GU9iT7MW0rs?t=94)
- Tarpit ideas are recognizable because they get unusually enthusiastic praise from friends ("that's a great idea!") compared to most startup ideas [tarpit-ideas-the-sequel @ 02:55](https://youtu.be/GU9iT7MW0rs?t=175)
- The list of tarpit ideas is not static — new technology (like LLMs) can make a previously tarpit idea newly viable, so founders should explain why new tech unlocks something that failed before [tarpit-ideas-the-sequel @ 04:05](https://youtu.be/GU9iT7MW0rs?t=245)
- "X for Y" pattern-matched ideas like "co-pilot for lawyers" or "co-pilot for dentists" aren't tarpits themselves (not enough people have tried them) but are extremely common, formulaic AI startup ideas [tarpit-ideas-the-sequel @ 05:16](https://youtu.be/GU9iT7MW0rs?t=316)
- YC funded an AI-generated podcast company that wouldn't have been fundable in the pre-LLM world, showing partners are in an "okay, let's see" phase for AI ideas versus the well-trodden B2B SaaS playbook [tarpit-ideas-the-sequel @ 06:31](https://youtu.be/GU9iT7MW0rs?t=391)
- One tarpit flavor is believing the world should work differently and that everyone needs to simultaneously adopt a new coordination scheme, such as apps for coordinating friend hangouts, which struggle because it's hard to change human behavior [tarpit-ideas-the-sequel @ 08:54](https://youtu.be/GU9iT7MW0rs?t=534)
- A second tarpit flavor is chasing a perceived arbitrage or get-rich-quick window, such as startups spun up around Wall Street Bets-style hype during the pandemic [tarpit-ideas-the-sequel @ 10:20](https://youtu.be/GU9iT7MW0rs?t=620)
- A warning sign for a tarpit idea is when it looks too good to be true or makes you wonder "why does this not already exist," which is not the typical shape of ideas that actually work [tarpit-ideas-the-sequel @ 12:42](https://youtu.be/GU9iT7MW0rs?t=762)
- The recurring fix for founders unsure if their idea is a tarpit is simply to talk to users and get a customer rather than asking a YC partner to judge the idea in the abstract [tarpit-ideas-the-sequel @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/GU9iT7MW0rs?t=0)
