# Uncut Interview with Sam Altman on Masters of Scale [Audio] — Compiled Truth

Reid Hoffman interviews Sam Altman on Masters of Scale about his path from Loopt through YC, and the mental models Altman uses for risk, scaling, hiring, and luck.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Young people with nothing to lose are the ones best positioned to take risk, yet they're usually the most risk-averse, when in fact failing at a startup barely changes your position [uncut-interview-with-sam-altman-on-masters-of-scale-audio @ 02:48](https://youtu.be/mUZKAhcxXWI?t=168)
- The best predictor of a large future TAM is how obsessively today's small user base already loves and uses the product, as with early iPhone adopters versus VR headsets left on a shelf [uncut-interview-with-sam-altman-on-masters-of-scale-audio @ 05:58](https://youtu.be/mUZKAhcxXWI?t=358)
- Good blitzscaling happens only after a product is so good people can't stop using it and telling friends; bad blitzscaling is raising huge money and hiring salespeople to generate demand for a mediocre product [uncut-interview-with-sam-altman-on-masters-of-scale-audio @ 28:06](https://youtu.be/mUZKAhcxXWI?t=1686)
- Nearly every great company, including Stripe and Airbnb, had to do unscalable things early (going door-to-door, manual onboarding) to get close to customers before they could scale [uncut-interview-with-sam-altman-on-masters-of-scale-audio @ 30:39](https://youtu.be/mUZKAhcxXWI?t=1839)
- Scaling well requires two or three senior team members you trust who know how to grow an organization, especially for a first-time CEO [uncut-interview-with-sam-altman-on-masters-of-scale-audio @ 33:17](https://youtu.be/mUZKAhcxXWI?t=1997)
- YC scaled by expanding into hard-tech and unfashionable sectors (crypto, energy, AI) where it faced no competition from other investors, rather than staying in the crowded software/mobile space [uncut-interview-with-sam-altman-on-masters-of-scale-audio @ 12:29](https://youtu.be/mUZKAhcxXWI?t=749)
- Scaling an organization requires a very clear vision/culture plus a reasonably clear org structure; YC's "no structure" approach worked at 10 people but fell apart around 30-40 [uncut-interview-with-sam-altman-on-masters-of-scale-audio @ 17:06](https://youtu.be/mUZKAhcxXWI?t=1026)
- Founders should minimize luck's effect by picking a big market, hiring a great team, and executing well, then trust that persistence eventually lets a random variable swing their way [uncut-interview-with-sam-altman-on-masters-of-scale-audio @ 36:31](https://youtu.be/mUZKAhcxXWI?t=2191)
- Founders should triage using an urgent/important matrix, delegate the urgent-but-unimportant, and ruthlessly focus on the few things that actually determine success [uncut-interview-with-sam-altman-on-masters-of-scale-audio @ 56:20](https://youtu.be/mUZKAhcxXWI?t=3380)
