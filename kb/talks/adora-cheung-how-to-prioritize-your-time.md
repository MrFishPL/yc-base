# Adora Cheung - How to Prioritize Your Time — Compiled Truth

Founders should ruthlessly filter tasks by whether they move the primary KPI, since the highest-leverage work is almost always talking to users and building/iterating product, not activities like conferences or awards.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Real startup progress is anything that grows your primary KPI (usually revenue or active users); fake progress is things like attending conferences, chasing awards, or optimizing the wrong metrics [adora-cheung-how-to-prioritize-your-time @ 02:19](https://youtu.be/XcCmMOWuAF4?t=139)
- The highest-leverage tasks almost always come down to two things: talking to users and building/iterating the product [adora-cheung-how-to-prioritize-your-time @ 03:00](https://youtu.be/XcCmMOWuAF4?t=180)
- An hour-by-hour journal of the past week, honestly rated for expected KPI impact, usually reveals that far more time went to low-value work than founders realize, because humans default to easy tasks that feel good to check off [adora-cheung-how-to-prioritize-your-time @ 04:51](https://youtu.be/XcCmMOWuAF4?t=291)
- Keep a running spreadsheet of KPI-moving ideas as they occur, but don't act on them immediately — jumping to the newest idea causes whiplash and stalls weekly progress [adora-cheung-how-to-prioritize-your-time @ 06:32](https://youtu.be/XcCmMOWuAF4?t=392)
- Grade every task on your list as high/medium/low probability of hitting the weekly primary-KPI goal, then separately grade complexity as easy (under a day), medium (1-2 days), or hard (many days, may not finish this week) [adora-cheung-how-to-prioritize-your-time @ 09:25](https://youtu.be/XcCmMOWuAF4?t=565)
- A common mistake by technical founders is building things first and only later talking to users, which the impact/complexity grid usually reveals is the least impactful choice for hitting a weekly customer goal [adora-cheung-how-to-prioritize-your-time @ 08:42](https://youtu.be/XcCmMOWuAF4?t=522)
- Always prioritize high-impact/easy tasks first, then high-impact/medium-complexity tasks, and avoid low-impact/hard tasks entirely; also avoid overloading the week with too many tasks at once [adora-cheung-how-to-prioritize-your-time @ 10:20](https://youtu.be/XcCmMOWuAF4?t=620)
- Weekly updates that log goal, outcome, biggest blocker, predicted vs. actual impact, and key learnings — reviewed periodically from the start of the company — help founders spot whether they're stuck in a bad loop of low-value work [adora-cheung-how-to-prioritize-your-time @ 11:59](https://youtu.be/XcCmMOWuAF4?t=719)
- If tasks routinely run over time, either break complex tasks into medium/easy pieces, or restructure the schedule using a modified "maker's schedule vs. manager's schedule," dedicating full days or half-days to coding versus talking to users to avoid costly context switching [adora-cheung-how-to-prioritize-your-time @ 13:18](https://youtu.be/XcCmMOWuAF4?t=798)
- Moving fast and being okay with making a wrong choice beats being indecisive, since a founder who picks the wrong task but moves quickly and learns will out-execute one who takes forever to find the "right" task [adora-cheung-how-to-prioritize-your-time @ 15:03](https://youtu.be/XcCmMOWuAF4?t=903)
