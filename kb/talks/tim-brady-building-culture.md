# Tim Brady - Building Culture — Compiled Truth

Tim Brady argues that culture is simply the implicit behaviors that guide employees, and that founders must deliberately shape it with their first ~20 employees because those hires later train everyone who comes after.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Culture is just implicit behavior that guides employees when things aren't explicitly spelled out, and it will support the business in intangible ways if done right [tim-brady-building-culture @ 00:38](https://youtu.be/qnNHW6TYv5I?t=38)
- The first ~20 employees are a company's "cultural DNA" because they will be the ones hiring and training the next wave, so mistakes made early get propagated [tim-brady-building-culture @ 02:48](https://youtu.be/qnNHW6TYv5I?t=168)
- Founders must be genuinely proud of the problem they're solving, since a lack of identification with customers causes people to give up when times get hard, illustrated by a founder whose team drifted into makeup for teenage girls and shut down [tim-brady-building-culture @ 05:27](https://youtu.be/qnNHW6TYv5I?t=327)
- Create a long-term, purpose-driven vision (like Tesla's "accelerate the world's transition to sustainable energy") rather than a description of the product, to attract a broad range of people [tim-brady-building-culture @ 07:07](https://youtu.be/qnNHW6TYv5I?t=427)
- Co-founders should agree on a short list (under five) of values and behaviors to use as an informal hiring filter, similar to Spotify's or Atlassian's value lists [tim-brady-building-culture @ 08:59](https://youtu.be/qnNHW6TYv5I?t=539)
- Founders must model the values themselves since early employees take their cultural cues directly from founder behavior, not from a document [tim-brady-building-culture @ 11:20](https://youtu.be/qnNHW6TYv5I?t=680)
- Externally-focused values (like Google's "don't be evil") give better guardrails than internally-focused ones (like Facebook's "move fast and break things"), which offer no guidance on what not to build [tim-brady-building-culture @ 12:06](https://youtu.be/qnNHW6TYv5I?t=726)
- Have an early conversation about diversity of opinion, not just demographics, since hiring only from your own network early tends to create a homogeneous team that's hard to fix later [tim-brady-building-culture @ 14:11](https://youtu.be/qnNHW6TYv5I?t=851)
- Put a deliberate hiring process in place from the first employee and revisit it after each hire to check whether it filtered for the right people [tim-brady-building-culture @ 15:49](https://youtu.be/qnNHW6TYv5I?t=949)
