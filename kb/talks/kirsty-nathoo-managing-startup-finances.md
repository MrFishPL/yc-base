# Kirsty Nathoo - Managing Startup Finances — Compiled Truth

YC's CFO walks through the basic cash numbers every founder must track and the common financial mistakes — from ignoring runway to hiring too fast — that kill startups.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Every founder should know three simple numbers pulled straight from bank statements: bank balance, money in, and money out; from these you derive burn, runway, growth rate, and default-alive status [kirsty-nathoo-managing-startup-finances @ 01:29](https://youtu.be/LBC16jhiwak?t=89)
- Burn is money in minus money out (or an averaged "average burn" over a few months if expenses are lumpy), and runway is bank balance divided by average burn [kirsty-nathoo-managing-startup-finances @ 02:09](https://youtu.be/LBC16jhiwak?t=129)
- Don't fudge burn numbers to make runway look better — you'll still run out of cash on the same day, you're only lying to yourself [kirsty-nathoo-managing-startup-finances @ 03:45](https://youtu.be/LBC16jhiwak?t=225)
- Trevor Blackwell's default-alive calculator projects, from constant expenses and current growth rate, whether a company reaches profitability before running out of cash, and being profitable gives founders freedom because they don't need to raise [kirsty-nathoo-managing-startup-finances @ 05:10](https://youtu.be/LBC16jhiwak?t=310)
- Check your numbers at least weekly (daily if runway is tight), not just quarterly, since many founders stop monitoring after the first calculation [kirsty-nathoo-managing-startup-finances @ 07:31](https://youtu.be/LBC16jhiwak?t=451)
- Expenses aren't constant: undervaluing founder time, the fully-loaded cost of hires (25-50% above salary), and rising paid-acquisition costs over time are commonly underestimated [kirsty-nathoo-managing-startup-finances @ 08:41](https://youtu.be/LBC16jhiwak?t=521)
- Hiring too quickly is dangerous because employee count is a vanity metric — the better measure is revenue-per-employee, and the best companies do more with less [kirsty-nathoo-managing-startup-finances @ 14:30](https://youtu.be/LBC16jhiwak?t=870)
- More salespeople or engineers will not manufacture product-market fit; if you have real PMF, customers already beat down the door even for a bare-bones product [kirsty-nathoo-managing-startup-finances @ 17:34](https://youtu.be/LBC16jhiwak?t=1054)
- Always assume the money you've raised is the last you'll ever raise, and aim to reach profitability on it rather than counting on investors to bridge you [kirsty-nathoo-managing-startup-finances @ 19:35](https://youtu.be/LBC16jhiwak?t=1175)
- Start fundraising with real runway cushion — around 12 months is the safe threshold, since raising can take three months and leverage erodes as cash declines [kirsty-nathoo-managing-startup-finances @ 20:49](https://youtu.be/LBC16jhiwak?t=1249)
- A full-time CFO is typically unnecessary until well after a Series A; bookkeepers handle reporting earlier and a CPA handles annual tax filings, but founders remain responsible for understanding the numbers [kirsty-nathoo-managing-startup-finances @ 24:39](https://youtu.be/LBC16jhiwak?t=1479)
