# Kevin Hale - How to Pitch Your Startup — Compiled Truth

Founders should describe their company with clear, concise, reproducible nouns (what, problem, customer) rather than jargon, mystery, or self-defensive padding, because clarity is what makes an idea spread by word of mouth and gets investors excited.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Founders don't need to "sell" YC partners; a good investor imagines the rare events that could make you a billion-dollar company on their own once they understand the idea [kevin-hale-how-to-pitch-your-startup @ 03:37](https://youtu.be/17XZGUX_9iM?t=217)
- A clear idea is the foundation for organic growth, because word of mouth requires people to remember and retell what your company does [kevin-hale-how-to-pitch-your-startup @ 05:13](https://youtu.be/17XZGUX_9iM?t=313)
- Things that make an idea unclear include ambiguity, complexity (mixing multiple ideas), mystery/jargon, and language people have learned to ignore like marketing speak and buzzwords [kevin-hale-how-to-pitch-your-startup @ 08:36](https://youtu.be/17XZGUX_9iM?t=516)
- A good pitch should be reproducible: the listener needs to know three nouns — what you're making, the problem, and the customer — to picture your company [kevin-hale-how-to-pitch-your-startup @ 11:28](https://youtu.be/17XZGUX_9iM?t=688)
- Airbnb's YC application description ("the first online marketplace that lets travelers book rooms with locals instead of hotels") works because it's concise and lets you immediately picture the product [kevin-hale-how-to-pitch-your-startup @ 13:24](https://youtu.be/17XZGUX_9iM?t=804)
- The "X for Y" framing (e.g. "LinkedIn for the next billion internet users") only works if X is a household name everyone agrees is successful, Y clearly wants that model, and Y is a huge market on its own [kevin-hale-how-to-pitch-your-startup @ 16:23](https://youtu.be/17XZGUX_9iM?t=983)
- A real example that failed this test was a company describing itself as "Buffer for Snapchat," since Buffer is a much smaller and less universally known company than Snapchat [kevin-hale-how-to-pitch-your-startup @ 18:14](https://youtu.be/17XZGUX_9iM?t=1094)
- Concision itself signals to investors that a founder has thought deeply about the idea and is efficient in thought and action, so use as few words as possible while still conveying the key nouns [kevin-hale-how-to-pitch-your-startup @ 20:23](https://youtu.be/17XZGUX_9iM?t=1223)
- To sharpen a description, strip it down to subject-verb-object (company, product, customer) first, then only add back modifiers that genuinely make the pitch more exciting, not buzzwords like "AI" or "IoT" [kevin-hale-how-to-pitch-your-startup @ 24:31](https://youtu.be/17XZGUX_9iM?t=1471)
- The overall rule for describing your company: lead with what, not why or how — extra narrative and defensive padding only gets in the way of clarity [kevin-hale-how-to-pitch-your-startup @ 18:55](https://youtu.be/17XZGUX_9iM?t=1135)
