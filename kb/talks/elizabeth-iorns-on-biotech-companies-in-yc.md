# Elizabeth Iorns on Biotech Companies in YC — Compiled Truth

Elizabeth Iorns, founder of Science Exchange and a YC biotech expert-partner, explains how biotech founders use a 3-month YC batch to de-risk technology and nail go-to-market rather than make fundamental scientific breakthroughs, and how the funding and IP landscape differs from traditional biotech VC.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- YC gives biotech founders (often PhD students/postdocs unfamiliar with entrepreneurship) the expertise and resources to build initial product-market fit and launch a company. [elizabeth-iorns-on-biotech-companies-in-yc @ 01:18](https://youtu.be/PzkyHKHRH2w?t=78)
- YC biotech companies have collectively raised over $200 million in crossover capital, more than they likely would have raised staying purely in traditional biotech VC. [elizabeth-iorns-on-biotech-companies-in-yc @ 02:42](https://youtu.be/PzkyHKHRH2w?t=162)
- In three months, biotech companies rarely make major scientific advances; instead YC provides focus to figure out the minimal viable product and go-to-market strategy needed to reach the next stage. [elizabeth-iorns-on-biotech-companies-in-yc @ 03:17](https://youtu.be/PzkyHKHRH2w?t=197)
- During the batch, companies put money to work by outsourcing critical-path experiments and working with regulatory consultants to devise a go-to-market and filing strategy. [elizabeth-iorns-on-biotech-companies-in-yc @ 04:38](https://youtu.be/PzkyHKHRH2w?t=278)
- Iorns argues biotech founders don't typically need a dedicated business co-founder, since scientists already have transferable skills like articulate presenting and writing complex grant proposals. [elizabeth-iorns-on-biotech-companies-in-yc @ 06:43](https://youtu.be/PzkyHKHRH2w?t=403)
- For biotech, "de-risking" during YC means generating experimental proof points — e.g., showing in an animal or cell model that a therapeutic intervention reduces disease impact — rather than proving market size. [elizabeth-iorns-on-biotech-companies-in-yc @ 07:28](https://youtu.be/PzkyHKHRH2w?t=448)
- Intellectual property is the cornerstone of biotech strategy because founders need a window of exclusivity to recoup the massive investment required before generic manufacturers can undercut them. [elizabeth-iorns-on-biotech-companies-in-yc @ 14:07](https://youtu.be/PzkyHKHRH2w?t=847)
- Because most IP from academic research is owned by the university, YC helps founders build the business case and navigate licensing it from the school's tech transfer office. [elizabeth-iorns-on-biotech-companies-in-yc @ 16:03](https://youtu.be/PzkyHKHRH2w?t=963)
- Silicon Valley funds not traditionally associated with biotech — Khosla Ventures, a16z, Data Collective, Founders Fund, NEA — are now actively investing in the space, sometimes funding large seed rounds straight out of YC. [elizabeth-iorns-on-biotech-companies-in-yc @ 17:58](https://youtu.be/PzkyHKHRH2w?t=1078)
