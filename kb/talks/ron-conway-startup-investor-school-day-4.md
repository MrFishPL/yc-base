# Ron Conway - Startup Investor School Day 4 — Compiled Truth

Legendary angel investor Ron Conway closes out Startup Investor School with a Q&A on how he built SV Angel, what he looks for in founders, and how to run an angel portfolio.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- SV Angel invests in the founder first and the idea second, deciding within minutes today whether they like a founder [ron-conway-startup-investor-school-day-4 @ 05:38](https://youtu.be/bgwqM6J3WeM?t=338)
- The single biggest predictor of success is a founder's determination, since starting a company is the hardest thing they'll ever do [ron-conway-startup-investor-school-day-4 @ 06:28](https://youtu.be/bgwqM6J3WeM?t=388)
- 40-60% of every portfolio will go out of business, and that failure rate is simply part of the angel investing business model, so winners have to win big enough to cover the losers [ron-conway-startup-investor-school-day-4 @ 08:36](https://youtu.be/bgwqM6J3WeM?t=516)
- Co-founders not getting along is one of the hardest failure modes to predict, so Conway watches how founding teams interact and divide turf during their first meeting [ron-conway-startup-investor-school-day-4 @ 09:56](https://youtu.be/bgwqM6J3WeM?t=596)
- Beyond the founders, investors should meet the whole team and get a feel for company culture before investing [ron-conway-startup-investor-school-day-4 @ 11:22](https://youtu.be/bgwqM6J3WeM?t=682)
- Post-investment, the biggest value-add is helping build out the management team and providing distribution partnerships and introductions, followed by pushing founders to stop procrastinating and make decisions [ron-conway-startup-investor-school-day-4 @ 12:47](https://youtu.be/bgwqM6J3WeM?t=767)
- New angel investors should take a portfolio approach: pick a sector with domain expertise, invest small checks (~$25k) across five to ten companies rather than concentrating in just three [ron-conway-startup-investor-school-day-4 @ 16:28](https://youtu.be/bgwqM6J3WeM?t=988)
- Y Combinator stood out from other accelerators from day one because of how well Paul Graham and Jessica Livingston screened founders and the quality of advice they gave [ron-conway-startup-investor-school-day-4 @ 15:08](https://youtu.be/bgwqM6J3WeM?t=908)
- Conway advocates for transparency in startup fundraising, including disclosing conversion prices and pro-rata terms to founders rather than burying them in convertible note paperwork [ron-conway-startup-investor-school-day-4 @ 27:39](https://youtu.be/bgwqM6J3WeM?t=1659)
