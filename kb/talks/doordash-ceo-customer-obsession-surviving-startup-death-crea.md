# DoorDash CEO: Customer Obsession, Surviving Startup Death & Creating A New Market — Compiled Truth

DoorDash CEO Tony Xu describes how the idea came from following a macaron shop owner around for a day, how the founders manually did every delivery to learn the business, and how customer obsession (refunding everyone and baking apology cookies after a near-fatal Stanford game outage) shaped both strategy and culture.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The idea originated when a macaron store owner showed the founders a thick booklet of turned-down delivery orders, which they "unraveled" into a broader thesis across bakeries, restaurants, and retailers [doordash-ceo-customer-obsession-surviving-startup-death-crea @ 02:40](https://youtu.be/3N3TnaViyjk?t=160)
- The founders built and shipped palotodelivery.com (a static HTML page with 8 PDF menus and a Google Voice number) in under an hour, and all four founders personally did every delivery in the earliest days [doordash-ceo-customer-obsession-surviving-startup-death-crea @ 05:32](https://youtu.be/3N3TnaViyjk?t=332)
- To test whether drivers were interchangeable with ride-hailing, they offered 40 Uber X and DoorDash drivers (both earning $20/hr) $25/hr to switch platforms — only 1 of 40 said yes, revealing the two driver pools were fundamentally different self-selected populations [doordash-ceo-customer-obsession-surviving-startup-death-crea @ 09:16](https://youtu.be/3N3TnaViyjk?t=556)
- Demo day was not a success and the company nearly ran out of cash weeks later, since investors saw only 10 weeks of data and had to make a "conviction bet" on an unproven market [doordash-ceo-customer-obsession-surviving-startup-death-crea @ 12:02](https://youtu.be/3N3TnaViyjk?t=722)
- After a Stanford football game overwhelmed the platform with orders and deliveries ran ~90 minutes late, the founders decided within seconds to refund every customer (costing ~40% of their bank account) and stayed up baking apology cookies to hand-deliver at 5am [doordash-ceo-customer-obsession-surviving-startup-death-crea @ 14:45](https://youtu.be/3N3TnaViyjk?t=885)
- That crisis became the origin of DoorDash's internal value "customer obsessed, not competitor focused" [doordash-ceo-customer-obsession-surviving-startup-death-crea @ 15:05](https://youtu.be/3N3TnaViyjk?t=905)
- While competitors fought over dense urban cores, DoorDash bet on suburbs based on direct delivery experience showing stronger demand there, which line-by-line unit economics later confirmed (bigger family baskets, easier parking, single-family homes) [doordash-ceo-customer-obsession-surviving-startup-death-crea @ 16:42](https://youtu.be/3N3TnaViyjk?t=1002)
- DoorDash struggled to raise capital for three years in a row (2016-2018) despite strong internal metrics, because investors were unsure who would emerge as the category winner in an undefined market [doordash-ceo-customer-obsession-surviving-startup-death-crea @ 21:08](https://youtu.be/3N3TnaViyjk?t=1268)
- During COVID, DoorDash cut commissions in half (costing over $200 million) and ran a national TV campaign promoting the whole industry, a decision justified by the long-term mission to "grow the GDP of every city" rather than near-term profitability [doordash-ceo-customer-obsession-surviving-startup-death-crea @ 26:26](https://youtu.be/3N3TnaViyjk?t=1586)
- Xu's advice to his younger self: "there's no better way to be the expert than just to do the work," pointing to how quickly hands-on delivery experience built his logistics expertise [doordash-ceo-customer-obsession-surviving-startup-death-crea @ 28:33](https://youtu.be/3N3TnaViyjk?t=1713)
