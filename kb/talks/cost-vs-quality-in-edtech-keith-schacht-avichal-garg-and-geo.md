# Cost vs Quality in Edtech – Keith Schacht, Avichal Garg, and Geoff Ralston — Compiled Truth

A debate between Mystery Science's Keith Schacht and Imagine K12's Avichal Garg on whether edtech startups win by being cheaper or by being better, framed by Avichal's 2011 thesis that education is a cost problem, not a quality problem.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Avichal's original 2011 thesis was that entrepreneurs mistakenly build education as a quality play when the mass market actually buys mostly on cost [cost-vs-quality-in-edtech-keith-schacht-avichal-garg-and-geo @ 00:53](https://youtu.be/ajO-h8NEcqQ?t=53).
- Selling into schools is fundamentally different from selling to parents directly, and the two are really different markets that shouldn't be lumped together as "the education space" [cost-vs-quality-in-edtech-keith-schacht-avichal-garg-and-geo @ 05:34](https://youtu.be/ajO-h8NEcqQ?t=334).
- Mystery Science took a pull, bottoms-up model with teachers (like Salesforce did against Oracle) instead of the traditional top-down district-technology-director sale [cost-vs-quality-in-edtech-keith-schacht-avichal-garg-and-geo @ 16:35](https://youtu.be/ajO-h8NEcqQ?t=995).
- Education companies historically don't grow like internet companies, and VCs who funded them expecting internet-style ramps mostly haven't seen the revenue to justify the valuations, unlike in China where edtech firms scale from zero to a billion-dollar IPO in about seven years [cost-vs-quality-in-edtech-keith-schacht-avichal-garg-and-geo @ 09:43](https://youtu.be/ajO-h8NEcqQ?t=583).
- Chegg's playbook wasn't reinventing digital learning, it was just making textbooks cheaper, then expanding into "smarter way to student" services from that cost wedge [cost-vs-quality-in-edtech-keith-schacht-avichal-garg-and-geo @ 11:39](https://youtu.be/ajO-h8NEcqQ?t=699).
- Avichal argues quality/cost trade-offs are common to many industries (not unique to edtech): the builder often overvalues incremental quality relative to what the average buyer will actually pay for [cost-vs-quality-in-edtech-keith-schacht-avichal-garg-and-geo @ 27:15](https://youtu.be/ajO-h8NEcqQ?t=1635).
- Keith counters that consumers do reward quality if you price it right, citing IKEA bringing good design to the masses at low price versus $20k designer couches remaining a small niche [cost-vs-quality-in-edtech-keith-schacht-avichal-garg-and-geo @ 24:38](https://youtu.be/ajO-h8NEcqQ?t=1478).
- Keith believes most "quality" measures schools use (standardized test proxies) are actually mismeasurement, and the better quality proxy is student engagement/motivation, which gives an immediate feedback loop [cost-vs-quality-in-edtech-keith-schacht-avichal-garg-and-geo @ 30:23](https://youtu.be/ajO-h8NEcqQ?t=1823).
- Avichal's advice to edtech founders: start from your own motivation and desired outcome, then ask whether an education product is even the best path to that outcome versus adjacent options like job retraining [cost-vs-quality-in-edtech-keith-schacht-avichal-garg-and-geo @ 45:21](https://youtu.be/ajO-h8NEcqQ?t=2721).
- Keith's advice: pick a business you'll stay excited about for ten years, since edtech outcomes take a long time to materialize, and think of yourself as a "learning company" since the science of learning is still in its infancy [cost-vs-quality-in-edtech-keith-schacht-avichal-garg-and-geo @ 47:33](https://youtu.be/ajO-h8NEcqQ?t=2853).
