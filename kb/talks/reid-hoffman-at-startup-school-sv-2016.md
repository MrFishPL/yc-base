# Reid Hoffman at Startup School SV 2016 — Compiled Truth

Reid Hoffman argues great founders need a deep theory of human nature, distribution, and persistence, and walks through what makes founders fundable and companies scalable.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Inventing great products requires a robust, articulable theory of human nature and where humanity is heading [reid-hoffman-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 02:14](https://youtu.be/ysz1xLMd37Y?t=134)
- Build the strongest possible network around you, and ask "why would my plan fail" rather than "what do you think of my idea" [reid-hoffman-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 03:54](https://youtu.be/ysz1xLMd37Y?t=234)
- A coherent theory of distribution — how the product will spread virally, via SEO, or via a sales process — is essential before traction exists [reid-hoffman-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 07:05](https://youtu.be/ysz1xLMd37Y?t=425)
- If you don't see a path to $100M/year in revenue with good growth after that, you have zero chance of being a standalone tech company [reid-hoffman-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 08:31](https://youtu.be/ysz1xLMd37Y?t=511)
- Great founders show "flexible persistence": they listen and adapt but keep conviction in a well-thought-out theory, and pivots are usually variations on the plan, not total resets [reid-hoffman-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 10:30](https://youtu.be/ysz1xLMd37Y?t=630)
- Warm referrals matter enormously for fundraising — top VCs fund essentially none of the 30-50 unsolicited decks they see daily [reid-hoffman-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 12:24](https://youtu.be/ysz1xLMd37Y?t=744)
- A pitch deck should open with the hypotheses behind the investment thesis (no more than ~7-8 bullets), followed by why you'll win, what a mature business looks like, and honest acknowledgment of risks [reid-hoffman-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 17:14](https://youtu.be/ysz1xLMd37Y?t=1034)
- Most VCs target 20%+ ownership per deal because their fund-return math requires it [reid-hoffman-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 20:44](https://youtu.be/ysz1xLMd37Y?t=1244)
- "Blitzscaling" means being first to scale, not first to market, and sometimes requires spending capital at less efficiency to win before competitors do [reid-hoffman-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 22:39](https://youtu.be/ysz1xLMd37Y?t=1359)
- Netflix codified its culture deck to prevent hiring "A players" who weren't cultural fits, treating culture as something everyone holds each other accountable to, not just founders [reid-hoffman-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 27:46](https://youtu.be/ysz1xLMd37Y?t=1666)
- For hiring, use your network but correct for its diversity bias, do light "rate 1-10" reference checks (watch for lukewarm 7s), and spend many hours with senior hires before deciding [reid-hoffman-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 30:32](https://youtu.be/ysz1xLMd37Y?t=1832)
