# How Should Business Schools Prepare Students for Startups? – Jeff Bussgang and Michael Seibel — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel and HBS lecturer Jeff Bussgang debate why MBA applicants to YC underperform expectations, and what business schools could change to better prepare students for startups.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- YC partners read roughly 500 of the 6,000-7,000 applications each batch, and a recurring pattern of MBA applicants missing basic startup fundamentals is what triggered the original tweet [how-should-business-schools-prepare-students-for-startups-je @ 01:19](https://youtu.be/KxN9dJKPbD8?t=79)
- The three biggest red flags Seibel sees in MBA applications are lack of a technical co-founder, lack of full commitment, and lack of traction/time spent building [how-should-business-schools-prepare-students-for-startups-je @ 07:38](https://youtu.be/KxN9dJKPbD8?t=458)
- Many successful startups have in fact been founded by MBAs, including Cloudflare, Rent the Runway, and Earnest, all recent HBS alumni companies [how-should-business-schools-prepare-students-for-startups-je @ 04:52](https://youtu.be/KxN9dJKPbD8?t=292)
- The number one reason people stay stuck doing research instead of shipping is that they can't build their own MVP [how-should-business-schools-prepare-students-for-startups-je @ 17:47](https://youtu.be/KxN9dJKPbD8?t=1067)
- Hustling around a foundational problem, like outsourcing all technical work instead of finding a technical co-founder, actually decreases a startup's chances of success [how-should-business-schools-prepare-students-for-startups-je @ 18:38](https://youtu.be/KxN9dJKPbD8?t=1118)
- VCs only invest in massive market opportunities and truly transformational ideas, which requires vision to see white space others are missing [how-should-business-schools-prepare-students-for-startups-je @ 32:23](https://youtu.be/KxN9dJKPbD8?t=1943)
- For someone deciding between two years of an MBA and two years at an early-stage startup, the tradeoff depends on long-term network and skills versus short-term ROI [how-should-business-schools-prepare-students-for-startups-je @ 24:02](https://youtu.be/KxN9dJKPbD8?t=1442)
- Bussgang's advice to students getting bad startup advice is to read the canon of startup literature (Paul Graham, Eric Ries, Steve Blank's "Four Steps to the Epiphany") since many classes don't cover it [how-should-business-schools-prepare-students-for-startups-je @ 31:13](https://youtu.be/KxN9dJKPbD8?t=1873)
- MBA programs are often siloed from technical talent elsewhere in the university, so students rarely mingle daily with engineers the way they do with future salespeople and marketers [how-should-business-schools-prepare-students-for-startups-je @ 08:44](https://youtu.be/KxN9dJKPbD8?t=524)
