# Morgan DeBaun on Reaching 20M Millennials — Compiled Truth

Blavity founder Morgan DeBaun describes how the company pivoted from a video site to a blog after tracking analytics, bootstrapped for a year with an unpaid team, and eventually raised funding once its numbers became too big to ignore.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Blavity started as a curated email newsletter of top videos, aimed at black audiences who over-index on TV viewing yet had no single place to find that content [morgan-debaun-on-reaching-20m-millennials-with-kat-manalac-a @ 07:05](https://youtu.be/hgzThPwCyHQ?t=425)
- When a dedicated video website failed to gain traction, a blog spun up over one weekend on WordPress got a thousand times more traffic than the video site the team had spent two months building, so DeBaun killed the video site and pivoted the whole company to the blog based on relentlessly watching Google Analytics [morgan-debaun-on-reaching-20m-millennials-with-kat-manalac-a @ 09:31](https://youtu.be/hgzThPwCyHQ?t=571)
- DeBaun describes being "agnostic about how we reach people" and treats killing failed initiatives fast as core to moving in the consumer/social space [morgan-debaun-on-reaching-20m-millennials-with-kat-manalac-a @ 10:27](https://youtu.be/hgzThPwCyHQ?t=627)
- Blavity's community-first values mean the team asks "how does this help someone else" rather than pushing an editorial vision, and it gave cameras and resources to early creators (like Quinta Brunson, then ~50,000 followers) before they got big [morgan-debaun-on-reaching-20m-millennials-with-kat-manalac-a @ 11:08](https://youtu.be/hgzThPwCyHQ?t=668)
- About 50% of Blavity's content today comes from user-generated submissions enabled by their own custom-built CMS, which is more scalable than editorial-only content despite added moderation problems [morgan-debaun-on-reaching-20m-millennials-with-kat-manalac-a @ 12:54](https://youtu.be/hgzThPwCyHQ?t=774)
- Early fundraising failed because DeBaun tried to pitch by comparing Blavity's numbers to already-funded competitors like Mic or Refinery29, whose metrics she couldn't match, and investors didn't understand the broader vision behind "a blog on WordPress" [morgan-debaun-on-reaching-20m-millennials-with-kat-manalac-a @ 14:10](https://youtu.be/hgzThPwCyHQ?t=850)
- The team bootstrapped for a year in San Francisco with six or seven people paid $100–$500/month, and only raised once growth numbers became "so ridiculous" that investors couldn't ignore them [morgan-debaun-on-reaching-20m-millennials-with-kat-manalac-a @ 17:05](https://youtu.be/hgzThPwCyHQ?t=1025)
- DeBaun advises founders to be unapologetic about a big vision from the start, saying she slowed her own growth by shrinking her story too long so investors could digest it [morgan-debaun-on-reaching-20m-millennials-with-kat-manalac-a @ 22:58](https://youtu.be/hgzThPwCyHQ?t=1378)
- A separate Instagram/content vertical aimed at black women outgrew the main Blavity brand faster, which signaled it deserved to become its own standalone brand (21Ninety) rather than staying a sub-section [morgan-debaun-on-reaching-20m-millennials-with-kat-manalac-a @ 19:33](https://youtu.be/hgzThPwCyHQ?t=1173)
