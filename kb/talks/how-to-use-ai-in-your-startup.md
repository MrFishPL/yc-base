# How To Use AI In Your Startup — Compiled Truth

YC group partners discuss how founders should think about incorporating LLMs into their startups, arguing AI adoption should follow the same fundamentals as any wave of technology change, not be a shortcut to success.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Just switching your existing idea to make calls to OpenAI won't change a startup's fate unless the founder still does the fundamental work of producing customer value [how-to-use-ai-in-your-startup @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/7Kt9ugD3bGQ?t=0)
- The strong claim is founders shouldn't pivot to AI just because it seems trendy, but nearly every startup today should be using LLMs at its core in some way [how-to-use-ai-in-your-startup @ 01:35](https://youtu.be/7Kt9ugD3bGQ?t=95)
- The current AI moment is compared to the early-2010s cloud migration and the 2007-era mobile/iPhone wave, where founders who lived through prior platform shifts can better recognize the pattern this time [how-to-use-ai-in-your-startup @ 02:48](https://youtu.be/7Kt9ugD3bGQ?t=168)
- A company called Superpowered pivoted from a Zoom productivity tool to become Bland (an AI voice company), growing from nothing to powering a large portion of YC's AI voice companies within about 15 months after moving from Toronto to San Francisco [how-to-use-ai-in-your-startup @ 05:23](https://youtu.be/7Kt9ugD3bGQ?t=323)
- Startups that pivoted into generic AI ideas (e.g., "customer support agent company number 50") without new insight, community embedding, or deeper customer research tended to fail just like their pre-AI pivots [how-to-use-ai-in-your-startup @ 07:13](https://youtu.be/7Kt9ugD3bGQ?t=433)
- Moving to the Bay Area is framed as critical right now because you can literally walk to the team that is world-class at the thing you want to learn, the way the speaker once learned SEO by visiting Pinterest [how-to-use-ai-in-your-startup @ 08:52](https://youtu.be/7Kt9ugD3bGQ?t=532)
- Examples like Replexica (AI localization) and Gecko Security (AI security engineer) show a pattern of automating a previously specialized skill and putting it behind a simple interface for engineers [how-to-use-ai-in-your-startup @ 10:11](https://youtu.be/7Kt9ugD3bGQ?t=611)
- US healthcare has roughly $1.3-1.4 trillion of administrative spend, much of which is legacy software plus a human moving data between systems, which the speakers argue is now largely automatable with AI agents [how-to-use-ai-in-your-startup @ 11:34](https://youtu.be/7Kt9ugD3bGQ?t=694)
