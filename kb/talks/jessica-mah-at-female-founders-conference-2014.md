# Jessica Mah at Female Founders Conference 2014 — Compiled Truth

inDinero founder Jessica Mah recounts how early hype, vanity metrics, and a hot-tub office masked a business that wasn't working, and how hitting bottom forced a painful pivot to real revenue.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- After YC and $1.2M raised, inDinero chased PR and vanity metrics (billions in tracked assets) while actual revenue was only $80,000 a year, not a month, which a friend called out as effectively "failing" [jessica-mah-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 05:13](https://youtu.be/bBGmSDQ1mAs?t=313).
- An investor's blunt question about whether she'd ever fired anyone gave her the courage to let go of her first hire, and within a year her entire original team had turned over [jessica-mah-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 03:49](https://youtu.be/bBGmSDQ1mAs?t=229).
- Mentor Steve Blank told her directly not to be afraid to pivot, prompting her to admit the original mint.com-for-businesses idea wasn't working [jessica-mah-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 06:14](https://youtu.be/bBGmSDQ1mAs?t=374).
- She shut down the office, let go of employees, and borrowed from her parents rather than keep pretending the business was healthy, which paradoxically brought relief instead of shame [jessica-mah-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 09:02](https://youtu.be/bBGmSDQ1mAs?t=542).
- Doing the math showed the original model would need 4 million paying businesses (and 40 million free users) to become "the next Intuit," a market that doesn't exist, forcing a rethink of the pricing and business model [jessica-mah-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 11:11](https://youtu.be/bBGmSDQ1mAs?t=671).
- The team pivoted from a self-serve accounting dashboard to a full-service model charging a few hundred dollars a month to actually do customers' accounting, taxes, and payroll [jessica-mah-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 12:13](https://youtu.be/bBGmSDQ1mAs?t=733).
- To serve the first paying customer, Mah personally studied at night and earned an IRS enrolled agent license so the company could actually prepare tax returns [jessica-mah-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 13:57](https://youtu.be/bBGmSDQ1mAs?t=837).
- After the pivot, the team stopped tracking vanity metrics like assets under management and focused solely on real revenue, eventually reaching 50 employees and their first profitable month [jessica-mah-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 15:00](https://youtu.be/bBGmSDQ1mAs?t=900).
- Mah reflects that going through near-failure made her far more grounded and less arrogant than in the early hot-tub-office days of quick PR and funding [jessica-mah-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 17:13](https://youtu.be/bBGmSDQ1mAs?t=1033).
