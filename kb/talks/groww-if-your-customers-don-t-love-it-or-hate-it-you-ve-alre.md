# Groww: If Your Customers Don't Love It or Hate It, You've Already Lost — Compiled Truth

Groww founder Lalit Keshre describes how the company abandoned its first idea (a robo-advisor) for a fully transparent, choice-first investing platform after reading customer signal, and how relentless customer contact and disciplined co-founder alignment carried it from zero revenue to a public company.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Groww's first product was a robo-advisor in 2016, modeled on Wealthfront and Betterment, which failed to attract India's under-penetrated investor base [groww-if-your-customers-don-t-love-it-or-hate-it-you-ve-alre @ 04:01](https://youtu.be/ObBAxL2dFzw?t=241)
- Customers kept asking "why this, why not that," which led the team to pivot toward a Flipkart-style model of full product choice and transparency [groww-if-your-customers-don-t-love-it-or-hate-it-you-ve-alre @ 04:50](https://youtu.be/ObBAxL2dFzw?t=290)
- Hitting 600 signups against a goal of 100 in the launch month gave the team a strong, concrete signal of product-market fit [groww-if-your-customers-don-t-love-it-or-hate-it-you-ve-alre @ 07:27](https://youtu.be/ObBAxL2dFzw?t=447)
- A useful risk-reduction heuristic: a startup should try to get down to a single open question mark (for Groww, that was monetization) rather than many unresolved unknowns [groww-if-your-customers-don-t-love-it-or-hate-it-you-ve-alre @ 08:36](https://youtu.be/ObBAxL2dFzw?t=516)
- Early on, founders put every new signup into a personal WhatsApp group and read between the lines of customer requests rather than building literally what was asked for [groww-if-your-customers-don-t-love-it-or-hate-it-you-ve-alre @ 09:43](https://youtu.be/ObBAxL2dFzw?t=583)
- Nearly 100% of Groww's early growth was organic word of mouth, which the team treated as the clearest signal of product-market fit [groww-if-your-customers-don-t-love-it-or-hate-it-you-ve-alre @ 11:57](https://youtu.be/ObBAxL2dFzw?t=717)
- Groww deliberately chose to operate only in regulated territory, taking licenses rather than exploiting gray zones, to reduce strategic variables [groww-if-your-customers-don-t-love-it-or-hate-it-you-ve-alre @ 13:21](https://youtu.be/ObBAxL2dFzw?t=801)
- Groww ran as a zero-revenue company for its first four years, betting that a low-CAC, high-retention, high-engagement product moving large sums of money would eventually monetize [groww-if-your-customers-don-t-love-it-or-hate-it-you-ve-alre @ 15:13](https://youtu.be/ObBAxL2dFzw?t=913)
- Keshre still personally uses the product roughly two hours a day and talks to customers for another two hours, judging a launch a success only if it provokes strong love or strong hate, never indifference [groww-if-your-customers-don-t-love-it-or-hate-it-you-ve-alre @ 17:00](https://youtu.be/ObBAxL2dFzw?t=1020)
- With four co-founders, Groww avoided conflict by fixing a shared, unchanging value system (customer-first) while letting strategy change yearly, and by assigning clear ownership for each function [groww-if-your-customers-don-t-love-it-or-hate-it-you-ve-alre @ 25:44](https://youtu.be/ObBAxL2dFzw?t=1544)
