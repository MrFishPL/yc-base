# Startup Experts Discuss Doing Things That Don't Scale — Compiled Truth

YC Group Partners walk through real examples (Airbnb, Fleek, Algolia, Instacart, DoorDash, Optimizely) of founders manually faking scale to learn fast, arguing that doing unscalable things is the core advantage startups have over bigger, better-funded competitors.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Paul Graham's 2013 essay told founders to stop worrying about theoretical scalability and instead fix whatever problem is directly in front of them right now [startup-experts-discuss-doing-things-that-don-t-scale @ 00:18](https://youtu.be/IjPDIjge81o?t=18)
- The biggest problem most startups have isn't an unscalable architecture, it's that they haven't built something people want [startup-experts-discuss-doing-things-that-don-t-scale @ 03:30](https://youtu.be/IjPDIjge81o?t=210)
- The Airbnb founders got their flywheel turning by personally taking professional-quality photos of listings instead of waiting months to build software to solve that problem [startup-experts-discuss-doing-things-that-don-t-scale @ 06:13](https://youtu.be/IjPDIjge81o?t=373)
- Fleek's founders spent four months manually carrying boxes of secondhand clothes between wholesalers and shops in London to learn pricing, demand elasticity, and timing before building a marketplace website [startup-experts-discuss-doing-things-that-don-t-scale @ 09:02](https://youtu.be/IjPDIjge81o?t=542)
- Algolia's founder personally implemented search via pull request on Product Hunt's own GitHub repo for founder Ryan Hoover, which built a much deeper, more candid customer relationship than a normal sales conversation would [startup-experts-discuss-doing-things-that-don-t-scale @ 11:21](https://youtu.be/IjPDIjge81o?t=681)
- Instacart, instead of pursuing slow corporate partnerships with grocery chains, used their YC money to buy every item at a Trader Joe's, photograph it, and manually list it online the same weekend [startup-experts-discuss-doing-things-that-don-t-scale @ 16:16](https://youtu.be/IjPDIjge81o?t=976)
- DoorDash's first version was built in one afternoon using Google Drive for menus, a founder's personal phone number, Google Forms for orders, and Find My Friends to simulate real-time delivery tracking [startup-experts-discuss-doing-things-that-don-t-scale @ 19:01](https://youtu.be/IjPDIjge81o?t=1141)
- Founders should think about what would get them fired at a big company, since avoiding errors at all costs is a big-company mindset that slows startups down [startup-experts-discuss-doing-things-that-don-t-scale @ 20:33](https://youtu.be/IjPDIjge81o?t=1233)
- Doing unscalable things lets founders fail fast and validate ideas before investing months of engineering, and if no one wants the product, that's a good outcome because it saves time [startup-experts-discuss-doing-things-that-don-t-scale @ 17:36](https://youtu.be/IjPDIjge81o?t=1056)
- At Optimizely, the founders made early money selling manual AB-testing consulting, which validated willingness to pay, but the failure mode is getting addicted to services revenue that can't scale like real software [startup-experts-discuss-doing-things-that-don-t-scale @ 23:07](https://youtu.be/IjPDIjge81o?t=1387)
