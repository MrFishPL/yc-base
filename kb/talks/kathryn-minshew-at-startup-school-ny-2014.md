# Kathryn Minshew at Startup School NY 2014 — Compiled Truth

Kathryn Minshew, founder of The Muse, argues that the polished growth stories people see hide years of slow, repetitive failure, and she walks through the scrappy tactics and hard lessons that got The Muse from a rough first launch to a million-plus monthly users.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Successful companies' polished public stories hide the messy, often flat-growth failure that comes before, as with Minshew's earlier startup that stayed near 3,000 users a month for eight or nine months. [kathryn-minshew-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 02:02](https://youtu.be/XS-kKX9wQk0?t=122)
- The Muse's first website, launched September 2011, was rough but still pulled in 20,000, then 26,000, then 70,000 users over its first three months. [kathryn-minshew-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 04:41](https://youtu.be/XS-kKX9wQk0?t=281)
- With no money or reputation, early growth came from personally emailing her entire Gmail contact list, reaching out to hundreds of niche professional groups, and publishing career content as top-of-funnel marketing. [kathryn-minshew-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 06:17](https://youtu.be/XS-kKX9wQk0?t=377)
- She pitched roughly 150 investors and got 148 rejections before Y Combinator became the first definitive yes. [kathryn-minshew-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 08:30](https://youtu.be/XS-kKX9wQk0?t=510)
- PG's blunt "just launch already" pushed the team to cut a seven-feature redesign down to one testable idea and ship it in 14 days. [kathryn-minshew-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 12:07](https://youtu.be/XS-kKX9wQk0?t=727)
- Honoring your word matters as much as honoring a contract, especially with early hires you can't yet pay on schedule. [kathryn-minshew-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 12:48](https://youtu.be/XS-kKX9wQk0?t=768)
- Done is better than perfect: ideas need to survive contact with real strangers who don't already love you, not just friends and family. [kathryn-minshew-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 14:59](https://youtu.be/XS-kKX9wQk0?t=899)
- Persistence paired with politeness eventually landed key partnerships, like a Forbes content-syndication deal that sent 5,000 early visitors to The Muse after being rejected by the first two people asked. [kathryn-minshew-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 16:18](https://youtu.be/XS-kKX9wQk0?t=978)
- Sharing values with cofounders, employees, and investors matters as much as the product itself — a values mismatch, not just weak growth, sank her first company. [kathryn-minshew-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 18:00](https://youtu.be/XS-kKX9wQk0?t=1080)
- When you can't compete on salary, figure out what actually motivates a candidate — visibility, learning, industry connections — and help them get it. [kathryn-minshew-at-startup-school-ny-2014 @ 20:59](https://youtu.be/XS-kKX9wQk0?t=1259)
