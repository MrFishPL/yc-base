# Kevin Hale - How to Work Together — Compiled Truth

Drawing on marriage researcher John Gottman's work, Kevin Hale argues that co-founder relationships fail for predictable reasons and can be protected with a few concrete practices agreed on before emotions run high.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Gottman's research shows all couples fight about the same topics (money, kids, sex, time, jealousy, in-laws), and the same holds for co-founders fighting about company issues [kevin-hale-how-to-work-together @ 01:33](https://youtu.be/30a5yFBd7Fo?t=93)
- Gottman identified four behaviors — criticism, contempt, defensiveness, and stonewalling — that predict relationship breakdown and should be avoided in founder disagreements [kevin-hale-how-to-work-together @ 02:24](https://youtu.be/30a5yFBd7Fo?t=144)
- Divide and conquer early: assign ownership of problem areas (e.g., fundraising, shipping, competition) to specific co-founders so defensiveness has a clear resolver [kevin-hale-how-to-work-together @ 04:35](https://youtu.be/30a5yFBd7Fo?t=275)
- Define ahead of time what counts as success (don't interfere) versus failure (must intervene) for each delegated area, decided while "emotionally sober" [kevin-hale-how-to-work-together @ 06:06](https://youtu.be/30a5yFBd7Fo?t=366)
- Know your and your co-founder's attachment style (secure, anxious, avoidant) since anxious and avoidant types are most commonly paired and need different handling during conflict [kevin-hale-how-to-work-together @ 08:00](https://youtu.be/30a5yFBd7Fo?t=480)
- Document a disagreement-resolution process in advance (e.g., Matter's decision-framework spreadsheet) so conflicts have a pre-agreed, low-drama procedure [kevin-hale-how-to-work-together @ 11:05](https://youtu.be/30a5yFBd7Fo?t=665)
- Use nonviolent communication (observation, feeling, universal need, request) to deliver honest feedback without criticism or contempt [kevin-hale-how-to-work-together @ 13:01](https://youtu.be/30a5yFBd7Fo?t=781)
- Requests should state the desired behavior specifically rather than a vague demand, e.g. "ask two or three probing questions before sharing a conclusion" instead of "don't dismiss ideas" [kevin-hale-how-to-work-together @ 21:40](https://youtu.be/30a5yFBd7Fo?t=1300)
- Just as software accrues technical debt, relationships accrue "emotional debt," and healthy co-founder pairs address small frictions immediately rather than letting them compound [kevin-hale-how-to-work-together @ 22:43](https://youtu.be/30a5yFBd7Fo?t=1363)
- YC recommends "level three conversations" — relational, in-the-moment discussions about goals, roles, and performance — as a regular practice to pay down emotional debt [kevin-hale-how-to-work-together @ 24:06](https://youtu.be/30a5yFBd7Fo?t=1446)
