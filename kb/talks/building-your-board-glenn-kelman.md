# Building Your Board | Glenn Kelman — Compiled Truth

Redfin CEO Glenn Kelman recounts how his early "siege mentality" toward his board gave way to a candid, document-driven partnership, and lays out concrete tactics for recruiting, running, and communicating with board members.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Because it's nearly impossible to fire a board member, founders should evaluate VC partners as people they want to spend 3-15 years with, not choose a firm for its brand name [building-your-board-glenn-kelman @ 02:17](https://youtu.be/MdLgVgJkOlQ?t=137)
- Early-stage founders often develop a "siege mentality" born of authority issues and stress, treating the board as an adversary, which stops the board from being able to help [building-your-board-glenn-kelman @ 08:03](https://youtu.be/MdLgVgJkOlQ?t=483)
- If your board is "driving you crazy" but your product isn't selling or working, the real problem is usually you, not the board [building-your-board-glenn-kelman @ 09:10](https://youtu.be/MdLgVgJkOlQ?t=550)
- A mentor told him to treat the board like the radio in the car — you can't control what's playing, but you're still the one driving [building-your-board-glenn-kelman @ 09:31](https://youtu.be/MdLgVgJkOlQ?t=571)
- Kelman recommends writing explicit job descriptions for board seats and recruiting operators who are peers at a similar company stage rather than "big name" executives with no relevant context [building-your-board-glenn-kelman @ 13:08](https://youtu.be/MdLgVgJkOlQ?t=788)
- Board candidates should be evaluated for whether they listen more than they talk, since many successful people ("a king without a kingdom") are used to being deferred to and won't understand a founder's specific problems [building-your-board-glenn-kelman @ 14:15](https://youtu.be/MdLgVgJkOlQ?t=855)
- He regrets not prioritizing board diversity earlier, since adding a female board member visibly meant a lot to employees and gave the board deeper perspective [building-your-board-glenn-kelman @ 15:29](https://youtu.be/MdLgVgJkOlQ?t=929)
- Redfin writes narrative documents (not slide decks) for the two deep-dive topics per board meeting, forcing slower, more explicit thinking and one coherent version of the truth [building-your-board-glenn-kelman @ 23:01](https://youtu.be/MdLgVgJkOlQ?t=1381)
- In board meetings, answers should start with only yes, no, "I don't know," or a number — admitting "I don't know" builds more credibility than false confidence [building-your-board-glenn-kelman @ 26:15](https://youtu.be/MdLgVgJkOlQ?t=1575)
- Kelman avoids making decisions on the spot in board meetings, instead saying he'll follow up in a few days so he has space to properly weigh the board's input [building-your-board-glenn-kelman @ 20:57](https://youtu.be/MdLgVgJkOlQ?t=1257)
