# Design for Startups by Garry Tan (Part 1) — Compiled Truth

Garry Tan argues design is not decoration but the discipline of making things that work well and delight users, and walks founders through the product/interaction/visual design process using Posterous as a running example.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Design is defined simply as creating things for users that work well and delight them, not merely how something looks [design-for-startups-by-garry-tan-part-1 @ 09:45](https://youtu.be/9urYWGx2uNk?t=585)
- Good design is as little design as possible, echoing Dieter Rams' minimalism as a guiding principle [design-for-startups-by-garry-tan-part-1 @ 11:36](https://youtu.be/9urYWGx2uNk?t=696)
- Delight is the "sizzle" and working well is the "steak" — founders must not let novelty (form) crowd out function [design-for-startups-by-garry-tan-part-1 @ 13:20](https://youtu.be/9urYWGx2uNk?t=800)
- Form should follow function, and empathy for the user's problem is the foundation of good design decisions [design-for-startups-by-garry-tan-part-1 @ 15:47](https://youtu.be/9urYWGx2uNk?t=947)
- Building detailed user personas (e.g. Posterous's "David the dad," "Grace the grandma") clarifies what features actually matter for different technical comfort levels [design-for-startups-by-garry-tan-part-1 @ 26:44](https://youtu.be/9urYWGx2uNk?t=1604)
- Prioritizing features as P0/P1/P2/P3 forces realistic scope decisions and prevents shipping broken, unprioritized products [design-for-startups-by-garry-tan-part-1 @ 33:13](https://youtu.be/9urYWGx2uNk?t=1993)
- Homepage and product copy should use direct command language rather than passive voice, which only large companies can get away with [design-for-startups-by-garry-tan-part-1 @ 44:34](https://youtu.be/9urYWGx2uNk?t=2674)
- Removing the "confirm password" field from a signup flow increased conversion by as much as 50% by cutting cognitive load [design-for-startups-by-garry-tan-part-1 @ 47:13](https://youtu.be/9urYWGx2uNk?t=2833)
- Founders should steal well-known interaction design patterns instead of trying to be novel, since novelty adds friction [design-for-startups-by-garry-tan-part-1 @ 48:48](https://youtu.be/9urYWGx2uNk?t=2928)
- Contrast (bold, color, size) is the primary visual design tool for signaling importance, testable with the "squint test" [design-for-startups-by-garry-tan-part-1 @ 54:08](https://youtu.be/9urYWGx2uNk?t=3248)
- Usability testing and treating customer support as a design feedback loop are essential, under-appreciated parts of the product process [design-for-startups-by-garry-tan-part-1 @ 1:03:05](https://youtu.be/9urYWGx2uNk?t=3785)
