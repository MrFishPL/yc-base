# Adora Cheung - How to Set KPIs and Goals — Compiled Truth

Adora Cheung explains how early-stage founders should pick a single primary metric to bet the company on, set weekly growth goals around it, and pair it with a small set of secondary metrics.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- You should pick only one primary metric — the one you'd be willing to bet the whole company on — to keep focus simple [adora-cheung-how-to-set-kpis-and-goals @ 01:37](https://youtu.be/lL6GdUHIBsM?t=97)
- A good primary metric must quantify real value delivered, capture recurring/enduring value, be a lagging indicator, and be usable as a fast feedback mechanism [adora-cheung-how-to-set-kpis-and-goals @ 02:15](https://youtu.be/lL6GdUHIBsM?t=135)
- Revenue, especially monthly recurring revenue, is the best primary metric because it proves people truly value what you're making [adora-cheung-how-to-set-kpis-and-goals @ 02:35](https://youtu.be/lL6GdUHIBsM?t=155)
- Metrics like email signups are traps because they're easy to move but are leading, not lagging, indicators of real value delivered [adora-cheung-how-to-set-kpis-and-goals @ 03:42](https://youtu.be/lL6GdUHIBsM?t=222)
- Active users is a reasonable primary metric only for ad-based businesses needing scale before monetization, or for strong network-effect marketplaces [adora-cheung-how-to-set-kpis-and-goals @ 06:59](https://youtu.be/lL6GdUHIBsM?t=419)
- Two-sided marketplaces should pick a metric that represents value to both sides, such as nights booked for Airbnb or weekly trips for Uber [adora-cheung-how-to-set-kpis-and-goals @ 08:38](https://youtu.be/lL6GdUHIBsM?t=518)
- Pair the primary metric with three to five secondary metrics for a fuller view of company health, without trying to track everything [adora-cheung-how-to-set-kpis-and-goals @ 11:38](https://youtu.be/lL6GdUHIBsM?t=698)
- Looking at recent YC demo day startups, good early growth rates clustered around 5-10% week-over-week (20-50% month-over-month) [adora-cheung-how-to-set-kpis-and-goals @ 15:35](https://youtu.be/lL6GdUHIBsM?t=935)
- Founders should favor organic over paid growth early on, treating paid acquisition in the early days as "cheating growth" [adora-cheung-how-to-set-kpis-and-goals @ 19:09](https://youtu.be/lL6GdUHIBsM?t=1149)
- Startups should set exponential growth goals rather than linear ones, since startup equals growth [adora-cheung-how-to-set-kpis-and-goals @ 19:29](https://youtu.be/lL6GdUHIBsM?t=1169)
