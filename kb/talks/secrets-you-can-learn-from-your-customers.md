# Secrets You Can Learn From Your Customers — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell argue that genuinely caring about and spending time with early customers — not just data or surveys — is the fastest way for founders to learn what they don't yet know about their problem and solution.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Founders start with overconfident foolishness about having the problem and solution figured out, and only truly learn once they're committed enough that quitting isn't an option [secrets-you-can-learn-from-your-customers @ 00:56](https://youtu.be/IdwYMfM2QMs?t=56)
- Airbnb's founders personally went to hosts' homes to take better photos even knowing it wouldn't scale and might not even help their own business, and that act of care built trust with users [secrets-you-can-learn-from-your-customers @ 01:50](https://youtu.be/IdwYMfM2QMs?t=110)
- By genuinely caring enough to sit down for coffee with a single host, Airbnb's Joe Gebbia learned things (like the host's ten years of notes on hosting) that could never have surfaced from casually talking to a hundred hosts [secrets-you-can-learn-from-your-customers @ 03:53](https://youtu.be/IdwYMfM2QMs?t=233)
- Airbnb's founders remember the names and life stories of their first customers rather than treating early growth as an anonymous numbers game of ads and landing pages [secrets-you-can-learn-from-your-customers @ 04:33](https://youtu.be/IdwYMfM2QMs?t=273)
- Brex's founders pivoted after directly asking people around them at YC whether they had trouble getting a credit card, discovering an underserved group (non-Americans, under-25s) being completely shut out by existing products [secrets-you-can-learn-from-your-customers @ 06:05](https://youtu.be/IdwYMfM2QMs?t=365)
- Because Brex's founders were themselves foreign founders facing these problems, they could be their own guinea pigs, giving them insight a hired PM at a big company could never replicate [secrets-you-can-learn-from-your-customers @ 06:40](https://youtu.be/IdwYMfM2QMs?t=400)
- At Justin.tv/Twitch, the relationship with streamers only improved once Emmett and Kevin started calling individual streamers one-on-one instead of mass-emailing them [secrets-you-can-learn-from-your-customers @ 09:10](https://youtu.be/IdwYMfM2QMs?t=550)
- Simple streamer requests like higher stream bitrate were trivial to implement once asked, showing that founders often never bothered to ask users what they actually needed [secrets-you-can-learn-from-your-customers @ 10:26](https://youtu.be/IdwYMfM2QMs?t=626)
- Personally calling streamers and even hand-mailing them small revenue-share checks led to Twitch's core insight: helping streamers make money so they could quit their jobs would produce content millions want to watch [secrets-you-can-learn-from-your-customers @ 12:17](https://youtu.be/IdwYMfM2QMs?t=737)
- Adding layers of people (surveys, reports, data science teams) between founders and customers is the opposite of what drives fast learning — caring personally and directly is what accelerates it [secrets-you-can-learn-from-your-customers @ 13:55](https://youtu.be/IdwYMfM2QMs?t=835)
