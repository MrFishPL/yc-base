# How AI Is Changing Enterprise — Compiled Truth

Box CEO Aaron Levie argues that as model intelligence gets commoditized and converges toward the cost of the underlying GPU, the durable value for AI startups shifts entirely to the software layer wrapped around the model — the workflow, proprietary data, and business logic that actually delivers an outcome to the customer.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Enterprises don't want a model, they want an outcome (e.g., customer support resolved, documents processed into a workflow), so the model should be abstracted away from the customer value proposition as much as possible [how-ai-is-changing-enterprise @ 03:09](https://youtu.be/aIKfA3gIXwo?t=189)
- A useful heuristic: the less software sits on top of the raw tokens, the higher the risk of commoditization or competition; the more software wraps the model, the stickier and more defensible the business [how-ai-is-changing-enterprise @ 17:17](https://youtu.be/aIKfA3gIXwo?t=1037)
- Because models are becoming "directionally fungible," pricing will converge the way cloud storage pricing converged across hyperscalers, so competitive advantage comes from data and workflows built on top, not which model is used [how-ai-is-changing-enterprise @ 14:47](https://youtu.be/aIKfA3gIXwo?t=887)
- A B2B AI startup scaled to $12M revenue in a year, kept switching underlying models without customers caring, and grew gross margin from ~30% to ~80% as token costs fell [how-ai-is-changing-enterprise @ 15:21](https://youtu.be/aIKfA3gIXwo?t=921)
- New usage-based and outcome-based pricing models (pay per lead, pay per qualified lead, pay per unit of compute) are emerging because AI makes capacity fully elastic compared to labor-based service businesses [how-ai-is-changing-enterprise @ 17:50](https://youtu.be/aIKfA3gIXwo?t=1070)
- Enterprises should split AI initiatives into "context" (necessary but non-differentiating, e.g., HR/ERP systems, best bought from vendors) versus "core" (the proprietary value proposition worth building in-house), and confusing the two wastes resources [how-ai-is-changing-enterprise @ 28:27](https://youtu.be/aIKfA3gIXwo?t=1707)
- Unlike cloud adoption, which was purely an efficiency story, AI adoption is a competitive necessity — companies that aren't "AI first" won't be able to hire AI-native talent or match competitors' output [how-ai-is-changing-enterprise @ 24:04](https://youtu.be/aIKfA3gIXwo?t=1444)
- SaaS TAMs ended up roughly 10x larger than on-prem software TAMs because lowering cost and friction let far more companies buy (e.g., Salesforce vs. Siebel); Levie expects AI to expand TAM similarly since it lets companies automate work they never previously staffed for at all [how-ai-is-changing-enterprise @ 39:56](https://youtu.be/aIKfA3gIXwo?t=2396)
- AI spend is largely additive rather than zero-sum against labor spend, since most companies aren't currently paying anyone to do most of the tasks AI newly makes possible (e.g., translating all ads, reviewing all contracts) [how-ai-is-changing-enterprise @ 43:12](https://youtu.be/aIKfA3gIXwo?t=2592)
- Competitive markets force companies to reinvest AI-driven efficiency gains into growth (hiring, product, sales) rather than just pocketing higher margins, which is why automation tends to look like an upgrade to how people work rather than a net loss of jobs [how-ai-is-changing-enterprise @ 45:49](https://youtu.be/aIKfA3gIXwo?t=2749)
