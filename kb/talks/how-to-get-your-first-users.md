# How To Get Your First Users — Compiled Truth

Finding a startup's first users is framed as a search problem, not a persuasion problem: you're looking for the rare early adopters and burning-need sufferers who will try an unproven "minimum evolvable product" and steer its evolution.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Most people are not early adopters, so almost no one wants to be a startup's first paying customer — you need to find the few who are willing [how-to-get-your-first-users @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/0kARDVL2nZg?t=0)
- Instead of a minimum viable product, founders should build a "minimum evolvable product" that only needs to survive contact with a tiny group of early users [how-to-get-your-first-users @ 00:17](https://youtu.be/0kARDVL2nZg?t=17)
- Finding first users is a search problem, not a persuasion problem: look for people who love trying new things or who have a burning problem you solve [how-to-get-your-first-users @ 01:11](https://youtu.be/0kARDVL2nZg?t=71)
- Charge real money early because early adopters are rarely price sensitive, and paying customers give sharper, more useful feedback than free users [how-to-get-your-first-users @ 01:26](https://youtu.be/0kARDVL2nZg?t=86)
- Use targeted, personal outreach like cold emails rather than broad-reach tactics like billboards to find these specific early users [how-to-get-your-first-users @ 01:45](https://youtu.be/0kARDVL2nZg?t=105)
- Launch early to create a wide surface area for early adopters to find you, since in the early days you don't yet know who they are [how-to-get-your-first-users @ 02:02](https://youtu.be/0kARDVL2nZg?t=122)
- Study early users like an anthropologist discovering a hidden civilization, and run constant experiments on pricing, landing pages, onboarding, and features without fearing churn [how-to-get-your-first-users @ 02:18](https://youtu.be/0kARDVL2nZg?t=138)
- In the AI era, consumer apps struggle more because ad revenue often doesn't cover AI costs and subscriptions must fit small personal budgets, pushing many AI founders toward prosumers or businesses instead [how-to-get-your-first-users @ 03:08](https://youtu.be/0kARDVL2nZg?t=188)
- Using Tesla's Roadster as a case study, a startup's early product choices are path-dependent on what its early adopters actually wanted, shaping the product for years afterward [how-to-get-your-first-users @ 04:03](https://youtu.be/0kARDVL2nZg?t=243)
- The product should be built as a "minimum evolvable product" — simple enough to survive contact with early users but able to adapt fast based on the market pressure they apply [how-to-get-your-first-users @ 05:02](https://youtu.be/0kARDVL2nZg?t=302)
