# Critiquing Startup Websites With Instacart's First Designer — Compiled Truth

In this inaugural "Design Review" episode, former Instacart first product designer Zane Ali joins a YC host to critique the landing pages of three W22 startups, focusing on headline clarity, above-the-fold noise, trust signals, and calls to action.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Discount pop-ups shown before a visitor knows what a company does can convert well but fatigue users, since people close the pop-up just to figure out what the product is first [critiquing-startup-websites-with-instacart-s-first-designer @ 01:01](https://youtu.be/gok9BZLMxDs?t=61)
- Sending traffic to dedicated landing pages for each specific service (e.g. a surf-lessons page instead of the generic homepage) dramatically raises conversion because visitors quickly confirm "is this for me" [critiquing-startup-websites-with-instacart-s-first-designer @ 03:32](https://youtu.be/gok9BZLMxDs?t=212)
- A real customer testimonial video of someone casually describing their experience is more persuasive than blocks of text, since most visitors won't read long paragraphs [critiquing-startup-websites-with-instacart-s-first-designer @ 07:18](https://youtu.be/gok9BZLMxDs?t=438)
- The best way to test a headline is to ask existing users how they'd describe the product in their own words, then put those exact words in the headline, since your users likely think like your future target users [critiquing-startup-websites-with-instacart-s-first-designer @ 09:36](https://youtu.be/gok9BZLMxDs?t=576)
- Cramming too many competing elements above the fold (headline, sub-headline, multiple buttons, ratings, screenshots) dilutes focus; founders should pick the single main action they want users to take and push everything else below the fold [critiquing-startup-websites-with-instacart-s-first-designer @ 10:36](https://youtu.be/gok9BZLMxDs?t=636)
- Tiny, unreadable product screenshots fail to build confidence; founders are too close to their own product to realize a first-time visitor can't parse an unlabeled screenshot [critiquing-startup-websites-with-instacart-s-first-designer @ 12:08](https://youtu.be/gok9BZLMxDs?t=728)
- Copy that lists features (e.g. "generate custom API responses") should instead be reframed around the painful problem it solves, which resonates more with visitors than a feature label [critiquing-startup-websites-with-instacart-s-first-designer @ 12:51](https://youtu.be/gok9BZLMxDs?t=771)
- A vague, jargon-heavy or "visionary" headline (e.g. "instant sustainability with automated storytelling") fails because most visitors skim only headlines, so the core value must be understandable without reading the supporting paragraph [critiquing-startup-websites-with-instacart-s-first-designer @ 18:45](https://youtu.be/gok9BZLMxDs?t=1125)
- Trust signals like "backed by Y Combinator," known partner/brand logos, team photos, and customer testimonials help visitors believe real people are behind the product and that they won't be the first customer [critiquing-startup-websites-with-instacart-s-first-designer @ 20:26](https://youtu.be/gok9BZLMxDs?t=1226)
