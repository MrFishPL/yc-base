# YC SUS: Kat Mañalac and Eric Migicovsky discuss Week 2 SUS Lectures — Compiled Truth

Kat Mañalac and Eric Migicovsky field live Startup School founder questions on launching, talking to users, and pricing, pushing founders to launch fast, treat user interviews as two distinct groups, and not undercharge enterprise customers.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Don't overinvest in any single launch channel — spend a few hours to a couple days on messaging, not weeks, since each launch only reaches a tiny fraction of the world so you should keep launching repeatedly [yc-sus-kat-ma-alac-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-week-2-sus-le @ 01:49](https://youtu.be/OK6sdErKWW0?t=109)
- Waitlist call-to-actions should be as short and jargon-free as possible ("sign up for our newsletter") since terms like alpha/beta mean nothing to users [yc-sus-kat-ma-alac-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-week-2-sus-le @ 03:24](https://youtu.be/OK6sdErKWW0?t=204)
- Separate user interviews into two groups — non-users (wide-open questions about their problem and existing solutions) and active users (feedback on the product) — and avoid mixing incentives/coupons into problem-discovery interviews [yc-sus-kat-ma-alac-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-week-2-sus-le @ 05:15](https://youtu.be/OK6sdErKWW0?t=315)
- Getting influencers or bloggers to cover you is a numbers game — expect roughly a 1-in-50 response rate, follow up multiple times, and aim at smaller channels like Facebook groups and subreddits, not just top-tier influencers [yc-sus-kat-ma-alac-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-week-2-sus-le @ 08:16](https://youtu.be/OK6sdErKWW0?t=496)
- For geo-targeted launches, do manual, unscalable groundwork like Lugg's founders physically approaching people outside IKEA to get the first users [yc-sus-kat-ma-alac-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-week-2-sus-le @ 11:39](https://youtu.be/OK6sdErKWW0?t=699)
- Never save your "real" launch for demo day — launch as early as possible so you already have traction and iteration by the time you present [yc-sus-kat-ma-alac-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-week-2-sus-le @ 17:41](https://youtu.be/OK6sdErKWW0?t=1061)
- Enterprise founders almost always underprice; since the alternative to your product is the customer's own engineering team building and maintaining a solution, don't be afraid to ask for more [yc-sus-kat-ma-alac-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-week-2-sus-le @ 23:01](https://youtu.be/OK6sdErKWW0?t=1381)
- When the paying customer differs from the end user (common in B2B/enterprise), figure out who controls the budget and whether they already pay for a solution before treating them as your first target users [yc-sus-kat-ma-alac-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-week-2-sus-le @ 24:26](https://youtu.be/OK6sdErKWW0?t=1466)
- Avoid "whale hunting" (chasing giants like Walmart) as an early customer strategy — go after smaller customers who can make a fast buying decision so you get feedback quickly [yc-sus-kat-ma-alac-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-week-2-sus-le @ 47:16](https://youtu.be/OK6sdErKWW0?t=2836)
- As CEO, personally emailing users from your own Gmail to re-engage them is a classic "do things that don't scale" tactic that a big company's CEO would never do [yc-sus-kat-ma-alac-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-week-2-sus-le @ 44:27](https://youtu.be/OK6sdErKWW0?t=2667)
