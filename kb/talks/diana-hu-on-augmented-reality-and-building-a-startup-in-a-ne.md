# Diana Hu on Augmented Reality and Building a Startup in a New Market — Compiled Truth

Diana Hu (Escher Reality, acquired by Niantic) discusses how to strategize, build, and fundraise for a startup in a nascent technology market like AR, plus lessons from being acquired and from her path as an immigrant founder.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Technology cycles have an "installation" phase (infrastructure, tooling, abstractions get built) followed by a "deployment" phase (explosion of applications), and AR is still in its installation phase [diana-hu-on-augmented-reality-and-building-a-startup-in-a-ne @ 04:30](https://youtu.be/USyurlvZr68?t=270)
- Timing is a factor founders can't fully control but can recognize: AR's moment arrived by riding on mobile's supply chain plus compounding trends like Moore's law, Koomey's law (power efficiency), and Edholm's law (bandwidth growth) [diana-hu-on-augmented-reality-and-building-a-startup-in-a-ne @ 08:50](https://youtu.be/USyurlvZr68?t=530)
- In an emerging, unproven market, betting on installation-phase, core-technology/tooling companies has better odds right now than betting on end-user applications, since the killer app for AR is still undetermined [diana-hu-on-augmented-reality-and-building-a-startup-in-a-ne @ 15:23](https://youtu.be/USyurlvZr68?t=923)
- Building a working, provable technology demo (they built AR Pong) rather than chasing paying customers first was how Escher Reality created buzz and got investor attention, differing from advice for more mature-market startups [diana-hu-on-augmented-reality-and-building-a-startup-in-a-ne @ 18:53](https://youtu.be/USyurlvZr68?t=1133)
- In a new/unproven space, look for independent-thinking investors willing to back the vision themselves, since many investors simply follow others once one commits [diana-hu-on-augmented-reality-and-building-a-startup-in-a-ne @ 20:16](https://youtu.be/USyurlvZr68?t=1216)
- After being acquired, staying motivated required that the acquiring company (Niantic) continued building toward the same vision, letting the team keep real ownership and freedom [diana-hu-on-augmented-reality-and-building-a-startup-in-a-ne @ 22:12](https://youtu.be/USyurlvZr68?t=1332)
- Coming from an immigrant background, she argues the US's relative abundance and stability psychologically frees people to take entrepreneurial risks they might not take elsewhere [diana-hu-on-augmented-reality-and-building-a-startup-in-a-ne @ 31:08](https://youtu.be/USyurlvZr68?t=1868)
- YC's core value in the fragile early stage is enforcing focus and high-leverage, non-scalable activities (like scrappy hand-built demos) that larger, better-resourced competitors won't bother doing [diana-hu-on-augmented-reality-and-building-a-startup-in-a-ne @ 33:53](https://youtu.be/USyurlvZr68?t=2033)
