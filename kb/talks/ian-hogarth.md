# Ian Hogarth — Compiled Truth

Songkick's co-founder argues that startup success in consolidated industries requires partnering with incumbents, and that three interdependent engines — gratification, growth, and economics — determine whether a company becomes a "unicorn."

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- In highly consolidated industries like music and entertainment, a startup must ask why the industry would embrace it, since rights holders control the market and waiting for their buy-in is often the path to success [ian-hogarth @ 11:15](https://youtu.be/GYtBR-APb7E?t=675)
- The core startup formula is "unicorn-ness" = gratification engine ^ growth engine ^ economic engine, and failing any one engine reduces the outcome by an order of magnitude [ian-hogarth @ 14:02](https://youtu.be/GYtBR-APb7E?t=842)
- Songkick's breakthrough on the gratification engine came from user surveys that revealed personalized concert alerts were the one truly compelling feature, after piling on features that added little usage [ian-hogarth @ 15:35](https://youtu.be/GYtBR-APb7E?t=935)
- Once something works, founders should shift from the 80/20 MVP mindset to the "20/80": grinding through the last 20% of value (in Songkick's case, comprehensive trusted concert data) even though it takes 80% of the effort [ian-hogarth @ 16:48](https://youtu.be/GYtBR-APb7E?t=1008)
- Consumer growth comes mainly through four channels — word of mouth/viral, paid acquisition, SEO, and API/widget distribution — and top companies like Airbnb combine several rather than relying on one [ian-hogarth @ 18:15](https://youtu.be/GYtBR-APb7E?t=1095)
- Songkick grew by becoming the canonical page for a concert or tour, similar to Yelp for restaurants or IMDb for movies, which unlocked SEO, social referrals, and API partnerships [ian-hogarth @ 19:29](https://youtu.be/GYtBR-APb7E?t=1169)
- Songkick's economic engine (affiliate cuts from ticket sales) got the company to a multi-million dollar revenue run rate but not the hundreds of millions needed to reach unicorn status [ian-hogarth @ 20:42](https://youtu.be/GYtBR-APb7E?t=1242)
- "Survival is a growth strategy" — pushing through bleak periods lets a company benefit from platform shifts (e.g., mobile) that can suddenly and dramatically accelerate growth [ian-hogarth @ 26:53](https://youtu.be/GYtBR-APb7E?t=1613)
- Founders should write down a "five whys" analysis of their motivation early on so they have something concrete to refer back to during the hardest moments [ian-hogarth @ 29:10](https://youtu.be/GYtBR-APb7E?t=1750)
- Starting a company with co-founders you can genuinely count on — built on real friendship, not just competence — is essential for surviving the hardest stretches [ian-hogarth @ 30:32](https://youtu.be/GYtBR-APb7E?t=1832)
