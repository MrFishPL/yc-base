# Building for the Enterprise with Aaron Levie (How to Start a Startup 2014: Lecture 12) — Compiled Truth

Box's CEO argues that cloud, mobile, and user-led IT have made this "the most magical time" to start an enterprise software company, and lays out patterns — start narrow, find asymmetries incumbents can't copy, keep consumer-grade product DNA — for building one.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Box's early crisis: it built more functionality than consumers needed and less security/capability than enterprises needed, leaving it stuck between two half-built products [building-for-the-enterprise-with-aaron-levie-how-to-start-a- @ 09:57](https://youtu.be/Kd3CLs-vLtw?t=597)
- Enterprise IT spend ($3.7 trillion/year) dwarfs the combined consumer app and digital ad markets (~$170 billion), which reframed enterprise as the much larger opportunity despite looking less "fun" [building-for-the-enterprise-with-aaron-levie-how-to-start-a- @ 12:52](https://youtu.be/Kd3CLs-vLtw?t=772)
- The five years before this talk changed everything about enterprise software — cloud infrastructure, standardized platforms, global reach, and mobile-driven user-led IT — making it an unusually good moment to start an enterprise company [building-for-the-enterprise-with-aaron-levie-how-to-start-a- @ 20:10](https://youtu.be/Kd3CLs-vLtw?t=1210)
- The shift from customized, on-premise software to standardized, open platforms means startups should build APIs for customization rather than baking custom integrations into the core product [building-for-the-enterprise-with-aaron-levie-how-to-start-a- @ 22:42](https://youtu.be/Kd3CLs-vLtw?t=1362)
- Mobile has flipped IT from an incumbent-favoring, IT-led model to a user-led one, where employees bring in tools themselves and the company later sells up to the enterprise for security and scale [building-for-the-enterprise-with-aaron-levie-how-to-start-a- @ 24:24](https://youtu.be/Kd3CLs-vLtw?t=1464)
- Start intentionally small: find a narrow wedge that slips between existing products' gaps (like ZenPayroll attacking just the payroll pain point) and expand up-market or into new use cases over time [building-for-the-enterprise-with-aaron-levie-how-to-start-a- @ 36:08](https://youtu.be/Kd3CLs-vLtw?t=2168)
- Look for asymmetries — build things incumbents can't or won't do, such as staying platform-agnostic when competitors are locked into vertically-integrated suites [building-for-the-enterprise-with-aaron-levie-how-to-start-a- @ 38:36](https://youtu.be/Kd3CLs-vLtw?t=2316)
- Zenefits found an economic asymmetry incumbents couldn't copy: it monetized via insurance-company commissions instead of charging the small-business customer directly [building-for-the-enterprise-with-aaron-levie-how-to-start-a- @ 40:17](https://youtu.be/Kd3CLs-vLtw?t=2417)
- Find customers "living/working in the future" — bleeding-edge adopters like construction and farming firms using drones (Skycatch) — and build with them to surface what's missing [building-for-the-enterprise-with-aaron-levie-how-to-start-a- @ 41:17](https://youtu.be/Kd3CLs-vLtw?t=2477)
- Listen to customer problems but don't build literally what they ask for — your job is to distill many complex requests into the simplest solution [building-for-the-enterprise-with-aaron-levie-how-to-start-a- @ 43:19](https://youtu.be/Kd3CLs-vLtw?t=2599)
- A great product should sell itself, but that doesn't eliminate the need for consultative salespeople to help customers navigate deployment and the competitive landscape [building-for-the-enterprise-with-aaron-levie-how-to-start-a- @ 44:41](https://youtu.be/Kd3CLs-vLtw?t=2681)
