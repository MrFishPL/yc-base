# Replit's CEO On The Only Two Jobs Left In The Company Of The Future — Compiled Truth

Amjad Masad describes how Replit evolved from a dev tool into a natural-language app builder for non-developers, and argues the future company is made almost entirely of builders and sales/evangelist people as AI abstracts away coding itself.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Replit's ambition is that anyone who can read and write can go from an idea to a deployed, scalable app without touching any technical details, having solved the dev environment, deployment, and now the coding itself via natural language [replit-s-ceo-on-the-only-two-jobs-left-in-the-company-of-the @ 00:49](https://youtu.be/kMYeTRqzAfc?t=49)
- The people getting the most value are not classic engineers but tech-adjacent users -- product managers, designers, and entrepreneurs -- who don't want to manage environment setup or deployment [replit-s-ceo-on-the-only-two-jobs-left-in-the-company-of-the @ 04:15](https://youtu.be/kMYeTRqzAfc?t=255)
- A physical therapist with no coding background built a sophisticated body-tracking health app on Replit after spending hundreds of thousands of dollars offshoring development unsuccessfully, illustrating that domain experts can now build their own products [replit-s-ceo-on-the-only-two-jobs-left-in-the-company-of-the @ 06:45](https://youtu.be/kMYeTRqzAfc?t=405)
- Enterprise clients like Whoop report the number of ideas they can try has grown by an order of magnitude (from ~5 tried out of 100 to ~50) because non-engineers can now build software themselves [replit-s-ceo-on-the-only-two-jobs-left-in-the-company-of-the @ 09:19](https://youtu.be/kMYeTRqzAfc?t=559)
- Go-to-market mirrors how developer tools once spread inside companies via bottom-up product-led growth, but now product managers, designers, and ops people are the ones bringing software in, followed by sales-assisted evangelism and hackathons to build internal champions [replit-s-ceo-on-the-only-two-jobs-left-in-the-company-of-the @ 11:14](https://youtu.be/kMYeTRqzAfc?t=674)
- Replit's ideal champion has an entrepreneurial, resourceful founder mindset -- someone who won't get blocked and will independently figure out what to learn or integrate next [replit-s-ceo-on-the-only-two-jobs-left-in-the-company-of-the @ 18:37](https://youtu.be/kMYeTRqzAfc?t=1117)
- Replit ships a new agent version roughly every 6 months by deliberately aligning its roadmap to predicted step-changes in AI capability, such as building long-running background containers ahead of true model autonomy [replit-s-ceo-on-the-only-two-jobs-left-in-the-company-of-the @ 24:00](https://youtu.be/kMYeTRqzAfc?t=1440)
- Masad believes the industry is headed to a "post-prompting world" where users give agents high-level goals rather than detailed prompts, and expects a future version could be told to "build me a SaaS company and try to market it" [replit-s-ceo-on-the-only-two-jobs-left-in-the-company-of-the @ 28:14](https://youtu.be/kMYeTRqzAfc?t=1694)
- Computer-use models remain surprisingly underdeveloped compared to language models, so coding agents became a workaround -- scripting and calling APIs -- for tasks that would otherwise require clicking through legacy software [replit-s-ceo-on-the-only-two-jobs-left-in-the-company-of-the @ 32:07](https://youtu.be/kMYeTRqzAfc?t=1927)
- Masad frames the company of the future as made of builders and salespeople: sales becomes evangelism/education (one of the more defensible jobs since people trust humans), while nearly everyone acts as a generalist founder finding problems and deputizing agents to solve them [replit-s-ceo-on-the-only-two-jobs-left-in-the-company-of-the @ 35:04](https://youtu.be/kMYeTRqzAfc?t=2104)
