# What Is ZIRP And How Did It Poison Startups? — Compiled Truth

Dalton and Michael explain the zero interest rate phenomenon (ZIRP) and argue that cheap capital flooded venture capital, distorted valuations, and made founders confuse abundant money with real product-market fit.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- ZIRP is the period when the Federal Reserve's interest rate was zero (some European banks even had negative rates), forcing banks and investors to chase yield anywhere they could find it, including venture capital [what-is-zirp-and-how-did-it-poison-startups @ 00:46](https://youtu.be/iMo5-4h_XwE?t=46)
- New entrants who had only done public-market or hedge-fund investing applied public-company valuation rubrics to startups making a fraction of the revenue, which the hosts call fundamentally irrational [what-is-zirp-and-how-did-it-poison-startups @ 04:31](https://youtu.be/iMo5-4h_XwE?t=271)
- When money is the only variable behind a company's success, it doesn't actually help, because incumbents already have more money than any startup, and giving founders too much money makes them stop innovating and start spending instead [what-is-zirp-and-how-did-it-poison-startups @ 06:45](https://youtu.be/iMo5-4h_XwE?t=405)
- Unicorns went from a rare designation to a weekly occurrence during ZIRP as investors handed out billion-dollar valuations casually [what-is-zirp-and-how-did-it-poison-startups @ 07:41](https://youtu.be/iMo5-4h_XwE?t=461)
- A revenue-to-valuation multiple sanity check (e.g. a company with $3M revenue raising at a $1B valuation, a 350x multiple) revealed how disconnected some ZIRP-era valuations were from fundamentals [what-is-zirp-and-how-did-it-poison-startups @ 09:23](https://youtu.be/iMo5-4h_XwE?t=563)
- YC was advising founders during this period that the music would stop and to make smart moves to prepare for a coming downturn, even though many founders didn't believe it [what-is-zirp-and-how-did-it-poison-startups @ 10:06](https://youtu.be/iMo5-4h_XwE?t=606)
- Cheap capital gave lending-style startups false "product-market fit" signals, since money itself is easy to get people to want, but when interest rates rose their cost of capital rose too and many of these businesses became unsustainable [what-is-zirp-and-how-did-it-poison-startups @ 11:04](https://youtu.be/iMo5-4h_XwE?t=664)
- Inflated valuations distorted founder expectations broadly, with YC founders starting to expect $10M seed rounds simply because peers had raised that much [what-is-zirp-and-how-did-it-poison-startups @ 12:05](https://youtu.be/iMo5-4h_XwE?t=725)
- Founders who built businesses around the ZIRP phenomenon rather than sustainable fundamentals faced "platform risk on the Fed" once conditions normalized and capital dried up [what-is-zirp-and-how-did-it-poison-startups @ 15:32](https://youtu.be/iMo5-4h_XwE?t=932)
- The right founder posture is to balance optimism with realism in any economic climate, and ideally build a business that works in bad conditions so it's even better in good ones [what-is-zirp-and-how-did-it-poison-startups @ 16:16](https://youtu.be/iMo5-4h_XwE?t=976)
