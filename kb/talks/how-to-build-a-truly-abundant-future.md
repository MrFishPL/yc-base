# How To Build A Truly Abundant Future — Compiled Truth

Derek Thompson, co-author of "Abundance," argues that a mix of hard-tech breakthroughs and bold, experimental public policy — not just entrepreneurs alone — is needed to build a future of energy, housing, and health abundance, and urges founders to work on ambitious, unfashionable problems.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The abundance agenda envisions 2050 built on housing abundance and clean energy superabundance (solar, wind, geothermal, nuclear) enabling energy-intensive technologies like vertical farming, desalination, and carbon removal [how-to-build-a-truly-abundant-future @ 01:04](https://youtu.be/VIkphkYlkaQ?t=64)
- Government has lost "speed" as a virtue, illustrated by California's $33B high-speed rail that still doesn't exist and a $42B rural broadband program bogged down by a 14-step bureaucratic process where only 3 of 56 applicants finished [how-to-build-a-truly-abundant-future @ 03:20](https://youtu.be/VIkphkYlkaQ?t=200)
- Environmental and legal reforms from the 1960s-70s that stopped the "growth machine" excesses of mid-century building have become "the disease of the 2020s," now blocking dense housing, solar/wind, and nuclear power [how-to-build-a-truly-abundant-future @ 09:39](https://youtu.be/VIkphkYlkaQ?t=579)
- Penicillin was discovered in 1928 but sat nearly unused for 13 years until a concerted WWII-era federal effort scaled its production — showing that breakthroughs can languish without deliberate implementation [how-to-build-a-truly-abundant-future @ 15:05](https://youtu.be/VIkphkYlkaQ?t=905)
- The NIH needs a "founder mode" moment: it funds older scientists over younger ones despite young people historically driving paradigm shifts, and scientists now spend 40% of their time on grant paperwork [how-to-build-a-truly-abundant-future @ 17:09](https://youtu.be/VIkphkYlkaQ?t=1029)
- Proposed reforms include "golden tickets" letting one person fund an idea without peer review, and giving scientists 10-15 years of unconditional funding instead of repeated grant cycles [how-to-build-a-truly-abundant-future @ 17:30](https://youtu.be/VIkphkYlkaQ?t=1050)
- "Pull funding" (e.g., Operation Warp Speed's advance market commitments, which cut vaccine development from ~10 years to 10 months) is a more effective innovation lever than upfront subsidies because it rewards outcomes and pulls in private capital [how-to-build-a-truly-abundant-future @ 29:38](https://youtu.be/VIkphkYlkaQ?t=1778)
- LLMs could be used to build a "map of meaning" across every FDA-approved drug's molecular effects, letting researchers query which existing drugs might address a newly identified biological problem [how-to-build-a-truly-abundant-future @ 27:01](https://youtu.be/VIkphkYlkaQ?t=1621)
- His advice to founders: don't aim to be part of "the mass" as consumers or producers — spend scarce time on niche, individually meaningful, important problems in hard tech, robotics, and energy [how-to-build-a-truly-abundant-future @ 32:18](https://youtu.be/VIkphkYlkaQ?t=1938)
