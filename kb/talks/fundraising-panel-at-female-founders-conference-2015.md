# Fundraising Panel at Female Founders Conference 2015 — Compiled Truth

Danielle Morrill (Mattermark) opens with a raw, unpolished account of three years of near-continuous fundraising before a panel of YC founders shares their own hard-won lessons on raising seed and Series A money.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Your first round is often easiest when it comes from people who already know and trust you personally, not from "smart money" [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 02:31](https://youtu.be/8eKkwX9q_Lc?t=151)
- When Referly's e-commerce model only supported ~5% margins instead of software-like economics, the founders shut it down, fired the team, and told investors they had no idea what to build next [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 06:15](https://youtu.be/8eKkwX9q_Lc?t=375)
- Product-market fit is largely luck, but the hustle that follows it is not [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 08:59](https://youtu.be/8eKkwX9q_Lc?t=539)
- A rule of thumb from YC: keep raising on notes as long as you don't sell more than 25% of the company in the seed round [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 14:17](https://youtu.be/8eKkwX9q_Lc?t=857)
- Sometimes surviving matters more than avoiding a "party round" with too many small investors and full pro-rata rights [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 13:22](https://youtu.be/8eKkwX9q_Lc?t=802)
- When you get an offer from an investor you believe in, take the money and move forward; if you get no's, assume the next meeting could be a yes [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 15:27](https://youtu.be/8eKkwX9q_Lc?t=927)
- Fundraising is a numbers game: get as many meetings as possible and iterate rapidly through the no's to reach the yes's [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 21:09](https://youtu.be/8eKkwX9q_Lc?t=1269)
- More important than pitch order or investor selection is genuinely believing your idea is good and being able to prove it with traction [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 23:21](https://youtu.be/8eKkwX9q_Lc?t=1401)
- Raising a seed round is like consumer sales (persuade one person in the room and close immediately), while raising a Series A is like enterprise sales (build a relationship so the investor can defend you to their partners) [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 27:46](https://youtu.be/8eKkwX9q_Lc?t=1666)
- YC manufactures fundraising momentum by compressing traction-building into three months that culminates in demo day, giving founders leverage they wouldn't otherwise have [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 25:37](https://youtu.be/8eKkwX9q_Lc?t=1537)
