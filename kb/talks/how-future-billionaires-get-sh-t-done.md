# How Future Billionaires Get Sh*t Done — Compiled Truth

Productive founders separate "maker" time (long uninterrupted blocks for deep work like coding or sales) from "manager" time (meetings, email, to-do lists), aggressively protect their attention from distractions like social media and excessive mentorship-seeking, and commit fully to one path rather than hedging across several.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Paul Graham's "maker's schedule, manager's schedule" post distinguishes how programmers need long uninterrupted blocks versus how managers operate in meeting-driven schedules [how-future-billionaires-get-sh-t-done @ 00:43](https://youtu.be/ephzgxgOjR0?t=43)
- A programmer needs an hour or two just to load the full context of a program into their head, so interrupting them in short increments destroys productivity — a good maker schedule is an uninterrupted eight-hour block [how-future-billionaires-get-sh-t-done @ 02:26](https://youtu.be/ephzgxgOjR0?t=146)
- YC itself is designed around maker time: minimal scheduled events, a hard deadline (demo day), and weekly accountability on what you said you'd accomplish [how-future-billionaires-get-sh-t-done @ 05:05](https://youtu.be/ephzgxgOjR0?t=305)
- In manager mode, work should start from the to-do list rather than being inbox-driven, since letting email and Slack drive your day means other people control your time [how-future-billionaires-get-sh-t-done @ 06:04](https://youtu.be/ephzgxgOjR0?t=364)
- Meetings are wasted if nothing gets written down afterward, forcing a repeat meeting to re-cover the same ground [how-future-billionaires-get-sh-t-done @ 06:30](https://youtu.be/ephzgxgOjR0?t=390)
