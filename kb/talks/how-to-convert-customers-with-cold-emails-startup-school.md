# How To Convert Customers With Cold Emails | Startup School — Compiled Truth

Aaron Epstein (YC group partner, ex-Creative Market founder) lays out a funnel-driven, highly personalized approach to writing cold emails that actually get responses.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A warm intro converts two to three times better than a cold email, so founders should exhaust their network (LinkedIn, alumni networks, former coworkers) before going cold [how-to-convert-customers-with-cold-emails-startup-school @ 00:09](https://youtu.be/7Kh_fpxP1yY?t=9)
- Founders should map their full conversion funnel backwards from the goal (e.g. one new customer) to figure out how many emails must be sent — in Epstein's example, roughly 800 emails to land one customer [how-to-convert-customers-with-cold-emails-startup-school @ 03:17](https://youtu.be/7Kh_fpxP1yY?t=197)
- Emails should be written manually and personalized at first, not automated, so founders learn what works before scaling [how-to-convert-customers-with-cold-emails-startup-school @ 03:38](https://youtu.be/7Kh_fpxP1yY?t=218)
- The highest-leverage way to improve open rates is better targeting — sending 100 targeted emails beats 1,000 untargeted ones [how-to-convert-customers-with-cold-emails-startup-school @ 05:39](https://youtu.be/7Kh_fpxP1yY?t=339)
- Effective cold emails follow seven principles: a single focused goal, sounding human, personalization via "uncommon commonalities," brevity, established credibility, reader-focused framing, and a clear call to action [how-to-convert-customers-with-cold-emails-startup-school @ 09:58](https://youtu.be/7Kh_fpxP1yY?t=598)
- "Uncommon commonalities" — specific, non-obvious shared details like the same college building — build real connection, unlike generic personalization such as swapping in a company name [how-to-convert-customers-with-cold-emails-startup-school @ 14:56](https://youtu.be/7Kh_fpxP1yY?t=896)
- Emails should tell the founder's story as solving a problem they personally felt, and should use the exact language customers use to describe the company rather than the founder's own framing [how-to-convert-customers-with-cold-emails-startup-school @ 18:16](https://youtu.be/7Kh_fpxP1yY?t=1096)
- Real example emails Epstein received show that lack of targeting (e.g. pitching a shipping service to a digital-goods company) can doom even a well-written email, while name-dropping shared alumni context drove a rare reply [how-to-convert-customers-with-cold-emails-startup-school @ 22:34](https://youtu.be/7Kh_fpxP1yY?t=1354)
- Reframing an in-office smoothie subscription pitch from "help us hit our demo day goal" to "free smoothie party for your team" made the ask feel like a no-brainer for recipients [how-to-convert-customers-with-cold-emails-startup-school @ 29:10](https://youtu.be/7Kh_fpxP1yY?t=1750)
- Founders themselves, not interns or automated tools, should send these emails, and should plan to manually follow up two to four times before giving up [how-to-convert-customers-with-cold-emails-startup-school @ 21:19](https://youtu.be/7Kh_fpxP1yY?t=1279)
