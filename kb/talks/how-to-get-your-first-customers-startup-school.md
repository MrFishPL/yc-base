# How to Get Your First Customers | Startup School — Compiled Truth

Founders, not outsourced salespeople or ad budgets, are the ones who must manually recruit a startup's first customers by doing sales themselves and tracking every step of the funnel.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Good products are rarely built in isolation — founders have to manually recruit customers rather than expecting growth to happen automatically [how-to-get-your-first-customers-startup-school @ 01:07](https://youtu.be/hyYCn_kAngI?t=67)
- The "startup curve" shows founders passing through a trough of sorrow and crash of ineptitude before reaching product-market fit, and it's the founders who persist that make the difference [how-to-get-your-first-customers-startup-school @ 02:30](https://youtu.be/hyYCn_kAngI?t=150)
- Founders should learn sales themselves before hiring a sales team, because you can't know if a product is bad until you've tried to sell it [how-to-get-your-first-customers-startup-school @ 04:02](https://youtu.be/hyYCn_kAngI?t=242)
- A great cold sales email should be short (6-8 sentences), jargon-free, address the customer's problem, use plain text, include social proof, and end with a clear call to action [how-to-get-your-first-customers-startup-school @ 06:27](https://youtu.be/hyYCn_kAngI?t=387)
- Your first customers should be the easiest to close — prioritize people you know, sell to startups (which have shorter decision lines than big companies), and don't be afraid to let slow-moving leads go [how-to-get-your-first-customers-startup-school @ 10:28](https://youtu.be/hyYCn_kAngI?t=628)
- Most people are not early adopters, so founders need to send many outbound messages to find the few who are willing to try something new [how-to-get-your-first-customers-startup-school @ 12:17](https://youtu.be/hyYCn_kAngI?t=737)
- If customers won't pay, that's a signal to move on — B2B founders should avoid free trials and instead offer a money-back guarantee or the ability to opt out of an annual contract [how-to-get-your-first-customers-startup-school @ 14:50](https://youtu.be/hyYCn_kAngI?t=890)
- Founders must work backwards from their goal through the sales funnel and track conversion rates with a CRM, because sending too few outreach emails can make sales look broken when it's really a data problem [how-to-get-your-first-customers-startup-school @ 15:27](https://youtu.be/hyYCn_kAngI?t=927)
- Even companies whose growth eventually looks like SEO, referrals, or paid ads (e.g., Airbnb) did not start that way — they did things that don't scale in the beginning [how-to-get-your-first-customers-startup-school @ 20:43](https://youtu.be/hyYCn_kAngI?t=1243)
