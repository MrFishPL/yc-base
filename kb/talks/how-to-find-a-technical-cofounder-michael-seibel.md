# How to Find a Technical Cofounder - Michael Seibel — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel lays out a step-by-step search order for non-technical founders to find a technical co-founder: friends first, then coworkers, then a deliberately built engineering network, always backed by a real offer rather than a vague ask.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Start by listing friends who know how to code and enjoy talking to, ranking the top five, and making them real offers rather than vague asks [how-to-find-a-technical-cofounder-michael-seibel @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/d7tDaFr5EYA?t=0)
- Many people never ask a friend to co-found because they assume the friend is too settled at their job, but you'll never know if you don't ask [how-to-find-a-technical-cofounder-michael-seibel @ 00:20](https://youtu.be/d7tDaFr5EYA?t=20)
- A real offer means specifying actual equity and salary terms up front — e.g. naming a percentage of the company and being upfront about what you can pay given funds raised — not just asking someone to "help out on weekends" [how-to-find-a-technical-cofounder-michael-seibel @ 01:02](https://youtu.be/d7tDaFr5EYA?t=62)
- If no friends fit, repeat the same ranked-list, real-offer process with coworkers [how-to-find-a-technical-cofounder-michael-seibel @ 01:42](https://youtu.be/d7tDaFr5EYA?t=102)
- If neither friends nor coworkers work, the next step is deliberately building a network of software engineers, typically by taking a job at a company where engineers work [how-to-find-a-technical-cofounder-michael-seibel @ 02:00](https://youtu.be/d7tDaFr5EYA?t=120)
- Working at a large company like Google or Facebook often isolates non-technical employees from engineers, so a small startup of 50 people or fewer, where everyone works closely together, is a better place to build that network [how-to-find-a-technical-cofounder-michael-seibel @ 02:21](https://youtu.be/d7tDaFr5EYA?t=141)
- Building this network can take a year or two, but that's a reasonable investment given a startup can be a decade-plus commitment [how-to-find-a-technical-cofounder-michael-seibel @ 03:03](https://youtu.be/d7tDaFr5EYA?t=183)
- Learning to code yourself, via resources like Code Academy or Lambda School, is another viable path [how-to-find-a-technical-cofounder-michael-seibel @ 03:40](https://youtu.be/d7tDaFr5EYA?t=220)
- College is one of the best places to find a future technical co-founder, even years in advance — Seibel himself found his Justin.tv co-founders this way [how-to-find-a-technical-cofounder-michael-seibel @ 03:57](https://youtu.be/d7tDaFr5EYA?t=237)
