# Diane Greene's Advice for Founders — Compiled Truth

Diane Greene draws lessons from three startups (VExtreme, VMware, Bebop) and a lifetime of sailing to argue that founder alignment, culture, and tenacity matter more than any single tactic.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A startup has a better chance of success with a limited number of founders who are solidly aligned, since "too many cooks can spoil the broth" [diane-greene-s-advice-for-founders @ 07:53](https://youtu.be/bNvUW_IBz0g?t=473)
- A profound innovation succeeds when it's designed and marketed to be non-disruptive and easy to integrate into the established order, even if it will eventually replace that order [diane-greene-s-advice-for-founders @ 08:19](https://youtu.be/bNvUW_IBz0g?t=499)
- VMware's sales took off after building a tool that could take a workload running on a physical machine and magically move it into a virtual machine with everything running the same [diane-greene-s-advice-for-founders @ 08:47](https://youtu.be/bNvUW_IBz0g?t=527)
- When VMware couldn't sell through IBM directly due to IP issues, they didn't stop thinking about the problem — they turned it into an agreement to work with IBM's star resellers instead [diane-greene-s-advice-for-founders @ 09:10](https://youtu.be/bNvUW_IBz0g?t=550)
- Creating the right corporate culture was essential at VMware: fostering a sense of each person's importance and designing the physical campus to make collaboration easy [diane-greene-s-advice-for-founders @ 09:32](https://youtu.be/bNvUW_IBz0g?t=572)
- At Bebop, the first product's look and feel was off, but tenaciousness paid off as the company persevered into a powerhouse for enterprise application design [diane-greene-s-advice-for-founders @ 10:23](https://youtu.be/bNvUW_IBz0g?t=623)
- Running an enterprise startup inside a mega-company like Google Cloud still comes down to the cultures of both parent and startup, and to having the right people in the right roles [diane-greene-s-advice-for-founders @ 10:47](https://youtu.be/bNvUW_IBz0g?t=647)
- Her closing advice: give a company your all not because you're supposed to win, but because you respect the goal — that commitment is what makes founders unafraid of failure [diane-greene-s-advice-for-founders @ 11:43](https://youtu.be/bNvUW_IBz0g?t=703)
