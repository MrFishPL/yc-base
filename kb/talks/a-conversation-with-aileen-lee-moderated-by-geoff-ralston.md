# A Conversation with Aileen Lee - Moderated by Geoff Ralston — Compiled Truth

Cowboy Ventures founder Aileen Lee, who coined the term "unicorn," discusses her path from Kleiner Perkins to seed investing and shares practical, empathetic advice on fundraising, storytelling, and how VC economics shape what investors look for.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Lead qualification matters as much in sales as in fundraising: don't waste time on customers or deals that look promising but aren't a real problem worth solving [a-conversation-with-aileen-lee-moderated-by-geoff-ralston @ 12:11](https://youtu.be/vrBhB48qPUM?t=731)
- Storytelling is a core fundraising skill — practice your pitch on friendly audiences well before you need to raise, not the month you start fundraising [a-conversation-with-aileen-lee-moderated-by-geoff-ralston @ 20:44](https://youtu.be/vrBhB48qPUM?t=1244)
- Understand venture math: a fund needs a handful of companies to become billion-dollar-plus outcomes to return the fund, so investors are betting on whether you can become a multi-billion dollar company, not just a $200-300M one [a-conversation-with-aileen-lee-moderated-by-geoff-ralston @ 23:50](https://youtu.be/vrBhB48qPUM?t=1430)
- A good pitch structure covers: team and why you're the right founders, the problem and market size (addressable market should be $5-10B+), the product/solution with a 10x differentiator, honest competitive landscape, financial plan, and a summary slide [a-conversation-with-aileen-lee-moderated-by-geoff-ralston @ 37:50](https://youtu.be/vrBhB48qPUM?t=2270)
- Founders typically sell 20-30% of their company per round across seed through later rounds, so think about cumulative dilution before setting a cap, and avoid offering different caps to different investors in the same round [a-conversation-with-aileen-lee-moderated-by-geoff-ralston @ 50:45](https://youtu.be/vrBhB48qPUM?t=3045)
- Non-traditional founders should lean on their personal hustle, insight into the problem, and how they solved roadblocks since they often lack data or shipped products at seed stage [a-conversation-with-aileen-lee-moderated-by-geoff-ralston @ 47:36](https://youtu.be/vrBhB48qPUM?t=2856)
- Founders should reference-check their investors just as investors reference-check founders, since it's a long-term relationship that's hard to exit [a-conversation-with-aileen-lee-moderated-by-geoff-ralston @ 49:20](https://youtu.be/vrBhB48qPUM?t=2960)
- The "unicorn" term emerged from Lee's analysis of ~39 US-born companies worth over $1B in the prior decade, finding most successful founders were in their 30s (older for enterprise vs. consumer) and had co-founders they'd previously worked with or known [a-conversation-with-aileen-lee-moderated-by-geoff-ralston @ 43:16](https://youtu.be/vrBhB48qPUM?t=2596)
- Studies show identical pitches raise roughly 2x more money when delivered by a man versus a woman with the same script, reflecting bias that both male and female evaluators exhibit [a-conversation-with-aileen-lee-moderated-by-geoff-ralston @ 33:01](https://youtu.be/vrBhB48qPUM?t=1981)
- Investors and founders share the same goal of getting to market fast and cheaply — for example, building only one mobile platform first to get market validation before spending on the second [a-conversation-with-aileen-lee-moderated-by-geoff-ralston @ 59:21](https://youtu.be/vrBhB48qPUM?t=3561)
