# This Startup Is Trying To Delete 29% Of All CO2 Emissions — Compiled Truth

Remora founder Paul Gross built a point-source carbon capture retrofit for semi-trucks and locomotives (transport is 29% of US emissions), and shares how he assembled a science-heavy company without a science background, built in Detroit instead of the Bay Area, and iterated fast with in-house hardware simulation.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Remora retrofits semi-trucks and locomotives to extract and purify CO2 from exhaust, producing beverage-grade CO2 that customers can recycle, since transportation is 29% of all US emissions [this-startup-is-trying-to-delete-29-of-all-co2-emissions @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/ENG_DQF5E60?t=0)
- Gross didn't have a mechanical or chemical engineering background, so instead of getting a PhD he built his own model, read papers, and recruited a recent PhD expert (Christina Reynolds) as a co-founder [this-startup-is-trying-to-delete-29-of-all-co2-emissions @ 02:50](https://youtu.be/ENG_DQF5E60?t=170)
- His advice to non-expert founders tackling hard science problems: start working and learning yourself, but know your job is to build a team of experts, not become one [this-startup-is-trying-to-delete-29-of-all-co2-emissions @ 03:48](https://youtu.be/ENG_DQF5E60?t=228)
- Remora applied to YC in winter 2021 with nothing built and not even incorporated, and Gross argues applying early is right because YC "sets the pace" before slower habits get baked in [this-startup-is-trying-to-delete-29-of-all-co2-emissions @ 05:27](https://youtu.be/ENG_DQF5E60?t=327)
- The company chose to build in Wixom, Michigan near Detroit rather than Silicon Valley because that's where the mechanical/electrical engineering talent for their specific problem lives [this-startup-is-trying-to-delete-29-of-all-co2-emissions @ 06:17](https://youtu.be/ENG_DQF5E60?t=377)
- Gross encourages founders to expand their ambitions and pick the hardest, most interesting problem, because that's what attracts the best engineers, advisers, and investors [this-startup-is-trying-to-delete-29-of-all-co2-emissions @ 07:14](https://youtu.be/ENG_DQF5E60?t=434)
- The biggest scaling challenge is building a truly modular product (kits that fit many truck/train models) and bringing manufacturing fully in-house to create a tight engineering iteration loop [this-startup-is-trying-to-delete-29-of-all-co2-emissions @ 09:50](https://youtu.be/ENG_DQF5E60?t=590)
- To avoid slow, expensive road testing, Remora built a simulator using a real semi-truck engine on a dynamometer inside a shipping container to test different exhaust temperatures, loads, and compositions [this-startup-is-trying-to-delete-29-of-all-co2-emissions @ 10:49](https://youtu.be/ENG_DQF5E60?t=649)
- Remora has raised $17 million, signed evaluation agreements with Ryder and Union Pacific, and is targeting capturing a billion tons of CO2 a year across trucks, trains, ships, and industrial plants [this-startup-is-trying-to-delete-29-of-all-co2-emissions @ 11:47](https://youtu.be/ENG_DQF5E60?t=707)
