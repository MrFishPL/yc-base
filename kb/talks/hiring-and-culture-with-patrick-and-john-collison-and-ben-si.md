# Hiring and Culture with Patrick and John Collison and Ben Silbermann (HtSaS 2014: 11) — Compiled Truth

The Stripe and Pinterest founders argue culture is less an architected plan than a gardening problem rooted in the first ten hires, and that transparency and disciplined hiring/onboarding practices are what let that culture scale from a handful of people to hundreds.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Culture boils down to four dimensions: who you hire and what they value, what you do daily, what you communicate, and what you celebrate (vs. punish) [hiring-and-culture-with-patrick-and-john-collison-and-ben-si @ 00:55](https://youtu.be/kTS7OIAMunM?t=55)
- Hiring your first 10 people is really hiring ~100, since each person tends to bring roughly 10 more people along with them over time [hiring-and-culture-with-patrick-and-john-collison-and-ben-si @ 04:17](https://youtu.be/kTS7OIAMunM?t=257)
- Early hires were often undervalued or early-career people (an 18-year-old designer, a CTO still in college), because already-proven talent is usually already spoken for [hiring-and-culture-with-patrick-and-john-collison-and-ben-si @ 08:55](https://youtu.be/kTS7OIAMunM?t=535)
- Pre-calibrate what "world class" looks like in a domain by asking recognized experts before you ever interview candidates, since learning good vs. bad during the interview itself is expensive [hiring-and-culture-with-patrick-and-john-collison-and-ben-si @ 16:27](https://youtu.be/kTS7OIAMunM?t=987)
- For the first ~10 hires, work alongside candidates (e.g., a week of paired work) before committing, since it's hard to fake competence over that time [hiring-and-culture-with-patrick-and-john-collison-and-ben-si @ 20:05](https://youtu.be/kTS7OIAMunM?t=1205)
- Force references to rank a candidate (top 1%, top 5%, top 10% of people they've worked with) instead of accepting generic praise, which creates useful scarcity [hiring-and-culture-with-patrick-and-john-collison-and-ben-si @ 23:05](https://youtu.be/kTS7OIAMunM?t=1385)
- Get new hires doing real work immediately — engineers committing code, business hires in real meetings — on their first day rather than easing them in [hiring-and-culture-with-patrick-and-john-collison-and-ben-si @ 27:01](https://youtu.be/kTS7OIAMunM?t=1621)
- As a company scales, referrals become the lifeblood of hiring; Pinterest's 14th or 15th hire was a dedicated professional recruiter who taught the team to screen for cultural fit at scale [hiring-and-culture-with-patrick-and-john-collison-and-ben-si @ 30:23](https://youtu.be/kTS7OIAMunM?t=1823)
- Stripe BCC'd the whole company on outgoing emails to scale transparency, but the approach strained badly around 50 people and at one point even broke Gmail [hiring-and-culture-with-patrick-and-john-collison-and-ben-si @ 35:36](https://youtu.be/kTS7OIAMunM?t=2136)
- When recruiting, lay out in detail why the work will be hard rather than whitewashing the risk — the right people select in with eyes open, and those who wanted only certainty won't stick around when things get tough [hiring-and-culture-with-patrick-and-john-collison-and-ben-si @ 44:32](https://youtu.be/kTS7OIAMunM?t=2672)
