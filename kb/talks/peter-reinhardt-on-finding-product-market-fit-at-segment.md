# Peter Reinhardt on Finding Product Market Fit at Segment — Compiled Truth

Segment's founder recounts pivoting twice (from a classroom attention tool, to a failed analytics product, to the analytics.js routing library) before Segment's Hacker News launch produced unmistakable, out-of-control product-market fit, and shares how sales-style qualification became Segment's core discovery method.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Segment's first idea, a classroom confusion-tracking tool, failed when the team discovered students were using it as cover to be on Facebook instead of paying attention [peter-reinhardt-on-finding-product-market-fit-at-segment @ 04:17](https://youtu.be/l-vfn97QTr0?t=257)
- A year spent building a competing analytics tool against Mixpanel and Google Analytics also failed because it was at parity, not 10x better, on the dimensions customers actually cared about [peter-reinhardt-on-finding-product-market-fit-at-segment @ 07:04](https://youtu.be/l-vfn97QTr0?t=424)
- Real product-market fit "pulls it out of you" and feels like losing control — a landing page for the analytics.js open-source library exploded overnight on Hacker News after two failed ideas [peter-reinhardt-on-finding-product-market-fit-at-segment @ 11:10](https://youtu.be/l-vfn97QTr0?t=670)
- The key distinction is that people previously said they wanted a feature but didn't use it, versus later actually using the product and asking for more — that's the "death spiral of user feedback" to avoid [peter-reinhardt-on-finding-product-market-fit-at-segment @ 12:43](https://youtu.be/l-vfn97QTr0?t=763)
- A second product-market-fit moment came from noticing five different customers were all independently piping data into S3 to feed it into a Redshift ETL pipeline, leading to a direct Redshift connector that doubled revenue growth [peter-reinhardt-on-finding-product-market-fit-at-segment @ 14:04](https://youtu.be/l-vfn97QTr0?t=844)
- Big product-market-fit moments are unmistakable, like "stepping on a landmine" — the iterative Airbnb story is often misused by founders as an excuse to keep stringing along a bad idea [peter-reinhardt-on-finding-product-market-fit-at-segment @ 18:42](https://youtu.be/l-vfn97QTr0?t=1122)
- Rather than asking "how long do you give an idea," pair an optimist with a genuine skeptic on the founding team who pushes for the fastest reasonable test [peter-reinhardt-on-finding-product-market-fit-at-segment @ 20:38](https://youtu.be/l-vfn97QTr0?t=1238)
- Segment adapted the MEDDIC sales qualification framework (metrics, economic buyer, decision criteria, decision process, identified pain, champion) as a discovery tool for testing new product ideas with customers [peter-reinhardt-on-finding-product-market-fit-at-segment @ 24:25](https://youtu.be/l-vfn97QTr0?t=1465)
- A sales advisor pushed Segment to ask for $120,000/year instead of their initial $10/month pricing; even after an 85% discount to $18K it was 150x their original ask [peter-reinhardt-on-finding-product-market-fit-at-segment @ 39:41](https://youtu.be/l-vfn97QTr0?t=2381)
- Segment operates around four values — karma, tribe, drive, and focus — synthesized around 25 employees by the founders reflecting on what they were and weren't proud of [peter-reinhardt-on-finding-product-market-fit-at-segment @ 32:40](https://youtu.be/l-vfn97QTr0?t=1960)
