# How To Build The Future: Sam Altman — Compiled Truth

Sam Altman argues this is the best moment yet to start a startup because AI is compounding fast while incumbents are stuck on slow planning cycles, and he traces how OpenAI's early conviction on scaling deep learning (against expert derision) became the template for founder conviction in general.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Big tech companies have the edge when markets move slowly, but platform shifts like AI (or mobile, the internet, semiconductors) give upstarts the edge, which is why now is the best time yet to start a company [how-to-build-the-future-sam-altman @ 01:21](https://youtu.be/xXCBz_8hM9w?t=81)
- OpenAI deliberately picked one bet (deep learning plus scaling) and concentrated fully on it rather than spreading across many approaches, because being far less resourced than DeepMind meant they had to win through focus [how-to-build-the-future-sam-altman @ 00:19](https://youtu.be/xXCBz_8hM9w?t=19)
- Most of the world still does not understand the value of extreme conviction on one bet, which is why Altman is so excited about startups today [how-to-build-the-future-sam-altman @ 21:17](https://youtu.be/xXCBz_8hM9w?t=1277)
- Conviction is valuable only up to the point you have data; OpenAI tried to believe whatever the results told them and fully embraced being wrong once evidence appeared [how-to-build-the-future-sam-altman @ 22:44](https://youtu.be/xXCBz_8hM9w?t=1364)
- OpenAI's earliest researchers (like Alec Radford, who found the neuron that flipped sentiment before GPT-1) had genuine outlier talent, and having a crazy-talented team let them push hard on a still-unproven direction [how-to-build-the-future-sam-altman @ 25:12](https://youtu.be/xXCBz_8hM9w?t=1512)
- GPT-3 could not support great businesses beyond copywriting, but GPT-3.5 and then GPT-4 changed that: founders stopped needing convincing and instead asked how many GPUs they could get [how-to-build-the-future-sam-altman @ 27:04](https://youtu.be/xXCBz_8hM9w?t=1624)
- Altman's own first startup, Loopt, never found product-market fit or escape velocity, but doing a startup still had the highest rate of generalized learning of anything he's encountered [how-to-build-the-future-sam-altman @ 32:22](https://youtu.be/xXCBz_8hM9w?t=1942)
- A startup's number-one edge right now is speed, focus, and the ability to react to how fast the technology is moving, versus big companies stuck on quarterly or decade-long planning cycles [how-to-build-the-future-sam-altman @ 44:26](https://youtu.be/xXCBz_8hM9w?t=2666)
- Using AI does not exempt a founder from building a real moat or competitive edge; new technology gives short-term growth explosions, but you still have to build a business [how-to-build-the-future-sam-altman @ 45:06](https://youtu.be/xXCBz_8hM9w?t=2706)
