# Grace Garey Speaks at Female Founders Conference 2015 — Compiled Truth

Grace Garey tells the origin story of Watsi, the first global crowdfunding platform for healthcare, from her nonprofit-sector disillusionment to its scrappy launch and growth inside YC.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Watsi lets donors see photos and stories of real patients worldwide who need healthcare they can't afford, and fund it directly [grace-garey-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 00:03](https://youtu.be/ftpoG2fSZBY?t=3)
- Working at a large humanitarian nonprofit after the Haiti earthquake, Garey saw that donor-restricted funding meant up to 94% of relief money never reached the people it was meant to help [grace-garey-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 05:02](https://youtu.be/ftpoG2fSZBY?t=302)
- A small, self-funded Indian hospital that stayed physically close to the people it served could act on their real needs directly, without donor bureaucracy getting in the way [grace-garey-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 06:26](https://youtu.be/ftpoG2fSZBY?t=386)
- Watsi's founder Chase had an epiphany on a bus in Costa Rica during the Peace Corps, asking why there was no way to connect a woman needing money for her son's healthcare to a global community of supporters, the way Kickstarter, DonorsChoose, and Kiva connect people for other causes [grace-garey-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 10:13](https://youtu.be/ftpoG2fSZBY?t=613)
- From the start the team committed to three core decisions: 100% of every donation goes to healthcare with no cut taken, full transparency of every transaction published online, and treating everyone (donor, patient, doctor) the way they'd want to be treated [grace-garey-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 12:32](https://youtu.be/ftpoG2fSZBY?t=752)
- On launch day almost no one donated except the founders' own mothers, until a Hacker News post sent 20,000 visitors and fully funded every posted patient within hours, far exceeding their six-month goal of funding 10 patients [grace-garey-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 13:14](https://youtu.be/ftpoG2fSZBY?t=794)
- In the early days, posting a new patient profile required a manual, multi-person relay by phone and email between a medical partner, a resident physician, and Garey typing profiles from her day-job bathroom [grace-garey-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 15:37](https://youtu.be/ftpoG2fSZBY?t=937)
- Desperate for funding since no one wanted to be the first investor, the team rallied friends and family (including a bar bouncer) to win a $10,000 online voting competition by a 1% margin [grace-garey-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 18:00](https://youtu.be/ftpoG2fSZBY?t=1080)
- A two-sentence email from Paul Graham after seeing a Hacker News post led to Watsi becoming the first nonprofit accepted into Y Combinator [grace-garey-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 19:10](https://youtu.be/ftpoG2fSZBY?t=1150)
- What stood out most about YC was that everyone was making a big bet on the future and simply assumed a good idea plus hard work would succeed, unlike outside reactions that dismissed Watsi as "a nice sweet nonprofit" idea [grace-garey-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 20:36](https://youtu.be/ftpoG2fSZBY?t=1236)
- Garey's closing advice: find a problem you're excited to spend your life solving, rather than a solution in search of a problem [grace-garey-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 27:05](https://youtu.be/ftpoG2fSZBY?t=1625)
