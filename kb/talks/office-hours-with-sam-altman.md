# Office Hours with Sam Altman — Compiled Truth

In this Q&A, Sam Altman answers founder questions on getting first B2B customers, whether being young or a student is an advantage, vetting technical co-founders, why startup crowdfunding underperformed, and how to think about impact and career direction.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- For B2B companies getting first customers outside your network, the better long-term move is expanding your network rather than just chasing outside leads; within YC, most B2B companies get their first 20 customers from other YC companies via Bookface [office-hours-with-sam-altman @ 00:39](https://youtu.be/45BvnJgwYjk?t=39)
- A strategy that works to expand your network as a founder is emailing other founders you respect in your city and organizing dinners, rather than waiting for conferences [office-hours-with-sam-altman @ 02:12](https://youtu.be/45BvnJgwYjk?t=132)
- If you build something impressive, your network will come to you — leading with "I built this awesome product, check it out" lands far better than asking people to network with you [office-hours-with-sam-altman @ 03:31](https://youtu.be/45BvnJgwYjk?t=211)
- Being young/a student founder has real advantages (fewer life commitments, huge focus, work stamina, frugality, lack of "scars of experience") but disadvantages in managing people and business experience, so young founders should supplement with experienced people [office-hours-with-sam-altman @ 05:40](https://youtu.be/45BvnJgwYjk?t=340)
- Non-technical founders like Evan Spiegel and Brian Chesky succeeded by being at least lightly technical/design-capable, being great at product, and being strong enough leaders to convince great technical people to follow them [office-hours-with-sam-altman @ 08:53](https://youtu.be/45BvnJgwYjk?t=533)
- Altman would not start a company with someone he hadn't known and worked with on a project for at least six months, since that reveals how good they actually are [office-hours-with-sam-altman @ 09:59](https://youtu.be/45BvnJgwYjk?t=599)
- Equity crowdfunding for startups has been a disappointment because the crowd is still less willing to back companies without professional investor support, so it's mostly become a way to multiply money already raised from pro investors [office-hours-with-sam-altman @ 11:09](https://youtu.be/45BvnJgwYjk?t=669)
- On career direction, Altman advises against letting someone else hand you a list of important problems; instead take a breadth-first look across the world before going depth-first into a few areas [office-hours-with-sam-altman @ 12:54](https://youtu.be/45BvnJgwYjk?t=774)
- For technical bar to build product, it depends on what you're building — a simple first version of an app doesn't require deep technical skill, but you'll need world-class technical talent later [office-hours-with-sam-altman @ 21:52](https://youtu.be/45BvnJgwYjk?t=1312)
- If acquired as a key non-founder employee, negotiate an agreement on the target company's autonomy before the deal closes, build relationships across the acquiring company, and give it six to nine months before deciding whether it's working [office-hours-with-sam-altman @ 22:32](https://youtu.be/45BvnJgwYjk?t=1352)
