# How to Get and Evaluate Startup Ideas | Startup School — Compiled Truth

Drawing on data from YC's top 100 companies and thousands of rejected applications, this talk gives a framework of common mistakes, ten evaluation questions, and seven recipes for finding a promising startup idea.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The most common mistake is building a "solution in search of a problem," e.g. starting from "AI is cool" and hunting for a problem to apply it to, rather than starting from a real problem people care about [how-to-get-and-evaluate-startup-ideas-startup-school @ 01:42](https://youtu.be/Th8JoIan4dg?t=102)
- "Tar pit ideas" like an app to coordinate meeting up with friends look deceptively simple but have structural reasons no one has solved them in 20 years of YC applications [how-to-get-and-evaluate-startup-ideas-startup-school @ 03:22](https://youtu.be/Th8JoIan4dg?t=202)
- Founder-market fit — being the right team for the idea — is the single most important criterion, as shown by PlanGrid's founders who combined construction industry knowledge with engineering skill [how-to-get-and-evaluate-startup-ideas-startup-school @ 06:41](https://youtu.be/Th8JoIan4dg?t=401)
- Counterintuitively, most good startup ideas have existing competitors; Dropbox launched as the 20th cloud storage company but won because it had a real UI insight (OS-level sync) competitors missed [how-to-get-and-evaluate-startup-ideas-startup-school @ 18:31](https://youtu.be/Th8JoIan4dg?t=1111)
- Ideas that seem "hard to get started" (Stripe needed a bank deal) or are in a "boring space" (Gusto's payroll software) are good because most founders shy away from them, leaving them for smarter founders to grab [how-to-get-and-evaluate-startup-ideas-startup-school @ 14:55](https://youtu.be/Th8JoIan4dg?t=895)
- Different idea spaces have wildly different hit rates — fintech infrastructure and vertical SaaS for enterprise produced far more billion-dollar companies than consumer hardware, social networks, or ad tech over the last decade [how-to-get-and-evaluate-startup-ideas-startup-school @ 12:27](https://youtu.be/Th8JoIan4dg?t=747)
- Fivetran pivoted through multiple data-analysis tools by repeatedly selling to the same companies, using each rejection to learn what customers actually wanted within a fertile idea space [how-to-get-and-evaluate-startup-ideas-startup-school @ 13:37](https://youtu.be/Th8JoIan4dg?t=817)
- At least 70% of YC's top 100 companies had their idea occur organically rather than from deliberately sitting down to brainstorm, which tends to produce tar pit ideas [how-to-get-and-evaluate-startup-ideas-startup-school @ 19:46](https://youtu.be/Th8JoIan4dg?t=1186)
- The best recipe for finding an idea is to start from your team's specific expertise, as Rezi's founders did by only exploring ideas at the intersection of real estate and fintech, where they had domain knowledge [how-to-get-and-evaluate-startup-ideas-startup-school @ 21:34](https://youtu.be/Th8JoIan4dg?t=1294)
- AtoB's founders systematically became experts in the trucking industry by physically visiting truck stops and interviewing drivers and founders before landing on fuel cards as their idea [how-to-get-and-evaluate-startup-ideas-startup-school @ 28:57](https://youtu.be/Th8JoIan4dg?t=1737)
