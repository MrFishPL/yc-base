# Tokenmaxxing: How Top Builders Use AI To Do The Work Of 400 Engineers — Compiled Truth

Garry Tan describes how he went from 13 years without coding to shipping hundreds of thousands of lines of code by "token maxing" — spending heavily on AI compute and building reusable markdown "skills" on top of Claude Code, while still supplying the human agency and QA that agents can't replace.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- He rebuilt his Posterous-style blog a third time as "Garry's List" in about 5 days for ~$200 using Claude Code, versus $4M and 1.5 years the first time and $100k and 3 months the second time [tokenmaxxing @ 04:28](https://youtu.be/57lDpTwiW6g?t=268)
- His "boil the ocean" philosophy: if incremental spend makes a piece of work more complete or accurate, you should keep paying for it — that's "token maxing" [tokenmaxxing @ 07:27](https://youtu.be/57lDpTwiW6g?t=447)
- He believes AI can now "token max" nearly all knowledge work, but people still need to supply the underlying agency, need, and desire behind what gets built [tokenmaxxing @ 08:44](https://youtu.be/57lDpTwiW6g?t=524)
- Having Claude draw ASCII diagrams of data flows, state machines, and error cases before writing code made it complete tasks far more thoroughly [tokenmaxxing @ 10:29](https://youtu.be/57lDpTwiW6g?t=629)
- He initially chased 100% test coverage but found 80-90% is the practical best-practice target [tokenmaxxing @ 11:28](https://youtu.be/57lDpTwiW6g?t=688)
- Claude-in-Chrome MCP was too slow (2-3 seconds per turn) for QA, so he wrapped Microsoft's Playwright himself to build a faster browser-testing tool [tokenmaxxing @ 15:35](https://youtu.be/57lDpTwiW6g?t=935)
- His "thin harness, fat skills" framing: don't rebuild the agent harness (the core loop that feeds input to the LLM and executes tool calls) — put your effort into writing good markdown skills instead [tokenmaxxing @ 20:43](https://youtu.be/57lDpTwiW6g?t=1243)
- After normalizing for logical lines of code, he found he was actually producing ~400x more code than in 2013 by directing about 15 agents at once [tokenmaxxing @ 31:08](https://youtu.be/57lDpTwiW6g?t=1868)
- He frames the current moment as a choice between personal AI — your own data, integrations, and prompts — versus corporate-controlled AI, echoing the personal computer revolution [tokenmaxxing @ 34:36](https://youtu.be/57lDpTwiW6g?t=2076)
- Founders should treat token spend like San Francisco rent: push hard to spend more on it rather than economizing there, and economize instead on things like office furniture [tokenmaxxing @ 37:23](https://youtu.be/57lDpTwiW6g?t=2243)
