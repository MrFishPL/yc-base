# What Shutting Down Your Startup Feels Like - Avni Patel Thompson of Poppy with Kat Manalac — Compiled Truth

Avni Patel Thompson, founder of childcare marketplace Poppy, walks through the multi-year economics that led to shutting the company down, the decision process among pivot/acquisition/shutdown, and the emotional and practical work of winding down responsibly.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Poppy operated in an inherently margin-challenged category (childcare), and the economics changed qualitatively at each scale step — 10 bookings a week looked nothing like 500 bookings a week — which took years to fully surface [what-shutting-down-your-startup-feels-like-avni-patel-thomps @ 03:14](https://youtu.be/FyhlY7XRxCo?t=194)
- Steady but modest growth of 5-10% a month (versus a hoped-for 15-20%) was a slow-building signal over roughly a year that things weren't working, rather than one dramatic moment [what-shutting-down-your-startup-feels-like-avni-patel-thomps @ 09:53](https://youtu.be/FyhlY7XRxCo?t=593)
- Facing about six to eight months of runway, she framed the decision as three options: pivot (cutting ~75% of the team), get acquired, or shut down cleanly, and pursued all three in parallel before concluding a pivot wasn't right given the mission the money was raised for [what-shutting-down-your-startup-feels-like-avni-patel-thomps @ 12:55](https://youtu.be/FyhlY7XRxCo?t=775)
- Monthly investor updates with full transparency, including exact bank balance, made the eventual shutdown conversation far less of a shock and preserved trust with investors [what-shutting-down-your-startup-feels-like-avni-patel-thomps @ 24:29](https://youtu.be/FyhlY7XRxCo?t=1469)
- She distinguished duty to four constituents when deciding how to wind down: investors, employees, users, and herself/her family, and chose to announce right before the December holidays to give the team forced time to process and job-search [what-shutting-down-your-startup-feels-like-avni-patel-thomps @ 30:01](https://youtu.be/FyhlY7XRxCo?t=1801)
- Because the company always had a revenue mindset even without profitability, roughly $2M raised lasted about three years and bought time for further experiments before the shutdown decision [what-shutting-down-your-startup-feels-like-avni-patel-thomps @ 10:30](https://youtu.be/FyhlY7XRxCo?t=630)
- She explicitly rejects the "pivot or die" narrative that not continuing equals failure, arguing that a company that did what it set out to do and generated real learning is not a failure even if it doesn't scale [what-shutting-down-your-startup-feels-like-avni-patel-thomps @ 50:29](https://youtu.be/FyhlY7XRxCo?t=3029)
- She describes an explicit grieving process after shutdown, giving herself a set window (through December 17th, then further through the holidays) before engaging with "what's next" conversations [what-shutting-down-your-startup-feels-like-avni-patel-thomps @ 34:23](https://youtu.be/FyhlY7XRxCo?t=2063)
