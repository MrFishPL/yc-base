# When is the Right Time to Apply to Y Combinator? - Jared Friedman — Compiled Truth

YC partner Jared Friedman argues the right time to apply to YC is as soon as you have a founding team and an idea you're excited about, not once you have traction or revenue.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The right time to apply to YC is when you have two ingredients: the right founding team and an idea that team is excited to work on [when-is-the-right-time-to-apply-to-y-combinator-jared-friedm @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/EEy2MYHxAe8?t=0)
- A common misconception is that applicants need significant traction or revenue to get into YC, but that's not true [when-is-the-right-time-to-apply-to-y-combinator-jared-friedm @ 00:51](https://youtu.be/EEy2MYHxAe8?t=51)
- Anywhere from a quarter to a half of the companies YC funds each batch are funded on just a founding team and an idea, with no traction [when-is-the-right-time-to-apply-to-y-combinator-jared-friedm @ 00:51](https://youtu.be/EEy2MYHxAe8?t=51)
- If you don't get in on your first application, YC may still fund the same team and idea later once you've made more progress, so reapplying is worthwhile [when-is-the-right-time-to-apply-to-y-combinator-jared-friedm @ 01:12](https://youtu.be/EEy2MYHxAe8?t=72)
- Founders have little to lose by applying as soon as they have a founding team and an idea, which is the exact stage Friedman's own company Scribd was at when it applied and got into YC's Summer 2006 batch [when-is-the-right-time-to-apply-to-y-combinator-jared-friedm @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/EEy2MYHxAe8?t=0)
