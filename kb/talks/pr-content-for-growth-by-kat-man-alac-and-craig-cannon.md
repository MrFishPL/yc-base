# PR + Content for Growth by Kat Mañalac and Craig Cannon — Compiled Truth

Kat Mañalac and Craig Cannon lay out when and how early-stage founders should invest in content marketing versus press, arguing both only work once you already have something people want.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Press is not a scalable user acquisition strategy — it can spark early inbound but shouldn't be relied on for long-term growth, and talking to users matters more first [pr-content-for-growth-by-kat-man-alac-and-craig-cannon @ 02:43](https://youtu.be/JL9yoVFHx-Q?t=163)
- Content marketing should start only after you've made something people want and confirmed users aren't churning, since content built on a leaky product wastes effort [pr-content-for-growth-by-kat-man-alac-and-craig-cannon @ 05:17](https://youtu.be/JL9yoVFHx-Q?t=317)
- Treat content like a product: apply the "make something people want" lesson but scope it to "make something your customers want," as Zapier learned when they optimized for Hacker News instead of their actual customers [pr-content-for-growth-by-kat-man-alac-and-craig-cannon @ 05:59](https://youtu.be/JL9yoVFHx-Q?t=359)
- Early-stage companies should pick one clear content goal — engagement or conversions — and instrument it with basic analytics like Mixpanel and a Facebook pixel before publishing [pr-content-for-growth-by-kat-man-alac-and-craig-cannon @ 07:03](https://youtu.be/JL9yoVFHx-Q?t=423)
- Find your platform by talking to users about where they hang out, then make the type of content that performs well there (essays on Hacker News, tutorials on YouTube, Instagram influencer content, etc.) [pr-content-for-growth-by-kat-man-alac-and-craig-cannon @ 08:25](https://youtu.be/JL9yoVFHx-Q?t=505)
- The Onion's headline process — many writers submit ideas anonymously and only 5-10% survive group selection — shows the value of real brainstorming and killing weak content [pr-content-for-growth-by-kat-man-alac-and-craig-cannon @ 10:57](https://youtu.be/JL9yoVFHx-Q?t=657)
- Don't hire PR firms at early stages; treat press outreach like business development you do yourself, since the best story ideas and relationships come from the founders [pr-content-for-growth-by-kat-man-alac-and-craig-cannon @ 21:08](https://youtu.be/JL9yoVFHx-Q?t=1268)
- Before pitching press, draft a one-sentence pitch, a three-to-five sentence pitch, and answers to common reporter FAQs, and wait for a real news hook such as a fundraising announcement [pr-content-for-growth-by-kat-man-alac-and-craig-cannon @ 24:41](https://youtu.be/JL9yoVFHx-Q?t=1481)
- Warm introductions to reporters convert far better than cold outreach, and offering one reporter an exclusive increases the odds they'll actually run the story [pr-content-for-growth-by-kat-man-alac-and-craig-cannon @ 33:37](https://youtu.be/JL9yoVFHx-Q?t=2017)
- After a story runs, promote it actively across your own channels and evaluate whether it actually achieved the goal you set, since press alone rarely moves the needle [pr-content-for-growth-by-kat-man-alac-and-craig-cannon @ 37:40](https://youtu.be/JL9yoVFHx-Q?t=2260)
