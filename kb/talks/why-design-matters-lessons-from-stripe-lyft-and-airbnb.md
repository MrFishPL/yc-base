# Why Design Matters: Lessons from Stripe, Lyft and Airbnb — Compiled Truth

Stripe's head of design Katie Dill argues that design builds the trust and confidence that let novel, risky-feeling products like Airbnb, Lyft, and Stripe succeed, and that great design culture requires founders who care about every detail rather than just numbers that move.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Design was instrumental to Airbnb and Stripe because both asked users to trust something novel (staying in a stranger's house, moving your money), and thoughtful detail built the confidence needed to try it [why-design-matters-lessons-from-stripe-lyft-and-airbnb @ 01:56](https://youtu.be/CKfERe55CeA?t=116)
- Products relying on high trust (Stripe, Lyft, Airbnb) lose credibility fast from small defects like typos, because users infer "what else aren't they getting right" [why-design-matters-lessons-from-stripe-lyft-and-airbnb @ 04:18](https://youtu.be/CKfERe55CeA?t=258)
- If founders only value design when it "moves the numbers," they'll skip it in the many cases where the payoff isn't provable up front; founders who deeply care about users and intentional detail create a different, harder-to-replicate culture [why-design-matters-lessons-from-stripe-lyft-and-airbnb @ 05:41](https://youtu.be/CKfERe55CeA?t=341)
- A high-quality product checklist, in priority order: it must be functional (solve the problem), then usable, then crafted/beautiful — craft only matters once the first two are true [why-design-matters-lessons-from-stripe-lyft-and-airbnb @ 10:26](https://youtu.be/CKfERe55CeA?t=626)
- Founders without a design background can build design judgment by treating product review like an aviation pre-flight checklist, actively hunting for what's wrong rather than assuming it's fine [why-design-matters-lessons-from-stripe-lyft-and-airbnb @ 12:47](https://youtu.be/CKfERe55CeA?t=767)
- Bringing real users in on an ongoing basis (not just a one-time research phase) is critical because founders are too close to their own product to see it fresh [why-design-matters-lessons-from-stripe-lyft-and-airbnb @ 15:25](https://youtu.be/CKfERe55CeA?t=925)
- Stripe embeds designers, PMs, and engineers on the same teams with shared goals (rather than a design "agency" people request work from) so the user experience feels coherent end to end [why-design-matters-lessons-from-stripe-lyft-and-airbnb @ 18:35](https://youtu.be/CKfERe55CeA?t=1115)
- Stripe runs "walk the store" exercises and tracks its top 17 user journeys on a public red/yellow/green scoreboard each quarter to catch regressions and create social pressure to fix them [why-design-matters-lessons-from-stripe-lyft-and-airbnb @ 23:04](https://youtu.be/CKfERe55CeA?t=1384)
- A small UX fix (removing a premature "invalid email" error while typing) came directly from an internal bugs@ email alias anyone in or outside the company can use [why-design-matters-lessons-from-stripe-lyft-and-airbnb @ 25:23](https://youtu.be/CKfERe55CeA?t=1523)
- Redesigning a single onboarding email's hierarchy and call-to-action increased product conversion from that email by 20% [why-design-matters-lessons-from-stripe-lyft-and-airbnb @ 27:17](https://youtu.be/CKfERe55CeA?t=1637)
