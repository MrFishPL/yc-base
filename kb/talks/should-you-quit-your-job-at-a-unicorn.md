# Should You Quit Your Job At A Unicorn? — Compiled Truth

Dalton and Michael argue that unicorn status is a poor signal of a company's health, and lay out concrete metrics employees should check for themselves before deciding whether to stay, leave, or job-hop.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Of roughly 1,400 unicorns, the hosts estimate only about a third will have a successful IPO, meaning employee equity at the rest is at risk [should-you-quit-your-job-at-a-unicorn @ 01:27](https://youtu.be/chAoH_AeGAg?t=87)
- Because option strike prices are tied to the valuation at which a company last raised, late joiners at an overvalued or later-sold unicorn are likely to end up underwater [should-you-quit-your-job-at-a-unicorn @ 02:10](https://youtu.be/chAoH_AeGAg?t=130)
- The first thing to check as an employee is revenue: a unicorn making $100M+ in revenue "pencils out," but one at $50M or less should prompt questions about retention, pricing, and product-market fit [should-you-quit-your-job-at-a-unicorn @ 04:07](https://youtu.be/chAoH_AeGAg?t=247)
- The simplest leading indicator of a good product is whether customers who bought it actually onboard, use it, and renew [should-you-quit-your-job-at-a-unicorn @ 05:33](https://youtu.be/chAoH_AeGAg?t=333)
- Fundraising announcements are an unreliable outside signal because a raise may have closed a year earlier and only be announced now to look healthy [should-you-quit-your-job-at-a-unicorn @ 06:34](https://youtu.be/chAoH_AeGAg?t=394)
- Moving from a late-stage unicorn to an earlier-stage company with similar revenue but a lower valuation can mean a better option strike price and more responsibility [should-you-quit-your-job-at-a-unicorn @ 07:09](https://youtu.be/chAoH_AeGAg?t=429)
- Job hopping between unicorns every 12-18 months makes it hard to build real expertise or a strong career [should-you-quit-your-job-at-a-unicorn @ 09:15](https://youtu.be/chAoH_AeGAg?t=555)
- Employees should watch whether founders and senior management seem checked out or disconnected from reality, citing WeWork as a classic example of leadership claiming things are "better than ever" despite bad numbers [should-you-quit-your-job-at-a-unicorn @ 10:22](https://youtu.be/chAoH_AeGAg?t=622)
- A company in decline often shows a malaise where good people have left and remaining staff do "make work" to avoid layoffs rather than serve customers [should-you-quit-your-job-at-a-unicorn @ 11:19](https://youtu.be/chAoH_AeGAg?t=679)
- Employees have better visibility into a company's true health than outside investors or the press, so they should trust their own first-principles analysis over hype [should-you-quit-your-job-at-a-unicorn @ 09:15](https://youtu.be/chAoH_AeGAg?t=555)
