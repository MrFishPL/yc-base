# Team and Execution with Sam Altman (How to Start a Startup 2014: Lecture 2) — Compiled Truth

Sam Altman argues that co-founder choice, disciplined hiring, and relentless execution — especially focus and intensity — are what separate startups that survive from those that fall apart.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Co-founder blowups are the number one cause of early startup death, yet founders often treat picking a co-founder with less care than hiring; one YC batch saw all nine teams that added a random late co-founder fall apart within a year [team-and-execution-with-sam-altman-how-to-start-a-startup-20 @ 02:54](https://youtu.be/crMVJAf5TjA?t=174)
- A bad early hire in the first three to five employees can kill a startup outright, so the goal in the early days should be to avoid hiring at all until there is a desperate need [team-and-execution-with-sam-altman-how-to-start-a-startup-20 @ 08:12](https://youtu.be/crMVJAf5TjA?t=492)
- The best source of candidates by far is personal referrals from founders and existing employees, and most great tech companies were built this way for at least their first 100 hires [team-and-execution-with-sam-altman-how-to-start-a-startup-20 @ 13:17](https://youtu.be/crMVJAf5TjA?t=797)
- When hiring, look for three things — is the person smart, do they get things done, and do you want to spend a lot of time around them — and working together on a short project beats an interview [team-and-execution-with-sam-altman-how-to-start-a-startup-20 @ 14:50](https://youtu.be/crMVJAf5TjA?t=890)
- Founders should aim to give roughly 10% of the company to the first 10 employees, since founders tend to be too stingy with employee equity and too generous with investor equity [team-and-execution-with-sam-altman-how-to-start-a-startup-20 @ 18:26](https://youtu.be/crMVJAf5TjA?t=1106)
- All co-founders need vesting (typically four years with a one-year cliff) so that a co-founder who leaves early doesn't leave dead equity weight that scares off investors [team-and-execution-with-sam-altman-how-to-start-a-startup-20 @ 26:33](https://youtu.be/crMVJAf5TjA?t=1593)
- Founders can only focus on two or three priorities a day, must say no roughly 97% of the time, and should repeat the company's key goals far more often than feels necessary [team-and-execution-with-sam-altman-how-to-start-a-startup-20 @ 32:08](https://youtu.be/crMVJAf5TjA?t=1928)
- Growth and momentum are the lifeblood of a startup — always know your metrics, hold a weekly review, and be suspicious anytime the team says it's "not focused on growth right now" [team-and-execution-with-sam-altman-how-to-start-a-startup-20 @ 34:38](https://youtu.be/crMVJAf5TjA?t=2078)
- When momentum sags, the fix is small wins (a board member's line: "sales fix everything in a startup"), not rallying speeches, which employees tune out when the company isn't winning [team-and-execution-with-sam-altman-how-to-start-a-startup-20 @ 42:53](https://youtu.be/crMVJAf5TjA?t=2573)
- Don't worry about a competitor until they are actually beating you with a real shipped product, since press releases are easier to write than code, and code is easier than a great product [team-and-execution-with-sam-altman-how-to-start-a-startup-20 @ 45:09](https://youtu.be/crMVJAf5TjA?t=2709)
