# Q&A with YC Partners at Startup School SV 2016 — Compiled Truth

A panel of YC partners answers crowdsourced founder questions on timing, co-founders, tools, growth vs. product tradeoffs, and what makes a strong YC application.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- If you have an idea, start now rather than waiting to become a domain expert first — you can build that expertise on your way, but within a year or two you should be the expert [q-a-with-yc-partners-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 00:46](https://youtu.be/bpJQN2WyD8g?t=46)
- To find startup ideas, look at problems in your own life, your family's, and your community's, and think about what you want your life's work — not just a "warm-up startup" — to be [q-a-with-yc-partners-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 01:42](https://youtu.be/bpJQN2WyD8g?t=102)
- Solo founders can get into YC, but they effectively need to be superhuman since they must cover selling, building, and fundraising alone, whereas co-founders can split those roles [q-a-with-yc-partners-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 03:32](https://youtu.be/bpJQN2WyD8g?t=212)
- Don't waste energy on funky voting structures, unusual vesting schedules, or secrecy about your idea — telling more people your idea is more likely to surface useful feedback than to create copycats [q-a-with-yc-partners-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 07:38](https://youtu.be/bpJQN2WyD8g?t=458)
- If retention is weak (e.g., only 10% of users return monthly), stop pushing PR and growth and focus entirely on the product and why people aren't sticking around before trying to grow further [q-a-with-yc-partners-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 11:30](https://youtu.be/bpJQN2WyD8g?t=690)
- When reading YC applications, partners look for founders who are action-oriented — people who have shipped something and given it to real users are rated far higher than those still asking permission to start [q-a-with-yc-partners-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 13:36](https://youtu.be/bpJQN2WyD8g?t=816)
- Evidence that you built something and got people to pay for it makes an application far stronger than polishing prose or video production quality [q-a-with-yc-partners-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 15:17](https://youtu.be/bpJQN2WyD8g?t=917)
- There is no "too early" or "too late" to apply to YC, and about half of a recent batch's companies had been rejected on a prior application before eventually getting in [q-a-with-yc-partners-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 19:39](https://youtu.be/bpJQN2WyD8g?t=1179)
- Founders should watch out for "cargo culting" startup culture (forced networking, copying famous founders' habits) and instead design their own working style since a startup lets you control every aspect of how you work [q-a-with-yc-partners-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 24:05](https://youtu.be/bpJQN2WyD8g?t=1445)
- Founders should find a few trusted people to be honest with about struggles, since the public narrative of startups "always killing it" is not reality and bottling that up adds unnecessary strain [q-a-with-yc-partners-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 25:50](https://youtu.be/bpJQN2WyD8g?t=1550)
