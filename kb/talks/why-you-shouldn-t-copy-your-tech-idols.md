# Why You Shouldn't Copy Your Tech Idols — Compiled Truth

Prominent figures like Sam Altman, Elon Musk, and Peter Thiel give advice today that doesn't match the ordinary, gradual path they actually took to succeed, so founders should study their real histories rather than literally copying their current-day advice.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Sam Altman's advice to go big and raise huge amounts of money reflects OpenAI-era thinking, but his own first startup, Loopt, raised a relatively small amount of money with a small team and iterated normally [why-you-shouldn-t-copy-your-tech-idols @ 03:45](https://youtu.be/09mXPGVkfVA?t=225)
- Sam used a pretty standard YC path (go to a good school, do YC, start a normal startup) to build the network and career that eventually led to OpenAI [why-you-shouldn-t-copy-your-tech-idols @ 04:46](https://youtu.be/09mXPGVkfVA?t=286)
- Literally 99.9999% of founders couldn't copy Sam's move of starting a lab like OpenAI, and even Sam couldn't have started there himself without the standard path first [why-you-shouldn-t-copy-your-tech-idols @ 05:29](https://youtu.be/09mXPGVkfVA?t=329)
- Elon Musk has said his early motivation for Zip2 and PayPal was purely to make money fast so he could eventually fund going to space, meaning his early startups were conventional, not deep-tech [why-you-shouldn-t-copy-your-tech-idols @ 06:44](https://youtu.be/09mXPGVkfVA?t=404)
- Elon later personally bailed out Tesla from his own checking account, meaning Tesla could not have survived without the wealth he built from his earlier, conventional startup exits [why-you-shouldn-t-copy-your-tech-idols @ 08:10](https://youtu.be/09mXPGVkfVA?t=490)
- Peter Thiel publicly critiques college, but he himself completed undergrad and a Stanford law degree and worked at a prestigious law firm and bank before his later career [why-you-shouldn-t-copy-your-tech-idols @ 09:31](https://youtu.be/09mXPGVkfVA?t=571)
- People giving this kind of advice are usually well-intentioned but are effectively telling their past selves what they wish they'd known, having become blind to their own backstory [why-you-shouldn-t-copy-your-tech-idols @ 10:53](https://youtu.be/09mXPGVkfVA?t=653)
- YC tries to avoid this trap by having partners share their own full, unfiltered backstories including their mistakes at the start of each batch [why-you-shouldn-t-copy-your-tech-idols @ 12:07](https://youtu.be/09mXPGVkfVA?t=727)
- A useful gut check for advice-giving is to imagine what you'd tell your own kids, which tends to produce more honest and situational advice than generic public commentary [why-you-shouldn-t-copy-your-tech-idols @ 13:19](https://youtu.be/09mXPGVkfVA?t=799)
