# Design Tips to Convert More Customers | Design Review — Compiled Truth

Optimizely co-founder Pete Koomen live-reviews founder landing pages, showing that mapping the conversion funnel and ruthlessly cutting friction and competing calls-to-action is what actually moves signups.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A conversion funnel is the set of steps a user takes from landing on a site to the goal action, and every step is a place to measure and cut drop-off [design-tips-to-convert-more-customers-design-review @ 01:05](https://youtu.be/tJTxDqjO4vg?t=65)
- Having too many competing calls-to-action (sign up, click to start, open, search) creates a paradox of choice that reduces the odds anyone takes action, so pages should reduce down to a single clear CTA [design-tips-to-convert-more-customers-design-review @ 02:24](https://youtu.be/tJTxDqjO4vg?t=144)
- Repeating the same call-to-action throughout the page (top, middle, bottom) keeps reminding convinced visitors to act and raises conversion [design-tips-to-convert-more-customers-design-review @ 07:43](https://youtu.be/tJTxDqjO4vg?t=463)
- Offering login via existing accounts (e.g. Google) instead of forcing a new username/password reduces signup friction and can meaningfully increase conversion [design-tips-to-convert-more-customers-design-review @ 08:16](https://youtu.be/tJTxDqjO4vg?t=496)
- Every product has an "aha moment," and the best way to improve conversion is to map every step to that moment and cut out any steps that aren't necessary to reach it [design-tips-to-convert-more-customers-design-review @ 10:30](https://youtu.be/tJTxDqjO4vg?t=630)
- Founders who map their funnel on a whiteboard often discover the aha moment was buried at step seven and can cut steps two through five to move it up to step two [design-tips-to-convert-more-customers-design-review @ 11:42](https://youtu.be/tJTxDqjO4vg?t=702)
- Explicitly addressing objections on the page itself, like stating "no credit card required" next to a free trial CTA, removes fear and makes clicking feel low-risk [design-tips-to-convert-more-customers-design-review @ 11:58](https://youtu.be/tJTxDqjO4vg?t=718)
- Showing social proof, like the number of customers or specific companies using the product, builds trust especially for high-stakes purchases like AI-written patents [design-tips-to-convert-more-customers-design-review @ 12:31](https://youtu.be/tJTxDqjO4vg?t=751)
- Burying important information behind tabs, sliders, or many sequential screens means most visitors never see it, so key content should be placed directly on the page [design-tips-to-convert-more-customers-design-review @ 20:36](https://youtu.be/tJTxDqjO4vg?t=1236)
- Vague, jargon-heavy headlines that don't state plainly what the product does or who it's for cause confusion and drop-off, even when the product itself is compelling [design-tips-to-convert-more-customers-design-review @ 18:42](https://youtu.be/tJTxDqjO4vg?t=1122)
