# Consumer Startup Metrics | Startup School — Compiled Truth

A former Monzo founder walks through the core metrics that matter for consumer startups: growth rate, organic vs. paid acquisition, unit economics, retention, and net promoter score.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A good consumer growth rate is 15% month-over-month (5x/year); 10% is okay (roughly triples/year); 5% or lower is unlikely to reach breakout success [consumer-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 00:54](https://youtu.be/fdD4y4Civp4?t=54)
- Organic growth comes from virality (users spreading the product through use) and network effects (the product gets more valuable as more people join), and both should be deliberately engineered into the product [consumer-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 01:40](https://youtu.be/fdD4y4Civp4?t=100)
- Paid referral schemes should be treated as paid acquisition, not organic, and founders must watch for cannibalization (paying for referrals that would have happened anyway) and fraud [consumer-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 05:13](https://youtu.be/fdD4y4Civp4?t=313)
- Customer acquisition cost must be tracked per channel and measured to an active, monetized, retaining user rather than to a raw sign-up, since a cheap channel can bring deeply unprofitable customers [consumer-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 07:22](https://youtu.be/fdD4y4Civp4?t=442)
- The best consumer companies get 80%+ of growth organically (even close to 100% for the very best), a 50/50 split is only okay, and relying on paid ads long-term is dangerous because platforms extract maximum value and can wipe out a business overnight with tracking changes [consumer-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 08:37](https://youtu.be/fdD4y4Civp4?t=517)
- Unit economics means revenue per customer minus that customer's variable/incremental costs, and scaling negative unit economics (as Monzo did early on) can burn a lot of capital before being fixed [consumer-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 10:57](https://youtu.be/fdD4y4Civp4?t=657)
- To measure retention in consumer products, find a "magic moment" — an early user behavior correlated with long-term retention, like adding 7 friends in 10 days at Facebook or adding 3 phonebook contacts at Monzo — and re-engineer onboarding to drive users to it [consumer-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 14:45](https://youtu.be/fdD4y4Civp4?t=885)
- Net promoter score (NPS) should be at least +50 for a new consumer company to signal strong word-of-mouth potential, and it must be measured consistently or the results become meaningless [consumer-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 17:06](https://youtu.be/fdD4y4Civp4?t=1026)
- Asking a qualitative follow-up question after the NPS score, and fixing the issues raised by detractors, is a reliable way to improve NPS over time [consumer-startup-metrics-startup-school @ 19:58](https://youtu.be/fdD4y4Civp4?t=1198)
