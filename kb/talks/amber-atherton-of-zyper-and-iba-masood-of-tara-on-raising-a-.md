# Amber Atherton of Zyper and Iba Masood of TARA on Raising a Series A as a Female Founder — Compiled Truth

Two YC founders who both raised Series A rounds compare notes on running a tight fundraising process, choosing the right partner over the highest valuation, and building culture and hiring practices afterward.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Writing an investment memo that essentially "does the VC's job for them" — explaining the problem, market size, and risks up front — was one of the most effective prep steps [amber-atherton-of-zyper-and-iba-masood-of-tara-on-raising-a- @ 06:23](https://youtu.be/TogTe74Zg7w?t=383)
- Iba's Series A process went from first coffee meeting to signed term sheet in just nine days, though closing took another ~60 days for legal work [amber-atherton-of-zyper-and-iba-masood-of-tara-on-raising-a- @ 08:44](https://youtu.be/TogTe74Zg7w?t=524)
- Founders should never fake urgency or claim other term sheets exist to pressure investors, because the startup community is small and dishonesty backfires [amber-atherton-of-zyper-and-iba-masood-of-tara-on-raising-a- @ 16:52](https://youtu.be/TogTe74Zg7w?t=1012)
- Don't maximize for valuation — a higher valuation can misalign incentives, so it's better to prioritize working with the right partner even at a lower offer [amber-atherton-of-zyper-and-iba-masood-of-tara-on-raising-a- @ 15:16](https://youtu.be/TogTe74Zg7w?t=916)
- Vet investors the way you'd vet a hire: notice whether they ask sharp questions on customer calls and show genuine enthusiasm for the product as an early signal of real interest [amber-atherton-of-zyper-and-iba-masood-of-tara-on-raising-a- @ 21:09](https://youtu.be/TogTe74Zg7w?t=1269)
- The typical process moves from a single-partner coffee or lunch meeting to a full-partnership meeting, so founders should have their data room organized and ready in advance [amber-atherton-of-zyper-and-iba-masood-of-tara-on-raising-a- @ 23:00](https://youtu.be/TogTe74Zg7w?t=1380)
- Working with an executive coach ahead of fundraising helped build the confidence needed to answer "how big can this get" pitch questions [amber-atherton-of-zyper-and-iba-masood-of-tara-on-raising-a- @ 39:06](https://youtu.be/TogTe74Zg7w?t=2346)
- After raising, Amber realized she'd neglected company culture while heads-down on revenue, and worked with her early team to define values, landing on the acronym "Tricep Flex" [amber-atherton-of-zyper-and-iba-masood-of-tara-on-raising-a- @ 47:37](https://youtu.be/TogTe74Zg7w?t=2857)
- Hiring "late-stage" people from big companies is very different from hiring people who were actually among the first 20 employees somewhere, and founders often mistake the two [amber-atherton-of-zyper-and-iba-masood-of-tara-on-raising-a- @ 50:05](https://youtu.be/TogTe74Zg7w?t=3005)
- Post-Series A, the team instituted OKRs for the first time, with three lessons: don't make OKRs too big, make sure they're measurable, and ensure team buy-in in setting them [amber-atherton-of-zyper-and-iba-masood-of-tara-on-raising-a- @ 57:29](https://youtu.be/TogTe74Zg7w?t=3449)
