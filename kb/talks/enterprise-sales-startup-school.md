# Enterprise Sales | Startup School — Compiled Truth

Pete Koomen, Optimizely co-founder and YC group partner, walks founders step by step through the enterprise sales funnel — prospecting, outreach, qualification, demo, pricing, closing, and implementation — arguing that founders themselves, not hired salespeople, must sell before product-market fit.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Sales before product-market fit is fundamentally entrepreneurial and is a founder's job, not something you can outsource to a hired salesperson or a business co-founder [enterprise-sales-startup-school @ 01:51](https://youtu.be/0fKYVl12VTA?t=111)
- Technical founders have a natural edge in selling because they have deep expertise in the problem and genuine conviction that their product solves it [enterprise-sales-startup-school @ 02:32](https://youtu.be/0fKYVl12VTA?t=152)
- Prospecting should start from a clear sales hypothesis ("customer X has problem Y and our product solves it") before using tools like BuiltWith, Apollo, or LinkedIn Sales Navigator to build a target list [enterprise-sales-startup-school @ 03:39](https://youtu.be/0fKYVl12VTA?t=219)
- A major founder anti-pattern is talking only to whoever will take a call — such as selling enterprise-only software to startups, or trying to go bottom-up with a product that actually needs top-down organizational buy-in — because it creates a false sense of progress [enterprise-sales-startup-school @ 07:08](https://youtu.be/0fKYVl12VTA?t=428)
- The first call's job is not to pitch but to qualify the prospect (do they have the problem, budget, and decision authority) and to book a demo, and most founders fail by not asking enough questions [enterprise-sales-startup-school @ 10:23](https://youtu.be/0fKYVl12VTA?t=623)
- A great demo is like a movie script recapping the customer's specific problem and telling a personalized story of how the product solves it, rather than a generic feature tour [enterprise-sales-startup-school @ 12:47](https://youtu.be/0fKYVl12VTA?t=767)
- Founders most often err by pricing too low or giving the product away free, when in fact higher prices filter for customers who are genuinely serious, as illustrated by a prospect who still bought after being quoted 5x what they ultimately paid [enterprise-sales-startup-school @ 16:23](https://youtu.be/0fKYVl12VTA?t=983)
- Early pricing conversations should be treated as experiments, since there is no formula, and founders should pick an uncomfortable price and learn from the prospect's reaction [enterprise-sales-startup-school @ 17:39](https://youtu.be/0fKYVl12VTA?t=1059)
- Closing is a multi-step process involving procurement, legal, and compliance reviews, and founders should proactively ask about these hurdles and use simple contract templates (like YC's Common Paper) to avoid surprises [enterprise-sales-startup-school @ 18:20](https://youtu.be/0fKYVl12VTA?t=1100)
- Implementation is the founder's responsibility, not the customer's — Optimizely lost renewals after customers who paid six figures never actually used the product because founders left rollout entirely to the customer's team [enterprise-sales-startup-school @ 20:02](https://youtu.be/0fKYVl12VTA?t=1202)
