# What Everyone Is Getting Wrong About AI And Jobs — Compiled Truth

This YC essay-video argues that both AI doomers and skeptics are wrong: history shows technology that cuts the cost of a service tends to increase demand for it (Jevons paradox) rather than destroy the jobs around it, so AI will transform work more than eliminate it.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Both AI-jobs camps are flawed: doomers predicting near-universal unemployment and skeptics dismissing AI as hype are each missing that history suggests AI will transform the economy without destroying it [what-everyone-is-getting-wrong-about-ai-and-jobs @ 00:46](https://youtu.be/IqwSb2hO1jE?t=46)
- Geoffrey Hinton predicted in 2016 that radiologist training should stop within 5 years, but nearly a decade later demand for radiologists is at an all-time high despite state-of-the-art diagnostic AI [what-everyone-is-getting-wrong-about-ai-and-jobs @ 01:12](https://youtu.be/IqwSb2hO1jE?t=72)
- When AI sped up one part of radiologists' work (reading scans), cheaper scans led to more scans, which increased demand for radiologists' complex diagnosis and treatment-planning work — an example of Jevons paradox [what-everyone-is-getting-wrong-about-ai-and-jobs @ 02:04](https://youtu.be/IqwSb2hO1jE?t=124)
- Jevons paradox originated from William Stanley Jevons' 19th-century observation that more efficient coal use increased, rather than decreased, total coal consumption [what-everyone-is-getting-wrong-about-ai-and-jobs @ 02:32](https://youtu.be/IqwSb2hO1jE?t=152)
- Historical parallels back this up: cheaper 1960s containerized shipping birthed freight/logistics empires, and 2010s cheap cloud computing turned server admins into DevOps/cloud architects rather than eliminating IT jobs [what-everyone-is-getting-wrong-about-ai-and-jobs @ 03:30](https://youtu.be/IqwSb2hO1jE?t=210)
- Box CEO Aaron Levie's framing is cited: when the cost of doing work goes down, demand for it goes up, often more than people expect, because of pent-up latent demand [what-everyone-is-getting-wrong-about-ai-and-jobs @ 04:31](https://youtu.be/IqwSb2hO1jE?t=271)
- Andrej Karpathy's view is that AI will first automate rote, low-context, mistake-tolerant jobs like customer service and data entry, but many of these get refactored into supervisor/manager-of-agents roles rather than vanishing [what-everyone-is-getting-wrong-about-ai-and-jobs @ 05:23](https://youtu.be/IqwSb2hO1jE?t=323)
- YC companies illustrate this: AOKA (AI sales agent for plumbing/HVAC) frees customer service staff for higher-value work, and Tenor automates healthcare paperwork so admin roles shift from data entry to patient-care coordination [what-everyone-is-getting-wrong-about-ai-and-jobs @ 05:48](https://youtu.be/IqwSb2hO1jE?t=348)
- The takeaway for AI founders: don't underestimate the AI transformation (like Krugman comparing the internet to a fax machine), but also don't assume mass unemployment — build for the real, ongoing transformation [what-everyone-is-getting-wrong-about-ai-and-jobs @ 07:04](https://youtu.be/IqwSb2hO1jE?t=424)
