# Stripe Head of Design Katie Dill Reviews Startup Websites — Compiled Truth

Stripe's head of design Katie Dill live-reviews five YC startup landing pages, showing how first impressions, information hierarchy, and copy clarity drive whether visitors understand a product and convert.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A site's first job is to fight distraction ("TikTok is calling") by making the value clear at every scroll step, and it's a bad sign if you have to tell users to scroll [stripe-head-of-design-katie-dill-reviews-startup-websites @ 01:40](https://youtu.be/DY5Z-uZ6ZMc?t=100)
- Every pixel should add value; redundant trust logos, decorative arrows, and glow effects that don't communicate anything should be cut [stripe-head-of-design-katie-dill-reviews-startup-websites @ 06:18](https://youtu.be/DY5Z-uZ6ZMc?t=378)
- To improve click-through, map the entire user journey (the ad or link that brought them, then every step after the CTA) because a good landing page can still fail if an earlier or later step misleads the visitor [stripe-head-of-design-katie-dill-reviews-startup-websites @ 09:42](https://youtu.be/DY5Z-uZ6ZMc?t=582)
- The best way to find your headline is to ask your own users how they'd describe the product, since their language often converts better than internal branding language [stripe-head-of-design-katie-dill-reviews-startup-websites @ 12:15](https://youtu.be/DY5Z-uZ6ZMc?t=735)
- Small consistency errors, like inconsistent capitalization between buttons, are a form of friction that erodes user trust in the product [stripe-head-of-design-katie-dill-reviews-startup-websites @ 12:48](https://youtu.be/DY5Z-uZ6ZMc?t=768)
- Before finalizing a design, write down the adjectives you want your brand to evoke and check whether visual choices (animations, colors, blobs) actually communicate those adjectives [stripe-head-of-design-katie-dill-reviews-startup-websites @ 14:38](https://youtu.be/DY5Z-uZ6ZMc?t=878)

- A B2B/technical company can absolutely use a fun, colorful, B2C-style design, but playful visuals must not come at the cost of readability or clarity about who the product is for [stripe-head-of-design-katie-dill-reviews-startup-websites @ 17:32](https://youtu.be/DY5Z-uZ6ZMc?t=1052)
