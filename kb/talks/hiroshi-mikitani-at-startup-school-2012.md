# Hiroshi Mikitani at Startup School 2012 — Compiled Truth

Rakuten's founder explains how he bootstrapped Japan's largest e-commerce company without VC funding by designing for cash-flow positivity and a deliberately patient, merchant-friendly business model.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Mikitani founded Rakuten in 1997 with only $200,000 of initial capital, never raised money from any VC, and took the company public in 2000 [hiroshi-mikitani-at-startup-school-2012 @ 00:19](https://youtu.be/tF7HXJJjRvY?t=19)
- The company was cash-flow positive from its second month because merchants were charged $500/month but had to pay six months upfront [hiroshi-mikitani-at-startup-school-2012 @ 02:56](https://youtu.be/tF7HXJJjRvY?t=176)
- Rakuten stayed patient for its first three years, refusing to rush the business model because Mikitani knew he'd be building this company for a long time [hiroshi-mikitani-at-startup-school-2012 @ 04:11](https://youtu.be/tF7HXJJjRvY?t=251)
- Instead of competing against retailers with technology like Amazon, Rakuten positioned itself as a liaison that helps small and medium merchants sell directly to consumers [hiroshi-mikitani-at-startup-school-2012 @ 05:06](https://youtu.be/tF7HXJJjRvY?t=306)
- Rakuten pursued two kinds of acquisitions: pure geographic expansion (buy.com, Play.com, PriceMinister) and additions to its ecosystem like Kobo for e-books [hiroshi-mikitani-at-startup-school-2012 @ 08:29](https://youtu.be/tF7HXJJjRvY?t=509)
- Scaling requires both innovation and a strong operational culture built on shared practices, numbers, and KPIs, even though most entrepreneurs resist running the numbers [hiroshi-mikitani-at-startup-school-2012 @ 11:48](https://youtu.be/tF7HXJJjRvY?t=708)
- Mikitani says he isn't afraid of competitors like Amazon or eBay; he learns from them but stays focused on Rakuten's own philosophical approach [hiroshi-mikitani-at-startup-school-2012 @ 17:56](https://youtu.be/tF7HXJJjRvY?t=1076)
- Rakuten converted its internal corporate language from Japanese to English to globalize, and despite initial public backlash, employee English scores rose about 200 points and 70% of new engineer hires are now non-Japanese [hiroshi-mikitani-at-startup-school-2012 @ 20:28](https://youtu.be/tF7HXJJjRvY?t=1228)
