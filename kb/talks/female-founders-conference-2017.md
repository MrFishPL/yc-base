# Female Founders Conference 2017 — Compiled Truth

A multi-speaker conference session on what separates massively successful startups from merely successful ones, and first-person founder stories on pivoting, fundraising, hiring, and company culture as women in a male-dominated industry.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The founders of truly huge startups are never in it mainly for money or to seem cool — they're fanatically interested in the problem itself, which makes them work harder and harder for acquirers to buy out [female-founders-conference-2017 @ 28:11](https://youtu.be/59c8yttMtHQ?t=1691)
- Most of the biggest startup ideas looked like bad or unpromising ideas at first (a site for a couple thousand Harvard students didn't sound like Facebook) — you need to be independent-minded enough to work on something that looks dubious to most people [female-founders-conference-2017 @ 30:41](https://youtu.be/59c8yttMtHQ?t=1841)
- Founders of the biggest companies have to be willing to morph from product-builder into manager as the company scales, even though the two skills are very different [female-founders-conference-2017 @ 34:37](https://youtu.be/59c8yttMtHQ?t=2077)
- After her first startup (a curated subscription box) failed from over-building product without validating demand, Avni Patel Thompson rebuilt Poppy by talking directly to parents, using SMS and off-the-shelf tools (Squarespace, Typeform, Stripe, Google Calendar, Excel) as a manual MVP, and setting a goal to grow bookings 10-20% every week [female-founders-conference-2017 @ 49:25](https://youtu.be/59c8yttMtHQ?t=2965)
- Being rejected by YC on a first application isn't fatal — reapplying after showing more growth and a stronger team (per the rejection's "reapply next batch" note) got Poppy funded the second time [female-founders-conference-2017 @ 52:39](https://youtu.be/59c8yttMtHQ?t=3159)
- Startup building isn't about product launches, it's about picking one key growth metric (for Poppy, bookings filled) and growing it consistently week over week [female-founders-conference-2017 @ 1:04:23](https://youtu.be/59c8yttMtHQ?t=3863)
- Laura Behrens Wu's advice from a mentor at Shippo's start was to "work on painkillers, not vitamins" — build something people can't live without, not a nice-to-have [female-founders-conference-2017 @ 1:39:05](https://youtu.be/59c8yttMtHQ?t=5945)
- Investors are like employees you can't fire — do back-channel reference checks on prospective investors just as they do on you, and communicate transparently even when things go wrong (co-founder departures, a lawsuit during diligence) [female-founders-conference-2017 @ 1:44:50](https://youtu.be/59c8yttMtHQ?t=6290)
- At NIO, Padmasree Warrior personally interviewed the first 150 hires because early hires set the hiring bar for everyone who comes after, and she mandated diverse interview panels to avoid leaders creating "micro-cultures" by hiring only people they already know [female-founders-conference-2017 @ 2:06:58](https://youtu.be/59c8yttMtHQ?t=7618)
- Aileen Lee moved from Kleiner Perkins to founding seed-stage Cowboy Ventures because large funds' return math forces them to chase only multi-billion-dollar outcomes, while smaller seed funds can be more hands-on and aligned with founders [female-founders-conference-2017 @ 2:48:37](https://youtu.be/59c8yttMtHQ?t=10117)
- Storytelling is a learnable skill, not an innate trait — many founders who started out weak storytellers became excellent ones through practice and feedback, which matters especially for women pitching a mostly male investor base [female-founders-conference-2017 @ 2:53:11](https://youtu.be/59c8yttMtHQ?t=10391)
