# Aaron Levie: Why Startups Win In The AI Era — Compiled Truth

Box CEO Aaron Levie argues AI has reopened a rare "new nouns and verbs" window for startups, unlike the cloud transition, because enterprises are already convinced AI matters and just need someone to build the agent-based solution.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Unlike the early cloud era, enterprises no longer need convincing that AI is the future because leaders have personally experienced ChatGPT; the barrier now is safe, reliable implementation, not belief [aaron-levie-why-startups-win-in-the-ai-era @ 06:12](https://youtu.be/uqc_vt95GJg?t=372)
- AI agents unlock enormous value because they can finally act on unstructured data (contracts, documents, presentations), which previously couldn't be queried or automated the way structured database data could [aaron-levie-why-startups-win-in-the-ai-era @ 07:49](https://youtu.be/uqc_vt95GJg?t=469)
- AI won't just cut jobs — a 50-person company that can act like a 500-person company via agents will likely grow into a 100-person company faster than it would have pre-AI, since more work becomes economically viable to attempt [aaron-levie-why-startups-win-in-the-ai-era @ 13:19](https://youtu.be/uqc_vt95GJg?t=799)
- After a decade (roughly 2008-2022) where most consumer and enterprise "nouns and verbs" had already been solved by incumbents, AI has created a genuinely new set of unsolved problems that startups are positioned to win [aaron-levie-why-startups-win-in-the-ai-era @ 17:53](https://youtu.be/uqc_vt95GJg?t=1073)
- The SaaS seat-based pricing model breaks down with agents because agents can perform unlimited work regardless of headcount, so pricing shifts to consumption tied to volume of work done, ideally anchored with a recurring baseline [aaron-levie-why-startups-win-in-the-ai-era @ 20:43](https://youtu.be/uqc_vt95GJg?t=1243)
- Long-term margin comes from the software and workflow layer built on top of AI tokens, not the tokens themselves — the more software you build around the model, the more pricing can exceed raw token cost [aaron-levie-why-startups-win-in-the-ai-era @ 22:44](https://youtu.be/uqc_vt95GJg?t=1364)
- Companies rarely build all their own software even when they technically could, per the "core vs. context" framework: businesses invest engineering effort only in what's core to their differentiation, not commodity functions like HR [aaron-levie-why-startups-win-in-the-ai-era @ 27:40](https://youtu.be/uqc_vt95GJg?t=1660)
- Incumbents like Workday will mostly build agents only for their existing install base, leaving huge whitespace for startups to serve the customers and use cases incumbents aren't reaching [aaron-levie-why-startups-win-in-the-ai-era @ 39:18](https://youtu.be/uqc_vt95GJg?t=2358)
- Startup advice: read Crossing the Chasm, Innovator's Dilemma, and Blue Ocean Strategy, get a great founding team, and only pursue markets that AI is truly transforming since the current opportunity window will close in two to three years [aaron-levie-why-startups-win-in-the-ai-era @ 30:59](https://youtu.be/uqc_vt95GJg?t=1859)
