# Fundraising Panel at Female Founders Conference 2016 — Compiled Truth

Four YC founders (L., Eligible, Lumoid, WayUp) trade war stories and tactics on raising money, from bootstrapping to profitability before ever pitching to keeping composure when an investor calls your company a scam.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Caitlin Gleason bootstrapped Eligible by focusing on what she was good at (selling) — landing real deals and revenue before raising made investors far more willing to write checks [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2016 @ 02:42](https://youtu.be/Ts9SOxgsbtw?t=162)
- Liz Wessel emailed roughly 250 investors and met 85 people before closing WayUp's first round eight months later, and the early rejection made later fundraising feel far less daunting [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2016 @ 04:43](https://youtu.be/Ts9SOxgsbtw?t=283)
- Liz Wessel skipped making a pitch deck and instead relied on knowing every one of her numbers cold so she could answer any investor question on the spot [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2016 @ 09:11](https://youtu.be/Ts9SOxgsbtw?t=551)
- Not every company needs to raise, and not every company needs to raise right now — founders should look at their own metrics and bottom line rather than raise just because it's the valley norm [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2016 @ 15:21](https://youtu.be/Ts9SOxgsbtw?t=921)
- Panelists advise reading resources like Fred Wilson's "MBA Mondays" to learn deal terms in advance, since not knowing a term mid-negotiation looks bad in the room [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2016 @ 13:52](https://youtu.be/Ts9SOxgsbtw?t=832)
- A "no" from an investor isn't necessarily final — one investor said no three times before ultimately investing once he saw the traction grow month over month [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2016 @ 17:15](https://youtu.be/Ts9SOxgsbtw?t=1035)
- Smart investors rarely say an outright "no" — soft deflections like "come back once someone else is leading it" or "let me talk to my partners" usually mean no, so founders shouldn't wait around for them to change their mind [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2016 @ 17:50](https://youtu.be/Ts9SOxgsbtw?t=1070)
- Confidence in a pitch means knowing your key performance indicators, getting to them quickly, and treating the meeting as a conversation rather than a monologue [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2016 @ 19:00](https://youtu.be/Ts9SOxgsbtw?t=1140)
- One founder worked with a YC partner to reshape her pitch into the story of how the company could become a multi-billion dollar business, which changed investor reactions dramatically [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2016 @ 20:26](https://youtu.be/Ts9SOxgsbtw?t=1226)
- A founder recounted an investor meeting where the investor angrily accused her company of being a scam, and she chose to stay composed in the room and process the emotion afterward rather than react [fundraising-panel-at-female-founders-conference-2016 @ 21:44](https://youtu.be/Ts9SOxgsbtw?t=1304)
