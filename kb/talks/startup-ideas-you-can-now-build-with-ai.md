# Startup Ideas You Can Now Build With AI — Compiled Truth

AI has reopened old, previously-failed startup categories (recruiting marketplaces, tech-enabled services, edtech personalization) because evaluation, personalization, and knowledge work that once required years of data collection or large human ops teams can now be done cheaply with LLMs from day one.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Recruiting marketplaces like Triple Byte once needed years of building proprietary labeled data to evaluate candidates; companies like Mercor can now do that evaluation with LLMs on day one, letting them expand across knowledge-work verticals quickly [startup-ideas-you-can-now-build-with-ai @ 02:29](https://youtu.be/K4s6Cgicw_A?t=149)
- A useful exercise for any three- or four-sided marketplace is to ask what happens if LLMs replace one side of it, since the "idea maze" has shifted entirely [startup-ideas-you-can-now-build-with-ai @ 03:48](https://youtu.be/K4s6Cgicw_A?t=228)
- Better AI products don't automatically get more distribution; consumer AI apps still need to solve distribution and monetization just as hard as before [startup-ideas-you-can-now-build-with-ai @ 09:49](https://youtu.be/K4s6Cgicw_A?t=589)
- When companies stop viewing a product as "software as a service" and instead see it as replacing an entire team (support, analytics), they pay dramatically more, and the same logic applies to consumer categories like tutoring [startup-ideas-you-can-now-build-with-ai @ 13:21](https://youtu.be/K4s6Cgicw_A?t=801)
- The "tech-enabled full-stack services" wave of the 2010s (Triple Byte, Atrium, WeWork) largely failed because those businesses lacked software-level gross margins, not because the "do everything" thesis was wrong [startup-ideas-you-can-now-build-with-ai @ 25:24](https://youtu.be/K4s6Cgicw_A?t=1524)
- AI now lets "full-stack 2.0" startups replace large human ops teams with agents, making full-stack companies look like software companies under the hood for the first time, as seen with Legora in legal [startup-ideas-you-can-now-build-with-ai @ 29:56](https://youtu.be/K4s6Cgicw_A?t=1796)
- Companies like Replicate, Ollama, and Deepgram spent years quietly building ML infrastructure before their market existed, only exploding once the underlying models (diffusion models, Llama, voice agents) became good enough [startup-ideas-you-can-now-build-with-ai @ 34:02](https://youtu.be/K4s6Cgicw_A?t=2042)
- Old startup canon like "sell before you build" and rigorous lean-startup customer discovery is outdated in the AI era; following your own curiosity at the technological edge is now more likely to surface great ideas [startup-ideas-you-can-now-build-with-ai @ 37:17](https://youtu.be/K4s6Cgicw_A?t=2237)
- Durable moats in AI products still come from classic sources: brand, switching costs, and integrations, not from the AI model itself [startup-ideas-you-can-now-build-with-ai @ 14:43](https://youtu.be/K4s6Cgicw_A?t=883)
