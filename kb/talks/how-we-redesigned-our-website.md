# How We Redesigned Our Website — Compiled Truth

YC's design team walks through why and how they rebuilt ycombinator.com, moving from a utilitarian B2B-SaaS-style layout toward a storytelling homepage centered on founders, and describes prototyping the new site directly with an AI coding tool instead of static Figma mockups.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The old YC homepage (built ~4-5 years earlier during COVID) felt like a generic B2B SaaS template and underplayed the founders behind top YC companies in favor of just showing logos [how-we-redesigned-our-website @ 01:28](https://youtu.be/K5JoLAauzq4?t=88)
- Vague claims like "the results speak for themselves" without quantification were identified as weak messaging that should instead state the actual outcome [how-we-redesigned-our-website @ 02:44](https://youtu.be/K5JoLAauzq4?t=164)
- The redesign's goal was to not "sell a program" but to make visitors dream, drawing on Paul Graham's use of the word "formidable" to describe extraordinary founders [how-we-redesigned-our-website @ 04:09](https://youtu.be/K5JoLAauzq4?t=249)
- The new homepage shows before/after photos of founders to let early-stage builders see themselves in the trajectory of past YC founders [how-we-redesigned-our-website @ 05:09](https://youtu.be/K5JoLAauzq4?t=309)
- The core "what is YC" paragraph deliberately kept Paul Graham's original copy from the site 15 years ago, only updating details like batch frequency and location [how-we-redesigned-our-website @ 05:46](https://youtu.be/K5JoLAauzq4?t=346)
- Founder testimonials were gathered as short interview sound bites and stitched together so the experience of YC is described in founders' own words rather than YC's marketing language, making it more credible [how-we-redesigned-our-website @ 06:56](https://youtu.be/K5JoLAauzq4?t=416)
- The team deliberately omitted an apply-button CTA in the main storytelling sections, reasoning that this isn't a conversion-optimization SaaS page and an extra CTA would just add noise to the narrative [how-we-redesigned-our-website @ 13:18](https://youtu.be/K5JoLAauzq4?t=798)
- After starting in Figma and feeling constrained, the team switched to building directly in a new code repo, chatting with Opus 4.5 in Cursor to iteratively prototype interactive sections live [how-we-redesigned-our-website @ 14:33](https://youtu.be/K5JoLAauzq4?t=873)
- Prototyping live (rather than in static design tools) let the team discover complex interactive concepts, like a before/after founder carousel, that they don't believe they'd have arrived at in Figma [how-we-redesigned-our-website @ 15:49](https://youtu.be/K5JoLAauzq4?t=949)
- Using AI coding tools freed the designers to spend most of their time on interaction design and storytelling rather than on basic implementation [how-we-redesigned-our-website @ 16:39](https://youtu.be/K5JoLAauzq4?t=999)
