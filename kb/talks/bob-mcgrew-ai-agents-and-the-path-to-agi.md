# Bob McGrew: AI Agents And The Path To AGI — Compiled Truth

Former OpenAI Chief Research Officer Bob McGrew traces how scale, reasoning, and test-time compute unlocked reliable AI agents, and argues founders should build on the frontier model first rather than optimizing cost too early.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Reasoning and test-time compute are unlocking reliable agents that can take actions on your behalf, something long theoretically possible but never good enough before [bob-mcgrew-ai-agents-and-the-path-to-agi @ 00:15](https://youtu.be/eW7rUtYHD9U?t=15)
- OpenAI's early "scale is the path" belief was forged by Dota 2 and robot-hand projects, which showed neural networks could generalize from huge amounts of experience, and this later applied directly to GPT-1 through GPT-4 [bob-mcgrew-ai-agents-and-the-path-to-agi @ 03:30](https://youtu.be/eW7rUtYHD9U?t=210)
- OpenAI avoided academia's credit-obsession problem early on by citing papers as "OpenAI" rather than crediting individual first authors, channeling recognition into internal reputation instead [bob-mcgrew-ai-agents-and-the-path-to-agi @ 07:34](https://youtu.be/eW7rUtYHD9U?t=454)
- The field is reaching a bottleneck in pretraining and data, but reasoning/test-time compute is the new mechanism extending progress, similar to how Moore's Law persisted via new S-curves after Dennard scaling broke [bob-mcgrew-ai-agents-and-the-path-to-agi @ 12:09](https://youtu.be/eW7rUtYHD9U?t=729)
- Going from 90% to 99% (or 99% to 99.9%) reliability historically required an order-of-magnitude compute increase via bigger models, and now reasoning lets you get there by letting models "think" longer instead [bob-mcgrew-ai-agents-and-the-path-to-agi @ 15:52](https://youtu.be/eW7rUtYHD9U?t=952)
- His advice to AI founders: start by building on the very best frontier model to prove out the idea, and only distill to a cheaper/smaller model once you've found where the value is, because time-to-market matters more than cost at that stage [bob-mcgrew-ai-agents-and-the-path-to-agi @ 17:25](https://youtu.be/eW7rUtYHD9U?t=1045)
- Drawing on his Palantir experience, he argues AI adoption is slow not because intelligence or data is missing but because a "forward deployed engineer" function is needed to package intelligence into software tuned to what each customer actually needs [bob-mcgrew-ai-agents-and-the-path-to-agi @ 22:26](https://youtu.be/eW7rUtYHD9U?t=1346)
- Robotics companies today are roughly where LLM companies were five years ago, and he expects a "ChatGPT moment" for robotics within the next five years as foundation models for robots mature [bob-mcgrew-ai-agents-and-the-path-to-agi @ 28:19](https://youtu.be/eW7rUtYHD9U?t=1699)
- He predicts two enduring human roles in an AI-saturated future: the "lone genius" who is massively leveraged by AI tools, and the "manager" who runs a mostly-AI firm [bob-mcgrew-ai-agents-and-the-path-to-agi @ 25:54](https://youtu.be/eW7rUtYHD9U?t=1554)
