# Scaling and the Road to Human-Level AI | Anthropic Co-founder Jared Kaplan — Compiled Truth

Anthropic co-founder Jared Kaplan argues that predictable scaling laws in both pre-training and reinforcement learning are the main engine of AI progress, and that the length of tasks AI can reliably do is doubling roughly every 7 months, with big implications for what founders should build now.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Scaling laws for pre-training show precise, predictable improvements in AI performance across many orders of magnitude of compute, data, and model size [scaling-and-the-road-to-human-level-ai-anthropic-co-founder- @ 04:17](https://youtu.be/p8Jx4qvDoSo?t=257)
- Scaling laws also apply to the reinforcement learning phase of training, first observed in a researcher's study of the simple board game Hex [scaling-and-the-road-to-human-level-ai-anthropic-co-founder- @ 06:09](https://youtu.be/p8Jx4qvDoSo?t=369)
- METR research found that the length of tasks AI models can complete is doubling roughly every 7 months, a key predictable trend for future capability [scaling-and-the-road-to-human-level-ai-anthropic-co-founder- @ 09:28](https://youtu.be/p8Jx4qvDoSo?t=568)
- What's still needed for broader human-level AI: organizational knowledge, persistent memory, and better oversight/reward signals for fuzzy, nuanced tasks [scaling-and-the-road-to-human-level-ai-anthropic-co-founder- @ 11:00](https://youtu.be/p8Jx4qvDoSo?t=660)
- Founders should build products that don't quite work yet, since models are improving so fast that a not-quite-good-enough product today may work with the next model release [scaling-and-the-road-to-human-level-ai-anthropic-co-founder- @ 13:41](https://youtu.be/p8Jx4qvDoSo?t=821)
- The biggest bottleneck isn't AI capability but integration speed — using AI itself to help integrate AI into products, companies, and science is high-leverage [scaling-and-the-road-to-human-level-ai-anthropic-co-founder- @ 14:19](https://youtu.be/p8Jx4qvDoSo?t=859)
- It's often more fun and tractable to build for use cases where 70-80% task success is acceptable, since that's where you can push the frontier of what AI can do [scaling-and-the-road-to-human-level-ai-anthropic-co-founder- @ 20:01](https://youtu.be/p8Jx4qvDoSo?t=1201)
- Green-field areas for AI adoption include finance, spreadsheet-heavy work, and law, plus the broader challenge of integrating AI into existing businesses the way electricity was integrated into factories [scaling-and-the-road-to-human-level-ai-anthropic-co-founder- @ 24:32](https://youtu.be/p8Jx4qvDoSo?t=1472)
- Longer task horizons likely come from models getting modestly better at noticing and self-correcting mistakes, which can double how far a model gets before getting stuck [scaling-and-the-road-to-human-level-ai-anthropic-co-founder- @ 35:23](https://youtu.be/p8Jx4qvDoSo?t=2123)
- Anthropic trains AI models to supervise other AI models on long, fuzzy tasks rather than relying only on end-to-end task success signals, to make RL training for long-horizon tasks more efficient [scaling-and-the-road-to-human-level-ai-anthropic-co-founder- @ 38:03](https://youtu.be/p8Jx4qvDoSo?t=2283)
