# How Intelligent Is AI, Really? — Compiled Truth

ARC Prize president Greg Kamradt explains why ARC Prize defines intelligence as the efficient acquisition of new skills rather than raw benchmark performance, and previews how ARC-AGI-3's interactive, instruction-free game environments will test AI generalization against humans.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- ARC Prize defines intelligence, per François Chollet's 2019 "measure of intelligence" paper, as the ability to learn new things efficiently rather than as scoring well on hard, static problems [how-intelligent-is-ai-really @ 00:29](https://youtu.be/pBlIgs6w7Ss?t=29)
- Base GPT-4 scored only about 4-5% on the original ARC-AGI benchmark in 2024, but the reasoning-focused o1 model jumped to 21%, which is what revealed the reasoning paradigm as a genuine breakthrough [how-intelligent-is-ai-really @ 02:46](https://youtu.be/pBlIgs6w7Ss?t=166)
- Major frontier labs (OpenAI, xAI, Gemini, Anthropic) now report ARC-AGI scores as part of their model releases, though that adoption alone isn't ARC Prize's actual mission [how-intelligent-is-ai-really @ 03:37](https://youtu.be/pBlIgs6w7Ss?t=217)
- A common false-positive in AI progress is over-relying on RL environments to hit benchmark scores, which is "whack-a-mole" and doesn't generalize to genuinely novel problems [how-intelligent-is-ai-really @ 05:10](https://youtu.be/pBlIgs6w7Ss?t=310)
- ARC-AGI-3, launching next year, will consist of roughly 150 interactive game-like environments given with zero instructions, forcing the test-taker to infer the goal purely from actions and feedback [how-intelligent-is-ai-really @ 06:46](https://youtu.be/pBlIgs6w7Ss?t=406)
- ARC-AGI-3 will measure efficiency, not just accuracy, by counting the number of actions a system needs to solve a task and normalizing that against average human performance, rather than allowing brute-force compute [how-intelligent-is-ai-really @ 09:23](https://youtu.be/pBlIgs6w7Ss?t=563)
- Solving ARC-AGI is considered necessary but not sufficient for AGI — even a system scoring 100% would not itself be declared AGI, just the most authoritative evidence yet of generalization [how-intelligent-is-ai-really @ 10:38](https://youtu.be/pBlIgs6w7Ss?t=638)
