# The Truth About The AI Bubble — Compiled Truth

The Lightcone hosts review 2025's biggest surprises in AI and startups, arguing that even if there's infrastructure overbuilding, the "bubble" question mostly matters for compute/chip companies, not for the application-layer startups that get to build on top of a glut of cheap intelligence.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The AI economy has stabilized into a normal layered stack (models, applications, infrastructure) with a repeatable playbook for building an AI-native company [the-truth-about-the-ai-bubble @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/cqrJzG03ENE?t=0)
- Anthropic overtook OpenAI as the top model choice among YC winter 26 batch applicants, after hovering around 20-25% through most of 2024 [the-truth-about-the-ai-bubble @ 01:04](https://youtu.be/cqrJzG03ENE?t=64)
- Founders and consumers alike are arbitraging across models, building orchestration layers that swap in whichever model performs best for a given task, using proprietary evals [the-truth-about-the-ai-bubble @ 07:09](https://youtu.be/cqrJzG03ENE?t=429)
- Drawing on Carlota Perez's framework, the hosts argue AI is in a CapEx "installation phase" (like telecom pre-2000) that looks like a bubble, but will be followed by a "deployment phase" where the real applications and value get built [the-truth-about-the-ai-bubble @ 12:43](https://youtu.be/cqrJzG03ENE?t=763)
- Whether or not there's an Nvidia bubble is mostly irrelevant to founders starting AI companies, since a compute glut (like the telecom glut that enabled YouTube) benefits application-layer startups [the-truth-about-the-ai-bubble @ 11:12](https://youtu.be/cqrJzG03ENE?t=672)
- Domain-specific fine-tuned models (e.g., an 8B-parameter healthcare model) can beat general frontier models on narrow benchmarks, but incumbents like GPT-4.5/5.1 can quickly erase that advantage, so founders must keep iterating [the-truth-about-the-ai-bubble @ 19:32](https://youtu.be/cqrJzG03ENE?t=1172)
- Startups that hit $1M ARR without hiring in 2024 mostly did not stay lean post-Series A; instead the playbook reverted to actively hiring teams, just often smaller ones for the same revenue [the-truth-about-the-ai-bubble @ 25:35](https://youtu.be/cqrJzG03ENE?t=1535)
- Rising customer expectations (not just AI efficiency) are driving companies to keep hiring, since AI lowers cost/time to produce things but raises what customers expect [the-truth-about-the-ai-bubble @ 28:15](https://youtu.be/cqrJzG03ENE?t=1695)
- Gamma reaching $100M ARR with only 50 employees is cited as an early example of the "reverse flex" trend of high revenue per employee [the-truth-about-the-ai-bubble @ 29:23](https://youtu.be/cqrJzG03ENE?t=1763)
- Finding new startup ideas has returned to "normal levels of difficulty" now that model releases are incremental rather than paradigm-shifting month to month [the-truth-about-the-ai-bubble @ 23:11](https://youtu.be/cqrJzG03ENE?t=1391)
