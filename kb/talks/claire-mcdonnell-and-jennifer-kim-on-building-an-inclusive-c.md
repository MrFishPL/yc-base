# Claire McDonnell and Jennifer Kim on Building an Inclusive Company Culture — Compiled Truth

TrueLink's Claire McDonnell and Lever's Jennifer Kim share concrete, early-stage tactics for building inclusive company culture, arguing it's a competitive advantage for hiring and retention rather than a later-stage luxury.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Culture is often mistaken for visible perks (ping-pong tables) when it's really about how you hire, how decisions get made, and who gets promoted — mostly invisible until it goes wrong [claire-mcdonnell-and-jennifer-kim-on-building-an-inclusive-c @ 03:20](https://youtu.be/OkrFzT5iLco?t=200).
- TrueLink built culture from day one via small daily investments, arguing that a trusted, fairly-treated team is how a startup survives existential threats, not a distraction from survival [claire-mcdonnell-and-jennifer-kim-on-building-an-inclusive-c @ 05:34](https://youtu.be/OkrFzT5iLco?t=334).
- Good culture is a direct recruiting and retention tool: at Lever, a rejected candidate loved the interview process so much he referred a better-qualified friend, and low attrition came from people feeling connected to the work and team, not from out-competing recruiters on equity [claire-mcdonnell-and-jennifer-kim-on-building-an-inclusive-c @ 08:05](https://youtu.be/OkrFzT5iLco?t=485).
- TrueLink uses a "no blame button" norm: when something breaks, the team fixes the problem first and only reflects on the root cause afterward, to avoid scapegoating patterns that disproportionately target women and people of color [claire-mcdonnell-and-jennifer-kim-on-building-an-inclusive-c @ 13:58](https://youtu.be/OkrFzT5iLco?t=838).
- They practice credit "amplification" (borrowed from the Obama White House), explicitly restating whose idea it was in meetings so contributions aren't erased [claire-mcdonnell-and-jennifer-kim-on-building-an-inclusive-c @ 15:46](https://youtu.be/OkrFzT5iLco?t=946).
- To avoid over-prizing engineering over other functions, TrueLink runs cross-team ride-alongs and onboarding sessions (e.g., Marketing 101, sitting with support for two hours) so every team's difficulty and value is understood [claire-mcdonnell-and-jennifer-kim-on-building-an-inclusive-c @ 19:48](https://youtu.be/OkrFzT5iLco?t=1188).
- Lever enforces a "no jerks" policy, screening for humility and respect by watching how candidates treat receptionists and junior interviewers, since polished performance with senior staff can mask disrespect toward others [claire-mcdonnell-and-jennifer-kim-on-building-an-inclusive-c @ 22:04](https://youtu.be/OkrFzT5iLco?t=1324).
- Both companies invest in communication skills at scale, using tools like nonviolent/compassionate communication training and the "Colors" personality framework to reduce misunderstanding as teams grow past the size where everyone knows each other closely [claire-mcdonnell-and-jennifer-kim-on-building-an-inclusive-c @ 28:12](https://youtu.be/OkrFzT5iLco?t=1692).
- Advice to founders starting today: figure out why you personally care about inclusion, write down implicit policies so people aren't left guessing, and deliberately empower non-founder team members to become culture leaders so it doesn't bottleneck on the founders [claire-mcdonnell-and-jennifer-kim-on-building-an-inclusive-c @ 42:05](https://youtu.be/OkrFzT5iLco?t=2525).
