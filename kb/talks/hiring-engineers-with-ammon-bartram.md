# Hiring Engineers with Ammon Bartram — Compiled Truth

Triple Byte co-founder Ammon Bartram, drawing on data from thousands of engineering interviews, argues that most hiring noise comes from companies having unstated and inconsistent definitions of a "good engineer," and that process consistency matters more than which specific questions get asked.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Different companies define "good engineer" in fundamentally different ways (fast/productive vs. deeply academic in CS), and this mismatch in unstated criteria is a major source of hiring noise across the industry [hiring-engineers-with-ammon-bartram @ 01:57](https://youtu.be/haxZDcTWdOo?t=117)
- Early-stage startups should identify the single trait actually holding the company back (e.g., raw productivity) rather than trying to hire a broadly well-rounded engineer [hiring-engineers-with-ammon-bartram @ 03:15](https://youtu.be/haxZDcTWdOo?t=195)
- Boot camp grads and fresh CS grads are more similar than people assume — both are essentially junior engineers who lack real-world experience, regardless of their educational background [hiring-engineers-with-ammon-bartram @ 06:30](https://youtu.be/haxZDcTWdOo?t=390)
- Interviews mainly measure the ability to solve small problems quickly, which only imperfectly correlates with the real job skill of carrying large, sprawling projects over a long period of time [hiring-engineers-with-ammon-bartram @ 07:16](https://youtu.be/haxZDcTWdOo?t=436)
- A single interview question is extraordinarily noisy — Triple Byte found the agreement rate between interviewers at different companies was statistically equivalent to the agreement rate between movie critics rating the same film [hiring-engineers-with-ammon-bartram @ 12:20](https://youtu.be/haxZDcTWdOo?t=740)
- Counterintuitively, easy interview questions (like building a simple checkers game) predict on-the-job performance better than hard graph-search-style questions, because hard questions carry a much higher false-negative rate [hiring-engineers-with-ammon-bartram @ 15:20](https://youtu.be/haxZDcTWdOo?t=920)
- Even the best engineers who eventually get hired typically pass only about 80% of the companies they interview with — almost no one passes 100%, so failing an interview is not a verdict on skill [hiring-engineers-with-ammon-bartram @ 22:22](https://youtu.be/haxZDcTWdOo?t=1342)
- Side projects rarely factor into the actual interview itself and mostly matter at the resume-screen stage, because it's nearly impossible to tell how much time or effort a candidate actually put into a project [hiring-engineers-with-ammon-bartram @ 23:40](https://youtu.be/haxZDcTWdOo?t=1420)
- The single biggest fix for hiring noise is process consistency — asking every candidate the same questions and evaluating them the same way matters more than which specific questions are chosen [hiring-engineers-with-ammon-bartram @ 29:13](https://youtu.be/haxZDcTWdOo?t=1753)
