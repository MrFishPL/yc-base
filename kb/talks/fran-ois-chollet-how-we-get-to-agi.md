# François Chollet: How We Get To AGI — Compiled Truth

François Chollet argues that scaling up pre-training alone cannot produce fluid, general intelligence, and that reaching AGI requires combining test-time adaptation with discrete program search on top of deep learning's pattern-recognition strengths.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Despite a roughly 50,000x scale-up in pre-training compute from 2019 to today, models only went from 0% to about 10% accuracy on his ARC-AGI-1 benchmark, showing fluid intelligence does not emerge from scaling pre-training alone [fran-ois-chollet-how-we-get-to-agi @ 02:02](https://youtu.be/5QcCeSsNRks?t=122)
- He defines intelligence not as displayed skill but as the efficiency ratio of converting past experience into the ability to handle new, unforeseen situations [fran-ois-chollet-how-we-get-to-agi @ 06:30](https://youtu.be/5QcCeSsNRks?t=390)
- The 2024 pivot to "test-time adaptation" — models that modify their own state at inference time via techniques like test-time training and program synthesis — finally produced genuine progress on ARC, unlike static pre-trained inference [fran-ois-chollet-how-we-get-to-agi @ 02:54](https://youtu.be/5QcCeSsNRks?t=174)
- Benchmarks that reward only task-specific skill lead to automation, not the "autonomous invention" that real AGI requires, illustrating the engineering "shortcut rule" of hitting the target but missing the point [fran-ois-chollet-how-we-get-to-agi @ 10:45](https://youtu.be/5QcCeSsNRks?t=645)
- His team tested ARC-AGI-2 on 400 random people (Uber drivers, students, unemployed people) and found a group of 10 with majority voting would score 100%, while base models like GPT-4.5 and Llama 4 score 0% [fran-ois-chollet-how-we-get-to-agi @ 17:17](https://youtu.be/5QcCeSsNRks?t=1037)
- Intelligence requires two kinds of abstraction — continuous "type one" abstraction behind perception and intuition (what transformers excel at) and discrete "type two" program-centric abstraction behind reasoning, which deep learning struggles with [fran-ois-chollet-how-we-get-to-agi @ 25:37](https://youtu.be/5QcCeSsNRks?t=1537)
- Discrete program search, not deep learning, is what powers real invention in AI systems, citing examples like antenna design search in the 90s, AlphaGo's move 37, and DeepMind's AlphaEvolve [fran-ois-chollet-how-we-get-to-agi @ 27:39](https://youtu.be/5QcCeSsNRks?t=1659)
- He founded a new research lab, Ndea, to build a "programmer-like metalearner" that blends deep-learning intuition with discrete program search over a constantly evolving library of reusable abstractions, aiming to accelerate scientific discovery [fran-ois-chollet-how-we-get-to-agi @ 33:39](https://youtu.be/5QcCeSsNRks?t=2019)
- ARC-AGI-3, launching in early 2026 with a developer preview in July, will test agency in novel interactive environments and grade models on action efficiency, not just task success [fran-ois-chollet-how-we-get-to-agi @ 19:13](https://youtu.be/5QcCeSsNRks?t=1153)
