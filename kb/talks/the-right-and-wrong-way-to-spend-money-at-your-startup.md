# The Right (And Wrong) Way To Spend Money At Your Startup — Compiled Truth

A YC office-hours panel on how a startup's relationship to money should evolve from spending almost nothing pre-product-market-fit to funding a scalable revenue engine once product-market fit is real.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Pre-product-market-fit, money can't buy product-market fit; the only thing it can buy is time to find it, so founders should spend almost nothing [the-right-and-wrong-way-to-spend-money-at-your-startup @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/IRROi-Q1V44?t=0).
- At seed stage, the money is best spent hiring one or two great engineers over 12 months; founders themselves should do sales and marketing until product-market fit [the-right-and-wrong-way-to-spend-money-at-your-startup @ 02:22](https://youtu.be/IRROi-Q1V44?t=142).
- Sending investors a monthly update and splitting money into two bank accounts (pretending you only have half) are concrete tactics to stay frugal and disciplined [the-right-and-wrong-way-to-spend-money-at-your-startup @ 05:35](https://youtu.be/IRROi-Q1V44?t=335).
- Once you have actual product-market fit and clear demand, hiring a sales team is a good way to spend money, since a good hire generates more money than they cost [the-right-and-wrong-way-to-spend-money-at-your-startup @ 06:05](https://youtu.be/IRROi-Q1V44?t=365).
- Startups win early on by having much better customer support than big companies, and founders should stay close to that channel even as the company scales [the-right-and-wrong-way-to-spend-money-at-your-startup @ 09:39](https://youtu.be/IRROi-Q1V44?t=579).
- At series B and beyond, companies fail when they don't actually understand the quality of their revenue and retention, even if multiple investors have already bet on them [the-right-and-wrong-way-to-spend-money-at-your-startup @ 11:17](https://youtu.be/IRROi-Q1V44?t=677).
- Spending on early-stage ads is tempting but outsources a founder's own understanding of customers, and becomes addictive as founders keep spending to maintain growth optics for investors [the-right-and-wrong-way-to-spend-money-at-your-startup @ 17:23](https://youtu.be/IRROi-Q1V44?t=1043).
- Startups aren't miniature big companies — most departments and functions big companies have simply shouldn't exist yet, since everything should go toward reaching product-market fit [the-right-and-wrong-way-to-spend-money-at-your-startup @ 20:08](https://youtu.be/IRROi-Q1V44?t=1208).
- During 2020-2022, some companies raised big rounds and let burn balloon toward $1M/month while still at $1M ARR, assuming revenue would catch up, which rarely happens and hurt otherwise-promising companies [the-right-and-wrong-way-to-spend-money-at-your-startup @ 25:20](https://youtu.be/IRROi-Q1V44?t=1520).
- A common founder mistake is dividing seed money by a target runway (e.g., 24 months) to compute an allowed burn rate, then finding ways to spend it, rather than staying as lean as possible until real product-market-fit signals appear [the-right-and-wrong-way-to-spend-money-at-your-startup @ 24:03](https://youtu.be/IRROi-Q1V44?t=1443).
