# How Startup Fundraising Works | Startup School — Compiled Truth

YC group partner Brad Flora debunks seven common myths about startup fundraising, arguing it is far less glamorous, less exclusive, and less risky to founder control than most people assume.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Real fundraising looks like quiet one-on-one coffee-shop conversations, not the high-pressure pitch-competition drama of Shark Tank [how-startup-fundraising-works-startup-school @ 04:56](https://youtu.be/zBUhQPPS9AY?t=296)
- Fresh Paint's actual seed round took meeting 160 investors over four months to close 39 checks ranging from $5K to $200K for $1.6M total [how-startup-fundraising-works-startup-school @ 05:57](https://youtu.be/zBUhQPPS9AY?t=357)
- The best founders build a first version of the product and get users before raising any money, because building and finding users has never been cheaper [how-startup-fundraising-works-startup-school @ 07:08](https://youtu.be/zBUhQPPS9AY?t=428)
- Solugen built a tiny desk-sized reactor and sold hydrogen peroxide to hot tub supply stores before raising capital-intensive manufacturing money, later raising $400M [how-startup-fundraising-works-startup-school @ 08:26](https://youtu.be/zBUhQPPS9AY?t=506)
- Startups don't need to impress investors, they need to convince them; the best startups (Airbnb, DoorDash, OpenSea) all sounded terrible at first [how-startup-fundraising-works-startup-school @ 10:28](https://youtu.be/zBUhQPPS9AY?t=628)
- Retool's founder raised his seed round simply by demoing the crude early product live on his laptop rather than using a slide deck [how-startup-fundraising-works-startup-school @ 12:41](https://youtu.be/zBUhQPPS9AY?t=761)
- YC's SAFE, created in 2013, made seed fundraising fast and cheap (5 pages, no lawyers, only amount/cap/discount to negotiate) [how-startup-fundraising-works-startup-school @ 15:25](https://youtu.be/zBUhQPPS9AY?t=925)
- SAFEs give founders more control than ever since no board seats, no share transfer, and no information rights are granted until the next round [how-startup-fundraising-works-startup-school @ 18:01](https://youtu.be/zBUhQPPS9AY?t=1081)
- Bootstrapping forever is defined as stretching the pain of fundraising across the entire life of the company, which the speaker argues is worse than raising once early [how-startup-fundraising-works-startup-school @ 21:05](https://youtu.be/zBUhQPPS9AY?t=1265)
- Investors are "coin operated" and care about making money, not pedigree or network, as shown by Podium's founders from Utah with no Silicon Valley connections [how-startup-fundraising-works-startup-school @ 21:58](https://youtu.be/zBUhQPPS9AY?t=1318)
- Rejection is universal and meaningless as a verdict on startup quality: Embrace/Envision's founder was rejected 50+ times before her first check, and WhatNot raised only a fraction of its target before reaching a $3.7B valuation [how-startup-fundraising-works-startup-school @ 23:46](https://youtu.be/zBUhQPPS9AY?t=1426)
