# How Pitching Investors is Different Than Pitching Customers - Michael Seibel — Compiled Truth

Founders should build two distinct pitches because investors and customers have different knowledge of the problem and different motivations for caring about the solution.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Customers usually understand the problem deeply and are personally invested in the solution, while investors may not understand the problem at all and only care whether it can become a big company [how-pitching-investors-is-different-than-pitching-customers- @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/pQnOBHNKlgs?t=0)
- With customers, using industry jargon builds credibility, but with investors jargon actively hurts the pitch [how-pitching-investors-is-different-than-pitching-customers- @ 00:38](https://youtu.be/pQnOBHNKlgs?t=38)
- Flowery "marketing language" like calling something a platform or the first-ever solution is fluff that investors have to filter out to understand what you actually do [how-pitching-investors-is-different-than-pitching-customers- @ 01:32](https://youtu.be/pQnOBHNKlgs?t=92)
- The Google example shows the fix: instead of "organizes the world's information," describe concretely what the user does and why the ranking mechanism makes results more relevant [how-pitching-investors-is-different-than-pitching-customers- @ 01:54](https://youtu.be/pQnOBHNKlgs?t=114)
- An investor pitch usually doesn't belong on your website or in customer-facing materials unless your product is a mass-market consumer product where the investor is also a plausible user, like Yelp or Uber [how-pitching-investors-is-different-than-pitching-customers- @ 02:51](https://youtu.be/pQnOBHNKlgs?t=171)
- Investors are mainly curious about six things: what you do in simple language, stage of progress, market size, pricing plan, your unique insight into the market, and who's on the founding team [how-pitching-investors-is-different-than-pitching-customers- @ 03:09](https://youtu.be/pQnOBHNKlgs?t=189)
- Customers instead care about functionality, onboarding, how pricing applies specifically to them, and whether the product solves their specific problem rather than a general one [how-pitching-investors-is-different-than-pitching-customers- @ 03:52](https://youtu.be/pQnOBHNKlgs?t=232)
- YC founders typically need about a month of practice before they accept that they need two separate pitches instead of trying to meld the customer and investor pitch into one [how-pitching-investors-is-different-than-pitching-customers- @ 05:13](https://youtu.be/pQnOBHNKlgs?t=313)
