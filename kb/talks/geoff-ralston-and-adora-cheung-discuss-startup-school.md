# Geoff Ralston and Adora Cheung Discuss Startup School — Compiled Truth

Geoff Ralston and Adora Cheung explain how YC's free 10-week Startup School course works, why it now includes a $10,000 equity-free grant, and why its real value is community and mentorship rather than just content.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Startup School is a free, open online 10-week course meant to reduce the barriers to starting a startup for founders anywhere in the world, not just Silicon Valley. [geoff-ralston-and-adora-cheung-discuss-startup-school @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/BTS-rFzROOg?t=0)
- Founders who officially join (versus just auditing) get a weekly advisor, a peer group, a founder forum, and exclusive deals from partners like AWS and Stripe Atlas. [geoff-ralston-and-adora-cheung-discuss-startup-school @ 00:45](https://youtu.be/BTS-rFzROOg?t=45)
- YC will give a $10,000 equity-free grant to 100 companies chosen based on their progress and adviser feedback during the course. [geoff-ralston-and-adora-cheung-discuss-startup-school @ 05:06](https://youtu.be/BTS-rFzROOg?t=306)
- 38 companies from the prior year's Startup School cohort went on to get into YC's core batch, largely because they used the course to iterate and talk to users. [geoff-ralston-and-adora-cheung-discuss-startup-school @ 06:33](https://youtu.be/BTS-rFzROOg?t=393)
- The curriculum follows an arc from startup basics, to product and "make something people want" (product-market fit), to growth, to hiring, to fundraising, to applying to YC, and finally to the psychological and emotional challenges of founding a company. [geoff-ralston-and-adora-cheung-discuss-startup-school @ 14:50](https://youtu.be/BTS-rFzROOg?t=890)
- Startup School helps founders get into YC less because of a dedicated application lecture and more because the forcing function of a ten-week group cohort genuinely makes the startup better. [geoff-ralston-and-adora-cheung-discuss-startup-school @ 14:15](https://youtu.be/BTS-rFzROOg?t=855)
- Advisors are unpaid volunteers who dedicate their time to help strangers' startups succeed, and that mentorship — more than any lecture or book — is what makes the course valuable. [geoff-ralston-and-adora-cheung-discuss-startup-school @ 20:06](https://youtu.be/BTS-rFzROOg?t=1206)
- To be eligible for the $10,000 grant, founders must attend at least 9 of 10 weekly group advisor meetings and complete at least 18 of 20 lecture views. [geoff-ralston-and-adora-cheung-discuss-startup-school @ 12:33](https://youtu.be/BTS-rFzROOg?t=753)
- YC believes the community and network — being able to talk with high-quality entrepreneurs from anywhere in the world — is worth more than the content itself, even though the content is free. [geoff-ralston-and-adora-cheung-discuss-startup-school @ 24:59](https://youtu.be/BTS-rFzROOg?t=1499)
