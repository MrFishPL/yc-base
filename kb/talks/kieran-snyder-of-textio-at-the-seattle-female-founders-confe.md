# Kieran Snyder of Textio at the Seattle Female Founders Conference — Compiled Truth

Textio co-founder/CEO Kieran Snyder walks through five decisions — choosing a co-founder, starting narrow, rebranding, changing the pricing model, and reshaping the press narrative — that accelerated Textio from a Kickstarter-predictor prototype to a Series B "augmented writing" company.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Textio's first prototype was a Kickstarter success predictor built to prove the core NLP technology worked, reaching 97% prediction accuracy in minute one of a project despite having nothing to do with the eventual product [kieran-snyder-of-textio-at-the-seattle-female-founders-confe @ 08:27](https://youtu.be/OI29HkzUElE?t=507)
- The team raised its seed round on the strength of that non-commercial Kickstarter demo, proving competence even though it was never going to be the actual business [kieran-snyder-of-textio-at-the-seattle-female-founders-confe @ 09:58](https://youtu.be/OI29HkzUElE?t=598)
- Co-founding with someone you deeply trust (Snyder's CTO co-founder is also her husband) gave the company complementary strengths — her NLP/backend expertise and his consumer-scale UX experience from Outlook and Office ribbon — even though the family relationship initially made fundraising harder [kieran-snyder-of-textio-at-the-seattle-female-founders-confe @ 13:20](https://youtu.be/OI29HkzUElE?t=800)
- Textio deliberately started with job postings instead of the larger sales-and-marketing writing market because Snyder's HR/bias research gave her unique relationships and credibility to win that specific underserved niche first [kieran-snyder-of-textio-at-the-seattle-female-founders-confe @ 15:54](https://youtu.be/OI29HkzUElE?t=954)
- Starting in HR/recruiting had a stealth benefit: because hiring touches every function, an enterprise-wide footprint formed organically, positioning Textio to expand into other writing domains later (the Series B) [kieran-snyder-of-textio-at-the-seattle-female-founders-confe @ 16:53](https://youtu.be/OI29HkzUElE?t=1013)
- A 2016 rebrand fixed a core positioning mistake: the team had been selling "ease of use" when the real purchase driver was outcomes like filling jobs two weeks faster or getting 23% more women into engineering pipelines [kieran-snyder-of-textio-at-the-seattle-female-founders-confe @ 18:14](https://youtu.be/OI29HkzUElE?t=1094)
- Textio's original self-serve pricing ($59/month per seat) got early love but blocked enterprise expansion, since companies could just share a handful of seats; switching to a flat fee based on open job count (with unlimited seats) unlocked enterprise revenue growth [kieran-snyder-of-textio-at-the-seattle-female-founders-confe @ 19:56](https://youtu.be/OI29HkzUElE?t=1196)
- Encouraging unlimited seat usage under the new pricing model also improved the product itself, since more writers on the platform meant more training data for the machine-learning engine [kieran-snyder-of-textio-at-the-seattle-female-founders-confe @ 20:37](https://youtu.be/OI29HkzUElE?t=1237)
- Customers who share more outcomes data get better guidance and pay less, creating a virtuous data-sharing cycle that scaled Textio's job-outcomes dataset to roughly 350 million jobs, growing by about 10 million a month [kieran-snyder-of-textio-at-the-seattle-female-founders-confe @ 23:07](https://youtu.be/OI29HkzUElE?t=1387)
- Early press coverage pigeonholed Textio as a "bias" company; the team spent a year deliberately reshaping the narrative toward the bigger "augmented writing" category story to avoid being boxed into a narrow positioning [kieran-snyder-of-textio-at-the-seattle-female-founders-confe @ 24:52](https://youtu.be/OI29HkzUElE?t=1492)
