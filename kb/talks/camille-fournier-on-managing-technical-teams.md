# Camille Fournier on Managing Technical Teams — Compiled Truth

Camille Fournier, author of The Manager's Path, argues that engineering management is a real technical discipline, not overhead, and lays out concrete practices for new managers around one-on-ones, team-building, and hard conversations.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Many individual contributors have never experienced a good manager, which is part of why she wrote her book [camille-fournier-on-managing-technical-teams @ 00:45](https://youtu.be/oxgfehnJ7GE?t=45)
- Around 2010-2011, a strong sentiment in Silicon Valley startups held that management is overhead, flat structures are best, and you should just hire smart people and get out of their way [camille-fournier-on-managing-technical-teams @ 01:25](https://youtu.be/oxgfehnJ7GE?t=85)
- Good management is actually a pretty important part of making engineering teams and companies effective — it simply isn't true that management is overhead or waste [camille-fournier-on-managing-technical-teams @ 02:07](https://youtu.be/oxgfehnJ7GE?t=127)
- New managers should hold regular one-on-ones, even with nothing planned, so people feel comfortable bringing up small issues before they become a resignation or a "raging fire" [camille-fournier-on-managing-technical-teams @ 08:00](https://youtu.be/oxgfehnJ7GE?t=480)
- In the first 90 days, a manager should deliberately make their reports feel like a team — via team meetings and shared identity — rather than letting engineers work like kids in "parallel play" [camille-fournier-on-managing-technical-teams @ 11:38](https://youtu.be/oxgfehnJ7GE?t=698)
- The most common rookie-manager mistake is defaulting to writing code to fix a struggling team instead of fixing planning, unblocking people, or descoping work [camille-fournier-on-managing-technical-teams @ 15:19](https://youtu.be/oxgfehnJ7GE?t=919)
- New managers often avoid proactively addressing interpersonal conflict on their team, but letting it fester just causes ongoing problems [camille-fournier-on-managing-technical-teams @ 17:33](https://youtu.be/oxgfehnJ7GE?t=1053)
- Removing a persistently negative person from a team, even a productive one, almost always makes the team happier and more productive than expected [camille-fournier-on-managing-technical-teams @ 22:22](https://youtu.be/oxgfehnJ7GE?t=1342)
- Non-technical managers can fall into playing "management telephone," relaying questions to engineers and back to the business, which adds little value [camille-fournier-on-managing-technical-teams @ 40:36](https://youtu.be/oxgfehnJ7GE?t=2436)
- Leadership is the ability to provide clarity in times of ambiguity, and while not all leadership is management, it's hard to be a good manager without some leadership skill [camille-fournier-on-managing-technical-teams @ 46:37](https://youtu.be/oxgfehnJ7GE?t=2797)
