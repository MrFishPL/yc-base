# The Most Important Decision is Getting Started – Laura Behrens Wu — Compiled Truth

Shippo co-founder Laura Behrens Wu tells the story of dropping out of grad school to intern at a YC startup, then building Shippo through rejection, fundraising, and co-founder turnover, arguing the hardest and most important decision is simply to start working on something.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The most important decision a founder can make is the decision to get started; there's no need for a grand idea, just start on something and eventually find the right problem [the-most-important-decision-is-getting-started-laura-behrens @ 04:00](https://youtu.be/ZAkzs4rgjOo?t=240)
- Advice from a former CEO shaped Shippo's pivot: build painkillers, not vitamins — products people can't live without, not just nice-to-haves [the-most-important-decision-is-getting-started-laura-behrens @ 04:21](https://youtu.be/ZAkzs4rgjOo?t=261)
- While building an unrelated e-commerce marketplace (a vitamin), the founders discovered shipping rates were opaque, hard to compare, and ate into merchants' margins — a real pain point with outdated technology [the-most-important-decision-is-getting-started-laura-behrens @ 04:41](https://youtu.be/ZAkzs4rgjOo?t=281)
- Shippo was rejected by Y Combinator and by 500 Startups on a first application, then got into 500 Startups on a second try to access an investor network [the-most-important-decision-is-getting-started-laura-behrens @ 06:07](https://youtu.be/ZAkzs4rgjOo?t=367)
- Raising the seed round meant pitching 125 angels and investors and hearing 150 nos before closing $2 million from 10 investors, and losing a third co-founder mid-raise [the-most-important-decision-is-getting-started-laura-behrens @ 06:26](https://youtu.be/ZAkzs4rgjOo?t=386)
- Key seed-stage advice was to focus on one single KPI that defines business success — for Shippo that was shipping volume, which grew 40% week over week [the-most-important-decision-is-getting-started-laura-behrens @ 08:10](https://youtu.be/ZAkzs4rgjOo?t=490)
- For the Series A, Shippo secured 30 warm investor intros and deliberately controlled the process timeline (a week each for first meetings, second meetings, partner meetings, diligence) to generate competitive term sheets, closing $7M from Union Square Ventures despite losing their most senior technical leader and facing a lawsuit during diligence [the-most-important-decision-is-getting-started-laura-behrens @ 08:53](https://youtu.be/ZAkzs4rgjOo?t=533)
- Investors are like employees you can't fire, so founders should do back-channel reference checks on investors just as investors do on founders [the-most-important-decision-is-getting-started-laura-behrens @ 10:21](https://youtu.be/ZAkzs4rgjOo?t=621)
- Key personal lessons: company worth is not equal to self-worth, be persistent about goals but flexible about tactics, and treat failures as pieces of a larger puzzle that only becomes clear later [the-most-important-decision-is-getting-started-laura-behrens @ 14:04](https://youtu.be/ZAkzs4rgjOo?t=844)
- Building company culture means being unapologetic about who you are, hiring for complementary skills, hiring slowly and firing fast, and not building a company you'll hate working at in five years [the-most-important-decision-is-getting-started-laura-behrens @ 16:20](https://youtu.be/ZAkzs4rgjOo?t=980)
