# Most Important Lifestyle Habits Of Successful Founders — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell discuss preventative lifestyle habits (sleep, home environment, social media, salary, co-founder conflict) that help founders absorb the inevitable setbacks of running a startup, plus how to recover when a "punch" lands.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Founders should audit how they live — sleep, home environment, diet, exercise — because a home that drains "hit points" is an unforced error that weakens resilience [most-important-lifestyle-habits-of-successful-founders @ 03:02](https://youtu.be/0IShllkCHtY?t=182)
- From a clinical perspective, sleep disruption, diet, and exercise are the first things to check when someone is struggling, and neglecting them leaves founders poorly equipped for hard times [most-important-lifestyle-habits-of-successful-founders @ 04:07](https://youtu.be/0IShllkCHtY?t=247)
- Bad habits sustained in one's 20s create a "personal health debt" that eventually comes due, especially since the best companies take multiple decades to build [most-important-lifestyle-habits-of-successful-founders @ 06:04](https://youtu.be/0IShllkCHtY?t=364)
- Successful founders treat their information diet like a food diet, explicitly blocking social media (grayscale phones, disabled notifications, screen-time tracking) because heavy consumption of investor/market chatter doesn't improve decisions [most-important-lifestyle-habits-of-successful-founders @ 08:39](https://youtu.be/0IShllkCHtY?t=519)
- There's no universal salary formula, but a rough philosophy is "salary is for living, equity is for upside" — pay enough to avoid debt and stay motivated without draining the company [most-important-lifestyle-habits-of-successful-founders @ 12:21](https://youtu.be/0IShllkCHtY?t=741)
- Co-founder conflict is often really a clash of frustration-handling styles (anxious vs. avoidant), not the surface disagreement itself, so learning that "dance" reduces damage [most-important-lifestyle-habits-of-successful-founders @ 13:32](https://youtu.be/0IShllkCHtY?t=812)
- Poor expectation-setting with yourself and others is a major self-inflicted cause of crisis, since unrealistic benchmarks (e.g., assuming a two-year path to a billion-dollar company) manufacture unnecessary despair [most-important-lifestyle-habits-of-successful-founders @ 16:29](https://youtu.be/0IShllkCHtY?t=989)
- When bad news hits, "no action" — sleeping on it, archiving the email, waiting for the weekend to pass — is an underused but powerful play that the best founders use often [most-important-lifestyle-habits-of-successful-founders @ 19:35](https://youtu.be/0IShllkCHtY?t=1175)
- Therapy and medication are destigmatized tools that many successful founders quietly rely on, and choosing a peer group that copes constructively (vs. one that turns to drinking/drugs) is highly predictive of how founders handle setbacks [most-important-lifestyle-habits-of-successful-founders @ 25:16](https://youtu.be/0IShllkCHtY?t=1516)
