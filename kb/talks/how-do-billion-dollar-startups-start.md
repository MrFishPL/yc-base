# How Do Billion Dollar Startups Start? — Compiled Truth

A compilation of YC partners recalling the humble, messy, pre-product-market-fit origins of now-famous startups (Solugen, Amplitude, Segment, Jeeves, Nourish) to show that big companies rarely look special on day one.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Solugen's founders showed up to their YC interview with a literal beaker of hydrogen peroxide, selling bottles out of their garage before ever raising money to build a plant [how-do-billion-dollar-startups-start @ 02:21](https://youtu.be/HB3l1BPi7zo?t=141)
- A bias for action and doer mentality — building and selling the product themselves rather than waiting for permission or perfect answers — stood out as a key interview signal [how-do-billion-dollar-startups-start @ 02:58](https://youtu.be/HB3l1BPi7zo?t=178)
- Claiming "no competition" or a "blue ocean" is actually a red flag; large, unloved, money-making incumbents are proof that people already pay for the solution [how-do-billion-dollar-startups-start @ 04:43](https://youtu.be/HB3l1BPi7zo?t=283)
- At interview stage, YC is evaluating founder quality far more than business progress — many founders haven't even quit their jobs yet [how-do-billion-dollar-startups-start @ 05:14](https://youtu.be/HB3l1BPi7zo?t=314)
- Amplitude's founders pitched an idea YC didn't believe in (voice-to-text texting for driving) but their irrational intensity and conviction were the real signal, and it took them 18 months post-YC to pivot to their actual product [how-do-billion-dollar-startups-start @ 08:13](https://youtu.be/HB3l1BPi7zo?t=493)
- Segment applied to YC with a near-zero-traction "poll your students" idea, but their obstinate commitment to a mission (fixing education) let them eventually pivot to developer tools and sell to Twilio for $3.2B [how-do-billion-dollar-startups-start @ 09:44](https://youtu.be/HB3l1BPi7zo?t=584)
- Jeeves' application stood out for being extremely plain, simple, and succinct rather than padded with pitch language, and in the interview the founders were upfront about what they didn't yet know [how-do-billion-dollar-startups-start @ 12:11](https://youtu.be/HB3l1BPi7zo?t=731)
- The best interviews feel like real conversations rather than pitches, echoing Paul Graham's advice that founders convince investors best by plainly explaining what they're working on [how-do-billion-dollar-startups-start @ 14:53](https://youtu.be/HB3l1BPi7zo?t=893)
- Nourish pivoted five times during its YC batch before finding the idea that led to a major Series A, showing that a strong team can outlast a wrong initial idea [how-do-billion-dollar-startups-start @ 17:23](https://youtu.be/HB3l1BPi7zo?t=1043)
