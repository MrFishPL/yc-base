# AI Is Eating Logistics — Compiled Truth

Flexport founder Ryan Petersen explains how AI is compounding with the company's "scale economies shared" model to drive down the cost of global freight, and how bottoms-up hackathon culture and non-engineer training are the real engines of that automation.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Flexport believes AI can make ocean container shipping 8-10% cheaper over the next few years by automating away the manual, email-heavy work in freight forwarding. [ai-is-eating-logistics @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/KTmxaMdUbHA?t=0)
- Flexport's scale economies shared model means the bigger and more automated the company gets, the cheaper it gets, and it passes those savings to customers to win more volume. [ai-is-eating-logistics @ 02:32](https://youtu.be/KTmxaMdUbHA?t=152)
- Incumbents have three structural AI advantages over startups: scale of proprietary data, domain experience to know which problems matter, and existing distribution to ship a feature to thousands of customers overnight. [ai-is-eating-logistics @ 04:28](https://youtu.be/KTmxaMdUbHA?t=268)
- An AI planning model that dynamically reassigns canceled containers to earlier sailings cut ocean freight spend 2% while improving transit time 20%, a trade-off that's normally either/or. [ai-is-eating-logistics @ 14:04](https://youtu.be/KTmxaMdUbHA?t=844)
- Company hackathons went from a handful of LLM projects 18 months ago to roughly 90% LLM-based projects today, and several have graduated straight into the product roadmap. [ai-is-eating-logistics @ 06:45](https://youtu.be/KTmxaMdUbHA?t=405)
- Flexport built a formal 90-day, one-day-a-week AI/vibe-coding training program for non-engineers so domain experts can automate their own repetitive freight-forwarding work instead of waiting on engineers. [ai-is-eating-logistics @ 09:40](https://youtu.be/KTmxaMdUbHA?t=580)
- The share of Flexport's work that's automated went from 20% at the start of the year to a projected 50% by year end, with 80-95% now seen as plausible next. [ai-is-eating-logistics @ 18:39](https://youtu.be/KTmxaMdUbHA?t=1119)
- Petersen's fundraising advice: raising a large up-round is fine, but founders should immediately impose a 90-day hiring freeze afterward because "money wants to spend itself" and headcount bloat is a bad substitute for actually solving problems. [ai-is-eating-logistics @ 29:34](https://youtu.be/KTmxaMdUbHA?t=1774)
- Flexport's 2035 vision is to operate in every legal country with automated, API/voice-accessible logistics covering ~95% of global container trade by 2028, a much bigger scope than its original YC Demo Day pitch of "customs plus some other stuff." [ai-is-eating-logistics @ 30:23](https://youtu.be/KTmxaMdUbHA?t=1823)
