# Jeff Clavier and Andrea Zurek - Startup Investor School Day 3 — Compiled Truth

Jeff Clavier covers angel asset allocation and portfolio construction while Andrea Zurek covers building a personal brand as an angel investor, both drawing on ~14 years of super-angel experience.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Only invest money you can truly afford to lose entirely — angel returns take 8-10 years, losses come fast, and a portfolio typically needs six years just to return the original cash [jeff-clavier-and-andrea-zurek-startup-investor-school-day-3 @ 04:55](https://youtu.be/86AblikwSlg?t=295)
- Valuation reflects the traction achieved, the risk taken off the table, and the company's potential; angel-stage investing means the highest risk, lowest valuation, longest holding period, and highest potential return multiple [jeff-clavier-and-andrea-zurek-startup-investor-school-day-3 @ 06:03](https://youtu.be/86AblikwSlg?t=363)
- A reasonable allocation is around 10% of net worth to angel investing, similar to the 5-10% pension funds allocate to VC/PE, with an initial budget (e.g. $100k) spread across many slots over several years rather than deployed all at once [jeff-clavier-and-andrea-zurek-startup-investor-school-day-3 @ 08:50](https://youtu.be/86AblikwSlg?t=530)
- Define your portfolio construction upfront — check size, number of investments per year, sectors, stage, geography, and follow-on strategy — since consistency, not one-off big or small checks, is what builds a coherent strategy [jeff-clavier-and-andrea-zurek-startup-investor-school-day-3 @ 11:12](https://youtu.be/86AblikwSlg?t=672)
- Spreading investments across sectors and over time avoids the "winner take most" trap seen in fad markets like meal-kit startups, where the first mover wins big, the second makes some money, and the rest wipe out [jeff-clavier-and-andrea-zurek-startup-investor-school-day-3 @ 19:09](https://youtu.be/86AblikwSlg?t=1149)
- Every investment made with doubt or hesitation ("the spidey thing kicking in") ended in a wipeout — ideally you only invest with total conviction, since partial conviction with a small check can mean missing a huge win [jeff-clavier-and-andrea-zurek-startup-investor-school-day-3 @ 51:07](https://youtu.be/86AblikwSlg?t=3067)
- A brand is "what other people say about you when you're not in the room" (Jeff Bezos) — reputation, trust, and integrity matter as much for angel investors as for consumer brands [jeff-clavier-and-andrea-zurek-startup-investor-school-day-3 @ 26:34](https://youtu.be/86AblikwSlg?t=1594)
- Building a brand requires consistency: show up to events, return calls and emails promptly, respond even to say no, and treat angel investing like a full-time job rather than an armchair hobby [jeff-clavier-and-andrea-zurek-startup-investor-school-day-3 @ 37:11](https://youtu.be/86AblikwSlg?t=2231)
- Portfolio construction and brand positioning are often retrofitted after the fact rather than planned from day one — Jeff's sector "quadrant map" was built three and a half years in, not from the start [jeff-clavier-and-andrea-zurek-startup-investor-school-day-3 @ 47:55](https://youtu.be/86AblikwSlg?t=2875)
- As a super angel/lead investor, check size and ownership target should be worked backward from the ownership percentage needed for a fund-returning outcome (e.g. 10% ownership at IPO for a fund-returner) [jeff-clavier-and-andrea-zurek-startup-investor-school-day-3 @ 58:14](https://youtu.be/86AblikwSlg?t=3494)
- Avoid overlapping or competing bets within the same sub-sector — backing one company in a niche (like meal kits) means you've made your bet and shouldn't hedge with a direct competitor [jeff-clavier-and-andrea-zurek-startup-investor-school-day-3 @ 1:03:40](https://youtu.be/86AblikwSlg?t=3820)
