# Andrew Ng: Building Faster with AI — Compiled Truth

Andrew Ng argues that execution speed is a strong predictor of startup success, and lays out concrete tactics — from picking concrete ideas to using AI coding assistants and rapid feedback loops — that AI Fund uses to move faster while staying responsible.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Execution speed is a strong predictor of a startup's odds of success, and new AI technology is enabling startups to move much faster [andrew-ng-building-faster-with-ai @ 00:38](https://youtu.be/RNJCfif1dPY?t=38)
- A "concrete" idea — specific enough that an engineer could go build it this afternoon — buys speed, whereas vague ideas like "use AI to optimize healthcare assets" get praised but can't be built quickly [andrew-ng-building-faster-with-ai @ 04:51](https://youtu.be/RNJCfif1dPY?t=291)
- Vague ideas are deceptive because you're "almost always right" about them, but concreteness lets you find out fast whether an idea is actually right or wrong [andrew-ng-building-faster-with-ai @ 06:02](https://youtu.be/RNJCfif1dPY?t=362)
- Startups should pursue one clear hypothesis with full determination rather than hedging across many ideas at once, and pivot on a dime when the data says to [andrew-ng-building-faster-with-ai @ 07:49](https://youtu.be/RNJCfif1dPY?t=469)
- Building quick-and-dirty prototypes with AI coding assistants is easily 10x faster (vs. only 30-50% faster for production-grade code), because requirements for reliability, scalability, and security are much lower [andrew-ng-building-faster-with-ai @ 10:18](https://youtu.be/RNJCfif1dPY?t=618)
- As engineering gets faster with AI coding assistants, product management and user feedback increasingly become the bottleneck, shifting PM-to-engineer ratios that used to be 1:4 or 1:7 [andrew-ng-building-faster-with-ai @ 17:00](https://youtu.be/RNJCfif1dPY?t=1020)
- Ng recommends a portfolio of feedback tactics ranging from fastest-but-least-accurate (trust your own gut) to slowest-but-most-accurate (AB testing at scale) [andrew-ng-building-faster-with-ai @ 18:49](https://youtu.be/RNJCfif1dPY?t=1129)
- Understanding AI deeply is itself a competitive advantage right now because AI knowledge is not yet widely diffused the way marketing or HR knowledge is [andrew-ng-building-faster-with-ai @ 21:29](https://youtu.be/RNJCfif1dPY?t=1289)
- Moats tend to be overhyped; the number one thing to worry about is building a product users truly want, with channel, pricing, and moat considerations coming after [andrew-ng-building-faster-with-ai @ 32:13](https://youtu.be/RNJCfif1dPY?t=1933)
- Ng architects software to keep switching costs low between foundation-model providers, running evals so teams can swap to a better model as soon as one appears [andrew-ng-building-faster-with-ai @ 36:20](https://youtu.be/RNJCfif1dPY?t=2180)
