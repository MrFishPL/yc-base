# How to Manage with Ben Horowitz (How to Start a Startup 2014: Lecture 15) — Compiled Truth

Horowitz teaches one core management concept — when making a critical decision, see it through the eyes of everyone in the company, not just your own view or the person in front of you — and applies it to demotions, raises, and stock-option policy.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The single most important management skill is seeing a critical decision through the eyes of the whole company, including people who aren't in the room [how-to-manage-with-ben-horowitz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014- @ 01:28](https://youtu.be/j7tr3IlH5Ro?t=88)
- Before demoting instead of firing a well-liked but underperforming executive, check their equity level against peers, since a demoted exec keeping outsized equity can feel deeply unfair to the rest of the team [how-to-manage-with-ben-horowitz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014- @ 06:04](https://youtu.be/j7tr3IlH5Ro?t=364)
- Giving raises only when employees ask for them "encourages behavior": it teaches everyone that asking, not performance, drives compensation [how-to-manage-with-ben-horowitz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014- @ 13:15](https://youtu.be/j7tr3IlH5Ro?t=795)
- Raises should follow a formal, periodic process the CEO evaluates everyone against, rather than off-cycle requests, to protect the culture and make employees feel fairly treated [how-to-manage-with-ben-horowitz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014- @ 13:15](https://youtu.be/j7tr3IlH5Ro?t=795)
- Adam D'Angelo's proposal of a 10-year option exercise window (instead of the standard 90 days) is worth every company re-evaluating, though not necessarily adopting outright [how-to-manage-with-ben-horowitz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014- @ 16:53](https://youtu.be/j7tr3IlH5Ro?t=1013)
- Extending exercise windows has real tradeoffs: it reduces the option pool freed up by departing employees, and can retain people for the wrong reason (handcuffs, not motivation) [how-to-manage-with-ben-horowitz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014- @ 21:02](https://youtu.be/j7tr3IlH5Ro?t=1262)
- When you fire or demote someone, you must take their job but not their dignity — how you talk about it to the rest of the company becomes that person's reputation [how-to-manage-with-ben-horowitz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014- @ 38:56](https://youtu.be/j7tr3IlH5Ro?t=2336)
- Toussaint Louverture's willingness to see conquered enemies', slaves', and slave-owners' perspectives — not just his own — enabled the only successful slave revolution in history and a booming post-revolution economy [how-to-manage-with-ben-horowitz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014- @ 36:50](https://youtu.be/j7tr3IlH5Ro?t=2210)
- Under pressure, the discipline is to pause before responding to high-stakes requests (like a raise) so you can think through all perspectives rather than reacting emotionally [how-to-manage-with-ben-horowitz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014- @ 47:24](https://youtu.be/j7tr3IlH5Ro?t=2844)
