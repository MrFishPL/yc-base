# Scaling Culture | Jason Kilar, former Hulu CEO — Compiled Truth

Drawing on his time at Disney, Amazon, and Hulu, Jason Kilar argues that culture is how people act when no one is looking, and that scaling it beyond good intentions requires being explicit, walking the talk, and inventing repeatable "mechanisms."

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Culture is how people act when no one is looking, and founders set that tone early since it propagates through the company as it scales. [scaling-culture-jason-kilar-former-hulu-ceo @ 01:32](https://youtu.be/08J1eDW8q5M?t=92)
- Great companies like Disney and Amazon are explicit about culture, writing precise principles down rather than leaving them to accident. [scaling-culture-jason-kilar-former-hulu-ceo @ 05:21](https://youtu.be/08J1eDW8q5M?t=321)
- Good intentions are enough at a team of 30 but not at scale, so companies need repeatable "mechanisms," such as Disney University, to embed culture across thousands of employees. [scaling-culture-jason-kilar-former-hulu-ceo @ 07:07](https://youtu.be/08J1eDW8q5M?t=427)
- Amazon's "Just Do It" award gave old sneakers to employees who took values-aligned action without asking permission, reinforcing a bias for action even when the idea didn't succeed. [scaling-culture-jason-kilar-former-hulu-ceo @ 10:37](https://youtu.be/08J1eDW8q5M?t=637)
- A culture document should act as both a magnet and a repellent — attracting the right people while discouraging those who wouldn't fit — as with Hulu's "What Defines Hulu" document. [scaling-culture-jason-kilar-former-hulu-ceo @ 14:35](https://youtu.be/08J1eDW8q5M?t=875)
- Amazon's leadership principle "have backbone; disagree and commit" explicitly rejects compromising for social cohesion, a value most people wouldn't be comfortable with but that suits Amazon's culture. [scaling-culture-jason-kilar-former-hulu-ceo @ 17:23](https://youtu.be/08J1eDW8q5M?t=1043)
- Leaders must walk the talk daily, including making hard calls like letting a beloved high performer go when their behavior doesn't match the company's values. [scaling-culture-jason-kilar-former-hulu-ceo @ 19:25](https://youtu.be/08J1eDW8q5M?t=1165)
- If a leader keeps having to overturn one particular person's decisions, that's a sign the wrong person is in the role, not a sign to intervene more. [scaling-culture-jason-kilar-former-hulu-ceo @ 24:00](https://youtu.be/08J1eDW8q5M?t=1440)
