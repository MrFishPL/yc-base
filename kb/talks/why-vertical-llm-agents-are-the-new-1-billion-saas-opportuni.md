# Why Vertical LLM Agents Are The New $1 Billion SaaS Opportunities — Compiled Truth

Casetext founder Jake Heller explains how his company pivoted its entire 120-person team to build an AI legal assistant on early GPT-4 access, reaching real product-market fit and a $650M acquisition by Thomson Reuters within months.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- After getting early access to GPT-4, Casetext shifted 100% of its ~120-person company from prior projects to a new AI product within 48 hours [why-vertical-llm-agents-are-the-new-1-billion-saas-opportuni @ 02:54](https://youtu.be/eBVi_sLaYsc?t=174)
- An early attempt to build a UGC-style legal content platform (like Wikipedia/Stack Overflow for lawyers) failed because lawyers bill by the hour and won't donate time for free, forcing a pivot to automated NLP-based approaches [why-vertical-llm-agents-are-the-new-1-billion-saas-opportuni @ 07:09](https://youtu.be/eBVi_sLaYsc?t=429)
- Incremental product improvements were easy for cautious, high-earning customers to ignore, but ChatGPT's public launch made the shift feel "fundamental" so customers proactively reached out wanting to adopt AI [why-vertical-llm-agents-are-the-new-1-billion-saas-opportuni @ 08:41](https://youtu.be/eBVi_sLaYsc?t=521)
- Real product-market fit matched Marc Andreessen's description exactly: servers going down, being unable to hire support and sales fast enough, and constant press coverage after launching Co-Counsel [why-vertical-llm-agents-are-the-new-1-billion-saas-opportuni @ 11:39](https://youtu.be/eBVi_sLaYsc?t=699)
- The team built "skills" by working backwards from the end result a user needs (e.g., a research memo with citations) and decomposing the best human expert's process into a dozen or more discrete prompted steps [why-vertical-llm-agents-are-the-new-1-billion-saas-opportuni @ 21:10](https://youtu.be/eBVi_sLaYsc?t=1270)
- They applied test-driven development to prompting, writing gold-standard answers and running batteries of hundreds to thousands of tests per prompt before trusting outputs [why-vertical-llm-agents-are-the-new-1-billion-saas-opportuni @ 23:30](https://youtu.be/eBVi_sLaYsc?t=1410)
- Durable moats in AI products come from proprietary data, deep integrations into customer systems (e.g., legal document management, OCR handling of scanned documents), and accumulated prompt/business logic, not the LLM call itself [why-vertical-llm-agents-are-the-new-1-billion-saas-opportuni @ 25:42](https://youtu.be/eBVi_sLaYsc?t=1542)
- Going from a demo that works 70% of the time to a product that works ~100% of the time is where most of the value and willingness-to-pay lies, turning a $20/month toy into a $500-$1,000/month tool [why-vertical-llm-agents-are-the-new-1-billion-saas-opportuni @ 27:30](https://youtu.be/eBVi_sLaYsc?t=1650)
- Because the first bad experience can permanently sour a skeptical professional user, the team optimized hard to make a new user's first week with the product work flawlessly [why-vertical-llm-agents-are-the-new-1-billion-saas-opportuni @ 30:11](https://youtu.be/eBVi_sLaYsc?t=1811)
