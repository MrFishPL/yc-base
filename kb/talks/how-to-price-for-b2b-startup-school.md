# How To Price For B2B | Startup School — Compiled Truth

Tom Blomfield lays out a practical framework for B2B pricing: build a value equation with your champion, use cost as a floor not a starting point, avoid price wars with competitors, and keep pricing structures simple and aligned with recurring revenue.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The most important pricing tool is the value equation: write down with your champion exactly what cost savings, time savings, or revenue increase your product will deliver, then have them challenge the assumptions [how-to-price-for-b2b-startup-school @ 01:10](https://youtu.be/4hjiRmgmHiU?t=70)
- A common pricing heuristic is to charge 25-50% of the quantified value delivered, letting the customer keep roughly two-thirds while you keep about a third [how-to-price-for-b2b-startup-school @ 02:50](https://youtu.be/4hjiRmgmHiU?t=170)
- Cost should only ever be a floor, never the basis for pricing — cost-plus-margin pricing systematically leads to underpricing software, which should target 80-90% gross margins [how-to-price-for-b2b-startup-school @ 04:29](https://youtu.be/4hjiRmgmHiU?t=269)
- Startups should treat free cloud/AI credits as a real cash cost rather than assuming they'll be unlimited forever, since that assumption will wreck margins later [how-to-price-for-b2b-startup-school @ 05:27](https://youtu.be/4hjiRmgmHiU?t=327)
- Competing head-on with a competitor's price cut leads to a race-to-the-bottom price war; the better response is to differentiate on functionality or value rather than compete on a commodity basis [how-to-price-for-b2b-startup-school @ 06:47](https://youtu.be/4hjiRmgmHiU?t=407)
- Committed recurring revenue (MRR/ARR) is preferable to usage-based pricing because it protects revenue during downturns until contract renewal, whereas usage-based revenue can fall off a cliff in a bad month [how-to-price-for-b2b-startup-school @ 08:52](https://youtu.be/4hjiRmgmHiU?t=532)
- Enterprise pricing should stay off the public website ("contact sales") since the value equation differs per customer; instead gate enterprise-only features like SSO, SOC 2, and audit logs behind custom-priced tiers [how-to-price-for-b2b-startup-school @ 10:16](https://youtu.be/4hjiRmgmHiU?t=616)
- A good rule of thumb for sales compensation is a 5:1 ratio of new signed ARR to total salesperson compensation, which also reveals whether your price point supports true account executives versus an inside-sales call center [how-to-price-for-b2b-startup-school @ 11:32](https://youtu.be/4hjiRmgmHiU?t=692)
- Pilots and free trials should be kept short (a couple of weeks to a month) with clear success criteria tied to the value equation, rather than long open-ended trials [how-to-price-for-b2b-startup-school @ 13:36](https://youtu.be/4hjiRmgmHiU?t=816)
- If pricing is truly uncertain, pick a number similar to comparable software and raise it 50% with each new pitch until you start losing more than 25% of deals on price, which signals the right ballpark [how-to-price-for-b2b-startup-school @ 14:49](https://youtu.be/4hjiRmgmHiU?t=889)
