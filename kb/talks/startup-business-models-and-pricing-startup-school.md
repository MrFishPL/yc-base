# Startup Business Models and Pricing | Startup School — Compiled Truth

A YC group partner analyzes which business models built the YC top 100 companies and shares five pricing insights drawn from thousands of YC startups.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Nearly every billion-dollar company uses one of nine proven business models (SaaS, transactional, marketplace, hard tech, usage-based, enterprise, advertising, e-commerce, bio), so founders should copy a proven model rather than reinvent one [startup-business-models-and-pricing-startup-school @ 01:13](https://youtu.be/oWZbWzAyHAE?t=73)
- SaaS, transactional, and marketplace models alone make up 67% of the YC top 100 companies, while advertising and e-commerce barely register [startup-business-models-and-pricing-startup-school @ 03:08](https://youtu.be/oWZbWzAyHAE?t=188)
- Marketplaces are only 14% of the top 100 but generate 30% of the value because they tend to become winner-take-all through network effects once they clear their chicken-and-egg problem [startup-business-models-and-pricing-startup-school @ 04:20](https://youtu.be/oWZbWzAyHAE?t=260)
- Transactional businesses outperform because they sit directly in the flow of funds, making it easy to take a cut and become critical infrastructure their customers won't rip out [startup-business-models-and-pricing-startup-school @ 06:16](https://youtu.be/oWZbWzAyHAE?t=376)
- A five-percentage-point difference in monthly retention (95% vs 90%) compounds into a huge gap in year-end customers, illustrating why recurring-revenue businesses need strong retention to avoid a leaky bucket [startup-business-models-and-pricing-startup-school @ 12:16](https://youtu.be/oWZbWzAyHAE?t=736)
- The most common founder mistake is being afraid to charge; pricing is actually a tool for learning who wants your product, which segments want it most, and how much value it provides [startup-business-models-and-pricing-startup-school @ 16:24](https://youtu.be/oWZbWzAyHAE?t=984)
- Stripe deliberately priced above competitors (5% vs. ~3% per transaction) to test how much value customers placed on its onboarding and documentation [startup-business-models-and-pricing-startup-school @ 17:44](https://youtu.be/oWZbWzAyHAE?t=1064)
- Founders should price on perceived value rather than cost-plus, and can find that value by asking customers what problem they hoped the product would solve, or by raising prices until customers complain but still pay [startup-business-models-and-pricing-startup-school @ 19:28](https://youtu.be/oWZbWzAyHAE?t=1168)
- Most startups are underpricing, and competing on price alone is not a durable advantage since a larger competitor can always undercut you [startup-business-models-and-pricing-startup-school @ 23:06](https://youtu.be/oWZbWzAyHAE?t=1386)
- Segment initially charged $120/year, was advised to charge $120,000/year for an enterprise deal, and settled at $18,000 — a 150x increase that came only from asking for the higher price [startup-business-models-and-pricing-startup-school @ 30:10](https://youtu.be/oWZbWzAyHAE?t=1810)
