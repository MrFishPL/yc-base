# An AI Primer with Wojciech Zaremba — Compiled Truth

OpenAI co-founder Wojciech Zaremba walks through the basics of AI, machine learning, and deep learning, and explains why supervised learning is the only paradigm currently mature enough for real business applications while robotics and reinforcement learning remain fundamentally harder.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- OpenAI's mission is to build AI for the good of humanity, backed by roughly $1 billion in funding gathered with support from Elon Musk and Sam Altman [an-ai-primer-with-wojciech-zaremba @ 00:45](https://youtu.be/hHpKa2iamF0?t=45)
- Of robotics' three core problem families — locomotion, navigation, and manipulation — manipulation (grasping an arbitrary object) is by far the least solved, since no general program can yet grasp any object the way hand-coded solutions can for specific ones [an-ai-primer-with-wojciech-zaremba @ 02:55](https://youtu.be/hHpKa2iamF0?t=175)
- Reinforcement learning's core weakness outside of games is that defining a reward signal (e.g., for "scrambling eggs") and resetting an environment to repeat trials millions of times is extremely difficult in the real world [an-ai-primer-with-wojciech-zaremba @ 07:36](https://youtu.be/hHpKa2iamF0?t=456)
- Deep learning's core idea is deceptively simple: rather than mapping data to a program in one computational step, it does so through many sequential steps [an-ai-primer-with-wojciech-zaremba @ 14:21](https://youtu.be/hHpKa2iamF0?t=861)
- The breakthrough that finally made deep neural networks trainable wasn't a new algorithm but simply initializing weight magnitudes correctly so signal neither blows up nor vanishes across many layers, discovered at Geoffrey Hinton's lab at the University of Toronto [an-ai-primer-with-wojciech-zaremba @ 29:26](https://youtu.be/hHpKa2iamF0?t=1766)
- On the ImageNet benchmark, a University of Toronto team led by Hinton (including Alex Krizhevsky) jumped from the ~25% error rate every other team was clustered around to ~15%, kicking off the modern deep learning wave [an-ai-primer-with-wojciech-zaremba @ 35:25](https://youtu.be/hHpKa2iamF0?t=2125)
- The same architectural trick of sharing parameters across positions in the input generalized from computer vision to speech recognition and then to sequence-to-sequence machine translation, a result attributed to Ilya Sutskever [an-ai-primer-with-wojciech-zaremba @ 39:33](https://youtu.be/hHpKa2iamF0?t=2373)
- On automation displacing blue-collar jobs, he believes society will need to offer people a basic income, since it's unrealistic to expect most workers, like a 40-year-old taxi driver, to reinvent their careers every decade [an-ai-primer-with-wojciech-zaremba @ 51:53](https://youtu.be/hHpKa2iamF0?t=3113)
