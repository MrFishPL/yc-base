# Emergent: How Six Months of Tinkering Led To A $100M ARR Company — Compiled Truth

Mukund, founder of Emergent (and previously Dunzo), describes how a burnt-out post-Dunzo period of pure, aimless tinkering with early LLMs led to a "vibe coding" platform that hit $100M ARR and 8.5M users in under a year.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- After leaving Dunzo depressed, he spent six months tinkering 10-12 hours a day on AI tools with no objective, which became the source of the insights behind Emergent [emergent-how-six-months-of-tinkering-led-to-a-100m-arr-compa @ 14:10](https://youtu.be/yyXCQHX55N4?t=850)
- Emergent pitched "automate all of software engineering" to VCs while everyone else was building copilots, and got rejected by most investors who thought the models weren't capable enough yet [emergent-how-six-months-of-tinkering-led-to-a-100m-arr-compa @ 16:24](https://youtu.be/yyXCQHX55N4?t=984)
- The team deliberately built for where AI models would be in the near future rather than their current limits, e.g. skipping investment in JSON-output parsing because they bet the next model generation would just solve it [emergent-how-six-months-of-tinkering-led-to-a-100m-arr-compa @ 20:08](https://youtu.be/yyXCQHX55N4?t=1208)
- Early on at YC the team pivoted ideas weekly and, partly to refocus a frustrated team, attacked the SWE-bench coding benchmark, becoming world #1 in three months — which became the technical foundation for Emergent's agents [emergent-how-six-months-of-tinkering-led-to-a-100m-arr-compa @ 21:57](https://youtu.be/yyXCQHX55N4?t=1317)
- Emergent won against earlier, more established AI website builders by targeting real shippable software with working backends and databases, not front-end demoware [emergent-how-six-months-of-tinkering-led-to-a-100m-arr-compa @ 23:47](https://youtu.be/yyXCQHX55N4?t=1427)
- The company converted growth into a "math problem" (views needed, impressions, clicks, users) and chose influencer marketing as its primary go-to-market channel [emergent-how-six-months-of-tinkering-led-to-a-100m-arr-compa @ 24:45](https://youtu.be/yyXCQHX55N4?t=1485)
- From his earlier company Dunzo, he learned that focus matters — Dunzo spread itself across marketplace, pickup-and-drop, and dark stores instead of doubling down on the one model that was working [emergent-how-six-months-of-tinkering-led-to-a-100m-arr-compa @ 11:56](https://youtu.be/yyXCQHX55N4?t=716)
- He carried Dunzo's "watchtower" operational-monitoring culture into Emergent, where the team actively monitors all AI-generated software builds and flags breakages [emergent-how-six-months-of-tinkering-led-to-a-100m-arr-compa @ 12:59](https://youtu.be/yyXCQHX55N4?t=779)
- His advice to founders is to think global from day one, since building a local company is just as hard as building a global one, and to aim at "attacking the ceiling," thinking 10x-100x bigger than their current ambition [emergent-how-six-months-of-tinkering-led-to-a-100m-arr-compa @ 27:12](https://youtu.be/yyXCQHX55N4?t=1632)
