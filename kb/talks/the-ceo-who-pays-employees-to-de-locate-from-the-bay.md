# The CEO Who Pays Employees to De-Locate From the Bay — Compiled Truth

Zapier's CEO explains how a $10,000 "de-location" relocation package, deliberate remote-first management practices, and disciplined fundraising decisions helped the fully remote company scale to ~95 employees with very high retention.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Zapier's de-location package, paying $10,000 to help new hires move away from the Bay Area, drove roughly a 30% increase in applicants after launch, with most applicants coming from outside the Bay Area entirely [the-ceo-who-pays-employees-to-de-locate-from-the-bay @ 01:47](https://youtu.be/g3mPvKUksag?t=107).
- New hires get flown to the Bay Area for a week of in-person onboarding with their manager and the founders, which lets even the 95th employee build real personal rapport with leadership [the-ceo-who-pays-employees-to-de-locate-from-the-bay @ 03:56](https://youtu.be/g3mPvKUksag?t=236).
- Starting fully remote from day one built organizational habits (documenting everything, working through pull requests and chat) that would have been much harder to retrofit onto an already in-office team [the-ceo-who-pays-employees-to-de-locate-from-the-bay @ 05:05](https://youtu.be/g3mPvKUksag?t=305).
- Zapier hit clear management inflection points around 20 employees (needing informal shared management) and 40-50 employees (needing a first layer of managers managing managers) [the-ceo-who-pays-employees-to-de-locate-from-the-bay @ 07:13](https://youtu.be/g3mPvKUksag?t=433).
- Rather than inventing a new management philosophy, Zapier adopted an existing external framework (the Manager Tools podcast approach) wholesale to give every manager a consistent baseline [the-ceo-who-pays-employees-to-de-locate-from-the-bay @ 09:56](https://youtu.be/g3mPvKUksag?t=596).
- Zapier joined YC primarily for network access and credibility as an unknown, unconnected team from Missouri, not primarily for the capital, and only raised money once to cover early living costs in the Bay Area [the-ceo-who-pays-employees-to-de-locate-from-the-bay @ 13:35](https://youtu.be/g3mPvKUksag?t=815).
- Fundraising should be treated as an optional tool rather than a core part of founder identity ("bootstrapper" vs. "VC-backed"); Zapier never raised again because they never needed to and didn't want unnecessary dilution [the-ceo-who-pays-employees-to-de-locate-from-the-bay @ 16:10](https://youtu.be/g3mPvKUksag?t=970).
- True winner-take-all dynamics mostly arise from network effects common in b2c products (Google, Facebook), which is why b2b companies like MailChimp or CRM vendors can win their market profitably without ever raising venture capital [the-ceo-who-pays-employees-to-de-locate-from-the-bay @ 18:58](https://youtu.be/g3mPvKUksag?t=1138).
- Zapier's moat comes from having built more integrations than any competitor, with partners building many of those integrations themselves, making a head-to-head copy very hard to replicate [the-ceo-who-pays-employees-to-de-locate-from-the-bay @ 25:23](https://youtu.be/g3mPvKUksag?t=1523).
- Zapier measured 97% employee retention over the prior twelve months and a 72 employee Net Promoter Score, attributing this to weekly one-on-ones, random cross-company "pair buddies" calls, and informal off-topic Slack channels [the-ceo-who-pays-employees-to-de-locate-from-the-bay @ 32:47](https://youtu.be/g3mPvKUksag?t=1967).
