# Startup Experts Reveal Their Favorite Pivot Stories — Compiled Truth

YC partners walk through real pivot stories (Brex, Goat, GoCardless, Clipboard Health, Niantic/Asher Reality, Creative Market) to show that pivoting is normal, often necessary, and teaches founders what problem is actually worth solving.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Brex's founders originally applied to YC with a VR headset idea and only pivoted back to payments (their prior expertise) after about three or four weeks of the batch, once they discovered they weren't the team to build hardware [startup-experts-reveal-their-favorite-pivot-stories @ 01:56](https://youtu.be/DmehFuCMtvc?t=116)
- Goat's founders were originally funded as Grubwithus, a group-dinner social startup with bad retention, and it took about two years post-YC before they pivoted into the sneaker marketplace that became Goat, years ahead of sneaker collecting becoming mainstream [startup-experts-reveal-their-favorite-pivot-stories @ 03:04](https://youtu.be/DmehFuCMtvc?t=184)
- Tom Blomfield's first YC company, Groupay (a group bill-splitting app), failed because users churned since it wasn't useful enough, and PG's advice to stop building features and just go get users led to cold-calling that revealed the real opportunity in business payments, becoming GoCardless [startup-experts-reveal-their-favorite-pivot-stories @ 05:55](https://youtu.be/DmehFuCMtvc?t=355)
- Clipboard Health started as an "Indeed for nurses" hiring platform, and the founder only found product-market fit after spending enormous time selling to hospitals and talking to people around them, discovering that nurses calling in sick always had to go through an agency [startup-experts-reveal-their-favorite-pivot-stories @ 09:07](https://youtu.be/DmehFuCMtvc?t=547)
- "Pivot hell" happens when founders, often driven by perfectionism, change to a completely new idea every week instead of committing to learn from one idea, and companies can escape it, sometimes surfacing a strong idea (like productizing a regulatory change) about six months after a rough batch [startup-experts-reveal-their-favorite-pivot-stories @ 10:56](https://youtu.be/DmehFuCMtvc?t=656)
- Asher Reality started as "cool technology in search of a problem" (an AR SDK) and marketing customers who gave enthusiastic but false positive feedback; real signal only came from asking for payment and from pivoting the go-to-market toward game developers before Pokémon Go [startup-experts-reveal-their-favorite-pivot-stories @ 14:22](https://youtu.be/DmehFuCMtvc?t=862)
- Creative Market's founders spent about a year and a half after YC "wandering in the wilderness" monetizing their Color Lovers community with ads, contests, and software sales because they lacked a single main KPI to focus on, until launching a marketplace made growth immediately measurable [startup-experts-reveal-their-favorite-pivot-stories @ 17:37](https://youtu.be/DmehFuCMtvc?t=1057)
- If you don't know what your main KPI is or what you're chasing when you wake up every morning, that's a signal you probably need to pivot [startup-experts-reveal-their-favorite-pivot-stories @ 18:52](https://youtu.be/DmehFuCMtvc?t=1132)
- Founders matter more than the idea in a pivot: the company can be knocked down to the studs and rebuilt around a totally different idea, and founders often become more formidable once working on something they're excited about and well-suited for [startup-experts-reveal-their-favorite-pivot-stories @ 19:26](https://youtu.be/DmehFuCMtvc?t=1166)
