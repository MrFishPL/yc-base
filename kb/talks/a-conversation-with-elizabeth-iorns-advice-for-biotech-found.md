# A Conversation with Elizabeth Iorns - Advice for Biotech Founders — Compiled Truth

Science Exchange founder and YC biotech partner Elizabeth Iorns discusses building a two-sided B2B marketplace for outsourced research and gives founders advice on MVPs, co-founders, and mistakes specific to biotech startups.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Science Exchange started from Iorns's own frustration as a cancer researcher: scientific outsourcing was fragmented and opaque, causing 10x price differences for equivalent experiments [a-conversation-with-elizabeth-iorns-advice-for-biotech-found @ 04:24](https://youtu.be/e_pN0P0zD8g?t=264)
- The first MVP was a bare listing site where the team quickly learned they had to build a true curated B2B marketplace with quality assurance and project management, not just a post-and-quote board [a-conversation-with-elizabeth-iorns-advice-for-biotech-found @ 11:56](https://youtu.be/e_pN0P0zD8g?t=716)
- The team defined product-market fit as the moment a first large pharma client (Amgen) adopted the platform for whole sectors of its discovery research, replacing an internal manual process [a-conversation-with-elizabeth-iorns-advice-for-biotech-found @ 17:02](https://youtu.be/e_pN0P0zD8g?t=1022)
- Science Exchange's biggest competitor is the status quo — companies running the process manually through ad hoc SharePoint sites rather than a rival product [a-conversation-with-elizabeth-iorns-advice-for-biotech-found @ 18:09](https://youtu.be/e_pN0P0zD8g?t=1089)
- In biotech there is effectively no MVP: most biotechs earn no commercial revenue until an approved product exists, so progress is measured by de-risking milestones instead of iterative shipping [a-conversation-with-elizabeth-iorns-advice-for-biotech-found @ 31:03](https://youtu.be/e_pN0P0zD8g?t=1863)
- A common founder mistake is avoiding the single "killer experiment" that would definitively prove or kill the science, because founders fear the answer [a-conversation-with-elizabeth-iorns-advice-for-biotech-found @ 36:10](https://youtu.be/e_pN0P0zD8g?t=2170)
- Iorns argues a technical/scientific co-founder doesn't need a business background to be effective — hustle and complementary fit matter more, and business fundamentals can be learned on the job [a-conversation-with-elizabeth-iorns-advice-for-biotech-found @ 37:25](https://youtu.be/e_pN0P0zD8g?t=2245)
- The biggest inflection point for Science Exchange was launching the (initially non-obvious, distraction-seeming) reproducibility initiative, which built high-profile brand credibility and opened pharma partnership doors [a-conversation-with-elizabeth-iorns-advice-for-biotech-found @ 42:18](https://youtu.be/e_pN0P0zD8g?t=2538)
- YC applicants without a technical co-founder can still get in if they show up with credible research and a working demo — Iorns's team found a technical co-founder in two weeks after being told they'd otherwise be rejected [a-conversation-with-elizabeth-iorns-advice-for-biotech-found @ 47:22](https://youtu.be/e_pN0P0zD8g?t=2842)
