# Reshma Shetty, Founder of Ginkgo Bioworks at the Female Founders Conference — Compiled Truth

Ginkgo Bioworks' co-founder argues that the popular advice to "not raise too much money" is misleading — the real skill is raising a lot of capital while keeping the spending discipline that got the company there in the first place.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Advice telling founders not to raise too much money is misleading; founders should raise lots of money but be smart about how they spend it, since extra dilution is often worth it if it's the difference between success and failure [reshma-shetty-founder-of-ginkgo-bioworks-at-the-female-found @ 04:33](https://youtu.be/OSZidU6R_mU?t=273).
- After a raise, both the founder and the whole team feel unconscious pressure to abandon frugality and scrappiness, which manifests as over-hiring, building out HR, and outsourcing annoying tasks [reshma-shetty-founder-of-ginkgo-bioworks-at-the-female-found @ 07:01](https://youtu.be/OSZidU6R_mU?t=421).
- Unchecked spending and headcount bloat is "the kiss of death" for a startup because it raises the revenue bar needed to sustain the team, no matter how much was raised [reshma-shetty-founder-of-ginkgo-bioworks-at-the-female-found @ 08:16](https://youtu.be/OSZidU6R_mU?t=496).
- Every organization needs a "Scrooge McDuck" figure who keeps the team focused on key metrics and pushes for efficiency in both headcount and spend [reshma-shetty-founder-of-ginkgo-bioworks-at-the-female-found @ 08:53](https://youtu.be/OSZidU6R_mU?t=533).
- Female founders who take on this "Scrooge" enforcer role face a higher personal reputational risk of being seen as an unsympathetic "battle ax," a stereotype that has to be weighed against the organizational risk of not enforcing efficiency [reshma-shetty-founder-of-ginkgo-bioworks-at-the-female-found @ 09:37](https://youtu.be/OSZidU6R_mU?t=577).
- The goal is to decouple growth in revenue/customers from growth in headcount and spend, since problems like recruiting difficulty and interpersonal conflict scale (the latter roughly with N-squared) as an organization grows [reshma-shetty-founder-of-ginkgo-bioworks-at-the-female-found @ 11:11](https://youtu.be/OSZidU6R_mU?t=671).
- YC growth partner Ali Rowghani's "mission to metrics" framework says a leader must map mission (purpose) to strategy (how you make money) to metrics (how you quantitatively measure success or failure) and communicate that chain to the whole team [reshma-shetty-founder-of-ginkgo-bioworks-at-the-female-found @ 12:47](https://youtu.be/OSZidU6R_mU?t=767).
- At least one company metric should specifically measure efficiency (e.g., output per person, revenue per salesperson) so the team is forced to think about the denominator, not just total output [reshma-shetty-founder-of-ginkgo-bioworks-at-the-female-found @ 15:17](https://youtu.be/OSZidU6R_mU?t=917).
- Ginkgo's own mission-to-metrics chain is: mission to make biology easy to engineer, strategy to aggregate genetic engineering R&D, and a metric of how much genetic engineering output is produced per person [reshma-shetty-founder-of-ginkgo-bioworks-at-the-female-found @ 16:16](https://youtu.be/OSZidU6R_mU?t=976).
