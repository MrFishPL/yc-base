# Ask These Questions Before Starting An AI Startup — Compiled Truth

Jordan Fisher, who runs an alignment research team at Anthropic and has founded startups through YC, argues that founders should stop assuming they know what's coming and instead interrogate a long list of open questions about how AGI-level capability will reshape products, teams, trust, and defensibility.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Founders should plan roughly two years ahead assuming AGI is likely to arrive soon, not just anticipate the next six months of model capability as common advice suggests [ask-these-questions-before-starting-an-ai-startup @ 03:44](https://youtu.be/DJjZzzPANBY?t=224)
- Enterprises are being armed with the same AGI-level agents as startups, so the assumption that slow corporate buying cycles protect startups may be wrong as the buy side accelerates its own adoption [ask-these-questions-before-starting-an-ai-startup @ 04:46](https://youtu.be/DJjZzzPANBY?t=286)
- It's an open question whether software fully commoditizes as enterprises build tools in-house with agents like Claude Code, or whether AI instead raises the bar for what counts as an exceptional product [ask-these-questions-before-starting-an-ai-startup @ 06:12](https://youtu.be/DJjZzzPANBY?t=372)
- Trust becomes the binding constraint on what agents can be allowed to do — letting an agent write and ship code on demand down to the database layer requires far more confidence than reviewing five minutes of Claude's output at a time [ask-these-questions-before-starting-an-ai-startup @ 07:44](https://youtu.be/DJjZzzPANBY?t=464)
- As teams shrink toward being "semi-automated," the traditional check on bad behavior — a diverse team where someone would object or whistleblow — weakens, motivating new mechanisms like AI-powered audits that could even delete their own findings once a company is cleared [ask-these-questions-before-starting-an-ai-startup @ 13:14](https://youtu.be/DJjZzzPANBY?t=794)
- Whether proprietary custom data still confers an edge depends heavily on the domain — general LLMs now beat most fine-tuned custom models, but tacit knowledge locked inside companies like TSMC or ASML may remain outside what frontier models know [ask-these-questions-before-starting-an-ai-startup @ 18:39](https://youtu.be/DJjZzzPANBY?t=1119)
- Founders should treat long-term defensibility as a central question post-AGI, since hard physical-world problems (infrastructure, energy, manufacturing, chips) are likely to stay hard longer than software problems a future frontier model could replicate on demand [ask-these-questions-before-starting-an-ai-startup @ 21:09](https://youtu.be/DJjZzzPANBY?t=1269)
- Whether a given task has an intelligence "ceiling" determines how fast it commoditizes — once a task like writing a poem or a PR diff is good enough, moving to the next model no longer buys an edge and commoditization pressure intensifies [ask-these-questions-before-starting-an-ai-startup @ 22:38](https://youtu.be/DJjZzzPANBY?t=1358)
- On passion versus defensibility: after six months of 100-hour weeks, what keeps a founder going is commitment to impact and to co-founders and team, not how passionate they originally were about the idea [ask-these-questions-before-starting-an-ai-startup @ 31:45](https://youtu.be/DJjZzzPANBY?t=1905)
- Even VCs who claim to be ahead of the AI curve are usually reacting to what's already obvious rather than asking what needs to be true two years out for a bet to stay resilient, which he sees as an industry-wide groupthink problem [ask-these-questions-before-starting-an-ai-startup @ 37:00](https://youtu.be/DJjZzzPANBY?t=2220)
