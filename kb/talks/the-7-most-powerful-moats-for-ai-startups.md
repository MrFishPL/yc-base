# The 7 Most Powerful Moats For AI Startups — Compiled Truth

The Lightcone hosts walk through Hamilton Helmer's "Seven Powers" framework and translate each classic moat into its AI-era equivalent, arguing the categories of moats haven't changed even though their concrete shape has.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Before a startup has anything worth defending, the only real moat is speed — find a painful problem and solve it fast rather than worrying about long-term defensibility [the-7-most-powerful-moats-for-ai-startups @ 06:04](https://youtu.be/bxBzsSsqQAM?t=364)
- Process power in AI shows up as deeply battle-tested, reliable agents (like Cursor's daily-shipped codegen or bank-focused agents like Greenlight and Casca) that are far harder to replicate than the weekend-hackathon version of the same product [the-7-most-powerful-moats-for-ai-startups @ 10:05](https://youtu.be/bxBzsSsqQAM?t=605)
- Cornered resources in AI often take the form of proprietary access — government relationships (Palantir/Scale AI with the DOD), or real customer data and workflows gathered by forward-deployed engineers embedded with a customer [the-7-most-powerful-moats-for-ai-startups @ 14:24](https://youtu.be/bxBzsSsqQAM?t=864)
- Switching costs are evolving from old SaaS-style data lock-in toward deep, custom-built agent workflows won through long enterprise pilots (Happy Robot with DHL, Salient with banks) that convert into multi-year, seven-figure contracts [the-7-most-powerful-moats-for-ai-startups @ 19:19](https://youtu.be/bxBzsSsqQAM?t=1159)
- Vertical AI agents can capture far more of a customer's wallet than legacy vertical SaaS because they take over actual labor rather than just software — Avoca went from the roughly 1% of HVAC-company spend that ServiceTitan captures to 4-10% by owning customer support outcomes [the-7-most-powerful-moats-for-ai-startups @ 28:16](https://youtu.be/bxBzsSsqQAM?t=1696)
- Counter-positioning shows up as legacy SaaS incumbents (Zendesk, Intercom) being structurally unable to cannibalize their own per-seat pricing model even as their AI agents make fewer human seats necessary [the-7-most-powerful-moats-for-ai-startups @ 25:04](https://youtu.be/bxBzsSsqQAM?t=1504)
- Second-movers can win by counter-positioning against an early leader that made the wrong strategic bet, as Legora out-executed Harvey in legal AI by focusing on the application layer instead of on fine-tuning [the-7-most-powerful-moats-for-ai-startups @ 31:56](https://youtu.be/bxBzsSsqQAM?t=1916)
- Brand as a moat can be built surprisingly fast in the AI era — ChatGPT has more daily consumer usage than Gemini despite Google having a vastly larger pre-existing user base [the-7-most-powerful-moats-for-ai-startups @ 35:06](https://youtu.be/bxBzsSsqQAM?t=2106)
- Network economies in AI increasingly take the shape of data and eval flywheels, where every user interaction (down to every keystroke in Cursor) feeds back into training a better model [the-7-most-powerful-moats-for-ai-startups @ 38:19](https://youtu.be/bxBzsSsqQAM?t=2299)
- Scale economies mainly show up at the foundation-model layer, where the huge capital cost of training a frontier model is a barrier to entry, with an application-layer analog in Exa's early, expensive investment in crawling a large chunk of the web [the-7-most-powerful-moats-for-ai-startups @ 40:47](https://youtu.be/bxBzsSsqQAM?t=2447)
