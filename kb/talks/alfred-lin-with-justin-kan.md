# Alfred Lin with Justin Kan — Compiled Truth

In this fireside chat, Sequoia partner Alfred Lin traces his path from LinkExchange through Tellme and Zappos to venture investing, drawing lessons about network effects, financial discipline, culture, and what makes a fundable founder.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- LinkExchange was born when Tony Hsieh and Sanjeev Kagen couldn't get traffic to the websites they built for clients, so they linked sites together into an ad-swap network that became more valuable to join as it grew via network effects [alfred-lin-with-justin-kan @ 01:36](https://youtu.be/T-D-JqxQtIY?t=96)
- Even a company as successful as eBay (originally "AuctionWeb") needed LinkExchange's promotion in its very early days, showing that network effects made joining progressively more compelling for new sites [alfred-lin-with-justin-kan @ 05:13](https://youtu.be/T-D-JqxQtIY?t=313)
- At Tellme Networks, raising $265 million turned out to be a liability: too much cash removed the discipline to prioritize, until the company pivoted to a focused enterprise SaaS model targeting just 12-24 large customers [alfred-lin-with-justin-kan @ 08:04](https://youtu.be/T-D-JqxQtIY?t=484)
- Many well-funded e-commerce competitors in 1999-2001 miscalculated customer lifetime value on small samples and overspent on acquisition with no margin for error, while Zappos instead aimed to be profitable on the first order [alfred-lin-with-justin-kan @ 14:04](https://youtu.be/T-D-JqxQtIY?t=844)
- Zappos was chronically hard to fund, forcing personal sacrifices like Tony Hsieh selling his apartments to keep the company afloat during a long, painful capital-raising period [alfred-lin-with-justin-kan @ 12:56](https://youtu.be/T-D-JqxQtIY?t=776)
- The core cultural lesson carried from LinkExchange to Zappos was that great culture decays without daily, habitual investment — you can't maintain it only in good times [alfred-lin-with-justin-kan @ 15:29](https://youtu.be/T-D-JqxQtIY?t=929)
- Zappos innovated operationally by picking, packing, and shipping orders within 4 hours, running fulfillment 24/7, and giving every warehouse item a unique license-plate number for near-100% inventory accuracy [alfred-lin-with-justin-kan @ 23:16](https://youtu.be/T-D-JqxQtIY?t=1396)
- Amazon acquired Zappos while letting it remain a separate brand, culture, and location — a structure more compelling to the team than being absorbed into the parent company [alfred-lin-with-justin-kan @ 25:05](https://youtu.be/T-D-JqxQtIY?t=1505)
- Sequoia looks for founders who are "unstoppable," have deep insight into their industry from asking "why" repeatedly, and can name the specific handful of things they'll do to disrupt it [alfred-lin-with-justin-kan @ 29:43](https://youtu.be/T-D-JqxQtIY?t=1783)
- Founders should vet investors like long-term partners rather than just capital sources, noting that roughly half of such long-term relationships (like marriages) still end badly [alfred-lin-with-justin-kan @ 28:38](https://youtu.be/T-D-JqxQtIY?t=1718)
