# Why Should I Start a Startup? by Michael Seibel — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel argues that starting a startup only makes sense for a specific personality type who performs best without a predictable path, and that people should be brutally honest with themselves about which type they are rather than following hype or peer pressure.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The core reason to start a startup: some people only work at their peak when there's no predictable path and they must take full personal responsibility for failure [why-should-i-start-a-startup-by-michael-seibel @ 00:41](https://youtu.be/qh8sHetf-Nk?t=41)
- Seibel splits people into groups: a tiny fraction (~1%) who can only thrive in radically entrepreneurial roles, a small "on the fence" group, an even smaller group equally capable as founder or corporate employee, and a large majority best suited to operating within an existing system [why-should-i-start-a-startup-by-michael-seibel @ 02:04](https://youtu.be/qh8sHetf-Nk?t=124)
- The majority-type person wants an existing system, existing path, and existing rules to optimize within, a mindset reinforced by K-12 and college [why-should-i-start-a-startup-by-michael-seibel @ 03:19](https://youtu.be/qh8sHetf-Nk?t=199)
- As a founder, almost by definition, the thing you're good at is the thing you eventually stop doing, since it's rare to delegate well enough to keep doing what you love [why-should-i-start-a-startup-by-michael-seibel @ 05:22](https://youtu.be/qh8sHetf-Nk?t=322)
- To figure out which group you're in, ask where you are at your best and where you organically apply 100% self-motivated effort [why-should-i-start-a-startup-by-michael-seibel @ 06:01](https://youtu.be/qh8sHetf-Nk?t=361)
- Look at the moments you actually outperformed and delivered your best results, not just where you did fine within a structured system like K-12 [why-should-i-start-a-startup-by-michael-seibel @ 06:27](https://youtu.be/qh8sHetf-Nk?t=387)
- Be careful taking career advice from biased sources: big companies like Google orchestrate the recruiting pitch to smart students, and the pitch serves the company's interests more than the student's [why-should-i-start-a-startup-by-michael-seibel @ 09:14](https://youtu.be/qh8sHetf-Nk?t=554)
- Peer advice that worked well through K-12 and into applying to college stops working after college, when the single track splits into thousands of tracks peers don't understand [why-should-i-start-a-startup-by-michael-seibel @ 11:18](https://youtu.be/qh8sHetf-Nk?t=678)
- Big companies benefit from students having poor information, since everything a big company does to attract a college recruit is orchestrated [why-should-i-start-a-startup-by-michael-seibel @ 11:58](https://youtu.be/qh8sHetf-Nk?t=718)
- If you're not irrationally motivated to be entrepreneurial, it will be frustrating and you won't understand the risk/reward, even if the business opportunity is objectively good [why-should-i-start-a-startup-by-michael-seibel @ 13:16](https://youtu.be/qh8sHetf-Nk?t=796)
