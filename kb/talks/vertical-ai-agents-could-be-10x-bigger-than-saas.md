# Vertical AI Agents Could Be 10X Bigger Than SaaS — Compiled Truth

The Lightcone hosts argue that vertical AI agents will replicate and surpass the ~$300B, 300-unicorn SaaS wave because they can replace not just software spend but the much larger payroll spend behind it.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- SaaS produced over 300 unicorns and captured more than 40% of venture dollars over 20 years because B2B software fragments into countless deep, domain-specific verticals that no single "Microsoft of SaaS" can serve [vertical-ai-agents-could-be-10x-bigger-than-saas @ 02:12](https://youtu.be/ASABxNenD_U?t=132)
- Incumbents almost never entered vertical B2B SaaS because it requires founders who are obsessively deep in one narrow domain (e.g., payroll), which isn't worth a big company's focus [vertical-ai-agents-could-be-10x-bigger-than-saas @ 09:09](https://youtu.be/ASABxNenD_U?t=549)
- Unlike SaaS, which still required an operations team to run the workflows, vertical AI agents can also absorb the payroll cost of the humans running those workflows, making the disruption potentially 10x larger than the SaaS company it replaces [vertical-ai-agents-could-be-10x-bigger-than-saas @ 20:39](https://youtu.be/ASABxNenD_U?t=1239)
- A recurring pattern for finding a vertical AI agent idea is spotting "boring, repetitive admin work," often by directly experiencing it (e.g., a founder shadowing his dentist mother to find dental medical-billing pain points) [vertical-ai-agents-could-be-10x-bigger-than-saas @ 40:00](https://youtu.be/ASABxNenD_U?t=2400)
- Selling a vertical AI agent that threatens to eliminate the very team you're selling to backfires — the pitch must go high enough (e.g., to engineering or the CEO) that the threatened team can't sabotage the deal [vertical-ai-agents-could-be-10x-bigger-than-saas @ 22:15](https://youtu.be/ASABxNenD_U?t=1335)
- A QA-agent company (mtic) reframed its pitch from "makes your QA team faster" to "you don't need a QA team at all," selling directly to engineering instead of needing QA's buy-in [vertical-ai-agents-could-be-10x-bigger-than-saas @ 23:49](https://youtu.be/ASABxNenD_U?t=1429)
- The hiring calculus for post-PMF startups is shifting: instead of hiring the best salesperson or customer-success lead, founders should hire strong software engineers who can build LLM systems that automate the functions that used to require large headcount [vertical-ai-agents-could-be-10x-bigger-than-saas @ 13:39](https://youtu.be/ASABxNenD_U?t=819)
- AI customer support looked crowded (~100 companies), but nearly all of them were shallow zero-shot LLM wrappers; only 3-4 companies were building software complex enough to actually replace a real support team, leaving the market wide open [vertical-ai-agents-could-be-10x-bigger-than-saas @ 26:19](https://youtu.be/ASABxNenD_U?t=1579)
- Highly specific, narrowly-trained vertical agents (e.g., a company handling 30,000 support tickets/day for one client, Zepto) beat general-purpose demos because customers need heavily tailored solutions, not one-size-fits-all platforms [vertical-ai-agents-could-be-10x-bigger-than-saas @ 27:33](https://youtu.be/ASABxNenD_U?t=1653)
- Because LLM-powered tools let "the rocks read," managers and CEOs may be able to extend their effective span of control well past the traditional Dunbar-number-style organizational limits [vertical-ai-agents-could-be-10x-bigger-than-saas @ 30:49](https://youtu.be/ASABxNenD_U?t=1849)
