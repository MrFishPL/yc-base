# Tips For Technical Startup Founders | Startup School — Compiled Truth

Diana Hu (YC Group Partner, former Escher Reality co-founder/CTO) lays out how a technical founder's role and build approach should evolve across the ideating, MVP, and launch/iterate-to-PMF stages, compiled from conversations with many YC technical founders.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A technical founder is a full commitment partner who must do all the tech things (frontend, backend, devops, UX, even IT) rather than a "person hired to build the app" [tips-for-technical-startup-founders-startup-school @ 02:08](https://youtu.be/rP7bpYsfa6Q?t=128)
- In the ideating stage, build a clickable/scrappy prototype in a matter of days purely to demo to users, as Optimizely did with a JavaScript A/B-test file thrown together in days [tips-for-technical-startup-founders-startup-school @ 06:08](https://youtu.be/rP7bpYsfa6Q?t=368)
- Hiring engineers too early to help build the MVP is usually a bad idea because it slows you down finding good people and causes founders to miss key product insights they'd only get by building it themselves [tips-for-technical-startup-founders-startup-school @ 09:31](https://youtu.be/rP7bpYsfa6Q?t=571)
- Apply "do things that don't scale" at the MVP stage — DoorDash's original site was static HTML/PDF menus with Google Forms/Docs as the backend and Find My Friends for driver tracking, built in one afternoon [tips-for-technical-startup-founders-startup-school @ 13:31](https://youtu.be/rP7bpYsfa6Q?t=811)
- Choose your tech stack for iteration speed and personal comfort, not prestige — WayUp's CTO chose Django/Python over the far more popular Rails because it let him ship fastest, and that choice never killed the company [tips-for-technical-startup-founders-startup-school @ 18:27](https://youtu.be/rP7bpYsfa6Q?t=1107)
- Only the tech choices tied directly to customer promises matter long-term; everything else can and often will be rewritten multiple times as you scale [tips-for-technical-startup-founders-startup-school @ 19:08](https://youtu.be/rP7bpYsfa6Q?t=1148)
- After launch, iterate toward product-market fit using both hard data (a simple analytics dashboard on core KPIs) and soft data (continued user conversations) to learn why users stay or churn [tips-for-technical-startup-founders-startup-school @ 19:48](https://youtu.be/rP7bpYsfa6Q?t=1188)
- Continuously launching in small increments, as Segment did with five launches in about a month while adding integrations users asked for, is a path to product-market fit [tips-for-technical-startup-founders-startup-school @ 21:34](https://youtu.be/rP7bpYsfa6Q?t=1294)
- Technical debt is fine before product-market fit — Pokemon Go launched so broken that nobody could log in, yet that didn't kill the company [tips-for-technical-startup-founders-startup-school @ 22:58](https://youtu.be/rP7bpYsfa6Q?t=1378)
- After product-market fit, the technical founder's role shifts toward scaling architecture and hiring, with time spent coding dropping from ~70% at 2-5 engineers to under 50% at 5-10 and further beyond 10 [tips-for-technical-startup-founders-startup-school @ 26:20](https://youtu.be/rP7bpYsfa6Q?t=1580)
