# AI and the Future of Law: The 10 Year Overnight Success Story — Compiled Truth

Jake Heller, co-founder of Casetext (sold for $650M), describes a decade-long grind through failed business models before GPT-4 access let his team build a product that finally hit true product-market fit.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Casetext started in 2013 as a crowdsourced case-law library (Wikipedia meets Reddit for law) before pivoting many times over ten years [ai-and-the-future-of-law-the-10-year-overnight-success-story @ 03:43](https://youtu.be/qFBUd0b8TjY?t=223)
- Early Enterprise sales to large law firms looked great at $50k-$150k per client, but the team wrongly assumed they could just scale by hiring more salespeople since not all firms were ready to adopt new technology [ai-and-the-future-of-law-the-10-year-overnight-success-story @ 05:08](https://youtu.be/qFBUd0b8TjY?t=308)
- After exhausting large-firm demand, they pivoted downmarket to small law firms and got an influx of thousands of customers until marketing channels stopped converting new customers [ai-and-the-future-of-law-the-10-year-overnight-success-story @ 05:58](https://youtu.be/qFBUd0b8TjY?t=358)
- Early access to GPT-4 before public release, after years of NLP experimentation since the BERT paper in 2016, was the breakthrough that produced real product-market fit [ai-and-the-future-of-law-the-10-year-overnight-success-story @ 06:32](https://youtu.be/qFBUd0b8TjY?t=392)
- True product-market fit felt like enterprise clients who used to take 9-18 months to decide now deciding in a month, with revenue set to triple in a year [ai-and-the-future-of-law-the-10-year-overnight-success-story @ 07:15](https://youtu.be/qFBUd0b8TjY?t=435)
- The winning pattern for LLM startups is a "golden demo" that compresses days of work into minutes and gets customers to sign immediately, such as flagging sarcastic fraud references in Enron emails [ai-and-the-future-of-law-the-10-year-overnight-success-story @ 08:54](https://youtu.be/qFBUd0b8TjY?t=534)
- Going from a raw LLM to a usable product required heavy engineering work around scale (millions of documents) and accuracy (avoiding hallucinations) [ai-and-the-future-of-law-the-10-year-overnight-success-story @ 11:26](https://youtu.be/qFBUd0b8TjY?t=686)
- Heller frames GPT-4 as a base "cloud-like" layer, with tooling/infrastructure companies (like LangChain) and application-layer companies each representing distinct startup opportunities [ai-and-the-future-of-law-the-10-year-overnight-success-story @ 14:40](https://youtu.be/qFBUd0b8TjY?t=880)
- Persisting through a decade of a "trough of sorrow" and repeated near-misses at product-market fit before the AI breakthrough finally hit is what let Casetext capitalize when the moment came [ai-and-the-future-of-law-the-10-year-overnight-success-story @ 04:27](https://youtu.be/qFBUd0b8TjY?t=267)
