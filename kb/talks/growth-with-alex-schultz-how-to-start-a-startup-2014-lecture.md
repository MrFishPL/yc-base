# Growth with Alex Schultz (How to Start a Startup 2014: Lecture 6) — Compiled Truth

Facebook's VP of Growth Alex Schultz argues that retention is the single most important driver of growth, that a whole company (not a siloed team) should own growth around a clear north-star metric, and that specific tactics like virality, SEO, and notifications only work once genuine retention and product-market fit exist.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Retention is the single most important thing for growth: plot percent monthly active against days since acquisition, and if the curve asymptotes to a flat line you have product-market fit, but if it slopes down to zero you don't have a viable business [growth-with-alex-schultz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014-lecture @ 02:40](https://youtu.be/vJqlG5ytLDs?t=160)
- If your retention curve doesn't flatten out, don't pursue growth tactics or hire a growth hacker — focus on reaching product-market fit first, since that is the number one problem Schultz has seen at both Facebook and the startups he's advised [growth-with-alex-schultz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014-lecture @ 05:48](https://youtu.be/vJqlG5ytLDs?t=348)
- Different verticals need different terminal retention rates for success — roughly 20-30% monthly retention is fine for e-commerce, but a social media product needs something like 80% retention among early users to become a massive site [growth-with-alex-schultz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014-lecture @ 09:14](https://youtu.be/vJqlG5ytLDs?t=554)
- Startups shouldn't have a separate growth team; the whole company should be the growth team with the CEO as the head of growth, and the company needs one clear "north star" metric (monthly active users for Facebook, sends for WhatsApp, nights booked for Airbnb, gross merchandise volume for eBay) [growth-with-alex-schultz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014-lecture @ 10:52](https://youtu.be/vJqlG5ytLDs?t=652)
- Changing eBay's affiliate payment model from paying for confirmed registered users to paying only for activated confirmed users (who bid, bought, or listed) dropped registered-user volume 20% but only cut activated users by 5%, and accelerated growth of the metric that actually mattered [growth-with-alex-schultz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014-lecture @ 14:42](https://youtu.be/vJqlG5ytLDs?t=882)
- Every product has a "magic moment" that hooks users — for Facebook it's seeing a friend's photo, which is why the team focused on getting users to 10 friends in 14 days rather than optimizing for signups alone [growth-with-alex-schultz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014-lecture @ 16:51](https://youtu.be/vJqlG5ytLDs?t=1011)
- When optimizing growth levers like notifications, focus on the marginal (least-engaged) user rather than your own experience or power users, since power users already return regardless [growth-with-alex-schultz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014-lecture @ 19:25](https://youtu.be/vJqlG5ytLDs?t=1165)
- Virality can be analyzed via payload, frequency, and conversion rate — Hotmail's footer link was low-payload but high-frequency and high-conversion, while Facebook actually grew mainly through word-of-mouth, not viral mechanics, since there was no native way to invite non-users [growth-with-alex-schultz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014-lecture @ 28:30](https://youtu.be/vJqlG5ytLDs?t=1710)
- For SEO, do keyword research based on real search volume and competition before optimizing — Schultz spent a year ranking his cocktail site for "cocktail making" (500 searches/month) when everyone actually searched "cocktail recipes," and adding an internal directory so Google could crawl Facebook's profile pages caused a 100x jump in SEO traffic [growth-with-alex-schultz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014-lecture @ 40:09](https://youtu.be/vJqlG5ytLDs?t=2409)
- For email, SMS, and push, deliverability comes first, newsletters are ineffective compared to personalized notifications, and internationalizing translation infrastructure early (Facebook's community translation platform got French translated in 12 hours) prevented being blocked out of growing non-English markets [growth-with-alex-schultz-how-to-start-a-startup-2014-lecture @ 26:11](https://youtu.be/vJqlG5ytLDs?t=1571)
