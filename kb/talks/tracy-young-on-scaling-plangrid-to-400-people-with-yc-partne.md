# Tracy Young on Scaling PlanGrid to 400+ People with YC Partner Kat Manalac — Compiled Truth

Tracy Young, co-founder and CEO of PlanGrid, recounts building a construction-industry product from a fun side project into a 450-person company acquired by Autodesk, emphasizing user obsession, doing unscalable onboarding, disciplined hiring/firing, and founder self-care.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- PlanGrid started as a fun project born from a first-generation iPad crashing when trying to open construction blueprints, with no initial expectation of building a real business [tracy-young-on-scaling-plangrid-to-400-people-with-yc-partne @ 02:19](https://youtu.be/QzUQiIMtHSE?t=139)
- The team deeply understood their users (superintendents, foremen, electricians, carpenters) who had never used software before, so they kept the product radically simple, cutting features from five buttons to two whenever possible [tracy-young-on-scaling-plangrid-to-400-people-with-yc-partne @ 07:47](https://youtu.be/QzUQiIMtHSE?t=467)
- Following YC's "do things that don't scale" advice, PlanGrid loaned out iPads for free (later charging just to cover hardware cost) and sat with customers to walk them through setup step by step [tracy-young-on-scaling-plangrid-to-400-people-with-yc-partne @ 09:11](https://youtu.be/QzUQiIMtHSE?t=551)
- PlanGrid deliberately went bottoms-up into the field rather than selling to enterprise buyers/CIOs first, unlike decades of prior construction software [tracy-young-on-scaling-plangrid-to-400-people-with-yc-partne @ 11:08](https://youtu.be/QzUQiIMtHSE?t=668)
- The most repeated advice Young heard from YC partners and speakers was not firing underperformers fast enough, since a bad leader's "blast radius" affects everyone around them [tracy-young-on-scaling-plangrid-to-400-people-with-yc-partne @ 16:42](https://youtu.be/QzUQiIMtHSE?t=1002)
- Young's advice on firing: timebox the decision, set explicit expectations and a deadline for improvement, and never let it be a surprise to the person [tracy-young-on-scaling-plangrid-to-400-people-with-yc-partne @ 18:22](https://youtu.be/QzUQiIMtHSE?t=1102)
- Early hiring (under ~50 people) prioritized high pain tolerance and generalists willing to do any task; past ~50-100 people, roles needed to specialize to avoid chaos [tracy-young-on-scaling-plangrid-to-400-people-with-yc-partne @ 19:47](https://youtu.be/QzUQiIMtHSE?t=1187)
- For later-stage leadership hires, Young looked for authenticity (sensing when someone is "bullshitting" in an interview) and a track record of having done the job successfully before, backed by reference checks [tracy-young-on-scaling-plangrid-to-400-people-with-yc-partne @ 21:11](https://youtu.be/QzUQiIMtHSE?t=1271)
- A key scaling lesson was learning to manage her own emotions under constant pressure and operating with insufficient resources, including through a co-founder's death during the company's journey [tracy-young-on-scaling-plangrid-to-400-people-with-yc-partne @ 38:21](https://youtu.be/QzUQiIMtHSE?t=2301)
- Young maintained herself through scaling via weekly yoga, meditation, a plant-based diet, and working with a coach who told her she carried a lot of "negative ego" that made her overly self-critical [tracy-young-on-scaling-plangrid-to-400-people-with-yc-partne @ 44:09](https://youtu.be/QzUQiIMtHSE?t=2649)
- Founders should prioritize honestly rather than filling their days with meetings that feel productive but don't actually move the company forward, and should avoid lying to themselves about what's working [tracy-young-on-scaling-plangrid-to-400-people-with-yc-partne @ 42:47](https://youtu.be/QzUQiIMtHSE?t=2567)
