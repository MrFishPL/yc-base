# Leah Culver of Breaker and Tom Sparks of YC Answer Your Questions About Security and Podcasting — Compiled Truth

Leah Culver (Breaker) and Tom Sparks (YC) field crowdsourced questions on security best practices, authentication, crypto, and Breaker's approach to building a social podcasting product.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Two-factor authentication is strongest when it combines genuinely different factor types — something you know, something you have, and something you are (biometric) — because each can be replaced except biometrics [leah-culver-of-breaker-and-tom-sparks-of-yc-answer-your-ques @ 09:44](https://youtu.be/im2vOo3dEwg?t=584).
- Startups should not reinvent security primitives themselves; outsourcing auth to established providers like OAuth or Facebook Login avoids the "one small mistake" failure mode that causes most big hacks [leah-culver-of-breaker-and-tom-sparks-of-yc-answer-your-ques @ 15:16](https://youtu.be/im2vOo3dEwg?t=916).
- Avoid SMS or phone-call based two-factor authentication because phone carriers' support staff are an easy social-engineering target, especially exploited in crypto-related phone-number porting attacks [leah-culver-of-breaker-and-tom-sparks-of-yc-answer-your-ques @ 20:32](https://youtu.be/im2vOo3dEwg?t=1232).
- The biggest early-startup money mistake is over-spending on office space, parties, and "playing startup" rather than sticking to frugal YC-style advice like working out of your house [leah-culver-of-breaker-and-tom-sparks-of-yc-answer-your-ques @ 29:40](https://youtu.be/im2vOo3dEwg?t=1780).
- Culver's own founder mistake was trying to build everything herself instead of asking for help; Breaker's current approach is instead deeply user-feedback driven, building what users actually ask for [leah-culver-of-breaker-and-tom-sparks-of-yc-answer-your-ques @ 31:30](https://youtu.be/im2vOo3dEwg?t=1890).
- Breaker collects user feedback through in-app bug reporting, Mixpanel-based implicit behavioral analysis (retention, funnels), and targeted A/B testing on search and discovery, treating what users actually do as more valuable than what they say [leah-culver-of-breaker-and-tom-sparks-of-yc-answer-your-ques @ 34:11](https://youtu.be/im2vOo3dEwg?t=2051).
- Breaker's product bet is on episode discovery rather than show discovery, surfacing whatever episode is currently "hot" based on real user likes/listens/comments rather than editorial curation like Apple's [leah-culver-of-breaker-and-tom-sparks-of-yc-answer-your-ques @ 37:10](https://youtu.be/im2vOo3dEwg?t=2230).
- Distribution is one of the hardest problems in podcasting because content is cheap to produce but hard to get discovered, which is part of why Breaker started in an underserved, growing market [leah-culver-of-breaker-and-tom-sparks-of-yc-answer-your-ques @ 36:33](https://youtu.be/im2vOo3dEwg?t=2193).
- Secret/credential management for growing engineering teams remains an unsolved, painful problem — as soon as a team has more than a couple people, sharing and revoking access to private data becomes messy [leah-culver-of-breaker-and-tom-sparks-of-yc-answer-your-ques @ 45:22](https://youtu.be/im2vOo3dEwg?t=2722).
