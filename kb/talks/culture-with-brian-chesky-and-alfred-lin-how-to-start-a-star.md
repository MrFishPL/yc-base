# Culture with Brian Chesky and Alfred Lin (How to Start a Startup 2014: Lecture 10) — Compiled Truth

Alfred Lin (Zappos/Sequoia) frames culture as "core values lived every day in pursuit of the mission," and Brian Chesky (Airbnb) shows how Airbnb designed its culture intentionally from the earliest days and used it to make hard strategic calls.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Alfred Lin defines company culture as the core values and real actions of each team member in pursuit of the mission, and cites data that Fortune's "100 Best Companies to Work For" had stock returns of 11.8%, roughly double the S&P 500 and Russell 3000 over the same period [culture-with-brian-chesky-and-alfred-lin-how-to-start-a-star @ 03:46](https://youtu.be/px5rgcNjOTc?t=226)
- To derive core values, found them on what the founders personally value, what they like and dislike in people they've worked with, and require that each value be credible and uniquely tied to the company's mission (Zappos's "Deliver WOW Through Service" and "Be Humble") [culture-with-brian-chesky-and-alfred-lin-how-to-start-a-star @ 04:34](https://youtu.be/px5rgcNjOTc?t=274)
- Companies should interview for culture/mission fit separately from technical competence — Airbnb used dedicated "core values interviewers" from outside a candidate's function specifically so functional skill wouldn't bias the values assessment [culture-with-brian-chesky-and-alfred-lin-how-to-start-a-star @ 09:44](https://youtu.be/px5rgcNjOTc?t=584)
- Airbnb wrote down its values before hiring any employees, spending roughly six to seven months developing them during and after YC, because Chesky believed the first engineer hired would set the "DNA" replicated across a thousand future hires [culture-with-brian-chesky-and-alfred-lin-how-to-start-a-star @ 17:22](https://youtu.be/px5rgcNjOTc?t=1042)
- Airbnb's mission, "belong anywhere," emerged from a host's story about guests calling to check on him during a riot before his own mother did — Chesky argues this sense of belonging, not "booking a room," is what endures even if the product changes [culture-with-brian-chesky-and-alfred-lin-how-to-start-a-star @ 19:37](https://youtu.be/px5rgcNjOTc?t=1177)
- In 2011, facing an aggressive Samwer-brothers clone (Airbnb 40 employees vs. their 400, after raising $90M to Airbnb's $7M), Chesky chose not to acquire them because he saw them as "mercenaries" rather than "missionaries," betting that mission-driven culture would outlast money-driven cloning [culture-with-brian-chesky-and-alfred-lin-how-to-start-a-star @ 30:26](https://youtu.be/px5rgcNjOTc?t=1826)
- Chesky argues culture and brand are two sides of the same coin: culture is the internal principles employees align around, brand is the external promise, and employees who are deeply passionate create the customers who become passionate — Airbnb dropped its early "cheap alternative to hotels" tagline because it undercut this idea [culture-with-brian-chesky-and-alfred-lin-how-to-start-a-star @ 32:19](https://youtu.be/px5rgcNjOTc?t=1939)
- Paraphrasing Paul Graham, Chesky says it's better to have 100 people who love you than a million who mildly like you, because passionate early users evangelize on your behalf — this justified spending unscalable time living with and door-knocking early Airbnb hosts [culture-with-brian-chesky-and-alfred-lin-how-to-start-a-star @ 41:01](https://youtu.be/px5rgcNjOTc?t=2461)
- Airbnb's professional-photography program started as a fully manual, by-hand service (Chesky personally photographing a host's home, then managing photographers via spreadsheets) built only after the team understood exactly what the "perfect" service should feel like for one person before scaling it [culture-with-brian-chesky-and-alfred-lin-how-to-start-a-star @ 43:32](https://youtu.be/px5rgcNjOTc?t=2612)
- Chesky rejects the framing of Airbnb as a marketing company, arguing it must be world-class simultaneously at technology, international expansion, payments licensing, trust and safety, and regulation across 34,000 cities with different laws [culture-with-brian-chesky-and-alfred-lin-how-to-start-a-star @ 47:12](https://youtu.be/px5rgcNjOTc?t=2832)
