# Before the Startup with Paul Graham (How to Start a Startup 2014: Lecture 3) — Compiled Truth

Paul Graham lays out six counterintuitive truths about startups aimed at students, arguing that starting a startup is not about mastering startup mechanics but about understanding your users and following genuine curiosity.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Startups are so counterintuitive that founders will predictably ignore YC partners' warnings and only realize the advice was right a year later, after it's too late to change the cap table [before-the-startup-with-paul-graham-how-to-start-a-startup-2 @ 02:20](https://youtu.be/f4_14pZlJBs?t=140)
- Trust your instincts about people even in business — if someone seems impressive but gives you misgivings, don't override that intuition just because it "seems normal for business" [before-the-startup-with-paul-graham-how-to-start-a-startup-2 @ 04:13](https://youtu.be/f4_14pZlJBs?t=253)
- What you need to succeed in a startup is not expertise in startups but expertise in your own users — Mark Zuckerberg succeeded despite being a "complete noob" at startup mechanics [before-the-startup-with-paul-graham-how-to-start-a-startup-2 @ 05:34](https://youtu.be/f4_14pZlJBs?t=334)
- Young founders often "play house" — raising money, renting a nice office, hiring friends — while neglecting the one essential thing, which is making something people want [before-the-startup-with-paul-graham-how-to-start-a-startup-2 @ 07:04](https://youtu.be/f4_14pZlJBs?t=424)
- Gaming the system, which works in school and at big companies, stops working in a startup because there is no boss to trick — only users, who are like sharks that can't be fooled by a red flag [before-the-startup-with-paul-graham-how-to-start-a-startup-2 @ 11:30](https://youtu.be/f4_14pZlJBs?t=690)
- Starting a startup takes over your life for years — even a hugely successful founder like Larry Page has effectively been "running as fast as he could" since age 25 with no real vacation [before-the-startup-with-paul-graham-how-to-start-a-startup-2 @ 14:25](https://youtu.be/f4_14pZlJBs?t=865)
- Paul Graham's direct advice to his own kids: do not start a startup in college, because there are things (aimless exploration, cheap travel) you can only do well in your early 20s and never again after a startup succeeds [before-the-startup-with-paul-graham-how-to-start-a-startup-2 @ 22:53](https://youtu.be/f4_14pZlJBs?t=1373)
- The way to get good startup ideas is not to consciously try to think of them, since deliberate brainstorming produces plausible-sounding but bad ideas; the best ideas (Yahoo, Google, Facebook, Apple) started as side projects [before-the-startup-with-paul-graham-how-to-start-a-startup-2 @ 24:36](https://youtu.be/f4_14pZlJBs?t=1476)
- To unconsciously generate startup ideas: learn a lot about things that matter, work on problems that interest you, and do it with people you like and respect — which is also how you find co-founders [before-the-startup-with-paul-graham-how-to-start-a-startup-2 @ 25:38](https://youtu.be/f4_14pZlJBs?t=1538)
- Women founders empirically have a harder time raising money, and the best fix is the same as always — make the startup do so well (via a stellar growth graph) that VCs fall in love with the numbers first [before-the-startup-with-paul-graham-how-to-start-a-startup-2 @ 39:15](https://youtu.be/f4_14pZlJBs?t=2355)
