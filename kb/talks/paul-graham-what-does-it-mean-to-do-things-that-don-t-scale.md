# Paul Graham: What does it mean to do things that don't scale? — Compiled Truth

Paul Graham explains that "doing things that don't scale" means handling early customers in a handmade, manual, unscalable way on purpose, because the learning is worth it even though the approach can't last forever.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Doing things that don't scale means doing things in a handmade, artisanal, painstaking way that you know you can't keep doing forever if the company gets big [paul-graham-what-does-it-mean-to-do-things-that-don-t-scale @ 00:05](https://youtu.be/5-TgqZ8nado?t=5)
- Founders should do these unscalable things early on anyway, because they have nothing to lose and learn a great deal from doing them [paul-graham-what-does-it-mean-to-do-things-that-don-t-scale @ 00:27](https://youtu.be/5-TgqZ8nado?t=27)
- Graham says he discovered many of the lessons YC now teaches startups by living them himself, only later realizing they were common startup lessons [paul-graham-what-does-it-mean-to-do-things-that-don-t-scale @ 00:27](https://youtu.be/5-TgqZ8nado?t=27)
- Doing very manual, hands-on work for your early customers is so important that it's worth doing a ton of manual stuff just to get those first customers [paul-graham-what-does-it-mean-to-do-things-that-don-t-scale @ 00:49](https://youtu.be/5-TgqZ8nado?t=49)
