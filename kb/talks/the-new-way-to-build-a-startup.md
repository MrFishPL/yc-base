# The New Way To Build A Startup — Compiled Truth

This YC talk argues that the best current startups are "20X companies" that automate every internal function, not just one or two, using their resulting leanness to beat much larger incumbents and delay hiring.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Anthropic's own engineers reportedly each manage three to eight Claude instances implementing features, fixing bugs, and researching solutions, while humans meet only to discuss foundational architecture and product decisions [the-new-way-to-build-a-startup @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/rWUWfj_PqmM?t=0)
- The best teams right now aren't automating one or two internal functions but all of them, and this internal-automation leanness is becoming their core competitive superpower ("20X companies") [the-new-way-to-build-a-startup @ 00:47](https://youtu.be/rWUWfj_PqmM?t=47)
- The term "20X company" was coined by GigaML, whose four-to-five engineer team beat competitors with roughly 100x the headcount to close DoorDash as a customer through a better product and better numbers [the-new-way-to-build-a-startup @ 02:29](https://youtu.be/rWUWfj_PqmM?t=149)
- GigaML's internal agent Atlas can use browsers, edit policies, and write code, roughly doubling or tripling each engineer's scope by removing customer-integration boilerplate, and lets a single human FTE service accounts for multiple Fortune 500 clients doing 500,000+ calls a day [the-new-way-to-build-a-startup @ 03:34](https://youtu.be/rWUWfj_PqmM?t=214)
- Legion Health built a single custom internal interface giving its care operations team instant access to patient history, scheduling, insurance codes and more, letting it 4x patient volume in a year without hiring a single net-new person [the-new-way-to-build-a-startup @ 04:39](https://youtu.be/rWUWfj_PqmM?t=279)
- Where a typical healthcare company would need whole departments and call centers for clinical, patient support, and billing work, Legion runs each of those functions with just one person because of its unified source-of-truth interface [the-new-way-to-build-a-startup @ 06:07](https://youtu.be/rWUWfj_PqmM?t=367)
- Feathr, a 12-person accounts-receivable automation startup competing against firms established since 2006 with hundreds of employees, builds custom AI agents for each employee by first asking them to document their manual daily tasks [the-new-way-to-build-a-startup @ 06:25](https://youtu.be/rWUWfj_PqmM?t=385)
- This relentless automation culture let Feathr avoid hiring a dedicated design function entirely, instead having its engineering team use AI design tooling for all front-end design [the-new-way-to-build-a-startup @ 07:03](https://youtu.be/rWUWfj_PqmM?t=423)
- These three approaches — AI teammates, a unified source-of-truth interface, and per-employee custom agents — aren't mutually exclusive, and the companies combining them are staying lean while posting record-high growth rates [the-new-way-to-build-a-startup @ 07:23](https://youtu.be/rWUWfj_PqmM?t=443)
