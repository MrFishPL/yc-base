# Danae Ringelmann at Startup School SV 2014 — Compiled Truth

Indiegogo co-founder Danae Ringelmann argues that a founder's authentic "why," combined with a deliberately-built culture, is what actually determines whether a startup survives its hardest years and builds something meaningful.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Knowing and being clear about your "why" — the real reason you're starting the company — matters because if that reason isn't authentic to your core, your chances of failing go way up [danae-ringelmann-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 07:41](https://youtu.be/QPbHf4i6CrQ?t=461)
- Being clear and vocal about her why — democratizing access to capital — is specifically what helped Ringelmann find her co-founders in business school [danae-ringelmann-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 08:21](https://youtu.be/QPbHf4i6CrQ?t=501)
- Her original idea was an offline fund with a democratic twist; it only became an online crowdfunding platform after a co-founder asked why she wasn't just using the internet [danae-ringelmann-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 09:08](https://youtu.be/QPbHf4i6CrQ?t=548)
