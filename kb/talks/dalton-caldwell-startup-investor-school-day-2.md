# Dalton Caldwell - Startup Investor School Day 2 — Compiled Truth

Dalton Caldwell walks angel investors through building a repeatable process for founder meetings and investment decisions, then runs a live example meeting to demonstrate probing for understanding and conviction.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Every investor needs an explicit process — a funnel from leads to intros to qualification to decision to close — because a wishy-washy approach leads to suboptimal decisions [dalton-caldwell-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 06:31](https://youtu.be/uyTOFU0Pn6E?t=391)
- Decide your investment budget and pass rate ahead of time (e.g. $500k/year, picking 5 out of a favorite 20) rather than negotiating against yourself meeting by meeting [dalton-caldwell-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 08:40](https://youtu.be/uyTOFU0Pn6E?t=520)
- Groupthink investing — chasing whatever criteria everyone else is talking about — produces non-optimal returns and over-funded companies; the best investments are often contrarian or deeply unappealing to everyone else [dalton-caldwell-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 15:32](https://youtu.be/uyTOFU0Pn6E?t=932)
- If you're not a clear "yes," you are a "no" — flip-flopping, endless meetings without a decision, and "I'm in once you find the rest of the round" are all anti-patterns that hurt founders more than a direct no [dalton-caldwell-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 11:20](https://youtu.be/uyTOFU0Pn6E?t=680)
- Personal conviction is essential to angel investing since there's no undo button on a check; investors should be comfortable looking "stupid" for backing unconventional ideas [dalton-caldwell-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 17:12](https://youtu.be/uyTOFU0Pn6E?t=1032)
- Start every founder meeting with first-principles questions (what does the company do, who's on the team, what progress have you made) to hear the founder's own story rather than relitigating the deck [dalton-caldwell-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 23:05](https://youtu.be/uyTOFU0Pn6E?t=1385)
- A good signal is whether conviction rises or falls the more you talk to a founder — getting more excited over time is a good sign, growing lukewarm is a red flag [dalton-caldwell-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 24:25](https://youtu.be/uyTOFU0Pn6E?t=1465)
- Bad investor behavior includes staring at your phone during meetings, making the meeting about yourself, overloading founders with unsolicited product/fundraising advice, and doing "value-add" intros nobody asked for [dalton-caldwell-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 26:36](https://youtu.be/uyTOFU0Pn6E?t=1596)
- When declining, tell founders directly rather than ghosting them, and frame it honestly (e.g. "not convinced given the current strategy") instead of leaving them in false-hope limbo [dalton-caldwell-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 29:16](https://youtu.be/uyTOFU0Pn6E?t=1756)
- In a live example meeting, he demonstrates zooming out repeatedly to confirm basic comprehension of a complex ISP-in-a-box business before evaluating why the idea could work [dalton-caldwell-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 38:17](https://youtu.be/uyTOFU0Pn6E?t=2297)
- Do not use proprietary information from your network to inform founders about competitive threats they don't know about — keep it to yourself and use it only to guide your own decision [dalton-caldwell-startup-investor-school-day-2 @ 52:36](https://youtu.be/uyTOFU0Pn6E?t=3156)
