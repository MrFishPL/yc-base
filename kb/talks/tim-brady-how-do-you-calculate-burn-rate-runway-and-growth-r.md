# Tim Brady - How do you calculate burn rate, runway and growth rate? — Compiled Truth

Tim Brady explains three metrics every investor will ask about—burn rate, runway, and growth rate—and how to calculate each correctly, including the common mistake of not compounding growth.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Burn rate, runway, and growth rate are critical metrics for managing an early-stage startup that every investor will ask about [tim-brady-how-do-you-calculate-burn-rate-runway-and-growth-r @ 00:07](https://youtu.be/aDM8CNnCOwk?t=7)
- Burn rate is calculated from cash going out minus cash coming in during a month; it measures cash flow, not profit and loss [tim-brady-how-do-you-calculate-burn-rate-runway-and-growth-r @ 00:29](https://youtu.be/aDM8CNnCOwk?t=29)
- Runway is cash on hand divided by burn rate, e.g. $200 in the bank with a $10 burn rate gives 20 months of runway [tim-brady-how-do-you-calculate-burn-rate-runway-and-growth-r @ 01:16](https://youtu.be/aDM8CNnCOwk?t=76)
- When cash flows are fluctuating, founders need a monthly financial forecast to estimate runway, and should review these metrics weekly [tim-brady-how-do-you-calculate-burn-rate-runway-and-growth-r @ 01:58](https://youtu.be/aDM8CNnCOwk?t=118)
- Growth rate measures how fast sales are growing and is a good indicator of whether you've built something people want [tim-brady-how-do-you-calculate-burn-rate-runway-and-growth-r @ 02:17](https://youtu.be/aDM8CNnCOwk?t=137)
- Growth rate should be expressed as a compounded number (CMGR, compounded monthly growth rate), not a simple average over the period [tim-brady-how-do-you-calculate-burn-rate-runway-and-growth-r @ 03:00](https://youtu.be/aDM8CNnCOwk?t=180)
- A common founder mistake is claiming revenue that grew 6x over six months means a 100% growth rate, when the correct compounded monthly rate is closer to 35% [tim-brady-how-do-you-calculate-burn-rate-runway-and-growth-r @ 03:46](https://youtu.be/aDM8CNnCOwk?t=226)
- Seasonal businesses can use quarterly or annual growth rates instead of monthly, as long as it's made clear to investors how it's calculated [tim-brady-how-do-you-calculate-burn-rate-runway-and-growth-r @ 04:09](https://youtu.be/aDM8CNnCOwk?t=249)
- Recurring revenue (like monthly subscriptions, tracked as MRR) is valued more highly by investors than non-recurring, one-off revenue like e-commerce sales [tim-brady-how-do-you-calculate-burn-rate-runway-and-growth-r @ 04:59](https://youtu.be/aDM8CNnCOwk?t=299)
- Misrepresenting non-recurring revenue as recurring, or overstating growth, will make investors think you're unsophisticated or dishonest and hurt your chances of raising [tim-brady-how-do-you-calculate-burn-rate-runway-and-growth-r @ 05:21](https://youtu.be/aDM8CNnCOwk?t=321)
