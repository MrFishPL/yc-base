# Y Combinator Is Back In Person — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell announce the Summer 2022 batch returning in person and discuss batch trends, the new YC standard deal, and how founders should respond soberly to a shifting economic climate.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The Summer 2022 batch will be in person for the first time since Winter 2020, including an in-person retreat in Sonoma and weekly meetups, while remaining friendly to remote founders [y-combinator-is-back-in-person @ 00:28](https://youtu.be/GiuxXzmvolU?t=28)
- Some of the best companies YC has funded (Stripe, Airbnb, Dropbox) were founded during downturns or blips, and founders who apply during less "hype" periods are often more genuinely motivated [y-combinator-is-back-in-person @ 03:14](https://youtu.be/GiuxXzmvolU?t=194)
- Strategic founders see a downturn as a time to go on offense, since hiring and customer access are harder for competitors during hype cycles and easier when the market cools [y-combinator-is-back-in-person @ 04:10](https://youtu.be/GiuxXzmvolU?t=250)
- After YC sent founders a letter about the economic downturn, the healthiest founder response was a sober, non-panicked check of their own situation (e.g., "am I default alive," runway) rather than blanket fear [y-combinator-is-back-in-person @ 08:36](https://youtu.be/GiuxXzmvolU?t=516)
- The new YC standard deal was designed so companies are fully funded immediately upon acceptance, driven by a sense that funding conditions looked unsustainably "toppy" [y-combinator-is-back-in-person @ 11:26](https://youtu.be/GiuxXzmvolU?t=686)
- Early YC-era companies like Airbnb raised well under a million dollars in seed funding while diluting 20-25%, showing large budgets aren't required to build hugely successful companies [y-combinator-is-back-in-person @ 12:15](https://youtu.be/GiuxXzmvolU?t=735)
- With the new standard deal, founders in the last batch raised seed rounds at roughly 10% dilution or less, compared to a historical 15-30%, preserving much more ownership [y-combinator-is-back-in-person @ 14:09](https://youtu.be/GiuxXzmvolU?t=849)
- Coming in with $500k already funded made founders more confident and less emotionally manipulable during fundraising negotiations, similar to negotiating any major financial purchase without fear or FOMO [y-combinator-is-back-in-person @ 14:51](https://youtu.be/GiuxXzmvolU?t=891)
- Investors sometimes simultaneously tell founders their plan won't work while pushing them to raise more from that same investor, and sober, non-emotional founders are better able to push back [y-combinator-is-back-in-person @ 15:47](https://youtu.be/GiuxXzmvolU?t=947)
