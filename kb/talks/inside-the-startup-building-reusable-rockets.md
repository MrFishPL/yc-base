# Inside The Startup Building Reusable Rockets — Compiled Truth

A founder-story documentary on Stoke Space, whose two co-founders left comfortable jobs at Blue Origin in 2019 to build a fully and rapidly reusable rocket, starting with a test engine in a backyard shipping container.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Stoke Space is building fully reusable rockets, including a stage-2 capsule that can survive re-entry, something no other rocket company had solved for the upper stage [inside-the-startup-building-reusable-rockets @ 00:01](https://youtu.be/2hgjgycOU_0?t=1)
- The founders' key insight was that focusing on reusability from day one would decrease cost and increase availability and reliability of access to space [inside-the-startup-building-reusable-rockets @ 05:48](https://youtu.be/2hgjgycOU_0?t=348)
- Leaving stable, well-paying jobs with a 3-month-old at home felt "wildly irresponsible," and the founders set themselves a 6-month timebox to find traction before re-evaluating [inside-the-startup-building-reusable-rockets @ 06:07](https://youtu.be/2hgjgycOU_0?t=367)
- With no rolodex or "rich uncles," they had to raise money organically, right as COVID hit and the fundraising market shut down [inside-the-startup-building-reusable-rockets @ 08:51](https://youtu.be/2hgjgycOU_0?t=531)
- The most important fundraising advice they carry with them is to "get good at hearing no" and keep the conviction alive through repeated rejection [inside-the-startup-building-reusable-rockets @ 09:52](https://youtu.be/2hgjgycOU_0?t=592)
- YC's network helped them learn the language of fundraising and added momentum to their round, despite most venture capital at the time being geared toward SaaS rather than hardware [inside-the-startup-building-reusable-rockets @ 09:14](https://youtu.be/2hgjgycOU_0?t=554)
- They deliberately built in-house manufacturing so they could make every rocket part themselves, shrinking a test-fail-fix iteration cycle from a month down to a day or two [inside-the-startup-building-reusable-rockets @ 11:56](https://youtu.be/2hgjgycOU_0?t=716)
- They built their own internal software tool ("Bolt Line") early on to run operations, betting on software as core infrastructure for scaling from a garage to FAA-overseen orbital flights [inside-the-startup-building-reusable-rockets @ 13:45](https://youtu.be/2hgjgycOU_0?t=825)
- The company has raised about $990 million to date but says it operates efficiently relative to other hardware companies in its industry [inside-the-startup-building-reusable-rockets @ 10:11](https://youtu.be/2hgjgycOU_0?t=611)
- The founders describe reaching a level of conviction where they felt they "owed it to the world to try this idea," regardless of whether it would ultimately succeed [inside-the-startup-building-reusable-rockets @ 15:07](https://youtu.be/2hgjgycOU_0?t=907)
