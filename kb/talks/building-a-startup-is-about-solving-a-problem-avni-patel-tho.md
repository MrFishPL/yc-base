# Building a Startup is About Solving a Problem - Avni Patel Thompson of Poppy — Compiled Truth

Avni Patel Thompson recounts how her first startup (a curated subscription box) failed from building a product nobody needed, and how she applied those lessons to bootstrap Poppy with SMS and spreadsheets before getting into YC.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Her first startup built a beautiful curated subscription box based on what the founders wanted, without validating it against real customer need, and orders trickled off after initial friends-and-family excitement [building-a-startup-is-about-solving-a-problem-avni-patel-tho @ 04:45](https://youtu.be/0W5Jaip5_-g?t=285)
- After that startup shut down, reading Paul Graham essays taught her to focus on talking to users and finding "the hundred people that really loved what you were doing" rather than chasing everyone [building-a-startup-is-about-solving-a-problem-avni-patel-tho @ 07:07](https://youtu.be/0W5Jaip5_-g?t=427)
- She validated Poppy's idea by talking to parents about their real pain points, which kept surfacing child care rather than her original box concept [building-a-startup-is-about-solving-a-problem-avni-patel-tho @ 07:46](https://youtu.be/0W5Jaip5_-g?t=466)
- With no programmer and about $200 left, she built an MVP using SMS plus free tools (Squarespace, Typeform, Stripe, Google Calendar, Excel) to manually match sitters and parents [building-a-startup-is-about-solving-a-problem-avni-patel-tho @ 11:06](https://youtu.be/0W5Jaip5_-g?t=666)
- She set a simple weekly growth goal of 10-20% and consistently beat it, which felt entirely different from the struggle of her first startup [building-a-startup-is-about-solving-a-problem-avni-patel-tho @ 12:13](https://youtu.be/0W5Jaip5_-g?t=733)
- After being rejected by YC's first interview (told to reapply with more growth and a team), she kept operating as if she'd gotten in, and doubled down on 10-20% weekly growth [building-a-startup-is-about-solving-a-problem-avni-patel-tho @ 14:19](https://youtu.be/0W5Jaip5_-g?t=859)
- She found her technical co-founder Richard through a "friend of a friend of a friend," starting with a low-commitment six-week trial project before formalizing the partnership [building-a-startup-is-about-solving-a-problem-avni-patel-tho @ 17:27](https://youtu.be/0W5Jaip5_-g?t=1047)
- She reframes fundraising: money isn't something to celebrate for its own sake, it just buys time to run experiments toward profitability or the next milestone [building-a-startup-is-about-solving-a-problem-avni-patel-tho @ 21:12](https://youtu.be/0W5Jaip5_-g?t=1272)
- Her core lesson is that building a startup is about solving a problem, not building a product — the product can be far from pretty as long as it solves the problem [building-a-startup-is-about-solving-a-problem-avni-patel-tho @ 24:20](https://youtu.be/0W5Jaip5_-g?t=1460)
- She advises founders to pick one key growth metric and focus on growing it consistently week over week rather than obsessing over product launches [building-a-startup-is-about-solving-a-problem-avni-patel-tho @ 25:25](https://youtu.be/0W5Jaip5_-g?t=1525)
