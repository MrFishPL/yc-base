# Building Dota Bots That Beat Pros - OpenAI's Greg Brockman, Szymon Sidor, and Sam Altman — Compiled Truth

Greg Brockman and Szymon Sidor of OpenAI, interviewed by Sam Altman, describe how a ~10-person team built a Dota 2 bot that beat professional players, emphasizing that the work was overwhelmingly engineering rather than novel machine learning research.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The team chose Dota 2 as a research testbed largely for pragmatic engineering reasons — it runs on Linux and has a scriptable API, unlike the more popular League of Legends [building-dota-bots-that-beat-pros-openai-s-greg-brockman-szy @ 08:20](https://youtu.be/UdIPveR__jw?t=500)
- The vast majority of day-to-day project work was engineering, not machine learning science — good bets on the ML side were more about what not to do than what to do [building-dota-bots-that-beat-pros-openai-s-greg-brockman-szy @ 07:01](https://youtu.be/UdIPveR__jw?t=421)
- A reinforcement-learning bot with no hand-coded assumptions beat the team's best hand-scripted bot (built over three months) after only about one to two weeks of engineering effort [building-dota-bots-that-beat-pros-openai-s-greg-brockman-szy @ 19:11](https://youtu.be/UdIPveR__jw?t=1151)
- Management shifted from outcome-based weekly milestones ("beat this person by this date") to input-based ones ("what will we try by next week"), since research progress on ideas is inherently unpredictable [building-dota-bots-that-beat-pros-openai-s-greg-brockman-szy @ 21:55](https://youtu.be/UdIPveR__jw?t=1315)
- The team tracked a single true-skill rating metric on a wall, producing a smooth, near-linear improvement curve week over week as ideas were tried [building-dota-bots-that-beat-pros-openai-s-greg-brockman-szy @ 20:35](https://youtu.be/UdIPveR__jw?t=1235)
- Just before The International, a professional player exposed a bug where the bot had never learned to handle an item build ("early wand") it had never seen, showing the bot could only learn what its training environment exposed it to [building-dota-bots-that-beat-pros-openai-s-greg-brockman-szy @ 27:10](https://youtu.be/UdIPveR__jw?t=1630)
- Under a hard competition deadline, the team performed live "surgery" on a running training experiment overnight, carefully reviewing every commit since a bug at that stage could not be fixed in time [building-dota-bots-that-beat-pros-openai-s-greg-brockman-szy @ 36:38](https://youtu.be/UdIPveR__jw?t=2198)
- Writing bug-free code was treated as unusually critical because subtle bugs manifest only as slightly worse training performance that can take a full day-long run to even detect [building-dota-bots-that-beat-pros-openai-s-greg-brockman-szy @ 51:12](https://youtu.be/UdIPveR__jw?t=3072)
- New engineers without any AI background can become productive at OpenAI from day one, since much of the highest-leverage work is infrastructure and engineering rather than research [building-dota-bots-that-beat-pros-openai-s-greg-brockman-szy @ 05:50](https://youtu.be/UdIPveR__jw?t=350)
- Non-technical people can still contribute to AI progress meaningfully by educating themselves and engaging with the ethical conversations shaping how the technology gets deployed [building-dota-bots-that-beat-pros-openai-s-greg-brockman-szy @ 55:13](https://youtu.be/UdIPveR__jw?t=3313)
