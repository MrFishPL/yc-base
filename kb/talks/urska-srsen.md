# Urska Srsen — Compiled Truth

Bellabeat's co-founder describes pivoting from a professional remote patient monitoring system to a consumer pregnancy-tracking wearable, and how resource scarcity as a European founder built the resilience that carried the company to YC and a fast seed round.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Bellabeat originally built a technically sound professional remote patient monitoring system for care providers, but nobody adopted it because it was too complex and lacked emotional engagement for patients [urska-srsen @ 08:08](https://youtu.be/QWB8zO9yl24?t=488)
- The team scaled the product down and rebuilt it from the bottom up as a consumer wearable that introduces patients to self-tracking through an easy, emotional experience shared with loved ones [urska-srsen @ 09:05](https://youtu.be/QWB8zO9yl24?t=545)
- Srsen had no tech background — she dropped out of art school, had never owned a computer, and had to learn Photoshop and UI design from scratch to build the product [urska-srsen @ 07:44](https://youtu.be/QWB8zO9yl24?t=464)
- The team came to believe wearables are less an end product and more a stepping stone paving the way for digitized professional healthcare, once devices become reliable and seamlessly integrated into daily life [urska-srsen @ 10:29](https://youtu.be/QWB8zO9yl24?t=629)
- As a European founder, market fragmentation, weak connections, and lack of resources made it especially hard to build and fund a tech company [urska-srsen @ 12:22](https://youtu.be/QWB8zO9yl24?t=742)
- Winning the Pioneers Festival competition led to meeting Michael Seibel, who encouraged them to apply to YC — a program Srsen had never even heard of at the time [urska-srsen @ 14:00](https://youtu.be/QWB8zO9yl24?t=840)
- The team lived and worked together nonstop for five months before YC, following the advice that any problem is best overcome by working more [urska-srsen @ 18:18](https://youtu.be/QWB8zO9yl24?t=1098)
- By YC demo day they had sold over 8,000 devices, which led to closing a seed round within two weeks with very few rejections [urska-srsen @ 19:11](https://youtu.be/QWB8zO9yl24?t=1151)
- Looking back, the team realized they actually did face plenty of rejection early on, but operating in a resource-scarce region had made them numb to it [urska-srsen @ 20:05](https://youtu.be/QWB8zO9yl24?t=1205)
- Building a working prototype for less than $5,000 out of necessity made the founders stronger and more resourceful [urska-srsen @ 20:29](https://youtu.be/QWB8zO9yl24?t=1229)
