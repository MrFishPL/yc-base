# Eric Migicovsky - How to Talk to Users — Compiled Truth

Eric Migicovsky gives tactical, stage-by-stage advice for how founders should talk to users, drawing on "The Mom Test" and his own experience building Pebble to argue that direct founder-user contact is non-negotiable at every stage of a company.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The best founders maintain a direct connection to users throughout the company's life and cannot outsource this to salespeople or product heads [eric-migicovsky-how-to-talk-to-users @ 00:22](https://youtu.be/MT4Ig2uqjTc?t=22)
- Per "The Mom Test," the three common mistakes in user interviews are pitching your idea, asking about hypotheticals, and talking too much instead of listening [eric-migicovsky-how-to-talk-to-users @ 03:14](https://youtu.be/MT4Ig2uqjTc?t=194)
- Five great early customer-interview questions include "what is the hardest part about doing X," "tell me about the last time you encountered this problem," and "why was this hard," illustrated through the Dropbox founding story [eric-migicovsky-how-to-talk-to-users @ 05:49](https://youtu.be/MT4Ig2uqjTc?t=349)
- Customers don't buy the "what," they buy the "why" — understanding why a past problem was painful informs both product decisions and marketing copy [eric-migicovsky-how-to-talk-to-users @ 10:18](https://youtu.be/MT4Ig2uqjTc?t=618)
- To find the best first customer, score prospects on three numeric factors — how much the problem costs them, how frequently they encounter it, and how large their budget is — and pick whoever sits at the center of that Venn diagram [eric-migicovsky-how-to-talk-to-users @ 19:43](https://youtu.be/MT4Ig2uqjTc?t=1183)
- At the idea stage, start user research on yourself, then move to friends and warm intros; some YC founders have succeeded simply by showing up in person (e.g. dropping by fire stations, working a booth-less CES) [eric-migicovsky-how-to-talk-to-users @ 15:21](https://youtu.be/MT4Ig2uqjTc?t=921)
- Superhuman's CEO built a quantitative product-market-fit signal by asking users weekly "how would you feel if you could no longer use this product," treating 40%+ "very disappointed" as the threshold for exponential growth [eric-migicovsky-how-to-talk-to-users @ 26:09](https://youtu.be/MT4Ig2uqjTc?t=1569)
- Practical tactics for the iteration stage include collecting phone numbers at signup, not designing by committee, and testing willingness to pay with a credit-card gate before building a feature [eric-migicovsky-how-to-talk-to-users @ 28:29](https://youtu.be/MT4Ig2uqjTc?t=1709)
- During interviews, discard "bad data" like generic compliments and hypothetical fluff, and steer conversations back to specific past experiences [eric-migicovsky-how-to-talk-to-users @ 29:51](https://youtu.be/MT4Ig2uqjTc?t=1791)
