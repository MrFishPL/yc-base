# Female Founders Conference - Mountain View — Compiled Truth

A multi-speaker YC Female Founders Conference session where founders of Promise, Vanta, Remix, Gemnote, Eight Sleep, and Sequoia partner Jess Lee (ex-Polyvore) share candid stories about building companies, raising money, and surviving the early grind.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Promise co-founder Phaedra Ellis-Lampkins got into YC without a technical co-founder, a prototype, or any money — what got them in was a deep, lived understanding of the criminal justice problem they were solving [female-founders-conference-mountain-view @ 24:05](https://youtu.be/9jvdl24lAI4?t=1445)
- Ellis-Lampkins found selling to government fastest through relationship-based sales rather than RFPs or cold outbound — Promise never sent a single cold email [female-founders-conference-mountain-view @ 32:11](https://youtu.be/9jvdl24lAI4?t=1931)
- Vanta founder Christina Cacioppo spent the first six to nine months doing manual, spreadsheet-based security audits for friends' companies before writing code, and knew they had something when strangers started asking for the same manual service [female-founders-conference-mountain-view @ 37:45](https://youtu.be/9jvdl24lAI4?t=2265)
- Remix co-founder Tiffany Chu says the worst user-research question is "what did you think," followed by silence — instead ask what specifically they disliked and let the uncomfortable silence force real answers [female-founders-conference-mountain-view @ 1:00:42](https://youtu.be/9jvdl24lAI4?t=3642)
- Gemnote's Ashley Wong describes taking on a 500-gift Airbnb order as a two-person company (plus her boyfriend fulfilling boxes on weekends) as the moment that taught her founders need to build the mental resilience to push through seemingly impossible challenges [female-founders-conference-mountain-view @ 46:12](https://youtu.be/9jvdl24lAI4?t=2772)
- Founders weigh firing a customer against unit economics, whether the contact will champion them internally, whether there's repeat business potential, and whether the customer gives honest feedback [female-founders-conference-mountain-view @ 58:30](https://youtu.be/9jvdl24lAI4?t=3510)
- Eight Sleep's Alexandra Zatarain says an investor raised her marriage to a co-founder as a red flag during fundraising, and that the team was rejected by YC twice before finally getting in on a third application [female-founders-conference-mountain-view @ 1:24:29](https://youtu.be/9jvdl24lAI4?t=5069)
- YC pushed Eight Sleep to get beta units out of founders' own bedrooms and into real customers' homes, including their own group partner's apartment, to force honest field-testing [female-founders-conference-mountain-view @ 1:27:06](https://youtu.be/9jvdl24lAI4?t=5226)
- Zatarain's four post-YC lessons for building a durable company: keep doing customer discovery (15 calls a month, years in), hire for culture fit not just skills, ignore vanity metrics like press or social following in favor of metrics tied to sustainable growth and margin, and find a tribe of mentors and peers to lean on [female-founders-conference-mountain-view @ 1:34:44](https://youtu.be/9jvdl24lAI4?t=5684)
- Jess Lee left an established job at Google for the unproven startup Polyvore because her learning had plateaued, on the advice to optimize career decisions for maximizing learning and growth rather than for winning [female-founders-conference-mountain-view @ 1:45:15](https://youtu.be/9jvdl24lAI4?t=6315)
