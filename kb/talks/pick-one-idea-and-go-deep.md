# Pick One Idea and Go Deep — Compiled Truth

A YC partner argues that founders overthink idea selection, and the fix is to stop hunting for the "perfect" idea, commit fully to one, and go deep enough to become a true domain expert while shipping product in a tight loop.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The two most common overthinking traps are searching for the "perfect" idea and doubting whether you're the "perfect" founder for it, both of which stall starting [pick-one-idea-and-go-deep @ 00:54](https://youtu.be/R56RJFZBasQ?t=54)
- You can only discover the right idea by making contact with reality and getting feedback from customers, not by reasoning about it in the abstract [pick-one-idea-and-go-deep @ 01:15](https://youtu.be/R56RJFZBasQ?t=75)
- Blake Scholl of Boom Supersonic came from ad tech with no aerospace background, showing that deep curiosity and customer conversations can substitute for years of domain experience [pick-one-idea-and-go-deep @ 02:03](https://youtu.be/R56RJFZBasQ?t=123)
- Working multiple ideas at once produces bad signal, since you never go deep enough on any one to know whether it's really working [pick-one-idea-and-go-deep @ 02:46](https://youtu.be/R56RJFZBasQ?t=166)
- Going deep means "burning the other boats": explicitly foreclosing other ideas, telling customers you've pivoted, and changing your name, emails, and internal narrative, as GovDash did across five pivots before its eventual Series B [pick-one-idea-and-go-deep @ 03:31](https://youtu.be/R56RJFZBasQ?t=211)
- A high bar for depth is whether you could actually run your customer's business or teach a class on their problem, learned through a tight loop of customer conversations and shipping product [pick-one-idea-and-go-deep @ 04:36](https://youtu.be/R56RJFZBasQ?t=276)
- Good AI-era ideas sit at the edge of what today's models can do, so the product improves automatically as frontier models get better [pick-one-idea-and-go-deep @ 06:04](https://youtu.be/R56RJFZBasQ?t=364)
- Good ideas should verticalize into owning an outcome (e.g., be the insurer, be the bank) rather than just selling software to an industry, since software's cost is heading to zero [pick-one-idea-and-go-deep @ 06:49](https://youtu.be/R56RJFZBasQ?t=409)
- Founders should aim for the most ambitious version of an idea, since ambitious and modest startups cost roughly the same effort but ambition better protects against competitors and attracts talent [pick-one-idea-and-go-deep @ 08:03](https://youtu.be/R56RJFZBasQ?t=483)
- Going deep on an idea that ultimately fails still leaves founders with unambiguous customer data and often surfaces a better, deeper structural problem to pursue next [pick-one-idea-and-go-deep @ 09:15](https://youtu.be/R56RJFZBasQ?t=555)
