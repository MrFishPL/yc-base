# Anne Wojcicki : How to Build the Future — Compiled Truth

23andMe founder Anne Wojcicki explains how ten years on Wall Street convinced her healthcare monetizes illness rather than health, and how she built a direct-to-consumer genetics company on persistence, hiring discipline, and data-driven communication with regulators.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Her founding insight from a decade investing in healthcare was that the industry effectively monetizes illness, with no one incentivized to keep people healthy, which pushed her to build a consumer-facing, incentive-realigned company [anne-wojcicki-how-to-build-the-future @ 01:39](https://youtu.be/zZN-3RpdjcY?t=99).
- 23andMe's core bet was combining a falling cost-of-genome-sequencing trend with the rise of social/crowdsourced platforms, empowering individuals with their own genetic data instead of relying on institutions like Stanford or Pfizer [anne-wojcicki-how-to-build-the-future @ 08:01](https://youtu.be/zZN-3RpdjcY?t=481).
- After a strong launch day, sales dropped to only 15-25 kits a day; basic market research revealed people didn't understand why they wanted their genetic information, so she rewrote her pitch to lead with "over 50% of customers get a medically meaningful result" [anne-wojcicki-how-to-build-the-future @ 13:34](https://youtu.be/zZN-3RpdjcY?t=814).
- With scientists and regulators, she learned you can't just claim good science, you have to hand over your published data since that's the language they trust [anne-wojcicki-how-to-build-the-future @ 14:25](https://youtu.be/zZN-3RpdjcY?t=865).
- The first two scientist hires set the company's scientific integrity bar and talent ceiling going forward, and she optimized hiring for humility and openness to feedback over raw brilliance, since a bad senior hire can sink a company [anne-wojcicki-how-to-build-the-future @ 14:56](https://youtu.be/zZN-3RpdjcY?t=896).
- Moving from an unregulated to a regulated company required a communication style shift: less reflexive questioning of every rule, more structured, data-backed explanations aimed at FDA staff who are genuinely optimizing for public safety [anne-wojcicki-how-to-build-the-future @ 17:43](https://youtu.be/zZN-3RpdjcY?t=1063).
- Series A documents from 2007 are remarkably similar to the company's strategy a decade later, illustrating her view that the best companies are steadfast and that persistence through the "trough of sorrow" is what turns years of grinding into what looks like overnight success [anne-wojcicki-how-to-build-the-future @ 21:04](https://youtu.be/zZN-3RpdjcY?t=1264).
- 23andMe now runs large-scale intervention studies (e.g., a 70,000-person, six-arm weight-loss study) to learn how DNA relates to outcomes like weight loss and Parkinson's risk, aiming to eventually tell customers concretely what to do to stay healthy [anne-wojcicki-how-to-build-the-future @ 27:11](https://youtu.be/zZN-3RpdjcY?t=1631).
