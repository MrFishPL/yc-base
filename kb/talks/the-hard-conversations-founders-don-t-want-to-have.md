# The Hard Conversations Founders Don't Want to Have — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel and Dalton Caldwell argue that avoiding hard, honest conversations — with advisors, co-founders, and employees — is comfortable but corrosive, while leaning into them (without blowing up relationships) is how founders actually learn and improve.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- A hard conversation is defined by high stakes and vulnerability — it's the opposite of the easy, superficial agreement everyone prefers [the-hard-conversations-founders-don-t-want-to-have @ 00:55](https://youtu.be/hUbM-bURoJE?t=55)
- Michael's effectiveness as a YC partner only improved once he stopped optimizing for founders having fun and started giving honest opinions about their product or work ethic [the-hard-conversations-founders-don-t-want-to-have @ 02:03](https://youtu.be/hUbM-bURoJE?t=123)
- When a founder has already made a decision and wants validation, the real skill is asking the "question behind the question" — what's actually driving the ask [the-hard-conversations-founders-don-t-want-to-have @ 04:49](https://youtu.be/hUbM-bURoJE?t=289)
- When a startup won't be able to raise or spend what it hoped, the advice is to set expectations low and prepare for the worst, like bringing a parachute — so any surprise is on the upside [the-hard-conversations-founders-don-t-want-to-have @ 07:23](https://youtu.be/hUbM-bURoJE?t=443)
- Founders sometimes wrongly assume YC partners are god-like advisors who can substitute for talking to actual customers, when they should be talking to their users instead [the-hard-conversations-founders-don-t-want-to-have @ 10:20](https://youtu.be/hUbM-bURoJE?t=620)
- Co-founders who've known each other before the startup are better able to survive hard conversations because they have a base relationship worth preserving beyond the company [the-hard-conversations-founders-don-t-want-to-have @ 12:13](https://youtu.be/hUbM-bURoJE?t=733)
- Founders need to learn to disagree and release pressure in a real conflict without crossing the line that irreparably harms the relationship — it's a learnable skill [the-hard-conversations-founders-don-t-want-to-have @ 13:16](https://youtu.be/hUbM-bURoJE?t=796)
- With employees, founders should allow for the possibility they hired the wrong person for the role, even if the person interviewed well [the-hard-conversations-founders-don-t-want-to-have @ 16:41](https://youtu.be/hUbM-bURoJE?t=1001)
- Good startup employees want honesty and transparency, even bad news like a two-month runway, because it lets them help fix the problem rather than being blindsided [the-hard-conversations-founders-don-t-want-to-have @ 17:22](https://youtu.be/hUbM-bURoJE?t=1042)
