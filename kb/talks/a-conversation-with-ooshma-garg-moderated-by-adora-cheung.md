# A Conversation with Ooshma Garg - Moderated by Adora Cheung — Compiled Truth

Gobble founder Ooshma Garg recounts iterating through four business models over three and a half years before finding product-market fit with 15-minute dinner kits, framing grit, mission-driven pivoting-vs-iterating, and disciplined attribution as the throughlines of her survival.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Garg distinguishes iterating (carrying forward learnings) from pivoting (starting over), arguing Gobble iterated through four models rather than pivoted [a-conversation-with-ooshma-garg-moderated-by-adora-cheung @ 14:09](https://youtu.be/tA5J79on7Vg?t=849)
- The peer-to-peer chef marketplace model failed because it was supply-constrained — individual home cooks couldn't scale batch size without losing what made the food special [a-conversation-with-ooshma-garg-moderated-by-adora-cheung @ 15:17](https://youtu.be/tA5J79on7Vg?t=917)
- She wound down a profitable enterprise-catering business because investors judged the company by what fraction of revenue came from the true consumer vision, not just top-line growth [a-conversation-with-ooshma-garg-moderated-by-adora-cheung @ 16:56](https://youtu.be/tA5J79on7Vg?t=1016)
- Garg defines product-market fit as the intersection of ability to grow and retention, and diagnoses each prior model's failure against that framework [a-conversation-with-ooshma-garg-moderated-by-adora-cheung @ 22:43](https://youtu.be/tA5J79on7Vg?t=1363)
- Early low-cost, unscalable growth tactics — printed promo cards placed in favorite local coffee shops — outperformed laundromats and salons, and built in attribution from day one [a-conversation-with-ooshma-garg-moderated-by-adora-cheung @ 12:17](https://youtu.be/tA5J79on7Vg?t=737)
- For pricing experiments as a scrappy company, she emailed different customer clusters different sets of plans (A/B/C) and picked the price that maximized profit times conversion [a-conversation-with-ooshma-garg-moderated-by-adora-cheung @ 43:56](https://youtu.be/tA5J79on7Vg?t=2636)

- She kept the team lean (about four people, $40k/month burn) during the multi-year product-market-fit search, stretching two small bridge rounds rather than raising big [a-conversation-with-ooshma-garg-moderated-by-adora-cheung @ 26:37](https://youtu.be/tA5J79on7Vg?t=1597)
