# Stop Innovating (On The Wrong Things) — Compiled Truth

Dalton and Michael argue founders have a limited amount of "innovation juice" and should spend nearly all of it on making something people want, using best practices everywhere else.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Making a startup work is already a miracle, so founders should focus their limited "miracle juice" on building something people want rather than spreading it across many risky bets [stop-innovating-on-the-wrong-things @ 01:13](https://youtu.be/FNwu440UxhU?t=73)
- A common anti-pattern seen on YC applications is picking unusual corporate structures, like incorporating as a Wyoming LLC, when a standard Delaware C Corp is fine [stop-innovating-on-the-wrong-things @ 02:56](https://youtu.be/FNwu440UxhU?t=176)
- OpenAI's nonprofit-owns-a-for-profit structure is cited as an example of corporate governance innovation not worth emulating, even by people who work there [stop-innovating-on-the-wrong-things @ 03:52](https://youtu.be/FNwu440UxhU?t=232)
- Some founders try to disprove widely accepted startup advice with an unrelated contrarian bet, like insisting their real thesis is that hydrogen will replace gasoline, which has nothing to do with their actual business [stop-innovating-on-the-wrong-things @ 05:13](https://youtu.be/FNwu440UxhU?t=313)
- Choosing idiosyncratic technical bets purely because they're fun to build, like writing your own programming language, is unnecessary risk that doesn't serve the customer [stop-innovating-on-the-wrong-things @ 06:49](https://youtu.be/FNwu440UxhU?t=409)
- Copying a competitor's cloud pricing model when it's the norm customers understand is usually smarter than inventing confusing new pricing schemes just to be different [stop-innovating-on-the-wrong-things @ 08:27](https://youtu.be/FNwu440UxhU?t=507)
- Branding advice that rewards being radically different doesn't transfer well to software, where the most successful products copy roughly 80% of existing conventions [stop-innovating-on-the-wrong-things @ 10:11](https://youtu.be/FNwu440UxhU?t=611)
- Innovating on basic usability, like reinventing how a cursor or buttons work on a website, creates unnecessary risk that isn't helpful to users [stop-innovating-on-the-wrong-things @ 10:39](https://youtu.be/FNwu440UxhU?t=639)
- Founders should save idiosyncratic, high-risk technical or lifestyle bets for after they've achieved one startup success, not bundle them into the first attempt [stop-innovating-on-the-wrong-things @ 11:20](https://youtu.be/FNwu440UxhU?t=680)
