# Jim Goetz and Jan Koum at Startup School SV 2014 — Compiled Truth

WhatsApp founder Jan Koum and Sequoia's Jim Goetz recount how WhatsApp deliberately rejected Silicon Valley norms — charging users, avoiding press, skipping marketing — to stay focused on product and infrastructure while scaling to hundreds of millions of users.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- WhatsApp's original idea wasn't messaging at all — it started as a status-indicator app and only pivoted to messaging once the status feature stalled, reusing the same contact-graph backend and iPhone client code [jim-goetz-and-jan-koum-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 07:28](https://youtu.be/8-pJa11YvCs?t=448)
- WhatsApp used its $1 iOS charge as a deliberate lever to slow user growth so the team could build servers that don't crash and support existing users well, rather than growing as fast as possible [jim-goetz-and-jan-koum-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 15:52](https://youtu.be/8-pJa11YvCs?t=952)
- Because the paid iOS app already generated real revenue to cover salaries and servers, Koum and Acton weren't forced to raise money out of desperation and could choose investors on their own timeline [jim-goetz-and-jan-koum-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 10:06](https://youtu.be/8-pJa11YvCs?t=606)
- Sequoia found WhatsApp not through outreach but by tracking app-store activity with an internal system called "Early Bird," which showed WhatsApp dominating in roughly 35 of 60 countries well before it was known in the US [jim-goetz-and-jan-koum-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 12:12](https://youtu.be/8-pJa11YvCs?t=732)
- WhatsApp avoided the press almost entirely because being written about would be a distraction for the founders and employees, pulling focus away from the product [jim-goetz-and-jan-koum-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 16:56](https://youtu.be/8-pJa11YvCs?t=1016)
- The company had essentially no marketing or finance function and only two people in non-technical roles, an unusually lean structure that investors were told not to try to "fix" [jim-goetz-and-jan-koum-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 17:33](https://youtu.be/8-pJa11YvCs?t=1053)
- WhatsApp only started hiring dedicated, highly multilingual customer-support staff once it reached roughly 100–150 million users, after years of the founders personally answering support emails [jim-goetz-and-jan-koum-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 14:05](https://youtu.be/8-pJa11YvCs?t=845)
- Choosing phone numbers instead of usernames let WhatsApp piggyback on a user's existing address book as its social graph instead of building a new identity/handshake layer from scratch [jim-goetz-and-jan-koum-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 18:33](https://youtu.be/8-pJa11YvCs?t=1113)
