# Michael Truell: Building Cursor At 23, Taking On GitHub Copilot & Advice To Engineering Students — Compiled Truth

Cursor co-founder Michael Truell traces the company's path from a failed CAD co-pilot and an encrypted-messaging side project through several pivots to building an AI code editor that grew from single digits to 80% of a YC batch's tech stack in one year.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The founders committed to coding-with-AI only after realizing that if they were consistent with their beliefs, all of software development would flow through models within five years and no one else in the space was taking that seriously enough [michael-truell-building-cursor-at-23-taking-on-github-copilo @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/TrXi3naD6Og?t=0)
- Their first real startup idea, a CAD co-pilot for mechanical engineers, failed after months of data scraping and model work because none of the founders were mechanical engineers and "the science wasn't really ready" [michael-truell-building-cursor-at-23-taking-on-github-copilo @ 07:32](https://youtu.be/TrXi3naD6Og?t=452)
- A parallel encrypted-messaging project was technically impressive but had bad scalability trade-offs and failed to gain traction whether given away free or sold B2B [michael-truell-building-cursor-at-23-taking-on-github-copilo @ 09:43](https://youtu.be/TrXi3naD6Og?t=583)
- They chose to build a coding tool despite GitHub Copilot already making roughly $100M in revenue, deciding desperation and genuine excitement about the space mattered more than fear of the incumbent [michael-truell-building-cursor-at-23-taking-on-github-copilo @ 10:32](https://youtu.be/TrXi3naD6Og?t=632)
- Cursor's first editor was built from scratch on open-source primitives like CodeMirror in about three months, but the team learned a feature-complete editor was too big a scope and switched to forking VS Code instead [michael-truell-building-cursor-at-23-taking-on-github-copilo @ 15:22](https://youtu.be/TrXi3naD6Og?t=922)
- Throughout 2023 the team resisted pulling in the direction of its loudest user segments (non-coders, or users wanting a tech-stack-specific tool) to keep Cursor a horizontal product [michael-truell-building-cursor-at-23-taking-on-github-copilo @ 18:55](https://youtu.be/TrXi3naD6Og?t=1135)
- Growth compounded through 2024 because the market rewarded direct product improvement: making Cursor codebase-aware, then faster and more accurate at next-edit prediction, drove visible jumps in usage [michael-truell-building-cursor-at-23-taking-on-github-copilo @ 20:17](https://youtu.be/TrXi3naD6Og?t=1217)
- Cursor adoption inside YC batches went from single-digit percentage in 2023 to about 80% in 2024, spreading almost entirely by word of mouth rather than paid growth efforts [michael-truell-building-cursor-at-23-taking-on-github-copilo @ 21:09](https://youtu.be/TrXi3naD6Og?t=1269)
- Early evangelism came from a co-founder who built an audience by posting deep, substantive AI analysis on Twitter rather than typical growth tactics, which helped bootstrap the initial user base [michael-truell-building-cursor-at-23-taking-on-github-copilo @ 21:44](https://youtu.be/TrXi3naD6Og?t=1304)
- The company stayed at under 10 people through the end of 2023, deliberately hiring slowly while four strong co-founding engineers carried the early product [michael-truell-building-cursor-at-23-taking-on-github-copilo @ 24:11](https://youtu.be/TrXi3naD6Og?t=1451)
