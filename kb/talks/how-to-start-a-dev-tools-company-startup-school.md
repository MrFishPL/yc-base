# How To Start A Dev Tools Company | Startup School — Compiled Truth

Algolia co-founder Nicolas Dessaigne lays out how to pick a dev-tool idea, build and validate a rough MVP with real users, and go to market through open source, founder-led outreach, and developer-native sales and marketing.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Prefer "runtime" dev-tool ideas (things your product can't run without, like an API) over "build-time" ideas (like docs or QA tooling), because runtime tools are must-haves and usage scales with the customer's own growth [how-to-start-a-dev-tools-company-startup-school @ 03:23](https://youtu.be/z1aKRhRnVNk?t=203)
- 74% of YC dev-tool companies had only technical co-founders, versus 45% for YC companies overall, showing a business co-founder isn't required to learn to sell [how-to-start-a-dev-tools-company-startup-school @ 06:42](https://youtu.be/z1aKRhRnVNk?t=402)
- Build prototypes quick and dirty, expecting to throw away 90% of the code, and show unfinished prototypes to users early rather than waiting for a polished product [how-to-start-a-dev-tools-company-startup-school @ 07:26](https://youtu.be/z1aKRhRnVNk?t=446)
- Algolia's first paying customer ($2,000/month) was closed with just a command-line demo and no admin UI or API client yet, illustrating that an MVP only needs to be viable, not polished [how-to-start-a-dev-tools-company-startup-school @ 08:33](https://youtu.be/z1aKRhRnVNk?t=513)
- Since nobody knows a new dev-tool startup yet, founders must start with personalized outreach to their network before any bottom-up/inbound motion can work [how-to-start-a-dev-tools-company-startup-school @ 10:25](https://youtu.be/z1aKRhRnVNk?t=625)
- Hacker News's "Show HN" is the best launch venue for dev tools; repeated launches (as Ollama did) compound community excitement and growth over time [how-to-start-a-dev-tools-company-startup-school @ 11:54](https://youtu.be/z1aKRhRnVNk?t=714)
- Doing things that don't scale — like Stripe engineers coding side-by-side with early customers — both satisfies early users and produces invaluable early feedback [how-to-start-a-dev-tools-company-startup-school @ 14:11](https://youtu.be/z1aKRhRnVNk?t=851)
- Open source is close to mandatory for libraries/frameworks or anything touching sensitive data, and can shorten enterprise sales cycles by a year or more, but still requires a clear monetization path (hosting, open-core, or support) [how-to-start-a-dev-tools-company-startup-school @ 16:25](https://youtu.be/z1aKRhRnVNk?t=985)
- Wait until roughly $1M ARR before hiring a first salesperson, hire technical salespeople, and lean on live demos rather than sales decks since developers distrust traditional sales pitches [how-to-start-a-dev-tools-company-startup-school @ 21:29](https://youtu.be/z1aKRhRnVNk?t=1289)
- Marketing for dev tools should be led by developers, not traditional marketers, through documentation-as-product, engineer-staffed support, and community engagement (Hacker News, Discord, subreddits) rather than paid promotion [how-to-start-a-dev-tools-company-startup-school @ 28:44](https://youtu.be/z1aKRhRnVNk?t=1724)
