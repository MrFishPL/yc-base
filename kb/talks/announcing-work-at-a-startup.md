# Announcing Work at a Startup — Compiled Truth

YC partners Jared Friedman and Matt Long describe Work at a Startup, YC's hiring platform for matching engineers with YC companies, and preview a revived Work at a Startup conference.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Work at a Startup is a common application for engineers interested in working at a YC company, giving founders searchable, filterable candidate profiles [announcing-work-at-a-startup @ 00:38](https://youtu.be/Oh0sEkqSVxM?t=38)
- Hiring, and hiring engineers in particular, is the number one problem YC founders report facing after going through the program [announcing-work-at-a-startup @ 01:17](https://youtu.be/Oh0sEkqSVxM?t=77)
- Matching is fundamentally harder for startups than for large companies, since a company like Microsoft hires for so many roles that almost anyone can find a fit, while startups hire for only a couple of unbranded roles [announcing-work-at-a-startup @ 03:47](https://youtu.be/Oh0sEkqSVxM?t=227)
- The platform matches on both objective signals (company size, profitability, tech stack) and cultural/role preferences (product-focused work vs. deep technical problems) [announcing-work-at-a-startup @ 04:53](https://youtu.be/Oh0sEkqSVxM?t=293)
- The product originally avoided asking for resumes so candidates would be judged more holistically rather than on pedigree, but the team added resumes back after founders repeatedly said they needed more context before reaching out [announcing-work-at-a-startup @ 08:45](https://youtu.be/Oh0sEkqSVxM?t=525)
- YC companies using the platform now hire in over 12 countries, including many remote-only roles spanning full-stack web, data science, DevOps, and more [announcing-work-at-a-startup @ 09:15](https://youtu.be/Oh0sEkqSVxM?t=555)
- A ~1,000-comment Hacker News thread on the pros/cons of working at a startup surfaced autonomy, tight-knit team bonds, and control over technology choices as the top attractions [announcing-work-at-a-startup @ 11:06](https://youtu.be/Oh0sEkqSVxM?t=666)
- The same HN thread found compensation was the most cited downside, alongside the need for transparency about how to value equity and concerns about job security [announcing-work-at-a-startup @ 11:57](https://youtu.be/Oh0sEkqSVxM?t=717)
- YC is reviving its Work at a Startup conference (last held in 2010) as a demo-day-style event for engineers, now roughly ten times bigger, with keynotes from Justin Kan and Sam Altman and 35 rapid-fire founder pitches [announcing-work-at-a-startup @ 16:36](https://youtu.be/Oh0sEkqSVxM?t=996)
- Run quietly for about six months with just a homepage link, the platform had already signed up over 3,000 engineers, with over 80% of YC companies hiring engineers using it [announcing-work-at-a-startup @ 24:26](https://youtu.be/Oh0sEkqSVxM?t=1466)
