# Mark Zuckerberg at Startup School 2013 — Compiled Truth

In a Q&A about Facebook's early years, Zuckerberg traces the path from personal side-projects (course match, the Rome of Augustus study tool) to Facebook, and reflects on competition, hiring, equity mistakes, and why caring more than better-resourced rivals is often the deciding factor.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Before Facebook, he built "course match," a tool scraping Harvard's course catalog so students could see what classes friends were taking — an early instance of building for himself first. [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2013 @ 02:21](https://youtu.be/MGsalg2f9js?t=141)
- Facebook beat competing college social sites by focusing on real identity and actual friend connections, which let content flow into something like a news feed rather than just a static profile. [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2013 @ 08:24](https://youtu.be/MGsalg2f9js?t=504)
- Because he knew nothing about business early on, he agreed to a cap-table split with co-founder Eduardo before understanding vesting schedules, which Peter Thiel later insisted on — a costly early equity mistake. [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2013 @ 19:32](https://youtu.be/MGsalg2f9js?t=1172)
- Don't try to avoid mistakes as a founder — you'll make tons of them; the key is learning fast and not letting a mistake demoralize you into giving up. [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2013 @ 17:18](https://youtu.be/MGsalg2f9js?t=1038)
- His hiring heuristic: only consider someone "good enough" if you'd genuinely want to work for them. [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2013 @ 22:00](https://youtu.be/MGsalg2f9js?t=1320)
- Facebook still uses an internal "lockdown" concept where teams drop everything to respond the moment a competitor gets ahead on something strategic. [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2013 @ 31:43](https://youtu.be/MGsalg2f9js?t=1903)
- He believes founders over-worry about strategic competitors but under-worry about direct clones, which can be a serious threat if allowed to get lodged into a market (e.g., Facebook never beat VKontakte in Russia). [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2013 @ 34:02](https://youtu.be/MGsalg2f9js?t=2042)
- Facebook won against companies with far more engineers, servers, and money largely because the founders cared about it more than anyone else did. [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2013 @ 14:49](https://youtu.be/MGsalg2f9js?t=889)
- Peter Thiel's key strategic lesson: as complexity grows, dozens of things compete for attention each day, but the job is to identify the one or two that actually matter and focus there. [mark-zuckerberg-at-startup-school-2013 @ 29:17](https://youtu.be/MGsalg2f9js?t=1757)
