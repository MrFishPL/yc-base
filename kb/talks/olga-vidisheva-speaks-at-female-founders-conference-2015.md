# Olga Vidisheva Speaks at Female Founders Conference 2015 — Compiled Truth

Olga Vidisheva, founder of Shoptiques, tells the story of bootstrapping and scaling a boutique marketplace, drawing five takeaways about talking to users, asking for what you want, staying honest about the business you want to build, forging your own path on co-founders, and believing in the mission.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Spending a full year talking to over 800 boutiques before building anything was the single most helpful thing she did in starting and running Shoptiques [olga-vidisheva-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 03:22](https://youtu.be/z7S7Kf88jog?t=202)
- Her rule is "always ask" — if you don't ask, the answer is always no, and applying to YC despite thinking single non-technical founders might not be a good fit is what got her in [olga-vidisheva-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 07:03](https://youtu.be/z7S7Kf88jog?t=423)
- She avoided raising money early because she didn't want to put her reputation on the line until she knew the idea was a real business, instead building a first prototype for $8,000 [olga-vidisheva-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 04:04](https://youtu.be/z7S7Kf88jog?t=244)
- Be honest with yourself from the start about what kind of business you want to build — a multi-billion-dollar venture-backed company requires different technology and team than a lifestyle business [olga-vidisheva-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 09:15](https://youtu.be/z7S7Kf88jog?t=555)
- She raised a seed round from Andreessen Horowitz, Greylock, Benchmark, and SV Angel specifically because they understood marketplaces and had backed similar companies like OpenTable and First Dibs [olga-vidisheva-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 10:30](https://youtu.be/z7S7Kf88jog?t=630)
- She fired two successive sets of technical co-founders because they didn't have the skills or the customer focus the business needed, even though one was her best friend [olga-vidisheva-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 11:51](https://youtu.be/z7S7Kf88jog?t=711)
- Her takeaway from those firings is to make your own path — it's okay not to have a technical co-founder, and she instead hired a CTO with goals and compensation tied to delivery, after which the site stopped crashing and grew 700% [olga-vidisheva-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 13:10](https://youtu.be/z7S7Kf88jog?t=790)
- Her final takeaway is to believe and be passionate — she never stopped believing Shoptiques would be a multi-billion-dollar business, and if you don't believe in an idea from the beginning you should find another one [olga-vidisheva-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 15:44](https://youtu.be/z7S7Kf88jog?t=944)
- Marketplaces like Etsy and eBay typically endure about two years of slow, painful growth before hitting an inflection point of exponential growth, which is why surrounding yourself with investors who have lived that pattern matters [olga-vidisheva-speaks-at-female-founders-conference-2015 @ 16:25](https://youtu.be/z7S7Kf88jog?t=985)
