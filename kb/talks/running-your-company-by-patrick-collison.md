# Running Your Company by Patrick Collison — Compiled Truth

Stripe co-founder and CEO Patrick Collison describes how Stripe found product-market fit through obsessive manual attention to users, and how founder mental models must shift as the company scales past product-market fit.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Before Stripe's public launch, Patrick and his brother John personally installed Stripe's API into users' apps in person — a "do things that don't scale" tactic PG later dubbed the "Collison installation" [running-your-company-by-patrick-collison @ 01:37](https://youtu.be/NprBQi0cSHU?t=97)
- Patrick defines Stripe's product-market fit moment as public launch, when the company became bottlenecked on serving demand rather than generating it [running-your-company-by-patrick-collison @ 11:05](https://youtu.be/NprBQi0cSHU?t=665)
- Pre-product-market-fit metrics are relatively unhelpful; founders should bias heavily toward direct inspection and high-throughput qualitative feedback since user counts are still small [running-your-company-by-patrick-collison @ 16:55](https://youtu.be/NprBQi0cSHU?t=1015)
- Stripe literally emailed the team on every API request and every error in its early years so they could see and fix issues within minutes [running-your-company-by-patrick-collison @ 14:32](https://youtu.be/NprBQi0cSHU?t=872)
- Post-product-market-fit growth should be treated not as a cosmic trajectory to nudge but as a knowable, buildable market-coverage problem: map concentric circles of your market and build the go-to-market org ahead of where you are today [running-your-company-by-patrick-collison @ 29:07](https://youtu.be/NprBQi0cSHU?t=1747)
- Pre-product-market-fit, the main thing to optimize is speed of fruitful iteration — tightening the feedback loop from user behavior to shipped fix, likened to the OODA loop concept from fighter pilot strategy [running-your-company-by-patrick-collison @ 34:38](https://youtu.be/NprBQi0cSHU?t=2078)
- Somewhere between roughly two and ten people tends to be the optimally responsive team size pre-product-market-fit, since each hire adds coordination costs that can be quadratic [running-your-company-by-patrick-collison @ 36:39](https://youtu.be/NprBQi0cSHU?t=2199)
- Stripe was too consensus-oriented between roughly five and fifty employees and should have shifted earlier to explicit, deliberate decision communication rather than assuming ambient shared context [running-your-company-by-patrick-collison @ 42:15](https://youtu.be/NprBQi0cSHU?t=2535)
- Stripe sustains new-idea generation at scale via an annual "crazy ideas" document open to the whole company, where submissions must be "probably a bad idea" that could be great if it worked — Stripe Press and Stellar support both originated there [running-your-company-by-patrick-collison @ 48:28](https://youtu.be/NprBQi0cSHU?t=2908)
- Patrick looks for intellectual honesty, general niceness/warmth, and being smart when hiring, since certainty in either direction on complex trade-off questions is a red flag [running-your-company-by-patrick-collison @ 39:59](https://youtu.be/NprBQi0cSHU?t=2399)
