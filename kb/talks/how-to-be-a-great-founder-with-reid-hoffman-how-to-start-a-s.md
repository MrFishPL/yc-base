# How to Be a Great Founder with Reid Hoffman (How to Start a Startup 2014: Lecture 13) — Compiled Truth

Reid Hoffman argues great founders aren't superhuman generalists but people with a couple of real edges who can navigate apparent paradoxes (persistence vs. flexibility, vision vs. data) using a running "investment thesis" to track confidence.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Great founders aren't universal "superpersons" good at everything; they generally have just a couple of genuine superpowers rather than scoring 10/10 across every skill [how-to-be-a-great-founder-with-reid-hoffman-how-to-start-a-s @ 03:48](https://youtu.be/pkAum45ubWc?t=228)
- Two or three co-founders is usually a better bet than a solo founder, since a team can compensate for each other's individual weaknesses across a broad set of needed skills [how-to-be-a-great-founder-with-reid-hoffman-how-to-start-a-s @ 04:12](https://youtu.be/pkAum45ubWc?t=252)
- Founders should locate where the network for their specific problem is strongest, not just default to Silicon Valley — Groupon needed Chicago's sales-force culture, not Valley capital-efficiency instincts [how-to-be-a-great-founder-with-reid-hoffman-how-to-start-a-s @ 07:08](https://youtu.be/pkAum45ubWc?t=428)
- Being contrarian only counts if a smart, informed person genuinely disagrees with you — otherwise it isn't actually a contrarian bet, it's just wrong or ignorant [how-to-be-a-great-founder-with-reid-hoffman-how-to-start-a-s @ 10:28](https://youtu.be/pkAum45ubWc?t=628)
- Keep an explicit "investment thesis" for the company, and track whether new evidence is increasing or decreasing your confidence in it — that's what tells you when to stay persistent versus get flexible [how-to-be-a-great-founder-with-reid-hoffman-how-to-start-a-s @ 14:55](https://youtu.be/pkAum45ubWc?t=895)
- PayPal's original plan — splitting dinner tabs via cash on Palm Pilots — was killed before launch once the team realized the odds of everyone at a table owning a Palm Pilot were near zero, forcing the pivot to email payments [how-to-be-a-great-founder-with-reid-hoffman-how-to-start-a-s @ 22:27](https://youtu.be/pkAum45ubWc?t=1347)
- Product distribution is more fundamental to strategy than the product itself, and financing sits below even that — a great idea still dies if the company runs out of money [how-to-be-a-great-founder-with-reid-hoffman-how-to-start-a-s @ 23:36](https://youtu.be/pkAum45ubWc?t=1416)
- A real signal to pivot is when your confidence in the investment thesis has been flat or declining for a long time despite actively trying to fix it [how-to-be-a-great-founder-with-reid-hoffman-how-to-start-a-s @ 40:28](https://youtu.be/pkAum45ubWc?t=2428)
- Before committing to a co-founder, spend 20+ hours discussing possible disagreements, work styles, and even what would make you split up — so tension later is easier to raise as problem-solving rather than betrayal [how-to-be-a-great-founder-with-reid-hoffman-how-to-start-a-s @ 48:15](https://youtu.be/pkAum45ubWc?t=2895)
