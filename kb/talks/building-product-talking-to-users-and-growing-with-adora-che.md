# Building Product, Talking to Users, and Growing with Adora Cheung (How to Start a Startup 2014: 4) — Compiled Truth

Adora Cheung (Homejoy) walks through the practical path from zero users to scale: pick a real problem you're passionate about, become an expert in the industry, build a truly minimal viable product, talk to users constantly, and choose growth tactics deliberately while tracking retention and unit economics.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Verify a problem is real and personal before building anything — Homejoy's founders wasted almost a year building for life coaches and therapists, a problem they didn't actually have [building-product-talking-to-users-and-growing-with-adora-che @ 03:55](https://youtu.be/1e4izfOsijE?t=235)
- To find inefficiencies worth exploiting, immerse yourself in the industry as a practitioner — Cheung took a job at a cleaning company to learn the business from the inside [building-product-talking-to-users-and-growing-with-adora-che @ 07:18](https://youtu.be/1e4izfOsijE?t=438)
- Before writing code, storyboard the entire ideal user experience end to end, from discovery through post-use evaluation [building-product-talking-to-users-and-growing-with-adora-che @ 10:15](https://youtu.be/1e4izfOsijE?t=615)
- An MVP must emphasize the "viable" part — Homejoy's early positioning was a wordy paragraph until they simplified to "get your place clean for $20 an hour," which is when users finally converted [building-product-talking-to-users-and-growing-with-adora-che @ 13:33](https://youtu.be/1e4izfOsijE?t=813)
- Early user acquisition can require unscalable, creative tactics, like handing out free cold water at a street fair to strike up conversations that led to cleaning bookings [building-product-talking-to-users-and-growing-with-adora-che @ 15:33](https://youtu.be/1e4izfOsijE?t=933)
- Track customer retention and cohort curves as the core metric of whether the product is actually working, alongside faster leading indicators like NPS and star ratings [building-product-talking-to-users-and-growing-with-adora-che @ 19:44](https://youtu.be/1e4izfOsijE?t=1184)
- Beware the "honesty curve": people closest to you (family, friends) will flatter a free product but tell the truth once they have to pay for it, so paid feedback is more reliable [building-product-talking-to-users-and-growing-with-adora-che @ 21:14](https://youtu.be/1e4izfOsijE?t=1274)
- Don't automate processes too early — manually onboarding cleaners by phone and in person let Homejoy learn which interview questions actually predicted good performers before building an online application [building-product-talking-to-users-and-growing-with-adora-che @ 24:27](https://youtu.be/1e4izfOsijE?t=1467)
- When users request features, dig into the underlying problem behind the request rather than building exactly what's asked, to avoid the "Frankenstein" trap of piling on features [building-product-talking-to-users-and-growing-with-adora-che @ 26:57](https://youtu.be/1e4izfOsijE?t=1617)
- For paid growth, only spend on acquisition where customer lifetime value (CLV) exceeds customer acquisition cost (CAC), and keep payback time short (about 3 months is safe, beyond 12 months is unsafe territory) [building-product-talking-to-users-and-growing-with-adora-che @ 44:24](https://youtu.be/1e4izfOsijE?t=2664)
- A useful pivot signal is three to four straight weeks of flat or backward growth despite full effort, which suggests something fundamental is wrong rather than just a temporary lull [building-product-talking-to-users-and-growing-with-adora-che @ 48:35](https://youtu.be/1e4izfOsijE?t=2915)
