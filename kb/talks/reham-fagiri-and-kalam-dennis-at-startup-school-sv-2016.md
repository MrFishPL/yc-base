# Reham Fagiri and Kalam Dennis at Startup School SV 2016 — Compiled Truth

AptDeco co-founders Reham Fagiri and Kalam Dennis describe how a scrappy, hands-on hack got their furniture marketplace off the ground, how refusing to abandon a "no-touch platform" vision cost them months, and how deliberately becoming default alive turned the company profitable.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- AptDeco emphasizes building a "real business" — one that's actually profitable — as a concept that can get lost in the Silicon Valley VC world [reham-fagiri-and-kalam-dennis-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 02:34](https://youtu.be/ANrzH3ra8e8?t=154)
- Their YC partner Kevin Hale pushed them to list Craigslist sellers' items on AptDeco without asking permission first, since it was a favor to the seller if the item sold [reham-fagiri-and-kalam-dennis-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 05:08](https://youtu.be/ANrzH3ra8e8?t=308)
- To fulfill their first sales they physically walked around New York City with a bag of cash paying sellers directly and picking up furniture themselves — a scrappy, unscalable hack that got the business from zero to one [reham-fagiri-and-kalam-dennis-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 08:16](https://youtu.be/ANrzH3ra8e8?t=496)
- The founders warn against getting married to a grand vision (they insisted on being a "no-touch" platform like Airbnb/Uber) instead of following what customers actually respond to [reham-fagiri-and-kalam-dennis-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 10:41](https://youtu.be/ANrzH3ra8e8?t=641)
- Outsourcing delivery to third-party movers and "man in a van" contractors produced declining service quality and unprofessional-looking trucks that hurt the brand [reham-fagiri-and-kalam-dennis-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 12:08](https://youtu.be/ANrzH3ra8e8?t=728)
- Switching to owning their own delivery trucks and drivers turned delivery into a delightful experience and grew word-of-mouth from ~10% to 60-70% of the business [reham-fagiri-and-kalam-dennis-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 15:04](https://youtu.be/ANrzH3ra8e8?t=904)
- Growing fast on top-line revenue while burning cash left them only 3-4 months of runway and unable to raise, forcing them to become "default alive" per Paul Graham's essay [reham-fagiri-and-kalam-dennis-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 17:48](https://youtu.be/ANrzH3ra8e8?t=1068)
- Becoming default alive meant cutting marketing spend to $0, scrutinizing every expense line item, and raising prices where they weren't covering costs, which made the business genuinely profitable [reham-fagiri-and-kalam-dennis-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 19:34](https://youtu.be/ANrzH3ra8e8?t=1174)
- A well-funded competitor that raised 20x their money and dominated press coverage eventually folded, teaching the founders to ignore competitor and market noise and focus only on what they control: their customers and unit economics [reham-fagiri-and-kalam-dennis-at-startup-school-sv-2016 @ 22:28](https://youtu.be/ANrzH3ra8e8?t=1348)
