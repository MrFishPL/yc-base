# Harj Taggar - Choosing a Startup to Work At — Compiled Truth

Y Combinator partner Harj Taggar argues that choosing a startup to work at should be approached like an investor picking a company to fund, weighing growth trajectory, team, and user love over market size or credentials.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Working at a startup isn't for everyone — if you're happy where you are and don't have a strong desire to do something else, staying at a stable, higher-paying job is the right call [harj-taggar-choosing-a-startup-to-work-at @ 00:16](https://youtu.be/evIZRpNQUx4?t=16)
- Choosing which startup to join should be approached the way an investor picks a company to invest in, since you're investing your time and energy the same way they invest money [harj-taggar-choosing-a-startup-to-work-at @ 00:51](https://youtu.be/evIZRpNQUx4?t=51)
- Look for signs the startup is already succeeding, since joining a company that's growing means more learning opportunities, better hires around you, and faster career growth [harj-taggar-choosing-a-startup-to-work-at @ 01:11](https://youtu.be/evIZRpNQUx4?t=71)
- When interviewing, ask about the metric the company tracks for success and how it's grown over time — the growth trajectory matters far more than the absolute numbers today [harj-taggar-choosing-a-startup-to-work-at @ 01:34](https://youtu.be/evIZRpNQUx4?t=94)
- Evaluate the team starting with the founders, but don't rely on traditional credentials like elite colleges or big-name employers, since many of the best founders didn't take that path [harj-taggar-choosing-a-startup-to-work-at @ 02:14](https://youtu.be/evIZRpNQUx4?t=134)
- Judge founders by how well they've done relative to their peer group and starting point — a 19-year-old from Milwaukee who raised a seed round can be more impressive than a 15-year Google veteran founding in the same place [harj-taggar-choosing-a-startup-to-work-at @ 02:55](https://youtu.be/evIZRpNQUx4?t=175)
- Don't try to evaluate market size, since that's the most common mistake investors make and how they miss the biggest winners — many top investors rejected Airbnb because they thought its market was too limited [harj-taggar-choosing-a-startup-to-work-at @ 03:41](https://youtu.be/evIZRpNQUx4?t=221)
- Every great startup has a core group of early users who are fanatical about the product, even if that group is small, so ask to talk to users and gauge the depth of their passion [harj-taggar-choosing-a-startup-to-work-at @ 04:00](https://youtu.be/evIZRpNQUx4?t=240)
