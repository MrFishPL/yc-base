# Ron Conway at Startup School 2013 — Compiled Truth

SV Angel's Ron Conway explains why he invests in people first, what separates founders like Zuckerberg, Dorsey, and Silbermann, and the fundraising tactics he's used at Twitter, Facebook, Pinterest, and Airbnb.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Conway invested $75k in Twitter sight-unseen because Evan Williams voluntarily repaid investors after his prior startup Odeo failed, showing SV Angel invests in people first [ron-conway-at-startup-school-2013 @ 01:41](https://youtu.be/7bm-Xj2jMCk?t=101)
- Ben Silbermann of Pinterest had to keep iterating for a year and a half, running focus groups with women in a coffee shop and calling them the next day to check they were still using the product [ron-conway-at-startup-school-2013 @ 08:53](https://youtu.be/7bm-Xj2jMCk?t=533)
- The biggest fundraising mistake founders make is over-focusing on valuation and dilution instead of finding the investor who adds the most value to the space [ron-conway-at-startup-school-2013 @ 14:29](https://youtu.be/7bm-Xj2jMCk?t=869)
- In Airbnb's financing, Brian Chesky chose Andreessen Horowitz's Jeff Jordan over investors offering a higher valuation because of the value Jordan could add, and thanked Conway for the introduction for a year afterward [ron-conway-at-startup-school-2013 @ 15:53](https://youtu.be/7bm-Xj2jMCk?t=953)
- Founders should get one term sheet as fast as possible to create a forcing function, then push other investors to match it, and immediately confirm any verbal commitment in writing over email [ron-conway-at-startup-school-2013 @ 16:16](https://youtu.be/7bm-Xj2jMCk?t=976)
- Great founders like Silbermann, Dorsey, and Zuckerberg stay "rifle focused on the product" and avoid distractions like press interviews [ron-conway-at-startup-school-2013 @ 19:21](https://youtu.be/7bm-Xj2jMCk?t=1161)
- Decisiveness matters as much in team-building as in product decisions: founders need to hire fast and fire fast, even when it means removing a co-founder [ron-conway-at-startup-school-2013 @ 20:36](https://youtu.be/7bm-Xj2jMCk?t=1236)
- The climate for starting a company has changed dramatically since 1979, when investors required a company to already be profitable before funding it; today the cost of starting a company is tiny and far more capital is willing to take risk [ron-conway-at-startup-school-2013 @ 23:54](https://youtu.be/7bm-Xj2jMCk?t=1434)
