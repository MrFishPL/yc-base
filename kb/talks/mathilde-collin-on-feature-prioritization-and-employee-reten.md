# Mathilde Collin on Feature Prioritization and Employee Retention at Front — Compiled Truth

Front's co-founder and CEO Mathilde Collin describes how she prioritizes features with a simple impact-vs-complexity score, why the product launched deliberately bad, and how mission, hiring bar, and transparency drove Front's high employee retention.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Front scores every candidate feature on a 1-3 scale for user impact and a 1-3 scale for build complexity, using public roadmap voting and support-inbox analytics as inputs to that decision [mathilde-collin-on-feature-prioritization-and-employee-reten @ 05:02](https://youtu.be/PaBx9WO1ruY?t=302)
- Reversing the order of comment threads (newest at the bottom) let conversations flow independent of a single message, which drove a measurable jump in comments per daily active user despite initial user backlash [mathilde-collin-on-feature-prioritization-and-employee-reten @ 07:44](https://youtu.be/PaBx9WO1ruY?t=464)
- Front launched as a deliberately bad, feature-stripped MVP because shipping fast and getting feedback mattered more than polish [mathilde-collin-on-feature-prioritization-and-employee-reten @ 09:29](https://youtu.be/PaBx9WO1ruY?t=569)
- Early growth came almost entirely from Collin personally writing content for Medium and Hacker News, then manually onboarding and calling beta signups to learn which single missing feature was blocking adoption [mathilde-collin-on-feature-prioritization-and-employee-reten @ 10:29](https://youtu.be/PaBx9WO1ruY?t=629)
- Of roughly 3,000 beta signups, only about 10 converted to real usage, but every rejected user interview taught the team something, and PR/content drove ~90% of growth for the first two and a half years before the first marketing hire [mathilde-collin-on-feature-prioritization-and-employee-reten @ 14:20](https://youtu.be/PaBx9WO1ruY?t=860)
- Collin didn't feel she had product-market fit until the Series A, at ~$1.5M ARR, three years after founding, when demos consistently beat every alternative [mathilde-collin-on-feature-prioritization-and-employee-reten @ 41:12](https://youtu.be/PaBx9WO1ruY?t=2472)
- Patrick Collison's hiring advice was to bar every early hire against "could this person be my co-founder" and to ask whether you'd want 10 more people just like them, since new hires tend to hire in their own image [mathilde-collin-on-feature-prioritization-and-employee-reten @ 26:30](https://youtu.be/PaBx9WO1ruY?t=1590)
- Front's retention rests on three levers: a clear mission ("work happier") that hooks people, a high hiring bar for quality co-workers, and radical transparency (open dashboards, board decks, and most inboxes) so employees can see their impact [mathilde-collin-on-feature-prioritization-and-employee-reten @ 25:32](https://youtu.be/PaBx9WO1ruY?t=1532)
- Every founder faces hard days regardless of outward success; Collin reads "It's Hard" and reminds herself that difficulty is the norm, not a signal of company health [mathilde-collin-on-feature-prioritization-and-employee-reten @ 23:57](https://youtu.be/PaBx9WO1ruY?t=1437)
