# Cursor CEO: Going Beyond Code, Superintelligent AI Agents, And Why Taste Still Matters — Compiled Truth

Cursor (Anysphere) co-founder and CEO Michael Truell explains how the company bet early that models would keep getting dramatically smarter, pivoted from a CAD-copilot idea into coding, and built an editor rather than an extension to reach a $9B valuation and 100M ARR in 20 months.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Cursor's goal is to replace coding with something higher-level, evolving away from "human compilation" of intent into for-loops and variables toward a form where computers fill in more of the implementation detail [cursor-ceo-going-beyond-code-superintelligent-ai-agents-and- @ 01:00](https://youtu.be/oOylEw3tPQ8?t=60)
- Context window size and continual learning (knowing an organization's history, past attempts, and coworkers) are cited as the key bottlenecks standing between current agents and human-level, let alone superhuman, coding [cursor-ceo-going-beyond-code-superintelligent-ai-agents-and- @ 06:05](https://youtu.be/oOylEw3tPQ8?t=365)
- "Taste" — defining what you actually want to build, including the logic and not just the visual design — is framed as the irreplaceable human skill even as programming itself gets automated [cursor-ceo-going-beyond-code-superintelligent-ai-agents-and- @ 09:46](https://youtu.be/oOylEw3tPQ8?t=586)
- The team's first venture was a CAD copilot for mechanical engineering (training 3D autocomplete models), which they abandoned because the underlying science and data weren't ready and because they personally were more excited about coding than mechanical engineering [cursor-ceo-going-beyond-code-superintelligent-ai-agents-and- @ 15:48](https://youtu.be/oOylEw3tPQ8?t=948)
- In hindsight, the founders wish they had gone undercover to work at a company employing mechanical engineers for three weeks rather than relying on hundreds of user interviews to build intuition for the CAD domain [cursor-ceo-going-beyond-code-superintelligent-ai-agents-and- @ 17:44](https://youtu.be/oOylEw3tPQ8?t=1064)
- The team's guiding phrase was "follow the line" — betting consistently that scaling laws would keep making models smarter, which justified a radical, non-obvious product decision to build a full standalone editor instead of a VS Code extension [cursor-ceo-going-beyond-code-superintelligent-ai-agents-and- @ 22:45](https://youtu.be/oOylEw3tPQ8?t=1365)
- Cursor deliberately avoided optimizing for the demo, instead tracking "paid power users" (people using the AI four or five days out of seven) as its true north metric, reasoning that a paid, professional tool has to be reliable and fast, not just impressive in a video [cursor-ceo-going-beyond-code-superintelligent-ai-agents-and- @ 28:41](https://youtu.be/oOylEw3tPQ8?t=1721)
- The company hired extremely slowly at first, agonizing over the first ten hires because a strong early team both raises the talent bar for future hires and acts as an "immune system" against bad fits [cursor-ceo-going-beyond-code-superintelligent-ai-agents-and- @ 31:10](https://youtu.be/oOylEw3tPQ8?t=1870)
- Cursor still interviews candidates on programming without AI tools (aside from autocomplete) because it remains a strong test of raw skill and avoids disadvantaging strong programmers who simply haven't used AI tools yet [cursor-ceo-going-beyond-code-superintelligent-ai-agents-and- @ 32:55](https://youtu.be/oOylEw3tPQ8?t=1975)
- Truell compares the AI coding market to search in the late 1990s: the product ceiling is very high, and distribution/scale directly improves the product by revealing where users accept, reject, or correct AI suggestions [cursor-ceo-going-beyond-code-superintelligent-ai-agents-and- @ 35:07](https://youtu.be/oOylEw3tPQ8?t=2107)
