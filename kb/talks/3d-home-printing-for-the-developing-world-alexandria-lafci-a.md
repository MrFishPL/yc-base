# 3D Home Printing for the Developing World – Alexandria Lafci and Brett Hagler of New Story Charity — Compiled Truth

New Story's co-founders describe how a scrappy, non-scalable MVP for transparent housing donations grew into a YC-backed nonprofit that is now 3D-printing homes to slash the cost and speed of building shelter in the developing world.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- New Story's founding insight was that donors distrust where their money goes, so the team built a "make something people want" experience: see the family, give 100% to the build, get a moving-in video back [3d-home-printing-for-the-developing-world-alexandria-lafci-a @ 08:49](https://youtu.be/rvDvGM70oy4?t=529)
- Their first MVP was a fake crowdfunding platform built on Webflow with no engineer, manually updated by the founders themselves whenever a donation came in [3d-home-printing-for-the-developing-world-alexandria-lafci-a @ 11:03](https://youtu.be/rvDvGM70oy4?t=663)
- That do-things-that-don't-scale MVP raised about $100,000 in a couple of months and funded their first six houses before they even applied to YC [3d-home-printing-for-the-developing-world-alexandria-lafci-a @ 12:28](https://youtu.be/rvDvGM70oy4?t=748)
- During YC, partner Kevin Hale took their 12-month goal of 100 homes and compressed it into a 3-month YC goal, which the team hit in 92 days by "thinking big, breaking down, and executing" [3d-home-printing-for-the-developing-world-alexandria-lafci-a @ 15:12](https://youtu.be/rvDvGM70oy4?t=912)
- After YC, their first hire was not marketing or sales but an impact data manager, because "number of homes" is a vanity metric and what matters is measurable life change (e.g., families' sleep increasing from four to almost eight hours a night) [3d-home-printing-for-the-developing-world-alexandria-lafci-a @ 20:06](https://youtu.be/rvDvGM70oy4?t=1206)
- Setting an audacious goal (800 homes) led directly to a new "Architects" program where companies fund entire communities instead of one-off homes, launched by pitching a small test package rather than betting the whole strategy [3d-home-printing-for-the-developing-world-alexandria-lafci-a @ 17:19](https://youtu.be/rvDvGM70oy4?t=1039)
- The 3D-printing initiative came from a team moonshot-brainstorming dinner, aimed at cutting home cost from ~$6,500 to ~$3,000 and build time from 15 days to under 12 hours without sacrificing quality [3d-home-printing-for-the-developing-world-alexandria-lafci-a @ 30:33](https://youtu.be/rvDvGM70oy4?t=1833)
- They found their printing partner Icon by simply talking to as many people as possible about the idea, which surfaced a for-profit team in Austin with the technical chops and shared values [3d-home-printing-for-the-developing-world-alexandria-lafci-a @ 38:09](https://youtu.be/rvDvGM70oy4?t=2289)
- The co-founders stayed disciplined, allocating only about 5-10% of their weekly time to the 3D-printing R&D project in its early days so it wouldn't derail core operations [3d-home-printing-for-the-developing-world-alexandria-lafci-a @ 46:52](https://youtu.be/rvDvGM70oy4?t=2812)
- They credit a highly deliberate, lengthy hiring process (candidates spend a day working in the office before joining) and strong co-founder cohesion — "check egos at the door" — as the foundation of the org's culture and results [3d-home-printing-for-the-developing-world-alexandria-lafci-a @ 52:00](https://youtu.be/rvDvGM70oy4?t=3120)
- Their closing advice: bold ideas attract bold people, and results plus creative, highly personalized outreach (e.g., mailing FedEx packages or Lego kits of the house) are what earn attention from busy, influential people [3d-home-printing-for-the-developing-world-alexandria-lafci-a @ 54:34](https://youtu.be/rvDvGM70oy4?t=3274)
