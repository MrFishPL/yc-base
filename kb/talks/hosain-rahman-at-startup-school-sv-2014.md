# Hosain Rahman at Startup School SV 2014 — Compiled Truth

Jawbone's founder recounts a string of near-death experiences — from a failed speech-recognition pivot to being shut out of his own company — and argues that staying obsessively close to the customer problem, and refusing to give up, is what carried the company through repeated brushes with collapse.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Jawbone started as an attempt to build "Siri for mobile devices," and the team's best periods always tracked staying close to that original customer problem, its worst periods came from straying from it [hosain-rahman-at-startup-school-sv-2014 @ 00:37](https://youtu.be/ZpINPjfSlZc?t=37)
