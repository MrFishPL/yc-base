# Why Your Startup Website Isn't Converting — Compiled Truth

Framer CEO Jorn Van Dijk reviews several startup landing pages, arguing that most sites over-invest in vague animation and marketing copy while failing to show the actual product, which is what really drives conversion.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- If a page has multiple CTAs, make one dominant call to action rather than splitting attention across several similarly-styled buttons [why-your-startup-website-isn-t-converting @ 01:16](https://youtu.be/leQ89XSHILw?t=76)
- A "book a demo" CTA converts poorly on a first-time visit because it asks for a big commitment (a 20-minute call) before the visitor understands what the product even is [why-your-startup-website-isn-t-converting @ 01:16](https://youtu.be/leQ89XSHILw?t=76)
- Vague generic illustrations don't anchor understanding — real screenshots or a short product video walkthrough communicate far more than abstract graphics [why-your-startup-website-isn-t-converting @ 01:36](https://youtu.be/leQ89XSHILw?t=96)
- Animation is powerful for drawing attention and making a point obvious, but is easy to overdo, and first-time animators should get feedback from someone experienced [why-your-startup-website-isn-t-converting @ 04:18](https://youtu.be/leQ89XSHILw?t=258)
- Visitors decide whether a site is "for them" within about 5-10 seconds, so messaging must be made obvious immediately rather than requiring visitors to piece it together [why-your-startup-website-isn-t-converting @ 10:20](https://youtu.be/leQ89XSHILw?t=620)
- Letting users experience the product before forcing signup (e.g. a live demo, a prompt box, or a "try for free" flow) gets people to the aha moment faster than gating everything behind login [why-your-startup-website-isn-t-converting @ 14:10](https://youtu.be/leQ89XSHILw?t=850)
- Testimonial quotes should look and read like real quotes; oddities like every sentence being capitalized or missing company names make readers doubt their authenticity, which undermines trust in the whole page [why-your-startup-website-isn-t-converting @ 36:12](https://youtu.be/leQ89XSHILw?t=2172)
- Visual style should match the target customer and positioning — an enterprise/call-center product needs visuals that reinforce scale and complexity, not a single-consumer-style phone screen [why-your-startup-website-isn-t-converting @ 24:24](https://youtu.be/leQ89XSHILw?t=1464)
- Small copy and spacing errors (double spaces, inconsistent fonts, misaligned line widths) create a subconscious impression that the team isn't detail-oriented [why-your-startup-website-isn-t-converting @ 31:25](https://youtu.be/leQ89XSHILw?t=1885)
- When a landing page tries to communicate too much at once, a good exercise is to strip it down to only what helps a visitor understand what the product is, and test that stripped version on people unfamiliar with it [why-your-startup-website-isn-t-converting @ 39:13](https://youtu.be/leQ89XSHILw?t=2353)
