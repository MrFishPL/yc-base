# YC SUS: Michael Seibel and Eric Migicovsky discuss How to Launch an MVP — Compiled Truth

Michael Seibel and Eric Migicovsky answer startup school forum questions live, giving concrete tactics for building, launching, pricing, and pivoting off an MVP.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- If you can find even a small population of people with a problem bad enough that they'll use something scrappy, that's a strong signal; needing a polished product to get any users at all is a sign the market is already well served [yc-sus-michael-seibel-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-how-to-lau @ 01:58](https://youtu.be/Z_ZzQmmSN5Y?t=118)
- A good MVP hack is to layer your product on top of an existing tool your users already use (e.g. a Chrome extension) so you can find and test with those users with minimal build effort [yc-sus-michael-seibel-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-how-to-lau @ 02:19](https://youtu.be/Z_ZzQmmSN5Y?t=139)
- When entering a business you don't have personal experience in, treat your first customers as an expensive education and immerse yourself in the industry (even volunteering to do the work) to learn what's actually generalizable [yc-sus-michael-seibel-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-how-to-lau @ 05:23](https://youtu.be/Z_ZzQmmSN5Y?t=323)
- Prioritize features by finding pain points users are already actively trying to solve (they're already shopping for alternatives), rather than pitching people to change an entire workflow [yc-sus-michael-seibel-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-how-to-lau @ 06:16](https://youtu.be/Z_ZzQmmSN5Y?t=376)
- For user-generated-content platforms, seed content yourself by scraping from existing sources (9gag scraped memes, early Reddit did the same, CrunchBase built profiles from press mentions) rather than waiting for users to generate it [yc-sus-michael-seibel-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-how-to-lau @ 09:31](https://youtu.be/Z_ZzQmmSN5Y?t=571)
- For hardware or regulated/high-risk products, build a much smaller off-the-shelf version of the vision (e.g. Embark started with a rigged golf cart) to demonstrate founder capability, and get letters of intent or pilot agreements as pre-launch sales evidence [yc-sus-michael-seibel-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-how-to-lau @ 13:04](https://youtu.be/Z_ZzQmmSN5Y?t=784)
- When there is real marginal cost to delivering your service, charge from day one, and charge more than you're comfortable with — pricing high forces a real test of whether people want the product versus just want it cheap [yc-sus-michael-seibel-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-how-to-lau @ 20:25](https://youtu.be/Z_ZzQmmSN5Y?t=1225)
- After launch, be a skeptic about your own MVP and repeatedly ask "is this providing any value to my users" — if not, find the small group in enough pain to use it even in bad form (citing Superhuman's iteration-by-demographics approach) [yc-sus-michael-seibel-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-how-to-lau @ 25:18](https://youtu.be/Z_ZzQmmSN5Y?t=1518)
- To decide whether to keep pushing on a struggling idea, time-bound the effort (e.g. six months) rather than judging by "have I tried everything," since it's easy to trick yourself you've exhausted ideas but hard to fake whether you worked on it for six months [yc-sus-michael-seibel-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-how-to-lau @ 29:12](https://youtu.be/Z_ZzQmmSN5Y?t=1752)
- Do things that don't scale to gather feedback early: call users yourself, invite them to your office, host dinners, put them in a WhatsApp group — high-bandwidth contact matters because you don't yet know the right questions to ask [yc-sus-michael-seibel-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-how-to-lau @ 33:16](https://youtu.be/Z_ZzQmmSN5Y?t=1996)
- Structure early pitches as: what you're working on now, traction, business model, then vision — leading with the big vision alone leaves investors no way to evaluate whether you can execute [yc-sus-michael-seibel-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-how-to-lau @ 36:41](https://youtu.be/Z_ZzQmmSN5Y?t=2201)
- Be upfront with early B2B customers that they're part of a beta rather than hiding it — customers who want to work closely with the founder and shape the product are often willing to pay more for that access [yc-sus-michael-seibel-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-how-to-lau @ 45:20](https://youtu.be/Z_ZzQmmSN5Y?t=2720)
- Patents are rarely a critical early step; focus on finding product-market fit first since most patent concerns are speculative problems for a future you haven't earned yet [yc-sus-michael-seibel-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-how-to-lau @ 49:28](https://youtu.be/Z_ZzQmmSN5Y?t=2968)
- If your business depends entirely and permanently on a platform like YouTube, be very cautious building a real company on it, since platforms now have every incentive to acquire or kill high-revenue dependents rather than stay open [yc-sus-michael-seibel-and-eric-migicovsky-discuss-how-to-lau @ 51:14](https://youtu.be/Z_ZzQmmSN5Y?t=3074)
