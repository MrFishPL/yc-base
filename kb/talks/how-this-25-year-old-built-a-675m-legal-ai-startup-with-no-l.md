# How This 25-Year-Old Built A $675M Legal AI Startup (With No Legal Experience) — Compiled Truth

Legora CEO Max Junior Strand describes how a non-lawyer founding team built an AI workspace for lawyers by talking to 100 lawyers, focusing on reliability before scaling sales, and selling top-down to senior partners rather than bottom-up.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Legora's founding was sparked by observing a lawyer friend spend four months manually summarizing court cases, and the team started with a simple stock option contract reader before GPT-3.5 enabled a broader end-to-end legal system [how-this-25-year-old-built-a-675m-legal-ai-startup-with-no-l @ 02:34](https://youtu.be/pHuXCzM2ntU?t=154)
- Selling to law firms was long considered nearly impossible for legal software companies, but Legora aligned incentives with a "we win if you win" framing and found that low-differentiation legal work becomes easy to disrupt once one competitor breaks the pricing equilibrium [how-this-25-year-old-built-a-675m-legal-ai-startup-with-no-l @ 11:39](https://youtu.be/pHuXCzM2ntU?t=699)
- Enterprise legal sales works by convincing one senior partner and team to become "rock stars" with the product first, since you cannot do a bottom-up motion in an industry where software goes through procurement and IT security checks [how-this-25-year-old-built-a-675m-legal-ai-startup-with-no-l @ 27:15](https://youtu.be/pHuXCzM2ntU?t=1635)
- Before scaling sales, the team deliberately paused new sales for four to five months after a raise to fix reliability and scalability, because a lawyer's first login experience is the one chance you get before they don't come back [how-this-25-year-old-built-a-675m-legal-ai-startup-with-no-l @ 31:55](https://youtu.be/pHuXCzM2ntU?t=1915)
- To learn an unfamiliar industry, the founder interviewed 100 lawyers by offering to pay their hourly rate for lunch, which built the relationships and domain understanding needed to build credible product [how-this-25-year-old-built-a-675m-legal-ai-startup-with-no-l @ 18:41](https://youtu.be/pHuXCzM2ntU?t=1121)
- Legora hired other former founders as some of its earliest employees because founders bring agency and problem-solving attitude, and the company runs internally like "multiple companies within the company" [how-this-25-year-old-built-a-675m-legal-ai-startup-with-no-l @ 33:35](https://youtu.be/pHuXCzM2ntU?t=2015)
- The company avoids building horizontal, broad products and instead advises vertical AI founders not to compete with AI labs, but to find narrow categories or creative model applications the labs won't reach, comparing it to how AI scribing needs custom domain-specific prompting [how-this-25-year-old-built-a-675m-legal-ai-startup-with-no-l @ 42:03](https://youtu.be/pHuXCzM2ntU?t=2523)
- Growing from 10 to 100 people in a year required shifting from being an individual contributor to delegating and hiring people better than yourself across different scaling stages [how-this-25-year-old-built-a-675m-legal-ai-startup-with-no-l @ 33:13](https://youtu.be/pHuXCzM2ntU?t=1993)
- The company chose to stay headquartered in Stockholm rather than move to San Francisco, using Europe's many smaller markets to prove a repeatable expansion playbook before entering the more competitive and narrowing US market [how-this-25-year-old-built-a-675m-legal-ai-startup-with-no-l @ 39:41](https://youtu.be/pHuXCzM2ntU?t=2381)
