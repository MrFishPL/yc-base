# Do Technical Founders Need Business Co-Founders? — Compiled Truth

Dalton and Michael argue that a startup doesn't strictly need a "business" co-founder — every non-coding task (sales, fundraising, hiring, customer support, back office) can be done well by a technical founder, and what actually matters is who on the team has the appetite to own those tasks.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Every company has non-coding tasks that must get done — incorporation, banking, payroll, taxes, talking to customers, hiring, sales, fundraising, customer support — and none of these inherently require someone who can't code [do-technical-founders-need-business-co-founders @ 01:09](https://youtu.be/43RhhwpiSk0?t=69)
- A technical co-founder is just as qualified as a non-technical co-founder to do these "smart generalist" tasks, since it's easier for a technical person to learn general business tasks than for a business person to learn to code [do-technical-founders-need-business-co-founders @ 02:26](https://youtu.be/43RhhwpiSk0?t=146)
- The real differentiator isn't ability but appetite — having the willingness to adopt these tasks and do them with vigor, rather than doing them grudgingly or treating the work as beneath you [do-technical-founders-need-business-co-founders @ 03:11](https://youtu.be/43RhhwpiSk0?t=191)
- If a technical founder is solving their own problem (e.g., a dev tool), a "business" co-founder isn't even obviously the right person to sell, fundraise, hire, or talk to customers [do-technical-founders-need-business-co-founders @ 04:12](https://youtu.be/43RhhwpiSk0?t=252)
- Many of the most valuable public companies (Google, Nvidia, Microsoft) and top YC companies (Stripe, Dropbox) were founded entirely by technical co-founders, proving a business co-founder is not strictly necessary [do-technical-founders-need-business-co-founders @ 06:09](https://youtu.be/43RhhwpiSk0?t=369)
- An exception: when selling into a specialized industry (e.g., legal or medical software), having a co-founder with domain expertise and fluency in the customer's language can genuinely help [do-technical-founders-need-business-co-founders @ 07:24](https://youtu.be/43RhhwpiSk0?t=444)
- When investors tell a founder "you need a business co-founder," they are often really giving indirect feedback about a perceived deficiency in that founder's appetite or execution quality, not a literal skills gap [do-technical-founders-need-business-co-founders @ 08:20](https://youtu.be/43RhhwpiSk0?t=500)
- The underlying reason you need a co-founder at all isn't to import a specific business skill set, but to have someone to go through the hard experience with and provide emotional support [do-technical-founders-need-business-co-founders @ 09:52](https://youtu.be/43RhhwpiSk0?t=592)
