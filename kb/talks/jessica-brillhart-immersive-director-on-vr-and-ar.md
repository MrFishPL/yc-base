# Jessica Brillhart, Immersive Director, on VR and AR — Compiled Truth

Jessica Brillhart, former principal VR filmmaker at Google, describes leaving to found her own immersive-media company and lays out a craft philosophy for VR storytelling built around "potential story" rather than traditional narrative telling.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- After feeling unable to steer Google's VR efforts toward what she thought mattered, she concluded the best course of action was to start her own company, naming it VRAI/"crai" after the French word "vrai" (true), blending visual arts, mixed reality, and AI. [jessica-brillhart-immersive-director-on-vr-and-ar @ 03:09](https://youtu.be/GTUUVqee_yI?t=189)
- She saw VR and Google's machine-learning team as parallel, converging mediums, and collaborated with the Deep Dream team to apply neural-style "dreaming" effects to VR footage as an experimental, off-roadmap side project. [jessica-brillhart-immersive-director-on-vr-and-ar @ 05:19](https://youtu.be/GTUUVqee_yI?t=319)
- Her central craft argument is that VR should abandon "storytelling" (kinetic, actively telling someone something) in favor of "potential story" — crafting worlds that transmit a core truth however a person chooses to explore them. [jessica-brillhart-immersive-director-on-vr-and-ar @ 10:01](https://youtu.be/GTUUVqee_yI?t=601)
- When she left Google she had no specific project lined up; she spent time surveying the landscape and, out of frustration, made a one-day "VR gif" (a Weather Channel parody) that became her most-loved piece because it was instantly, humorously understandable to anyone. [jessica-brillhart-immersive-director-on-vr-and-ar @ 19:03](https://youtu.be/GTUUVqee_yI?t=1143)
- Her practical process for building any immersive piece: write the story as a long-winded block on a whiteboard, then hack away at it until only the essential "truth statement" remains, and design the whole experience to keep transmitting that truth regardless of how someone engages. [jessica-brillhart-immersive-director-on-vr-and-ar @ 44:39](https://youtu.be/GTUUVqee_yI?t=2679)
- She argues VR and AR are complementary technologies whose individual weaknesses offset each other — e.g., an accessible phone-based AR experience can build an emotional relationship with users that then funnels them into VR. [jessica-brillhart-immersive-director-on-vr-and-ar @ 31:15](https://youtu.be/GTUUVqee_yI?t=1875)
- She believes VR needs to expand deliberately into education, architecture, and other professional use cases beyond entertainment and games, warning that fast-growing technologies otherwise just become "a hammer looking for nails." [jessica-brillhart-immersive-director-on-vr-and-ar @ 50:02](https://youtu.be/GTUUVqee_yI?t=3002)
- Asked where VR will be in ten years, she predicts the term "VR" itself won't survive — the medium will merge with AR and other immersive tech into something with a different name. [jessica-brillhart-immersive-director-on-vr-and-ar @ 29:39](https://youtu.be/GTUUVqee_yI?t=1779)
- She cautions against building tech that lets people access and relive every personal memory perfectly, arguing there is something poetic and important about forgetting that shouldn't be engineered away. [jessica-brillhart-immersive-director-on-vr-and-ar @ 38:13](https://youtu.be/GTUUVqee_yI?t=2293)
