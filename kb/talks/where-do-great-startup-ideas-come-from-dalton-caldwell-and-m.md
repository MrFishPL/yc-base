# Where Do Great Startup Ideas Come From? – Dalton Caldwell and Michael Seibel — Compiled Truth

Using Airbnb, Coinbase, and Stripe as case studies, great startups are rarely first-movers — they enter markets with a bad incumbent product, get dismissed as bad ideas by experts, and end up bigger than the founders imagined.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Airbnb wasn't first — VRBO and Couchsurfing already existed — but those products failed to facilitate payments and VRBO charged hosts to list, leaving room for a 10x-better product [where-do-great-startup-ideas-come-from-dalton-caldwell-and-m @ 02:30](https://youtu.be/Jcuqq48CNj8?t=150)
- Airbnb's founders were solving their own problem (needing rent money during a conference) and only discovered the need to facilitate payments after using their own product and getting mistaken for a fraud [where-do-great-startup-ideas-come-from-dalton-caldwell-and-m @ 03:37](https://youtu.be/Jcuqq48CNj8?t=217)
- At the time, renting a room from a stranger felt scary and "uncool," and the community ethos around Couchsurfing viewed charging money as impure — conventional wisdom said build the network first, charge later [where-do-great-startup-ideas-come-from-dalton-caldwell-and-m @ 05:19](https://youtu.be/Jcuqq48CNj8?t=319)
- Before Coinbase, buying bitcoin meant sending money orders to foreign countries or trusting Mt. Gox, which got hacked and lost user funds — the existing options were so bad a simple, non-sketchy way to buy bitcoin was enough to build a huge company [where-do-great-startup-ideas-come-from-dalton-caldwell-and-m @ 07:35](https://youtu.be/Jcuqq48CNj8?t=455)
- Brian Armstrong's own YC application undersold the core insight (simple bitcoin buying), pitching instead a P2P payments angle, and Coinbase still struggled to raise money on demo day despite building a massive company [where-do-great-startup-ideas-come-from-dalton-caldwell-and-m @ 10:21](https://youtu.be/Jcuqq48CNj8?t=621)
- Before Stripe, accepting credit cards required a merchant account application as painful as a mortgage — Stripe's founders had personally felt this pain and saw their YC peers suffer through the same "authorize.net" problem repeatedly [where-do-great-startup-ideas-come-from-dalton-caldwell-and-m @ 12:49](https://youtu.be/Jcuqq48CNj8?t=769)
- Stripe won by obsessing over developer experience — building the most beautiful docs and website in payments, running an exclusive beta invite, and even pricing itself as the most expensive option to prove real demand [where-do-great-startup-ideas-come-from-dalton-caldwell-and-m @ 14:44](https://youtu.be/Jcuqq48CNj8?t=884)
- Stripe faced "hot takes" dismissing two teenagers entering the heavily regulated banking space against an entrenched incumbent (PayPal) that could easily copy them [where-do-great-startup-ideas-come-from-dalton-caldwell-and-m @ 16:45](https://youtu.be/Jcuqq48CNj8?t=1005)
- The overarching lesson: don't worry if an existing product already dominates a market users hate, don't defer to experts who lack firsthand pain with the problem, and be wary of underestimating market size since 10x-better products unlock unforeseen use cases [where-do-great-startup-ideas-come-from-dalton-caldwell-and-m @ 19:40](https://youtu.be/Jcuqq48CNj8?t=1180)
