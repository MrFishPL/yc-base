# How to Create Luck - Dalton Caldwell, Y Combinator Partner — Compiled Truth

Dalton Caldwell argues that "luck" in startups is manufactured by moving much faster than everyone else, which multiplies the number of chances a founder gets to stumble onto something that works.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Luck is created by moving faster than everyone else, which gives founders more opportunities to talk to customers, change ideas, and write code, increasing their surface area for a lucky break [how-to-create-luck-dalton-caldwell-y-combinator-partner @ 00:00](https://youtu.be/NmEyx9TEkRw?t=0)
- Brex was originally funded to build a VR headset; the founders got "lucky" only because they quickly realized within about six weeks that the idea wasn't working and iterated through half a dozen other ideas before landing on the corporate card business [how-to-create-luck-dalton-caldwell-y-combinator-partner @ 01:01](https://youtu.be/NmEyx9TEkRw?t=61)
- Retool and Magic are cited as other companies that dramatically increased their "surface area of luck" by rapidly building and even launching prototypes of different ideas until one took off [how-to-create-luck-dalton-caldwell-y-combinator-partner @ 02:00](https://youtu.be/NmEyx9TEkRw?t=120)
- Before starting a company, founders create luck by meeting and learning from as many interesting people as possible, and by doing things that make them uncomfortable rather than waiting at home to be discovered [how-to-create-luck-dalton-caldwell-y-combinator-partner @ 02:17](https://youtu.be/NmEyx9TEkRw?t=137)
- Mark Zuckerberg's pre-Facebook habit of constantly building small prototypes (like Winamp music plugins) and giving them to people is offered as the model for how founders should build and distribute things quickly to create their own luck [how-to-create-luck-dalton-caldwell-y-combinator-partner @ 03:01](https://youtu.be/NmEyx9TEkRw?t=181)
- On playing to your strengths versus going outside your expertise: if you have zero relevant technical skills, tackling something like curing cancer as a total outsider is a poor bet, but domain outsiders can still succeed if they have the technical skills to build the product themselves [how-to-create-luck-dalton-caldwell-y-combinator-partner @ 03:53](https://youtu.be/NmEyx9TEkRw?t=233)
- At Series A and later, founders keep creating luck by maintaining a culture of fast-moving experimentation and by hiring people who themselves move fast enough to get lucky too [how-to-create-luck-dalton-caldwell-y-combinator-partner @ 04:58](https://youtu.be/NmEyx9TEkRw?t=298)
