# Signs Your Company Is Recovering From ZIRP — Compiled Truth

Dalton and Michael argue that companies are slowly recovering from the excesses of the zero-interest-rate-phenomenon (ZIRP) era, and that many changes employees dislike are actually strong signs of a healthier, more accountable company.

- Turnover on the executive team, especially "big company" executives used to infinite resources and low accountability, is a good sign of recovery, not a bad one [signs-your-company-is-recovering-from-zirp @ 02:11](https://youtu.be/kVGvC7Ix_nc?t=131)
- Founders becoming more hands-on and involved again after a period of stepping back is a healthy correction, even if it creates friction with executives hired during ZIRP [signs-your-company-is-recovering-from-zirp @ 02:58](https://youtu.be/kVGvC7Ix_nc?t=178)
- Companies mandating return to office is a sign senior management has gotten serious about productivity, even if individually unpopular [signs-your-company-is-recovering-from-zirp @ 04:41](https://youtu.be/kVGvC7Ix_nc?t=281)
