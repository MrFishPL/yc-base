# When Should You Trust Your Gut? — Compiled Truth

Whether a founder should "commit" (trust their gut) or "validate" (talk to users) depends on whether they already have real expertise in the problem, not on a one-size-fits-all rule.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The extreme "trust your gut" comic-book version is talking to no customers at all and just manifesting a vision, like the popular myth of Steve Jobs and the iPhone [when-should-you-trust-your-gut @ 01:26](https://youtu.be/2ViqdR5E2wU?t=86)
- The opposite extreme is having zero opinions and outsourcing all ideas to user interviews, which is effectively an investor-style hedge rather than a founder's conviction [when-should-you-trust-your-gut @ 02:04](https://youtu.be/2ViqdR5E2wU?t=124)
- Founders who built and shipped a valuable tool inside a company they used to work at usually know more than they think and should trust that instinct more [when-should-you-trust-your-gut @ 03:01](https://youtu.be/2ViqdR5E2wU?t=181)
- A good self-test for founders with real expertise is "make something that would impress you" — if you can judge whether it's good because it solves a problem you've lived, you have a real signal [when-should-you-trust-your-gut @ 04:11](https://youtu.be/2ViqdR5E2wU?t=251)
- A red flag that your gut isn't trustworthy yet is having no strong opinions on what to work on, or opinions indistinguishable from the mainstream 50th percentile [when-should-you-trust-your-gut @ 05:20](https://youtu.be/2ViqdR5E2wU?t=320)
- Founders without domain expertise aren't doomed, but part of their job becomes building expertise during the startup rather than bringing it in on day one [when-should-you-trust-your-gut @ 05:59](https://youtu.be/2ViqdR5E2wU?t=359)
- The failure mode is inverted confidence: the expert founder gets fearful and hedges (e.g. bolting on AI just to seem fundable) while the inexperienced founder overbuilds a "comprehensive" two-year plan based on market-size reports [when-should-you-trust-your-gut @ 08:35](https://youtu.be/2ViqdR5E2wU?t=515)
- Founders without expertise should build the smallest thing possible, stay open-minded, and ideally pick a problem area they're genuinely excited to learn about [when-should-you-trust-your-gut @ 10:40](https://youtu.be/2ViqdR5E2wU?t=640)
- The same startup idea (e.g. mortgage-underwriting software) warrants very different advice depending on whether the founders have 10 years of industry experience or none at all [when-should-you-trust-your-gut @ 06:57](https://youtu.be/2ViqdR5E2wU?t=417)
