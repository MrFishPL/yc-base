# Nate Blecharczyk at Startup School 2013 — Compiled Truth

Airbnb co-founder Nate Blecharczyk recounts the company's slow, grinding path from "airbed and breakfast" to hockey-stick growth, emphasizing that choosing co-founders is irreversible, traction beats pitching, and doing unscalable manual work with early users made the difference.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Airbnb's growth looked like a compounding hockey stick — a 73x increase in guests over three years since founders first attended Startup School. [nate-blecharczyk-at-startup-school-2013 @ 01:44](https://youtu.be/Ya0I6oz7q9U?t=104)
- Choosing co-founders is the most important decision you'll make: you can pivot your idea but you can't easily change your partners. [nate-blecharczyk-at-startup-school-2013 @ 07:11](https://youtu.be/Ya0I6oz7q9U?t=431)
- The original "air bed and breakfast" idea came from a scrappy need to cover rent by renting out air mattresses to designers during a sold-out conference. [nate-blecharczyk-at-startup-school-2013 @ 08:19](https://youtu.be/Ya0I6oz7q9U?t=499)
- The decision to handle payments up front came after a host had an awkward experience with a guest who kept forgetting to pay — the goal was protecting the hospitality experience, not building a business model. [nate-blecharczyk-at-startup-school-2013 @ 12:15](https://youtu.be/Ya0I6oz7q9U?t=735)
- After being told investors "don't want M's, they want B's," they revised their 3-year revenue projection slide upward the night before a VC pitch — and it still didn't work. [nate-blecharczyk-at-startup-school-2013 @ 18:16](https://youtu.be/Ya0I6oz7q9U?t=1096)
- Paul Buchheit's advice at YC: it's better to have a few users who love you than a thousand who merely like you. [nate-blecharczyk-at-startup-school-2013 @ 23:53](https://youtu.be/Ya0I6oz7q9U?t=1433)
- Following Paul Graham's "do things that don't scale" advice, the founders flew to New York, met every one of their ~40 users in person, took professional photos, and personally rewrote listings and prices. [nate-blecharczyk-at-startup-school-2013 @ 24:38](https://youtu.be/Ya0I6oz7q9U?t=1478)
- Curating those ~40 New York listings by hand is when the listings actually started getting booked and traction began. [nate-blecharczyk-at-startup-school-2013 @ 26:17](https://youtu.be/Ya0I6oz7q9U?t=1577)
- After Sequoia's Greg McAdoo re-pitched their own idea back to them better than they had, they signed a term sheet within two weeks and raised $600K in seed funding. [nate-blecharczyk-at-startup-school-2013 @ 27:43](https://youtu.be/Ya0I6oz7q9U?t=1663)
