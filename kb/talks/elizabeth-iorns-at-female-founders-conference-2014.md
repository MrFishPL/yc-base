# Elizabeth Iorns at Female Founders Conference 2014 — Compiled Truth

Science Exchange founder Elizabeth Iorns shares nine lessons from three years of building a marketplace for scientific experiments, from picking a problem to persisting through hard times.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Solve your own problem: Iorns founded Science Exchange after struggling as a breast cancer researcher to farm out experiments, echoing Paul Graham's advice that the best problems are ones that affect you personally [elizabeth-iorns-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 02:28](https://youtu.be/CII2SmXOB60?t=148).
- Solve a real problem, not just a small one, since founding a company is equally hard whether the problem is big or small, so validate it by talking to potential users and shipping an MVP fast [elizabeth-iorns-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 04:13](https://youtu.be/CII2SmXOB60?t=253).
- Build the right team by finding people who share your vision, not just people with great skills, and deliberately mix with people outside your own field to cover different skill sets [elizabeth-iorns-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 06:05](https://youtu.be/CII2SmXOB60?t=365).
- Focus on company culture early: Science Exchange defined its values with the acronym FORCE (Fearless, Open, Respectful, Curious, Entrepreneurial) and reinforces them monthly with a team award [elizabeth-iorns-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 07:20](https://youtu.be/CII2SmXOB60?t=440).
- Choose investors and advisors who share your company's values, since "investors are employees you can never fire," citing her board member Andy Weissman of Union Square Ventures as an example [elizabeth-iorns-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 08:41](https://youtu.be/CII2SmXOB60?t=521).
- "Get done" by using a quarterly planning meeting plus two-week agile sprint cycles for both product and business teams to create momentum and transparency [elizabeth-iorns-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 09:43](https://youtu.be/CII2SmXOB60?t=583).
- Do fewer things by outsourcing non-core infrastructure (payments, hosting, A/B testing) to other YC-alumni tools instead of building everything in-house [elizabeth-iorns-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 11:21](https://youtu.be/CII2SmXOB60?t=681).
- Do things that don't scale, following Airbnb's example, and focus deeply on your first 1,000 users and on customer support, which is a place startups can easily beat large companies [elizabeth-iorns-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 12:04](https://youtu.be/CII2SmXOB60?t=724).
- Don't give up: after being negatively branded "the eBay for science," the team had to work hard and persist to earn trust from the scientific community as genuine scientists themselves [elizabeth-iorns-at-female-founders-conference-2014 @ 14:29](https://youtu.be/CII2SmXOB60?t=869).
