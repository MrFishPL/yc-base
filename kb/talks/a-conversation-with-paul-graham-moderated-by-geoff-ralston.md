# A Conversation with Paul Graham - Moderated by Geoff Ralston — Compiled Truth

In a freewheeling conversation, Paul Graham draws on his own Viaweb experience to explain why startups are counterintuitive, why determination beats intelligence, why founders should launch early and do unscalable things, and why competitors rarely matter as much as founders think.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- Even a perfect startup picker would still need to fund around 20 startups to get roughly 5 giant outcomes, because outcomes are governed by a power law and a huge amount of irreducible luck [a-conversation-with-paul-graham-moderated-by-geoff-ralston @ 08:01](https://youtu.be/4WO5kJChg3w?t=481)
- When picking co-founders or hires, find someone you trust and then trust who they vouch for on intelligence — but be careful, because trustworthy people are often bad judges of *trustworthiness* in others and get fooled by sneaky schemers [a-conversation-with-paul-graham-moderated-by-geoff-ralston @ 20:01](https://youtu.be/4WO5kJChg3w?t=1201)
- "Doing things that don't scale" means serving early customers in a handmade, artisanal, obviously-unscalable way anyway, because you learn enormously from it even though you know you can't keep doing it forever [a-conversation-with-paul-graham-moderated-by-geoff-ralston @ 23:57](https://youtu.be/4WO5kJChg3w?t=1437)
- Startups almost never die from competitors — in YC's dataset only about 1 in 1,900 companies was killed by a competitor, so founders should run their own race rather than reacting to a rival [a-conversation-with-paul-graham-moderated-by-geoff-ralston @ 31:28](https://youtu.be/4WO5kJChg3w?t=1888)
- Determination matters more than intelligence for founder success: a highly determined but less-smart founder will outperform a brilliant but undetermined one [a-conversation-with-paul-graham-moderated-by-geoff-ralston @ 35:22](https://youtu.be/4WO5kJChg3w?t=2122)
- The single biggest mistake founders make is not paying enough attention to users — building an internally-generated "vision" alone in a cafe instead of doing the humbling work of talking to users and facing harsh feedback [a-conversation-with-paul-graham-moderated-by-geoff-ralston @ 40:43](https://youtu.be/4WO5kJChg3w?t=2443)
- When co-founders have unequal commitment, the practical test is: would you rather have 30% of this person's time/brain, or 100% of someone else's — for a truly exceptional co-founder, partial commitment can still be the easy choice [a-conversation-with-paul-graham-moderated-by-geoff-ralston @ 46:00](https://youtu.be/4WO5kJChg3w?t=2760)
- Launch as soon as you have a "quantum of utility" — at least one real person who is genuinely glad the product exists — rather than waiting until it feels finished [a-conversation-with-paul-graham-moderated-by-geoff-ralston @ 51:47](https://youtu.be/4WO5kJChg3w?t=3107)
