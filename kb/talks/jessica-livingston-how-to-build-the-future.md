# Jessica Livingston : How to Build the Future — Compiled Truth

Jessica Livingston, co-founder of Y Combinator, explains that the most successful startups stay hyper-focused on building product and talking to users while avoiding distractions, and that determination in founders matters more than idea quality or intelligence.

**Key ideas (with timestamps):**
- The most successful founders are totally focused on building their product and talking to users, and do not let themselves get distracted by anything else [jessica-livingston-how-to-build-the-future @ 01:37](https://youtu.be/nFOC-cgIWaY?t=97)
- Distractions that look like real business but derail early founders include chasing big-company partnerships, doing PR before the product is nailed down, meeting corp dev people, and networking at conferences [jessica-livingston-how-to-build-the-future @ 02:25](https://youtu.be/nFOC-cgIWaY?t=145)
- You know you've hit product-market fit when you can measure growth, users come back, and they're willing to pay, with growth rate going up [jessica-livingston-how-to-build-the-future @ 03:13](https://youtu.be/nFOC-cgIWaY?t=193)
- It's more important to get started building something than to get the idea exactly right, since the idea will evolve — Airbnb morphed from renting airbeds during conferences to renting whole homes over about a year [jessica-livingston-how-to-build-the-future @ 06:28](https://youtu.be/nFOC-cgIWaY?t=388)
- Airbnb's founders convinced YC largely on the strength of the founders themselves, not the idea, and their own experience as hosts gave them insights no one else had [jessica-livingston-how-to-build-the-future @ 13:27](https://youtu.be/nFOC-cgIWaY?t=807)
- Determination is the single most important founder trait, ranked above intelligence and past success, followed by understanding users and building a great user experience, and being flexible-minded [jessica-livingston-how-to-build-the-future @ 15:26](https://youtu.be/nFOC-cgIWaY?t=926)
- Having a co-founder is critical because solo founders face extreme emotional burden with no peer for moral support, but founders should already know their co-founder well rather than bolting on someone met at a hackathon [jessica-livingston-how-to-build-the-future @ 23:56](https://youtu.be/nFOC-cgIWaY?t=1436)
- Y Combinator itself started as a lightweight, small experiment funding eight companies in a batch to learn about angel investing, and moved to Silicon Valley quickly to avoid being copied [jessica-livingston-how-to-build-the-future @ 18:45](https://youtu.be/nFOC-cgIWaY?t=1125)
- Y Combinator's core differentiator has been staying founder-friendly and not optimizing to squeeze the best deal terms, because YC wasn't primarily driven by making money [jessica-livingston-how-to-build-the-future @ 29:41](https://youtu.be/nFOC-cgIWaY?t=1781)
- Aspiring founders should learn to code, build things to solve their own problems, work with other people (especially in college) to find co-founders, and consider working at an early-stage startup first [jessica-livingston-how-to-build-the-future @ 32:53](https://youtu.be/nFOC-cgIWaY?t=1973)
